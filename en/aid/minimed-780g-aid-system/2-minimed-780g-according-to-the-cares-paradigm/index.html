    2. MiniMed 780G according to the CARES paradigm (3.5 min)

    2. MiniMed 780G according to the CARES paradigm (3.5 min)¶

    Welcome to this lesson about the MiniMed 780G according to the CARES paradigm.

    Let's begin our exploration of the MiniMed 780G insulin pump, delving into its insulin delivery calculation and adjustment methods, the process of reverting to manual mode, user education on its usage, and the unique features of its sensor and share function. We'll also cover its indications and the importance of software updates. Let's get started!

    1) The Minimed 780G according to the CARES paradigm

    #1 Calculate¶

    The MiniMed 780G operates by delivering microboluses of insulin every 5 minutes, based on the sensor glucose value and the total insulin delivery over the past 2 to 6 days.

    Additionally, an autocorrection bolus is given every 5 minutes when the glucose levels exceed 120 mg/dl or 6.7 mmol/l and the maximum basal insulin dose is reached. These autocorrection boluses are constrained to a maximum of 8% of the total daily insulin dose within the last 45 minutes.

    You can set the target value at 100, 110, or 120 mg/dl or 5.6, 6.1, or 6.7 mmol/l.

    The MiniMed 780G incorporates a helpful feature called Safe Meal Bolus, which aims to prevent hypoglycemia by adjusting your insulin dose at mealtimes. If the system predicts a potential risk of hypoglycemia, it may lower your insulin dose or even suggest not bolusing at all for that particular meal. The primary goal of this feature is to ensure your safety and prevent low blood sugar levels.

    #2 Adjust¶

    The MiniMed 780G allows you to adjust various parameters:

    • Target value: You can choose between 100, 110, or 120 mg/dl (or 5.5, 6.1, or 6.7 mmol/l).

    • Temporary target value: You can set a temporary higher target value of 150 mg/dl or 8.3 mmol/l specifically for sports activities. It allows for an adjustable duration of up to 24 hours. It's important to note that if you use the temporary target value, no autocorrections will be given during that period.

    • Carbohydrate ratio: You can modify the ratio based on your individual needs.

    • Active insulin time: This parameter determines how long the system considers the insulin to be active in your body and can be set between 2 and 8 hours. It is a means to make the algorithm more aggressive and is usually set at 2 hours.

    • Autocorrection boluses: you have the option to disable this in the system, although they are usually left enabled as they enhance the SmartGuard algorithm.

    #3 Revert¶

    There are certain conditions that will cause the MiniMed 780G to revert back to manual mode:

    • If the maximum basal is given for more than 7 hours or the minimal basal is given for 3-6 hours, you will be asked to enter a blood glucose (BG) or go into baseline delivery (which is the same as the former safe basal) for 4 hours. If the problem is not fixed after 4 hours, you will revert to manual mode.

    • You will also revert back to manual mode if there is no sensor data for more than 4 hours, indicated by a blue shield with three dots.

    • Lastly, if there are concerns about the integrity of the sensor, the MiniMed 780G might revert back to manual mode.

    In CareLink, you can review the reason for auto mode being stopped. In most cases, auto mode is discontinued because of sensor failure.

    In manual mode, the system automatically activates the suspend-before-low function, provided it is enabled in the settings. To switch back to auto mode, you will need to perform a finger prick test.

    #4 Educate¶

    When starting with an automated insulin delivery system like the MiniMed 780G, it is important to be educated on both general automated insulin delivery system concepts and specific details of the MiniMed 780G.

    The general education points covered in previous modules include:

    • Using fewer carbohydrates to correct hypoglycemia.

    • Considering infusion set problems if blood glucose levels remain high without an apparent reason.

    • Trusting the automated insulin delivery system and avoiding unnecessary manual interventions.

    • Timing meal boluses 15 minutes before meals.

    • Utilizing Exercise Mode 1 to 2 hours before activity.

    • Stopping insulin delivery if the pump is disconnected for more than 15 minutes.

    • Responding to alarms and being mindful of automatic updates on mobile phones.

    • And seeking peer support for guidance and sharing experiences.

    In addition to the general education, specific education points for the MiniMed 780G include:

    • If possible, opt for the lowest target value of 100 mg/dl or 5.5 mmol/l and the shortest active insulin time of 2 hours for a more aggressive algorithm and improved time-in-range results.

    • Avoid fake carbohydrate entries as it can lead to hypoglycemia and disrupt the system's calculation of basal insulin.

    • To maintain auto mode, comply with the MiniMed 780G's request for additional finger pricks. These finger pricks are necessary for the algorithm and are different from sensor calibrations. Follow the pump's instructions, which will indicate when a finger prick is required through the "BG required" message and countdown timer.

    • If the entered BG is not accepted and the pump asks for a new BG, wait at least an hour before doing so. Failing to wait could increase the likelihood of your sensor being completely deactivated.

    • Be aware of the Safe Meal Bolus feature, which can reduce insulin delivery during meals to prevent hypoglycemia. Verify this before making any adjustments to your carbohydrate ratio. If you have a low carbohydrate ratio (like less than 5 grams per unit), you may be at a higher risk of experiencing the Safe Meal Bolus effect. If you encounter postprandial hyperglycemia due to this feature, consider using a higher carb ratio. If you find that your mealtime insulin is still insufficient, you can try adding additional carbs to your meal, such as increasing the amount by 20%.

    • Autocorrection boluses are limited to a maximum of 8% of the total insulin dose in the last 45 minutes. If you are experiencing high glucose levels and have not received any autocorrection boluses, you can restart them by performing a finger prick test.

    • Understand the distribution of the total daily insulin dose in CareLink reports. Autocorrection boluses are included in the bolus percentage, so a relatively higher bolus percentage may be observed (for example 30% basal and 70% bolus instead of 50/50). Ideally, the percentage of autocorrection boluses should be kept below 30% of the bolus percentage.

    #5 Sensor¶

    The MiniMed 780G works with the Guardian 4 sensor and, in some regions, also with the newer Simplera Sync and Instinct sensors. Availability of these sensor options varies by region, and not all are currently accessible everywhere.

    If you have doubts about the accuracy of your sensor reading or do not feel it reflects your condition, perform a finger prick test to confirm. If there is a significant difference between your sensor glucose and finger prick reading, calibrating the Guardian 4 or Simplera sensor is an option, although it may lead to sensor failure.

    • It's important to note that if the finger prick value differs from the sensor glucose by more than 36 mg/dl or 2 mmol/l, the sensor will display an error and request a second finger prick test.

    • If the subsequent blood glucose entry still shows a significant difference from the sensor glucose, the sensor will be stopped. To avoid sensor loss, you may consider delaying the second glucose entry.

    This is why it is advisable not to link your blood glucose meter to your pump, as finger prick tests would then be automatically accounted for as calibrations, potentially leading to sensor failure. Another option is to wait an hour before doing a new finger prick test.

    A common issue with the Guardian 4 sensor is the 'sensor update' alarm. This alarm indicates that the sensor is not functioning properly, as it has temporarily lost the subcutaneous signal, and the system is trying to reestablish it. There can be several reasons for this signal disruption. For guidance on how to address this issue, please refer to the module on the Guardian 4 sensor for helpful tips and troubleshooting.

    #6 Share¶

    The MiniMed 780G allows you to share insulin and glucose data with your healthcare provider and family members. Real-time monitoring of glucose values is possible for both parties.

    • If you use the MiniMed Mobile app, the data is accessible in real-time on CareLink, eliminating the need for manual pump readings.

    • Data from the pump can also be manually uploaded to CareLink using the blue USB stick.

    • Healthcare providers can access the data through CareLink .

    • Family members and caretakers can track the data by downloading the CareLink Connect app as a mirror app if the user is utilizing the MiniMed Mobile app.

    2) Indications¶

    The MiniMed 780G has both a CE-label and FDA-label for use in individuals with type 1 diabetes who are at least 7 years old. In Europe, the CE approval extends its use to children as young as 2 years old. The system is also approved for adults aged eighteen years and older with insulin-dependent type 2 diabetes.

    It is suitable for individuals with a minimum total daily insulin dose of 8 units.

    The system is approved for use with Novorapid, Novolog or Humalog 100 U/ml insulins. The use of Fiasp and Lyumjev 100 U/ml is approved in Europe.

    Please note that, as of the latest update of this course, the MiniMed 780G is approved for use during pregnancy in Europe, but it is not approved for use in individuals requiring dialysis.

    3) Software updates¶

    The MiniMed 780G pumps with software version 6.7 or higher can be updated if necessary. To use the Simplera Sync or Instinct sensor, your pump might need a higher software version. You can check your pump’s software version by going to Device Info in the Settings menu.

    You can update the software version of your pump via the MiniMed Mobile app, as long as Medtronic has approved the update for your pump’s serial number.

    • The update download, which requires an internet connection, may take 45 to 90 minutes, depending on your internet speed.

    • After downloading the update on your phone, you’ll need to install it on your insulin pump. This installation process takes 10 to 20 minutes, during which you must pause insulin delivery and disconnect your pump.

    • Once updated, the pump will enter Manual Mode since the SmartGuard function requires a 5-hour warm-up period. Therefore, it’s best to avoid updating in the evening and to enable the suspend-before-low function.

    In conclusion, to maximize the performance of the MiniMed 780G, it is crucial to familiarize yourself with its features, parameters, and recommended practices. Should you encounter any difficulties or have questions, don't hesitate to reach out to Medtronic's customer service, known for their friendly and helpful assistance. Additionally, always consult with your healthcare provider for personalized guidance in managing your diabetes journey. Together, with knowledge, support, and resilience, you can navigate the path to better health and well-being.

    3. Linking components together

    Welcome, in this video, we will guide you on how to create links between the different components of the MiniMed 780G automated insulin delivery system.

    The MiniMed 780G system operates as follows:

    • Glycemia is measured every 5 minutes by the Guardian 4, Simplera Sync or Instinct sensor.

    • The sensor transmits the glycemia readings to the MiniMed 780G insulin pump, which calculates the insulin delivery and adjusts it automatically.

    • The MiniMed 780G can be manually uploaded to CareLink via the blue CareLink USB, or it can be linked to the MiniMed Mobile app via Bluetooth, allowing continuous data transmission to CareLink.

    • The CareLink account can also be connected to the CareLink Connect app for caregivers.

    • Additionally, the MiniMed 780G pump can be paired with the AccuCheck Guide Link glucose meter, enabling automatic transmission of blood glucose readings to the pump.

    In this video, we will cover the process of establishing these different links, including:

    1. Pairing the sensor to the MiniMed 780G insulin pump.

    2. Pairing the AccuCheck Guide Link blood glucose meter with the MiniMed 780G insulin pump.

    3. Connecting the MiniMed Mobile app to the MiniMed 780G insulin pump.

    4. Creating a CareLink account.

    5. Manually uploading pump data to CareLink via the blue CareLink USB.

    6. Linking the MiniMed Mobile app with CareLink.

    7. And linking the CareLink Connect app with a CareLink account.

    So, let's get started and learn how to establish these important connections within the MiniMed 780G system.

    3.1 Sensor >\< MiniMed 780G¶

    *Guardian 4

    If you’re using the Guardian 4 sensor, the Guardian 4 transmitter must first be paired to the MiniMed 780G pump. When paired, the pump and transmitter communicate with each other through Bluetooth.

    Only one transmitter can be paired with the pump. If a transmitter is already paired with the pump, delete the transmitter and then continue.

    - To pair the pump and transmitter attach the transmitter to the charger. Fully charge the transmitter and keep the transmitter attached to the charger. Note both lights on the charger are off when the transmitter is fully charged.

    - If the red light on the charger starts blinking, it means the charger battery is low and needs to be replaced with a new AAA battery.

    - After confirming that the transmitter is fully charged, place the transmitter still attached to the charger next to the pump.

    - Go to the paired devices option on your pump and select pair new device. The searching screen appears. Note the search process can take up to two minutes.

    - Remove the transmitter from the charger. The transmitter light flashes 10 times and turns off.

    - The select device screen appears with a list of available devices. Select the CGM device that matches the serial number indicated on the back of the transmitter. If the transmitter's serial number on the pump screen matches the serial number on the back of the transmitter select confirm. Press the back button if the serial number is incorrect. If the connection is successful a pairing successful message appears on the pump. When the transmitter is communicating with the pump the sensor feature is turned on and appears on the home screen

    - To ensure the transmitter is functioning correctly, you can use the green tester. When you plug it in, the green light should come on, indicating that the transmitter is working.

    *Simplera Sync¶

    When using the Simplera Sync sensor, it activates as soon as you place it on your body.

    To connect your Simplera Sync sensor to the MiniMed 780G pump, follow these steps:

    • Go to the "Paired Devices" option on your pump and select "Pair New Device."

    • The pump will detect the Bluetooth signal from the inserted Simplera Sync sensor.

    • You’ll then be prompted to confirm the sensor code, after which the sensor will be connected.

    A screen will appear showing a countdown timer for the remaining warmup time of your sensor.

    • If you’ve just inserted the sensor, the warmup time will be 2 hours.

    • If you pre-inserted the sensor, the pump will either show the remaining warmup time

    • or display the sensor values immediately if the sensor has been pre-inserted for more than 2 hours.

    *Instinct CGM¶

    When using the Instinct sensor, you’ll need the MiniMed Mobile app on a compatible smartphone paired with your MiniMed 780G pump to use the sensor.

    On your phone, open the MiniMed Mobile app. Tap the Menu button, then select Start sensor, and tap to confirm "Yes, Instinct." After applying the sensor, tap Next in the MiniMed Mobile app. You can follow the “How to scan a sensor” steps in the app — which adjusts automatically depending if you’re using an iOS or Android device — or scan on your own. Make sure your pump is on the Home screen, with no active alarms or alerts. Then hold your phone near the sensor. If you’re using an iOS device, hold the top of your device near the sensor. If you’re using an Android device, hold the back of your device near the sensor. Once you hear the beep or feel the vibration, this confirms that the sensor has been started and begins the warm-up period.

    When the sensor is starting, keep your pump close to the sensor until pairing is complete. You’ll see a warm-up message on both the pump and app home screen. Once pairing is successful, the connection icon turns green. Once the countdown ends, your pump will begin receiving sensor glucose (SG) readings.

    3.2 AccuCheck Guide Link blood glucose meter >\< MiniMed 780G¶

    As previously mentioned, some people prefer not to connect the Accu-Chek Guide Link blood glucose meter, as doing so may lead to sensor failure during the calibration process.

    However, if you still wish to pair your Accu-Chek Guide Link blood glucose meter, you can follow these steps:

    • Turn on the meter and go to the settings menu.

    • Select wireless, then pairing, and choose to pair with the pump.

    • On the pump, go to Paired Devices, select Pair New Device, click on Meter and confirm the meter's serial number.

    The meter will then appear as a paired device, and blood glucose readings will be automatically sent to the pump.

    After performing a finger prick test with the Accu-Chek Guide Link meter, it is important to press the back arrow on the meter. This action will send the blood glucose value faster to the MiniMed 780G pump. On the pump, you can then confirm the value of the finger prick and proceed with any necessary adjustments to your insulin delivery.

    3.3 MiniMed Mobile app >\< MiniMed 780G¶

    To connect the MiniMed Mobile app to the MiniMed 780G insulin pump, you can follow these steps:

    - Download the MiniMed Mobile app and choose the pair device option in the app menu.

    - Follow the prompts on the screen that explain how the app works. - Create or sign into your CareLink personal account. - Read the agreement and tap agree. - Follow the prompts and select get started. - Select your pump and pair your pump by selecting 'Allow'. - Allow use of Bluetooth by selecting 'OK'. - Read the dosing disclaimer, then tap agree. The app is now ready to use.

    It's important to note that the MiniMed Mobile app is designed as a viewing tool and does not have the functionality to administer boluses or modify settings. Its primary purpose is to provide users with access to their sensor and insulin data, allowing them to monitor their glucose levels, insulin delivery, and time in range. Any adjustments or actions regarding bolus administration or settings should be made directly on the MiniMed 780G insulin pump itself.

    3.4 Creating a CareLink account¶

    Users, caregivers, and healthcare providers can create a CareLink account on the CareLink server.

    • Users can create a CareLink Personal account for free at carelink.minimed.com by agreeing to the terms, and selecting a username and password. It's recommended to consult with your diabetes team regarding any specific guidelines for choosing a username and password. Often, a combination of your name and date of birth is suggested for easy password retrieval if needed. Make sure to keep track of your username and password for convenient access to upload your pump data as necessary.

    • Healthcare providers can request a free CareLink Clinic account from Medtronic and access it via carelink.medtronic.eu or carelink.medtronic.com using their provided username and password. This account provides healthcare providers with an overview of their patients and their respective information.

    If you have a CareLink Clinic account, you have the option to link it to a CareLink Personal account. Here's how you can do it:

    1. In your CareLink Clinic account, navigate to the patient section.

    2. Add the user to your patient list if this has not been done yet.

    3. Within the patient profile, select the option to link to a CareLink Personal account.

    4. Here you can generate a unique code to share with your patient, send a link request using the patient’s username or link now using the patient’s username and password.

    By following these steps, you can successfully establish a connection between your CareLink Clinic account and the patient's CareLink Personal account for seamless data sharing and monitoring.

    3.5 Manually uploading pump data to CareLink via the blue CareLink USB¶

    To manually upload the MiniMed 780G insulin pump to CareLink, follow these steps:

    • Log into your CareLink account and click on the Upload button in CareLink to initiate the upload process.

    • If this is not done yet, you will be prompted to install the uploader software on your computer.

    • Follow the software's instructions, including plugging in the blue CareLink USB, selecting Paired Devices on the pump, and linking to CareLink.

    • Keep the pump near the connected blue USB during data transfer.

    • The uploading process may take 5-10 minutes, so it's recommended to perform this at home to avoid delays during consultations.

    3.6 MiniMed Mobile app >\< CareLink¶

    Linking the MiniMed Mobile app to CareLink is very easy

    • The MiniMed Mobile app automatically syncs data with CareLink by default when you link the app to your CareLink account during setup.

    • Note that the data transmission occurs via Wi-Fi or your data plan, and additional charges may apply based on your subscription.

    • The data is transferred to your CareLink account every 5 minutes, but it may take up to 24 hours for the latest information to appear in your CareLink reports. To have the most up-to-date data in your reports, you can select the "Upload Now" option in the MiniMed Mobile app.

    3.7 CareLink Connect app >\< CareLink¶

    Finally, you can also connect a caregiver or family member to a personal CareLink account via the CareLink Connect app.

    As a caregiver you can download the CareLink Connect app and follow the prompts. Create your account. After you read through the terms, you have to enter your login details. Enter your information and verify your email. When the enrollment is complete, the app is ready to be used. Press proceed to login and send a follow request by entering the CareLink personal account user name. Once the request is approved, the CareLink Connect app serves as a mirror app. You will also be able to view time in range and history data.

    By following these steps, you can successfully establish the necessary links between the components of the MiniMed 780G automated insulin delivery system, enabling seamless communication and data transmission for effective diabetes management.
