    4. How the DBLG1 System with Kaleido works (11 min)¶

    Welcome to this lesson on the functionality of the DBLG1 system with Kaleido. Controlling your insulin delivery is done through the dedicated DBLG1 handset.

    In this video, we will delve into the functionality of the DBLG1 handset in general, and then explore how you can use it in both Closed Loop mode or Auto Mode, and Open Loop mode or Manual Mode.

    4.1 DBLG1 handset¶

    The DBLG1 handset is a locked mobile phone that operates on a rechargeable lithium battery. It is recommended to charge the handset using the supplied cable through the microUSB port at the bottom. The battery can last up to 35 hours, and it is advisable to charge it every night. While charging, you can continue to use the handset. Caution: the DBLG1 handset is not waterproof.

    The handset has a single button on the right side, which is used to turn it on and off by pressing and holding it. The DBLG1 can take up to 5 minutes to start when it has been switched off. The rear camera can be used to scan the QR code of the Dexcom G6 sensor and transmitter during the pairing process..

    If no action has been taken on the DBLG1 for more than a few seconds, the screen switches to sleep mode. Briefly press the ON/OFF button to turn the screen back on. The handset is always locked with a PIN code, which should be changed from the default 0000 to a personal PIN for security.

    Upon turning on the handset and after pairing with the Dexcom G6 and with an insulin pump, you will be greeted by the home screen.

    • The status bar at the top displays notifications, Bluetooth connection, network signal, battery status, date and the time.

    • When swiping down, you can see the remaining life of the sensor, the pump's battery level and the remaining amount of insulin in the cartridge.

    • The main menu icon on the top left leads to the main menu, while the ZEN mode button on the top right allows you to enable or disable it. When turned on, the button and the whole screen is green; when turned off, it's gray.

    • The center of the screen shows the Dexcom G6 sensor reading with a trend arrow, and the color of the circle around it represents the hypo- and hyperglycemia thresholds you have set. The active insulin on board is displayed below the sensor glycemia if there is any and if Loop mode is on. The Loop mode status is shown below the sensor glycemia on the right.

    • The sensor glycemia curve and events of the past three hours are displayed in the lower section of the screen.

    • At the bottom, there are buttons to declare meals and physical activities.

    When you tap on the glucose curve on the home screen, it enters landscape mode which shows a history graph, where you can view your glycemia curve and insulin delivery for the past few hours. Three distinct graphs show

    • your glycemia, along with your hypoglycemia and hyperglycemia thresholds, your rescue carbs intakes, your physical activities, and your meals,

    • any administered correction or meal boluses

    • and your basal rate

    You can slide back and forth with your finger to see previous data.

    Within the main menu of the DBLG1 handset, you have several options to navigate and configure the DBLG1 system.

    • You can tap on the profile image to see your personal profile as defined during the initialization phase of your DBLG1.

    • Under rescue carbs, you can announce a rescue carb intake.

    • Via System Status you can pair your sensor and pump, start and stop your sensor, pump and loop mode, and calibrate your sensor.

    • Via History you can see your daily graph, see events like alarms, alerts and notifications, manage your declared meals and physical activities, see your glycemia related statistics, see your calibrations, declare an external bolus injected with a pen or syringe and see the administered boluses.

    • Via Settings you can adjust your medical settings, DBLG1 settings and see your profile information.

    • Via the help sections you can find support on the various components of your DBLG1 system. You can also test the sounds for alarms, alerts and notifications and check your software version and the general release notes of the software version.

    We will delve a little deeper in the settings menu and go over the Medical settings and the DBLG1 settings.

    In the Medical settings,

    • you can set your hypo- and hyperglycemia threshold.

    • Via Loop mode, you can set your target glucose level and the aggressiveness in normoglycemia, in hyperglycemia and for each type of meal.

    • Via Loop mode OFF you can set 3 alerts for when your Loop mode is off but your Dexcom sensor is connected. This is a signal loss alert, a hyperglycemia alert and a hypoglycemia alert.

    • Via Patient, you can set your weight and height, average amount of carbs per meal type, your basal safety profile and your total daily insulin dose.

    • Finally via the Medical settings, you can also adjust the target glucose level and the duration of Zen mode.

    In the DBLG1 settings, you can adjust the sound and vibration of your alerts, change your PIN code or login details of Yourloops, set Confidential mode or airplane mode, change the language used by the DBLG1 handset, display settings, and time settings. And finally you can also check here for software updates or set the update time on your handset.

    These menu options and settings allow you to personalize and configure your DBLG1 handset.

    4.2 Loop mode ON or Auto Mode (Closed Loop mode)¶

    Starting Loop Mode¶

    In the DBLG1 algorithm, Auto Mode is called Loop mode ON. To activate this on the DBLG1 handset, simply tap "Start” in the Loop mode section in the System Status menu. This is only possible if the pump and sensor are properly paired.

    Once Loop mode is active, you can see that all check marks in the System Status are green, and that the Loop mode logo on the homescreen is also green. If you tap on the glucose graph, you will see that your basal insulin delivery is modified automatically every 5 minutes according to your need.

    Using Physical Activity and Zen mode¶

    To utilize the Physical Activity mode, simply access the Physical Activity button on the home screen. From there, you can input the name or type of the activity, start time, duration, and intensity of your planned physical activity, and then confirm your selections.

    In Loop mode ON, you have the option to start or stop Zen mode directly from the home screen. Zen mode will automatically end after 3 hours, but you have the flexibility to adjust the duration, up to a maximum of 8 hours.

    Adjusting parameters¶

    During the intialization phase, the DBLG1 handset will prompt you to enter your total daily insulin dose, weight, and average grams of carb intake during breakfast, lunch, and dinner, as well as your safety basal profile.

    Later you can adjust the following parameters in the settings:

    • Target glucose level and aggressiveness via loop mode settings

    • Hypoglycemia threshold via thresholds

    • And total daily insulin dose, weight, average meals and basal safety profile via the patient settings.

    Delivering a bolus¶

    To administer a meal bolus, simply press the Meal button and follow these steps:

    • First select the time of your meal. Remember, it is recommended to announce meals 15 minutes in advance to allow the algorithm to account for them. If you forget to announce your meal, you can enter a meal in the past. In such cases, the system might suggest an alternative bolus based on your current glucose level.

    • Next, enter the amount of carbs you are going to eat. You can also choose a preset portion size, like small, average or large meal. The proposed portion sizes can be set via the settings. If you select Medical, then Patient and then Favorite meals, you can set different portion sizes for breakfast, lunch and dinner.

    • Finally you can select if your meal is high fat or not, and press Confirm. DBLG1 will display a “bolus recommendation alert” with a certain amount of insulin, which you can change or confirm.

    • While it is possible to override the system's advice, it is generally recommended to follow the recommendations for optimal learning and effectiveness.

    • Finally, you will be asked to confirm again the administration of the meal bolus.

    • On the homescreen, you'll see "Bolus in progress" during the bolus.

    Here are some things to keep in mind about giving a meal insulin bolus with the DBLG1 system:

    • If a meal is announced in advance, the alert with the recommended bolus will be displayed 6 minutes before the designated mealtime. Remember to validate the alert bolus recommendation, to ensure the bolus is administered.

    • The DBLG1 algorithm may decide to deliver the bolus in one or two parts, and if it suggests a two-part bolus, you don't need to confirm the second part separately. It will be automatically delivered.

    • If the algorithm is currently giving an autocorrection or a meal bolus, you will not be able to give a bolus. You will need to wait for it to complete, before declaring a meal to administer a meal bolus.

    • If you want to enter carbohydrates without receiving insulin, such as in the case of treating hypoglycemia, you have to input them as rescue carbohydrates via the menu.

    Suspending insulin¶

    To temporarily stop the pump, such as when you need to disconnect it for activities like contact sports or enjoying a jacuzzi, follow these steps:

    • Go to "System Status" and select "More."

    • Then, press the "Stop" button to halt the pump's operation.

    • If you want to prevent alarms from being triggered because the handset is no longer connected to the sensor, some users choose to turn off the handset using the ON/OFF button.

    4.3 Loop Mode OFF or Manual Mode (Open Loop mode)¶

    If the Loop mode of the DBLG1 handset is not functioning, you can still manually control your pump using the DBLG1 handset. The integrated bolus calculator is not available in this mode.

    • The "Pump control" button allows you to initiate or stop a bolus and/or temporary basal rate adjustments.

    • To adjust the basal insulin delivery in Loop mode OFF, navigate to the Main menu, select Settings, medical, then Patient, and choose "Basal safety profile."

    Here you can see and modify the basal insulin rate when Loop mode is OFF.

    If you forget your handset, you will only receive your safety basal profile, and you will not be able to administer an insulin bolus for meals or corrections.

    In this video, we explored the functionality of the DBLG1 system, with a focus on the DBLG1 handset and its features and settings. Remember, you can do it!

    If you ever need additional guidance, don't forget to consult the manual or your healthcare provider for further assistance.
