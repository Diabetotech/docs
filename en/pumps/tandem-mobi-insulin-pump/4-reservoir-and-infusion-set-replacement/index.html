    4. Reservoir and infusion set replacement (7.5 min)

    4. Reservoir and infusion set replacement¶

    Welcome to this lesson on reservoir and infusion set replacement on the Tandem Mobi insulin pump!

    In this video, we will cover the different infusion set options, step-by-step instructions for replacing the reservoir and infusion set, and helpful tips for success.

    4.1 Different infusion sets¶

    The Autosoft 90 infusion set is the most widely used infusion set for the Tandem pump. Other alternatives include the Autosoft 30, the Varisoft with an angled cannula, and the TruSteel with a steel cannula.

    Additionally, Tandem provides different lengths of tubing. Most people use the short tubing of (12 cm or 5 inches) to keep the pump close to the infusion site on the skin, but you can also opt for the longer tubing options of (60 to 110 cm or 23 to 43 inches) to wear the pump in your pocket.

    A detailed overview of these infusion sets, including tubing and cannula lengths and filling volumes, can be found in the download below this video.

    4.2 Reservoir & Infusion Set Replacement¶

    Start by washing your hands and ensuring your workspace is clean. Gather these items: a reservoir, a cartridge, your insulin (at room temperature), an infusion set, some alcohol wipes, and your pump.

    #1 Reservoir replacement¶

    Make sure your hands are clean and you've swabbed the top of the vial with alcohol first. Let's prepare the new cartridge. You do this by pulling down the fill rod and then pushing it back up to force air out of the cartridge. Next pull the fill rod down to the desired volume. You can measure this by using the top ring on the plunger to align with the desired amount of insulin. Now it's time to fill the cartridge. Start by placing the insulin vial upright on a flat surface. You'll push the vial adapter down onto the vial. Next push the fill rod down to force air from the cartridge into the vial. Be sure to maintain pressure on the fill Rod. Then turn the set with the vial still secured upside down and slowly release the fill Rod. Insulin will begin to flow from the vial into the cartridge. From here, you can slowly pull back the fill Rod to the desired amount of insulin. Keep in mind that there must be at least 30 units of insulin remaining in the cartridge once the tubing is filled. Next tap and rotate the cartridge to release any potential air bubbles. If you see air bubbles, bubbles push up on the fill Rod to force them out. Keep in mind that pushing out air bubbles may also push out some insulin. Make sure to check that you have enough insulin in the cartridge. Finish the fill process by removing the vial from the vial adapter.

    Next, it's time to remove the cartridge from the set. Start by unscrewing the fill Rod counterclockwise then gently release the cartridge from the set. Now unscrew the

    vial adapter counterclockwise to remove it from the t-lock connector.

    You'll be prompted to remove the empty cartridge from the pump by

    rotating it counterclockwise. Be sure the piston on your pump is in the down position. It should look like this, not this. Now place the newly filled cartridge on the pump and rotate it clockwise until it clicks into place then tap continue.

    Be sure you're following all proper disposal methods for needles, cartridges, set components and infusion sets.

    #2 Filling the tube¶

    Next you'll fill the tube. Confirm that the infusion set is disconnected from your body. Attach tubing from a new infusion set to the t-lock connector on the cartridge. Then tap continue. Hold the pump with the t-lock connector facing up. This will make sure any air in the cartridge will be pushed through the tubing first. Then tap yes on the mobile app. Make sure that your infusion set is disconnected from your body. Then press and hold the pump button to fill the infusion set tubing with insulin. Hold the pump upright and keep the pump button pressed, until you see drops of insulin at the end of the infusion set. Once you see drops of insulin release the pump button. This process is much faster with tandem Mobi than with the t-slim X2 pump. So pay close attention. Keep in mind that the pump may pause at regular intervals during the process. This is normal and allows you to check for drops of insulin. Tap yes if you see drops of insulin at the end of your tubing

    #3 Infusion set placement¶

    Now, we're ready to apply the infusion set. We will demonstrate the Autosoft 90 infusion set. Start by sanitizing your skin and peel away the paper backing from the infusion set patch. Be sure to keep the infusion set firmly in place on the needle during this process. Next, prep the insertion set by tugging on the white plastic until you hear a click. At this point, you can safely remove the needle guard. Locate one of the two depressions on the insertion device and insert the tube. Position the prepared device on your cleaned skin and press the needle into place by applying gentle pressure on the two circular depressions. Press the white plastic one more time. Lastly, take care to gently remove the insertion device.

    Don't forget to click "Finish" when prompted to continue insulin delivery. If you forget, an alarm will sound as a reminder.

    You've now successfully replaced the insulin cartridge and infusion set! Remember to do this at least once every three days, even if the cartridge is not yet empty.

    #4 Cannula Filling¶

    When you fill the tubing of the Autosoft infusion set, the cannula is filled automatically. If you ever replace only the infusion set but keep the existing tubing, you should manually fill the cannula. This can be done through the same pump menu. Fill the cannula of the Autosoft 90 infusion set with 0.3 units for 6-mm needles or 0.5 units for 9-mm needles. Angled cannula's need a filling volume of 0.7 units, and steel cannula's do not need to be filled.

    4.3 Do's & don’ts

    • The insulin approved in the Tandem Mobi Insulin pump is NovoLog or NovoRapid and Humalog with a concentration of 100 U/ml..

    • Remember, 2 hours after inserting a new set, you should check your blood glucose. Checking your blood glucose is the only way to confirm your infusion set is properly inserted and that you're receiving insulin.

    • Prior to disposing of the insertion device, reattach the lid until it clicks.

    • When temporarily removing your insulin pump, it’s best to cover the connect piece of your infusion set. If you leave the protective cover open, dirt may enter into the opening which may cause infections or occlusions.

    • Make sure you're inspecting the tubing for air bubbles. Air and the tubing occupy space where insulin should be. And this could affect insulin delivery. Remember that changes in temperature or weather can cause bubbles. If you find bubbles in your cartridge, consider wearing the pump with the tubing angled down or to the side to help prevent the air from getting into the tubing. If you see air bubbles in the tubing, disconnect the infusion site from your body. Then under the load cartridge menu, select fill tubing and prime the tubing until the air is purged completely out. This will push the air out of the tubing.

    • Be aware that some skin care products such as lotions, sunscreens and insect repellant can cause cracks in the plastic of the cartridge or pump. Do not allow these products to come into contact with the pump or cartridge. Remember to always wash your hands before handling your pump or cartridge after using these types of products.

    So now, you're equipped with the knowledge to replace the insulin cartridge and infusion set of the Tandem Mobi Insulin pump. If you make a mistake, don't worry – it's perfectly normal. Keep calm, follow the steps. Remember, if all else fails, there's always the Customer Service and a lot of useful YouTube videos available. Also, don't forget that you can contact your diabetes team, who also have an on-call service, for medical support. Keep up the good work! Good luck!
