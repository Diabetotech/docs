    2. Pump Operation (7 min)

    2. Pump Operation¶

    Welcome to this video on how the Kaleido insulin pump works! In this segment, we will explore the parts and buttons of the pump and handset, how to charge them, how to unlock the handset, the home screen and menu of the handset, and some do's and don'ts.

    2.1 Parts & buttons¶

    The Kaleido insulin pump consists of two main components: the pump and the handset, which controls the insulin delivery of the pump.

    The pump is very small, weighing only 19 grams. It consists of a durable and colorful pump base that can be recharged and reused for 4 years, and a disposable insulin cartridge that can be used for 3 days. There are no buttons on the pump, only a space for the insulin reservoir, and a groove for the tubing. The cartridge has to be manually filled with insulin. The insulin cartridge comes with the tubing attached, which can be a short tubing to place the pump on your body, or a long tubing to keep the pump in your pockets. The infusion sets come in separate packaging, and should be connected to the tubing connector.

    The handset is also small and weighs only 60 grams. On the bottom there is a micro USB port and a speaker. The micro-USB port is used to charge the rechargeable lithium battery and for data upload. The handset has a power button on top, and a color screen in the front. Below there are 4 buttons to navigate up and down, and left and right. And in the center there is an “enter” button. On the back of the handset, you can find specifics of the handset like the serial number.

    The handset should be kept with you at all times, as it is not only necessary for delivering boluses but also for responding to alarms. Please note that there are no buttons on the pump, so control must be managed using a separate device. The handset communicates with the pump via Bluetooth and needs to be within a range of 2 meters (or 6.5 feet) to establish a connection. Once programmed, the pump does not require continuous communication with the handset for basal insulin delivery.

    2.2 Charging the pump base and handset¶

    It is advisable to charge the handset using the provided cable and charger. The optimal practice is to charge the battery each night while you sleep, ensuring a fully charged battery for approximately two days of use. When charging for the first time or when the battery is completely depleted, it may take up to 4 hours to reach a full charge.

    When the battery is fully drained, the handset will power off, but the pump will continue delivering basal insulin. You can continue to use the handset while it is charging.

    For each of your Kaleido pumps, which you wear for maximum three days at a time, it's a good practice to charge them promptly after removal so they're ready for your next use. This can be done by placing an empty pump into the dedicated charging dock, using the same charging cable as for your handset. An orange light will indicate the charging process. Once your pump is fully charged, the status light on the dock will change from orange to green, signifying that your pump is ready for use. Charging the pump takes about 2 hours.

    To remove your pump from the charging dock, simultaneously press the release buttons on both sides of the charging dock – there are two buttons in total. This should release the pump without requiring excessive force.

    2.3 Unlocking the handset¶

    You can send your handset to sleep mode at any time by briefly pressing the Power button. This will help you save your handset battery. You can wake it up again at any time just by pressing the Power button or the Enter button.

    It is also possible to set a passcode for the lock screen and an emergency message via the Systems Settings. This message functionality allows you to display the name and phone number of your emergency contact, or you can input your own name and phone number so that individuals can reach you if your device is lost.

    2.4 Home screen and pump menu¶

    The home screen is divided into five sections, each of which leads to a different menu. At the top, you’ll also see handy details like the time and how full your insulin cartridge, pump and handset batteries are.

    The 5 sections are Basal, Bolus, Resource Levels, Menu and Status.

    • Once everything is set, your Basal section will show the basal rate you are currently receiving. If you need to change or review your basal profile, just navigate to Basal and select it by pressing the Enter button.

    • Your Bolus section shows you the details of the last bolus you delivered – that’s the date, time and the amount of insulin you got. When you need to deliver your next bolus, navigate to Bolus using the buttons and press Enter.

    • If you select the Resource levels, you can see the level of your handset battery, pump battery, amount of insulin in the cartridge, insulin on board, and the maximum time until the next insulin cartridge placement.

    • Via the Menu, you can check your History, do a data upload and check and edit Personal Information and System Settings. Although the handset stores up to 3 months of data, the History only shows the data of the past 8 days.

    • To pause, stop and restart your pump, navigate to Status, press Enter and go from there.

    Each menu ends with a back or home option, to navigate to the previous screen or home screen.

    2.5 Do's & don’ts¶

    Here are some important do's and don'ts when using the Kaleido insulin pump:

    • We recommend you to save the menu overview provided in the downloads for easy reference. As a healthcare provider, it will help you guide individuals through the pump menu remotely.

    • Kaleido pumps are waterproof up to 1.5 meters or 5 feet for up to 1 hour.

    • The handset is not waterproof, so avoid exposing it to water.

    • Take good care of the handset and pumps, as it should last for approximately 4 years.

    • The handset does not automatically adjust the time, date, and time zone. You need to manually update the time when transitioning between winter and summer time or traveling to different time zones. This can be done in the system settings under the yellow menu option.

    • It’s important to note that it is not possible to change your glucose units in the Kaleido handset. There are different handsets for mg/dl and mmol/l. The type of handset you have is marked on the back.

    With this information, you now have a better understanding of the main features of the Kaleido system. One of the key advantages is the small size of the pump and handset, and the fact that you can wear it on your body, without the hassle of long tubing. The pump is waterproof, but it is also possible to disconnect the pump and temporarily remove it from your body. However, keep in mind that you will need to carry both the pump and the handset, to be able to control your pump. Knowing this in advance will help you adjust and adapt.

    3. Insulin setup

    In this video, we will guide you through the process of setting the insulin delivery on the Kaleido handset.

    Please note that the insulin will only start running once you have successfully paired a pump with your handset.

    We will cover the following topics:

    • Programming basal insulin

    • Setting up temporary basal rates

    • Giving an insulin bolus

    • and some extra do’s and don'ts.

    Let's get started!

    3.1 Programming basal insulin¶

    Before you can start using the Kaleido pump, you’ll need to set a basal insulin rate on your handset.

    • To do this, go to the home screen, scroll to Basal and press Enter to select. Scroll to and select Create new profile.

    • Press the left or right buttons to scroll between each hourly segment. You have to set a basal rate for all 24 hours of the day. Press the up or down buttons to select the basal rate recommended by your healthcare provider for each hour. Below the basal profile graph, you’ll see details of the hour you’re currently setting, along with the total basal insulin dose. When you’ve entered a basal rate for each hour and your new basal profile is ready, press the Enter button. Press Enter again to select Next.

    • Next up, it’s time to enter a name for your new basal profile. Use the up or down buttons to scroll to the letter or number you want to use. Press the right button to move onto the next letter or enter a space. To scroll through the alphabet quickly, just press and hold the up or down button. When you’ve named your basal profile, press Enter and select Save.

    • Your handset will confirm that your basal profile has been saved. Press Enter to return to the Home screen a little quicker.

    Many people opt to use different profiles for different situations, for example work day, weekend, sports and illness. Your handset can store up to seven basal profiles at a time.

    If you need to check which basal profile your pump is using, you can go to the basal section and select view current profile. Here you can also change your profile, or edit your profile. It’s important to note that it is not possible to change a profile that is currently active. To edit the current profile, you’ll need to duplicate it first.

    To pause your basal insulin delivery, navigate to the Status section and tap "pause pumping," then confirm with "Yes." Your handset will display the amount of missed insulin since pausing. To resume basal insulin delivery, tap "continue pumping." If you opt to "stop pumping," your handset disconnects from the pump. To restart, access the Status screen, where your handset will ask whether you need to fill your cannula or not. Select the appropriate option, and your pump will restart insulin delivery.

    3.2 Temporary basal¶

    If you want to temporarily change the amount of basal insulin you’re receiving, you’ll need to set a temporary basal rate.

    • To do this, go to the Basal section via the homescreen, and select Set temporary basal rate.

    • Here you can set a basal insulin rate percentage between 0% to 200%. Once you have your chosen percentage, press the Enter button.

    • Set the amount of time you’d like your temporary basal rate to run for. You can choose a minimum of 0.5 hours and a maximum of 24 hours. Once you’ve chosen the amount of time, press the Enter button again.

    • Press the Enter button to select Next.

    • Your handset will now show you details of the temporary basal rate that you’ve set. If the details are correct and you’d like to start your temporary basal rate immediately, press Enter to select Deliver.

    • A confirmation will pop up. You can press Enter to return to the Home screen a little quicker.

    • To help you keep track, the Basal section of the Home screen will show you your temporary basal rate details for as long as the temporary basal rate is running.

    It’s important to note that you won't receive an alert when your preset temporary basal rate ends and your normal basal profile resumes.

    To stop the temporary basal, you can go to the Basal section, and select Stop temporary basal rate.

    3.3 Delivering a bolus¶

    You have several options to administer a bolus using the handset. You can utilize the Kaleidoculator, which is an integrated bolus calculator. Or you can opt for a manual Quick bolus, which is a standard bolus, or a manual Extended bolus, which is a dual-wave bolus.

    Before you can use the Kaleidoculator, you have to configure it via the Personal Info in the Menu section. There you can set the Target BG, Insulin to carb ratio, Insulin sensitivity, and the Duration of insulin action. For more information about these parameters, please check the general module on insulin pumps.

    To deliver a bolus with the bolus calculator, navigate to the Bolus section, and choose Kaleidoculator.

    • You will first be asked to enter the grams of carbohydrate you are going to eat. You can enter for example 25 grams and tap Next. If you’re not eating, but want to use Kaleidoculator to help correct your BG, you can leave the number boxes empty and press the Enter button to select Next.

    • You’ll need to enter your current BG reading, then press next. It is also possible to give a bolus without entering a BG-reading. Once you select Next, the BG reading and amount of carbs you’ve entered will be saved in History.

    • The Kaleidoculator will show you your current Insulin on Board, that’s how many units of insulin are still active in your body. You can accept or reject the Insulin on Board to be included in your calculation.

    • The Kaleidoculator will now show you details of its recommended bolus. You have 2 options to choose from:

    • to deliver the recommendation as a Quick Bolus

    • or to deliver the recommendation as an Extended Bolus.

    • Via the next step you can also adjust the amount of insulin to be delivered.

    • If you decide you don’t want to deliver a bolus after all, just scroll left to Cancel. If you still want to give the bolus, tap Next, and then Deliver. The bolus amount that's being delivered is visible on your home screen.

    With the Kaleido handset, you can also give a manual bolus without the bolus calculator. To do that, go to the Bolus section and choose Quick bolus. Here you can choose the amount of insulin you want to give and tap Next and then Deliver.

    For a manual Extended bolus, you can also choose that option. First you enter the amount of insulin you wish to receive, and the amount you wish to deliver immediately, and finally the duration over which you wish to deliver the remaining bolus.

    3.4 Do's & don’ts¶

    Here are some do's and don'ts to keep in mind:

    • Ensure your maximum bolus limit is set slightly above your expected bolus doses. You can set this between 0 and 30 units. 

    • If the Kaleidoculator recommends a bolus that is bigger than the maximum bolus, you’ll get a popup that warns you about this. If you'd like to continue with this high bolus, don’t forget to deliver another bolus of the remaining units after your first bolus has finished.

    • Sometimes, Kaleidoculator may indicate a negative amount of insulin as its recommended bolus for you. If you’re not eating or not eating very much carbs, and if your blood glucose is already below your target blood glucose, you don’t need more insulin, you actually need less. If you select either Quick Bolus or Extended Bolus when your recommended bolus is a negative number, your handset will pop up with a quick reminder to tell you that giving a bolus isn’t the safest thing to do at this time.

    In conclusion, setting the insulin delivery on the Kaleido handset is a straightforward and intuitive process. As a healthcare provider, it is crucial to familiarize yourself with the different pumps and their settings. With Kaleido, you can easily access all the necessary settings under the Basal section and you can find the settings for the bolus calculator under the Personal Info in the Menu section.
