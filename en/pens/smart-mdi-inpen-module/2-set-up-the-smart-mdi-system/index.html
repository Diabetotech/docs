    2. Set Up the Smart MDI System (2.5 min)¶

    Welcome to this lesson on how to set up the Smart MDI System.

    2.1 Components of the Smart MDI System¶

    In this section, we’ll review the core components that make up the Smart MDI system — a connected solution that links insulin dosing with real-time glucose monitoring.

    The system consists of:

    • The Simplera CGM: A wearable continuous glucose monitor that transmits glucose data every 5 minutes to the Simplera app on the user’s smartphone.The InPen: A reusable, Bluetooth-enabled insulin pen designed for rapid-acting insulin. It connects with the InPen app, which features

    • An integrated bolus calculator and smart insulin dose reminders.

    • A standard long-acting insulin pen: Since the InPen is for rapid-acting insulin only, basal insulin must be injected using a separate pen. Users are encouraged to manually log their long-acting insulin doses in the InPen app to maintain a complete data record.

    • Data sharing tools:

    • CareLink allows healthcare teams to access insulin and CGM data remotely.

    • And the CareLink Connect app enables real-time glucose sharing with up to five care partners, such as family members or caregivers.\

    The InPen is thoughtfully designed for usability and safety:

    • It includes a dose knob for selecting insulin in half-unit increments, offering precision dosing for both adults and children.

    • It accommodates a 3 mL insulin cartridge. Two versions are available:

    • The blue InPen is compatible with aspart insulin Novo Nordisk cartridges

    • The grey InPen is compatible with Lilly cartridges

    • The pen has a built-in Bluetooth transmitter, which logs each dose automatically to the app.

    • It contains a non-rechargeable battery that lasts approximately one year. No charging is required.

    Together, these components bring intelligence and automation to diabetes therapy with multiple daily injections — making it easier to track insulin doses, avoid insulin stacking, and make informed insulin decisions based on real-time glucose trends.

    2.2 Set Up the Simplera Sensor & App¶

    This section explains how to set up the Simplera CGM and start using the Simplera app to view and track glucose data.

    The Simplera app is available to download on specific Android and iOS devices. You can check the compatibility online at medtronic-diabetes.com.

    When you open the app, one of the first things you'll need to do is create or log into your CareLink account. Once you've signed in, you'll need to accept a user agreement. Then the app will take you through some phone settings. It will ask if you want to share your data and teach you how to customize alerts. Then it is time to insert and pair your sensor. The app will take you through the onboarding education.

    The Simplera sensor is approved for placement on the back of the upper arm. Children from 2 to 17 years may place the sensor on the upper thirds of the buttocks.

    The sensor has a built-in-Bluetooth transmitter, so make sure Bluetooth is enabled on your smart device. Each time you insert a new sensor, it’s important to remove the old sensor from your Bluetooth list.

    Begin by washing your hands and disinfecting the insertion site. Allow your skin to air dry completely before proceeding. Remove the cap and place the inserter on the back of your upper arm. You can reach this area by either lifting your arm or reaching around it. Make sure all edges of the inserter touch your skin. To insert the sensor, press the inserter against your skin in a fluent movement until you hear a click. Gently pull the inserter away from your body in a straight line. Smooth down the sensor adhesive with your finger to ensure the sensor stays on the body for the entire length of wear. You may use over the counter tape if needed to help the sensor stick better.

    Dispose of the inserter in accordance with local laws and regulations, but keep the serial number and sensor code until expiration time as you may need them in case of sensor failure. In that case you can call Medtronic’s customer service for a replacement.

    When you've inserted the inserter you can tap "I inserted it" on the Simplera app. Confirm the sensor serial number and enter the sensor code, and the app will connect to the sensor.

    After the 2-hour warmup time, you will start getting glucose readings every 5 minutes. Note that the sensor readings within the first 12 hours are not always reliable (as in most glucose sensors from any other brand). So check with finger pricks during the warmup time and also within the first 12 hours if you don’t feel confident about the glucose shown on the screen. When necessary you can calibrate the sensor.

    The homescreen shows the current glucose value and trend value, and also a sensor graph.

    Below there are 4 buttons: settings, sensor, logbook and events.

    • Via the settings you can customize alert and sync data with CareLink and caregivers

    • Via sensor, you can see the lifespan of your sensor.

    • When you tap the sensor and then options, you can replace your sensor or enter an optional calibration.

    • Via events you can log different events like blood glucose for calibration, insulin, carbs and exercise. These icons can be found on the sensor graph and also sync to CareLink.

    To customize alarms on your Simplera app, navigate to the settings menu.

    • Use the toggle bar in the settings menu to change the alert volume. Here, you can also opt to mute all alerts for a duration of maximum 4 hours.

    • Via Glucose Alerts, you can set your Low alerts, High alerts, fall & rise alerts and snooze time.

    • For the Low Alerts, you can choose to set them at maximum volume at night or create a Predict Low alert.

    • The Simplera app also has status alerts that provide information about actions needed to ensure the correct functioning of the system. Make sure to act on these alerts to continue to receive sensor glucose information in the future.

    • It is important to note that when power saving settings are enabled, the mobile device may delay the alerts and notifications from the Simplera app.

    A separate Simplera CGM module is available for users or educators seeking more advanced configuration tips, troubleshooting support, or use-case guidance.

    2.3 Prepare the InPen Device¶

    This section explains how to prepare the InPen smart insulin pen for use, insert the insulin cartridge, attach a needle, and administer a dose safely and effectively.

    Step 1: Insert the Insulin Cartridge

    Before starting, wash hands thoroughly with soap and water.

    • Remove the cartridge holder by twisting it to the right.

    • Turn the internal screw counterclockwise until it stops to make space for the cartridge.

    • Insert the 3 mL insulin cartridge (small end first) into the cartridge holder.

    • Reattach the cartridge holder by twisting it to the left until it clicks.\

    Step 2: Attach the Pen Needle

    • Use a new, sterile pen needle. Remove the peel tab and push the needle straight onto the pen — not at an angle.

    • Screw the needle on securely.

    • Remove the outer needle cover and save it for later disposal.

    • Remove the inner needle shield and discard it safely.

    Step 3: Prime the Pen

    Priming ensures proper insulin flow and removes air from the system.

    • Dial a few units.

    • Hold the pen upright.

    • Press the injection button fully to release insulin.

    • A small drop should appear at the needle tip. If not, repeat the priming step until it does.

    Step 4: Select and Prepare the Injection Site

    Choose an appropriate subcutaneous injection site, such as the abdomen, thighs, buttocks or back of the upper arms.

    • The skin should be clean and dry, and free of scars, bruises, tattoos, or lipodystrophy

    • Injection site rotation is essential. Divide each area into zones and rotate weekly. Space each injection at least one finger-width apart from the previous one.

    • If hands are washed and skin is visibly clean, disinfection with alcohol is usually unnecessary. However, alcohol swabs are recommended if the area is visibly dirty, sweaty, or if injections are administered in a hospital or care facility. Allow alcohol to dry completely before injecting.

    Step 5: Administer the Insulin Dose

    • Dial the prescribed dose.

    • Insert the pen at a 90° angle into the skin. Avoid injecting through clothing.

    • Press the injection button all the way down and count slowly to 10 before removing the pen.\

    Step 6: Remove and Dispose of the Needle

    • Place the outer needle cap back on.

    • Unscrew the needle and dispose of it in a sharps container.\

    Best Practices and Clinical Tips

    • Check insulin type and expiration date before use.

    • Allow cold insulin to warm to room temperature for 30 to 60 minutes prior to injection to reduce pain and risk of lipodystrophy.

    • Never store the InPen in the refrigerator.

    • Use 4 mm needles for most patients to reduce risk of intramuscular injection. If using 6 mm needles, apply the skin-lift technique (gently pinch a 5 cm or 2 inch skin fold).

    • Prefer thin, sharp needles (32 gauge or higher) for comfort.

    • Avoid needle reuse. While occasional reuse is common and may not increase pain in the short term, repeated use increases the risk of infection, injection site trauma, lipodystrophy and injection pain due to dull or bent needles. If reuse is unavoidable, limit it to no more than 4–5 times and monitor for skin changes.

    • Avoid pressing the needle too firmly into the skin; the base should rest lightly without causing an indentation.

    Recognizing Unsuccessful Injections

    Watch for signs such as:

    • Insulin leakage at the site or from the needle tip

    • Intradermal injection, indicated by a small raised bump under the skin

    If these occur, the insulin dose may not have been fully delivered. Log the event in the app, and if this happens repeatedly, advise follow-up with a healthcare provider.

    With these preparation steps and best practices in place, users can administer rapid-acting insulin using the InPen safely and confidently.

    The next section covers how to connect the InPen to the app and configure therapy settings, including the bolus calculator.

    2.4 Set Up the InPen App¶

    This section explains how to connect the InPen smart insulin pen to the mobile app, customize therapy settings, and enable integration with CGM data from the Simplera or Guardian app.

    Step 1: Download and Connect the InPen App

    Begin by downloading the InPen app from the App Store or Google Play Store. After installation, open the app and log in with an existing account or create a new one. To pair the InPen with the app, the system will prompt the user to deliver a recommended pairing dose, which can be different for every new InPen. This can be done without insulin in the cartridge. The app uses this step to recognize and connect to the specific InPen device via Bluetooth. After successful pairing, the app walks users through a brief onboarding tutorial.

    Step 2: Enter Insulin Therapy Settings

    From the home screen, tap the Settings icon and navigate to Therapy Settings. Here, several key therapy parameters can be configured:

    • Short-acting insulin dosing can be set up using one of three strategies:

    • Carbohydrate counting

    • Meal estimation

    • Or fixed insulin doses per meal\

    Based on the strategy selected, users then enter:

    • Insulin-to-carbohydrate ratio (for carb counting) or units per meal (for meal estimation or fixed doses)

    • Insulin sensitivity

    • Glucose target range

    • Duration of insulin action

    • And maximum bolus dose (for safety)

    • There is also an option to enable or disable the Insulin on Board display\

    For long-acting insulin, the app allows users to enter:

    • Insulin type

    • Total daily dose

    • Frequency of administration (once or twice daily)

    • And the typical time(s) of day the basal insulin is taken\

    Additionally, users can define standard meal times (e.g., breakfast, lunch, dinner) so that bolus doses are linked accurately to meals for reporting purposes.

    Step 3: Integrate with the Simplera App and CareLink

    To enable glucose data integration, go to Settings > Connections and link the InPen app to the user’s CareLink Personal account. This connection allows for automatic syncing of CGM data from the Simplera app.

    To ensure reports are comprehensive, users should also authorize InPen Reports to be shared with CareLink.

    Step 4: Navigating the InPen App Interface

    Once the pen and therapy settings are configured, the home screen will display:

    • A shortcut to the bolus calculator

    • The most recent CGM glucose value and trend arrow

    • The estimated active insulin on board

    • A daily insulin graph, showing glucose data over time and logged insulin doses. The graph can be adjusted to show 3, 6, 12, or 24-hour views by tapping the screen.

    Throughout the day, the app may display alert icons on the home screen. These notifications inform the user about:

    • Missed mealtime or long-acting insulin doses

    • Low battery or end-of-life status for the InPen

    • Temperature warnings if the insulin has been exposed to excessive heat or cold

    • Cartridge usage beyond the recommended 28-day period\

    At the bottom of the app are four main tabs:

    1. Home

    2. Logbook – a detailed record of insulin doses, glucose readings, and events

    3. Reports – visual summaries of sensor glucose, insulin use and missed boluses

    4. And Settings – access to therapy setup, alerts, and data connections\

    With the InPen app connected and configured, the system is ready to support accurate insulin dosing, bolus calculations, and seamless glucose integration.

    2.5 Share your data¶

    Sharing glucose and insulin data can support collaborative diabetes care and enable timely intervention. The Smart MDI system allows users to share data with healthcare professionals and care partners through the CareLink platform.

    Sharing with Healthcare Providers

    To enable clinical review of CGM and insulin data, users must connect their CareLink Personal account to their provider’s CareLink Clinic account. There are several ways to establish this connection:

    • Option 1: Link Code Provided by Clinic => The clinic creates a patient profile using name and date of birth, then generates a unique link code. The user enters this code into their CareLink Personal account to complete the connection.

    • Option 2: Username-Based Follow Request => The provider sends a follow request using the patient’s CareLink username. The user.can approve this request directly in the Simplera app. To do so, they navigate to settings, CareLink, Manage Care Partners, where they can view and approve follow requests.

    • Option 3: Direct Login (In-Clinic Setup) => If the user is present at the clinic, the provider can link accounts directly by entering the user’s CareLink login credentials — with consent.

    Once connected, insulin and CGM data from both the InPen app and Simplera app are available to the provider via CareLink Clinic, supporting remote monitoring and therapy adjustments.

    Sharing with Family Members or Caregivers

    In addition to clinical sharing, the Simplera app supports real-time glucose sharing with up to five care partners, such as parents, partners, or trusted friends.

    To set this up:

    1. The care partner downloads the CareLink Connect app on their phone.

    2. They create a Care Partner account and send a follow request using the user’s CareLink username.

    3. The user accepts the request in either the Simplera app (under Manage Care Partners) or through their CareLink Personal account.\

    Once approved, care partners can view real-time glucose values and trend arrows, historical data and receive alerts. This feature can enhance safety and peace of mind — particularly for children, older adults, or people with impaired hypoglycemia awareness.

    With data sharing in place, users benefit from greater connectivity across their care network, helping to align support and clinical decisions with real-time insights.
