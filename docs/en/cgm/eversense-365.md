## **<u>2. Placing the sensor</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on placing the Eversense 365 sensor.

In this video, we’ll guide you through the process of inserting and removing the Eversense 365 sensor, and cover some do’s and don’ts.

### ***2.1 Supplies***

Let's begin by gathering the essential supplies:

- A sensor kit, which contains the sensor within a sensor holder;

- An insertion tools kit, where you'll find the incision template, the blunt dissector, and the insertion tool; and

- A transmitter kit, which houses the smart transmitter.

You’ll also need:

- Sterile, non-latex surgical gloves of your size;

- Chlorhexidine OR Betadine solution;

- A sterile drape, roughly 22 by 25 inches or 50 by 60 centimeters — with a hole in it;

- A surgical skin marker;

- 2 mL of lidocaine hydrochloride with epinephrine;

- A sterile syringe and needle;

- A sterile towel drape;

- 10 mL of 0.9% sterile saline-filled syringe;

- A disposable sterile scalpel (number 15);

- Sterile gauze pads;

- A sterile surgical clamp (of 10 to 16 cm);

- Steri-Strip™ adhesive skin closure; and

- Tegaderm™ + pad film dressing.

<span class="mark">The sterile drapes are in the kits supplied by Senseonics.</span>

### ***2.2 Inserting the sensor*** 

[<u>Eversense Placement & Removal – Training Video on Vimeo</u>](https://vimeo.com/1086098653?fl=pl&fe=sh)

Sensor placement is done while the patient is fully awake. The entire procedure takes about five minutes and consists of five steps.

1\. Site preparation

2\. Incision

3\. Sensor insertion

4\. Site closure

5\. Sensor and smart transmitter linking

Begin by washing your hands. Next, identify the location for the sensor placement. Ideally, the insertion area is approximately at the midway point between the acromion process and the lateral epicondyle, parallel to the humerus, in the subcutaneous tissue. Ensure that the selected insertion site allows the smart transmitter to stay flat during movement and that the patient can easily reach it. Using the transmitter, mark the edges on the skin, then align the incision template inside the marked lines. Use the incision template’s slots to mark a vertical sensor pocket path and horizontal incision line. Place the patient in a reclined position preferably on their side, with the upper arm on a flat horizontal plane.

Now that the patient has been positioned, let's move to sensor preparation.

Set up a flat clean surface designated as the sterile field. <span class="mark">Put on the surgical gloves and present the tool tray.</span>

Remove the red locking tab from the white sensor insertion tool by pulling it back. Loosen the tools from the tray.

Cycle and test the blue slide forward and back twice. Test the lock, and leave the blue slide in the forward and lock position.

Snap the insertion tool back into the tray. Wet the cannula by filling the preformed well with approximately 10 mL of sterile saline to completely cover the cannula.

Place the sensor holder on a flat clean surface designated as the sterile field. Unsnap the insertion tool. Pick up the sensor holder and squeeze the ridges. Remove and discard the cap.

Next, keeping the insertion tool in the slightly upright position, retract the blue slide. Firmly support the tip of the sensor holder. Align slots of holder and the tool. Align the arrows and slide onto the cannula until it clicks.

Cover the sensor by slowly advancing the blue slide. Squeeze the ridges and remove the holder. Visually check the tip of the cannula for the sensor.

Snap the insertion tool into place, and hydrate for five minutes.

With the sensor prepared, you’re ready to insert it.

Start by scrubbing the antiseptic at the incision line and working outward in circles. Repeat this process two more times, for three total passes. Allow the antiseptic to dry between passes.

When dry, drape the patient. Inject lid<span class="mark">ocaine with epinephrine at 2</span>0 degrees. Bevel up, injectin<span class="mark">g 1 milliliter at the incision line and 1</span> milliliter up the sensor pocket path. Apply light pressure over the area. Wait 1½ to 2 minutes and check effect.

Hold the scalpel with a pencil grip. Punch through the dermal layer to a depth of 5 millimeters. Remove the scalpel. Rotate 180 degrees, reinsert, and advance for uniform edges. Apply pressure to control any bleeding.

Hold the blunt dissector tool with the index finger resting in the finger well. Avoid placing the fingers underneath.

Introduce into the midline of the incision at a 45-degree angle. When the depth guards touch the skin, drop the tool flat. The depth guards assist with proper placement in the subcutaneous tissue. Rotate the tool slightly as you advance the entire length.

Keep the tool in the pocket for 30 to 45 seconds to decrease bleeding. Remove the tool and apply pressure to control bleeding.

Hold the insertion tool with fingers on the side, not underneath. Introduce the insertion tool under the lip of the incision into the subcutaneous pocket. Using a 45- to 90-degree angle, drop the tool flat. Follow the same pocket and advance to between the marks on the cannula. Stabilize the tool with the dominant hand. Use the other hand to unlock the blue slide at the widest end and slide back. This deploys the sensor into the pocket.

Remove the tool, and check to make sure that the sensor is not in the cannula. Remember, a correctly placed sensor will be in the subcutaneous tissue parallel to the humerus.

Apply pressure for several minutes as needed to control any bleeding.

<span class="mark">Align the edges of the incision and apply 3 to 4 parallel Steri-Strips to fully close the wound. If desired, place 2 additional Steri-Strips in a criss-cross pattern over the top.</span> Cover with Tegaderm, ensuring the Steri-Strips are completely under the gauze pad.

Lastly, link the sensor and the transmitter. We’ll go into detail on linking these two components in the next lesson.

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

[<u>Eversense Placement & Removal – Training Video on Vimeo</u>](https://vimeo.com/1086098653?fl=pl&fe=sh)

Remember that removal of the Eversense 365 sensor takes place up to one year after its placement.

Like sensor insertion, removal takes place while the patient is fully awake. The approximately 5-minute process involves four steps, namely:

1\. Site preparation

2\. Incision

3\. Sensor removal

4\. Site closure

Start with the patient in the upright position. Assess the sensor location and clean the skin. Make a reference dot on each end of the sensor.

Place the patient in a lateral recumbent position with the upper arm on a flat horizontal plane and sensor markings facing up toward the ceiling.

With the reference dots as a guide, use the placement guide to find an excellent signal. Mark the transmitter edges.

Using the reference dots, palpate and mark the sensor ends with horizontal lines. Draw a rectangle around the sensor and a horizontal line through the middle of the rectangle. Mark the incision line about 3 to 5 millimeters below the distal end of the sensor and 8 millimeters in width.

Now scrub the antiseptic at the incision line, working outward in circles. Repeat this process two more times for three total passes. Allow the antiseptic to dry between passes.

When dry, drape the patient. Inject lidocaine at 20 degrees bevel up, injecting 1 milliliter at the incision line, and 1 milliliters on each side of the sensor. Apply light pressure over the area. Wait 1½ to 2 minutes, and check effect.

Holding the scalpel with the pencil grip, pop through the dermal layer, and make an 8-millimeter-wide incision. Remove the scalpel, rotate 180 degrees, reinsert, and advance for uniform edges. Apply pressure to control any bleeding.

Stretch the skin taut using the thumb and index finger of the non-dominant hand. Push down on the lower lip of the incision as you introduce the clamp tip at 30 degrees. Open and close the clamp to the width of the incision 3 to 5 times.

Now, with the clamp open the full width of the incision, maintain gentle pressure on the lower lip and advance to the horizontal line. Close the clamp. If there is no gap in the clamp jaws, the sensor hasn’t been grasped. Maintain the clamp location and lift the wrist slightly to change the clamp angle, and close. A gap indicates you have the sensor.

Release the thumb and finger tension. Make a quarter turn of the wrist and remove the sensor.

Apply pressure to the incision for several minutes as needed to control any bleeding.

<span class="mark">Align the edges of the incision and apply 3 to 4 parallel Steri-Strips to fully close the wound. If desired, place 2 additional Steri-Strips in a criss-cross pattern over the top.</span> Cover With Tegaderm. Make sure the Steri-Strips are completely under the gauze pad.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

Let's go over some do’s and don’ts when placing the Eversense 365 sensor.

- <span class="mark">Be aware that you need to be properly trained and certified by Sensionics before performing the insertion procedure.</span>

- Choose the insertion site such that the patient is comfortable wearing the smart transmitter 24/7.

- Ensure the insertion area is not too far to the side to allow the patient to easily apply the adhesive patch.

- Avoid areas with loose skin, such as the back of the arm, as well as areas with scar tissue, tattoo, nevus, or apparent or noticeable blood vessels that could be incised.

- Before inserting the sensor, confirm that the patient does not have allergies to the antiseptic and lidocaine to be used during insertion.

- Insert the sensor using only the insertion tools provided in the insertion tool kit. Other insertion tools may damage the sensor.

- <span class="mark">Pinch and tent the skin to aid in forming a small pocket in the skin for insertion.</span>

- <span class="mark">Do not create a pocket more than 3-5 mm below the skin. If the sensor is placed too deep, it may be difficult to communicate with the smart transmitter or to remove later.</span>

- <span class="mark">Make sure that the subcutaneous pocket is parallel to and along the same axis as the humerus bone. When you insert the sensor, it should be level in the pocket, which will facilitate communication between the sensor and the smart transmitter.</span>

- <span class="mark">Link the sensor and the smart transmitter on the day of insertion. Failure to link the sensor and smart transmitter could lead to a delay in receiving glucose readings.</span>

- <span class="mark">Finally, alternate arms for next insertion sites.</span>

We’ve now covered all the steps for the Eversense 365 sensor insertion and removal, from preparing the insertion site to closing the incision, as well as do’s and don’ts. By mastering this procedure, you are building a solid foundation for reliable, long-term monitoring, which ensures accurate data that empowers you to make the most informed treatment decisions.

## **<u>3. Starting the sensor</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on starting the Eversense 365 sensor.

In this video, we will delve into linking the Eversense 365 sensor to the smart transmitter and getting started with the mobile app. We will also highlight key do’s and don’ts for using the Eversense 365 system.

### ***3.1 Smart transmitter***

The first thing to do is download the Eversense 365 CGM Mobile App from the App Store or Google Play. The Eversense 365 App can be downloaded on most mobile phones, with separate versions available for iOS and Android devices. You can find a list of all compatible devices and software versions on this webpage: https://www.eversensecgm.com/compatibility/

Open the app, then review and accept the End User License Agreement. If you already have an Eversense account, you can log in to the Eversense 365 app with your existing login credentials. Otherwise, tap "Create an account" and complete the Eversense account creation screens. Be sure to use an email address you can access. Take note of your Eversense account email and password, then tap "Submit."

Check your email for the account activation link. Click the link in your email to activate your account. Go back to the Eversense 365 CGM Mobile App, tap to log in, and enter the account email and password you just created.

Enable Notifications and Bluetooth to allow system communication and to receive system alerts. Confirm your unit of measurement and tap "Next." Review and confirm the app tutorial.

From the Welcome screen, you can pair your mobile phone with your smart transmitter. Make sure the transmitter is fully charged and powered on.

To start pairing mode, tap the soft-touch button three times on your smart transmitter until the light blinks blue. The smart transmitter serial number should match. Tap on the serial number and accept the pairing request. Once connected, tap "Next."

To begin the linking process, tap "Sensor." Hold the smart transmitter, without the adhesive patch, in place over the sensor to find the best signal. Tap "Next." Continue to hold your smart transmitter in place until the sensor linking is complete. This could take several minutes.

When the detected serial number appears, tap "Link detected sensor." You'll see the linking process progress. When the sensor is successfully linked, you can remove the smart transmitter from over the sensor. Tap "Next."

Now the 24-hour Warm-Up Phase begins; we also call this period “Day 1.” Note the date and time when the 24-hour Warm-up Phase will be complete.

Also, you won't need to wear your smart transmitter over your sensor during this phase. But it’s a great time to program your personalized glucose settings into your Eversense 365 CGM System.

After that, you can return your smart transmitter to the charger until the Warm-Up Phase is complete.

During the Warm-Up Phase, you won't be getting any glucose data. Continue to monitor your glucose levels with your blood glucose meter throughout this period and until you begin to receive glucose values from your Eversense 365 CGM.

When you receive the "Warm-Up Complete" notification, tap "Continue." Remove your smart transmitter from the charger. It will automatically reconnect with your mobile app. The System Setup screen will take you to the next step, the Initialization Phase.

To begin, get an adhesive patch and peel off the paper backing with the Eversense logo. The center of the patch is sticky, and this is where you should place your smart transmitter, as shown. Press the smart transmitter firmly onto the adhesive. Then, remove the larger piece of the remaining clear backing on the adhesive patch. Finally, position the smart transmitter over the sensor.

If you have an over wrap on your arm, you will need to remove it so that you can see the Tegaderm bandage. It’s helpful to understand where your sensor is located. Your health care provider inserted the sensor above the incision, as shown here. This means that the sensor is likely above the center of your Tegaderm bandage.

There may also be visible marks where your health care provider marked the corners of the smart transmitter before placing your sensor. Aligning the smart transmitter in this location helps to place it appropriately over the sensor. If needed, go to a mirror in order to clearly see your Tegaderm bandage.

Open the Placement Guide in your app by going to Main Menu — Placement Guide. You’ll see a black X at first.

Hold the smart transmitter with adhesive, with the sides of the adhesive patch pulled back. If visible, use the smart transmitter corner marks on your skin to position the smart transmitter toward the top half of the Tegaderm bandage. Note that the smart transmitter needs to be touching your arm. Gently lift and slightly move the smart transmitter plus adhesive to find the location with the strongest signal, somewhere between 2 to 3 bars or good to excellent. Rotating the smart transmitter slightly may help. When moving the transmitter, allow a few seconds for the signal to update before moving the transmitter again. Once you have obtained a signal of two to three bars, leave the smart transmitter on your arm there.

Remove the smaller remaining piece of clear backing and smooth out the adhesive patch. Finally, close the Placement Guide by tapping the Next icon.

Now that you’ve successfully positioned your smart transmitter, you’ll see a Calibrate Now notification appear in your mobile app within a few minutes. This means it’s time to do your first of four fingerstick calibrations.

You can complete the calibrations in as little as 6 hours, since you can calibrate as often as every 2 hours. You must enter calibrations no more than 12 hours apart. But you won’t have to remember the time for each calibration because your Eversense 365 CGM System will remind you each time with a Calibrate Now notification.

To enter a calibration, do a fingerstick blood glucose check and tap on the blue Calibrate button.

Tap on Glucose and scroll to enter the correct blood glucose value, then tap Done. Tap Submit to confirm that the correct glucose value has been selected and the fingerstick glucose test was performed no more than 10 minutes ago. You'll receive a Calibration in Progress notification which you can clear by tapping OK.

Here’s an example. Let’s say you linked your sensor and smart transmitter at 9:45 am on Monday, so your Warm-Up phase ends at 9:45 AM on Tuesday.

You use the Placement Guide to put the smart transmitter on your arm over the sensor. Around 10:00 AM on Tuesday, you receive your first "Calibrate Now" notification. You perform a fingerstick blood glucose check and enter the result into the mobile app.

Two hours later, the 2nd Calibrate Now notification appears, and once you enter that 2nd blood glucose value, sensor glucose data begins to appear on your My Glucose home screen.

Calibration prompts 3 and 4 will follow in the same fashion. But remember, you don’t have to calibrate right away – you have up to 12 hours to perform the next required calibration. But it is ideal to complete all 4 before going to bed, so that you will have the benefit of continuous data that first night

Once the initialization phase is complete, the system requires just 1 calibration per day for 13 days.

After day 13, the system requires only 1 calibration per week.

- Calibrate;

- Event Log;

- Reports;

Meanwhile, the icons at the top right indicate your smart transmitter ID, the strength of your smart connection with your sensor or mobile device, and the battery power left in your smart transmitter.

The current glucose reading is your real-time glucose level in the indicated unit of measurement. The reading is updated every 5 minutes.

### ***3.3 Do's & don’ts***

Now, let's discuss some do's and don'ts for using the Eversense 365 System.

- Link the sensor and the smart transmitter on the day of insertion.

- If the smart transmitter has been turned off to avoid vibrations during the Warm-Up Phase, turn it back on at the 24th hour. It will take about 5 minutes after the smart transmitter is placed over the sensor for the first calibration prompt to be displayed.

- If possible, start System Initialization early in the day. This will allow you to complete all four required initialization calibrations before going to bed.

- You can remove your smart transmitter from your upper arm at any time, except during calibration. Keep your smart transmitter in place over the sensor for at least five minutes before and 15 minutes after each calibration.

- Always wash your hands well and dry them thoroughly before calibration.

- Avoid calibrating when glucose levels may be changing rapidly such as after meals, after taking insulin, or during or after exercise.

- The smart transmitter comes with a 12-month warranty. The system will notify you once the transmitter has been in use for over 365 days.

- Your package contains a USB charging kit and silicone-based adhesive patches. Use only the supplied AC adapter and USB cable. Using other power sources can damage the transmitter or cause fire risk.

- When fully charged, the transmitter battery lasts approximately 24 hours. The transmitter must be removed from your body for charging. Charge it daily for about 15 minutes (or until the LED turns green). Reattach it only after charging is complete.

- While the transmitter is off your body — during charging or for any other reason — no glucose data or alerts are generated, and once reattached, the app will not backfill the missing data. To limit data gaps, keep the transmitter off your body for as short a time as possible.

- The smart transmitter has a soft-touch button. To use the smart transmitter, touch and hold or tap your finger directly on this button, similar to using a smartphone. The soft-touch button cannot detect a touch through clothing.

  - To turn the smart transmitter ON, press and hold the soft touch button for 3 seconds. You’ll feel a quick vibration, and the LED will blink once, indicating the power is ON.

  - To turn the smart transmitter OFF, touch and hold your finger on the soft-touch button for about 5 seconds. The LED will turn on, you will feel a quick vibe, and the LED will turn off, signaling the power is OFF.

- The communication distance between the app and the smart transmitter is up to 24.9 <span class="mark">feet or 7.6 meter. But this range will decrease in water, such as when you are in a bathtub or pool. When the transmitter and mobile device are not within range of each other, any data gathered by the transmitter is stored and sent to the app when the two devices are back within range.</span>

- <span class="mark">The smart transmitter can only be in water up to 1 meter (or 3.2 feet) deep for 30 minutes max. Exposing the smart transmitter to conditions beyond this will result in damage and void your warranty.</span>

- It is safe for you to wear your sensor and smart transmitter when you go through metal detectors at airports. Let security know you have a medical device and show your Medical Device Notification Card.

- While flying, the smart transmitter performs similarly to any other Bluetooth device.

- Do not use the smart transmitter if it is damaged or cracked as this could result in electrical shock.

- Do not wear the smart transmitter during X-rays, CT scans, or MRI scans to avoid interference with results. The sensor itself can safely remain in place during these procedures.

- Do not exchange smart transmitters with another person. Each smart transmitter can be linked to only one sensor at a time.

- As for the adhesive patches, you'll have to replace them daily. You can remove and replace the same adhesive patch more than once a day, but repeated removal and replacement may reduce adhesion strength.

- You can choose from either white or clear variants of the patch. The white adhesive is perforated and may be better at wicking away moisture. The clear adhesive is best for discretion. Try both types to determine which one works best for you.

- Only apply the adhesives to clean, dry skin. Remove any lotion or soap before placing the adhesive patch and smart transmitter.

- Although the patches use a medical-grade, non-reactive silicone adhesive, they may still cause a reaction or skin irritation.

## **<u>4. Setting alerts</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alerts with the Eversense 365 sensor.

In this session, we will explore available alerts and notifications with the Eversense 365 system and guide you through the process of setting them on the mobile app. Let's dive right in!

### ***4.1 Available alerts***

The Eversense 365 system gives you alerts on your glucose readings in three ways: vibrations of your smart transmitter, sounds from your mobile device, and visual notifications on the My Glucose screen. For example, if your glucose falls to, or below, your low glucose alert level, you’ll feel your smart transmitter vibrate in a unique pattern, hear a sound from your mobile device, and see the alert pop-up on your app.

In addition to low, high, and out-of-range glucose alerts, you can receive predictive and rate of change alerts with the Eversense 365. Predictive alerts allow you to receive an “early” warning 10, 20, or 30 minutes before your sensor glucose level is expected to cross the Low or High Glucose Alert threshold, based on current glucose trends. On the other hand, you can get Rate of Change Alerts when your sensor glucose level is rising or falling faster than the Rate of Change threshold you’ve set. If you are new to CGM, wait to set predictive or rate-of-change alerts until you are accustomed to wearing your system.

Another alert is the Calibrate Now alert. The Eversense 365 system maintains accuracy during the 13 days through once-daily calibrations. Then, starting at day 14, your system prompts you to calibrate once weekly. It will alert you with the Calibrate Now alert when a calibration is needed. Please note that when you do receive the Calibrate Now alert, you have a 24-hour window to complete the calibration to maintain uninterrupted access to your CGM data.

Besides alerts on your glucose levels, you may also receive system status alerts, including alerts on your sensor, smart transmitter, Eversense 365 account, and Internet connection.

With the smart transmitter, there are four vibration patterns dedicated to high-priority glucose alerts. The transmitter also uses four other patterns for lower-priority alerts and notifications. You'll keep receiving these on-body vibe alerts even when you're away from your mobile device. But keep in mind that you cannot change any of these patterns.

Also, take note that alerts are different from notifications. An alert is an important message that needs your attention. Alerts appear in yellow on your app. In contrast, a notification is a low priority message, such as a calibration reminder, that appears in blue.

If you ever need to look back and review any alert or notification that you have received, just go to the Main Menu and tap Alert History. The ALERT HISTORY screen will list ALL alerts and notifications for that day. Tap on any message to get more information. Alternatively, you can choose to review only certain messages by tapping the icons on the top of the screen.

### ***4.2 Setting alerts on the Eversense 365 app***

You can choose different ringtones for low vs. high alerts and determine how often they repeat. Let's say you want to customize the tone you hear for the Low Glucose Alert. To do this, tap Menu, then, Settings, and Sound Settings. Tap the different tones that are available to listen to a preview. Tones should get your attention and be different from any other sound notifications you use with other apps.

By setting the snooze alert, you can set how often an alert repeats after you have received a Low Glucose and High Glucose alert. Tap each snooze alert to set how often the alert repeats, then tap Done when complete.

If your mobile device is running iOS 12 and above or Android 6 and above, you can optionally allow Low and Out of Range Low Glucose alerts to always override your mobile device sound settings. When turned on, the Low and Out of Range Low Glucose alerts will be played at your phone's maximum volume. You cannot change the volume level for this override setting. This allows you to still receive Low and Out of Range Low Glucose alerts on your phone, even if your phone's Silent Mode is on, or Do Not Disturb is on. This feature works similar to apps with an alarm clock feature that will always sound regardless of your phone sound settings. You must allow this in your mobile device.

For iOS devices, start by tapping the button to turn on the override. Tap Settings, then tap Allow.

For Android devices, tap the button to turn on the override. Tap settings, then tap on Eversense 365 from the Do Not Disturb access page.

On a final note, turn on only the alerts that fit your diabetes management plan and your daily life. If you're unsure where to start, talk to your healthcare provider for advice on choosing and setting the right alerts. They can help you get the most out of your Eversense 365 system. Start with the most basic alerts, like low and high glucose alerts. You can add more alerts to your routine later. This helps you slowly adjust to the system.

In this lesson, we've examined the Eversense 365 alert system. By correctly utilizing its features and staying resilient, we can set and rely on alerts to signal critical shifts in our glucose levels, enabling us to take the necessary action to stay in control.

## **<u>5. Data sharing</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on sharing data with the Eversense 365 system.

In this session, you’ll learn how to share your data with your healthcare provider and loved ones and create glucose reports.

### ***5.1 Sharing data with your healthcare provider***

<span class="mark">The Eversense Data Management Software Program, or DMS Pro for short, is a free web-based app that enables patients, caregivers, and health care professionals to view and analyze glucose data that has been transmitted from the smart transmitter or the app.</span>

<span class="mark">Clinic Share is an optional feature on your app that allows you to either invite or accept an invitation from your healthcare provider's clinic using DMS Pro. To invite your healthcare provider, you'll need their DMS Pro Clinic ID number. Contact your clinic for this information.</span>

<span class="mark">From the Main Menu, go to "Share My Data” and “Clinic Share.” Then tap "Clinic Requests" and "Send Requests." Enter the DMS Pro clinic ID number, and tap "Add." Confirm the name of your clinic, then tap "Yes" to send the request. Tap "OK" to acknowledge the request has been sent. Your pending invitation will be displayed in red until your clinic accepts, upon which the name will change to black. To stop sharing data, tap on the clinic name and choose "Stop Sharing."</span>

<span class="mark">To receive a request, your clinic must use the email associated with your Eversense account. To check for pending requests, go to "Share My Data." Tap "Clinic Share," "Clinic Requests," and "Accept Requests." Tap on the clinic name in red, then tap "Accept" or "Decline" before confirming your selection. The clinic will appear on our "My Clinics" list.</span>

### ***5.2 Sharing data with your loved ones***

Using your Eversense 365 app, you also can invite your family and friends to join your "Circle." From the Share My Data screen, tap "My Circle" and "Invite to My Circle." Enter the email of the person you'd like to invite; a nickname is optional. You can also tap the "+" sign to select an email address from your Contacts. Tap "Send" then "OK" on the pop-up.

Your family member or friend will use the Eversense NOW Remote Monitoring App to monitor your CGM data. They can accept your invitation through the Eversense NOW app, or can follow the instructions in the email they receive. In your CGM app, their name will appear in red until they accept your invitation.

To remove someone from your circle, tap on their name, then tap "Remove" and confirm the pop-up notification. Finally, tap "OK" to confirm they are no longer in your Circle.

To temporarily stop sharing data with a member without removing them from your Circle, tap the member's name in the My Circle list to open the MEMBER screen. Then tap the Share My Data button to turn on/off data share with this member.

It’s important to note that you should not use glucose information displayed on the Eversense NOW App to make treatment decisions. The Eversense NOW App does not communicate directly with your sensor and/or smart transmitter. This makes the Eversense NOW App a secondary display of your CGM data that should not be used in place of the primary Eversense 365 App display.

### ***5.3 Creating reports***

Your Eversense 365 app automatically creates four reports you can view at any time, right in the app. These reports are the:

- Glucose Pie Chart, which shows what percent of your readings within a given time period are within, below or above your Glucose Target levels;

- Time in Range, which displays percentage of time you have spent in pre-set glucose ranges, based on standardized guidelines;

- Weekly Modal Summary, which provides a seven-day summary of your glucose information in a 24-hour line graph; and

- Glucose Statistics, which shows average, low, and high glucose values based on preset times.

To view your glucose reports on the app, tap Menu then Reports, and swipe to move across the different reports. You can also email each report by tapping the email icon in the top right-hand corner.

As a reminder, be sure to set your mobile device date and time correctly. The accuracy of the graphs and reports depends upon the accuracy of the date and time.

Furthermore, you can access reports on DMS Pro. To do this, go to https://us.eversensedms.com and enter your log-in information. On the left <span class="mark">navigation pa</span>ne, click on Reports to open the list of reports.

You may print or email your reports. Click on the printer icon to view and print your report, or click on the envelope icon to email them.

Located in the settings menu, REPORT SUMMARY settings allow you to set up a regularly-scheduled automated report that will be emailed to you. You can select how often the reports are sent, and which reports you want to receive.

In this video, you've learned how to use Eversense 365’s data sharing tools. Remember, a supportive network is vital for managing this journey. By sharing your CGM data, you empower your team with the actionable insights required to foster resilience and drive optimal health outcomes.

### ***6.1 Smartwatches***

Within the Eversense 365 App, the Share My Data feature allows you to share your Eversense data with Apple Health. In the Menu options, tap Share My Data, then Application Sharing, and Apple Health. Turn on Share Data. You may also need to allow sharing via the health app.

You can view a snapshot of your CGM data on your Apple Watch. Once you’ve downloaded and installed the Eversense 365 App on your mobile device, follow the device’s instructions for adding the app to your watch.

Tap the Eversense 365 icon on your watch HOME screen to open the app. The My Glucose screen shows your current glucose with a trend arrow, and a trend graph of your last three hours of CGM data.

You can also access the MY GLUCOSE screen if you turn on notifications from the Eversense 365 App in your Apple Watch settings. When you receive a notification, you can also tap on the message to see the MY GLUCOSE screen.

Swipe left to the next screen showing a pie chart of your total time within and outside your target range for the past 24 hours. Swipe up to display the same data shown as percentages. Lastly, swipe left to the next screen showing your current glucose with trend arrow, your next calibration time, the current system calibration phase, and battery level of your smart transmitter.

Take note that the Apple Watch is a secondary display of Eversense data and should not be used in place of the Eversense 365 App display. Any alerts and notifications that appear on your Apple Watch should be confirmed within the mobile device app. Any problems with mobile devices, wireless internet, data connection, the Eversense DMS, the smart transmitter being out of range of their mobile device, or charging the smart transmitter may cause data transfer to be delayed or not to be displayed.

### ***6.2 Smartpens***

The Eversense 365 system currently does not offer direct integration with smart insulin pens, but there is a workaround available.

Beyond Apple Health, the Eversense 365 system integrates with Glooko . Because smart insulin pens like the Novopen 6, NovoPen Echo Plus, inPen, Tempo Smart, and SoloSmart can also connect to Glooko, you can consolidate both your glucose and insulin data on a single screen, although this integration requires manual setup.

### ***6.3 Smart insulin pumps***

The Eversense CGM is also compatible with open-source AID systems such as AndroidAPS. iPhone-based open-source systems do not yet support Eversense.
