## **<u>1. Introduction</u>**

Welcome to this module on the Accu-Chek SmartGuide CGM! In the videos ahead, we’ll explore the key features of this sensor and how it works with an AI-enabled predictive algorithm to support proactive diabetes management.

The Accu-Chek SmartGuide solution consists of three elements: the sensor, the SmartGuide app, and the Predict app. The sensor, which has a diameter of 3 cm and weighs about 5 grams, is placed on the back of your upper arm using an all-in-one applicator and lasts for up to 14 days. After a one-hour warm-up, it sends real-time glucose readings every five minutes to the SmartGuide app on your smartphone via Bluetooth. The Predict app, with its AI-powered algorithm, then uses these glucose values and other available information to detect your glucose patterns and estimate your levels over the next 30 minutes, 2 hours, and during the night, helping you stay ahead of potential changes. ## **<u>2. Placing the sensor</u>**

Welcome to the first lesson of the Accu-Chek SmartGuide CGM module. In this video, we’ll demonstrate how to apply and remove the SmartGuide sensor. Let’s begin.

### ***2.1 Supplies***

Prepare the following:

- an Accu-Chek SmartGuide CGM pack, which contains an applicator with a built-in sensor, a product leaflet;

- and an alcohol wipe.

### ***2.2 Applying the sensor*** 

Start by downloading the Accu-Chek SmartGuide app from the Apple App Store or Google Play Store. Then, open the app. You will need to create an Accu-Chek account or sign in to your account if you already have one. You will need to enter some information about yourself. Next, consent to the terms to use the app. Finally, verify your email address and sign in to the app. To personalize the app, select your units of measurement. Then select your target range. Next, the app will ask you to grant it some vital permissions and to turn on certain settings. The names of these differ depending on which operating system your phone has. Enabling these features allows the app to send you alarms and notifications regardless of settings like Mute or Do Not Disturb. Now that your account and profile are set up, you can apply your first sensor and pair it to your phone.

Disinfect your skin before applying the sensor. Make sure to let it dry completely before you proceed. Flip the pull tab to open the device. Unscrew the blue twist cap. You’ll hear a sou<span class="mark">nd that i</span>ndicates that the device is new and has not been previously opened. If it has already been opened, discard it immediately and get a new one. Look inside of the applicator to check that the needle is pointing straight upwards, but make sure not to touch it. If you notice any damage, discard the device and use a new one. Do not put the blue twist cap back on after you have removed it. Make sure to hold on to the cap because you will need the PIN printed on it to pair the sensor with your smartphone. You are now ready to apply the sensor. Place your hand on your opposite shoulder to make it easier to apply the sensor. Reach underneath your arm and place the applicator on your skin. Firmly press the applicator onto your skin until you hear it click. Remove the applicator without turning or wiggling it. Smooth the adhesive to make sure it is applied properly.

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

You can wear your CGM sensor for up to 14 days. When it is time for you to replace it, the Accu-Chek SmartGuide app will alert you. Find the flat edge of the sensor and carefully start peeling it off by pulling outwards. Make sure all sensor parts have been removed from your skin. If the sensing element has remained in your skin, contact your health care professional. To protect the environment and to safeguard others from harm, dispose of the sensor according to your local medical waste guidelines.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

- Do not apply skincare or hygiene products (such as insect repellent or sunscreen) on the sensor or around the application area, as these may damage the device or loosen the adhesive.

- If only the outer edges of the adhesive pad begin to lift from the skin, the sensor should still work correctly. If any part of the adhesive pad directly under the sensor comes away from the skin, do not try to press it back down or tape it in place. A reattached sensor may not function properly. Instead, apply a new sensor.

- Some companies offer dedicated stickers to make your CGM look more fashionable. These are completely optional, of course. You can also find additional patches that can help improve adhesion.

In this video, you learned the correct procedure for placing and removing the Accu-Chek SmartGuide sensor. By adhering to these guidelines, you help ensure dependable monitoring over time and gain trustworthy glucose data to guide your treatment decisions.

## **<u>3. Starting the sensor</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on starting the Accu-Chek SmartGuide CGM. In this video, we’ll guide you through starting the sensor and navigating the SmartGuide and Predict apps. Plus, we’ll share tips for safe and effective use of the CGM as part of your daily routine. Let’s dive in.

### ***3.1 Starting the sensor***

Open the app to get started. If this is the first time you are pairing, the app will prompt you to Pair Now. Otherwise, simply tap the Pair New Sensor button on the home screen to start the process. The app will then walk you through the necessary steps. Tap Search to look for your sensor device. Depending on your smartphone model, you may need to grant the app permission to use Bluetooth. Once Bluetooth permission is granted, the app will begin to look for your sensor. Make sure the sensor is within range. After completing the search, the app will display a list of available devices. On the twist cap that you saved earlier, look for the serial number of your sensor. Tap the matching serial number in the app and then tap Next. You will be asked to enter the six-digit PIN found on the blue twist cap. Enter the PIN and select Pair. The app and the sensor will take a little while to pair in order to form a secure connection. This is normal. After the sensor has warmed up to your body, you’ll need to calibrate it to ensure your glucose values are as accurate as possible.

The calibration process takes place in two stages. The first calibration prompt appears 12 hours after you place and start the sensor. The second prompt shows up 30 minutes to 3 hours after the first calibration.

When the app notifies you 12 hours after sensor application, tap Calibrate Now. Check your blood glucose with your meter and enter the result on the Calibrate screen. The value must fall between 40 and 400 mg/dL, or 2 and 22 mmol/l, and should be based on a reading that is no more than 3 minutes old. After typing in the result, tap Save and double-check that the number is correct, as it cannot be edited later. Tap Confirm to continue or Cancel to go back. If an incorrect value is confirmed, the system can no longer guarantee accurate performance; in that case, you should discard the current sensor, insert a new one, and repeat the previous steps. When calibration is accepted, a confirmation message appears; tap OK to return to the Home screen. If the calibration fails, wait at least 15 minutes, then repeat the procedure with a fresh blood glucose reading from your meter.

<span class="mark">Calibrate the sensor whenever the app prompts you to do so. This will occur only twice per sensor, as described earlier. Enter the second calibration value approximately 30 minutes after completing the first calibration. Tap Calibrate and repeat steps 2–4.</span>

<span class="mark">After each successful calibration, the Home screen will reappear. Once two successful calibrations have been completed, the system automatically switches from Trend Mode to Therapy Mode for the remainder of the sensor wear period. From that point on, the CGM values can be used to support therapy-related decisions.</span>

<span class="mark">If the sensor is not calibrated, the system will remain in Trend Mode. Glucose values will still be displayed and can be used to follow glucose trends and as a general reference, but not for therapy decisions.</span>

### ***3.2. The Accu-Chek SmartGuide app***

With the Accu-Chek SmartGuide app, you can view your glucose values and record insulin doses, carbohydrate intake, and daily activities.

The Home screen presents your current CGM reading in text and numbers, shows the unit of measurement, and displays a trend arrow indicating how your glucose is changing. Both the color of the glucose value and the direction of the trend arrow serve as visual cues.

The sensor’s operating mode appears beneath the CGM value. A sensor that has not been calibrated yet operates in Trend Mode, while a calibrated sensor runs in Therapy Mode.

In the top-right corner of the screen, you can see the status of alarms and notifications, as well as the icon that lets you switch to the Accu-Chek SmartGuide Predict app. We’ll talk more about the Predict app later.

The Home graph displays your recent glucose values in relation to your target range.

The message area provides a brief summary of important current information. For example, it can show the remaining warm-up time after pairing a new sensor. You can tap the buttons in this area to respond to the specific message. In the example, the message area shows the latest logbook entries.

In the lower part of the screen, you'll find navigation elements, namely the Home, Logbook, Add Entry, Graphs, and Menu icons. The icons appear in blue to show which section of the app you are currently viewing.

Record and track your insulin, meals, and other key details in the Logbook to see how these factors influence your glucose levels. You can use it to enter manual blood glucose meter readings, carbohydrate intake, insulin injections, and personal notes to add context to your data. To add a new entry, tap Add Entry in the tab bar on the Home screen. Follow the prompts to fill in the required fields, and include any extra details you want to remember in the Notes section. To edit an entry, tap Logbook, then select the item you want to modify or delete.

Tap the Graphs icon and choose from different data displays of your glucose data.

The Trend Graph offers an expanded version of the SmartGuide trend graph shown on the Home screen. This graph presents your continuous glucose readings from the last 6, 12, or 24 hours. By swiping the graph to the right, you can look back at earlier data. You can also tap the Calendar icon in the top right corner to display your glucose information for a specific day. Logbook entries can be layered onto the graph to provide additional context.

The Time in Range view shows how much time your glucose values have stayed within the target range, including your personal targets, over the last 7, 14, or 28 days. The main aim is to maximize the amount of time spent In Range, represented by the green section of the graph.

In the Statistics section, you can review your CGM usage and key data for the past 7, 14, or 28 days. This includes your active CGM time, average glucose values, and your Glucose Management Indicator (GMI).

The Accu-Chek SmartGuide Predict app can help you forecast your upcoming glucose values, such as the likelihood of highs and lows, and identify glucose patterns and trends, so you can decide what action to take for your diabetes management. ### ***3.4 Do's & don’ts***

- After inserting a new sensor, pair it with the app within 30 minutes. If pairing is delayed beyond 30 minutes, the sensor may take longer to connect in order to conserve battery. The same 30-minute rule applies after a lost connection – reconnect the sensor to the app within this time frame.

- The sensor can communicate with a mobile device over a distance of up to 10 meters (or 33 feet) in a clear line of sight. This range may be shorter depending on the type of mobile device and surrounding conditions (for example, other nearby electronic devices).

- If the link to your sensor is interrupted, you will stop receiving glucose readings and alarms until the connection is restored. The sensor can store up to 8 hours of data if it cannot send readings to the app. To prevent losing information, make sure the data is transferred before the sensor battery is depleted.

- Repeated connection drops between the sensor and the app may shorten the sensor’s battery life. To help maintain a stable connection, keep your sensor and mobile device close together.

- Try not to calibrate in environments that are extremely hot, extremely cold, or where the temperature is changing quickly.

- If you accidentally confirm an incorrect calibration value, it cannot be undone. In this case, you must remove the sensor and start again with a new one.

- <span class="mark">Calibration is only possible during the scheduled calibration process. Once this process has passed, the sensor cannot be calibrated later.</span>

- <span class="mark">The sensor is water-resistant and can be worn while bathing, swimming, or showering. However, do not submerge it for more than 60 minutes or deeper than 1 meter (or 3.3 feet).</span>

- <span class="mark">Do not bring portable radio frequency communication equipment (including accessories such as antenna cables and external antennas) closer than 30 cm (or 12 inches) to the sensor, as this may interfere with its performance.</span>

- <span class="mark">Remove the sensor during an MRI, CT or X-ray. Some centers may allow the sensor to remain in place during CT scans or X-rays; check with your healthcare professional in advance.</span>

- <span class="mark">If the app fails to show glucose values for more th</span>an 20 minutes and there is no notification or alarm recorded in the event log, contact customer support.

- Certain substances can interfere with sensor accuracy and may falsely increase the CGM readings shown in the app, including:

  - more than 500 mg of vitamin C per day taken orally, or any amount given intravenously

  - Supplements containing gentisic acid

  - and Methyldopa

- When your CGM reading is outside the device’s measuring range (above 400 mg/dL or 22.2 mmol/L or below 40 mg/dL or 2.2 mmol/L), the app will show “HI” or “LO” instead of a numeric value.

- Logbook entries that include glucose values used for calibration cannot be edited or deleted.

- Once a logbook entry is deleted, it cannot be restored.

- The Predict app relies on a continuous stream of data from your Accu-Chek SmartGuide device to function properly.

  - Ensure you have a stable internet connection, that the CGM app is installed on your smartphone, and that it is paired with an active CGM sensor.

  - Keep your smartphone within communication range of the device and stay signed in to your Accu-Chek account in both apps.

  - Install new app updates whenever they are released.

## **<u>4. Setting alarms</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alarms with the Accu-Chek SmartGuide CGM. In this video, we'll discuss the various alarms the sensor can issue and show you how to customize them in the SmartGuide and Predict apps. Let’s get started.

### ***4.1 Available alarms***

The Accu-Chek SmartGuide app can alert you when your glucose goes very high, low, or very low. The Very High Glucose Alarm notifies you when your glucose value rises above your very high glucose limit. By default, this is set to 250 mg/dL or 13.9 mmol/L. The Low Glucose Alarm alerts you when your glucose value drops below your low glucose limit. The default low glucose threshold is 70 mg/dL or 3.9 mmol/L. The Very Low Glucose Alarm warns you when your glucose value falls below 54 mg/dL or 3.0 mmol/L. For safety reasons, this very low limit cannot be changed.

You will only receive one alarm when your glucose first goes above the very high limit or below the low limit. In contrast, if your glucose remains below the very low limit, the app will continue to send a glucose alarm every 5 minutes until your value rises above that level or you dismiss the alarm.

Glucose alarms are active around the clock unless you have enabled sleep alarms. Sleep alarms let you choose different alarm thresholds during the night so that you are not disturbed unnecessarily while sleeping.

Glucose alarms are turned on by default, although they can be switched off. Ho<span class="mark">wever, disabling them is not recommended for safety reasons.</span>

<span class="mark">The “Do not disturb” override makes sure you still receive important glucose notifications even when your phone is silenced or set to Do Not Disturb. To enable this, tap Menu, tap “Do not disturb”, and turn them on in your phone settings so that you can respond quickly to glucose excursions.</span>

<span class="mark">In addition, you can receive a reminder when your CGM sensor is due to</span> expire and should be replaced. Tap Menu, tap Sensor Expiration Reminders, and select 24 hours before, 2 hours before, or both.

You will also receive a notification if the app loses connection to the sensor. To adjust this setting, go to Menu, then tap Sensor Connection Loss. This alert is enabled by default.

The SmartGuide app also uses various other notifications to keep you informed about sensor status, issues, and required actions. These may appear as error messages, maintenance messages, warnings, informational messages, or reminders.

On the other hand, the Predict app focuses on forecasting your glucose levels. Its purpose is to give you advanced insights so you can act early and help keep your glucose within your target range, instead of responding only after a high or low has occurred.

The Predict app offers three types of glucose predictions: Glucose Predict, Low Glucose Predict, and Night Low Predict.

Glucose Predict displays an estimate of your glucose levels for the next 2 hours. To access this feature, you need at least 1 hour of CGM data, an active internet connection on your device, and a current glucose reading that is above your low glucose threshold of 70 mg/dL <span class="mark">(or 3.9</span> mmol/L).

On the graph, the large green dot and the number shown beneath it both represent your current glucose value. To the left of this dot, a solid line shows your glucose readings from the previous 60 minutes. To the right, a dotted line indicates the best estimate of your future glucose values. Behind this dotted line, vertical grey bars illustrate the degree of uncertainty in the prediction. As with any prediction, the further into the future it extends, the less certain it becomes. This is shown by the length of the grey bars: longer bars mean higher uncertainty. Your real glucose values may still end up outside these grey bars. Glucose values are color-coded: readings in your target range are shown in green, while values above the range appear in yellow or orange, and low values are marked in red or dark red.

Low Glucose Predict alerts you when a low glucose event is likely within the next 30 minutes. When the system detects an increased likelihood of low glucose, it sends a push notification. You can <span class="mark">adjust the glucose threshold that triggers this notification between 60 and 100 mg/dL (or 3.3 to 5.6 mmol/L) by changing the Low Glucose alarm setting in the Therapy Settings of the Accu-Chek SmartGuide app. The feature is switched on by default but can be disabled via Notification Settings in the Menu options of the Predict app. When you tap the notification, additional details and suggested actions are displayed.</span>

<span class="mark">The Low Glucose Predict function will only operat</span>e if your low glucose alarm in the Accu-Chek SmartGuide app is set between 60 mg/dL (or 3.3 mmol/L) and 100 mg/dL (or 5.5 mmol/L). If your setting is above this range, the Predict feature will not be active.

Night Low Predict shows how likely you are to experience low glucose during the night. You can receive a notification if your risk of night-time low glucose is high or very high. Set your usual bedtime for the week, and, as long as notifications are enabled, a reminder will appear on your lock screen sometime between 9 PM and 2 AM. During this time window, the Home screen displays your risk of low glucose over the next 7 hours of the night. For this feature, “low glucose” is always defined as a value below 70 mg/dL (or 3.9 mmol/L), regardless of your personal Low Glucose threshold.

If your risk is different in the first 3.5 hours of the night compared with the last 3.5 hours, the prediction will highlight the part of the night with the greater risk. Predictions are generally more reliable for the earlier hours of the night, as short-term forecasts are more accurate.

Night Low Predict uses three risk levels, each represented by a different color. Green indicates a normal or average risk of low glucose. In this case, you can get ready for bed as you usually do. Yellow signals a higher-than-normal risk, and red indicates a very high risk of low glucose. When a high or very high risk is displayed, you may choose to take action, such as eating long-acting carbohydrates combined with protein and fat and/or adjusting your basal insulin or basal rate before going to sleep. The specific steps you take should be based on your diabetes treatment plan, prior experience, and whether the low is expected in the first or second half of the night. If you are unsure, consult your healthcare provider for guidance.

Finally, the Glucose Patterns feature highlights repeating trends in your glucose levels. A pattern is defined as an event that happens more than once within the same day or during the same calendar week.

When the Predict app has sufficient data to recognize patterns in your glucose values, these patterns are displayed and can be tapped to view more detailed information. They may appear very differently depending on how often they occur and what is causing them, and are represented using various icons and colors.

Patterns from the last 24 hours are shown on the Home screen. If no patterns are identified, the Predict app will display a message to let you know. When a new pattern is detected, you’ll receive a notification; tap it to see further details.

To explore pattern graphs, tap the Graph icon in the bottom menu, select a pattern and switch the view to Week or Month. Possible causes for each pattern are listed below the graph, and you can tap these causes to see suggestions for changes you might consider.

Some patterns may be improved or resolved by adjusting your behavior. Once a pattern has been resolved, the Predict app moves it to the Past tab, where you can review all your previous patterns if you need to refer to them later.

Your Logbook entries play an important role in identifying patterns. For instance, patterns related to meals can only be detected if you consistently log your meals in the SmartGuide app.

### ***4.2 Setting alarms on the SmartGuide and Predict apps***

To set up your glucose alarms, open the Menu and select Glucose Alarms. Use the toggles to switch specific alarms on or off. If you enable sleep alarms, you can also adjust your Bedtime (start time) and Wake-Up Time (end time) in 15-minute increments. Next, enter the glucose limits you want for each alarm, then tap Save to apply your settings.

To enable or adjust Critical Alerts, Sensor Expiration Reminders, or Sensor Connection Loss, tap Menu and toggle the relevant settings on or off.

In the Predict app, the Glucose Predict, Low Glucose Predict and Night Low Predict feature are enabled by default. You can disable them via the menu.

You can also set reminders, including push notifications, to help you actively work on particular patterns you want to improve. By creating a reminder for a detected pattern, the app can warn you ahead of time so that alarms may help you prevent the same event from recurring.

A summary of the previous week’s patterns is generated automatically every Monday. If you would like to be notified when this summary is ready, go again to Patterns via the bottom navigation, tap Enable on the Weekly Summary card, turn on the notification toggle, and tap Next. Then select Notify me daily at and adjust the time at which you want to receive the notification, tap Next, and confirm on the final screen.

## **<u>6. Connecting other devices</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your Accu-Chek SmartGuide CGM to other devices. In this video, you’ll discover how to integrate your sensor with smartwatches. We’ll also outline options involving smart insulin pens and pumps. Let’s jump right in.

### ***6.1 Smartwatches***

If you are using an iPhone, you can pair the app with an Apple Watch. Once your Apple Watch is connected to your iPhone, you can see your latest glucose value, the trend arrow, and a trend graph directly on the watch. You’ll also receive all error, maintenance, and warning messages, as well as reminders, straight on your Apple Watch.

On Android, you can install the third-party app Juggluco and link it with the native SmartGuide app to display your glucose data on your smartwatch. More information can be found on the website of Juggluco and in our CGM to smartwatch guide. Please be aware that Juggluco is not supported by Roche Diagnostics and lacks approval from regulatory authorities like the FDA or EMA, making its usage carry inherent risks that should be approached cautiously.

### ***6.2 Smart insulin pens***

At present, the Accu-Chek SmartGuide CGM does not offer direct integration with most commercially available smart insulin pens.

### ***6.3 Smart insulin pumps***

Currently, the Accu-Chek SmartGuide CGM operates as a standalone system and is not used in commercial automated insulin delivery systems. On a relevant note, the SmartGuide sensor can be linked to Juggluco, which is capable of communicating with AndroidAPS, a well-known open-source AID platform.

Keep in mind that open-source apps are not officially supported by Roche Diagnostics and have no FDA or European regulatory approval. If you decide to use them, you do so solely at your own risk. Although we don't encourage this practice, we are committed to openly sharing all existing options.
