## **<u>1. Introduction</u>**

Welcome to this module on the Sibionics GS3 CGM. In this series of videos, we’ll unpack the essentials of using the world’s thinnest sensor.

Measuring 24.8 by 7.8 by 2.9 mm and weighing 1.5 g, the Sibionics GS3 sensor is placed on the back of your upper arm using an all-in-one applicator. After a one-hour warm-up, it measures glucose levels in your interstitial fluid and sends readings to the Sibionics GS3 app on your smartphone via Bluetooth every five minutes. The sensor lasts for up to 14 days. It is intended for people with diabetes aged 3 and above.

Our "Master Module on Glucose Sensors" introduced the key principles of CGM use, including when to trust sensor readings and how to manage issues such as disconnections and allergic reactions. ## **<u>2. Placing the sensor</u>**

Welcome to the first lesson of the Sibionics GS3 CGM module. This video will show you how to apply and remove the GS3 sensor and point out essential dos and don’ts. Let’s dive in.

### ***2.1 Supplies***

To get started, have the following ready:

- A GS3 sensor kit containing a sensor applicator and a product insert; and

- an alcohol wipe, which may or may not be included in the box depending on regulatory approvals in your country.

### ***2.2 Applying the sensor*** 

Scan the QR code on the product insert to download the SIBIONICS GS3 app, or download the app from your app store. Register an account with your email address or log in with your existing SIBIONICS account. Then follow the in-app instructions to set up your profile and preferences.

Wash your hands and get prepared. Choose a soft and visible area on the upper third part of the back of your upper arm. Then clean the site with an alcohol wipe and allow it to air dry completely for secure sensor application. Twist off the cap from the sensor applicator, and set the cap aside. Rest the sensor applicator over the prepared site, and then push the button down to apply the sensor to your body. After hearing the click sound, move the sensor applicator away. Secure the sensor by rubbing the adhesive edges. The sensor is now applied.

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

Your sensor automatically stops working after 14 days and must be replaced. To remove it, rub the adhesive edges with an alcohol wipe. Gently peel off the sensor and dispose of it properly. Then apply a new sensor as needed.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

- Use a smartphone running Android 8.1 or later, or iOS 13.0 or later to ensure compatibility with the SIBIONICS GS3 app.

- Do not use the sensor applicator if:

  - the twist-to-open label has been tampered with,

  - it seems to be damaged or already opened, or

  - it is past the expiration date.

- Push down on the sensor applicator only when it is placed over the prepared site to prevent unintended results or injury.

- Placement is correct as long as the sensor is applied in a vertical position. The round hole can point either upward or downward without affecting accuracy.

- Applying the sensor may cause bruising or bleeding. In case of persistent bleeding, remove the sensor and contact your healthcare provider.

In this video, you learned the steps for proper placement and removal of the Sibionics GS3 CGM. By following these best practices, you promote reliable long-term monitoring, which provides accurate data to guide your treatment decisions.

## **<u>3. Starting the sensor</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on starting the Sibionics GS3 CGM. In this video, we’ll dive into starting the sensor and navigating the GS3 app. We’ll also cover some tips for safe and proper use of the sensor in your daily life. Let’s jump right in.

### ***3.1 Starting the sensor***

To pair the sensor with the SIBIONICS GS3 app, enable Bluetooth on your phone. For NFC-enabled phones, you can tap the sensor for instant connection. If your phone doesn’t have NFC function, you can scan the QR code or enter the six-digit code from the GS3 kit.

After pairing the sensor with GS3 app, wait 60 minutes for sensor warm-up before glucose data appears. It is recommended to remain on the page for at least one minute to ensure the connection is properly established.

### ***3.2 The Sibionics GS3 app***

Here’s what the GS3 app Home screen looks like. On it, you’ll see the most recent glucose reading, the remaining sensor life, and the glucose trend graph.

Glucose data from the past 3 to 24 hours are shown as a trend line, with the most recent glucose reading displayed on the far right. Tap any point on the graph to highlight the corresponding glucose value. Switch between 3-hour, 6-hour, 12-hour, and 24-hour views of your glucose data by tapping the desired time range above the graph. View the graph in full screen by tapping the full-screen control icon above the graph. Logged events appear as icons at the time they were recorded. Tap any event icon to see detailed information about that event.

The trend arrows reflect both the speed and direction of glucose changes based on recent readings. They indicate whether your glucose is rapidly rising, slowly rising, steady, slowly falling, or rapidly falling.

The event record is designed to capture information that may influence glucose levels. Using the Event feature on the smartphone app, you can log and save events such as meals, exercise, medication, insulin delivery and corrections, blood-glucose meter readings, sleep, and mood.

To add an event, tap the Plus icon at the bottom of the Home screen. Select or enter the required information, then tap Save. You can tap the event icon on the glucose trend graph to view detailed information about that event. Alternatively, tap the icon and then tap Logbook to review all the events you have added.

<span class="mark">When you tap the avatar icon in the top left corner, the app’s Profile screen opens with the following options: Sensor, Logbook, Alarm Settings, Target Glucose Range, Reminders, Datahelper Setting, Glucose Bubble, Health Care, Help, Desktop Widget, and Contact Us.\
</span>

<span class="mark">Tapping the cogwheel icon in the top right corner gives you access to additional settings.</span>

### ***3.3 Do's & don’ts***

- Pair the sensor with your smartphone immediately after application. This helps prevent the built-in lithium battery from running out of power before the end of the 14-day wear period.

- Only one sensor can be paired at a time. Pairing a new sensor will automatically disconnect the existing one.

- Keep the sensor and smartphone within 10 meters (or 32.8 feet) of each other, with no obstacles such as walls or metal between them. Otherwise, communication may be affected.

- For the best user experience, keep Bluetooth turned on and allow the app to run in the background to avoid missing data. Do not force close the app.

- The sensor is designed to function normally within a temperature range of 5°C to 40°C. When the temperature is too high or too low, the CGM may display abnormal glucose readings. Normal operation will resume once the user returns to a suitable temperature environment.

- Taking painkillers, high-dose vitamin C, or hydroxyurea can result in falsely high CGM glucose readings. Consult your healthcare provider first if you plan to use any of these medications while wearing your sensor.

- The sensor must not be used with automated insulin dosing systems, including low-glucose-suspend systems.

- Remove the sensor before undergoing an MRI or CT scan.

This video walked you through starting the Sibionics GS3 CGM, including pairing the sensor with your smartphone and finding your way around the app. When guiding your patients through their initial experience with the sensor, keep in mind that technology can sometimes present challenges, such as connectivity issues, incorrect sensor readings, or a sensor that stops prematurely. When these situations arise, stay calm and persistent. Review the available troubleshooting options and contact customer support if needed. With your knowledge, patience, and commitment, you can confidently empower your patients to fully benefit from their CGM.

## **<u>4. Setting alarms</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alarms with the Sibionics GS3 CGM. In this video, we’ll look at the different types of alarms the GS3 sensor can generate and show you how to configure them in the app. Let’s start.

### ***4.1 Available alarms***

With the GS3 CGM, alarms fall into two main categories: glucose level and trend alarms and system status alarms.

Among alarms for glucose levels, the Urgent Low Glucose Alarm lets you know when your glucose is at or below 56 mg/dl or 3.1 mmol/L. The Low Glucose Alarm warns you when your glucose is at or below the low limit you have set. Meanwhile, the High Glucose Alarm notifies you when your glucose is at or above your selected high limit.

The GS3 sensor also provides alarms for rapidly changing glucose levels. The Rising Fast Alarm is issued when your glucose is increasing faster than 2 mg/dl or 0.11 mmol/L per minute and is above your specified threshold. Meanwhile, the Falling Fast Alarm appears when your glucose is dropping faster than 2 mg/dl or 0.11 mmol/L per minute and is below your specified threshold.

For any of these glucose-related alarms, you can tap Later to acknowledge the alert, or log an event you believe may have contributed to the change.

Until you acknowledge the alarm or log an event, the alarm box will remain on your app screen. The sound and/or vibration will repeat when the system issues the next alarm. Remember that the system analyzes your glucose readings every five minutes.

Under system status, a Signal Loss Alarm indicates that your sensor has temporarily stopped communicating with the app and you are no longer receiving glucose readings or Low or High Glucose Alarms. Follow the on-screen instructions to restore the connection. If the issue persists, contact Customer Service at support@sibionics.com.

### ***4.2 Setting alarms on the app***

> To customize your Low and High Glucose Alarms, go to the app’s Profile screen, tap Alarm Settings, then select Low Glucose Alarm, High Glucose Alarm, or both. Here you can set the Alarm value and choose an alarm frequency between 5 minutes and 4 hours, then select Sound, Vibration, or Vibration & Sound as the alarm style. If you select Sound or Vibration & Sound, choose an alarm tone; the volume will follow your device settings. When you’re done, tap Confirm to save your settings.
>
> You can customize the Rising Fast and Falling Alarms too. Simply follow the on-screen prompts when these alarms appear.
>
> If you want to adjust the Signal Loss Alarm, tap Alarm Settings, then Signal Loss Alarm. Set how long the sensor can go without communicating with the app before an alert is triggered. Next, choose your alarm style, and tap confirm to save your settings.
>
> To restore the default alarm settings, tap Reset Alarm Settings.

### ***4.3 Tips***

# Turn Override Do Not Disturb on in the app for Urgent Low Glucose, Low Glucose, High Glucose, and Signal Loss alarms to ensure you receive audible alerts even when your device is on Do Not Disturb or has silent media volume.

# You must allow access to Do Not Disturb on your device to use the Override Do Not Disturb setting.

# For iOS: Allow Critical Alerts when prompted, or enable Critical Alerts from the app's notification settings.

# For Android: Allow Do Not Disturb access when prompted, or enable Do Not Disturb access for the app in your notification settings.

# The Urgent Low Glucose alarm will still trigger a vibration, even if your device is muted or Do Not Disturb is on.

Once enabled, you will be able to select a SIBIONICS Companion through the Health Care Connections tab and invite a follower who can monitor your data remotely.

### ***5.3 Creating reports***

Daily data shows the average of your sensor glucose readings, plus your Time in Range. Meanwhile, Daily Trending displays daily glucose readings as a graph, with event icons shown at the times they were recorded.

To choose the date for a report, tap Previous or Next at the top of the screen to move through daily data, or tap the date button to select a specific day to review.

AGP reports summarize data over multiple days and show the pattern and variability of sensor glucose levels over a typical day. The green band in the AGP represents the target glucose range. Note that the AGP requires at least 7 days of glucose readings to ensure statistical validity.

To export an AGP report, tap Report in the GS3 app. Set the time range to 7, 14, 30, or 90 days, or tap Custom to adjust the start and end dates manually. Then tap View Report to generate the report for the selected period. You can download or share the report using the icons in the top-right corner of the screen.

In the SIBIOINCS ProView Platform, it is possible to see 3 types of reports when you click on a patient from the homepage: AGP Report, Compare Report and a Daily Glucose Report. For each report, it is possible to change the date range. In the Compare Report, you can compare the AGP details with a previous period. In the Daily Report, you can hover over the glucose curve to see the specific glucose value, and also the specific record information like pictures of the food if they were taken at that time. Finally, it is always possible to download the reports from the download icon in the top right corner.

## **<u>6. Connecting other devices</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your Sibionics GS3 CGM to other devices. In this video, you’ll learn how to link your sensor with smartwatches and discover possibilities with smart insulin pens and pumps. Let’s begin.

### ***6.1 Smartwatches***

SIBIONICS does not yet offer a dedicated smartwatch app. A native Apple Watch app and an Android Watch app with direct-to-watch capability are in development.

There is a dedicated SiWatch, but it appears to work only with the GS1 sensor.

On Android, you can use third-party apps to view GS3 data on a smartwatch. Juggluco can connect to the native Sibionics app. xDrip+ can connect via a patched Sibionics app, and xDrip+ Companion mode also appears to work with the native app. Both Juggluco and xDrip+ can display glucose values on Wear OS and Garmin watches.

For Apple Watch users, there is currently no working solution.

### ***6.2 Smart insulin pens***

Currently, the Sibionics GS3 CGM does not connect directly to most commercially available smart insulin pens.

### ***6.3 Smart insulin pumps***

At present, the GS3 sensor is not compatible with any commercial AID systems. However, Sibionics is actively working toward integrating its sensor technology with insulin delivery. In particular, Sibionics has entered into a strategic partnership with PharmaSens to jointly develop an all-in-one insulin patch pump system that combines glucose monitoring and insulin delivery in a single device.

Separately, the GS3 can be linked to Juggluco or xDrip+, both of which can interface with AndroidAPS, a known open-source AID system. However, the GS3 is not considered a trusted glucose source for automated insulin delivery. It is not approved for use in any commercial AID system, and publicly available accuracy data remain very limited.

It's important to note that using open-source apps is not supported by Sibionics, nor is it approved by the FDA or European authorities. Engaging in this practice is entirely at your own risk. While we don't recommend it, we believe in being transparent about all available options.
