## **<span class="mark"><u>4. Setting alarms</u></span>**

<span class="mark">Welcome to this lesson on setting alarms with the Simplera sensor.</span>

<span class="mark">Throughout this session, we'll walk you through the available alarm options and demonstrate how to set them via your Simplera app or your MiniMed 780G insulin pump. Let's get started!</span>

### ***<span class="mark">4.1 Available alarms</span>***

<span class="mark">The Simplera sensor offers a diverse range of alarm options to keep you informed about specific glucose levels and sensor-related issues.</span>

- <span class="mark">You have the flexibility to set alarms for low glucose, high glucose, and rising or falling glucose.</span>

- <span class="mark">Additionally, there are predictive low and high alerts that activate when the system foresees your glucose reaching the user-defined threshold up to 60 minutes ahead.</span>

<span class="mark">All alerts are optional, except for the Urgent Low Alert that triggers when the sensor glucose value is at or below 54 mg/dl or 3.0 mmol/l.</span>

<span class="mark">The system includes an adjustable snooze function, allowing reminders for consistently high or low glucose levels.</span>

<span class="mark">Additionally, the system offers flexibility in setting an alert schedule, allowing you to customize alerts based on specific time blocks that suit your individual needs. There is also an option to silence all alerts for a specific duration of time.</span>

<span class="mark">To receive alarms, ensure your Simplera app or your MiniMed 780G pump is within 1.8 meters (6 feet) of the sensor. This proximity ensures timely alerts and seamless monitoring of your glucose levels.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">4.2 Setting alarms in the Simplera app</span>***

- <span class="mark">To customize alarms on your Simplera app, navigate to the settings menu. Use the toggle bar in the settings menu to change the alert volume.</span>

<!-- -->

- <span class="mark">Here, you can also opt to mute all alerts for a duration of maximum 4 hours.</span>

- <span class="mark">Via Glucose Alerts, you can set your Low alerts, High alerts, fall & rise alerts and snooze time.</span>

- <span class="mark">For the Low Alerts, you can choose to set them at maximum volume at night.</span>

<span class="mark">The Simplera app also has status alerts that provide information about actions needed to ensure the correct functioning of the system. Make sure to act on these alerts to continue to receive sensor glucose information in the future.</span>

<span class="mark">It is important to note that when power saving settings are enabled, the mobile device may delay the alerts and notifications from the Simplera app.</span>

### ***4.3 Setting alarms on the MiniMed 780G pump***

<span class="mark">To set alarms on your pump, navigate to the alarms section in the settings menu. Under the high setup, you can configure alarms for high glucose levels or rising glucose trends within specific time blocks. During the initial setup, high alarms are usually left off. In the low setup, you can set alarms for low glucose levels or falling glucose trends within specific time blocks. Typically, the lower limit is set at 70 mg/dL (3.9 mmol/L). In the alarm settings, you can also adjust the snooze function, which determines how long the alarm repeats. The default snooze time is 1 hour for high alarms and 20 minutes for low alarms.</span>

<span class="mark">The audio options on your pump also allow you to choose Alert Silence, which temporarily disables all entered sensor alarms for up to 24 hours. If you use the Minimed Mobile app in conjunction with your MiniMed 780G insulin pump, you can customise your alerts via the Notification section in the menu. However, please note that the MiniMed 780G has 3 alerts that cannot be turned off. The mandatory alerts on the Minimed 780G are:</span>

- <span class="mark">the Urgent Low Alert, when your sensor glucose is below 54 mg/dl or 3.0 mmol/l,</span>

- <span class="mark">the High alert when your sensor glucose is above 250 mg/dl or 13.8 mmol/l for more than 3 hours,</span>

- <span class="mark">and the SmartGuard exit alert, when the pump exits the SmartGuard feature.</span>

<span class="mark">By utilizing these alarm settings effectively, you can stay informed and proactive in managing your glucose levels with the Simplera sensor. When managing alarms, stay vigilant and ensure you're using the settings that best suit your needs. This proactive approach empowers you to respond effectively to changing glucose levels.</span>
