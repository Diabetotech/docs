## **<span class="mark"><u>6. Connecting other devices (12.5 min)</u></span>**

<span class="mark">Welcome to this lesson on connecting your Simplera sensor to other devices.</span>

<span class="mark">In this chapter, we explore the exciting possibilities of connecting your Simplera sensor with various devices, such as smartwatches, smart pens, and insulin pumps.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">6.1 Smart watch</span>***

<span class="mark">If you use the Simplera app or the MiniMed Mobile app connected to your MiniMed 780G insulin pump, you can enable pop-up alarms on your compatible phone and smartphone watch, providing quick access to crucial information.</span>

<span class="mark">This chapter delves into the process of connecting your Simplera sensor with popular smartwatches like Garmin, Samsung Galaxy, and Apple Watch.</span>

<span class="mark">An Apple Watch integration is provided natively if you download the MiniMed Mobile app or Simplera app on your iPhone. However, for Android watches, connectivity requires the use of a third-party app like xDrip+. Please be aware that xDrip+ is not supported by Medtronic and lacks approval from regulatory authorities like the FDA or EMA, making its usage carry inherent risks that should be approached cautiously.</span>

#### **\#1 Garmin smartwatches**

<span class="mark">For Garmin smartwatches, download the xDrip+ app from</span> <span class="mark">[<u>github.com/nightscoutfoundation/xdrip.</u>](http://github.com/nightscoutfoundation/xdrip.) Go to the settings of the xDrip+ app and set the data source to Companion app. This allows xDrip+ to receive sensor data from the Simplera app and MiniMed Mobile app. Also in the settings, adjust the inter-app settings as shown here to send the xDrip data to your connected Wear OS smartwatch. Then go to the Connect IQ store app on your phone, download the xDrip+/Spike/Nightscout Watch Face, and set xDrip as your companion app. On your watch you can now choose the xDrip+/Spike/Nightscout watch face to see your real-time Simplera data on your Garmin smartwatch.</span>

<span class="mark">If you have a Garmin Edge cycling computer, you can utilize the</span> <span class="mark">[<u>xDrip+/Spike/Nightscout Data Field</u>](https://apps.garmin.com/en-US/apps/5a3e2cda-12f0-4afd-88ed-000e67a68d84#0) feature to display Simplera data on it.</span>

#### **\#2 Samsung Galaxy smartwatch** 

<span class="mark">For Samsung Galaxy smartwatches, follow similar steps by downloading the xDrip+ app and setting the data source to "Companion App" in the settings. Adjust the xDrip+ app settings to send data to the G-Watch App by checking the Smartwatch and Inter-app settings. Next, install the G-Watch Wear app on your phone and set xDrip as your companion app. Finally, choose the according watch face on your Galaxy watch, and customise the watch face and alerts on your watch as you prefer.</span>

<span class="mark">Please remember that using open-source apps carries inherent risks and is not supported by Medtronic or approved by regulatory authorities. We provide this information transparently, though we don't recommend engaging in this practice without understanding the risks fully.</span>

#### **\#3 Apple Watch**

<span class="mark">Lastly, for Apple Watch, ensure you have the latest version of the Simplera and MiniMed Mobile app on your iPhone and install the Simplera or MiniMed app on your Apple Watch. Access the MiniMed app on your Apple Watch to view your glycemia within your SmartGuard shield, and swiping left will display your sensor graph and Time In Range.</span>

<span class="mark">To have easier access to your glucose data, you can integrate your sensor glycemia and SmartGuard Shield as a complication on compatible watch faces. These watch faces include Portrait, Nike (hybrid), Breathe, Utility, Simple, Photos, Motion, Gradient, XL, and Astronomy. Clicking on the complication will allow you to access the full MiniMed Watch app.</span>

<span class="mark">Remember, the setup steps may vary based on your phone and smartwatch, but you can find detailed tutorials in the provided downloads, making it easier to perform the setup with the devices on hand.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">6.2 Smart pens</span>***

<span class="mark">In addition to its integration with the Minimed 780g system, the Simplera sensor can also be used in conjunction with the InPen. To do this, simply download the Simplera app and the InPen app and follow the steps.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">6.3 Smart insulin pumps</span>***

<span class="mark">The Simplera Sync sensor is seamlessly integrated with the Minimed 780G system, working together flawlessly, thanks to both being offered by Medtronic and designed for compatibility.</span>

<span class="mark">The Simplera sensor is not compatible with iPhone-based open-source closed-loop systems. It appears to be compatible with AndroidAPS via xDrip+ when used in companion mode. Use of open-source applications carries inherent risks, is not supported by Medtronic, and is not approved by regulatory authorities. This information is provided for transparency only and does not constitute a recommendation to use such systems.</span>

<span class="mark">In conclusion, connecting your Simplera sensor to other devices opens up a world of possibilities for personalized diabetes management. From smartwatches to smart pens and insulin pumps, these connections offer valuable insights and real-time data. While utilizing open-source apps carries inherent risks, we provide transparency about all available options. Embrace the power of adaptability and empower yourself to optimize your diabetes management strategy with these innovative connections.</span>
