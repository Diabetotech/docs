## **Syai Ultra CGM System**

## **<u>1. Introduction</u>**

Welcome to this module on the Syai Ultra CGM System! In the upcoming videos, we'll examine the core capabilities of this compact, AI-powered sensor that generates high-frequency glucose readings.

The Syai Ultra CGM System measures approximately 19 × 20 × 4 mm and weighs 1.2 g. The sensor and transmitter are integrated into a single unit called the monitor, which is applied to the back of the upper arm using a disposable applicator. After a 30-minute warm-up period, the device transmits real-time glucose readings to the Syai app every minute. It uses factory calibration and does not allow user-performed calibration with fingerstick glucose measurements. The system incorporates AI-based technology to generate predictive alerts, personalized dietary insights, and glucose trend reports. The Syai Ultra is cleared for up to 14 days of wear and is indicated for adults aged 18 years and older.

Having covered the fundamentals in the Master Module on Glucose Sensors — such as understanding sensor readings and resolving common issues — we’ll now shift our focus to detailed training on the Syai Ultra CGM System.

Our goal in this module is to help you use the Syai Ultra CGM effectively, whether you’re a clinician supporting patients or an individual living with diabetes. Please note that the material presented is educational in nature and should not replace medical guidance. Be sure to speak with your healthcare provider before adjusting your treatment plan.

This module was produced by Dr. Inge Van Boxelaer, the founder of Diabetotech and an endocrinologist at the St-Lucas Hospital in Ghent, in Belgium. The content was reviewed by Lukas Trcka, a person living with type 1 diabetes from the Czech Republic who blogged about his experience with the Syai Ultra sensor. Together, their technical knowledge and clinical experience support an evidence-based, impartial analysis of the Syai Ultra CGM.

This material was created independently of Syai Health Technology, the CGM manufacturer, ensuring an objective assessment of the Syai Ultra’s benefits and drawbacks.

Upon completing this module, you will be confident in using the Syai Ultra CGM, share glucose data, and link the sensor to compatible smart devices. Also, you’ll be equipped with expert-backed tips to help you use the device safely and confidently.

Healthcare professionals will enhance their patient support skills, while Syai Ultra users will gain practical strategies they can implement right away. Let's dive in. I wish you all the best ahead!

## **<u>2. Placing the sensor</u>**

Welcome to the first lesson of the Syai Ultra CGM System module. In this video, you’ll learn the correct method for applying and removing the Syai Ultra sensor, including safety tips and best practices. Let’s get started.

### ***2.1 Supplies***

Prepare the following:

- a Syai Ultra CGM pack, which includes

  - a disposable applicator with a built-in monitor composed of an integrated sensor and transmitter;

  - and an adhesive patch;

- and an alcohol wipe.

### ***2.2 Applying the sensor*** 

### Download the Syai app by scanning the QR code on the product packaging or by searching for “Syai” in the App Store or Google Play Store. Follow the on-screen instructions to install the app. Open the app and tap “Sign up.” Enter your email address as your account, then tap “Send.” A verification code will be sent to your email. Enter the verification code, tap “Next,” and create a password for your account. Once registration is complete, tap the Syai app icon on your mobile device to launch the application and log in using your registered account details.

### Select the unit of measurement, either mmol/L or mg/dL, to be used in the app and in your glucose reports, and confirm your selection. If you are unsure which unit to choose, consult your healthcare provider.

### Follow the on-screen instructions to complete the app setup. This includes enabling Bluetooth, enabling Location Services, allowing Notifications, and entering your personal information. After entering your personal information, you will be directed to the “Apply New Monitor” page. 

### Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water. Choose an area on the back of your upper arm as the application site. Clean the site with an alcohol wipe, and let it dry completely. 

Check whether the sensor packaging is damaged or compromised. Rotate the package open and remove the device. Next, rotate the applicator to prepare it for application. Ensure the applicator is positioned relatively vertically during insertion. Place the applicator over the sanitized area on your arm, and press the button to apply the sensor. Finally, gently pull the applicator away from your arm.

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

When the monitor has been worn for 14 days, or after you tap the “End My Session” button, the session will be terminated. Before disposing of the monitor, ensure that all data has been uploaded by following the instructions provided in the app.

To remove the monitor, gently lift one corner of the adhesive at the edge of the device and slowly peel it away from the skin. If any adhesive residue remains, clean the area with warm, soapy water.

Dispose of the used monitor in accordance with local guidelines for items that have been in contact with body fluids.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

- When setting up the Syai app, select the correct unit of measurement to ensure accurate interpretation of your glucose data. Once the unit is confirmed, it cannot be changed unless a new account is registered.

- When choosing an application site, avoid areas with scars, moles, stretch marks, or lumps. The selected area should remain generally flat most of the time. Consider shaving the site to ensure proper adhesion.

- Do not use the applicator if it has been opened or damaged before use. The needle is sterile unless the applicator has been opened or damaged. Do not put the cover back on, as this may damage the monitor. Do not touch the inside of the applicator, as it contains a needle. During application, hold the applicator flat against your arm and ensure the bottom edge is fully adhered to the skin to avoid application failure. Before removing the applicator, keep it pressed against your arm for a few seconds to help the adhesive attach properly.

- Applying the monitor may cause minor bleeding. If bleeding occurs, wipe the area with a cotton swab. If necessary, use the cotton swab to apply gentle pressure on the small opening on the monitor, or apply an ice pack to help stop the bleeding. Remove the monitor and apply a new one at a different site only if the bleeding does not stop.

- Replace the monitor immediately if there is irritation or discomfort at the application site, if the Syai app reports a severe problem and indicates removal, or if the monitor filament tip has completely detached from the application site.

This video demonstrated how to apply and remove the Syai Ultra CGM System. By performing this process correctly each time, you ensure accurate glucose monitoring and build confidence in your therapy decisions.

## **<u>3. Starting the sensor</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on starting the Syai Ultra CGM System. In this video, we’ll show you how to get started with the Syai Ultra sensor and navigate the Syai app. In addition, we’ll share essential tips to promote safe and proper daily wear of the sensor. Let’s begin.

### ***3.1 Starting the sensor*** 

### Tap “Apply New Monitor” on the Syai Ultra app screen. Read the Instructions for Use carefully for important user guidance and additional information. Then scan the QR code located on the exterior of the applicator to verify the monitor. Be sure to follow the on-screen instructions for the best possible experience. Once the connection is established, you will be directed to the warm-up page. Real-time glucose readings will become available in the app once the 30-minute warm-up period is complete. 

### ***3.2 The Syai app***

The Syai app consists of four functional pages: Dashboard, Summary, Logbook, and Profile.

The Dashboard displays your current glucose value, a trend arrow, a glucose graph, and a glucose card on the graph.

Your glucose reading updates automatically every minute. The background uses a color scale to indicate whether your glucose level is high or low.

The trend arrow shows the direction and speed of change in your glucose level. It may indicate that your glucose is rapidly rising, rising fast, gradually rising, steady, gradually falling, falling fast, or rapidly falling.

The glucose graph displays your readings over the past 3 hours, 6 hours, 12 hours, or 24 hours.

The glucose card shows the time and glucose value for a selected point on the graph. When the label remains on the right side of the graph, it displays the current glucose reading and its time stamp. The data appears in different colors depending on whether your glucose level is within your target range.

The glucose graph also shows your selected target glucose range, which is used to calculate related glucose metrics, including Time in Range (TIR) and other analytics. The default target range is set at 70–180 mg/dL or 3.9–10.0 mmol/L. This range can be adjusted to accommodate different clinical needs or usage scenarios.

Below the glucose graph, you will find Today’s TIR and Average glucose.

To view additional glucose insights, open the Summary page. Tap the bar at the top of the screen to select different time periods, including 1, 7, 14, 30, or 90 days, and review your TIR and other glucose metrics.

On the Profile page, tap the purple device card to view details about the current monitor, including the remaining session time, serial number, and other device information. You can also end the current monitoring session from this page. In addition, the Profile page allows you to connect to compatible devices and tools within the Syai Health Ecosystem. You can manage data sharing with caregivers and healthcare providers, and access your glucose reports too.

You can tap the plus icon in the upper right corner or select Logbook at the bottom of the Home screen to record events such as blood glucose test results, meals, medications, insulin injections, exercise, sleep, or other custom entries. There is also an AI logging feature that allows you to record events using voice input. The system automatically recognizes the content and converts it into the appropriate event categories. You will then be prompted to review, edit, and save the entries in the app.

Beyond event logging, AI integration is a key component of the Syai Ultra system. The app includes an LLM-based AI expert that is available 24/7 to respond to user inquiries. It continuously learns from user interactions and provides personalized responses. To access the Syai AI feature, swipe up on the Home screen.

### ***3.3 Do's & don’ts***

- If startup fails, close the app and scan the QR code again to retry monitor activation.

- During startup, you may occasionally receive a prompt to update the system. If this occurs, follow the on-screen instructions to complete the upgrade.

- After the 30-minute warm-up period, the monitor requires additional time to stabilize with your body. During the first hour of wearing a new monitor, use a blood glucose meter to make treatment decisions.

- If your glucose readings do not match how you feel, check whether the monitor is loose or has become dislodged. If the filament is partially or fully out of the skin, remove the monitor immediately and apply a new one at a different site.

- Certain medications may affect the accuracy of glucose readings measured by the monitor. These include ascorbic acid (or vitamin C), salicylic acid, aspirin, methyldopa, tolbutamide, and acetaminophen (or paracetamol).

- To use Syai AI, ensure your device is connected to a trusted Wi-Fi network. Syai AI is enabled by default. If the portal is not responding, review your settings and confirm that the feature is turned on.

- Strenuous physical activity may cause the monitor to loosen due to sweating, increased body temperature, or muscle movement around the application site. If the device becomes loose, glucose readings may not reflect your actual levels. For this reason, it is important to follow the application guidelines carefully and choose an appropriate site.

- The Syai monitor is waterproof for normal activities such as showering, bathing, or swimming. However, it may not communicate properly while submerged because Bluetooth signals are weakened in water. Do not expose the device to water deeper than 1.5 meters (or 5 feet) or for longer than 30 minutes. After contact with water, dry the monitor with a clean towel.

- Exposure to hot water may reduce the monitor’s service life. Do not use the device near heat sources, such as fireplaces or radiant heaters, where temperatures may exceed the recommended operating range.

- Do not clean the monitor with household cleaners, chemicals, solvents, bleach, abrasive pads, or sharp objects. To clean the surface, use a small amount of rubbing alcohol only.

- You may continue wearing the monitor when passing through walk-through metal detectors or body scanners.

- Remove the monitor before undergoing an MRI, CT scan, or X-ray procedure.

In this video, we covered the Syai Ultra CGM system startup process, including pairing the sensor and finding your way around the app. Although technical challenges may arise from time to time, most can be resolved with a systematic troubleshooting approach and timely support. With your guidance and reassurance, you can help your patients optimize their CGM experience.

## **<u>4. Setting alarms</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alarms with the Syai Ultra CGM system. In this video, we’ll explore the sensor’s alarm features and show you how to customize them on the Syai app to suit your personal preferences. Let’s jump right in.

### ***4.1 Available alarms***

The system provides two main types of alerts: glucose alerts and system alerts.

Let’s start with glucose alerts.

- You will receive an Urgent Low alert when your glucose reading is at or below 55 mg/dL, or 3.1 mmol/L. This alert cannot be adjusted.

- A Low alert is triggered when your glucose reaches or falls below the low threshold that you have set.

- A High alert occurs when your glucose reaches or exceeds your selected high threshold.

- You can also enable optional Rising Fast and Falling Fast alerts. These notify you when your glucose level is increasing or decreasing rapidly, based on your chosen rate-of-change settings.

Now let’s look at how alerts are delivered.

- All alerts include a snooze function, which can be set between 5 and 60 minutes.

- You can also activate predictive alerts. These can warn you 20 or 30 minutes before your glucose is expected to reach your high or low threshold.

- You can choose how you want to be notified: via normal phone sound, high-pitch sound, a beep, vibration, or silence.

There is also a “Vibrate” option and a “Silence All” option.

- Silence All mutes every alert — including Urgent Low and system alerts — for a selected duration between 30 minutes and 6 hours.

Now let’s move to system alerts.

- You will receive a Signal Loss alert if there is a problem with the connection between the monitor and the app, or if network issues are delaying data transmission. This alert can be turned on or off in the settings.

- You will also be notified when your monitor is nearing the end of its wear period, and if the device is not functioning properly.

In the system notification window, you can see your current glucose value, the trend arrow, and the time of the most recent data update. The information displayed changes depending on the status of your monitor.

Finally, mobile home screen widgets are available. These widgets display different information depending on the current status of your monitor, allowing you to quickly view your glucose data without opening the app.

### ***4.2 Setting alarms on the Syai app***

To configure alerts in the Syai app, go to the Profile page, tap the Settings icon, and then select Alerts.

Under the Quiet Modes section, you can choose preset options that control how alerts notify you. These modes do not override your phone’s system notification settings, but they do affect the sound settings for individual Glucose and System alerts. Read each description carefully to avoid missing important alerts.

Under the Glucose Alerts section, you can choose the specific glucose alert you want to customize.

The Urgent Low alert cannot be turned off and its threshold cannot be changed. But you can set how you want to be notified and choose from different ringtones. If the condition continues, you will be alerted repeatedly based on the interval you have set.

You can also enable Predictive Alerts for Urgent Low and Low alerts. Specifically, you can set a timeframe for Urgent Low Soon or Low Soon notifications. The app will then alert you if your glucose level is predicted to reach or fall below your selected threshold within the chosen time period.

For Rising Fast and Falling Fast alerts, use the toggle to enable or disable each alert. In addition, you can set the rate-of-change threshold so that you are notified when your glucose is rising or falling at or beyond the selected rate. Regarding Signal Loss alerts, you will only be notified if the connection issue continues beyond the time threshold you have set.

You can create an additional Alert Profile to customize how alerts behave in specific situations, such as during meetings. After setting your alert preferences, you can define a schedule by selecting the active time period for each day and choosing the days of the week for repetition. If you do not enable the Repeat option, the profile will apply only to the selected time period for that day and will not carry over to other days. The original alert configuration is saved as the Primary profile, which allows you to switch between the default and your custom profile as needed. After adding a new profile, you will have a total of two alert profiles available. Finally, If you select Reset to Default on the Alerts page, your custom alert profile will be deleted, and all alert settings will return to their original defaults. Use this option with caution.

### ***4.3 Do’s & don’ts***

## Enable notifications for the Syai app during the initial setup. Do not disable app notifications in your mobile device settings. If notifications are turned off, you will not receive alerts and may be at risk of missing significant glycaemic events.

## Although the alert system has been clinically tested, always pay attention to how you feel. If your symptoms do not match the alert notifications, if alerts are not triggered during suspected hypoglycaemia or hyperglycaemia, or if you believe your readings may be inaccurate, perform a fingerstick test using a blood glucose meter for confirmation and consult your healthcare provider if necessary.

## **<u>5. Sharing data</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on sharing data with the Syai Ultra CGM System. This video covers how to grant sensor data access to loved ones and clinicians, as well as how to create glucose reports. Let’s start.

### ***5.1 Sharing data with your loved ones***

<span class="mark">You can share your glucose data with a family member or friend by sending an invitation through the Syai app. Go to Profile and select Home Care. Tap Invite to follow on the Home Care page. On the next screen, enter the caregiver’s email address or phone number, then set the visibility permissions for that account. Tap Confirm to send the invitation.</span>

<span class="mark">The caregiver must download the Syai Link mobile app and register for a Syai Link account before they can accept the invitation. They must complete the registration using the same email address that you entered in the Syai app.</span>

<span class="mark">Note that you can invite up to 15 caregivers per Syai account.</span>

### ***5.2 Sharing data with healthcare provider***

On the Profile page of the Syai app, tap Doctor Care. Send an invitation to your provider using their email address or phone number. You may link up to five doctor accounts to a single Syai account.

Your healthcare provider can accept the invitation using a registered Syai Doctor account through the Syai Doctor web portal.

If you don’t have an account yet, click Create Now below the Log In button. Enter your email address, then click Register Now, accept Syai’s privacy policy and terms of use by ticking the checkbox, and click Register Now again. Enter the verification code sent to your email, then click Next.

After that, create and confirm your password. Your password must be 8 to 36 characters long and include at least one digit, one uppercase letter, and one lowercase letter. Click Confirm to proceed.

Next, provide the required account information, including your first name, last name, country, state or province, city, patient grouping phone number, and patient grouping. Review your details, then click Next. Leave the Partner Allocation option toggled on to ensure you receive optimal support from a Syai Partner or from Syai Health, depending on your location. Click Next again.

Finally, select and confirm your preferred blood glucose unit—either mg/dL or mmol/L. Once completed, you will be taken to the dashboard, also known as the Patient Management page.

Alternatively, clinicians can use the Syai Doctor mobile app. After receiving the email invitation with instructions, they must download the app, register using the same email address, and follow the steps to begin viewing your data.

### ***5.3 Creating reports***

Aside from the Profile Report, which summarizes your personal details and CGM information, the Syai app provides several types of glucose reports.

The Snapshot report presents an overview of glucose data, low glucose events, insulin, meals, and Time in Range or TIR for your selected date range.

The Daily Glucose Profiles display glucose data from midnight to midnight for each day, with the corresponding date shown in the top-left corner.

The Ambulatory Glucose Profile (AGP) presents a graph of the 10th, 25th, 50th, 75th, and 90th percentiles of your glucose readings over the selected report period, showing overall glucose variability.

The Mealtime Patterns report analyzes glucose and insulin data for typical meals based on meal logs recorded within the chosen timeframe. It identifies patterns for users who log rapid-acting insulin and meals in the app. Entries that are not tagged with a meal type are excluded from the analysis.

The Statistics report provides a daily and hourly summary of your glucose data, including Time in Range; high and low glucose percentages; average, maximum, and minimum values; as well as variability metrics such as standard deviation, coefficient of variation, MAGE, and MODD.

Lastly, The Daily Log offers a detailed view of meal entries, insulin records, other logged events, and corresponding glucose readings for each day within the selected date range.

To view your glucose reports, select Glucose Report on the Profile page. From the list of available reports, choose the report you want to open in the report viewer. From there, tap the icon in the upper-right corner to download or share the report.

## **<u>6. Connecting other devices</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your Syai Ultra CGM System to other devices. In this video, we'll examine how the sensor works in sync with smartwatches, insulin pens, and insulin pumps to support diabetes care. Let’s get started.

### ***6.1 Smartwatches***

Glucose data from the Syai app is synced to supported applications and then mirrored to compatible smartwatches.

Supported Apple devices include Apple Watch models running watchOS 9.0 or later. To install the app, search for “Syai” in the Watch App Store or download it from the App Store on your paired iPhone, which will then sync to your watch.

For Garmin devices, including selected Forerunner, Fenix, MARQ Adventurer, and other models that support the Connect IQ Store, search for “Syai” in the Connect IQ Store and download it.

For Samsung Galaxy Watch series, Galaxy Watch Active series, and other models running Google Wear OS, download the app from the Google Play Store.

Google Pixel Watch 2 is also supported and can install the app from the Google Play Store.

To connect your Syai Ultra CGM with a Garmin or Galaxy smartwatch, open the Syai app and go to the Profile tab. Tap Connect with Watch under the Connections section. On the next screen, tap Garmin under App Bridging. You will be taken to the Garmin data authorization page. Tap Connect Now, then follow the on-screen prompts.

Once you’ve installed the Syai app on your Garmin, Galaxy or Pixel watch, open the Syai app on your phone and go to the Profile page. Tap Connect with Watch under the Connections section. For Garmin, tap Garmin under App Bridging. You will be taken to the Garmin data authorization page. Tap Connect Now, then follow the on-screen prompts. For Galaxy and Pixel, under Direct to Monitor, select Wear OS watches. You will be taken to a screen with a QR code scanner. Tap Scan now, then scan the QR code on the Syai app on your watch to log in.

Note that smartwatch compatibility is based on internal testing, and availability may vary depending on the specific model, region, or regulatory requirements.

The Syai watch app displays your current glucose reading, trend arrow, and glucose graph, similar to what you see in the mobile app. You will also receive notifications on your watch for events such as Urgent Low, Low, and High alerts, and you can acknowledge these alerts directly from the notification. Moreover, the watch app shows the monitor status, including messages such as “No Active Monitor,” “Warming Up,” or “Disconnected.”

For users who prefer other smartwatch ecosystems, the Syai app can locally broadcast glucose data to xDrip, enabling display on additional devices such as Amazfit, Pebble, MiBands, and other compatible smart bands. Glucose data can also be transmitted from the Syai app to Nightscout (CGM in the Cloud) for remote monitoring and integration with other diabetes management tools.

### ***6.2 Smart insulin pens***

At this time, direct Bluetooth pairing with smart insulin pens is not available for the Syai Ultra.

However, connection with NovoPen® 6 is possible via NFC scan. By scanning the pen with a compatible smartphone, insulin dose data can be transferred and integrated within the app.

### ***6.3 Smart insulin pumps***

# Currently, the Syai Ultra does not offer native, built-in integration with smart insulin pumps for commercial automated insulin delivery systems. However, it can connect to insulin pumps through AndroidAPS. To set this up, install the Syai app from the Google Play Store and start your sensor session as usual. Once the sensor is active, open AndroidAPS, go to Config Builder, and select Syai as the primary BG Source. Then return to the Syai app. Open your Profile, and go to the Syai Health Ecosystem menu. Select “Connect Now” with AAPS, accept the third-party data transfer agreement, and enable the data-sharing toggle. This allows real-time glucose readings from Syai to broadcast directly into AndroidAPS.

# Please be aware that open-source applications like AndroidAPS and xDrip+ are not endorsed by Syai Health and are not approved by the FDA or European regulators. If you choose to use these systems, you assume full responsibility for any associated risks. While we do not recommend this pathway, we are providing this information in the interest of transparency.
