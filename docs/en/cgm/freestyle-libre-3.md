## **<u>2. Placing the sensor</u>**

Welcome to this lesson about placement of the FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor. In this video, we will guide you through the process of placing and removing a FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor, and go over some do’s and don’ts.

### ***2.1 Supplies***

Let's get started by gathering the essential supplies: a sensor box and an alcohol swab. Within the box, you'll discover the sensor applicator, pre-loaded with the sensor.

### ***2.2 Inserting the sensor***

Properly inserting the FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor involves the following steps:

- Start by washing your hands and selecting the spot on the back of your upper arm where the sensor will be placed. Remember to rotate sites between applications.

- Wash the application site with plain soap and water, wipe dry with a clean towel, clean with an alcohol wipe, and let air dry. Following these three easy steps helps ensure the sensor stays on your skin.

- The FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor and applicator already come assembled for you. Simply unscrew the cap and set it aside. Do not put the cap back on, as it may damage the sensor. Place the sensor applicator over the prepared site on the back of your upper arm and push down firmly to apply the sensor to your body.

- The sensor should now be attached to your skin. Then make sure the sensor is secure by gently pressing down with your finger around the edge.

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

To remove the FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor, gently and slowly pull it away from your skin. Dispose of the sensor in the appropriate bin.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

- For ease of placement on the upper arm, lift your arm and elbow to avoid excessive twisting.

- When placing the sensor, aim for a 90-degree angle.

- The System is designed to detect certain conditions which may occur where the Sensor is not working as intended and shut it off, telling you to replace your Sensor. This may occur if the Sensor gets knocked off from the skin or if the System detects that the Sensor may not be performing as intended. Contact Customer Service if you receive a Replace Sensor message before the end of the wear duration specified by your Sensor insert.

- You can find the contact details of your local Abbott Customer Service online. In many countries it is not even necessary to call them, you can just fill in an online form, to request a new sensor to be send to your home.

If you ever need a new sensor, you'll need the serial number from the back of your sensor applicator. So, it's a good idea to keep that somewhere safe, instead of throwing it away.

By mastering the art of sensor placement, you are building a solid foundation for seamless monitoring. With each successful insertion and removal, you ensure accurate data for informed decision-making.

## **<u>3. Starting and scanning the sensor</u>** 

Welcome to this video where we will explore how to start the FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor. Whether you have the FreeStyle Libre 3 reader or the FreeStyle Libre 3 app on your mobile phone, this video will guide you through the process step by step.

<span class="mark">For simplicity, we will only discuss the FreeStyle Libre 3 app. In some regions, however, the dedicated app for the FreeStyle Libre 3 or Libre Plus sensor may appear as the “Libre by Abbott” app.</span>

Please note that you cannot simultaneously use both the reader and the FreeStyle Libre 3 app to access glucose data. Data can only be transmitted to one device at a time. Therefore, you'll need to decide which device you want to use before initiating a sensor. If you happen to use two mobile phones, such as a work phone and a personal phone, you can log out of the FreeStyle Libre 3 app on one phone and then log in on the other phone using the same sensor. To make a connection to the new phone, you will need to scan the sensor using the “Start new sensor“ option. You’ll get a message that your sensor session will end, but you can ignore this.

### ***3.1 FreeStyle Libre 3 reader***

If you opt to use the FreeStyle Libre 3 reader, let's explore how it works.

The front of the reader features a touchscreen and a home button. On the side, you'll find a microUSB port for charging the reader and reading data. And at the bottom, there is a test strip port, allowing you to measure your blood glucose with a simple finger prick.

The FreeStyle Libre 3 reader is only compatible with FreeStyle Precision Neo blood glucose test strips. By inserting the test strip and applying blood to it, you can utilize the built-in blood glucose meter. The Reader's built-in meter does not have ketone testing functionality. And it is not used to calibrate the sensor.

Don't forget to charge the reader daily, preferably using the provided cable. Many users find it convenient to charge it overnight next to their bed, ensuring uninterrupted alerts while charging. Rest assured, while charging the reader, you will still be able to use it.

Now, let's walk through the step-by-step process of starting the sensor using the reader:

1.  Start by pressing the yellow power button to activate the reader**.**

2.  If you haven't connected a sensor yet, the reader will display the home screen with three options: "View Glucose," "Review History," and "Start New Sensor." If you already have a connected sensor, the reader will show your current glucose curve. You can return to the home screen by pressing the yellow button once more. The status bar at the top of the screen shows the current time, battery level, sound and vibration settings, sensor end time, and a cogwheel icon for accessing settings.

3.  Tap "Start New Sensor" and hold the reader so that the screen makes contact with the FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor. You may need to move the reader back and forth until a signal is established. If you have enabled sound, the reader will emit a beep when the sensor is activated. Otherwise, it may vibrate.

4.  After the one hour sensor warm-up, you can view your sensor glucose on the reader. It will display your current sensor glucose level, a glucose trend arrow, and your recent glucose curve.

5.  If your glucose level drops below 40 mg/dL or 2.2 mmol/L, the reader will indicate "LO." Conversely, if it exceeds 500 mg/dL or 27.8 mmol/L, it will display "HI." In such cases, it's crucial to confirm your glucose level with a finger prick and take appropriate action to address low or high glucose levels.

6.  The top-left corner of the screen provides additional information about your glucose level. It can show "Low Glucose" if the sensor glucose is below 70 mg/dL or 3.9 mmol/L, "High Glucose" if it's above 250 mg/dL or 13.9 mmol/L, or "Going Low" or "Going High" if the glucose is projected to reach these thresholds within the next 15 minutes.

7.  By tapping the pencil icon in the top-right corner, you can add important details such as insulin dose, food intake, or exercise. Remember to press "OK" to save this information. These actions will be represented by small markers, such as an insulin syringe or an apple, on the sensor graph, which tracks your glucose levels for the past 12 hours.

To explore your history, navigate to Review History on the Home Screen. Here you find the Logbook, Daily Graph, Average Glucose, Daily Patterns, Time In Target, Low-Glucose Events, and Sensor Usage.

The home screen also features a settings cog, offering you further customization options. Within the settings, you can set alarms, adjust sound & vibration preferences, adjust time and date, set reminders, test the built-in blood glucose meter using a control solution, change the language, view the system status, change the report settings and consult reader basics.

Ultimately, in the European version of the FreeStyle Libre 3 reader, there are professional options available as well. To access them, you need to confirm that you are a healthcare provider and enter the code CAA1C. Once inside, you can modify the insulin dose increment and configure a bolus calculator.

We will not go deeper into how to use the built-in bolus calculator, as the calculator is only for use with fingerstick blood glucose results from the built-in meter. You cannot use the insulin bolus calculator with sensor glucose readings.

### ***3.2 FreeStyle Libre 3 app***

Now, let's explore the process of starting the sensor using the FreeStyle Libre 3 app on your mobile phone.

To get started, download the FreeStyle Libre 3 app from the app store, and tap the icon to open it. Swipe left to see some helpful tips or tap get started now at any point. If you already have a LibreView account, tap ”Sign in”. Or you can create one right in the app. Continue following the on-screen messages to finish setting up the app. Setup is complete when you see an illustration to scan a new sensor, then you'll be ready to start your sensor. To start a new sensor, you'll need to scan it with your smartphone. From the app home screen or from the app’s menu, tap “Scan New Sensor”. Your phone is now ready to scan the sensor. To start it, touch the sensor with the top of your phone slowly. If needed, slowly move the phone around. You will receive a tone and vibration after you have successfully started the sensor. This is the only time you'll need to scan your sensor. The sensor can be used to check your glucose after 60 minutes. A countdown in the app will indicate how much time is left.

After 60 minutes, the home screen will display your glucose information with automatic updates every minute from your sensor. This is your current glucose that is measured every minute. A message appears here letting you know about your glucose. For instance, if your glucose is in range or going low, the trend arrow shows the direction your glucose is going. It tells you if your glucose is steady, rising slowly, rising quickly, falling slowly, or falling quickly. In the middle of the home screen is your 12-hour glucose graph. It shows your current and historical glucose readings, along with your target glucose range. These two dotted lines represent your high and low glucose alarm levels. They appear only when alarms are turned on. You can add notes by tapping “add note” or “edit note” here. To see a note you've already added, tap the symbol or icon at the top of the glucose graph. At the bottom is the sensor life bar. It shows the number of days remaining on your sensor. In the top left corner is the main menu. Tapping here gives you access to features like alarms, your logbook, connected apps, and much more.

Another feature of the home screen is the background color, which makes it easy to see if your glucose is in range with a quick glance. Green indicates you are in your glucose target range, red means low glucose, or below 70 milligrams per deciliter or 3.9 millimole per liter, and yellow indicates you are out of your target range. Orange means high glucose, or above 250 milligrams per deciliter or 13.9 millimole per liter.

Using the FreeStyle Libre 3 app might have a noticeable effect on your phone's battery life, because the sensor sends data every minute. If your phone's battery runs out, you won’t be able to see your glucose data, so use a phone with a good battery and charge more often as needed.

When setting up a new sensor, it's important to note that the location of the NFC (Near Field Communication) antenna differs between iPhone and Android devices. On an iPhone, you'll typically find it on the front of the device, often in the top center. For Android devices, it's usually located at the back, either in the middle or towards the top.

Take a moment to gently move the phone over the sensor, observing where the scanning functionality is situated.

If you encounter any issues with scanning, it's possible that your NFC antenna may be damaged or your NFC function could be turned off. Make sure to check these settings before troubleshooting further.

Some phone cases block NFC, preventing you from scanning. This is usually due to a protective layer built into the cases to prevent unwanted NFC payments to the phone. If your phone case is made of plastic or silicone or another non-conductive material, you can probably scan the sensor without removing the case. If the case is made of aluminum, metal or another material with a conductive surface, you may need to remove the case before scanning the sensor. The thickness of the case can also make a difference. If your case is very thick, it may prevent the phone from scanning the sensor.

With the FreeStyle Libre 3 app, you have the convenience of adding and viewing notes in the Logbook at any time, providing you with greater flexibility in recording important information. This feature sets it apart from the reader, where note-taking can not be done in retrospect.

The app also offers more extensive reporting capabilities, including real-time tracking of GMI which stands for glucose management indicator, which is similar to the estimated HbA1c.

### ***3.3 Do's & don’ts***

Now, let's discuss some do's and don'ts for using the system:

- The sensor is waterproof up to 1 meter (3 feet) for 30 minutes. Keep in mind that swimming beyond this depth or duration may affect its functionality.

- The reader itself is not waterproof and comes with a 1-year warranty, although if your reader is broken, customer service might replace it even if the 1-year warranty has passed.

- Remember that the reader's time and date do not adjust automatically. You'll need to manually update them via the settings, especially when changing time zones. On the other hand, if you use the FreeStyle Libre 3 app on your mobile phone, the time will synchronize automatically with your phone's time. When the time is adjusted, a watch symbol will appear on the charts.

- The reader may display different error messages, some with explanatory texts and others with an E followed by a number. Refer to the user manual for detailed explanations of these messages.

- The FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor has a very good transmission range of 10 meters or 33 feet. If you lose the connection between your sensor and your reader or phone, don’t worry. The FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor stores 14 days of data. Therefore, once you reconnect to your sensor, you'll still have access to your glucose curve.

- Be aware that the glucose graph displays glucose readings above 350 mg/dL or 19.4 mmol/l as 350 mg/dL or 19.4 mmol/l. For consecutive readings above that threshold, a line is displayed. The Current Glucose number can be as high as 400 mg/dL or 22.2 mmol/l.

- The FreeStyle Libre 3 does not have the following features: it is not possible to calibrate the sensor with a finger prick value, it doesn’t have a text-to-speech function, and it doesn’t have compatibility with any smart insulin pen, although this might change in the future.

- The FreeStyle Libre 3 app also doesn’t offer a landscape view of the glucose curve and you cannot see your exact glucose values from the past, or zoom in on the graph. You can only estimate your glucose value from the past, by looking at the gra<span class="mark">ph. The Libre by Abbott and LibreLinkUp app do allow you to see exact glucose values from the past. We will co</span>ver the LibreLinkUp app later in this course.

Remember, technology can occasionally present us with unexpected hurdles. If you encounter difficulties scanning the sensor or connecting your devices, don't be discouraged. Stay persistent, explore troubleshooting options, and reach out to customer support for assistance. With patience and a determined spirit, you can overcome these obstacles and continue your path towards better health and well-being.

## **<u>4. Setting alarms</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alarms with the FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor. During this session, we will guide you through the process of setting alarms and inform you about the available options. We'll cover how to set alarms on both the reader and the FreeStyle Libre 3 app on your phone, ensuring you have a comprehensive understanding of the process. Let's get started!

### ***4.1 Available alarms***

With the FreeStyle Libre 3 system, all alarms are optional. You can set high and/or low alarms, as well as an alarm on signal loss. There is no possibility to set predictive alarms for hyper or hypoglycemia.

In some countries, there is an additional Urgent Low Glucose alarm, which will be delivered when your glucose goes below 55 mg/dl or 3 mmol/L. This alarm cannot be turned off, but it can be silenced with your other glucose alarms for a set period.

Whether you opt for the reader or the mobile phone, alarms will work as long as the device is within 10 meters (or 33 feet) of your sensor, provided there are no walls obstructing the signal.

Upon receiving an alarm, it's important to react properly and dismiss it. If you're using the FreeStyle Libre 3 reader, you can do so by tapping "Dismiss Alarm" or pressing the Home Button. If you're utilizing the FreeStyle Libre 3 app, simply tap the notification on your phone to dismiss the alarm. Alarms will repeat again until acknowledged.

### ***4.2 Setting alarms on the reader***

On the reader’s home screen, you can access the alarms feature through the cogwheel icon. Here, you can define thresholds for the low and high alarms, as well as opt for an alarm on signal loss. Adjust sound and vibration preferences according to your preference. The reader offers a single type of sound and two levels of volume.

### ***4.3 Setting alarms on the mobile phone***

To set or turn on alarms go to the main menu, tap alarms, and tap the slider to turn the low glucose alarm on. If the alarm is on you'll be notified the minute your glucose crosses the alarm level you set, which is initially set at 70 milligrams per deciliter or 3.9 mmol per liter. You can choose a value between 60 and 100 mg/dl or 3.3 and 5.6 mmol/l. Then tap save. Tap Override Do Not Disturb, to always play an alarm sound and appear on the lock screen, even if your phone is muted, or if do not disturb is enabled on your phone. To receive alarms do not force close the app. If you restart your phone, open the app to ensure it's running in the background. Follow the same steps to set a high glucose alarm. You can also customize your signal loss alarm which automatically turns on, the first time you turn the low or high glucose alarm on. When you receive an alarm such as a low glucose alarm, it will notify you the minute your glucose crosses the alarm level you set. If your phone is locked a notification about the alarm will appear on the lock screen. It will indicate your current glucose reading and trend arrow indicating where your glucose is going. Tap the notification to open the app and dismiss the alarm.

It's worth mentioning that if you encounter a red exclamation mark in the alarms section, it indicates an issue with your phone's settings. Simply click on it and follow the provided instructions to resolve the matter.

The customization options for alarm sound type and volume in the FreeStyle Libre 3 app are limited. The default alarm sound is set to a very loud level, referred to as "Custom." If you are using an iPhone, you can switch this to "Standard" for a less loud alarm.

To change the volume of the alarm sound, you can turn OFF the "Override Do Not Disturb" setting for the optional alarms. When "Override Do Not Disturb" is turned OFF, the FreeStyle Libre 3 app will follow your smartphone's volume and vibration settings.

In our pursuit of optimal glucose management, let us remain resilient, and set alarms that serve as our trusted companions, alerting us to crucial shifts in our glucose levels.

## **<u>5. Sharing data</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on sharing data with the FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor.

In this chapter, we explore the process of sharing data with your healthcare provider and loved ones, and creating reports.

### ***5.1 Sharing data with your healthcare provider***

Your healthcare provider accesses data through LibreView, Abbott's reading software. Patient and professional versions are available, and you can create your account on libreview.com at any time.

In Libreview for professionals, you can create your practice and obtain an ID number. This number is necessary to link to your patients' personal Libreview accounts.

Data can be uploaded to Libreview in two ways, depending on your scanning device. By using the FreeStyle Libre 3 app, all your data will be automatically sent to LibreView. If you use the reader, manual upload is required. The reader can store up to 3 months of data.

Connect the reader to your PC via a USB cable and follow the instructions in Libreview. You can choose to read out the reader in the patient's name for easy retrieval.

If you use your mobile phone, sign up for LibreView in the FreeStyle Libre 3 app. Enter your practice's ID number and join the practice. Once linked, real-time and remote tracking of patients is possible.

It's also possible for the practice to add a patient by entering their email. After that, the patient will receive an invitation in their app under LibreView.

In Libreview, you can verify the success of the update by ensuring that the latest date within the data range matches the current date.

***5.2 Create reports***

Once all data is synced to LibreView, you can generate various reports by selecting "glucose reports." Among the nine available reports, commonly used ones include:

- AGP report, providing TIR, TBR, and AGP profile for the last two weeks.

- Daily Log, offering insights into the timing and impact of individual insulin boluses on a day-to-day basis.

- Snapshot, displaying the number of events of hypoglycemia and the frequency of sensor scans per day.

- And Device details, where alarm settings are found.

The Glucose Pattern Insights Report is a newer report that offers an interpretation of the Ambulatory Glucose Profile by highlighting glycaemic patterns and supplying medication and lifestyle considerations to address them.

Interpretation guidance for the reports can be found in our general module on glucose sensors.

### ***5.3 Sharing data with family***

As a caregiver, you can download the LibreLinkUp app and connect it to the user's personal Libreview account.

To establish the connection:

- The user must approve data sharing by accessing the connected apps menu in the FreeStyle Libre 3 app and selecting LibreLinkUp. Add the follower's name and email address, then press add.

- The follower will receive an invitation email where he can then download the LibreLinkUp app, create an account, and follow the provided instructions.

- The owner of the LibreLinkUp app can set his own notifications.

One advantage the LibreLinkUp app has over the FreeStyle 3 app is the ability to view exactly glucose values from the past 12 hours. You can hold down on the screen and slide over the graph to see each reading.

Embracing these sharing features enhances collaboration, resilience, and informed decision-making for a more empowered journey towards better health.

## **<u>6. Connecting other devices</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your FreeStyle 3 Libre sensor to other devices.

In this chapter, we explore the exciting possibilities of connecting your FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor with various devices, such as smartwatches, smart pens, and insulin pumps.

Some individuals opt to use open-source apps that allow for broader access to sensor data from their FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor. The most used apps are Gluroo and Nightguard for iPhones, and xDrip+ and Juggluco for Android phones. Gluroo and Nightguard are available for download from the Apple Store, while xDrip+ and Juggluco cannot be downloaded from the App store. For further guidance on using these apps, you can refer to the AndroidAPS documentation site. It's important to note that these third-party apps lack support from Abbott and have not received approval from the FDA or EMA. Users should be aware of and accept the associated risks when utilizing them.

### ***6.1 Smart watch***

Currently, there is no native application that allows you to follow your FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor data in real-time on your smartwatch. However, some compatible smartwatches have the ability to mirror smartphone notifications, including alarms and other app alerts. On supported models, you will not only see that there is an alarm, but also see the exact glucose value of that moment. Among the supported models are the Apple Watch Series 7 and newer models, Fitbit Versa 3, and Samsung Galaxy Watch 4. To get the most recent list, you can search online for FreeStyle 3 compatibility.

Alternatively, if you use open-source apps to access your FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor data on your smartphone, it is also possible to use these apps to receive real-time data on your smartwatch.

Let's explore how this functionality works on three common types of smartwatches: Garmin smartwatches, Samsung Galaxy watches, and Apple Watch.

#### ***\#1 Garmin Smartwatch***

Here are the step-by-step instructions for getting your FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor data on your Garmin smartwatch.

- Download the Juggluco app via the [<u>Androidaps documentation site</u>](https://androidaps.readthedocs.io/nl/latest/Hardware/Libre3.html) and [<u>follow the directions</u>](https://www.juggluco.nl/Juggluco/libre3/) to get the FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor data on the Juggluco app.

- Tap on the left side of the Juggluco to open the left menu, open the Watch menu, and toggle on Kerfstok. In the status of Kerfstok, you can toggle on glucose.

- Install the [<u>Garmin Connect IQ Store app</u>](https://apps.garmin.com/nl-BE/) on your phone, and download the [<u>Kerfstok app</u>](https://www.juggluco.nl/Kerfstok/).

- If your Garmin watch is connected to your phone, you can open the Kerfstok app and choose for Watch face. This Watch Face like screen shows the current time, date, battery level, heart rate, glucose level and glucose trend.

#### ***\#2 Samsung Galaxy Smartwatch*** 

Here are the step-by-step instructions for getting your FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor data on your Samsung Galaxy Smart watch, or other smartwatches that work with Wear OS.

1.  Install the LibreLinkUp app and register as a follower within your FreeStyle Libre 3 app, using a different email address than the one associated with your LibreView account. After completing this step, you will have two separate accounts: one for logging into the Libre 3 app, referred to as a LibreView account, and another for accessing the LibreLinkUp app, known as a LibreLinkUp account. Once you log in to LibreLinkUp with your LibreLinkUp account, you will start receiving readings from your FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor.

2.  Install the Gluroo Diabetes Logger app from the Play Store. To link your FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor as a CGM data source in Gluroo: Open the app and press the hamburger button in the top right corner. Select "CGM." Choose "Freestyle Libre via Link-Up." Enter your LibreLinkUp email and password. And press "Connect" to establish the connection.

3.  On your Wear OS watch: Navigate to the Google Play Store on your watch. Search for "Gluroo" and install the Gluroo app. Return to your watch's home screen, long-press on it to access customization options. Swipe to the right to add a new watch face. In the list of watch faces, choose “Big Info” or “Analog dashboard”. Tap "customize", then swipe to the left to select a “complication”. Select the complication you want to change and pick "BLG via Gluroo". After a short wait, you will see your FreeStyle Libre 3 glucose value and glucose trend on your watch. If you tap on the complication, you can see a full glucose graph.

#### ***\#3 Apple Watch***

Here are step-by-step instructions for syncing your FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor data with your Apple Watch:

1.  Begin by installing the LibreLinkUp app and registering as a follower within your FreeStyle Libre 3 app, following the same process as discussed for the Galaxy Watch. Ensure that your sensor glucose readings are visible within the LibreLinkUp app.

2.  Next, head to the Apple Store, install the Gluroo Diabetes Logger app. Link your FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor as a Continuous Glucose Monitoring data source, using the same steps as discussed for the Galaxy Watch.

3.  Once connected, you can go to the “Data tab”, tap “Other formats for apps”, locate the Nightguard link and copy it, using the copy button.

4.  Download the Nightguard app from the Apple Store. Inside the app, tap on "More," then select "Preferences." Input the Nightguard link obtained from Gluroo where it prompts you for a Nightscout URL. After this step, you should be able to view your FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor data in your Nightguard app.

5.  On your Apple Watch, navigate to a modular watch face and tap and hold on the watch face to access customization options. Choose one of the complications, and then select a Nightguard complication, either "BG Values as Gauge" or "BG Values as Text." These options are shown here in the bottom left and bottom middle complications. When you tap on the Nightguard complication, you can view your complete glucose curve. You can scroll through the curve using the dial. In the next tab, there's a refresh button that you may need to use occasionally.

It's worth noting that there might be a slight delay of about 5 minutes between the FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor readings and the values displayed on the watch. Nevertheless, this solution allows you to monitor your glucose trend in real-time on your Apple Watch.

It's important to note that the steps may vary depending on your specific phone and smartwatch models. There are multiple ways to achieve the desired outcome, and if you find abstract instructions challenging to follow, you can refer to online tutorials. Additionally, setting up the devices while having them on hand is often easier than trying to comprehend the process in an abstract form.

<span class="mark">Finally, note that the Libre by Abbott app available in the US does offer native Apple Watch integration.</span>
