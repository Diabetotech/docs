## **<u>1. Introduction (3 min)</u>**

Welcome t<span class="mark">o this module on the LinX CGM. In this video series, we’ll break down the essentials of this continuous glucose monitor, one of the smallest CGM sensors on the market designed for both children and adults.</span>

<span class="mark">The LinX sensor measures 22 mm in diameter and 4.2 mm in thickness and weighs only 2.2 grams. It is applied to the back of the upper arm or to the abdomen using an all-in-one applicator. After the sensor insertion a warm-up period of approximately one hour is required. Once the warm-up period is complete, the sensor monitors glucose levels in interstitial fluid and transmits real-time readings to the LinX smartphone app via Bluetooth every minute. The sensor lasts up to 15 days and is intended for people with diabetes aged 2 years and older.</span>

<span class="mark">The sensor housing can be round or droplet-shaped. The droplet- or tear-shaped version is marketed as LinX Vista. Branding varies by country: in some regions, the same sensor is sold under the distributor’s name. Examples include the DiaExpert sensor, a droplet-shaped sensor distributed by Mediq in the Netherlands and Germany, and BUZUD CGM, distributed by BUZUD in Singapore and India.</span>

<span class="mark">Our “Master Module on Glucose Sensors” covered CGM fu</span>ndamentals, including when to rely on sensor readings and how to manage issues such as disconnections and allergic reactions. We'll now focus on in-depth LinX CGM training and practical tips for getting optimal results with the device.

This module aims to support healthcare providers and individuals with diabetes in using the LinX CGM with competence and confidence. That said, these videos are for educational purposes only and do not replace medical advice. Please speak with your healthcare provider before changing any part of your treatment plan.
