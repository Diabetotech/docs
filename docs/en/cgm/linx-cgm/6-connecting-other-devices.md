## **<u>6. Connecting other devices</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your LinX CGM to other devices. In this video, you'll learn how to integrate your sensor with smartwatches and outline possibilities with smart insulin pens and insulin pumps. Let's begin.

### ***6.1 Smartwatches***

Your phone and a compatible smartwatch can receive data directly from the LinX sensor at the same time. This allows you to view your real-time glucose readings, trends, and alerts directly on your wrist. On Android Watch, the LinX watch app comes pre-installed. On Apple Watch, the LinX watch app is automatically installed on the paired Apple Watch when you install the LinX app on your iPhone. A reliable connection is required for pairing and data transmission. Make sure Bluetooth is turned on for both your phone and watch, and that both devices are connected to a Wi-Fi or mobile network.

Confirm that you are logged in to the LinX app on your phone, then open the LinX app on your watch. On Android Watch, you can either double-tap the LinX watch face to open the app or select “LinX” from the watch’s app list. A QR code will appear on the watch screen. To link the watch to your account, use the scanner in the LinX phone app. From the Home screen, you can access the scanner by going to “Personal Center”, then tapping “Compatible Watch”, “Android Watch”, and “Scan”.

On Apple Watch, the LinX app can be accessed from the app list. Follow the prompts in the mobile app to complete login on the watch. Once your watch is logged in and linked to your account, it will automatically show data from the active sensor.

To pair the LinX sensor with an Android Watch, tap “SENSOR SEARCHING” to display the list of nearby sensor serial numbers (SN). Select your device, verify the SN against the label on the sensor box, then tap “PAIR.” After pairing is successful and the warm-up period is complete, real-time glucose values will appear in the watch app. For Apple Watch, follow the instructions in the mobile app to complete pairing automatically.

### ***6.2 Smart insulin pens***

As of now, the LinX CGM does not connect directly with most commercially available smart insulin pens.

### ***6.3 Smart insulin pumps***

The LinX CGM is designed to integrate seamlessly with the Equil S Patch Insulin <span class="mark">Pump, with the long-term goal of enabling an automated insulin delivery system.</span>

<span class="mark">Currently, data from the LinX sensor and the Equil pump can be viewed together in the PanCares web platform; however, the AID algorithm is not yet commercially available.</span>

<span class="mark">In addition, the LinX CGM is mentioned to be compatible with AndroidAPS, an open-source AID system, via xDrip+ in companion mode.</span>

<span class="mark">Be aware that using open-source apps like AndroidAPS and xDrip+ carries inherent risks and is not supported by MicroTech Medical or approved by regulatory authorities. We provide this information transparently, though we don't recommend engaging in this practice without understanding the risks fully.</span>
