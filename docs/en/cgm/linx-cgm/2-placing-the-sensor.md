## **<u>2. Placing the sensor</u>**

Welcome to the first lesson of the LinX CGM module. In this video, we’ll show you how to apply and remove the LinX sensor and outline the critical dos and don’ts at each step. Let’s jump right in.

### ***2.1 Supplies***

Have the following ready:

- A LinX CGM pack, which contains an applicator with a built-in sensor and a quick guide; and

- an alcohol wipe.

### ***2.2 Applying the sensor*** 

<span class="mark">To use the sensor, install the LinX app on your smartphone. First, search for the term “LinX CGM” in the Google Play or the Apple Store. Download and open the app. Create an account using your email address. Then selec</span>t a password. The password must include an uppercase letter, a lowercase letter, numbers, and be in English with at least 8 characters. Afterward, press Send to receive the verification code. Check your email to retrieve the verification code, and then enter it into the app within 60 seconds. Now you can log into the ap<span class="mark">p. After logging in, the app will prompt you to select the preferred measuring unit, either in millimoles per liter or milligrams per deciliter. Now the app will check the required phone permissions to ensure it functions properly. Following this, enable Do Not Disturb Mode. The ap</span>p will highlight when blood glucose measurements are needed. Next, you will be guided in setting the lower and upper target ranges. You will see information about the app’s directional arrows, background colors, and recommendations for the optimal sensor insertion locations, which include the back of the upper arm <span class="mark">for adults or the abdomen on either side of the navel for children aged 2 to 17 years.</span>

After disinfecting the skin with alcohol, let it air dry without using a towel or tissue. Now place the sensor applicator directly on the skin without applying much pressure or stretching the skin at the application site. Press the white button on the sensor applicator. Wait for 10 seconds. Carefully lift the applicator off the skin. The insertion is now successfully completed.

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

<span class="mark">When the app indicates that it has expired or if the user experiences any irritation or discomfort at the site while wearing it, the sensor should be taken off the insertion site. To remove it, gently lift one edge of the adhesive that secures the sensor to the skin, the</span>n slowly peel it off in a single, steady motion.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

- You can only be signed in to your LinX account on a single mobile device at any given time.

- Ensure the skin is thoroughly clean and completely dry before insertion; otherwise, the sensor may not adhere properly.

- Do not use the sensor applicator if it appears damaged or if the safety seal is broken or missing.

- Avoid touching or holding the inside of the sensor applicator, as the introducer needle is exposed.

- Some bruising or bleeding may occur after insertion. If bleeding continues, remove the sensor and insert a new one at an alternative recommended site.

- When changing sensors, you do not need to remove or unpair the existing sensor connection.

In this video, you learned how to place and remove the LinX sensor properly. Following these steps helps maintain dependable glucose monitoring over time and supports reliable data to inform your treatment decisions.
