## **Anytime CT3 / AT4**

## **<u>1. Introduction</u>**

Welcome to this module on the Anytime CT3 and AT4 CGMs. In this series of videos, we'll take a closer look at the main features and functions of these split-design continuous glucose sensors that provide high-frequency glucose data.

The Anytime CT3 and AT4 sensors measure approximately 39 × 30 × 8 mm and weigh 6.5 grams. They are applied to the side or back of the upper arm or to the abdomen using an all-in-one applicator. After a 1-hour warm-up, the sensor sends real-time readings to the Yuwell Anytime app every three minutes. The disposable sensor is designed for up to 14 days of wear for the CT-3 and up to 15 days for the AT4. The rechargeable transmitter has a lifespan of up to two years. Both variants are intended for people with diabetes aged 18 years and older, although in some regions the CT3 is also approved for individuals aged 14 years and above.

A key distinction is that the AT4 (Anytime 4 Pro) includes a green safety lock rather than an orange one and supports optional calibration. In certain regions, alternative versions exist under different branding—for example, the Tracky CGM in India. This module focuses specifically on the Anytime CT3 and the comparable AT4 system.

We’ve already covered the fundamentals in the Master Module on Glucose Sensors, from interpreting sensor readings to troubleshooting common issues. This module builds on that knowledge with practical, device-specific training.

The objective is to support both healthcare professionals and people living with diabetes in using the Anytime CGMs effectively. This content is for educational purposes only and does not replace medical advice. Any therapy adjustments should be discussed with a qualified healthcare provider.

This module was created by Dr. Inge Van Boxelaer, the founder of Diabetotech and an endocrinologist at the St-Lucas Hospital in Ghent, in Belgium. The content was reviewed by Lukas Trcka, a person living with type 1 diabetes from the Czech Republic who blogged about his experience with the Anytime sensor. With their combined expertise, you can expect an objective and trustworthy assessment of the Anytime CT3 and AT4 CGMs.

This material was developed independently of Yuwell, the CGM manufacturer, allowing for an unbiased evaluation of the strengths and weaknesses of the Anytime CT3 and AT4.

By the end of this module, you’ll be equipped to use the Anytime CT3 and AT4 with confidence in daily life. You’ll learn how to apply and start the sensor, configure alarms, share glucose data, and connect the sensor with compatible smart devices. You’ll also benefit from expert tips to ensure safe and reliable use. If you’re a healthcare provider, you’ll enhance your ability to guide Anytime users effectively. If you’re using the sensor yourself, you’ll walk away with practical strategies you can apply from day one. Let’s get started — best wishes as you move forward.

## **<u>2. Placing the sensor</u>**

Welcome to the first lesson of the Anytime CT3 AT4 module. In this video, we’ll walk you through how to apply and remove the Anytime CT3 sensor, along with best practices and key precautions. The procedure is the same for the Anytime AT4. Let’s dive in.

### 

### ***2.1 Supplies***

Get the following ready:

- an Anytime CT3 sensor pack, which contains an auto-applicator with a built-in sensor;

- an Anytime CT3 transmitter pack consisting of a transmitter, charging base, charging cable, and user manual;

- and an alcohol wipe.

### ***2.2 Applying the sensor*** 

The first step is preparation. Place the transmitter in the charging stand. Observe the status of the charging indicator. A flashing indicator means charging is in progress. A constant blue light means the battery is fully charged. After charging is complete, remove the transmitter and prepare to wear it. Now, the transmitter activates Bluetooth mode.

Step 2 is to download the app and connect the transmitter. Scan the QR code on the product packaging with your phone. Download and install the Yuwell Anytime app. Log in, then enter your personal information. Turn on Bluetooth. Please do not turn off Bluetooth during the period of transmitter wear. Now, the app displays the transmitter number. Confirm that the number matches the transmitter being used. Click to confirm the connection. Scan the QR code on the sensor packaging box with the app, or manually enter the number below the QR code. Enter the sensor information. When the one-hour initialization countdown appears, it means the pairing has been successful. Please confirm that the transmitter is fully charged.

Step 3 is to select and clean the application site. To select the application site, keep away from scars, muscle tissue, and ulcerated skin areas. To wear on the arm, the user can choose the side or back of the upper arm where it is rich in fat. If the user is relatively thin or has well-developed arm muscles, they can choose to wear the transmitter on the abdomen. Avoid the ribs, the belt line, and the area within 5 centimeters around the navel. After determining the application site, disinfect the skin in this area with alcohol cotton pads. Doing so helps prevent dirt from affecting sensor adhesion. Apply the sensor once the alcohol has completely dried.

Step 4 is sensor placement. The sensor is a disposable, sterile consumable. After opening the sterile packaging of the sensor, placement should be completed immediately to avoid contamination from long-term exposure to the environment. Confirm that the packaging is intact and within its validity period before use. Open the sterile packaging and hold the orange area in the middle of the protective film. Remove the protective film from the sensor tape. While relaxed, attach the sensor to the cleaned skin. Press the insertion device so that the adhesive tape firmly adheres to the skin. Slide to open the safety lock of the insertion device, then press the gray buttons on both sides simultaneously. A “click” sound indicates that placement has been successfully completed. Remove the insertion device slowly.

### Step 5 is to install the transmitter, which we will demonstrate in the next lesson.

### 

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

After the wear period, the transmitter and sensor need to be removed together by slowly peeling off the sensor tape. The transmitter can be reused. After removing the sensor, the transmitter must be detached. Peel the buckle outward from the side gap, then slide the transmitter apart to separate the two. Place the transmitter into the charging stand for next time use.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

- Be aware that the safety lock and activation buttons are small and relatively stiff. To deploy the sensor, both grey buttons must be pressed simultaneously using the fingertips with firm, precise pressure. This may be difficult for users with reduced hand mobility.

- If the transmitter is not found during CGM pairing, first check your environment and device positioning.

  - If you are in a location such as an airport, subway, railway station, shopping mall, store, exhibition hall with electrical equipment, transformer substation, MRI room, or small enclosed space, move to a different area before continuing.

  - Make sure the transmitter is within 6 meters or 20 feet of your phone. For best results, both devices should be in the same room and positioned close to each other.

  - If the transmitter is still not detected, try the following troubleshooting steps.

> **Step 1: Check Bluetooth settings.**

- Confirm that Bluetooth is turned on.

- Go to Settings → Bluetooth → Device List on your smartphone.

- Even if the transmitter appears in the list, do not connect to it from the phone’s Bluetooth settings.

- Disconnect any other connected Bluetooth devices.

- Retry connecting the transmitter through the Anytime App.

> – If that doesn’t work, move to Step 2.
>
> **Step 2: Recharge the transmitter.**

- Place the transmitter on the charging base. Charge it until the blue indicator light turns on. Keep the transmitter on the charging stand while attempting initialization again.

> – If the transmitter is still not found, continue to Step 3.
>
> **Step 3: Try a different phone.**

- Try searching for the transmitter using another phone. Check the Bluetooth settings on the second phone to see if the transmitter can be found.

> – If the transmitter still cannot be found, proceed to Step 4.
>
> **Step 4: Restart the phone.**

- After restarting your phone, attempt the initialization process again.

> – If necessary, proceed to Step 5.
>
> **Step 5: Contact Yuwell Customer Service for after-sales support.**

- If you feel pain after the sensor is applied, take a moment to assess how strong the pain is and how you feel overall.

  - If the pain is mild, try to stay relaxed and continue using the CGM. Pay close attention to the sensor’s position and watch for any unusual sensations or signs that it may not be placed correctly. If you notice anything abnormal in how the sensor feels or sits on your skin, remove the CGM promptly.

  - If the pain is intense or becomes unbearable at any point, remove the CGM immediately. If you need to apply a new sensor, choose a different application site with a thicker fat layer, as this may help reduce discomfort.

  - If the problem continues or cannot be resolved, follow the after-sales support process and contact Yuwell Customer Service for further assistance.

In this video, you learned the correct procedure for applying and removing the Anytime CT3 and AT4 sensors. Each time you perform this process correctly, you reinforce accurate monitoring and empower confident therapy decisions.

## **<u>3. Starting the sensor</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on getting started with the Anytime CT3 and AT4 CGMs. In this video, we’ll show you how to get your Anytime sensor up and running and guide you through the Anytime app. We’ll also share practical tips to help you wear the sensor safely and correctly every day. Let’s jump right in.

### ***3.1 Starting the sensor and transmitter***

Tilt the transmitter into its designated slot on the sensor. Snap the transmitter into place until a “click” sound is heard. Rotate it in reverse to confirm that it is locked. If it does not fall off, this indicates that it is installed correctly. Transmitter application is complete. Please do not remove the transmitter during the wear period. In addition, avoid scratching or squeezing the device. After one hour, sensor initialization is complete. The Yuwell Anytime app begins displaying blood glucose values and performs blood glucose monitoring.

### ***3.2 The Yuwell Anytime app***

This is the home screen of the Yuwell Anytime app. It shows your most recent CGM reading along with the time, and the reading updates every three minutes. The screen also displays a glucose trend arrow, which indicates how your glucose levels are changing. Here’s what the different trend arrows mean.

Below the current glucose reading, you can see your Time in Range. The interface also includes a glucose trend graph that can be viewed over different time ranges. In addition, the graph area highlights the target range and shows a record of events.

Tap an event icon on the home screen to open the Event Log page. Then tap the plus icon in the lower right corner to add a new event. Your CGM allows you to log four types of events: insulin, medication, diet, and exercise. Enter events as they occur to ensure accurate tracking.

### ***3.3 Do's & don’ts***

- Attaching the transmitter requires a push-and-rotate movement and the slot on the CGM may not always be easily visible, which can make this step difficult for users with limited mobility. When applying the sensor, it is advisable to orient the slot toward the front side of the body so it remains visible and easier to access when mounting the transmitter. If the slot is oriented toward the back and the CGM is placed on the back of the upper arm, the user may need to rely on tactile feedback or use a mirror to correctly attach the transmitter.

- The Anytime CT3 and AT4 sensor and transmitter are water-resistant up to a depth of 1.5 meters or 5 feet for up to two hours. However, because adhesion strength varies from person to person, it is strongly recommended that you avoid prolonged exposure of the transmitter and sensor to water.

- Avoid vigorous physical activity, impacts, or bumps, as these may cause the sensor to partially or completely detach and result in inaccurate readings.

- If the adhesive tape loosens or the sensor falls off while you are wearing your sensor, first check whether the buckle is securely fastened. If it is not, remove the transmitter and reattach it to the sensor, then rotate the buckle in reverse to ensure it is properly secured. Next, inspect the adhesive tape to see if it has become loose. If the tape is loose, apply a strengthening sticker to help keep the sensor in place. You can also gently press the CGM for a short time and check whether data transmission resumes.

- If the message “Check sensor” appears on the app home page while you are wearing your CGM, remove the transmitter and inspect it along with the sensor base. Check for steam, sweat, water, or blood on either part. If you notice any moisture or residue, clean both the transmitter and the sensor base and make sure they are completely dry. Once dry, reattach the transmitter to the sensor and ensure the buckle is securely fastened by rotating it in reverse. Gently press the sensor for a short time, then wait about 30 minutes to see whether data transmission is restored.

- If the glucose values shown on your app seem inaccurate compared with your blood glucose meter, check how long you have been wearing the sensor. If you have been wearing it for 48 hours or less, be aware that the CGM is still in its adaptive phase. During this initial period, glucose readings may be unstable, but they usually become more accurate once the adaptation period is complete.

  - If you have been wearing the sensor for more than 48 hours, remove the transmitter and check both the transmitter and the sensor base for steam, sweat, water, or blood. If you find any moisture or residue, clean and thoroughly dry both parts. Then reattach the transmitter to the sensor, making sure the buckle is securely fastened by rotating it in reverse. Gently press the CGM and wait about 30 minutes to see whether the readings stabilize. If the glucose values return to a stable range, no further action is needed.

  - If the readings remain inaccurate despite a stable glucose trend, the sensor should be replaced. For the Anytime AT4, there is also the option to calibrate the sensor to determine whether this improves accuracy before changing it.

- Remove both the transmitter and sensor before undergoing an MRI examination.

In this video, we explored the start-up process for the Anytime CT3 and AT4 CGMs, from pairing the sensor to navigating the app. While occasional technical issues may occur, a structured troubleshooting approach and timely support can resolve most concerns. Your expertise and reassurance will help patients build confidence and make the most of their CGM experience.

## **<u>4. Setting alarms</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alarms with the Anytime CT3 and AT4 CGMs. In this video, we’ll examine the sensor’s alarm settings and demonstrate how to tailor them to your needs and preferences in the Anytime app. Let’s begin.

### ***4.1 Available alarms***

The Anytime CT3 and AT4 sensors provide several glucose-related alerts to help you monitor changes in your glucose levels. These alerts are delivered through audio, vibration, and message notifications.

Importantly, the default alert thresholds depend on the user profile selected during setup. The system adjusts its initial settings based on how you identify yourself in the app.

For example, if you select that you have diabetes, the default Low Alert is set at 70 milligrams per deciliter, or 3.9 millimoles per liter, and the default High Alert is set at 180 milligrams per deciliter, or 10 millimoles per liter.

If you select “not specified,” the default High Alert may be set lower — that is, at 140 milligrams per deciliter, or 7.8 millimoles per liter — while the Low Alert remains at 70 milligrams per deciliter, or 3.9 millimoles per liter.

For both the Low and High alerts, an optional snooze function is available. This allows you to temporarily silence the alert and receive a reminder after a selected interval, ranging from 3 minutes up to 3 hours, if the condition persists.

In addition to the High and Low alerts, there is an Urgent Low Glucose Alert, typically set at 56 milligrams per deciliter, or 3.1 millimoles per liter.

Besides these threshold alerts, optional rate-of-change alerts can be enabled. If glucose is rising or falling rapidly — defined as 3 milligrams per deciliter per minute, or 0.15 millimoles per liter per minute or more — the system generates a Rapid Rise or Rapid Fall alert. This allows users to respond not only to absolute glucose values, but also to the speed of change.

Optional predictive alerts are available as well. The CT3 can notify you in advance if your glucose is expected to reach the Low Alert threshold within the next 20 minutes, or the High Alert threshold within the next 30 minutes.

Finally, there is a Quiet Time option. When activated, Quiet Time temporarily silences all alerts and alarms until it is manually toggled off. This can be useful during meetings, sleep, or other situations where notifications need to be minimized.

### Aside from glucose alerts, the Anytime CT3 and AT4 CGMs also issue notifications related to the status of the sensor and transmitter. The system may send alerts and temporarily stop displaying glucose data if the sensor current falls outside the normal range. The sensor will also notify you if it is unable to establish an initial connection with the transmitter, particularly when the transmitter battery level is low.

### 

### ***4.2 Setting alarms on the Yuwell Anytime app***

To set High and Low alerts, open the menu by tapping the three-dash icon in the upper left corner of the home screen or by swiping from left to right. Select High/Low Target, scroll to choose your desired alert levels, and tap Save to confirm.

From the same menu, tap Reminder Setting to customize the glucose alerts you receive. You can tap an individual alert to adjust its threshold or turn it on or off. However, the Urgent Low Alert cannot be disabled and only its sound setting can be changed. In addition, you can enable or disable Bluetooth disconnection notifications and the Do Not Disturb setting.

***4.3 Tips***

## To receive alerts and notifications in a timely manner:

## Keep your phone with you at all times and ensure Bluetooth remains connected. 

## Make sure the app’s sound volume is set high enough to hear alerts.

- Close unnecessary apps while allowing the Anytime app to continue running in the background.

## Do not clear the app cache, as this may cause serious issues such as app restarts or data loss.

- If the CGM app is put to sleep by your phone’s system, battery management, or energy-optimization settings, this can lead to missed readings and connection errors. Ensure the CGM app is excluded from battery-saving restrictions. Guidance for different phone brands can be found at: [<u>https://dontkillmyapp.com/</u>](https://dontkillmyapp.com/).

## You’ve now seen how the Anytime CT3 and AT4 alarms work. By setting them correctly and staying attentive, you can respond quickly to glucose shifts and remain in control of your health.

## **<u>5. Sharing data</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on sharing data with the Anytime CT-3 and AT4 CGMs. In this video, you’ll learn how to share sensor data with loved ones and healthcare providers, as well as create glucose reports. Let’s get started.

### ***5.1 Sharing data with your loved ones***

<span class="mark">You can share your real-time glucose monitoring data with your healthcare provider, guardians, or family members through the Anytime Follow app.</span>

<span class="mark">To begin sharing, open the menu by tapping the icon in the upper-left corner of the home screen or by swiping from left to right, then tap “Share with others”. Tap Add to invite a follower, enter the follower’s email address, and include a note if needed. You can view the list of followers you have invited and check related notifications in the Followers and Notifications sections. Tap the arrow next to a follower’s name to open their settings, where you can edit notes or stop sharing your data as needed.</span>

<span class="mark">Your followers will open the invitation email on the smartphone they plan to use for following you, then download, install, and set up the Anytime Follow app, or log in to an existing account to access your monitoring information. Once logged in, your follower can add you as a sharer. To do this, they tap Add, enter your email address, optionally include a note, and tap Invite. You will then receive a sharing request notification in your Anytime app.</span>

<span class="mark">After the connection is established, your follower will see a list of sharers on the Anytime Follow home screen. This list displays each sharer along with their glucose readings. By tapping on a specific sharer, your follower can view detailed information.</span>

<span class="mark">In particular, your follower will enter the real-time follow view. This screen provides access to your current glucose readings, trend graphs, event logs, and daily views. In the daily view, your follower can review glucose data for a selected date, including diagrams and recorded events. From the real-time follow screen, your follower can also open the settings menu by tapping the icon in the upper-right corner. Here, they can add notes, customize notifications, or stop following you. If they choose to stop following, a notification will be sent to your Anytime app.</span>

<span class="mark">Your follower will receive glucose alerts based on your CT3 readings. Except for the Urgent Low Alert, the follower can adjust alert thresholds, switch alerts on or off, and set alert intervals according to their personal preference.</span>

### ***5.2 Sharing data with healthcare provider***

CGM data from the Anytime app can be shared with healthcare professionals in two ways.

First, through **Anytime View**. This software is intended for medical staff and can be linked to the Anytime app through an email-based account connection. Once connected, CGM data collected in the Anytime app is transmitted over the network to the Anytime View platform, where healthcare professionals can review and monitor the data.

Alternatively, data can be shared through the **Glooko** portal.\
To connect the Anytime CGM app to Glooko, open the Main Menu in the top left corner, select “Share with others,” choose Glooko, and tap Confirm. Enter your Glooko username and password, select Login, and tap Agree to authorize data sharing.

After the accounts are successfully connected, glucose data automatically syncs to your Glooko account approximately once per hour.

Please note that the Yuwell Anytime–Glooko integration may not be available in all countries.

### ***5.3 Creating reports***

You can access several report options from the Anytime app menu. For the Daily View report, tap the Menu icon in the upper-left corner of the home screen or swipe from left to right to open the menu, then select Daily Overview. This screen displays your CT3 glucose readings for the current day. You can also select a specific date to review corresponding CT3 data, including the glucose curve and key indicators.

To access more detailed summaries, open the menu and tap Data Report. Within the Data Report, you can review multiple sections, including the selected sensor session, device information, monitoring results, a blood glucose overview, a 24-hour overlay graph, a 24-hour trend graph showing daily glucose patterns, a graph highlighting daily high and low values, time-based percentages for high and low glucose levels, and the distribution of glucose readings. To share data reports or data lists, tap the Share icon in the upper-right corner of the screen.

You can also view and share the Ambulatory Glucose Profile (AGP) Report by selecting AGP Report from the menu. This section allows you to share the report as needed.

### This video covered the data-sharing capabilities of the Anytime CT3 and AT4 CGM systems. Keep in mind that collaboration with your care team is essential. Sharing your glucose data enables them to monitor patterns, intervene when needed, and support your ongoing progress. 

## **<u>6. Connecting other devices</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your Anytime CT3 and AT4 CGMs to other devices. In this video, we'll take a look at how these sensors work together with smartwatches, insulin pens, and insulin pumps. Let’s dive in.

### ***6.1 Smartwatches***

The Anytime CT3 and AT4 connect to an Apple Watch through the iPhone, rather than pairing directly with the sensor. After installing the Anytime app (version 1.1.26.0 or later) on an iPhone, you can enable the companion app on an Apple Watch running watchOS 9.6 or later. The watch then displays real-time glucose readings, trend arrows, and graph data that are transmitted from the sensor to the phone via Bluetooth.

In contrast, the CT3 and AT4 CGMs cannot be paired directly with Android smartwatches, as no compatible Wear OS app is available.

### 6.2 Smart insulin pens

At present, the Anytime CT3 and AT4 do not support direct, real-time pairing with smart insulin pens. Instead, data can be integrated indirectly through the Glooko cloud platform. When you link both your Yuwell account and your smart insulin pen account to Glooko, you can view your past insulin doses alongside your glucose trends in a single combined report for clinical review.
