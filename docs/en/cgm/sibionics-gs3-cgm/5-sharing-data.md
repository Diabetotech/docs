## **<u>5. Sharing data</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on sharing data with the Sibionics GS3 CGM. In this video, we’ll outline steps for sharing your sensor data with your healthcare provider and loved ones. We’ll also discuss generating reports with the GS3 system. Let’s get started.

### ***5.1 Sharing data with your healthcare provider*** 

<span class="mark">Tap the Profile icon on the app Home Screen. Under Connections, tap Health Care to invite a healthcare provider to view your sensor glucose readings and trend graphs. Tap Connect with New Clinic and enter the Practice ID of your clinic.</span>

<span class="mark">As a healthcare provider you can make a free account on SIBIONICS Proview via proview.sisensing.com/login. You can register as a healthcare provider to access the SIBIONICS ProView platform, and via the menu you can create your practice. When you create a practice, you will be able to set your Practice ID that you can later share with your patients.</span>

<span class="mark">Tapping the SIBIONICS ProView logo brings you to the homepage where you will be able to see your patients. Via the Patient tab you can add new patients. In your patient list, you can manage your view to see only starred patients, or patients with a specific glucose pattern. On the SIBIONICS ProView homepage you can tap a specific patient to view their data.</span>

### ***5.2 Sharing data with your family***

### At the time of creating this course, real-time sharing of GS3 data with family members is not yet available, but it will be released soon. Once enabled, you will be able to select a SIBIONICS Companion through the Health Care Connections tab and invite a follower who can monitor your data remotely.

### ***5.3 Creating reports***

Daily data shows the average of your sensor glucose readings, plus your Time in Range. Meanwhile, Daily Trending displays daily glucose readings as a graph, with event icons shown at the times they were recorded.

To choose the date for a report, tap Previous or Next at the top of the screen to move through daily data, or tap the date button to select a specific day to review.

AGP reports summarize data over multiple days and show the pattern and variability of sensor glucose levels over a typical day. The green band in the AGP represents the target glucose range. Note that the AGP requires at least 7 days of glucose readings to ensure statistical validity.

To export an AGP report, tap Report in the GS3 app. Set the time range to 7, 14, 30, or 90 days, or tap Custom to adjust the start and end dates manually. Then tap View Report to generate the report for the selected period. You can download or share the report using the icons in the top-right corner of the screen.

In the SIBIOINCS ProView Platform, it is possible to see 3 types of reports when you click on a patient from the homepage: AGP Report, Compare Report and a Daily Glucose Report. For each report, it is possible to change the date range. In the Compare Report, you can compare the AGP details with a previous period. In the Daily Report, you can hover over the glucose curve to see the specific glucose value, and also the specific record information like pictures of the food if they were taken at that time. Finally, it is always possible to download the reports from the download icon in the top right corner.

In this video, we explored the Sibionics GS3 CGM’s tools for sharing glucose data and generating reports. By utilizing these features, you and your care team can work together more effectively, enabling timely, data-guided decisions that support better health.
