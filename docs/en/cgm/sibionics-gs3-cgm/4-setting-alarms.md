## **<u>4. Setting alarms</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alarms with the Sibionics GS3 CGM. In this video, we’ll look at the different types of alarms the GS3 sensor can generate and show you how to configure them in the app. Let’s start.

### ***4.1 Available alarms***

With the GS3 CGM, alarms fall into two main categories: glucose level and trend alarms and system status alarms.

Among alarms for glucose levels, the Urgent Low Glucose Alarm lets you know when your glucose is at or below 56 mg/dl or 3.1 mmol/L. The Low Glucose Alarm warns you when your glucose is at or below the low limit you have set. Meanwhile, the High Glucose Alarm notifies you when your glucose is at or above your selected high limit.

The GS3 sensor also provides alarms for rapidly changing glucose levels. The Rising Fast Alarm is issued when your glucose is increasing faster than 2 mg/dl or 0.11 mmol/L per minute and is above your specified threshold. Meanwhile, the Falling Fast Alarm appears when your glucose is dropping faster than 2 mg/dl or 0.11 mmol/L per minute and is below your specified threshold.

For any of these glucose-related alarms, you can tap Later to acknowledge the alert, or log an event you believe may have contributed to the change.

Until you acknowledge the alarm or log an event, the alarm box will remain on your app screen. The sound and/or vibration will repeat when the system issues the next alarm. Remember that the system analyzes your glucose readings every five minutes.

Under system status, a Signal Loss Alarm indicates that your sensor has temporarily stopped communicating with the app and you are no longer receiving glucose readings or Low or High Glucose Alarms. Follow the on-screen instructions to restore the connection. If the issue persists, contact Customer Service at support@sibionics.com.

### ***4.2 Setting alarms on the app***

> To customize your Low and High Glucose Alarms, go to the app’s Profile screen, tap Alarm Settings, then select Low Glucose Alarm, High Glucose Alarm, or both. Here you can set the Alarm value and choose an alarm frequency between 5 minutes and 4 hours, then select Sound, Vibration, or Vibration & Sound as the alarm style. If you select Sound or Vibration & Sound, choose an alarm tone; the volume will follow your device settings. When you’re done, tap Confirm to save your settings.
>
> You can customize the Rising Fast and Falling Alarms too. Simply follow the on-screen prompts when these alarms appear.
>
> If you want to adjust the Signal Loss Alarm, tap Alarm Settings, then Signal Loss Alarm. Set how long the sensor can go without communicating with the app before an alert is triggered. Next, choose your alarm style, and tap confirm to save your settings.
>
> To restore the default alarm settings, tap Reset Alarm Settings.

### ***4.3 Tips***

# Turn Override Do Not Disturb on in the app for Urgent Low Glucose, Low Glucose, High Glucose, and Signal Loss alarms to ensure you receive audible alerts even when your device is on Do Not Disturb or has silent media volume.

# You must allow access to Do Not Disturb on your device to use the Override Do Not Disturb setting.

# For iOS: Allow Critical Alerts when prompted, or enable Critical Alerts from the app's notification settings.

# For Android: Allow Do Not Disturb access when prompted, or enable Do Not Disturb access for the app in your notification settings.

# The Urgent Low Glucose alarm will still trigger a vibration, even if your device is muted or Do Not Disturb is on.
