## **<u>3. Starting the sensor</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on starting the Sibionics GS3 CGM. In this video, we’ll dive into starting the sensor and navigating the GS3 app. We’ll also cover some tips for safe and proper use of the sensor in your daily life. Let’s jump right in.

### ***3.1 Starting the sensor***

To pair the sensor with the SIBIONICS GS3 app, enable Bluetooth on your phone. For NFC-enabled phones, you can tap the sensor for instant connection. If your phone doesn’t have NFC function, you can scan the QR code or enter the six-digit code from the GS3 kit.

After pairing the sensor with GS3 app, wait 60 minutes for sensor warm-up before glucose data appears. It is recommended to remain on the page for at least one minute to ensure the connection is properly established.

### ***3.2 The Sibionics GS3 app***

Here’s what the GS3 app Home screen looks like. On it, you’ll see the most recent glucose reading, the remaining sensor life, and the glucose trend graph.

Glucose data from the past 3 to 24 hours are shown as a trend line, with the most recent glucose reading displayed on the far right. Tap any point on the graph to highlight the corresponding glucose value. Switch between 3-hour, 6-hour, 12-hour, and 24-hour views of your glucose data by tapping the desired time range above the graph. View the graph in full screen by tapping the full-screen control icon above the graph. Logged events appear as icons at the time they were recorded. Tap any event icon to see detailed information about that event.

The trend arrows reflect both the speed and direction of glucose changes based on recent readings. They indicate whether your glucose is rapidly rising, slowly rising, steady, slowly falling, or rapidly falling.

The event record is designed to capture information that may influence glucose levels. Using the Event feature on the smartphone app, you can log and save events such as meals, exercise, medication, insulin delivery and corrections, blood-glucose meter readings, sleep, and mood.

To add an event, tap the Plus icon at the bottom of the Home screen. Select or enter the required information, then tap Save. You can tap the event icon on the glucose trend graph to view detailed information about that event. Alternatively, tap the icon and then tap Logbook to review all the events you have added.

<span class="mark">When you tap the avatar icon in the top left corner, the app’s Profile screen opens with the following options: Sensor, Logbook, Alarm Settings, Target Glucose Range, Reminders, Datahelper Setting, Glucose Bubble, Health Care, Help, Desktop Widget, and Contact Us.\
</span>

<span class="mark">Tapping the cogwheel icon in the top right corner gives you access to additional settings.</span>

### ***3.3 Do's & don’ts***

- Pair the sensor with your smartphone immediately after application. This helps prevent the built-in lithium battery from running out of power before the end of the 14-day wear period.

- Only one sensor can be paired at a time. Pairing a new sensor will automatically disconnect the existing one.

- Keep the sensor and smartphone within 10 meters (or 32.8 feet) of each other, with no obstacles such as walls or metal between them. Otherwise, communication may be affected.

- For the best user experience, keep Bluetooth turned on and allow the app to run in the background to avoid missing data. Do not force close the app.

- The sensor is designed to function normally within a temperature range of 5°C to 40°C. When the temperature is too high or too low, the CGM may display abnormal glucose readings. Normal operation will resume once the user returns to a suitable temperature environment.

- Taking painkillers, high-dose vitamin C, or hydroxyurea can result in falsely high CGM glucose readings. Consult your healthcare provider first if you plan to use any of these medications while wearing your sensor.

- The sensor must not be used with automated insulin dosing systems, including low-glucose-suspend systems.

- Remove the sensor before undergoing an MRI or CT scan.
