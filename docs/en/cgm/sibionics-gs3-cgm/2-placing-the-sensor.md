## **<u>2. Placing the sensor (3 min)</u>**

Welcome to the first lesson of the Sibionics GS3 CGM module. This video will show you how to apply and remove the GS3 sensor and point out essential dos and don’ts. Let’s dive in.

### ***2.1 Supplies***

To get started, have the following ready:

- A GS3 sensor kit containing a sensor applicator and a product insert; and

- an alcohol wipe, which may or may not be included in the box depending on regulatory approvals in your country.

### ***2.2 Applying the sensor*** 

Scan the QR code on the product insert to download the SIBIONICS GS3 app, or download the app from your app store. Register an account with your email address or log in with your existing SIBIONICS account. Then follow the in-app instructions to set up your profile and preferences.

Wash your hands and get prepared. Choose a soft and visible area on the upper third part of the back of your upper arm. Then clean the site with an alcohol wipe and allow it to air dry completely for secure sensor application. Twist off the cap from the sensor applicator, and set the cap aside. Rest the sensor applicator over the prepared site, and then push the button down to apply the sensor to your body. After hearing the click sound, move the sensor applicator away. Secure the sensor by rubbing the adhesive edges. The sensor is now applied.

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

Your sensor automatically stops working after 14 days and must be replaced. To remove it, rub the adhesive edges with an alcohol wipe. Gently peel off the sensor and dispose of it properly. Then apply a new sensor as needed.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

- Use a smartphone running Android 8.1 or later, or iOS 13.0 or later to ensure compatibility with the SIBIONICS GS3 app.

- Do not use the sensor applicator if:

  - the twist-to-open label has been tampered with,

  - it seems to be damaged or already opened, or

  - it is past the expiration date.

- Push down on the sensor applicator only when it is placed over the prepared site to prevent unintended results or injury.

- Placement is correct as long as the sensor is applied in a vertical position. The round hole can point either upward or downward without affecting accuracy.

- Applying the sensor may cause bruising or bleeding. In case of persistent bleeding, remove the sensor and contact your healthcare provider.

In this video, you learned the steps for proper placement and removal of the Sibionics GS3 CGM. By following these best practices, you promote reliable long-term monitoring, which provides accurate data to guide your treatment decisions.
