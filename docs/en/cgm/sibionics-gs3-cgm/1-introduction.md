## **<u>1. Introduction</u>**

Welcome to this module on the Sibionics GS3 CGM. In this series of videos, we’ll unpack the essentials of using the world’s thinnest sensor.

Measuring 24.8 by 7.8 by 2.9 mm and weighing 1.5 g, the Sibionics GS3 sensor is placed on the back of your upper arm using an all-in-one applicator. After a one-hour warm-up, it measures glucose levels in your interstitial fluid and sends readings to the Sibionics GS3 app on your smartphone via Bluetooth every five minutes. The sensor lasts for up to 14 days. It is intended for people with diabetes aged 3 and above.

Our "Master Module on Glucose Sensors" introduced the key principles of CGM use, including when to trust sensor readings and how to manage issues such as disconnections and allergic reactions. We now move forward with core training on the Sibionics GS3 sensor and practical tips for achieving the best results with the device in daily life.

Our goal in this module is to support both healthcare providers and individuals with diabetes in maximizing the benefits of the Sibionics GS3. That said, please note that these videos do not constitute medical advice. Always speak with your healthcare provider before modifying your therapy in any way.

These videos were produced by Dr. Inge Van Boxelaer, the founder of Diabetotech and an endocrinologist at the St-Lucas Hospital in Ghent, Belgium. The content was reviewed by Dr Daniela Petrache, a diabetologist in Romania with hands-on clinical experience using the Sibionics GS3, including published reflections on its real-world use. Together, their complementary perspectives ensure a balanced, clinically relevant assessment of the Sibionics GS3 CGM system.

Rest assured that this material is produced independently of Sibionics, the sensor manufacturer, enabling a neutral and balanced review of the GS3 sensor’s benefits and drawbacks.

By the end of this module, you will have a solid, practical understanding of the Sibionics GS3 CGM. You will learn how to place and start the sensor, configure alarms, share your glucose data, and link the sensor with other devices. You’ll also receive advanced tips for safe and effective use of the device. For healthcare providers, this means confidence in supporting GS3 users; for sensor users, it translates into clear, practical guidance for everyday use of the CGM. Together, let's begin this meaningful journey. Good luck!
