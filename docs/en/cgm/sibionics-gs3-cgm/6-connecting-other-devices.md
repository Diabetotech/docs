## **<u>6. Connecting other devices</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your Sibionics GS3 CGM to other devices. In this video, you’ll learn how to link your sensor with smartwatches and discover possibilities with smart insulin pens and pumps. Let’s begin.

### ***6.1 Smartwatches***

SIBIONICS does not yet offer a dedicated smartwatch app. A native Apple Watch app and an Android Watch app with direct-to-watch capability are in development.

There is a dedicated SiWatch, but it appears to work only with the GS1 sensor.

On Android, you can use third-party apps to view GS3 data on a smartwatch. Juggluco can connect to the native Sibionics app. xDrip+ can connect via a patched Sibionics app, and xDrip+ Companion mode also appears to work with the native app. Both Juggluco and xDrip+ can display glucose values on Wear OS and Garmin watches.

For Apple Watch users, there is currently no working solution.

### ***6.2 Smart insulin pens***

Currently, the Sibionics GS3 CGM does not connect directly to most commercially available smart insulin pens.

### ***6.3 Smart insulin pumps***

At present, the GS3 sensor is not compatible with any commercial AID systems. However, Sibionics is actively working toward integrating its sensor technology with insulin delivery. In particular, Sibionics has entered into a strategic partnership with PharmaSens to jointly develop an all-in-one insulin patch pump system that combines glucose monitoring and insulin delivery in a single device.

Separately, the GS3 can be linked to Juggluco or xDrip+, both of which can interface with AndroidAPS, a known open-source AID system. However, the GS3 is not considered a trusted glucose source for automated insulin delivery. It is not approved for use in any commercial AID system, and publicly available accuracy data remain very limited.

It's important to note that using open-source apps is not supported by Sibionics, nor is it approved by the FDA or European authorities. Engaging in this practice is entirely at your own risk. While we don't recommend it, we believe in being transparent about all available options.

## In this video, we described how the Sibionics GS3 app links with smartwatches, insulin pens, and insulin pumps. These connections could create a smooth, coordinated system that supports smarter and more personalized diabetes care.
