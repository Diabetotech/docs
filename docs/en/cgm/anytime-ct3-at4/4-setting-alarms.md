## **<u>4. Setting alarms</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alarms with the Anytime CT3 and AT4 CGMs. In this video, we’ll examine the sensor’s alarm settings and demonstrate how to tailor them to your needs and preferences in the Anytime app. Let’s begin.

### ***4.1 Available alarms***

The Anytime CT3 and AT4 sensors provide several glucose-related alerts to help you monitor changes in your glucose levels. These alerts are delivered through audio, vibration, and message notifications.

Importantly, the default alert thresholds depend on the user profile selected during setup. The system adjusts its initial settings based on how you identify yourself in the app.

For example, if you select that you have diabetes, the default Low Alert is set at 70 milligrams per deciliter, or 3.9 millimoles per liter, and the default High Alert is set at 180 milligrams per deciliter, or 10 millimoles per liter.

If you select “not specified,” the default High Alert may be set lower — that is, at 140 milligrams per deciliter, or 7.8 millimoles per liter — while the Low Alert remains at 70 milligrams per deciliter, or 3.9 millimoles per liter.

For both the Low and High alerts, an optional snooze function is available. This allows you to temporarily silence the alert and receive a reminder after a selected interval, ranging from 3 minutes up to 3 hours, if the condition persists.

In addition to the High and Low alerts, there is an Urgent Low Glucose Alert, typically set at 56 milligrams per deciliter, or 3.1 millimoles per liter.

Besides these threshold alerts, optional rate-of-change alerts can be enabled. If glucose is rising or falling rapidly — defined as 3 milligrams per deciliter per minute, or 0.15 millimoles per liter per minute or more — the system generates a Rapid Rise or Rapid Fall alert. This allows users to respond not only to absolute glucose values, but also to the speed of change.

Optional predictive alerts are available as well. The CT3 can notify you in advance if your glucose is expected to reach the Low Alert threshold within the next 20 minutes, or the High Alert threshold within the next 30 minutes.

Finally, there is a Quiet Time option. When activated, Quiet Time temporarily silences all alerts and alarms until it is manually toggled off. This can be useful during meetings, sleep, or other situations where notifications need to be minimized.

### Aside from glucose alerts, the Anytime CT3 and AT4 CGMs also issue notifications related to the status of the sensor and transmitter. The system may send alerts and temporarily stop displaying glucose data if the sensor current falls outside the normal range. The sensor will also notify you if it is unable to establish an initial connection with the transmitter, particularly when the transmitter battery level is low.

### ***4.2 Setting alarms on the Yuwell Anytime app***

To set High and Low alerts, open the menu by tapping the three-dash icon in the upper left corner of the home screen or by swiping from left to right. Select High/Low Target, scroll to choose your desired alert levels, and tap Save to confirm.

From the same menu, tap Reminder Setting to customize the glucose alerts you receive. You can tap an individual alert to adjust its threshold or turn it on or off. However, the Urgent Low Alert cannot be disabled and only its sound setting can be changed. In addition, you can enable or disable Bluetooth disconnection notifications and the Do Not Disturb setting.

***4.3 Tips***

## To receive alerts and notifications in a timely manner:

## Keep your phone with you at all times and ensure Bluetooth remains connected. 

## Make sure the app’s sound volume is set high enough to hear alerts.

- Close unnecessary apps while allowing the Anytime app to continue running in the background.

## Do not clear the app cache, as this may cause serious issues such as app restarts or data loss.

- If the CGM app is put to sleep by your phone’s system, battery management, or energy-optimization settings, this can lead to missed readings and connection errors. Ensure the CGM app is excluded from battery-saving restrictions. Guidance for different phone brands can be found at: [<u>https://dontkillmyapp.com/</u>](https://dontkillmyapp.com/).

## You’ve now seen how the Anytime CT3 and AT4 alarms work. By setting them correctly and staying attentive, you can respond quickly to glucose shifts and remain in control of your health.
