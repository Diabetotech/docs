Welcome to this video on the Dexcom G6, a continuous glucose monitor that can greatly improve your diabetes management. In this chapter, we will explore the key features of the Dexcom G6 and how to maximize its benefits. Let's dive in!

The Dexcom G6 is primarily used in conjunction with an insulin pump and offers continuous glucose monitoring for up to 10 days. The transmitter, on the other hand, can be reused for a period of up to 3 months. The Dexcom G6 sensor measures your glycemia every 5 minutes. You can conveniently access your sensor data through your compatible mobile phone or, in some cases, via your insulin pump. ## **<u>2. Placing the sensor</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on placing the Dexcom G6 sensor.

In this video, we will focus on correctly placing and removing the Dexcom G6 sensor to ensure accurate readings and comfortable wear. Let's walk through the process step by step.

### ***2.1 Supplies***

To successfully place the sensor, gather the following supplies: a sensor with sensor applicator, a transmitter, and alcohol wipes for proper hygiene.

### ***2.2 Inserting the sensor***

Follow these steps to insert the sensor effectively:

- Start by washing your hands and disinfecting the area of skin where the sensor will be placed.

- Remove the protective film from the Dexcom G6 applicator, taking care not to touch the adhesive patch.

- Position the applicator on the designated area of the skin.

- Fold and break off the orange safety clip.Press the orange button to insert the sensor and then remove the applicator. This ensures the sensor is securely inserted and can remain in place for 10 days. However, remember that you still need to insert the transmitter.

- Disinfect the transmitter connection using an alcohol wipe and let it dry.

- Insert the transmitter, aligning the narrow side first into the sensor and snapping it into place. Two clicks will confirm that the transmitter was inserted correctly.

- Gently rub the area around the sensor patch three times to ensure the adhesive patch sticks firmly. Now, your transmitter is also securely in place. Remember, this transmitter can be reused for the next 3 months.

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

When it's time to remove the sensor after the 10-day wear period, perform the following steps for a safe removal. Remove the patch from your skin. Break off the sensor tab on the wide round side of the sensor holder. Slide the transmitter out of the sensor holder. Keep the transmitter for reuse in your next sensor.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

Here are a few key tips to keep in mind:

- When placing the sensor, it is recommended not to press the inserter too firmly in order to avoid bleeding. Bleeding is not automatically a reason to remove the sensor. Clean the sensor holder if there is blood before inserting the transmitter. If the bleeding persists or the insertion site is uncomfortable, remove the sensor.

- After placing the sensor, the transmitter can be clicked into the sensor holder. Caution! First click in the transmitter tab on the narrow side and then press down the round side.

- Keep the sensor and transmitter code as it will be required for future sensor start-ups. Consider taking a picture of it to avoid misplacing or losing the code.

- Remove old transmitter numbers from your phone's Bluetooth settings. Failure to do so may result in the device continuously searching for the old transmitter.

- If you encounter any technical difficulties, please reach out to your local Dexcom Technical Support for assistance. While Dexcom absolutely does not support this, some people attempt to resolve the issue by disconnecting the sensor and transmitter and repeating the setup process. To remove the transmitter from the holder, tools like a bank card, test strip, or hair clip are sometimes utilized. Instructional videos demonstrating these techniques can be found on YouTube, although these methods are not endorsed by Dexcom.

By following these guidelines and being resilient in the face of challenges, you can confidently place and remove your Dexcom G6 sensor, optimizing its performance for accurate and reliable glucose monitoring. Even if the start-up process doesn't go smoothly, remind yourself that you have options to troubleshoot and get the sensor and transmitter properly connected.

## **<u>3. Starting the sensor</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on starting the Dexcom G6 sensor.

In this video, we will cover the process of starting the Dexcom G6 sensor and scanning it using the Dexcom G6 app. Let's delve into the details.

<span class="mark">For some automated insulin delivery systems, starting the Dexcom G6 sensor is seamlessly integrated into the automated insulin delivery system's software, eliminating the need for the Dexcom G6 app.</span>

Although there is a Dexcom G6 receiver available, we won't focus on it extensively since it is not widely used.

### ***3.1 Dexcom G6 app***

The Dexcom G6 app can be downloaded on most mobile phones, with separate versions available for Android and iPhone devices. You can find a list of all compatible devices and software versions on this website: dexcom.com/compatibility

The Dexcom G6 app has minimal impact on battery life during regular use. If your phone's battery dies, your data will not be lost. Additionally, you can view your sensor data while your phone is charging.

To receive sensor data on your Dexcom G6 app, follow these steps:

- When starting the Dexcom G6 app for the first time, you will be prompted to enter the serial number of your transmitter and establish a connection.

- Enter the sensor code provided with your sensor.

- Insert the sensor and attach the transmitter. Wait for the transmitter to pair with the app.

- Tap "Start New Sensor" to initiate the sensor.

- Please note that the sensor requires a 2-hour warm-up period before your glucose data becomes available. Keep the receiver within 6 meters of the transmitter for optimal data reception.

Once the sensor has completed the warm-up period, the initial screen will display the following information:

- In the middle, your sensor's current glucose reading and the associated trend arrow. If your glucose falls below 40 mg/dL (2.2 mmol/L), the reading will display as "LOW." Conversely, if your glucose exceeds 400 mg/dL (22.2 mmol/L), it will show as "HIGH." In such cases, perform a finger prick test to verify your glucose level and take appropriate actions for hypoglycemia or hyperglycemia.

- Below the glucose reading, you can view your glucose graph.

- Finally, you can access the settings located at either the bottom or top of the homescreen, depending on whether you're using an iPhone or an Android phone.

- You can also go to the Share icon to set up Dexcom Share & Follow.

The events section allows you to record additional information, such as insulin dosages.

By rotating your mobile phone to landscape mode, you can view your glucose readings of the last 24 hours along with any insulin dosages you have entered.

### ***3.2 Do's & don’ts***

Here are a few important considerations:

- The Dexcom G6 sensor is water-resistant up to 2.4 meters) for up to 24 hours. You can shower and swim while wearing your Dexcom G6 sensor.

- The time and date on the Dexcom G6 app automatically synchronize with your mobile phone's settings.

<!-- -->

- If there is a temporary loss of connection to the transmitter, your glucose graph may display a gap in the data. Once the connection is re-established, the gap will be filled with readings from the last 3 hours.

- You may encounter issues when a new sensor has the same code as the previous one. In such cases, the new sensor may not be accepted or recognized by the system. To prevent this, you can try not to use sensors with the same code consecutively. Instead, select a different sensor from the box to ensure proper acceptance and recognition by the system.

By adhering to these guidelines and maintaining resilience throughout your glucose monitoring journey, you can leverage the Dexcom G6 app to access accurate and valuable data for effective diabetes management.

## **<u>4. Setting alarms</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alarms with the Dexcom G6 sensor.

Throughout this session, we'll walk you through the available alarm options and demonstrate how to set them on the Dexcom G6 app on your phone. Let's get started!

### ***4.1 Available alarms***

The Dexcom G6 system provides an extensive range of alarm options to keep you informed about specific glucose levels and sensor-related issues. You have the ability to set alarms for Low glucose, High glucose, signal loss (when values are not visible due to Bluetooth problems), and no readings (when the sensor cannot detect any values). Additionally, there are two crucial alerts: the Urgent Low alert and the Urgent Low Soon alert.

The Urgent Low alert is automatically triggered when your glucose level reaches 55 mg/dL (3.1 mmol/L) or lower. It's important to note that this alert is mandatory and cannot be turned off for your safety.

The Urgent Low Soon Alert is designed to notify you when your glucose is rapidly decreasing and is projected to reach 55 mg/dL or 3.1 mmol/L within the next 20 minutes. This early warning provides you with ample time to take necessary actions and prevent your glucose levels from dropping dangerously low.

Moreover, you can utilize the snooze function, which enables reminders for consistently high or low glucose levels. Additionally, the system offers flexibility in setting an alert schedule, allowing you to customize alerts based on specific time blocks that suit your individual needs.

You will receive alarms on the Dexcom G6 app as long as your phone and sensor are within 6 meters (20 feet) of each other without obstructions.

### ***4.2 Setting alarms on the Dexcom G6 app***

Using the Dexcom G6 app, you can configure alarms via the settings menu.

You can customize the alarm experience by choosing different vibrations and melodies. The alarm volume corresponds to the sound volume settings on your mobile phone.

By utilizing these alarm settings effectively and maintaining resilience, you can stay informed and proactive in managing your glucose levels with the Dexcom G6. When managing alarms, stay vigilant and ensure you're using the settings that best suit your needs. This proactive approach empowers you to respond effectively to changing glucose levels.

## **<u>5. Data sharing</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on sharing data with the Dexcom G6 sensor.

In this video, we will discuss how to share your data with healthcare providers, create reports, and share data with your loved ones who play a significant role in supporting your diabetes management.

### ***5.1 Sharing data with your healthcare provider***

<span class="mark">Your healthcare provider can access your data through Clarity, Dexcom's reading software. There are separate versions of Clarity available for patients and healthcare professionals.</span> <span class="mark">Both versions are free, and you can create your account at clarity.dexcom.eu at any time.</span> <span class="mark">Use the same log-in details for your Dexcom G6 app as for Dexcom Clarity. You can also download the Dexcom Clarity app and log in with your Dexcom account. Looking at your glucose trends regularly can help you to fine tune your insulin setting to achieve an even better glucose control.</span>

<span class="mark">In Clarity for professionals, you can add a patient by entering their name and date of birth. Once added, you can send an email invitation to the patient from Clarity, inviting them to share their data. The patient needs to log into their personal Clarity account via the Clarity app or via the Clarity website and enter the provided code via the settings or via clarity.dexcom.eu/share.</span>

### ***5.2 Creating reports***

Once the glucose sensor data is uploaded, Clarity offers eight to nine different reports, which are found in the left side bar.

- The OVERVIEW report provides key glucose metrics from the date range you select within the last 90 days. Patterns and the best glucose day are summarized here, which you click for more detail. There's also information about the Dexcom CGM system in use and the alerts and alarms the patient has set. Talk with your patients if they need to adjust their alerts to help them stay in range.

- PATTERNS provides detailed graphical views and statistics regarding the patterns found, and offers some possible considerations for improved glucose control. Day and night highs and lows are potential patterns that could be found within the date range selected. Help patients stay positive with best day which shows them when they were most within their target range for the selected date range.

- TRENDS provides a visual representation of where patients spend the majority of their time, but eliminates outliers. The black line indicates the average.

- OVERLAY features all CGM data points to help visualize patterns and individual events. Hover over each day to view just that day.

- The DAILY report shows every glucose value and patient-entered events for each day. Highs and lows outside the customized target range are highlighted here, and in the trends report.

- Clarity's COMPARE report is useful to analyze how well diabetes is managed. Compare different days, weeks or even months to see the impact of a medication adjustment, or simply to see how a patient has progressed.

- STATISTICS provides detailed metrics displayed by time and day of the week. The Ambulatory Glucose Profile, AGP, is a standardized glucose report where you can see your Time In Range, a trends graph and daily profiles.

- PATIENT GLUCOSE RANGE is only available in Clarity for Clinics. Use this page to customize the patient target range. Changes you make on this page only apply to this Clarity Clinic view and do not change any settings on the patient's Dexcom CGM system or the patient's personal Clarity account. All the interactive reports are available to print, download or email for any date range.

Our general module on glucose sensors provides additional guidance on interpreting these reports effectively.

Sharing data with healthcare providers may seem daunting at first. Take it step by step, creating your Clarity account and exploring the options available. Remember that sharing data empowers your healthcare team to provide you with better care, making it worth the effort.

### ***5.3 Sharing data with family***

Using the Dexcom G6 app, you can share your data with up to 10 caregivers through the Dexcom Follow app.

The process involves these steps:

- The user of the Dexcom G6 app enables the Dexcom Share feature in their app and invites a caregiver.

- The caregiver needs to accept the invitation, install the Dexcom Follow app, and complete the setup process.

This video demonstrates how to enable the Dexcom Share feature and walks through the necessary screens to invite a caregiver. When creating the invitation, you can define specific settings for the caregiver, including data visibility and alert preferences. When the invitation is sent, the caregiver will receive an email with two options: to download the Dexcom Follow app or start following immediately if they already have the app. Upon accepting, they will see informational screens in the app, which they can accept. The caregiver can modify the suggested settings without affecting the patient's personal settings. However, the patient retains control over whether the caregiver can view glucose readings. This choice remains with the patient.

Sharing data with your healthcare provider and loved ones is a collaborative step towards better diabetes management. Remember, it takes a supportive network to navigate this journey successfully.

## **<u>6. Connecting other devices</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your Dexcom G6 sensor to other devices.

In this chapter, we explore the exciting possibilities of connecting your Dexcom G6 sensor with various devices, such as smartwatches, smart pens, and insulin pumps.

### ***6.1 Smart watch***

Dexcom provides extensive support for smartwatch integrations with the Dexcom G6 sensor, and we'll explore options with popular smartwatches such as Garmin, Samsung Galaxy, and Apple Watch.

To begin, you must activate your Dexcom Share account, which can be easily done by adding a follower in your Dexcom G6 app, even if you choose yourself as the follower.

Let's look at the setup process for each smartwatch.

**1) Connecting the Dexcom G6 app to your Garmin smartwatch.**

- Install the Garmin Connect app and Garmin Connect IQ app on your Android or iPhone.

- Download the Dex CGM Watch Face from the Connect IQ store on your phone.

- Enter your Dexcom username and password in the settings of the Dex CGM Watch Face.

- If the Dex CGM Watch Face doesn't appear automatically on your Garmin watch, you can manually select it from the available watch faces.

- Alternatively, you can use the Dexcom Widget or Dexcom Data Field from the Connect IQ store.

- If you have a Garmin Edge cycling computer, you can also display Dexcom <span class="mark">data using the Dexcom Data Field feature.</span>

- <span class="mark">Garmin recently introduced a new feature allowing you to place dexcom readings on the main screen of the Garmin smartwatch. You can find more information about this feature on Garmins website.</span>

**2) Connecting the Dexcom G6 app to your Samsung Galaxy smartwatch.**

- Install the Galaxy Wearable app on your Android phone.

- Download the Blose app on your smartwatch and enter your Dexcom credentials directly.

- Find and install the Blose watch face from the available options.

<span class="mark">Alternatively, you can explore the following options for Samsung Galaxy Smartwatch integration:</span>

- G-Watch Wear app: Install the G-Watch Wear app on both your phone and smartwatch, and enter your Dexcom credentials via settings.

- Wearable Widgets app: Combine the Wearable Widgets app with the G-Watch Wear app to display Dexcom data as a complication or a tile on your Samsung Galaxy smartwatch.

- Dexcom G6 app via Wear Installer (2) app: If you prefer a typical Dexcom watch face on your smartwatch, you can install the Dexcom G6 app on your phone using the Wear Installer (2) app. Additional steps and customization are involved in this method.

**3) Connecting the Dexcom G6 app to your Apple Watch**

- Open the Watch app on your iPhone, find the Dexcom G6 app, and install it on your Apple Watch.

- Once installed, access the Dexcom app on your watch from the app icons, displaying your Dexcom sensor graph.

- You can also integrate Dexcom sensor data into a complication on a modular watch face. Keep in mind that Apple Watch complication<span class="mark">s might only</span> update information every 15 to 45 minutes, and tapping the complication will show a larger Dexcom sensor graph with the most recent blood glucose reading.

- Alternatively, download the Sugarmate app from the App Store to display sensor data as a calendar appointment. Follow the app instructions, connecting Sugarmate to your Dexcom account and creating a new calendar in your iCloud account.

- On some modular watch faces, you can change the middle complication to "my calendar" to view real-time Dexcom data.

Please note that the setup steps may vary based on the type of phone and smartwatch you use. To simplify the process, a downloadable PDF with tutorial links is provided below this video. It's generally easier to follow the setup with the actual devices in front of you rather than trying to understand abstract instructions.

### ***6.2 Smartpens***

Currently, the Dexcom G6 app does not have direct compatibility with any smartpens. Howev<span class="mark">er, there is a workaround available. You can install the Glooko app and link it to your Clarity account. Additionally, in the Glooko app, you can connect your smart insulin pen, for example a Novopen 6 or Echo Plus pen, or a Tempo Smart or SoloSmart Button.</span>

<span class="mark">By logging into Glooko, you'll have access to integrated data from both Dexcom and Novopen, providing a convenient solution for monitoring your glucose levels and insulin data in one place.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">6.3 Smart insulin pumps</span>***

<span class="mark">Dexcom G6 is widely used in automated insulin delivery systems, including Tandem Control IQ, Omnipod 5, mylife Loop system, Diabeloop, and the iLet Bionic pancreas.</span>

<span class="mark">Moreover, Dexcom G6 is the preferred sensor for open-source automated insulin delivery systems. While DIY Loop, iAPS and Trio users don't need additional tools, AndroidAPS users require a third-party app like xDrip+ to transmit Dexcom G6 data to the AndroidAPS app.</span>

It's important to note that using open-source apps is not supported by Dexcom, nor is it approved by the FDA or European authorities. Engaging in this practice is entirely at your own risk. While we don't recommend it, we believe in being transparent about all available options.

Embrace the possibilities of connecting your Dexcom G6 sensor to other devices, as they allow you to personalize your Dexcom G6 experience and optimize your diabetes management strategy.
