## **<u>3. Starting the sensor</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on starting and using the Dexcom ONE Plus sensor.

As soon as you apply the Dexcom ONE Plus sensor, it automatically activates, and the 30-minute warm-up <span class="mark">period will start. You may apply a new sensor while an older sensor is working and activate the new one whenever you choose. Keep in mind the 10 day wear period begins once the sensor exits the applicator.</span>

To access the data from your Dexcom ONE Plus sensor, you will need to make a connection between the sensor and either your smartphone or a Dexcom ONE Plus receiver, using the provided pairing code. You can simultaneously use both the Dexcom ONE Plus app and receiver.

To ensure your phone or receiver receives the sensor data, they should always be within a 6-meter or 20-foot range of the sensor.

<span class="mark">In this video, we will guide you through the steps to connect your Dexcom ONE Plus sensor to your Dexcom ONE Plus receiver and Dexcom ONE Plus app and how to use the Dexcom ONE Plus receiver and app.</span>

### ***3.1 Dexcom ONE Plus receiver***

The Dexcom ONE Plus receiver has a coloured screen and four navigation buttons on the front: Select, Scroll down, Go back and Scroll up.

At the bottom, you will find a microUSB port for charging and data transfer. It is recommended to charge the receiver using the provided cable. Full charge can take up to 3 hours, but the <span class="mark">battery can last for</span> up to 7 days of use on a single battery charge<span class="mark">.</span>

<span class="mark">To turn on your receiver, press and hold the select button for three seconds. The receiver walks you through the entire setup process step by step. Select next to continue. First you will accept the Dexcom terms of use and privacy policy, and set your date and time. The receiver will then display a series of educational screens so you can learn everything you need to know about your Dexcom ONE Plus. This mini tutorial will teach you all about safety, treatment decisions and trend arrows. Next, you will be given the option to turn alerts on or off.</span>

<span class="mark">After learning all about your system and choosing your alert settings, it is time to insert your sensor. You can now insert your sensor. On the receiver, you can select that the sensor is inserted. You will then enter the pairing code. You will find the four digit pairing code on the applicator you used to insert the sensor. Wait for your sensor to pair. Once pairing is complete, select finish and you will enter into your 30 minute sensor warm-up. The sensor warm-up timer lets you know when you'll start getting readings and alerts. If your sensor has been inserted for over 30 minutes, you'll immediately start receiving readings since the warm-up period initiates upon sensor insertion.</span>

<span class="mark">After your warm up you will start getting readings an</span>d alerts.

The receiver home screen will display:

- The most recent sensor glucose reading and trend arrow.

  - If your glucose is below 40 mg/dl or 2.2 mmol/L, it will show as "LOW." If it exceeds 400 mg/dl or 22.2 mmol/L, it will display as "HIGH." In such cases, it is recommended to confirm your glucose level with a finger prick and take appropriate action for low or high glucose.

- The sensor glucose graph is displayed below the reading.

  - You can adjust the time frame for the graph and access the menu using the buttons on the device.

Within the menu, you can explore options such as reports, events, settings, information, help, replace sensor, stop sensor session, or turn off the receiver. It is advisable to keep the receiver turned on to receive important alerts.

For a comprehensive understanding of these menu choices, we suggest downloading the menu overview provided below this video and reviewing all the available options.

### ***3.2 Dexcom ON<span class="mark">E Plus</span> app***

The Dexcom ONE Plus app can be downloaded on <span class="mark">the most recent</span> mobile phones, with separate versions available for Android and iPhone devices. You can find a list of all compatible devices and software versions on this website: dexcom.com/compatibility

The Dexcom ONE Plus app has minimal impact on battery life during regular use. However, it is recommended to keep the battery Saver Mode off, because this can cause the Dexcom alerts to be delayed. It is also recommended to keep the “Adaptive Battery”, “Battery Optimizing”, and “Optimized Battery Charging” setting off, as this can prevent the Dexcom ONE Plus app from running in the background. <span class="mark">If the Dexcom ONE PLus app is not running in the background, you won't get alerts.</span>

<span class="mark">The Dexcom ONE Plus app uses Bluetooth to connect with your sensor. You must keep your device’s Bluetooth turned on to get sensor readings and alerts. It is also good practice to delete old Dexcom ONE Plus sensors from your device’s Bluetooth list. Note: Dexcom ONE PLus sensors connect, send data, and disconnect every 5 minutes. The pairing in Bluetooth for your current Dexcom ONE Plus sensor will likely say “not connected” and is expected.</span>

When you open the Dexcom ONE Plus app for the first time, you will need to create a Dexcom account or log in with your existing Dexcom account. Once logged in the app walks you through the setup process step by step. First the overview section will provide you with educational videos and screens, so you can learn everything you need to know about your Dexcom ONE Plus. <span class="mark">You will also be given the option to turn alerts on or off.</span> Then you will connect your Dexcom ONE Plus to your phone. You will enable Bluetooth and set other required phone settings. The app will then provide you with instructions on how to insert your sensor.

Enter the pairing code. You will find the four digit pairing code on the applicator of the sensor you used. Take a photo of the QR code or enter the code manually to pair. Wait for the sensor to pair. Once complete, you will enter into your 30 minute sensor warm-up. The sensor warm-up timer lets you know when you'll start gett<span class="mark">ing readings and alerts. After your warm-up you will start getting readings and alerts. If your sensor has been inserted for over 30 minutes, you'll immediately start receiving readings since the warm-up period initiates upon sensor insertion.</span>

<span class="mark">Tip: We advise you to take a separate photo of the applicator code outside of the Dexcom ONE Plus app. This can be used in the rare case the Dexcom ONE Plus app crashes and needs to be redownloaded and reconnected to your activated sensor.</span>

The home screen will open to the glucose tab, where you'll find the most recent sensor glucose reading along with a trend arrow.

- If your glucose level drops below 40 mg/dL (2.2 mmol/L), it will display as "LOW," while readings exceeding 400 mg/dL (22.2 mmol/L) will show as "HIGH."

- In such cases, it's important to perform a finger prick test to confirm your glucose level and take appropriate actions for either hypoglycemia or hyperglycemia.

Beneath the glucose reading, you can access your glucose graph and choose the desired time range for the data. The yellow line represents your high alert level, and the red line represents your low alert level.

Further down, you'll find a Clarity tab displaying your average glucose, GMI, and Time In Range. You can customize the time range for these reports.

On the bottom of the screen, there are four icons to navigate through different tabs: Glucose, History, Connections, and Profile.

- In the history tab, you can log events such as blood glucose readings, insulin doses, meals, activity, or other relevant information.

- The connections tab allows you to see details of the sensor, or to replace or stop the current sensor. Additionally, there is a link to access Share function and add followers. On iPhones, you'll also find an option to link with Apple Health. When activated, the ONE Plus app will transmit data to the health app with a 3-hour delay.

- The profile tab offers settings for configuring alerts, customizing the Glucose Tab, enabling or disabling Dexcom ONE Plus notifications, optimizing app performance through phone settings, and accessing support information.

- You can download an overview of the menu options in the downloads below this video.

On the Dexcom ONE Plus app home screen, when you turn your mobile phone horizontally into landscape mode, you can access a larger glucose graph, with your glucose readings from the past 3 to 24 hours, as well as events you've recorded. By sliding your finger across the graph, you'll be able to see the precise sensor glucose value for that specific moment.

<span class="mark">Tip: At the top right corner the home screen “+” may be used to enter events. At the top right corner of the graph, there is an ellipsis (...) which can be used to adjust ranges or turn on and off high and low alerts, as well as activate Quiet Modes which we will cover shortly.</span>

### ***3.3 Do's & don’ts***

Here are a few important considerations:

- The Dexcom ONE Plus sensor is water-resistant up to 2.4 meters (8 feet) for up to 24 h<span class="mark">ours. You can shower and swim while wearing your Dexcom ONE Plus sensor, although it is best to avoid this during the first 12 hours after insertion of the sensor.</span>

- The time and date on the Dexcom ONE Plus app automatically synchronize with your mobile phone's settings, while on the Dexcom ONE Plus receiver, you will have to manually adjust this. <span class="mark">You can do this by navigating to the settings menu, selecting Date / Time and then adjust it.</span>

<!-- -->

- Upon triggering the Dexcom ONE Plus applicator, the sensor initiates automatically, triggering a 30-minute warm-up phase. This feature allows you to bridge the usual data gap caused by the warm-up period. <span class="mark">When you insert the new sensor and wait 30 minutes before replacing the previous sensor in your Dexcom ONE Plus receiver or app, your app will seamlessly acquire the sensor data from the new sensor, ensuring uninterrupted data transmission between sensors.</span>

- <span class="mark">The Dexcom ONE Plus sensor readings may seem a bit bouncier than those from the Dexcom G6. This happens because the ONE Plus system doesn't smooth out the numbers as much as the G6 system does. But don't worry, the Dexcom ONE Plus sensor is still very accurate, with an overall MARD or accuracy rating of 8.2%.</span>

- <span class="mark">The Dexcom ONE Plus sensor has the option to be calibrated with a finger prick value. You can do this by tapping the + icon on the homescreen or selecting the History icon, select Blood Glucose, and then the option “Use as calibration”.</span>

- The Dexcom ONE Plus sensor may encounter occasional connectivity issues. If your display device fails to receive sensor readings for two consecutive attempts, a Signal Loss banner will appear. After approximately 20 minutes without sensor readings, your display device will alert you. Once the connectivity is resolved, up to 24 hours of missed data will be filled in on the trend graph. To minimize the risk of signal loss, Dexcom recommends wearing your phone or receiver closer to the sensor and in line of sight. Additionally, ensure that your phone has sufficient battery charge, as a battery level below 20% may trigger low power mode and disable Bluetooth. If you encounter issues, consider stopping and restarting the Bluetooth connection on your phone or power-cycling the phone itself. Dexcom is actively working on improving the Bluetooth connection for future updates.

By adhering to these guidelines and maintaining resilience throughout your glucose monitoring journey, you can leverage the Dexcom ONE Plus system to access accurate and valuable data for effective diabetes management.
