## **<u>4. Setting alarms (2.5 min)</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alarms with the Dexcom ONE Plus sensor.

Throughout this session, we'll walk you through the available alarm options and demonstrate how to set them on the Dexcom ONE Plus receiver and app on your phone. Let's get started!

### ***4.1 Available alarms***

The Dexcom ONE Plus system provides a range of alarm options to keep you informed about specific glucose levels and sensor-related issues. You have the ability to set alarms for Low glucose, High glucose, Signal Loss and Brief Sensor Issue.

The default Low alert triggers at 70 mg/dL or 3.9 mmol/L, while the High alert defaults to 250 mg/dL or 14 mmol/L, but these settings are customizable. You have control over the glucose level, sound, and the snooze intervals for both Low and High alerts. For the High alert, there's an option to delay the initial alert, activating only if your sensor reading remains high for an extended period.

When you receive an alert, it's important to take action, such as making a treatment decision or addressing any system issues. Afterward, you must acknowledge the alert on your receiver, phone, or compatible smartwatch by tapping "OK." Until you acknowledge the alert, it will re-alert every 5 minutes.

### ***4.2 Setting alarms on the Dexcom ONE Plus receiver***

To configure alarms on the Dexcom ONE Plus receiver, navigate to the Settings within the Menu. From there, select the Alerts option, where you can specify the different alerts. Per alert, you can specify the desired alert level and repetition interval when the condition persists.

For customizing alert sounds, access the Alert Sounds menu, where you have several options for vibration, sound volume, and alarm melodies.

Keep in mind that the receiver must remain powered on to receive alarms. If the receiver is fully powered off, it won't receive any alerts.

### ***4.3 Setting alarms on the Dexcom ONE Plus app***

With the Dexcom ONE Plus app, y<span class="mark">ou can access Alerts settings within the Profile tab.</span> For each alert, you can customize the level, sound, and snooze preferences. <span class="mark">For the sound you can opt for vibrate only or choose between different melodies.</span>

The app also features some rare Technical Alerts that cannot be disabled but for which you can choose the sound.

Effectively utilizing these alarm settings empowers you to stay informed and proactive in managing your glucose levels with the Dexcom ONE Plus. When managing alarms, it's essential to configure only the alerts that serve your needs and to disable unnecessary ones. Dexcom's options for different alert sounds and vibrating alerts can help create a quieter experience. Additionally, these alerts can contribute to improving your Time In Range, offering a proactive approach to respond to changing glucose levels.
