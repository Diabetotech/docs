## **<u>5. Data sharing</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on sharing data with the Dexcom ONE Plus sensor.

In this video, we will discuss how to share your data with healthcare providers, create reports, and share data with your loved ones who play a significant role in supporting your diabetes management.

### ***5.1 Sharing data with your healthcare provider***

<span class="mark">If you are using the Dexcom ONE Plus app, it will automatically link to your Clarity account during the start-up process.</span>

There are separate versions of Clarity available for patients and healthcare professionals. Both versions are free, and you can create your account at clarity.dexcom.eu for Europe, or [<u>clarity.dexcom.com</u>](http://clarity.dexcom.com/), at any time.

<span class="mark">In Dexcom Clarity for professionals, you can add a patient by entering their name and date of birth. Once the patient is added, you can connect the Dexcom receiver to the computer and upload the data after installing the Dexcom uploader software.</span>

<span class="mark">If the patient is using the Dexcom ONE plus app, the easiest way to link the patient’s personal Dexcom Clarity account to the professional Clarity account is by using a Clinic Code. The clinic code can be found in the professional Clarity settings.</span>

<span class="mark">The patient enters this clinic code in the Dexcom ONE Plus app by going to Connections, selecting Clarity Clinic, and confirming the link. Once completed, the healthcare provider has real-time access to that person’s glucose sensor data.</span>

### ***5.2 Creating reports***

Once the glucose sensor data is uploaded, Clarity offers eight to nine different reports, which are found in the left side bar.

- The OVERVIEW report provides key glucose metrics from the date range you select within the last seven to 90 days. Patterns and the best glucose day are summarized here, which you can click for more detail. There's also information about the Dexcom CGM system in use and the alerts and alarms the patient has set. Talk with your patients if they need to adjust their alerts to help them stay in range.

- PATTERNS provides detailed graphical views and statistics regarding the patterns found, and offers some possible considerations for improved glucose control. Day and night highs and lows are potential patterns that could be found within the date range selected. Help patients stay positive with best day which shows them when they were most within their target range for the selected date range.

- TRENDS provides a visual representation of where patients spend the majority of their time, but eliminates outliers. The black line indicates the average.

- OVERLAY features all CGM data points to help visualize patterns and individual events <span class="mark">for an entire week.</span> Hover over each day to view just that day. The DAILY report shows every glucose value and patient-entered events for each day. Highs and lows outside the customized target range are highlighted here, and in the trends report.

- Clarity's COMPARE report is useful to analyze how well diabetes is managed. Compare different days, weeks or even months to see the impact of a medication adjustment, or simply to see how a patient has progressed.

- STATISTICS provides detailed metrics displayed by time or day of the week. The Ambulatory Glucose Profile, AGP, is a standardized glucose report where you can see your Time In Range, a trends graph and daily profiles.

- PATIENT GLUCOSE RANGE is only available in Clarity for Clinics. Use this page to customize the patient target range. Changes you make on this page only apply to this Clarity Clinic view and do not change any settings on the patient's Dexcom CGM system or the patient's personal Clarity account. All the interactive reports are available to print, download or email for any date range.

Our general module on glucose sensors provides additional guidance on interpreting these reports effectively.

Sharing data with healthcare providers may seem daunting at first. Take it step by step, creating your Clarity account and exploring the options available. Remember that sharing data empowers your healthcare team to provide you with better care, making it worth the effort.

### ***5.3 Sharing data with family***

Using the Dexcom ONE Plus app, you can share your data with up to 10 caregivers through the Dexcom Follow app.

The process involves these steps:

- In the Dexcom ONE Plus app, users can navigate to the "Connections" section and select "Share" to invite a follower <span class="mark">using their email address.</span> Within this process, they have the option to share the current sensor reading along with the trend graph or opt to share without it. Additionally, they can make suggestions for notifications that the follower will receive.

- <span class="mark">The follower will then receive an email with an invitation to download the app from the app store or start following if they already downloaded the app. In the Dexcom Follow app, they can go through any information screens. They can now see the glucose information of the person wearing the Dexcom ONE Plus sensor. The follower automatically gets the settings that were set for them, but they can go into settings and change them at any time. This only changes their settings and does not impact the alerts on the app of the person wearing the sensor. For example if they want to be notified when the user is over 300 mg/dl or 16.6 mmol/l, they can set a high notification. Using the Share and Follow function of the Dexcom ONE Plus app can really offer peace of mind to the user and the follower.</span>

Sharing data with your healthcare provider and loved ones is a collaborative step towards better diabetes management. Remember, it takes a supportive network to navigate this journey successfully. <span class="mark">You can pause sharing or remove individual followers at any time.</span>
