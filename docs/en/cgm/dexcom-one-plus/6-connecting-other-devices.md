## **<u>6. Connecting other devices</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your Dexcom ONE Plus sensor to other devices.

In this chapter, we explore the exciting possibilities of connecting your Dexcom ONE Plus sensor with various devices, such as smartwatches, smart pens, and insulin pumps.

### ***6.1 Smartwatch***

Dexcom offers native applications that enable real-time monitoring of your Dexcom ONE Plus sensor data on your smartwatch. <span class="mark">Additionally, some compatible smartwatches have the capability to replicate smartphone notifications, including alarms and other app alerts.</span>

Any brand of smartwatch may display some alerts, even if it isn't set up with your <span class="mark">Dexcom ONE Plus sensor.</span> On your smartwatch lock screen, you can tap OK to acknowledge the alert. That will also acknowledge the alert in your app.

It's worth noting that while the future holds the promise of a direct connection between your smartwatch and Dexcom ONE Plus sensor, this feature is not yet available at the time of this video. Therefore, all the discussed options will still require you to have your phone with you to receive your sensor data on your smartwatch.

In this guide, we will explore the setup options for popular smartwatches such as Garmin, Samsung Galaxy, and the Apple Watch.

#### ***\#1 Garmin Smartwatch***

To view your Dexcom ONE Plus glucose data on your Garmin watch, follow these steps:

- Install the Garmin Connect IQ Store app on your phone.

- In the Garmin Connect IQ Store app, search for "Dexcom", and select the "Dex CGM Watch Face" from the search results. If your Garmin watch is connected to your phone, you can proceed to install the watch face.

- Configure the Dex CGM Watch Face by entering your Dexcom username and password in its settings. If the watch face doesn't automatically appear on your Garmin watch, you can manually select it from the available watch faces.

#### ***\#2 Samsung Galaxy Smartwatch*** 

<span class="mark">To access your Dexcom ONE Plus glucose data on your Samsung Galaxy Watch, follow these steps:</span>

- Access the App Store on your Galaxy Watch and search for the "Blose" app.

- Download and install the Blose app on your Galaxy Watch.

- Configure the Blose app by following the provided instructions. This entails signing in to your Dexcom Clarity account via your connected phone.

- You can customize various settings such as glucose units, glucose target values, alert preferences, and the appearance of the watch face.

- Search for and install the Blose watch face from the available options provided within the app.

- Once installed, you will be able to view your Dexcom ONE Plus sensor data directly on your Samsung Galaxy Watch.

#### ***\#3 Apple Watch***

<span class="mark">The Dexcom ONE Plus app includes an Apple Watch app. If the watch app hasn't been automatically installed, you can manually install it by accessing the Watch app on your iPhone.</span>

<span class="mark">On your Dexcom ONE Plus app of your Apple Watch, you can conveniently monitor your current glucose levels, observe the trend arrow, and view your glucose graph from the past 1 to 6 hours. You can zoom in and out by tapping or swiping on the screen.</span>

<span class="mark">To quickly access the ONE Plus app from your watch, consider adding a watchface complication. This allows you to open the app with a simple tap on the complication.</span>

<span class="mark">Please note that these Dexcom ONE Plus watchface complications are only compatible with specific watch faces. For instance, you can use the Infograph Modular watch face and place the Dexcom Complication in one of the available complication positions.</span>

<span class="mark">Keep in mind that Apple Watch complications update information at intervals of 15-45 minutes. Tapping the complication will display a larger Dexcom sensor graph with the most recent blood glucose reading.</span>

<span class="mark">It’s clear that the process of setting up your Dexcom ONE Plus data on your smartwatch depends heavily on the specific combination of phone and smartwatch you own. If the provided instructions appear challenging to follow, there's no need to worry. It often becomes much simpler when you have your devices with you and can follow along in real-time.</span>

### ***6.2 Smartpens***

Currently, the Dexcom ONE Plus app does not have direct compatibility with any smartpens. However, there is a workaround available. You can make a Glooko account and link it to your Clarity account. Additionally, you can connect your Novopen 6, Novopen Echo Plus, SoloSmart Button, Tempo Smart Button or InPen to Glooko.

By logging into Glooko, you'll have access to integrated data from both Dexcom and your smartpen or smartpen cap. This setup offers a comprehensive solution for monitoring both your glucose levels and insulin data within a single platform.

### ***6.3 Smart insulin pumps***

<span class="mark">The Dexcom ONE Plus sensor is not compatible with commercial automated insulin delivery systems, but it can be utilized in open-source automated insulin delivery systems.</span>

DIY Loop, iA<span class="mark">PS and Trio u</span>sers can integrate <span class="mark">the Dexcom ONE Plus sensor data</span> without additional tools, while AndroidAPS users will require a third-party app like xDrip to transmit the Dexcom ONE Plus data to the AndroidAPS app.

<span class="mark">It's crucial to understand that open-source apps are not supported by Dexcom, nor are they approved by the FDA or European authorities. Engaging in this practice carries inherent risks and is a personal choice. While we don't endorse it, we believe in providing transparency about all available options.</span>

To conclude, we recommend embracing the flexibility of connecting your Dexcom ONE Plus sensor to various devices, as it enables you to tailor your Dexcom ONE Plus experience and enhance your diabetes management approach. Adaptability empowers you to make choices that align with your unique needs and preferences.
