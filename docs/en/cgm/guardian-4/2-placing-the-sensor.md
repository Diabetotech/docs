## **<u>2. Placing the sensor</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on how to place the Guardian 4 sensor.

In this chapter, we will guide you through the process of placing and removing the Guardian 4 sensor.

### ***2.1 Supplies***

To install a sensor, you will need the following supplies: a sensor with two oval plasters, the transmitter, the charger and the sensor inserter that come in 1 box, and an alcohol wipe.

### ***2.2 Inserting the sensor***

Before starting, ensure that you have the Guardian 4 sensors in hand, as indicated on the box and packaging. Similarly, confirm that the transmitter is a Guardian 4 transmitter.

When inserting the sensor, it's crucial to follow a few steps carefully.

- Begin by washing your hands and disinfecting the insertion site with an alcohol wipe. Allow your skin to dry completely before proceeding.

- Take the sensor out of the package, making sure to handle it with care and keep the litz in place. Hold the inserter with your thumb on the thumb mark, ensuring your fingers do not touch the green buttons. Press the inserter onto the sensor until you hear a click. You can then remove the base and see the sensor inside the inserter. The arrow on the inserter indicates the needle's location.

- Next, place the inserter on your skin and simultaneously push the two green buttons on either side. Release the buttons to engage the sensor automatically. Hold the inserter against your skin for an additional 5 seconds to ensure adhesive effectiveness.

- Slowly remove the inserter without pushing the buttons, and gently pull out the top of the needle housing to detach it.

- Remove the foil from the plaster and press it against the skin. Secure the plaster with an oval tape like this, ensuring proper adhesion.

- Now it is time to connect the transmitter. Remove the charged transmitter from the charger and snap it onto the sensor. Upon insertion, you will notice a green light starting to flash on the transmitter. Please wait for about 15 seconds and check if it flashes 5-6 times. If you do not see the green light blinking, there might be an issue that needs to be addressed before proceeding further.

- Then place the adhesive tag over the transmitter and take the second oval tape and place it with the wide part over the transmitter.

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

To remove the sensor after the 7-day wear period, follow these steps: remove the oval tape covering the transmitter, peel back the adhesive tag, pinch the flexible arm of the sensor, pull the transmitter away, remove the oval tape covering the sensor, and finally, remove the adhesive and sensor from your body. After removing the sensor, make sure to clean your transmitter and place it back into the charger for recharging.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

- To prevent sensor wire dislocation during placement, we recommend removing the foil from the plaster while it's still in the inserter. This approach is more convenient for most people as it reduces the need for manipulation of the sensor once it's on the skin. Additionally, it helps the sensor adhere better immediately after insertion. Please note that while this is not the official method, it has proven to be a more practical approach for many users.

- Remember to take your time and be cautious while applying the sensor to avoid any failure. Refer to the short instruction manual included in the sensor box for additional guidance.

- Before using your Guardian 4 sensor with an insulin pump, it's important to first link them together. You can find detailed instructions on how to do this in the Minimed 780G closed-loop system module.

- Make sure that your transmitter is fully charged before attachment to the sensor. You can check this by making sure that both lights on the charger are off when the transmitter is connected to the charger.

- Always verify that the green light on the transmitter is flashing after attaching it, as it becomes difficult to adjust the placement after applying the second oval patch.

- In case of allergies to the oval tape, alternative patches may be more suitable.

- One common error message with the Guardian 4 sensor is "sensor updating." This message indicates that the sensor is not functioning properly because it temporarily lost the subcutaneous signal, and the system is attempting to restore it. Without the correct signal, the sensor cannot calculate the sensor glucose value, and calibration is also not possible. There can be various reasons for this signal disruption:

  - If you receive this message right after inserting the sensor, there is a high chance that the sensor is in the wrong position. This can occur due to scar tissue (to prevent this, rotate your sensor placement), hitting a blood vessel (to prevent this, you can cool the insertion site with ice before inserting the sensor), or placement in an area with low moisture in the muscle tissue (drinking extra fluids or massaging the sensor area might help).

  - Another cause in the Sensor Updating alert right after insertion**,** could be that the sensor electrode came out along with the needle. This is not visible, but often felt. The electrode may have been damaged during insertion, folded or bent under the skin, or hit against something. Additionally, if the insertion device was removed too early, the sensor may have partially or completely come out of the body. It is also possible that the skin was not properly cleaned before insertion or the sensor electrode was inserted through a layer of protective spray or glue. If the sensor is not securely taped, excessive movement can occur beneath the skin. Alternative patches can be used to secure the sensor and transmitter.

  - On later days (day 2-6), potential causes of the Sensor Update alert may include:

    - the sensor being placed in a skin fold, causing it to partially emerge from the skin when sitting;

    - engaging in intense physical activity, especially strength training that involves prolonged muscle contractions;

    - experiencing the error message only in the morning, which could be due to pressure on the sensor while sleeping;

    - high external temperatures causing the adhesive patches to loosen, resulting in an insecure sensor placement;

    - experiencing irritation on the skin beneath the patches;

    - being in a sauna or hot tub, or swimming frequently;

    - having pets that jump against you or regularly lifting children/grandchildren.

  - If you frequently encounter “sensor update” alarms, it may be due to a transmitter problem. Difficulties with the transmitter's charging are also an indication of a transmitter issue. In such cases, we recommend reaching out to Medtronic's customer service for assistance. They can provide support and, if necessary, arrange for a replacement transmitter.

  - If the sensor fails to restore the signal on its own, the pump will display the message "replace sensor." It is also advised to replace the sensor if the error message appears continuously on the pump for more than 3 hours.

If your sensor fails before the expected 7-day duration, you can contact Medtronic's customer service to request a new sensor. Generally, if the sensor fails within 5 days, there is a higher likelihood of receiving a replacement sensor.

For users of the MiniMed 780G pump, Medtronic also offers a WeCare portal and app. Through registration, users gain access to Medtronic's 24/7 customer service and online services, including the ability to request replacements for defective sensors.

By applying these additional tips and tricks, you will gain the confidence to have a successful journey with the Guardian 4 sensors. Despite their known sensor update issues, most users are content and can effectively manage them with these extra insights. Embrace these helpful tips to maximize the potential of your Guardian 4 sensors and ensure a positive experience.

**<u>3. Starting the sensor</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on starting the Guardian 4 sensor.

In this module, we will walk you through the process of starting the Guardian 4 sensor using the MiniMed 780G insulin pump.

### ***3.1 Guardian 4 sensor with the MiniMed 780G pump***

To connect t<span class="mark">he Guardian 4</span> transmitter <span class="mark">to the MiniMed 780G insulin pump, please refer to the module on the MiniMed 780G automated insulin delivery sys</span>tem.

Before connecting the Guardian 4 transmitter to the Guardian 4 sensor, it is important that the transmitter is fully charged. You can check if your transmitter is fully charged by connecting it to the charger and waiting until the blinking of the green light stops.

When you attach a fully charged, connected Guardian transmitter to your inserted Guardian 4 sensor, the transmitter will begin communicating with the pump. The insulin pump will prompt you to start the sensor, which usually takes less than a minute, but in some cases, it may take up to 10 minutes.

Upon clicking "start new sensor," a message indicating "warming up sensor in progress" will appear. The warm-up period lasts for 2 hours, during which the sensor glucose readings will be visible on your pump. Calibration is not required with the Guardian 4 sensor.

Once the sensor has completed its warm-up period, you can view your sensor glucose readings on the home screen of your MiniMed 780G pump and on the sensor graph. If you download the MiniMed Mobile app on your compatible mobile phone, you will also be able to track your sensor glucose readings there. You can find a list of compatible phones on the website of Medtronic.
