## **<u>4. Setting alarms (3 min)</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alarms with the Guardian 4 sensor.

Throughout this session, we'll walk you through the available alarm options and demonstrate how to set them via your MiniMed 780G insulin pump. Let's get started!

### ***4.1 Available alarms***

The Guardian 4 sensor offers a diverse range of alarm options to keep you informed about specific glucose levels and sensor-related issues. You have the flexibility to set alarms for low glucose, high glucose, and rising or falling glucose. Additionally, there are predictive low and high alerts that activate when the system foresees your glucose reaching the user-defined threshold within 5-30 minutes.

While all alarms are optional, two alarms are mandatory and cannot be turned off. These are the Low alarm at 54 mg/dL (3 mmol/L) and the High alarm that triggers if your glucose remains above 250 mg/dL (13.9 mmol/L) for more than 3 hours.

For your convenience, the system includes an adjustable snooze function, allowing reminders for consistently high or low glucose levels. Additionally, the system offers flexibility in setting an alert schedule, allowing you to customize alerts based on specific time blocks that suit your individual needs.

To receive alarms, ensure your MiniMed 780G pump is within 1.8 meters (6 feet) of the sensor. This proximity ensures timely alerts and seamless monitoring of your glucose levels.

### ***4.2 Setting alarms on the MiniMed 780G pump***

To set alarms on your pump, navigate to the alarms section in the settings menu.

- Under the high setup, you can configure alarms for high glucose levels or rising glucose trends within specific time blocks. During the initial setup, high alarms are usually left off.

- In the low setup, you can set alarms for low glucose levels or falling glucose trends within specific time blocks. Typically, the lower limit is set at 70 mg/dL (<span class="mark">or 3.9 m</span>mol/L).

- In the alarm settings, you can also adjust the snooze function, which determines how long the alarm repeats. The default snooze time is 1 hour for high alarms and 20 minutes for low alarms.

The audio options on your pump also allow you to choose alarm silence, which temporarily disables all entered sensor alarms for up to 24 hours. However, please note that the alarm at 54 mg/dL (3 mmol/L) and longer than 3 hours above 250 mg/dL (13.9 mmol/L) cannot be turned off.

By utilizing these alarm settings effectively and maintaining resilience, you can stay informed and proactive in managing your glucose levels with the Guardian 4. When managing alarms, stay vigilant and ensure you're using the settings that best suit your needs. This proactive approach empowers you to respond effectively to changing glucose levels.
