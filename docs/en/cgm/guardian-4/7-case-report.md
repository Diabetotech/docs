## **<u>7. Case Report</u>** 

Welcome to the final chapter of this module! In this video, we'll explore some insights shared by Peta, a Type 1 diabetic, on how to extend the lifespan of the Guardian sensors. Please note that while Peta shares her personal experience and tips, it's important to consult with your healthcare advisor.

It's important to note that Medtronic officially recommends replacing the sensor every seven days. However, in some cases, the Guardian 4 sensors may stop working prematurely, resulting in a potential shortage of sensors. In such situations, you might try to restart a sensor that has worked for the full seven days. It's important to remember that restarting a sensor is generally possible up to two times, allowing you to reuse a sensor for a maximum of three weeks. However, please be cautious and follow the guidelines provided by your healthcare provider.

Keep in mind that if you choose to use a sensor beyond the recommended seven-day period, it's crucial to periodically verify its accuracy with a finger-prick test. Medtronic does not provide any guarantee for the accuracy or reliability of sensors used beyond their intended usage period.
