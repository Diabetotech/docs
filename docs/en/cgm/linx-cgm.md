## **LinX CGM**

## **<u>1. Introduction</u>**

Welcome t<span class="mark">o this module on the LinX CGM. In this video series, we’ll break down the essentials of this continuous glucose monitor, one of the smallest CGM sensors on the market designed for both children and adults.</span>

<span class="mark">The LinX sensor measures 22 mm in diameter and 4.2 mm in thickness and weighs only 2.2 grams. It is applied to the back of the upper arm or to the abdomen using an all-in-one applicator. After the sensor insertion a warm-up period of approximately one hour is required. Once the warm-up period is complete, the sensor monitors glucose levels in interstitial fluid and transmits real-time readings to the LinX smartphone app via Bluetooth every minute. The sensor lasts up to 15 days and is intended for people with diabetes aged 2 years and older.</span>

<span class="mark">The sensor housing can be round or droplet-shaped. The droplet- or tear-shaped version is marketed as LinX Vista. Branding varies by country: in some regions, the same sensor is sold under the distributor’s name. Examples include the DiaExpert sensor, a droplet-shaped sensor distributed by Mediq in the Netherlands and Germany, and BUZUD CGM, distributed by BUZUD in Singapore and India.</span>

## **<u>2. Placing the sensor</u>**

Welcome to the first lesson of the LinX CGM module. In this video, we’ll show you how to apply and remove the LinX sensor and outline the critical dos and don’ts at each step. Let’s jump right in.

### ***2.1 Supplies***

Have the following ready:

- A LinX CGM pack, which contains an applicator with a built-in sensor and a quick guide; and

- an alcohol wipe.

### ***2.2 Applying the sensor*** 

<span class="mark">To use the sensor, install the LinX app on your smartphone. First, search for the term “LinX CGM” in the Google Play or the Apple Store. Download and open the app. Create an account using your email address. Then selec</span>t a password. The password must include an uppercase letter, a lowercase letter, numbers, and be in English with at least 8 characters. Afterward, press Send to receive the verification code. Check your email to retrieve the verification code, and then enter it into the app within 60 seconds. Now you can log into the ap<span class="mark">p. After logging in, the app will prompt you to select the preferred measuring unit, either in millimoles per liter or milligrams per deciliter. Now the app will check the required phone permissions to ensure it functions properly. Following this, enable Do Not Disturb Mode. The ap</span>p will highlight when blood glucose measurements are needed. Next, you will be guided in setting the lower and upper target ranges. You will see information about the app’s directional arrows, background colors, and recommendations for the optimal sensor insertion locations, which include the back of the upper arm <span class="mark">for adults or the abdomen on either side of the navel for children aged 2 to 17 years.</span>

After disinfecting the skin with alcohol, let it air dry without using a towel or tissue. Now place the sensor applicator directly on the skin without applying much pressure or stretching the skin at the application site. Press the white button on the sensor applicator. Wait for 10 seconds. Carefully lift the applicator off the skin. The insertion is now successfully completed.

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

<span class="mark">When the app indicates that it has expired or if the user experiences any irritation or discomfort at the site while wearing it, the sensor should be taken off the insertion site. To remove it, gently lift one edge of the adhesive that secures the sensor to the skin, the</span>n slowly peel it off in a single, steady motion.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

- You can only be signed in to your LinX account on a single mobile device at any given time.

- Ensure the skin is thoroughly clean and completely dry before insertion; otherwise, the sensor may not adhere properly.

- Do not use the sensor applicator if it appears damaged or if the safety seal is broken or missing.

- Avoid touching or holding the inside of the sensor applicator, as the introducer needle is exposed.

- Some bruising or bleeding may occur after insertion. If bleeding continues, remove the sensor and insert a new one at an alternative recommended site.

- When changing sensors, you do not need to remove or unpair the existing sensor connection.

In this video, you learned how to place and remove the LinX sensor properly. Following these steps helps maintain dependable glucose monitoring over time and supports reliable data to inform your treatment decisions.

## **<u>3. Starting the sensor</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on getting started with the LinX CGM. In this video, you’ll learn how to start the LinX sensor and become familiar with the key features of the LinX app. We’ll also share tips for proper sensor use and wear in everyday life. Let’s dive in.

### ***3.1 Starting the sensor***

Go back to the app and press the Pairing icon. The sensor’s serial number will appear in the list of nearby devices. Select it. It will pair successfully, and the system will enter the warm-up phase. After 60 minutes, the first sensor blood glucose reading will be displayed on the main screen.

### ***3.2 The LinX CGM app***

The Home dashboard of the LinX app provides an overview of your blood glucose levels. Your real-time glucose reading appears in the upper left area and is automatically updated every minute. Beside this value is the trend arrow, which shows both the direction and rate of change in your glucose level. Different arrows indicate whether your glucose is stable, slowly decreasing, decreasing, rapidly decreasing, rapidly increasing, increasing, or slowly increasing.

Next to the real-time glucose reading and trend arrow, you can see the remaining sensor life. Once the sensor reaches the end of its wear period, its status on the app will change <span class="mark">to “expired.”</span>

<span class="mark">In the top-right corner of the home dashboard, two icons are displayed: a bell icon and a personal account icon. Tapping the bell allows you to mute sensor alerts for six hours or to reactivate them. By tapping the personal account icon, you can access the share/follow, help center and multiple personal settings options of the LinX CGM app.</span>

You can adjust the display period to view glucose trends and history from the past 6, 12, or 24 hours.

The glucose trends graph shows all recorded data from the time you first registered your LinX account. You can scroll across the graph to review glucose values over different time frames. Each data point indicates the glucose reading and the exact time it was captured. Readings outside the target range are highlighted in a different color.

At the bottom of the Home dashboard, you will find icons that take you to the History, Trends, Blood Glucose (BG), and Event dashboards.

The History dashboard lists your glucose alerts, logged events, and daily glucose summaries.

The Trends dashboard presents your glucose analysis over selectable periods (such as the last 7, 14, or 30 days, or a custom time interval), allowing you to compare patterns over time.

On the BG dashboard, you can carry out sensor calibration by entering a fingerstick blood glucose result from your blood glucose meter, provided it is measured and entered within 5 minutes of testing.

The Events dashboard lets you record and monitor factors that influence your glucose levels. You can choose from event types such as “Carbs,” “Exercise,” “Medicine,” “Insulin,” and “Other,” and specify the time each event occurred. These logged events are also shown in the History dashboard and are uploaded to the cloud server, so you can review your event history via the Cloud using your LinX App account.

### ***3.3 Do's & don’ts***

- <span class="mark">The LinX CGM is factory calibrated, which means it does not require calibration. However, calibration with a finger prick value is possible. From the app home screen, select Fingerstick BG. Enter the fingerstick BG value and recording period, then select Calibrate.</span>

- Selecting Record will just record the BG measurement value on the trend curve, and it will be identified by a green dot, while calibration is identified by a red mark.

- Taking paracetamol in doses higher than the recommended maximum (for example, more than 1 gram every 6 hours in adults) can interfere with sensor accuracy and may cause glucose readings to appear falsely elevated.

- The sensor is water-resistant and designed to function reliably during activities such as swimming and showering. It remains operational when submerged in water up to 1.5 meters deep for as long as 30 minutes.

- Remove the sensor before undergoing any X-ray, MRI, or CT scan. After the procedure, apply a new sensor to ensure accurate glucose monitoring.

This video guided you through the LinX CGM start-up process, including sensor pairing, app navigation, and optional calibration. As you support your patients during first use of the sensor, remember that technical issues can occur, such as login errors or readings that do not match how they feel. In these moments, encourage a calm, step-by-step approach. Work through the troubleshooting options and contact customer support if required. Your expertise and reassurance can help patients build confidence and get the most from long-term CGM use.

## **<u>4. Setting alarms</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alarms with the LinX CGM. In this video, we’ll explore the sensor’s alarm options and show you how to personalise them in the LinX app. Let’s start.

### ***4.1 Available alarms***

The LinX CGM provides alerts for Low Glucose, Urgent Low Glucose, High Glucose, Extreme High Glucose, as well as rapid changes, namely Glucose Rising Fast and Glucose Falling Fast. In addition to these glucose-related alarms, the app also generates a Signal Loss alarm if communication with the sensor is interrupted. You can choose to rec<span class="mark">eive all these notifications as Sound, Vibration, or a combination of Sound & Vibration, and set the alert repeat interval anywhere between 5 and 60 minutes. The Urgent Low Glucose alert threshold is preset at 56 mg/dL (or 3 mmol/L) and is not user-adjustable. It automatically triggers both sound and vibration and repeats every five minutes.</span>

### ***4.2 Setting alarms on the app***

To adjust your CGM alerts, tap the Person<span class="mark">al Account icon in</span> the top right corner of the app’s Home screen, then select Alert Settings/Reminder Settings. On this page, you can turn on or off, and set threshold values for Low Glucose, Urgent Low Glucose, High Glucose, and Extreme High Glucose. You can also enable or disable the Falling Fast and Rising Fast alerts, and change how and how often alerts are delivered. In addition, the Alert Settings page lets you switch the Signal Loss Alarm and the Sound Always On option on or off.

- To receive alerts, ensure that:

  - the alerts are enabled;

  - your smartphone is within approximately 2 meters (6.56 feet) of you (the effective transmission range is 2 meters in an open environment);

  - the app is running in the background at all times; and

  - the app is granted the phone permissions it requests

## **<u>5. Sharing data</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on sharing data with LinX CGM. In this video, we’ll discuss how to share your sensor data with your healthcare provider and loved ones and create reports with the LinX app. Let’s get started.

### ***5.1 Sharing data with your healthcare provider and loved ones***

<span class="mark">To share your glucose data with a healthcare provider or a caregiver, they must first create a separate account on the LinX app, following the same steps for sensor application. Note that caregivers may not use a sensor themselves, but may use the LinX CGM app to remotely access your glucose data and get notified for your alarms. From your LinX app, tap the Personal Account icon. Select Share/Follow. By default, My Shares is selected. Click + Account Authorization. Here, enter the email account of the healthcare provider or the caregiver to whom you want to share your data. You can also assign a nickname to distinguish between your followers. On the caregiver’s side they should select Personal Center, Follow/Share. Here they would choose My Follows, and they find your account listed among their followers. They can assign a nickname to you, and select which alarms they wish to receive. On the caregiver’s home screen, an additional icon will appear next to the Personal Center, allowing them to switch to remote monitoring of their list of followers’ trends.</span>

<span class="mark">Another option is Pancares, a diabetes data management platform that integrates with the LinX CGM and can be accessed from any device via a web browser. Sensor users can create a Personal Account to share CGM data with family and loved ones, and they can also grant access to their healthcare professional for remote data viewing. Meanwhile, healthcare providers can set up a Clinician Account for their practice.</span>

<span class="mark">If you're a healthcare provider, you can go to the Patients tab of the Pancares dashboard, then click Add New Patient. Fill in all the required fields marked with a red asterisk; once done, click Create. Refresh the page to ensure the new patient appears in your list. After selecting the patient, click on CGMs Monitoring, then click on CGMs App Account to link the patient's Pancares account to their LinX CGM data. There are two methods to complete this link. If the patient is not physically present in your clinic, select Enter App Account and input their email address. The patient must then open the Settings menu in their LinX CGM app, navigate to Permission Management, and select Share with Pancares Account to accept the pending request. If the patient is in the clinic, select Scan OR Code. They should then open the same Share with Pancares Account screen in their app, tap Scan Pancares QR Code, and scan the QR code on your Pancares screen to establish a connection.</span>

### ***5.2 Creating reports***

<span class="mark">To export an AGP report directly from the LinX app, navigate to the Trends screen. From this view, you can scroll through a general overview of your data or select a specific date range. Once the appropriate dates are set, tap the Export button located in the top right corner of the screen, then tap Confirm to generate a report.</span>

<span class="mark">After waiting a few seconds, a PDF report will display on the phone screen. Tap the Share button in the top right corner to send the PDF directly to your healthcare provider's or loved one's email address.</span>

### <span class="mark">As a healthcare provider, you can opt to use the PanCares platform to view and generate reports. Once you have linked a patient’s CGM account to PanCares, you refresh the webpage to ensure the most recent data is synchronized and visible on the dashboard. Upon refreshing, you will be able to view the patient's LinX CGM serial number, Time in Range statistics, and live glucose data if the patient is currently wearing their sensor.</span> 

### <span class="mark">To generate a report, select the patient in your list, then click AGP. Choose a date range to include in the report; look for dates marked with a small dot, which indicate that data is available for a specific day. After selecting the desired timeframe, click Generate Report to produce a PDF version of the AGP report.</span> 

### <span class="mark">In this video, we tackled the LinX CGM data-sharing and reporting features. Used regularly, these options make it easier for you and your healthcare provider to review trends, align on next steps, and make timely, data-informed treatment decisions.</span>

## **<u>6. Connecting other devices</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your LinX CGM to other devices. In this video, you'll learn how to integrate your sensor with smartwatches and outline possibilities with smart insulin pens and insulin pumps. Let's begin.

### ***6.1 Smartwatches***

Your phone and a compatible smartwatch can receive data directly from the LinX sensor at the same time. This allows you to view your real-time glucose readings, trends, and alerts directly on your wrist. On Android Watch, the LinX watch app comes pre-installed. On Apple Watch, the LinX watch app is automatically installed on the paired Apple Watch when you install the LinX app on your iPhone. A reliable connection is required for pairing and data transmission. Make sure Bluetooth is turned on for both your phone and watch, and that both devices are connected to a Wi-Fi or mobile network.

Confirm that you are logged in to the LinX app on your phone, then open the LinX app on your watch. On Android Watch, you can either double-tap the LinX watch face to open the app or select “LinX” from the watch’s app list. A QR code will appear on the watch screen. To link the watch to your account, use the scanner in the LinX phone app. From the Home screen, you can access the scanner by going to “Personal Center”, then tapping “Compatible Watch”, “Android Watch”, and “Scan”.

On Apple Watch, the LinX app can be accessed from the app list. Follow the prompts in the mobile app to complete login on the watch. Once your watch is logged in and linked to your account, it will automatically show data from the active sensor.

To pair the LinX sensor with an Android Watch, tap “SENSOR SEARCHING” to display the list of nearby sensor serial numbers (SN). Select your device, verify the SN against the label on the sensor box, then tap “PAIR.” After pairing is successful and the warm-up period is complete, real-time glucose values will appear in the watch app. For Apple Watch, follow the instructions in the mobile app to complete pairing automatically.

### ***6.2 Smart insulin pens***

As of now, the LinX CGM does not connect directly with most commercially available smart insulin pens.

### ***6.3 Smart insulin pumps***

The LinX CGM is designed to integrate seamlessly with the Equil S Patch Insulin <span class="mark">Pump, with the long-term goal of enabling an automated insulin delivery system.</span>

<span class="mark">Currently, data from the LinX sensor and the Equil pump can be viewed together in the PanCares web platform; however, the AID algorithm is not yet commercially available.</span>

<span class="mark">In addition, the LinX CGM is mentioned to be compatible with AndroidAPS, an open-source AID system, via xDrip+ in companion mode.</span>

<span class="mark">Be aware that using open-source apps like AndroidAPS and xDrip+ carries inherent risks and is not supported by MicroTech Medical or approved by regulatory authorities. We provide this information transparently, though we don't recommend engaging in this practice without understanding the risks fully.</span>
