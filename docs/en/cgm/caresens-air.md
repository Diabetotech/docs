## **<u>2. Placing the sensor</u>**

Welcome to the first lesson of the CareSens Air CGM module. In this video, we’ll walk you through placing and removing the CareSens Air sensor, along with key dos and don’ts. Let’s dive in.

### ***2.1 Supplies***

To start, you'll need a CareSens Air kit, which consists of:

- An applicator with a built-in sensor;

- Sensor tape; and

- An alcohol swab, which may or may not be included in the box depending on regulatory approvals in your country.

### ***2.2 Placing the sensor*** 

<span class="mark">Download the CareSens Air app, and create an i-SENS account and sign in. Scan the barcode on the sensor package label to enter the sensor information.</span>

<span class="mark">To prevent infection, wash your hands before attachment. Then open the CareSens Air sensor package. Take the applicator out of the package and set it on a flat, stable surface.</span>

<span class="mark">Choose the area where it will be attached. The sensor should be attached to the back of the upper arm, and the area should have enough fat to avoid muscles and bones. Clean the area thoroughly with an alcohol swab and let it dry completely.</span>

<span class="mark">Hold the safety cap down with one hand, and grab the applicator with the other hand to pull upward to remove the safety cap.</span>

<span class="mark">Place the applicator on the area. Be careful not to press the applicator onto the skin too hard or tilt it.</span>

<span class="mark">Press the blue release button on the applicator. Then remove the applicator. Make sure that the sensor has been properly attached. Gently press the white tape around the sensor to secure the placement.</span>

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

The CareSens Air sensor can be used for up to 15 days. The sensor will be automatically disconnected when it expires.

If you wish to replace the sensor before it expires, you need to manually disconnect it. Tap the Settings icon at the bottom of the app home screen to manually disconnect the sensor. Tap Disconnect on the right side of Connection Management. The pop-up window will appear, asking you to enter the four-digit code to disconnect. Enter the four-digit code.

Once the sensor is successfully disconnected, the status will be shown as not connected.

Remove the sensor slowly from the edge of the sensor tape.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

Here are essential guidelines for placement and removal of the CareSens Air sensor.

- CareSens Air has not been evaluated or approved for the following individuals:

  - Infants and children under 18 years of age

  - Pregnant and breastfeeding women, and

  - <span class="mark">Individuals that are on dialysis or critic</span>ally ill.

- Do not use the sensor if the package is damaged or opened, or if the sensor itself is damaged or defective, as this may lead to infection.

- Do not remove the safety cap of the applicator until you are ready to attach the sensor.

- After you separate the safety cap from the applicator, be careful not to face it toward anybody.

- If a sensor part breaks or disconnects in the process of attaching the sensor, you must check whether the part has remained under the skin. If you experience inflammation, redness, swelling, or pain due to an infection at the site where the sensor was attached, seek assistance from your healthcare provider.

You’ve now learned the steps for placing and removing the CareSens Air sensor. <span class="mark">Following these best practices helps ensure reliable glucose readings day after day, supporting confident treatment decisions.</span>

## **<u>3. Starting the sensor</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on starting the CareSens Air sensor. In this video, you'll learn how to start the sensor and navigate the app. Plus, we'll tackle practical tips that will allow you to make the most of your CareSens Air system. Let’s jump right in.

### ***3.1 Starting the sensor***

To connect the sensor with the app, press the power button until it clicks. After the sensor is successfully connected, you can configure your alert settings. We'll dive deeper into alerts in the next lesson.

Sensor warm-up begins automatically and takes 30 minutes. Your glucose level will appear on the home screen once the sensor is ready.

### ***3.2 The CareSens Air app*** 

In the CareSens Air app, instant glucose data and glucose trend arrows are displayed on the home screen. Your dynamic glucose data will appear in different colors depending on where it is in your target glucose range. It will appear green if it is within, orange if it is high, and red if it is low.

Your glucose reading will be tagged as HIGH if it is greater than 500 mg/dL (27.8 mmol/L), and LOW if it is less than 40 mg/dL (2.2 mmol/L). If no glucose data has been received within the last 25 minutes, a dash sign will appear.

The Glucose Trend Arrow indicates the direction and the rate of change in blood glucose compared to the previous reading. It is displayed as an arrow on the right side of the latest glucose reading on the home screen.

There are seven trend arrows indicating: stable, slowly increasing, increasing, rapidly increasing, slowly decreasing, decreasing, and rapidly decreasing glucose changes. If there is insufficient data to determine direction and rate, the change is considered “Unknown,” and three dots (…) are displayed.

Tap the Glucose Statistics icon at the top left corner of the home screen to view the statistics of glucose levels in the past 24 hours. Tap the Screen Mode icon on the home screen to see your glucose trend in more detail. In the horizontal mode, you can see recorded events along with your glucose trends. Each event is displayed with an icon above the timeline. Tap the event icon for more details. You can see the 24-hour glucose trends and events. Zoom in with your fingers to see 6- or 12-hour trends.

If you need to reinstall the app due to changing your smart device or other reasons, you can download the previous data uploaded in the cloud server. After reinstalling the CareSens Air app and logging in with the previous account, Data Restoration page will pop up. To restore data while using the app, tap on the arrow on the top right corner of Previous Data.

You can add a variety of events related to glucose care in the Logbook page. Tap Logbook at the bottom of the home screen. Recorded event logs are displayed from the most recent events. To record a new event, tap the plus icon at the bottom right corner of the screen. The Event Record page will appear. Choose an event you want to record from Blood Glucose, Exercise, Insulin, Ketone, Meal, Medication, or Note. Fill in the details including the event time and date. Then tap Save. The event is recorded. You can find the recorded events from Logbook or Glucose Trends in horizontal mode.

You may choose to calibrate your glucose readings if you feel the need or think it does not reflect your physical condition, particularly when the difference between the sensor reading and a finger-prick test exceeds 20%. Remember to only calibrate your sensor if your glucose is stable and in range. CareSens Air supports a widget so you can see key info on your smart device’s main screen. Use the widget to check your sensor connection status, glucose readings, and trend arrows, or to enter a calibration value. Android offers three widget layouts; iOS offers one.

You can enter a calibration value when the Calibration icon is blue. Tap the icon to open the Calibration screen. On the other hand, calibration is not available when the icon is gray.

Meanwhile, tap the Plus icon to open the "Add New Event" screen.

### ***3.3 The CareSens Air receiver*** 

If you cannot use the CareSens Air smartphone app or choose not to, you can use the CareSens Air Receiver instead. The receiver is operated using four buttons: a round Select/Next button, a Previous button, and Move Menu Up and Down buttons. Refer to the table for the other functions these buttons perf<span class="mark">orm.</span>

To turn on the CareSens Air Receiver, press the round button. Review the device terms, then tap Agree. Next, complete the initial setup by selecting the date format, entering the date, picking the time format, setting the time, and choosing the unit.

After reading the product description and safety information, connect the receiver to the sensor by entering the six-digit PIN found on the package label. If pairing is successful, you’ll see a confirmation message. If the connection is cancelled, a cancellation pop-up will appear instead.

Once pairing is complete, you will be prompted to set threshold values for Very Low, Low, and High glucose alerts, as well as the Rapidly Changing Alert settings. You will also select your preferred alert type.

Once the 30-minute warm-up is complete, the receiver will take you to the Home Screen where you’ll see the latest CGM value, the trend arrow, the unit, the CGM trend graph, the average blood glucose for the graph period shown, and the time remaining until the sensor expires.

To open the Menu from the Home Screen, press the round button. Menu options include Start New Sensor, Calibration, <span class="mark">Events, Report,</span> Information, Settings, and Shutdown. Selecting Events opens a sub-menu where you can enter event records for blood glucose, ketones, insulin, meals, oral medication, and exercise. You can also access the event log from the same section.

### ***3.4 Do's & don’ts***

Now, let’s highlight some do's and don'ts for using the CareSens Air system.

- To connect the sensor with the app, ensure Bluetooth is enabled on your smartphone.

- For iOS users:

  - Enter your PIN code and tap "Pair" to finish connecting the sensor with the app.

  - Do not close the app after connecting the sensor. Otherwise, they will be disconnected.

  - You will not receive glucose data in real time if the app is closed in the background. Make sure to reopen the app once it is closed.

- If the sensor fails to warm up, contact the i-SENS customer service center or refer to the company website.

- A sensor in use with a logged-in account can be reconnected to the same device as long as the sensor remains valid. Once the sensor is connected to the app, it cannot be connected to another app. When reconnecting or changing devices, the sensor can be connected only to the app that it was first connected to.

- The glucose value shown in the iOS widget may differ from the most recent reading. Open the app to check the latest value.

- Keep the sensor and your smartphone with<span class="mark">in 6 meters or 20 feet of e</span>ach other, without any obstacles such as walls or metal objects in between. The distance from the sensor to the smart device must be closer if there is any solid object between them. Otherwise, the connection may fail.

- The sensor is waterproof for up to 24 hours at a depth of 1 meter. You can wear it during a shower or bath or while swimming. However, it is recommended to attach waterproof tape to ensure safe use of the sensor when exposed to water for extended periods.

- If a skin care product such as sunscreen or insect repellent gets on the sensor, wipe it immediately with a clean cloth. Do not wash the sensor. Using an unsuitable solution could damage the device.

- Remove the sensor before an X-ray, MRI, or CT scan.

- Comply with your country’s regulations for handling and managing medical waste when you dispose of the sensor.

## **<u>4. Setting alerts</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alerts with the CareSens Air CGM. In this video, you’ll learn about the types of alerts you can receive from the CareSens Air app and how to customize them. Let’s get started.

### ***4.1 Available alerts***

The CareSens Air app sends alerts for:

- Glucose level (Very Low, Low, High)

- Rapidly changing glucose level

- Signal anomaly detection

- Signal loss

- Sensor replacement

- Sensor error

- Care provider connection

With the app running, the seven alert types appear as popup alerts. If the app is closed or your smart device screen is locked, you'll see the alerts as banners.

Glucose Level alerts occur when your readings fall below your Low or Very Low thresholds or above your High threshold. These alerts require treatment decisions.

You'll get a Rapidly Changing Glucose Level alert if your glucose changes faster than the rate you set. You may have to make treatment decisions in response to these alerts.

Signal Anomaly Detection alerts result from factors like improper sensor attachment or pressure on the sensor, which cause temporary inaccuracies in blood glucose readings. If you receive this alert, check that the sensor is properly attached, ensure nothing is pressing on it, and wait for readings to stabilize. Use a glucose meter if treatment decisions are needed during a signal anomaly.

A Signal Loss alert arises when the connection between your sensor and smart device is lost for 25 minutes or longer.

You'll receive Sensor Replacement alerts 5 days, 3 days, 1 day, and 1 hour before the sensor expires. Recall that the sensor can be used for a maximum of 15 days.

If an error in the sensor occurs during warm-up, 'Sensor Warmup Failure' will appear. If an error occurs after warmup has been completed, a 'Sensor Error' alert will occur. When there is a Sensor Error, the app disconnects from the sensor and can no longer be used. Remove the sensor from your arm as instructed.

Last but not least, you'll get a Care Provider Connection alert when a new care provider is registered in the app. We'll explore data sharing with care providers in the next lesson.

<span class="mark">With the CareSens Air Receiver, you’ll receive the same glucose and trend alerts that are generated in the CareSens Air app. Here’s how those glucose alerts appear on the receiver.</span>

<span class="mark">In addition, the Receiver provides system alerts for Sensor Warmup,, Signal Loss, Sensor Expiration, Sensor Error, Abnormal Signal and Low Battery.</span>

### ***4.2 Setting alerts on the CareSens Air app***

You can configure your alert settings right after you've successfully connected the sensor to your app, and right before sensor warm-up begins.

Later on, you can customize alerts for out-of-range glucose, rapidly changing glucose levels, system alerts, and text-to-speech. Start by tapping the Settings icon on the home screen.

You can customize Glucose Level alerts by setting the VERY LOW, LOW, and HIGH threshold values; the alert type; and whether or not to activate them. Switch the alerts on and off b<span class="mark">ased on your preferences.</span>

<span class="mark">For Rapidly Changing Glucose Level alerts, you can choose a rapid-change threshold of above 2 or 3 mg/dL/min or above 0.1 or 0.15 mmol/l/min.</span>

<span class="mark">Tap System Alerts to change settings for Signal Loss, Sensor Replacement, Sensor Error, and Signal Anomaly Detection.</span>

When you enable Mute Mode, alerts are silenced. Once you turn it off, the alerts return to your previously selected alert settings.

With Text-to-Speech on, you’ll hear an additional voice notification whenever any of the seven alerts is triggered.

Finally, you can find out more details on alerts by tapping the bell-shaped icon in the upper right corner of the home screen. You can see the history of glucose alerts, sensor alerts, and care provider’s connection alerts that you’ve received while using the app.

### ***<span class="mark">4.3 Setting alerts on the CareSens Air Receiver</span>***

<span class="mark">From the Main Menu, go to Settings, then Alerts. Here you can customise the Very Low, Low, High, and Rapidly Changing Glucose alerts. You can also adjust system alerts, enable Mute mode, and reset your alert settings. Use the Up or Down arrow to select the alert you want to configure, then press the round button.</span>

<span class="mark">In particular, you can change thresholds for Very Low, Low, High, and Rapidly Changing alerts. By default, the Receiver sends these alerts with sound cues, but the alert type can be modified as well. Save your selections by pressing the round button.</span>

<span class="mark">From the Alerts sub-menu, select System to view the system alerts. Use the Up and Down buttons to navigate, then press the Round button to confirm your selection. Keep in mind that for system alerts, only the alert type can be adjusted.</span>

<span class="mark">You can also silence notifications by selecting Mute from the Alerts sub-menu. Confirm that you want to enable Mute mode by pressing the Round button. Keep in mind that Very Low, Signal Loss, Sensor Error, Sensor Warmup, and Sensor Expiration alerts are not affected by Mute mode.</span>

<span class="mark">Finally, you can restore all alerts to their default sound settings by selecting Reset Alerts.</span>

### ***4.4 Do's & don’ts***

Keep the following tips in mind when setting alerts with the CareSens Air.

- To receive alerts from the app, you must enable notifications in your smart device’s settings. Go to Settings on your device. Tap Notifications. A list of apps that send notifications will appear. Find “CareSens Air” on the Notifications screen and tap it. In the app details, tap Notifications and turn on “Show notifications.”

- When customizing your alert settings, you can choose from Sound, Vibrate, Sound + Vibrate, or Mute. It may be difficult to distinguish CareSens Air alerts from notifications sent by your device or other apps when the app’s alert type is set to sound or vibration. Adjust your alert settings to more easily recognizable options to help you make appropriate treatment decisions.

- Very Low Glucose, Signal Loss, Sensor Error, Sensor Warm-up, and Sensor Expiration alerts are not included in Mute Mode. These alerts must be muted manually.

- A pop-up alert will always be displayed, even if the smart device is in Mute or "Do not disturb" mode.

- If multiple alerts are triggered at the same time, the alerts will be delivered in the following order of priority: Signal Anomaly Detection alert \> Glucose Level alert \> Rapidly Changing Glucose Level alert.

We’ve now examined how the CareSens Air alert system works. By leveraging these customizable alerts, you transform potential challenges in your diabetes management into actionable moments. Ultimately, you’re empowered to stay on top of your well-being.

## **<u>6. Connecting other devices</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your CareSens Air CGM to other devices. In this video, you'll discover how to integrate your CGM with blood glucose meters and explore options with smartwatches, smart pens, and insulin pumps. Let's start.

### ***6.1 Blood glucose meters***

CareSens blood glucose meters with Bluetooth functionality can be linked to the CareSens Air app to automatically send glucose measurements. Tap Settings at the bottom of the home screen. Tap Blood Glucose Meter. Tap the Blood Glucose Meter icon at the top right corner of the screen to connect with a new glucose meter. Tap the blood glucose meter you wish to pair within the list of connectable devices. Enter the PIN code shown in the glucose meter display. Be sure to follow the specific instructions for your BGM model. Once pairing is complete, data from your previous blood glucose meter can be imported. Choose data you wish to import and tap Save.

### ***6.2 Smartwatches***

You can connect the CareSens Air app to a Galaxy watch or an Apple watch, provided the minimum system requirements are met. Visit the official CareSens Air website ([<u>https://caresensair.com/content/compatibility</u>](https://caresensair.com/content/compatibility)) to find smartwatches that have passed i-SENS’s compatibility tests.

To connect your Galaxy Watch to your phone app, launch the Play Store app on your smartwatch. Search for “CareSens Air,” then tap Install.

For iOS users, Search for “CareSens Air” in the App Store and tap Install. Launch the Watch app on the iPhone where the CareSens Air app is installed.

If the watch app is already installed, close and restart it. The CareSens Air app will appear in the AVAILABLE APPS list, and the watch app will install automatically on your Apple watch. Installation may take a few minutes to complete.

On the watch app screen, you'll see the status of the Bluetooth connection between your smart device and sensor; the date and time when the blood glucose level was last received; the most recently measured glucose level; the current rate of change in your glucose level compared with the previous measurement; and a graph displaying changes in your glucose level while the sensor is in use.

You can choose from the following four types of widgets for Galaxy smartwatches:

- Glucose trends

- Glucose level

- Gauge + glucose level, and

- Reception date and time + glucose level

For Apple watches, five widget options include different combinations of gauge, glucose level, reception date and time, chart data, trend arrows, and changes.

When the app is closed or your smartwatch is locked, your watch can still display glucose and sensor alerts. Just ensure that alerts are set to be sent to both your smart device and smartwatch in your device settings.

Take note that the smartwatch communicates with the mobile phone, not the sensor. If the smartwatch is not connected to the phone and the phone is not connected to the sensor, the smartwatch will not receive alerts or glucose readings.

Finally, you can connect other smartwatches to your CareSens CGM via xDrip+. In the CareSens Air app, tap Settings, tap Manage Data & Connections, and turn on the switch next to xDrip+. Then, in xDrip+, go to Settings and Hardware Data Source, and select CareSens Air.

### ***6.3 Smart insulin pens***

At this time, the CareSens Air system does not support direct connectivity with smart insulin pens, nor can it be integrated with the Glooko Health app for this purpose. However, these integrations may be considered for future versions of CareSens Air.

### ***6.4 Smart insulin pumps***

The CareSens Air sensor does not integrate with commercial AID systems, although it is compatible with open-source AID systems such as AndroidAPS via xDrip. iPhone-based open-source systems do not yet support CareSens Air.

Be aware <span class="mark">that using open-source apps like xDrip+ carries inherent risks and is not supported by i-SENS or approved by regulatory authorities. We provide this information transparently, though we don't recommend engaging in this practice without understanding the risks fully.</span>
