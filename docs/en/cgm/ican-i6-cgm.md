## **iCan i6 CGM**

## **<u>1. Introduction</u>**

Welcome to this module on the iCan i6 CGM! In the upcoming videos, we’ll explore the key features of this continuous glucose sensor, designed for children and adults with diabetes.

The iCan i6 sensor is about 23 mm in diameter, 4 mm thick, and weighs around 2 g. It can be applied on the abdomen or on the back of the upper arm using an all-in-one applicator. After a two-hour warm-up, it tracks glucose levels in interstitial fluid and sends real-time readings to the iCan smartphone app or the iCan view Reader via Bluetooth every three minutes. Each sensor is designed to last up to 15 days and is intended for people with diabetes aged 2 years and older.

In the “Master Module on Glucose Sensors”, we covered the basics, including when to trust sensor readings and how to manage disconnections and allergic reactions. The next videos will focus on in-depth iCan i6 CGM training and advanced guidance for healthcare providers.

The goal of this module is to empower both healthcare providers and individuals with diabetes to unlock the full potential of the iCan i6. However, please note that these videos are for educational purposes only and do not constitute medical advice. Always consult your healthcare provider before adjusting your therapy.

This module was produced by Dr. Inge Van Boxelaer, the founder of Diabetotech and an endocrinologist at the St-Lucas Hospital in Ghent, in Belgium. The content was reviewed by Dr Amit Gupta, the founder of DTech and a diabetologist and medical director of the Centre of Diabetes Care in Greater Noida, in India. Their expertise and experience make for a credible, balanced review of the iCan i6 CGM.

All the information provided in this material is completely independent of Sinocare, the sensor manufacturer, ensuring an honest perspective on the pros and cons of the iCan i6.

After completing this module, you'll be ready to use the iCan i6 CGM confidently in everyday life. You’ll learn how to apply and start the sensor, set and adjust alarms, share glucose data, and connect the sensor with compatible devices. You’ll also get expert advice on safe use and dependable CGM performance. Healthcare providers will strengthen their ability to support iCan i6 users in clinical practice, while sensor users will take away clear, step-by-step strategies they can use immediately. Let’s dive in — may you succeed every step of the way!

## **<u>2. Placing the sensor</u>**

Welcome to the first lesson of the iCan i6 CGM module. In this video, we’ll guide you through the process of applying and removing the sensor, and highlight some do’s and don’ts. Let’s begin.

### ***2.1 Supplies***

Prepare the following:

- An iCan i6 CGM pack, which contains an applicator with a built-in sensor, an adhesive patch, and a user manual; and

- an alcohol wipe.

### ***2.2 Applying the sensor*** 

The first thing you need to do is download the iCan CGM app from the App Store or Google Play. When you download the app for the first time, there will be a guideline to help you learn cautions and important functions of the app. Then you will need to create an iCan account with your email address. Once logged in, the app walks you through training videos step by step first, so you can follow these instructions to start your 15 days with the iCan i6. (00:15 - 00:45)

Remove the all-in-one applicator from the box and check that the tamper-evident label is intact. Open the app, ensure Bluetooth is turned on, and use the app to scan the QR code on the bottom of the applicator.

Next, wash your hands with soap and water. Then choose the sensor site based on age: for ages 2–12, use the abdomen; for ages 13 and older, use either the abdomen or the back of the upper arm. Clean the insertion site with an alcohol pad and allow it to dry completely before inserting the sensor. Unscrew the protective cap in the direction shown by the tamper-evident label on the applicator. Place the armed sensor applicator on the selected site, and press the center button until you hear the injection sound. Gently lift the applicator away from your body. Finally, press down the adhesive around the sensor so that it sticks securely to your skin.

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

Moisten the edges of the adhesive tape with medical alcohol. Peel back the edge of the adhesive tape, then remove the sensor from your skin in one slow, steady motion. Dispose of the used device in accordance with local medical waste disposal requirements.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

- Ensure the insertion site is clean, dry, and free from lotions, perfumes, and any topical medications. If necessary, shave the area so the adhesive tape can stick firmly and stay secure.

- After inserting the sensor, check the site for any bleeding. If bleeding occurs and does not stop, remove the sensor and apply firm, steady pressure with sterile gauze until the bleeding stops.

- Once the sensor is removed, you may clean any adhesive residue with alcohol or soap and water, or apply a warm compress to help soften the adhesive, then gently wipe away any remaining residue.

In this video, you learned the correct procedure for applying and removing the iCan i6 sensor. By mastering this procedure, you help maintain dependable glucose monitoring and support reliable data to guide treatment decisions.

## **<u>3. Starting the sensor</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on getting started with the iCan i6 CGM. In this video, we’ll discuss starting the iCan i6 sensor and finding your way around the iCan app and view Reader. You’ll also get practical tips for correct sensor wear and daily use. Let’s begin.

### ***3.1 Starting the sensor***

After inserting your sensor, press “Start Sensor” in the iCan app to enter into your 2-hour sensor warmup. Once warm-up is complete, you will start getting readings and alerts. Your iCan i6 CGM is intended to last for 15 days. (02:58 - 03:05; 03:10-03:15)

### ***3.2 The iCan CGM app***

The number at the top displays your current glucose reading next to the trend arrow. The trend graph below shows your CGM history over the past 3 hours, plotting glucose values every three minutes. The orange and red zones indicate your low and urgent low threshold settings. The small round icons just under the graph represent events that you logged manually. In the navigation bar, you will find five icons: Data for the home screen, Events to view history and event report, a plus icon to add events, Help, and Settings.

Within the Settings menu, you can access alert history; personal settings to adjust alert thresholds; system settings to change units of measurement, language, and view the current software version; iCan ACCESS for sharing data with care partners; and the option to end the sensor session. From the Settings screen, you can also tap the profile icon in the top-right corner to change your personal details.

Be aware that in some regions the updated iCan Health app is also available. Like the iCan app, it can connect to both the iCan i3 and iCan i6 sensors, but it features an updated layout with a cartoon-like figure on the home screen and only four navigation buttons in the navigation bar.

### ***3.3 The iCan view Reader***

If you are unable or prefer not to use the iCan CGM app, you can use the sensor with the iCan view Reader instead. This black reader is supplied with a USB charging cable and is operated using four buttons: a return button, up and down buttons, and a square confirm/next button.

When you use the Reader for the first time, it will be powered off by default. To switch it on, press and hold the Square button for five seconds. Charge the Reader before your first use to avoid startup issues caused by a low battery,

After selecting your language, proceed to the next screen. Confirm the unit of measurement to continue. Set the preferred date format, then move to the time format settings. Confirm the time format and continue to set the current time. As you continue, read each on-screen guide before moving on, and press the Square button to advance through the pages. For sensor insertion steps, refer to the previous video. When scanning the QR code, aim the camera at the QR code; note that the camera is for scanning only and does not show a live image. You will then be prompted to confirm that the sensor has been inserted successfully. After confirmation, press the Square button to proceed and wait for the Reader to connect. The Reader will display a warm-up countdown showing the remaining warm-up time. Once warm-up is complete, the Reader will automatically open the monitoring Home screen and display the first glucose reading.

The Reader Home screen shows your current glucose reading, which refreshes automatically every three minutes. The font color indicates whether your reading is within range: white means it is between your high and low alert levels, yellow indicates High Glucose, orange indicates Low Glucose, and red indicates Urgent Low Glucose. The screen also displays the trend arrow, the high alert yellow line, the white trend graph, the low alert orange line, and the urgent low alert red line. You can use the up and down buttons to change how many hours are shown on the trend graph. Selecting Menu opens the menu bar, where you can access Glucose Reports, Event Records, System Settings, Share Your Glucose, Information, Help, End Monitoring, and Power Off.

### ***3.4 Do's & don’ts***

- To help the CGM device and the iCan app work correctly, make sure your phone is set to the correct local time. If you travel across time zones, avoid changing the time manually; instead, enable the setting for automatic date and time so your phone updates to the new time zone and local time when you arrive.

- If you use the iCan app, you can activate the sensor and view your glucose data only on a compatible smartphone. For a list of compatible smartphones, visit this page: [<u>iCan CGM System \| Sinocare Continuous Glucose Monitoring</u>](https://ph.icancgm.com/support/device-list)

- Each sensor can be paired with either the iCan app on a compatible smartphone or with the Reader only. Once it is paired, you cannot switch to a different device type during the wear period.

- Remove the sensor before undergoing an MRI or CT scan. There is no need to remove the sensor for a regular X-ray.

- You can wear the sensor while exercising, bathing, or swimming. It is waterproof up to a depth of 2 meters for up to 2 hours. The iCan view Reader, however, is not waterproof and should be kept dry.

- If the ambient temperature rises above 42°C (for example, during high-heat exposure or while bathing), the sensor reading might be affected.

In this video, we detailed the iCan i6 CGM start-up process, from sensor pairing to app and Reader navigation. As you guide your patients through their first days with the iCan i6, remember that technical issues can occur from time to time. If you or your patients run into challenges, encourage a calm, step-by-step approach. Work through troubleshooting options and contact customer support when needed. With your knowledge, patience and determination, you can empower your patients to make the most of their CGM.

## **<u>4. Setting alarms</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alarms with the iCan i6 CGM. In this video, we’ll take a close look at the sensor’s alarms and show you how to customize them in the iCan app and on the iCan view Reader. Let’s get started.

### ***4.1 Available alarms***

The iCan i6 sensor provides glucose alerts, system alerts, and abnormal alerts.

- Glucose alerts include Urgent Low, Glucose Rising Rapidly, Glucose Falling Rapidly, High Glucose, Low Glucose, Out of Range High, and Out of Range Low. When your glucose moves from your target range into a preset alert level, your phone will notify you visually and use both vibration and sound cues. If you do not acknowledge a glucose alert, the alert screen will reappear every three minutes with another notification and vibration, and the alert message will remain on the Home screen until your readings return to the target range.

- System alerts occur when Bluetooth on your mobile device is turned off or when the sensor cannot pair with your smartphone.

- Meanwhile, abnormal alerts are triggered when sensor readings are not available, or when the sensor detects electrical signal problems, abnormal values, startup failure, or temperature-related issues.

The iCan View Reader shows the same glucose-related alerts as the app, along with system and device problem alerts.

- On the Reader, glucose alerts appear as these on-screen notifications. In addition to the visual message, the Reader also provides sound and vibration cues.

- For system alerts, the Reader notifies you about failed sensor scanning, device pairing issues, and Wi-Fi connection problems.

- Device-problem alerts appear when the Reader or sensor battery is low, when the sensor fails to start, or when other abnormalities are detected. The Reader issues alerts for expired sensors, Bluetooth disconnection, and CGM version updates as well.

### ***4.2 Setting alarms on the iCan CGM app***

By default, the app sets the High Glucose alert to 200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L) and the Low Glucose alert to 70 mg/dL (3.9 mmol/L). To adjust these default alert thresholds, tap Settings at the bottom of the Home screen, open Personal Settings, and select the alert level you want to change. The High Glucose and Low Glucose alerts are switched on by default. When an alert is enabled, you can tap the slider to set the Low Glucose threshold anywhere from 60 to 110 mg/dL (or 3.3 to 5.6 mmol/L), and the High Glucose threshold from 117 to 450 mg/dL (or 6.5 to 25.0 mmol/L). You can also choose the sound and vibration settings for these alerts, noting that the actual behavior will follow your mobile device’s sound and vibration settings.

The iCan i6 app offers three alert modes: Silence All, Vibrate Only, and Override Do Not Disturb.

- Silence All controls both sounds and vibrations. When it is turned on, notifications will not produce sound or vibration, but floating windows, the notification bar, and pop-up prompts will still appear. Silence All is off by default; when you enable it, a pop-up lets you confirm the duration of up to 8 hours and presents a warning about the associated risks.

- Vibrate Only controls sound alerts. When enabled, it mutes notification sounds while still allowing floating windows, the notification bar, and pop-up prompts to appear as usual.

- If you use your phone’s Do Not Disturb mode or keep your phone silenced but still want to reliably receive Urgent Low Alerts and other critical glucose alerts, ensure Override Do Not Disturb is enabled in the app. On iOS, you must grant the required system permissions, while on Android, the exact steps may vary depending on your device model and Android version.

### ***4.3 Setting alarms on the iCan view Reader***

## To configure alerts on the Reader, select Menu on the main screen, then go to System Settings and choose Alerts. From top to bottom, the Alerts bar lists: Silence All, Vibrate Only, High, Low, Urgent Low, Rising Rapidly, Falling Rapidly, Out of Range, System Alerts, and Default Settings. By default, all notifications are enabled, along with their sound and vibration settings. If you want to restore the original settings, open the Default Settings page, then select Confirm to reset all notifications to their default values and return to the notification settings page. 

## Silence All turns off sound and vibration for all notifications and can be set from a minimum of 0.5 hours up to a maximum of 6 hours, adjustable in 0.5-hour increments. When Silence All is enabled, an indicator will appear on the screen, and you will still receive on-screen text notifications even with sound and vibration turned off. Vibrate Only mutes all alarm sounds while keeping vibration active. When this mode is enabled, a corresponding indicator will also be shown on the screen.

## **<u>5. Sharing data</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on sharing data with the iCan i6 CGM. In this video, you’ll learn how to share sensor data and generate reports using the iCan app and the iCan View Reader. Let’s dive in.

### ***5.1 Sharing data with your loved ones***

<span class="mark">Use the iCan CGM app’s iCan Access feature to share your glucose information with up to 10 trusted care partners, such as friends or family members. You can enable high and low glucose notifications for them, similar to the alerts you receive in your own iCan CGM app. You can manage sharing at any time by editing a care partner’s access, pausing sharing, or removing them. Care partners do not need the iCan CGM app; they only need to download the iCan REACH app. To invite someone, open Settings and select iCan Access. Tap Invite Care Partners, then enter the person’s name and email address. Tap Next, adjust the notification settings as needed, and confirm your selections by tapping Start Reaching.</span>

<span class="mark">The iCan Access screen shows each care partner’s status and allows you to invite additional people. You can customize what they will see in REACH by toggling items on or off before tapping Start Reaching.</span>

Using the Reader, you can share your glucose data with caregivers or a healthcare provider through the Share Your Glucose option in the menu bar. This cloud-sharing feature requires a Wi-Fi connection, and there are two ways to set up the network. First, you can scan a QR code to automatically obtain the Wi-Fi password. Or second, you can select Connect Manually and press the Square button to search for nearby Wi-Fi networks, then choose your preferred network and enter its password. Once the Reader connects successfully, you can generate and share the QR code or the invitation code with your care partner. Care partners can then monitor your glucose using the iCan Reach app. If you no longer want to share your data with a care partner, select Stop Sharing on the iCan Access screen, and press the Square button to confirm.

### ***5.2 Sharing data with healthcare provider***

Sharing your data with your healthcare provider is done via the iCan Review platform. The ability to share data via this platform is only enabled in certain regions, possibly due to GDPR constrictions.

If you are a healthcare provider and do not yet have an iCan Review account, go to [<u>https://sg.icancgm.com/review/</u>](https://sg.icancgm.com/review/) and select Register on the homepage to create one. After logging in, the system opens to the **Workbench page**, which displays glucose cards for all patients currently authorized to the healthcare professional or care team and under active monitoring. Each glucose card shows key patient information, including basic details, the most recent glucose reading, remaining device wear time, and the latest glucose trend curve. By default, glucose data from the past 12 hours is shown, but this view can be switched to display data from the past 4 hours or 24 hours. When managing a large number of patients, the search box in the upper-left corner lets you quickly locate a patient by first name, last name, or email address.

The **Patient Management** tab displays a list of patients who have authorised you or your healthcare team. The list can be searched using multiple filters, including patient name, email address, diabetes type, monitoring status, duration of non-CGM use, and glucose metrics.

Via **Personal Data**, you can view your clinic’s **personal ID**. Patients who already have an iCan app account can share their glucose data by entering this personal ID. For unregistered patients, you can invite them to share their glucose data using the **Invite Patients** button in the system.

### ***5.3 Creating reports***

Selecting a patient in the Patient List of the iCan Review data platform opens the Patient Details page, which provides a comprehensive view of an individual patient’s information and data. At the top of the page, you can see basic patient details such as name, gender, age, and diabetes type, along with the current glucose value and device status for the sensor being used. From the Patient Details page, authorized healthcare professionals can view and edit patient information by selecting Patient Details in the top-right corner. This opens a window where basic information, notes, glucose targets, and alert thresholds can be reviewed or updated. Patients can also be removed entirely by selecting Remove Patient from the same area.

Daily glucose patterns are accessed by selecting Daily Glucose, where you can view glucose indices and the glucose curve for a chosen date, along with events recorded by the patient in the user app, such as fingerstick readings, meals, exercise, medications, insulin, and other activities. A date selector allows you to switch between days, with the calendar visually indicating dates that contain glucose data or dates where a device was worn but no data were uploaded.

If you don’t have access to the iCan Review platform, you can still access glucose reports from the iCan app and iCan View Reader itself.

In the iCan app, the Glucose Overview Report lets you generate and share a summary of your past glucose data for up to the previous 15 days. To create one, tap Events at the bottom of the screen, select History Report, and choose the date range you want to review. You can also tap Event History to view all alert information, then tap Share to send the report by email to the person you want to share it with.

On the iCan View Reader, you can access your glucose reports by opening the Menu and pressing the Square button to go to the Glucose Reports page. From there, you can choose a 3-, 7-, 15-, or 30-day report, and pressing the Square button again takes you to the corresponding Monitor Details page. Each report includes your average glucose for the selected period and also shows your Glucose Management Indicator, the Standard Deviation, along with your Time in Range.

If you connect your reader to your computer using a data cable, you can also upload reports after downloading the **iCan Glucose Data Explore** program from the Sinocare website.

### In this video, we explored the iCan i6 system’s data sharing tools. Remember, a strong support network matters. By sharing your CGM data, you enable your care team to see patterns, respond promptly, and help you stay on track.

## **<u>6. Connecting other devices</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your iCan i6 CGM to other devices. In this video, we'll outline sensor integration options with smartwatches, insulin pens, and insulin pumps. Let's start.

### 

### ***6.1 Smartwatches***

Right now, the iCan i6 CGM cannot connect directly to an Apple Watch or any Android smartwatch.

That said, the open-source app xDrip+ can be set up to work alongside the iCan app as a Companion App. This means if the iCan app is running on your phone, xDrip+ can capture the iCan app’s notifications or locally available data and use it to display your glucose readings on your Android smartwatch.

Please note that connecting via open-source apps is not supported by Sinocare and is not approved by the FDA or European authorities. Choosing to do so carries personal risk and responsibility. We do not recommend this approach, but we are disclosing it so you have a complete picture of what is available.

### ***6.2 Smart insulin pens***

The iCan i6 CGM does not support direct pairing with any smart insulin pen at this time.

### ***6.3 Smart insulin pumps***

# While the iCan CGM cannot be linked directly to any smart insulin pump at present, it is possible to connect an iCan CGM to AndroidAPS. However, the use of open-source applications is not supported by Sinocare and is not approved by the FDA or European regulatory authorities. Use is therefore at your own risk.
