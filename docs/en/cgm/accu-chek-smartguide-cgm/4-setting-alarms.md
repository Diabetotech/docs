## **<u>4. Setting alarms</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alarms with the Accu-Chek SmartGuide CGM. In this video, we'll discuss the various alarms the sensor can issue and show you how to customize them in the SmartGuide and Predict apps. Let’s get started.

### ***4.1 Available alarms***

The Accu-Chek SmartGuide app can alert you when your glucose goes very high, low, or very low. The Very High Glucose Alarm notifies you when your glucose value rises above your very high glucose limit. By default, this is set to 250 mg/dL or 13.9 mmol/L. The Low Glucose Alarm alerts you when your glucose value drops below your low glucose limit. The default low glucose threshold is 70 mg/dL or 3.9 mmol/L. The Very Low Glucose Alarm warns you when your glucose value falls below 54 mg/dL or 3.0 mmol/L. For safety reasons, this very low limit cannot be changed.

You will only receive one alarm when your glucose first goes above the very high limit or below the low limit. In contrast, if your glucose remains below the very low limit, the app will continue to send a glucose alarm every 5 minutes until your value rises above that level or you dismiss the alarm.

Glucose alarms are active around the clock unless you have enabled sleep alarms. Sleep alarms let you choose different alarm thresholds during the night so that you are not disturbed unnecessarily while sleeping.

Glucose alarms are turned on by default, although they can be switched off. Ho<span class="mark">wever, disabling them is not recommended for safety reasons.</span>

<span class="mark">The “Do not disturb” override makes sure you still receive important glucose notifications even when your phone is silenced or set to Do Not Disturb. To enable this, tap Menu, tap “Do not disturb”, and turn them on in your phone settings so that you can respond quickly to glucose excursions.</span>

<span class="mark">In addition, you can receive a reminder when your CGM sensor is due to</span> expire and should be replaced. Tap Menu, tap Sensor Expiration Reminders, and select 24 hours before, 2 hours before, or both.

You will also receive a notification if the app loses connection to the sensor. To adjust this setting, go to Menu, then tap Sensor Connection Loss. This alert is enabled by default.

The SmartGuide app also uses various other notifications to keep you informed about sensor status, issues, and required actions. These may appear as error messages, maintenance messages, warnings, informational messages, or reminders.

On the other hand, the Predict app focuses on forecasting your glucose levels. Its purpose is to give you advanced insights so you can act early and help keep your glucose within your target range, instead of responding only after a high or low has occurred.

The Predict app offers three types of glucose predictions: Glucose Predict, Low Glucose Predict, and Night Low Predict.

Glucose Predict displays an estimate of your glucose levels for the next 2 hours. To access this feature, you need at least 1 hour of CGM data, an active internet connection on your device, and a current glucose reading that is above your low glucose threshold of 70 mg/dL <span class="mark">(or 3.9</span> mmol/L).

On the graph, the large green dot and the number shown beneath it both represent your current glucose value. To the left of this dot, a solid line shows your glucose readings from the previous 60 minutes. To the right, a dotted line indicates the best estimate of your future glucose values. Behind this dotted line, vertical grey bars illustrate the degree of uncertainty in the prediction. As with any prediction, the further into the future it extends, the less certain it becomes. This is shown by the length of the grey bars: longer bars mean higher uncertainty. Your real glucose values may still end up outside these grey bars. Glucose values are color-coded: readings in your target range are shown in green, while values above the range appear in yellow or orange, and low values are marked in red or dark red.

Low Glucose Predict alerts you when a low glucose event is likely within the next 30 minutes. When the system detects an increased likelihood of low glucose, it sends a push notification. You can <span class="mark">adjust the glucose threshold that triggers this notification between 60 and 100 mg/dL (or 3.3 to 5.6 mmol/L) by changing the Low Glucose alarm setting in the Therapy Settings of the Accu-Chek SmartGuide app. The feature is switched on by default but can be disabled via Notification Settings in the Menu options of the Predict app. When you tap the notification, additional details and suggested actions are displayed.</span>

<span class="mark">The Low Glucose Predict function will only operat</span>e if your low glucose alarm in the Accu-Chek SmartGuide app is set between 60 mg/dL (or 3.3 mmol/L) and 100 mg/dL (or 5.5 mmol/L). If your setting is above this range, the Predict feature will not be active.

Night Low Predict shows how likely you are to experience low glucose during the night. You can receive a notification if your risk of night-time low glucose is high or very high. Set your usual bedtime for the week, and, as long as notifications are enabled, a reminder will appear on your lock screen sometime between 9 PM and 2 AM. During this time window, the Home screen displays your risk of low glucose over the next 7 hours of the night. For this feature, “low glucose” is always defined as a value below 70 mg/dL (or 3.9 mmol/L), regardless of your personal Low Glucose threshold.

If your risk is different in the first 3.5 hours of the night compared with the last 3.5 hours, the prediction will highlight the part of the night with the greater risk. Predictions are generally more reliable for the earlier hours of the night, as short-term forecasts are more accurate.

Night Low Predict uses three risk levels, each represented by a different color. Green indicates a normal or average risk of low glucose. In this case, you can get ready for bed as you usually do. Yellow signals a higher-than-normal risk, and red indicates a very high risk of low glucose. When a high or very high risk is displayed, you may choose to take action, such as eating long-acting carbohydrates combined with protein and fat and/or adjusting your basal insulin or basal rate before going to sleep. The specific steps you take should be based on your diabetes treatment plan, prior experience, and whether the low is expected in the first or second half of the night. If you are unsure, consult your healthcare provider for guidance.

Finally, the Glucose Patterns feature highlights repeating trends in your glucose levels. A pattern is defined as an event that happens more than once within the same day or during the same calendar week.

When the Predict app has sufficient data to recognize patterns in your glucose values, these patterns are displayed and can be tapped to view more detailed information. They may appear very differently depending on how often they occur and what is causing them, and are represented using various icons and colors.

Patterns from the last 24 hours are shown on the Home screen. If no patterns are identified, the Predict app will display a message to let you know. When a new pattern is detected, you’ll receive a notification; tap it to see further details.

To explore pattern graphs, tap the Graph icon in the bottom menu, select a pattern and switch the view to Week or Month. Possible causes for each pattern are listed below the graph, and you can tap these causes to see suggestions for changes you might consider.

Some patterns may be improved or resolved by adjusting your behavior. Once a pattern has been resolved, the Predict app moves it to the Past tab, where you can review all your previous patterns if you need to refer to them later.

Your Logbook entries play an important role in identifying patterns. For instance, patterns related to meals can only be detected if you consistently log your meals in the SmartGuide app.

### ***4.2 Setting alarms on the SmartGuide and Predict apps***

To set up your glucose alarms, open the Menu and select Glucose Alarms. Use the toggles to switch specific alarms on or off. If you enable sleep alarms, you can also adjust your Bedtime (start time) and Wake-Up Time (end time) in 15-minute increments. Next, enter the glucose limits you want for each alarm, then tap Save to apply your settings.

To enable or adjust Critical Alerts, Sensor Expiration Reminders, or Sensor Connection Loss, tap Menu and toggle the relevant settings on or off.

In the Predict app, the Glucose Predict, Low Glucose Predict and Night Low Predict feature are enabled by default. You can disable them via the menu.

You can also set reminders, including push notifications, to help you actively work on particular patterns you want to improve. By creating a reminder for a detected pattern, the app can warn you ahead of time so that alarms may help you prevent the same event from recurring.

A summary of the previous week’s patterns is generated automatically every Monday. If you would like to be notified when this summary is ready, go again to Patterns via the bottom navigation, tap Enable on the Weekly Summary card, turn on the notification toggle, and tap Next. Then select Notify me daily at and adjust the time at which you want to receive the notification, tap Next, and confirm on the final screen.

### ***4.3 Tips***

# To ensure you receive CGM alarms, understand how your phone operates and review its settings regularly to confirm that:

# The CGM app is open and running.

# App notifications are turned ON.

# Power-saving or battery optimisation modes are turned OFF.

# The volume is set high enough.

# The ringer is switched ON.

# Do Not Disturb or Focus mode is turned OFF.

# Airplane mode is turned OFF.

# Bluetooth is turned ON.

# Your phone stays close to you.

# The app alerts you mainly through sound. It m<span class="mark">ay also use other types of notifications, such as vibration or visual signals. The alert sound volume follows the sound volume settings of your phone.</span> 

# <span class="mark">When the sensor is operating in Trend Mode, CGM r</span>eadings may be less precise. This can lead to alarms sounding even if your actual glucose is within range, or no alarms being triggered even if your true glucose is too high or too low.

# Make sure that both the Very Low Glucose Alarm and the Low Glucose Alarm are switched on in the Accu-Chek SmartGuide app during the night.

# The Predict app bases its forecasts on your CGM readings and your logged carbohydrate and insulin entries. Any actions you take in the following two hours — such as eating, taking insulin, or exercising — are not included in the prediction. A 2-hour prediction is mainly intended to inform you when the predicted change is more than 45 minutes away, so you can prepare for possible future steps such as checking your glucose more often, planning insulin doses, or keeping carbohydrates within reach. When high or low glucose is predicted within the next 45 minutes, you should actively consider what actions you may need to take.

# An active internet connection is required for the Low Glucose Predict feature. If your device is offline or in airplane mode, you will not receive a “Low glucose soon!” notification.

# You may not hear or notice notifications while you are asleep. To reduce the risk of night-time hypoglycemia, use the Night Low Predict feature and take preventive steps before going to bed. You can also enable a Low Glucose Alarm in the Accu-Chek SmartGuide app for additional safety.

# Alarm notifications cannot be guaranteed in every situation because there are inherent limitations to any alarm system. Do not depend solely on alarms, as this could result in missing serious low or high glucose events. Open the app regularly to review your glucose levels as advised by your healthcare provider, or whenever you suspect your glucose may be too low or too high. Never ignore symptoms of low or high blood glucose.
