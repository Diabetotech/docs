## **<u>2. Placing the sensor (4 min)</u>**

Welcome to the first lesson of the Accu-Chek SmartGuide CGM module. In this video, we’ll demonstrate how to apply and remove the SmartGuide sensor. Let’s begin.

### ***2.1 Supplies***

Prepare the following:

- an Accu-Chek SmartGuide CGM pack, which contains an applicator with a built-in sensor, a product leaflet;

- and an alcohol wipe.

### ***2.2 Applying the sensor*** 

Start by downloading the Accu-Chek SmartGuide app from the Apple App Store or Google Play Store. Then, open the app. You will need to create an Accu-Chek account or sign in to your account if you already have one. You will need to enter some information about yourself. Next, consent to the terms to use the app. Finally, verify your email address and sign in to the app. To personalize the app, select your units of measurement. Then select your target range. Next, the app will ask you to grant it some vital permissions and to turn on certain settings. The names of these differ depending on which operating system your phone has. Enabling these features allows the app to send you alarms and notifications regardless of settings like Mute or Do Not Disturb. Now that your account and profile are set up, you can apply your first sensor and pair it to your phone.

Disinfect your skin before applying the sensor. Make sure to let it dry completely before you proceed. Flip the pull tab to open the device. Unscrew the blue twist cap. You’ll hear a sou<span class="mark">nd that i</span>ndicates that the device is new and has not been previously opened. If it has already been opened, discard it immediately and get a new one. Look inside of the applicator to check that the needle is pointing straight upwards, but make sure not to touch it. If you notice any damage, discard the device and use a new one. Do not put the blue twist cap back on after you have removed it. Make sure to hold on to the cap because you will need the PIN printed on it to pair the sensor with your smartphone. You are now ready to apply the sensor. Place your hand on your opposite shoulder to make it easier to apply the sensor. Reach underneath your arm and place the applicator on your skin. Firmly press the applicator onto your skin until you hear it click. Remove the applicator without turning or wiggling it. Smooth the adhesive to make sure it is applied properly.

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

You can wear your CGM sensor for up to 14 days. When it is time for you to replace it, the Accu-Chek SmartGuide app will alert you. Find the flat edge of the sensor and carefully start peeling it off by pulling outwards. Make sure all sensor parts have been removed from your skin. If the sensing element has remained in your skin, contact your health care professional. To protect the environment and to safeguard others from harm, dispose of the sensor according to your local medical waste guidelines.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

- Do not apply skincare or hygiene products (such as insect repellent or sunscreen) on the sensor or around the application area, as these may damage the device or loosen the adhesive.

- If only the outer edges of the adhesive pad begin to lift from the skin, the sensor should still work correctly. If any part of the adhesive pad directly under the sensor comes away from the skin, do not try to press it back down or tape it in place. A reattached sensor may not function properly. Instead, apply a new sensor.

- Some companies offer dedicated stickers to make your CGM look more fashionable. These are completely optional, of course. You can also find additional patches that can help improve adhesion.

In this video, you learned the correct procedure for placing and removing the Accu-Chek SmartGuide sensor. By adhering to these guidelines, you help ensure dependable monitoring over time and gain trustworthy glucose data to guide your treatment decisions.
