## **<span class="mark"><u>2. Placing the sensor</u></span>**

<span class="mark">Welcome to this lesson on how to place the Simplera sensor.</span>

<span class="mark">In this chapter, we will guide you through the process of placing and removing the Simplera sensor.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">2.1 Supplies</span>***

<span class="mark">To successfully place the sensor, gather the following supplies: a sensor applicator with a preloaded Simplera sensor, and alcohol wipes for proper hygiene.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">2.2 Inserting the sensor</span>***

<span class="mark">To insert your sensor, first choose your sensor site. The Simplera sensor is approved for placement on the back of the upper arm. Children from 2 to 17 years may place the sensor on the upper thirds of the buttocks.</span>

<span class="mark">When inserting the sensor, it's crucial to follow a few steps carefully.</span>

- <span class="mark">Begin by washing your hands and disinfecting the insertion site with an alcohol wipe. Allow your skin to air dry completely before proceeding.</span>

- <span class="mark">Remove the cap and immediately insert the sensor on your body. Do not use the sensor if it has been contaminated. For example, if you have dropped the uncapped sensor on the floor. Place the inserter on the back of your upper arm. You can reach this area by either lifting your arm or reaching around it. Make sure all edges of the inserter touch your skin. To insert the sensor, press the inserter against your skin in a fluent movement until you hear a click. Gently pull the inserter away from your body in a straight line.</span>

- <span class="mark">Keep the serial number, SN, and the code to use later, to pair to the Simplera sensor with your compatible display device. The SN and code label are also on the inside of the Simplera sensor box lid. Smooth down the sensor adhesive with your finger to ensure the sensor stays on the body for the entire length of wear.</span>

- <span class="mark">You may use over the counter tape if needed to help the sensor stick better.</span>

- <span class="mark">The inserter is single use and contains a needle which has been in contact with blood and other bodily fluids. Dispose of the sensor in accordance with local laws and regulations.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">2.3 Removing the sensor</span>***

<span class="mark">When it's time to remove the sensor, carefully peel off the patch like a bandage.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">2.4 Do's & don’ts</span>***

- <span class="mark">Before insertion, check the expiration date of the Simplera sensor and make note of the serial number (SN) and the CODE. Both numbers will be used later to pair the sensor with the Simplera app. Also keep the LOT number, which you can find on the box the sensors came in. Inspect the tamper band and cap label for any signs of damage before insertion. Do not use the Simplera sensor if the tamper band or cap label is damaged or missing from the cap. In such cases, contact the Medtronic support line to report the issue and have the serial number and LOT number ready to report the issue.</span>

- <span class="mark">Avoid replacing the cap on the device after removal, as this could damage the needle and hinder successful insertion. Some skin care products, such as sunscreens and insect repellents, can damage the Simplera sensor. Do not allow skin care products to touch the sensor. Wash hands after using skin care products before touching the sensor. If any skin care products touch the sensor, immediately wipe the sensor with a clean cloth.</span>

- <span class="mark">If you encounter difficulties during sensor insertion, ensure all edges of the inserter touch your skin. Push on top of the inserter either with the palm of your hand or your fingers to fire the inserter, and use a smooth, firm and continuous motion when inserting.</span>

- <span class="mark">Should you notice any bleeding post-insertion, it’s usually not a concern, and your sensor should still function. Applying ice to the skin before insertion, may help to prevent bleeding.</span>

- <span class="mark">After sensor removal, a small red circle may appear on your skin where the sensor wire was located. This mark should fade within a few days.</span>

- <span class="mark">If your sensor fails before the expected 7-day duration because of a technical issue, you can contact Medtronic's customer service to request a new sensor. Make sure to always have the serial number and LOT number at hand when you call the helpline.</span>

<span class="mark">For users of the MiniMed 780G pump, Medtronic also offers a WeCare portal and app. Through registration, users gain access to Medtronic's 24/7 customer service and online services, including the ability to request replacements for defective sensors.</span>

<span class="mark">By applying these additional tips and tricks, you will gain the confidence to have a successful journey with the Simplera sensors. Embrace these helpful tips to maximize the potential of your Simplera sensors and ensure a positive experience.</span>

**<span class="mark"><u>3. Starting the sensor</u></span>**

<span class="mark">Welcome to this lesson on starting the Simplera sensor.</span>

<span class="mark">In this module, we will walk you through the process of starting the Simplera sensor using the Simplera app and the MiniMed 780G insulin pump.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">3.1 Simplera sensor with the Simplera app</span>***

<span class="mark">The Simplera app is available to download on specific Android and iOS devices. You can check the compatibility online at</span> <span class="mark">[medtronic-diabetes.com](http://medtronic-diabetes.com/).</span>

<span class="mark">When you open the app, one of the first things you'll need to do is create or log into your CareLink account. Once you've signed in, you'll need to accept a user agreement. Then the app will take you through some phone settings. It will ask if you want to share your data and teach you how to customize alerts. Then it is time to insert and pair your sensor. The app will take you through the onboarding education. When you've inserted the inserter you can tap "I inserted it", and the app will connect to the sensor. After the 2-hour warmup time, you will start getting glucose readings every 5 minutes. The homescreen shows the current glucose value and trend value, and also a sensor graph.</span>

<span class="mark">Below there are 4 buttons: settings, sensor, logbook and events.</span>

- <span class="mark">Via the settings you can customize alert and sync data with CareLink and caregivers</span>

- <span class="mark">Via sensor, you can see the lifespan of your sensor.</span>

- <span class="mark">When you tap the sensor and then options, you can replace your sensor or enter an optional calibration.</span>

- <span class="mark">Via events you can log different events like blood glucose, insulin, carbs and exercise. These icons can be found on the sensor graph and also sync to CareLink.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">3.2 Simplera Sync sensor with the MiniMed 780G pump</span>***

<span class="mark">Please be aware that to connect with the Simplera Sync sensor, your MiniMed 780G pump may require an update to the latest software version. If your pump is running on an older software version, you will need to update it using the MiniMed Mobile app on a compatible smartphone.</span>

<span class="mark">To connect the Simplera Sync sensor to the MiniMed 780G insulin pump, follow these steps:</span>

1.  <span class="mark">Open the pump menu and select "Paired Devices."</span>

2.  <span class="mark">Choose "Pair a New Device" and then "New Sensor."</span>

3.  <span class="mark">The pump will search for nearby devices via Bluetooth and should detect the Simplera Sync sensor.</span>

4.  <span class="mark">Locate the CGM with the correct serial number and select it to pair your Simplera Sync sensor.</span>

5.  <span class="mark">If the serial number does not appear, press "Search Again."</span>

6.  <span class="mark">Once detected, confirm the sensor code to complete the connection.</span>

<span class="mark">If you don’t pair your sensor with the pump within 20 minutes of opening the inserter, you’ll need to manually enter the sensor code.</span>

<span class="mark">After you’ve connected your new sensor, the pump will show the sensor warmup time that is left since you placed the sensor. The sensor warmup is 2 hours, but it starts when you actually place the sensor, so if you place the sensor 2 hours before pairing it to the pump, you will be able to see your glucose sensor data immediately.</span>

<span class="mark">Once the sensor has completed its warm-up period, you can view your sensor glucose readings on the home screen of your MiniMed 780G pump and on the sensor graph. If you download the MiniMed Mobile app on your compatible mobile phone, you will also be able to track your sensor glucose readings there. You can find a list of compatible phones on the website of Medtronic.</span>

**<span class="mark">3.3 Do's & don’ts</span>**

<span class="mark">While on the body, the Sensor is protected against continuous immersion in water at a depth of 2.4 meters (8 feet) for up to 30 minutes. It is important to keep this in mind and avoid swimming at depths exceeding 2.4 meter or spending more than 30 minutes in the bath while wearing the sensor.</span>

<span class="mark">When you use the Simplera app, there are a few things to keep in mind to make sure that your phone keeps receiving sensor data.</span>

- <span class="mark">Do not force close the Simplera app. If the app is closed, the app will not send sensor glucose information or alerts.</span>

- <span class="mark">Check the Simplera app occasionally to make sure it is running. The mobile device may close the Simplera app automatically when another app is in use, such as a game. If the Simplera app is closed, the app will not send sensor glucose alerts.</span>

- <span class="mark">Do not let the mobile device shut down due to low battery, or the app will not send sensor glucose alerts. Use of the app may deplete the mobile device battery more quickly.</span>

- <span class="mark">Have a charger available to charge the battery if needed. Turning off automatic updates on the mobile device may help to avoid unintentionally updating to an operating system that is not confirmed as compatible with the Simplera app.</span>

<span class="mark">By following these guidelines and understanding the behavior of the sensor and transmitter, you can effectively utilize the Simplera system and make the most of its features.</span>

## **<span class="mark"><u>4. Setting alarms</u></span>**

<span class="mark">Welcome to this lesson on setting alarms with the Simplera sensor.</span>

<span class="mark">Throughout this session, we'll walk you through the available alarm options and demonstrate how to set them via your Simplera app or your MiniMed 780G insulin pump. Let's get started!</span>

### ***<span class="mark">4.1 Available alarms</span>***

<span class="mark">The Simplera sensor offers a diverse range of alarm options to keep you informed about specific glucose levels and sensor-related issues.</span>

- <span class="mark">You have the flexibility to set alarms for low glucose, high glucose, and rising or falling glucose.</span>

- <span class="mark">Additionally, there are predictive low and high alerts that activate when the system foresees your glucose reaching the user-defined threshold up to 60 minutes ahead.</span>

<span class="mark">All alerts are optional, except for the Urgent Low Alert that triggers when the sensor glucose value is at or below 54 mg/dl or 3.0 mmol/l.</span>

<span class="mark">The system includes an adjustable snooze function, allowing reminders for consistently high or low glucose levels.</span>

<span class="mark">Additionally, the system offers flexibility in setting an alert schedule, allowing you to customize alerts based on specific time blocks that suit your individual needs. There is also an option to silence all alerts for a specific duration of time.</span>

<span class="mark">To receive alarms, ensure your Simplera app or your MiniMed 780G pump is within 1.8 meters (6 feet) of the sensor. This proximity ensures timely alerts and seamless monitoring of your glucose levels.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">4.2 Setting alarms in the Simplera app</span>***

- <span class="mark">To customize alarms on your Simplera app, navigate to the settings menu. Use the toggle bar in the settings menu to change the alert volume.</span>

<!-- -->

- <span class="mark">Here, you can also opt to mute all alerts for a duration of maximum 4 hours.</span>

- <span class="mark">Via Glucose Alerts, you can set your Low alerts, High alerts, fall & rise alerts and snooze time.</span>

- <span class="mark">For the Low Alerts, you can choose to set them at maximum volume at night.</span>

<span class="mark">The Simplera app also has status alerts that provide information about actions needed to ensure the correct functioning of the system. Make sure to act on these alerts to continue to receive sensor glucose information in the future.</span>

<span class="mark">It is important to note that when power saving settings are enabled, the mobile device may delay the alerts and notifications from the Simplera app.</span>

### ***4.3 Setting alarms on the MiniMed 780G pump***

<span class="mark">To set alarms on your pump, navigate to the alarms section in the settings menu. Under the high setup, you can configure alarms for high glucose levels or rising glucose trends within specific time blocks. During the initial setup, high alarms are usually left off. In the low setup, you can set alarms for low glucose levels or falling glucose trends within specific time blocks. Typically, the lower limit is set at 70 mg/dL (3.9 mmol/L). In the alarm settings, you can also adjust the snooze function, which determines how long the alarm repeats. The default snooze time is 1 hour for high alarms and 20 minutes for low alarms.</span>

<span class="mark">The audio options on your pump also allow you to choose Alert Silence, which temporarily disables all entered sensor alarms for up to 24 hours. If you use the Minimed Mobile app in conjunction with your MiniMed 780G insulin pump, you can customise your alerts via the Notification section in the menu. However, please note that the MiniMed 780G has 3 alerts that cannot be turned off. The mandatory alerts on the Minimed 780G are:</span>

- <span class="mark">the Urgent Low Alert, when your sensor glucose is below 54 mg/dl or 3.0 mmol/l,</span>

- <span class="mark">the High alert when your sensor glucose is above 250 mg/dl or 13.8 mmol/l for more than 3 hours,</span>

- <span class="mark">and the SmartGuard exit alert, when the pump exits the SmartGuard feature.</span>

<span class="mark">By utilizing these alarm settings effectively, you can stay informed and proactive in managing your glucose levels with the Simplera sensor. When managing alarms, stay vigilant and ensure you're using the settings that best suit your needs. This proactive approach empowers you to respond effectively to changing glucose levels.</span>

## **<span class="mark"><u>5. Data sharing</u></span>**

<span class="mark">Welcome to this lesson on sharing data with the Simplera sensor.</span>

<span class="mark">In this video, we will explore how to share data with your healthcare provider, generate reports, and share data with family members or caregivers.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">5.1 Sharing data with your healthcare provider</span>***

<span class="mark">To share your data with your healthcare provider, Medtronic provides the CareLink platform. As a user or a family member, you can create a CareLink Personal account at carelink.minimed.com. As a healthcare provider, you can create a CareLink Clinic account at carelink.medtronic.eu, or via carelink.medtronic.com if you live in the US.</span>

<span class="mark">If you are a healthcare professional, you can add your patients to CareLink Clinic by entering their name and date of birth.</span>

- <span class="mark">You can then invite the patient to share their data by sending an email through CareLink.</span>

- <span class="mark">The patient can accept the invitation and enter the code from the email in their personal CareLink account or via the MiniMed Mobile app.</span>

<span class="mark">When using the Simplera app or MiniMed Mobile app, these are linked to your personal CareLink account during the initial setup. If your personal CareLink account is connected to your hospital's account, your healthcare provider can view your data in real-time.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">5.2 Generating Reports</span>***

<span class="mark">Once your data is uploaded to CareLink, you can generate reports by selecting the reports option. The process of creating and interpreting reports is explained in detail in the MiniMed 780G automated insulin delivery system reports video.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">5.3 Sharing data with loved-ones</span>***

<span class="mark">To share Simplera data with family members or caregivers, you have two options.</span>

- <span class="mark">Firstly, you can view CareLink data on your PC by creating a CareLink Personal account as a care partner. You will need to request access to the CareLink data of the user you want to track by entering their username. The user can then approve you as a caregiver through their own CareLink platform.</span>

- <span class="mark">Alternatively, if you use the Simplera app or the MiniMed Mobile app, you can share your data with up to 5 care partners using the CareLink Connect app. To begin, care partners need to download the CareLink Connect app on their compatible mobile phone. They must then create a care partner account and send a follow request by entering the username of the patient they wish to follow. The patient must accept this request through their MiniMed Mobile or Simplera app, or directly via their CareLink account on a computer.</span>

<span class="mark">Embracing these sharing features enhances collaboration, resilience, and informed decision-making for a more empowered journey towards better health.</span>

## **<span class="mark"><u>6. Connecting other devices</u></span>**

<span class="mark">Welcome to this lesson on connecting your Simplera sensor to other devices.</span>

<span class="mark">In this chapter, we explore the exciting possibilities of connecting your Simplera sensor with various devices, such as smartwatches, smart pens, and insulin pumps.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">6.1 Smart watch</span>***

<span class="mark">If you use the Simplera app or the MiniMed Mobile app connected to your MiniMed 780G insulin pump, you can enable pop-up alarms on your compatible phone and smartphone watch, providing quick access to crucial information.</span>

<span class="mark">This chapter delves into the process of connecting your Simplera sensor with popular smartwatches like Garmin, Samsung Galaxy, and Apple Watch.</span>

<span class="mark">An Apple Watch integration is provided natively if you download the MiniMed Mobile app or Simplera app on your iPhone. However, for Android watches, connectivity requires the use of a third-party app like xDrip+. Please be aware that xDrip+ is not supported by Medtronic and lacks approval from regulatory authorities like the FDA or EMA, making its usage carry inherent risks that should be approached cautiously.</span>

#### **\#1 Garmin smartwatches**

<span class="mark">For Garmin smartwatches, download the xDrip+ app from</span> <span class="mark">[<u>github.com/nightscoutfoundation/xdrip.</u>](http://github.com/nightscoutfoundation/xdrip.) Go to the settings of the xDrip+ app and set the data source to Companion app. This allows xDrip+ to receive sensor data from the Simplera app and MiniMed Mobile app. Also in the settings, adjust the inter-app settings as shown here to send the xDrip data to your connected Wear OS smartwatch. Then go to the Connect IQ store app on your phone, download the xDrip+/Spike/Nightscout Watch Face, and set xDrip as your companion app. On your watch you can now choose the xDrip+/Spike/Nightscout watch face to see your real-time Simplera data on your Garmin smartwatch.</span>

<span class="mark">If you have a Garmin Edge cycling computer, you can utilize the</span> <span class="mark">[<u>xDrip+/Spike/Nightscout Data Field</u>](https://apps.garmin.com/en-US/apps/5a3e2cda-12f0-4afd-88ed-000e67a68d84#0) feature to display Simplera data on it.</span>

#### **\#2 Samsung Galaxy smartwatch** 

<span class="mark">For Samsung Galaxy smartwatches, follow similar steps by downloading the xDrip+ app and setting the data source to "Companion App" in the settings. Adjust the xDrip+ app settings to send data to the G-Watch App by checking the Smartwatch and Inter-app settings. Next, install the G-Watch Wear app on your phone and set xDrip as your companion app. Finally, choose the according watch face on your Galaxy watch, and customise the watch face and alerts on your watch as you prefer.</span>

<span class="mark">Please remember that using open-source apps carries inherent risks and is not supported by Medtronic or approved by regulatory authorities. We provide this information transparently, though we don't recommend engaging in this practice without understanding the risks fully.</span>

#### **\#3 Apple Watch**

<span class="mark">Lastly, for Apple Watch, ensure you have the latest version of the Simplera and MiniMed Mobile app on your iPhone and install the Simplera or MiniMed app on your Apple Watch. Access the MiniMed app on your Apple Watch to view your glycemia within your SmartGuard shield, and swiping left will display your sensor graph and Time In Range.</span>

<span class="mark">To have easier access to your glucose data, you can integrate your sensor glycemia and SmartGuard Shield as a complication on compatible watch faces. These watch faces include Portrait, Nike (hybrid), Breathe, Utility, Simple, Photos, Motion, Gradient, XL, and Astronomy. Clicking on the complication will allow you to access the full MiniMed Watch app.</span>

<span class="mark">Remember, the setup steps may vary based on your phone and smartwatch, but you can find detailed tutorials in the provided downloads, making it easier to perform the setup with the devices on hand.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">6.2 Smart pens</span>***

<span class="mark">In addition to its integration with the Minimed 780g system, the Simplera sensor can also be used in conjunction with the InPen. To do this, simply download the Simplera app and the InPen app and follow the steps.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">6.3 Smart insulin pumps</span>***

<span class="mark">The Simplera Sync sensor is seamlessly integrated with the Minimed 780G system, working together flawlessly, thanks to both being offered by Medtronic and designed for compatibility.</span>

<span class="mark">The Simplera sensor is not compatible with iPhone-based open-source closed-loop systems. It appears to be compatible with AndroidAPS via xDrip+ when used in companion mode. Use of open-source applications carries inherent risks, is not supported by Medtronic, and is not approved by regulatory authorities. This information is provided for transparency only and does not constitute a recommendation to use such systems.</span>

<span class="mark">In conclusion, connecting your Simplera sensor to other devices opens up a world of possibilities for personalized diabetes management. From smartwatches to smart pens and insulin pumps, these connections offer valuable insights and real-time data. While utilizing open-source apps carries inherent risks, we provide transparency about all available options. Embrace the power of adaptability and empower yourself to optimize your diabetes management strategy with these innovative connections.</span>
