## **Placing the Sensor**

Welcome to this lesson on placing the TouchCare Nano sensor.

In this section, we will focus on correctly placing the TouchCare Nano sensor to ensure accurate glucose readings and comfortable wear. Let’s go through the process step by step.

### ***2.1 Supplies*** 

To successfully place the sensor, gather the following supplies: a TouchCare Nano sensor package with a loaded inserter, a TouchCare Nano transmitter, and alcohol wipes for hygiene.

### ***2.2 Inserting the Sensor*** 

Follow these steps for proper insertion:

- Start by washing your hands with soap and water and allow them to air dry.

- Now choose a fl<span class="mark">at and pinchable vertical placement site on the back of the upper arm or a horizontal placement site on the abdomen.. For children you can use a horizontal placement site on the abdomen or upper buttocks. Clean it with an alcohol wipe and let it air dry completely.</span>

- <span class="mark">Open the sensor package. Gently remove</span> the sensor.

- Use your thumb and index finger to bend off the Safety Lock by pushing it to the left or right.

- Remove the two pieces of protective liners. Avoid touching the adhesive area.

- <span class="mark">Place the sensor vertically on the back of the upper arm.</span>

- <span class="mark">Place one finger on the top of the inserter and apply gentle downward pressure toward the skin to ensure the adhesive is securely attached to the skin.</span>

- <span class="mark">While maintaining pressure, press the gray button firmly until you hear a “click” to insert the Sensor.</span>

- <span class="mark">Remove the inserter perpendicularly away from the support mount.</span>

- <span class="mark">Discard the inserter into a sharps container.</span>

<span class="mark">Now it's time to attach the transmitter. The triangle symbol on the transmitter should align with the rounded edge that extends from the sensor base. Keep the transmitter parallel to the sensor base. Then snap it in. Make sure the transmitter is securely connected. The green indicator light on the transmitter will flash three times after a successful connection. It will fla</span>sh another six times within 1 minute indicating the system check has finished. Run your finger around the edge of the adhesive pad to make sure the pad remains tightly adhered to your skin.

### 

### ***2.3 Removing the Sensor*** 

When the 14-day wear period is over or if early removal is necessary, follow these ste<span class="mark">ps:</span>

- <span class="mark">Under normal use, the sensor session will end automatically after 14 days. After that, you can remove the sensor from your body.</span>

- <span class="mark">If removing early, disconnect the sensor from the PDM or dedicated mobile app first.</span>

- <span class="mark">Gently lift the edge of the adhesive tape from your skin and remove the sensor and transmitter. We recommend that you wipe around the tape with alcohol wipes or baby oil to make the tape peel off easily.</span>

- <span class="mark">Bend down the rounded edge of the sensor base to break it. Pull the transmitter away from the sensor base.</span>

- <span class="mark">Safely discard the sensor and reuse the transmitter. Please charge the transmitter.</span>

### ***2.4 Do's & Don’ts***

- <span class="mark">Before using the transmitter for the first time, make sure the battery is fully charged. For the very first charge, allow about 2 hours to fully power up the transmitter. After that, it's recommended to recharge the transmitter after each sensor session—this should not take longer than 20 minutes. To charge the transmitter, use the charging cable included in the transmitter box, along with a power adapter that delivers exactly 5 volts. Using the correct adapter helps ensure safe and efficient charging. Connect the USB end of the charging cable to the adapter's USB port. Plug in the power adapter. Align the transmitter parallel to the charging dock and snap it in securely.</span> The indicator light on the transmitter will flash green, indicating that the transmitter is being charged. If it doesn't immediately flash green, don't worry, as it may take some time. Once it's fully charged, the green indicator light will turn off. Remove the transmitter from the dock once it's fully charged, to prevent battery degradation.

- For added security and to keep the transmitter from moving it is strongly recommended that you apply a double-sided over tape to the transmitter. Prepare the enhanced tape. Turn the white part of the tape to your face. Peel off the sticky paper on the top. Hold one side of the enhanced tape and then peel off the sticky paper on the bottom. Peel off the round part of the white paper liners. Do not remove the smaller part of the over tape just yet. Hold the blue over tape by its covered ends, and position it according to the head direction of the CGM. Now apply one side of the blue overtapes, so that it covers the center of the transmitter, and apply the other side of the tape directly on your skin. The hole in the center of the tape should fully expose the transmitter. Smooth down the tape on your skin to reduce air pockets. Now remove the smaller part of the white paper liners and press the remaining over tapes against your skin. Peel off the blue protective film with the overtape, leaving only the transparent layer on your skin. Run your fingers around the edges of the overtape to further secure the adhesion.

- Save your transmitter serial number and sensor code for future use; taking a picture is a helpful back-up.

- For technical assistance, refer to the official Medtrum support team.

By following these guidelines and troubleshooting steps, you can confidently place and remove your TouchCare Nano sensor, ensuring optimal performance and reliable glucose monitoring.

## **Starting the Sensor**

In this lesson, we will show you how to start the TouchCare Nano sensor.

You can connect the sensor using either the PDM or the EasyPatch app. We’ll explain everything step by step.

If you don’t have a TouchCare Nano pump, you can also use the EasySense app. But since most people use the sensor with the pump, we will not talk about the EasySense app in this guide.

### ***3.1 PDM***

The PDM, or Personal Diabetes Manager, is a small device that connects to both the TouchCare Nano sensor and pump.

<span class="mark">It has a touchscreen and a Home Key on the front, a power button on the top, and a charging port and sound hole on the bottom. On the upper left corner, there is also an indicator light that can flash green, yellow or blue in different situations. When charging for example it flashes blue, while a continuous green light indicates full charge.</span>

- To turn it off, long-press the power button for about 2 seconds, then slide to power off. Or long-press the power button for about 8 seconds.

- When the screen goes black, press the power button and then slide to unlock.

To start the TouchCare Nano sensor using the PDM, follow these steps:

- First, connect your transmitter to th<span class="mark">e PDM. Slide to the left from the Main Screen. Tap Sensor from the Main Menu. Tap Transmitter SN. Manually enter the serial number printed on the transmitter or on</span> the transmitter packaging label.

- Next, connect your sens<span class="mark">or to the PDM. Slide to the left from the Main Screen. Tap Sensor from the Main Menu.</span>

- <span class="mark">Tap Connect Sensor. Tap Enter. Enter the sensor code from the sensor package. Tap Done to connect the sensor. Tap Next.</span>

- <span class="mark">Next apply your TouchCare Nano sensor and transmitter on your skin if you haven’t done so yet.</span>

- <span class="mark">After the warm up, which takes up to 30 minutes, you will receive glucose readings. On your main screen, you’ll see:</span>

  - <span class="mark">Your current glucose level along with a trend arrow. If your reading is below 40 mg/dL (or 2.2 mmol/L), it will show “LOW,” and if it's above 400 mg/dL (or 22.2 mmol/L), it will show “HIGH.” Always double-check these extreme readings with a fingerstick and act accordingly.</span>

  - <span class="mark">The home screen of the PDM also shows an estimate of the insulin you still have on board, and a glucose trend graph shown below your current reading.</span>

- <span class="mark">You can also:</span>

- <span class="mark">Long-press the trend graph to open a larger view in landscape mode.</span>

- <span class="mark">Swipe left to open the Main Menu.</span>

- <span class="mark">Or swipe right to see the status screen.</span>

- <span class="mark">Before you remove a sensor from your body make sure it has been disconnected. Go to the main menu of your PDM, select sensor, select disconnect sensor. If you don't see the disconnect sensor option, skip this step.</span>

### ***3.2 EasyPatch App*** 

The EasyPatch app can connect to both the TouchCare Nano sensor and pump. You can download it on most iPhones and Android phones. To check if your phone works with the app, go to medtrum.com/compatibility. You can find the app by searching “Medtrum” in the App Store or Google Play.

To start the TouchCare Nano sensor using the app, follow these steps:

- <span class="mark">Open the Medtrum EasyPatch App, and register or log into your account.</span>

- <span class="mark">First, connect the transmitter to the EasyPatch app. From the homescreen, Go to the Main Menu. Tap sett</span>ings, tap CGM system. First, ensure Factory calibration is turned on, so you can receive sensor glucose readings immedia<span class="mark">tely after warm up, without manual calibration. Tap add transmitter SN. You can scan the QR code for the transmitter serial number on the label of the transmitter packaging box. Tap OK. Or you can choose to manually enter the serial number printed on the transmitter or on the transmitter packaging label.</span>

- <span class="mark">Next, connect your sensor to the EasyPatch app. From the home screen, tap Main Menu, tap CGM, tap connect sensor, tap enter code. You can scan the sensor QR code on the sensor package or manually enter the sensor code. If you scan the code tap OK to confirm the code is correct and the App will return to the home screen. If you manually enter the code, tap done to go back to the home screen.</span>

- <span class="mark">Next apply your TouchCare Nano sensor and transmitter on your skin if you haven’t done so.</span>

- <span class="mark">After the warm up, which takes up to 30 minutes, you will receive glucose readings. Your home screen will show:</span>

  - <span class="mark">The current glucose reading with a trend arrow. Readings below 40 mg/dl or 2.2 mmol/L will display as “LOW,” and those above 400 mg/dl or 22.2 mmol/L will show as “HIGH.” Always verify extreme readings with a fingerstick and take necessary action.</span>

  - <span class="mark">The home screen also shows a glucose trend graph below the current reading.</span>

  - <span class="mark">In landscape mode, you can view the glucose trends over the past 24 hours.</span>

- <span class="mark">Before you remove a sensor from your body make sure it has been disconnected from your Medtrum app.</span> Go to the main menu. Select CGM. Select disconnect sensor. If you don't see the disconnect sensor option, skip this step.

### ***3.3 Do’s & Don’ts***

- The sensor is water-resistant up to 2.5 meters for 60 minutes, allowing for <span class="mark">showering and swimming.</span>

- <span class="mark">If you’re using the PDM, make sure to charge it with the included charger. The initial charge typically takes around 2 hours and can power the device for up to one week. The PDM can store up to 90 days of data. Even if the battery runs out or a system error occurs, your data will remain safe. To keep everything running smoothly, ensure the date and time are set correctly. You can adjust the date and time from the Settings Menu when there is no active Patch</span>

- <span class="mark">If you're using the EasyPatch app, you may notice your phone’s battery running down a bit faster. Even if your phone powers off, your data remains secure and will update once the app restarts. The app can store up to 90 days of data, so you won't lose valuable information.</span>

- Bluetooth communication is essential for the EasyPatch App to connect to your sensor. The App can detect and notify you about a disconnection. As soon as the problem is solved, communication will be resumed.

- The sensor can be calibrated if needed.

  - On the PDM: go to the Main Menu, tap Sensor, then select Sensor Calibration. Here you can enter your finger prick blood glucose value, tap done and tap yes to calib<span class="mark">rate your sensor.</span>

  - <span class="mark">In the EasyPatch app: tap the Enter BG shortcut button located on the bottom menu of the home screen. Here you can enter your finger prick blood glucose value, tap done a</span>nd tap OK to calibrate your sensor.

- Notes can be made in the app. From the Main Menu, tap events to add events like food intake, exercise, or insulin delivery for comprehensive data.

By following these steps and being proactive in addressing any issues, you can make the most of your TouchCare Nano sensor and achieve effective, continuous glucose monitoring.

## **Setting Alarms**

Welcome! In this lesson, we will discuss setting alarms with the TouchCare Nano sensor.

In this section, we will walk you through the available alarm options and demonstrate how to set them up using the PDM and the EasyPatch app. Let’s get started!

### ***4.1 Available Alarms*** 

The TouchCare Nano CGM provides various alarm options to keep you informed about your glucose levels and sensor conditions. These include alarms for:

- Low glucose levels

- <span class="mark">High glucose levels</span>

- <span class="mark">Lost Sensor, whe</span>n the sensor signal is lost for more than 10 minutes due to Bluetooth issues

- No readings, when the sensor can’t detect glucose values. If the sensor isn’t properly inserted, replace it. Otherwise, wait—it may recover. Don’t remove the sensor unless you receive a Sensor Failure alert.

Additionally, the system features predictive alerts to warn you of upcoming high or low glucose values and rate-of-change alerts for rapidly rising or falling glucose levels.

One critical alert is the Low Glucose Alert below 54 mg/dl or 3.1 mmol/L, which is mandatory and cannot be turned off for your safety.

You can also use the snooze function to manage repeated alerts for ongoing high or low glucose levels. Moreover, the alert schedule feature allows you to customize alarms based on diffe<span class="mark">rent time segments to fit your daily routine.</span>

<span class="mark">To ensure you receive these alarms, keep your phone and sensor within 10 meters (or 33 feet) of each other without obst</span>ructions.

### ***4.2 Setting alerts on the PDM***

To set up your glucose alerts on your PDM, slide to unlock your PDM. Slide your finger from right to left to access the main menu. Tap on EasyLoop. Tap on glucose alerts. Now turn on your glucose alerts. Tap on glucose limits you can add up to eight different time segments for the day depending on your need for different glucose alerts at different times. Tap add time segment to add a new time segment. You can adjust the glucose limits for low and high by tapping on the blue numbers below low and high. Once you have completed this, tap the save icon in the top right hand corner of the PDM to save your glucose limits.

From the glucose alerts menu:

- You can also set predictive alerts to be alerted of a predicted high or low glucose value between 5 and 30 minutes in advance.

- Via the rate alerts you can set a rapid fall or rapid rise alert

- And via the repeat function, you can set the amount of time between alerts after the first alert. You can set the Repeat time of High Alerts between 5 minutes and 3 hours, and Low Alerts between 5 minutes and 1 hour.

Alerts can be given as sounds, vibrations, or both. To adjust this setting, go to the Main Menu, tap Settings, then General, and select Audio Options.

### ***4.3 Setting alerts on the EasyPatch App*** 

To set up your glucose alerts on your EasyPatch app tap on the three lines in the top left hand corner to access the main menu. Tap on EasyLoop. Tap on glucose alerts. Now turn on your glucose alerts. Tap on glucose limits. You can add up to 8 different time segments for the day depending on your need for different glucose alerts at different times. Tap add time segment to add a new time segment. You can adjust the glucose limits for your low and high by tapping on the blue numbers below low and high. Once you have completed this, tap on the save icon in the top right hand corner of your smart device to save your glucose limits.

From the Glucose Alerts menu, you can customize several alert options:

- Set predictive high and low alerts, as well as rapid rise and rapid fall alerts.

- Choose a specific tone for the mandatory low glucose alert below 54 mg/dL or 3.1 mmol/L.

- For other high and low alerts, you can set the tone, repeat time, and snooze time:

  - Repeat time controls how long to wait before getting another alert if you *don’t* clear the original one.

  - Snooze time controls how long to wait before getting another alert even if you *do* clear or acknowledge it.

By managing these settings thoughtfully, you can stay informed and proactive in maintaining your glucose levels with the TouchCare Nano CGM. This approach empowers you to respond quickly to changes and improve your diabetes management.

## **Data Sharing**

In this lesson, we will explain how to share your glucose data with healthcare providers and family members who support your diabetes management.

If you use the EasyPatch app, your data will automatically be sent to the Medtrum cloud every 2 minutes. If you use the PDM, you can connect the PDM to your phone using the EasyTouch app, so data is uploaded automatically from your PDM to the EasyTouch app every 2 minutes. This app mirrors the information on your PDM and also sends data to the cloud.

### ***5.1 Sharing Data with Your Healthcare Provider*** 

<span class="mark">Your healthcare provider can access your glucose data through the EasyView Pro platform, while patients and caregivers can use EasyView to monitor and review CGM data. Both EasyView (for users and caregivers) and EasyView Pro (for healthcare providers) accounts can be created free of charge.</span>

To get started:

- <span class="mark">Ensure that your EasyPatch or EasyTouch app is connected to your sensor and transmitting data.</span>

- <span class="mark">Your provider can add you as a patient using your registered email and invite you to connect.</span>

- <span class="mark">Once you accept the follow request, your healthcare provider will be able to follow you.</span>

### ***5.2 Creating and Viewing Reports*** 

Within the EasyPatch app, limited reports are available for the user. You can select daily sensor reports and trend analysis over 7, 30, or 90 days.

To see more comprehensive reports, you can log into the EasyView platform. Here, multiple reports are available:

- <span class="mark">Dashboard, which gives you an overview and averages on the patient's data.</span>

- <span class="mark">Daily summary, which gives you a detailed overview of a selected date.</span>

- <span class="mark">Day by day overview, which gives you either the last 7 or 14 days worth of data on one page.</span>

- <span class="mark">Sensor overlay, which gives you the last seven days worth of CGM readings all on top of each other.</span>

- <span class="mark">Trend analysis, which gives you details of both pump and CGM averages.</span>

- <span class="mark">Events, which gives you the history of every event that has been logged through the patient's phone or PDM handset.</span>

- <span class="mark">Logbook, which gives you details of the last 7 or 14 days worth of data in regards to BG levels, carbohydrate intake, bolus dosages along with further information.</span>

- <span class="mark">Alert, which gives you a history of every alert that has appeared on the mobile phone or PDM handset, as well as the pump.</span>

- <span class="mark">And finally Device Settings Review, which gives you details on the settings of the senssor.</span>

Reports can be printed, downloaded, or shared electronically, helping you and your care team make informed decisions.

### ***5.3 Sharing Data with Family and Caregivers*** 

Family and Caregivers can use the EasyFollow app. This app allows you to share your <span class="mark">real-time glucose data with an unlimited amount of caregivers. Here’s how:</span>

- <span class="mark">To connect, caregivers can find the user account by entering their email address in the EasyFollow app or on the EasyView website.</span>

- To accept follow requests, the user then goes to Main Menu → Settings → Account Security → Remote View Permission in the EasyPatch app, or to Profile and Connections on the EasyView website.

- <span class="mark">Once connected, caregivers can view real-time glucose readings, trend arrows, and receive alerts, with the ability to adjust notification preferences while the user maintains control over shared data.</span>

Sharing data with healthcare providers and loved ones creates a support system that enhances diabetes management. By taking this step, you ensure that those who care for you can help keep you safe and supported.

## **6. Connecting Other Devices**

Welcome to this lesson on how to connect your TouchCare Nano sensor to other devices. In this section, we’ll explore which devices are compatible and how they can support your diabetes management.

### ***6.1 Smartwatches*** 

If you use your TouchCare Nano sensor with the EasyPatch or EasyTouch app connected to your PDM, you can enable pop-up alarms on your compatible phone and smartwatch, giving you quick access to important information.

<span class="mark">For iPhone users, a native Apple Watch integration is available through a dedicated Watch App, allowing you to view your sensor data in real time on your watch. However, if you use an Android phone or another type of smartwatch, real-time glucose display on your watch is currently not supported.</span>

### ***6.2 Smartpens*** 

The EasyPatch and EasyTouch apps are primarily designed for users of the TouchCare Nano Pump. At this time, they do not offer direct integration with smart insulin pens.

### ***6.3 Smart Insulin Pumps*** 

The TouchCare Nano sensor is not compatible with third-party insulin pumps or open-source closed-loop systems.

However, it pairs seamlessly with the TouchCare Nano 200 U or 300 U patch pump. When used together with the EasyPatch app or PDM, they offer a closed-loop system, automatically adjusting insulin delivery based on your CGM readings.

This makes the TouchCare Nano sensor and pump a powerful and convenient combination for managing diabetes.
