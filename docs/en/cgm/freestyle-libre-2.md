## **<u>2. Placing the sensor</u>**

Wel<span class="mark">come to this lesson about placement of the FreeStyle Libre 2 sensor. In this video, we will guide you through the process of placing and removing a FreeStyle Libre 2 sensor, and go over some do’s and don’ts. The steps we show also work for the FreeStyle Libre 2 Plus sensor.</span>

### ***2.1 Supplies***

To begin, gather the necessary supplies: a sensor box and an alcohol swab. Inside the box, you'll find the sensor applicator and the sensor itself.

### ***2.2 Inserting the sensor***

Properly inserting the FreeStyle Libre 2 sensor involves the following steps:

- Start by washing your hands and selecting the spot on your upper arm where the sensor will be placed. Clean the chosen area wi<span class="mark">th water and soap and let it dry.</span>

- Open the sensor box by removing the lid and take off the cap from the sensor applicator.

- Place the sensor applicator inside the open box, aligning the marks on the applicator and the sensor box.

- Apply firm pressure to the sensor applicator until it stops.

- Position the loaded sensor applicator on the cleaned and dried spot on your upper arm, then push it firmly until the sensor is securely in place.

- After application, check the edges of the sensor patch to ensure proper adhesion.

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

To remove the FreeStyle Libre 2 sensor, gently and slowly pull it away from your skin. Dispose of the sensor in the appropriate bin.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

- For ease of placement on the upper arm, lift your arm and elbow to avoid excessive twisting.

- When placing the sensor, aim for a 90-degree angle.

<span class="mark">As you can see, placing the FreeStyle Libre 2 sensor is quite straightforward, and there’s little room for error. If you’re unsure about any step, an instructional manual is included in every box, so there’s no need to worry.</span>

## **<u>3. Starting and scanning the sensor</u>** 

<span class="mark">Welcome to this video, where we'll show you how to start and scan the FreeStyle Libre 2 and FreeStyle Libre 2 Plus sensors. You can do this using an app on your phone or with a FreeStyle Libre 2 reader.</span>

<span class="mark">For simplicity, we will only discuss the LibreLink app. In some regions, however, the dedicated app for the FreeStyle Libre 2 or Libre 2 Plus sensor may appear as the “FreeStyle Libre 2” app or simply the “Libre” app.</span>

<span class="mark">If you're using a regular FreeStyle Libre 2 sensor and have both the reader and the LibreLink app on your phone, you can choose which device to use for scanning. You can even use both devices simultaneously by first starting and scanning the sensor with the reader, then with your phone. However, alarms will only be sent to the first device you scan with.</span>

<span class="mark">If you're using a FreeStyle Libre 2 Plus sensor or have access to an updated version of the LibreLink app, you can monitor your glucose in real-time on your phone using the FreeStyle LibreLink app without needing to scan each time. Scanning is only required once to start the sensor. If you only have access to a FreeStyle Libre 2 reader, you can still use it, but it will work as a flash glucose monitor, meaning you'll need to scan the sensor each time to see your glucose levels.</span>

### ***3.1 <span class="mark">FreeStyle Li</span>bre 2 reader***

If you opt to <span class="mark">use the FreeStyle Libre 2 reader, let's explore how it works.</span>

<span class="mark">The front of the reader features a touchscreen and a home button. On the side, you'll find a micro USB port for charging the reader and reading data. And at the bottom, there is a test strip port, allowing you to measure your blood glucose or blood ketones with a simple finger prick.</span>

<span class="mark">The FreeStyle Libre 2 reader is compatible with both FreeStyle Precision blood glucose test strips and Abbott's ketone strips. By inserting the test strip and applying blood to it, you can utilize th</span>e built-in blood glucose meter or ketone meter.

Don't forget to charge the reader daily, preferably using the provided cable. Many users find it convenient to charge it overnight next to their bed, ensuring uninterrupted alerts while charging. Rest assured, if you charge the reader, you will still be able to use it.

Now, let's delve into the step-by-step process of scanning the sensor using the reader:

- Press the blue start button to power on the scanner.

- Once powered on, the home screen displays essential information such as the current time, battery level, sensor end time, and a cogwheel icon for accessing settings at the top. In the center of the screen, you'll find two options: "Check Glucose" and "Review History." If you don’t have an active sensor, the first option will be “Start New Sensor”.

- Tap "Start New Sensor" and hold the reader within 4 centimeters (or 1.5 inches) of the sensor to initiate the scan. If sounds are enabled, the reader emits a beep upon sensor activation.

- Approximately 1 hour after startup, you can view your sensor glucose data everytime you scan the sensor. To scan the sensor, press on the blue button to start the reader and simply hold the reader within 4 centimeters (or 1.5 inches) of the sensor again. It can scan rig<span class="mark">ht through your clothes. If sounds are enabled, the scanner emits a reassuring beep onc</span>e it successfully scans the sensor. As you scan, the reader will display your current sensor glucose level and a glucose trend arrow.

- If your glucose level falls below 40 mg/dL or 2.2 mmol/L, the reader will display "LO<span class="mark">". Co</span>nversely, if it exceeds 500 mg/dL or 27.8 mmol/L, it will show "HI". In such instances, it is important to verify your glucose level with a finger prick and take appropriate action to address low or high glucose levels.

- The top left corner of the screen provides additional information about your glucose level.

- By tapping the pencil icon in the top right corner, you can add important details such as insulin dose, food intake, or exercise. Remember to press "OK" to save the information. This action will be reflected by a small marker, such as an insulin syringe or an apple, on the sensor graph.

- The sensor graph showcases your glucose levels for the past 8 hours. If you wait longer than 8 hours to scan your sensor, there will be a gap in the scan information.

To explore your history, navigate to Review History on the Home Screen. Here you find the log, daily graph, average glucose, daily trends, time within range, <span class="mark">low glucose events,</span> and sensor usage.

The home screen also features a settings cog, offering you further customization options. Within the settings, you can:

- set alarms

- adjust sound & vibration preferences

- adjust time and date

- set reminders

- test the built-in blood or ketone meter using a control solution

- change the language

- view system status

- change report settings

- consult reader basics

Ultimately, there are professional options available as well. To access them, you need to confirm that you are a healthcare provider and enter the code CAA1C. Once inside, you can modify the insulin dose increment and configure a bolus calculator.

We will not go deeper into how <span class="mark">to use th</span>e built-in bolus calculator, as the calculator is only for use with fingerstick blood glucose results from the built-in meter. You cannot use the insulin calculator with sensor glucose readings.

### ***3.<span class="mark">2</span> LibreLink app***

Now, let's explore the process of starting and scanning the sensor using the LibreLink app on your mobile phone<span class="mark">.</span>

<span class="mark">The first step is to download the LibreLink app,</span> which is available for both Android and iPhone devices.

Using the LibreLink app will have no noticeable effect on your phone's battery life during normal use. Even if your phone's battery runs out, don't worry—your data will remain safe. You can confidently scan the sensor or receive glucose data while your phone charges.

Upon launching the app, you'll notice a hamburger menu icon on the top left. In some Android phones, part of the settings is available in an additional Overflow menu in the top right corner. Here you can see what features are available via the Main Menu. This overview of the homescreen and menu of the FreeStyle LibreLink app can be downloaded below this video

Starting and scanning the sensor is a little bit different between the Android and iOS version of the LibreLink app:

- On an iPhone, you have to tap "Start New Sensor" or “Scan” before scanning the sensor

- On an Android device, you don’t have to press any button to do this. You can just open the LibreLink app and scan the sensor to start it.

It's important to note that the location of the NFC (Near Field Communication) antenna differs between iPhone and Android devices.

- On an iPhone, you'll typically find it on the front of the device, often in the top center.

- For Android devices, it's usually located at the back, either in the middle or towards the top.

- Take a moment to gently move the phone over the sensor, observing where the scanning functionality is situated. Familiarize yourself with the NFC antenna's position to ensure successful scans.

- If you encounter any issues with scanning, it's possible that your NFC antenna may be damaged or your NFC function could be turned off. Make sure to check these settings before troubleshooting further.

- Some phone cases block NFC, preventing you from scanning. If your phone case is made of plastic or silicone or another non-conductive material, you can probably scan the sensor without removing the case. If the case is made of aluminium, metal or another material with a conductive surface, you may need to remove the case before scanning the sensor. The thickness of the case can also make a difference. If your case is very thick, it may prevent the phone from scanning the sensor.

<span class="mark">One hour after activating the FreeStyle Libre 2 sensor by scanning it, you'll be able to see your glucose levels in the LibreLink app.</span>

- <span class="mark">For the regular FreeStyle Libre 2 sensor, you'll need to scan the sensor each time to see your glucose level.</span>

- <span class="mark">If you're using the FreeStyle Libre 2 Plus sensor or have access to the updated LibreLink app, you can monitor your glucose in real-time without needing to scan.</span>

<span class="mark">In the LibreLink app, you can easily add additional information from the home screen, such as your insulin dose, the number of carbs you've eaten, or any exercise you've done.</span>

<span class="mark">The LibreLink app also allows you to add and view notes in the Logbook at any time, providing greater flexibility in recording important details. This is more convenient than the reader, where note-taking is limited to within 15 minutes of a glucose reading.</span>

<span class="mark">Additionally, the app offers more extensive reporting capabilities, including real-time tracking of estimated HbA1c. To use this feature, you'll need at least 5 days of sensor data.</span>

### ***3.3 Do's & don’ts***

Now, let's discuss some do's and don'ts for using the system:

- The sensor is waterproof up to 1 meter (3 feet) for 30 minutes. Keep in mind that swimming beyond this depth or duration may affect its functionality.

- The reader itself is not waterproof and comes with a 2-year warranty.

- Remember that the reader's time and date do not adjust automatically. You'll need to manually update them via the settings, especially when changing time zones. When adjusted, a watch symbol will appear on the charts. On the other hand, if you use the LibreLink app on your mobile phone, the time will synchronize automatically with your phone's time.

- The reader may display different error messages, some with explanatory texts and others with an E followed by a number. Refer to the user manual for detailed explanations of these messages.

- If you use the Novopen 6 for insulin injections, you can directly upload insulin data to the LibreLink app by scanning the Novopen. This feature requires scanning to upload the data.

- <span class="mark">Remember to scan the sensor every 8 hours to ensure continuous data collection. If you don’t, there may be gaps in your sensor data. However, if you’re using the FreeStyle Libre 2 Plus or have access to the updated LibreLink app, scanning is not required after starting the sensor.</span>

- <span class="mark">The LibreLink app has text-to-speech functions, which you can turn on in the settings via the hamburger menu. In that case, you can hear the glucose readings after each scan, along with the measurement unit an</span>d direction of the trend arrow.

Remember, <span class="mark">technology can occasionally present us with unexpected hurdles. If you encounter difficulties scanning the sensor or connecting your devices, don't be discouraged. Stay persistent, explore troubleshooting options, and reach out to customer support for assistance.</span>

**<u>4. Setting alarms</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alarms with the FreeStyle Libre 2 sensor. During this session, we will guide you through the process of setting alarms and inform you about the available options. We'll cover how to set alarms on both the reader and the LibreLink app on your phone, ensuring you have a comprehensive understanding of the process. Let's get started!

### ***4.1 Available alarms***

With t<span class="mark">he FreeStyle Libre 2 system, all alarms are optional. You can set high and/or low alarms, as well as an alar</span>m on signal loss. There is no possibility to set predictive alarms for hyper or hypoglycemia.

Whether you opt for the reader or the mobile phone as your scanning device, alarms will only work if the device is within 6 met<span class="mark">ers (or 20 feet</span>) of your sensor.

### ***4.2 Setting alarms on the reader***

On the reader’s home screen, you can access the alarms feature through the cogwheel icon. Here, you can define thresholds for the low and high alarms, as well as opt for an alarm on signal loss. Adjust sound and vibration preferences according to your preference. The reader offers a single type of sound and two levels of volume.

Remember that if an alarm is ignored, it will reappear after 5 minutes if the condition persists.

### ***4.3 Setting alarms on the mobile phone***

In the LibreLin<span class="mark">k app y</span>ou can navigate to the alarms section by accessing the hamburger menu on the left. Here, you have a broader range of options to tailor your alarm experience. Choose your preferred sound and decide if you want a popup notification. The volume of the alarm will match your mobile phone's sound settings.

It's worth mentioning that if you encounter a red exclamation mark in the alarms section, it indicates an issue with your phone's settings. Simply click on it and follow the provided instructions to resolve the matter.

To ensure proper alarm functionality, make sure to adjust the following settings if you’re using the LibreLink app:

- Enable Bluetooth for a stable connection.

- Don’t use Battery Saving Mode to maintain a strong signal.

- On Android devices: enable location services to locate the sensor accurately and allow access to media to ensure proper alarm sounds.

- On iPhones: keep the LibreLink app running in the background to maintain a continuous connection.

<span class="mark">As we conclude this lesson on setting alarms with the FreeStyle Libre 2 sensor, it's important to remember to set your high glucose alarm thoughtfully—only set it to prompt you to take action. Overloading yourself with alarms can lead to desensitization over time, reducing their effectiveness when you need them most. However, it’s best to keep the low glucose and signal loss alarms on for safety.</span>

## **<u>5. Sharing data</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on sharing data with the FreeStyle Libre 2 sensor.

In this chapter, we explore the process of sharing data with your healthcare provider and loved ones, and creating reports.

### ***5.1 Sharing data with your healthcare provider***

<span class="mark">Your healthcare provider accesses your data through LibreView, Abbott's report and readout software. Both patient and professional versions are available, and you can create your account at libreview.com at any time.</span>

<span class="mark">In LibreView for professionals, you can create your practice and obtain an ID number. This number is needed to link to your patients' personal LibreView accounts. You have the option to change this ID number later for easier remembering and sharing with patients.</span>

<span class="mark">Data can be uploaded to LibreView in two ways, depending on your scanning device:</span>

<span class="mark">- If you use the reader, a manual upload is required. The reader can store up to 3 months of data. To upload, connect the reader to your PC using a USB cable and follow the instructions in LibreView. This can be done either by you as the user or by your healthcare provider. As a healthcare provider, you can upload the data under the patient’s name for easy retrieval.</span>

<span class="mark"></span>

<span class="mark">- If you use your mobile phone, all your data will be automatically sent to LibreView once you sign up through the LibreLink app. In the app, you can also enter your practice's ID number to join the practice. Once linked, real-time and remote tracking of patients is possible.</span>

***5.2 Create reports***

Once all data is synced to LibreView, you can generate various reports by selecting "glucose reports." Among the nine available reports, commonly used ones include:

- AGP report, providing TIR, TBR, and AGP profile for the last two weeks.

- Daily Log, offering insights into the timing and impact of individual insulin boluses on a day-to-day basis.

- Snapshot, displaying the frequency of sensor scans per day.

- And Device details, where alarm settings are found.

Interpretation guidance for the reports can be found in our general module on glucose sensors.

### ***5.3 Sharing data with family***

As <span class="mark">a family member or caregiver, you can download the LibreLinkUp app and connect it to the user's personal L</span>ibreview account.

To establish the connection:

- The user must approve data sharing by accessing the connected apps menu in the LibreLink app and selecting LibreLinkUp. Add the follower's name and email address, then press add.

- The follower can then download the LibreLinkUp app, create an account, and follow the provided instructions.

- The owner of the LibreLinkUp app can set his own notifications.

Embracing these sharing features enhances collaboration and informed decision-making for a more empowered journey towards better health.

## **<u>6. Connecting other devices</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your FreeStyle Libre 2 sensor to other devices.

In this chapter, we will explore the exciting possibilities of connecting your FreeStyle Libre <span class="mark">2 and FreeStyle Libre 2 Plus se</span>nsors with various devices, such as smartwatches, smart pens, and insulin pumps.

### **6.1 Smartwatches**

<span class="mark">Currently, there is no native application that directly connects the FreeStyle LibreLink or LibreLinkUp apps to smartwatches. However, most smartwatches can mirror smartphone notifications, including glucose alerts and other app notifications.</span>

<span class="mark">Since there is limited experience with the FreeStyle Libre 2 Plus sensors, we will focus on how to view real-time glucose values from the regular FreeStyle Libre 2 sensor on your smartwatch.</span>

<span class="mark">As the regular FreeStyle Libre 2 sensor is a flash glucose monitor, you will first need to use open-source apps to capture the real-time glucose data on your smartphone. You can then use these or other open-source apps to send the real-time glucose data to your smartwatch.</span>

<span class="mark">It's important to note that open-source apps are not supported by Abbott, are not approved by any medical authorities, and their use is at your own risk. We are simply providing this information so that you are aware of the possibilities if this feature is important to you.</span>

<span class="mark">Additionally, these apps typically do not transmit sensor data to LibreView. Therefore, if you want a comprehensive graph in LibreView, you will still need to scan your FreeStyle Libre 2 sensor using the official LibreLink app every 8 hours. Juggluco is an exception, as it can be configured to send data to LibreView.</span>

<span class="mark">To get your glucose data on your smartwatch, the first step is to get real-time glucose data on your phone. Here's how you can use open-source apps to achieve this with your FreeStyle Libre 2 sensor:</span>

<span class="mark">For European FreeStyle Libre 2 sensors, you can use an xDrip-like app such as Juggluco or Diabox for Android phones, or Shuggah for iPhones. For US or Canadian FreeStyle Libre 2 sensors, you can use Juggluco.</span>

<span class="mark">xDrip-like apps can only pick up the glucose sensor data after the sensor has been started via the LibreLink app. So, you’ll first need to scan your sensor with the FreeStyle LibreLink app to start it. One hour after starting the sensor, disconnect the sensor from your phone by disabling the LibreLink app in the Bluetooth settings, and then scan the sensor with your xDrip-like app.</span>

<span class="mark">Juggluco offers the added benefit of allowing you to start FreeStyle Libre 2 sensors directly in the app if you’re using European FreeStyle Libre 2 sensors.</span>

<span class="mark">Once you have your real-time glucose data on your phone, you can use your xDrip app to send the data to your smartwatch using the same or other open-source apps.</span>

<span class="mark">Let’s now explore how this functionality works with three common types of smartwatches: Garmin, Samsung Galaxy, and Apple Watch.</span>

#### *\#1 Garmin Smartwatch*

First up is the Garmin Smartwatch:

- Install the Garmin Connect app and the Garmin Connect IQ app on your Android phone. (For iPhone, additional steps are required.)

- Download the Diabox app from diaboxapp.com and configure it to share data with smartwatches in the settings menu.

- Install the xDrip+/Spike/Nightscout Watch Face, Widget, or Data Field from the Connect IQ store on your Garmin watch.

- Alternatively, if you have a Garmin Edge cycling computer, you can use the xDrip+/Spike/Nightscout Data Field feature to display Libre2 data on it.

#### *\#2 Samsung Galaxy Smartwatch (4 or 5)*

Next is the Samsung Galaxy Watch:

- Install the [<u>Galaxy Wearable app</u>](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.samsung.android.app.watchmanager&hl=en_US) on your Android phone.

- Download the Diabox app from [<u>diaboxapp.com</u>](https://diaboxapp.com/) and configure it to share data with GWatch.

- Install the [<u>G-Watch Wear app</u>](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=sk.trupici.gwatch.wear&hl=nl&gl=US) on both your phone and your smartwatch. Set Diabox as the data source in the app settings.

- Optionally, you can use the Wearable Widgets app along with G-Watch Wear to display Libre 2 data as a complication or a tile on your Samsung Galaxy smartwatch.

#### *\#3 Apple Watch*

Finally, let's discuss the integration with the Apple Watch:

- Download the Shuggah app from the App Store on your iPhone and scan the sensor using the Shuggah app.

- Open the Watch app on your iPhone, find the Shuggah app, and install it on your Apple Watch.

- Once installed, access the Shuggah app on your watch by locating it among the app icons. Tapping on the Shuggah app will display your Libre sensor's glucose level and trend arrow.

- For easier access to your glucose data, you can integrate the Shuggah logo as a complication on a modular watch face.

- If you prefer real-time glucose data without tapping on your watch, you can configure the Shuggah app to create calendar events. In the app settings, toggle on the "create calendar events" option. On a modular or photo watch face, change the middle complication to "my calendar" to see your real-time sensor data.

- Note: The Shuggah app uses its own algorithm to calculate blood glucose readings, which may result in slight differences compared to your Libre 2 readings.

It's evident that the steps vary based on the type of phone and smartwatch you want to connect. Multiple possibilities exist to achieve the desired result. If you find it challenging to follow abstract instructions, don't worry. The downloads provided include a PDF with links to tutorials for each step along the way. It's often easier to perform the setup when you have the devices on hand rather than trying to comprehend it in an abstract form.
