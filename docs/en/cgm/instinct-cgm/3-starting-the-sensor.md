## **3. Starting the sensor (7.5 min)**

Welcome to this lesson on starting the Instinct sensor. In this video, you'll learn how to start the sensor and navigate the pump and the app. In addition, we'll go over some practical tips for living with your sensor. Let’s dive in.

### ***3.1 Starting the sensor***

After applying the sensor, tap Next in the app. You can follow the “How to scan a sensor” steps in the app — which adjusts automatically depending if you’re using an iOS or Android device — or scan on your own. Make sure your pump is on the Home screen, with no active alarms or alerts. Then hold your phone near the sensor. If you’re using an iOS device, hold the top of your device near the sensor. If you’re using an Android device, hold the back of your device near the sensor. Once you hear the beep or feel the vibration, this confirms that the sensor has been started and begins the warm-up period.

When the sensor is starting, keep your pump close to the sensor until pairing is complete. You’ll see a warm-up message on both the pump and app home screen. Once pairing is successful, the connection icon turns green. Once the countdown ends, your pump will begin receiving sensor glucose (SG) readings.

### ***3.2 The MiniMed™ 780G pump***

The MiniMed™ 780G pump's Sensor feature automatically turns on when your Instinct sensor is paired to the pump. When the Sensor feature is active, the Home screen with CGM in Manual Mode displays your SG reading, as well as a trend arrow and a real-time graph. Also displayed on the pump Home screen are status icons, the Suspend Icon, and Active Insulin.

When the pump is using the SmartGuard feature, the Home screen displays a shield with your current SG, if available.

In the Manual Mode sensor display, trend arrows indicate the rate at which the most recent SG readings are rising or falling. They could be single, double, or triple downward or upward arrows; the more arrows that are shown, the faster the SG value is changing. It's also possible for trend arrows to be absent, if the rate of SG change is less than 1 mg/dL per minute, or if the sensor may be outside of operating limits.

As for the sensor graph, it includes the following information:

- The most recent SG reading;

- Historical SG readings for the last 3-hour, 6-hour, 12-hour, or 24-hour periods;

- High and low SG limits;

- Carb entries;

- Delivered boluses;

- Suspend events; and

- Blood Glucose (BG) entries.

The sensor graph may also show a dotted line to represent backfilled SG values, which will be recorded anytime the sensor is out of wireless range from the pump.

To view the sensor graph from the home screen, press the Graph button on the pump. A full-screen view of the 3-hour graph appears. Press the Up button to navigate to the 6-hour, 12-hour, and 24-hour graphs. Press Left to view the SG readings and event details. To exit the full-screen view, press Back or press the Graph button again.

### ***3.3 The MiniMed™ Mobile app*** 

After downloading the MiniMed<sup>TM</sup> Mobile app on the Apple or Google Play app store, follow the prompts on the screen that explains how the app works. Create or sign in to your CareLink™ personal account. Read the agreement and tap ‘Agree’. Follow the prompts and select ‘Get started’. Select your pump. Pair your pump by selecting ‘Allow’. Allow use of Bluetooth by selecting ‘OK’. Read the Dosing Disclaimer and tap ‘Agree’. The app is now ready to use.

The MiniMed<sup>TM</sup> Mobile app Home screen shows an overview of data received from the pump. The Home screen is divided into two main areas: the status area and the graph. There is also a Menu button at the top of the Home screen.

The status area shows the most current data that the pump has sent to the MiniMed<sup>TM</sup> Mobile app and status messages that may require your attention. It shows your current SG value, trend arrows, active insulin, basal rate, status messages, and status icons.

The graph shows glucose values and event markers based on the data the app receives from the paired pump. To navigate the graph:

- Swipe left or right over the graph to navigate through past and current data.

- Stretch and pinch to zoom in and out from the 1-hour view to a 24-hour view.

- Double-tap the graph to cycle through the various graph zoom levels, namely 24 hour, 12 hours, 6 hours, and 3 hours.

If you turn on high and low SG limits on the pump, the limits appear on the graph. The high limit line appears in orange, and the low limit line appears in red. The SG target range of 70 to 180 mg/dL or 3.9 to 10 mmol/L appears as a green band across the graph.

From the Home screen, swipe left to access your Time in Range (TIR) data and graphs.

Tap on Menu on the Home screen. The Menu screen shows the status of the insulin pump system and paired devices. The Menu screen also gives you access to the Notifications settings, Display Options, Pair Device function, Sync to CareLink<sup>TM</sup> feature, Update pump feature, and About screen.

It's important to note that the MiniMed<sup>TM</sup> Mobile app is designed as a viewing tool and does not have the functionality to administer boluses or modify settings. Its primary purpose is to provide users with access to their Instinct sensor and insulin data, allowing them to monitor their glucose levels, insulin delivery, and Time in Range. Any adjustments or actions regarding bolus administration or settings should be made directly on the insulin pump itself.

### ***3.4 Do's & don’ts***

Now, we’ll outline some important tips when using the Instinct CGM.

- The sensor transmission range is 33 feet (or 10 meters) when unobstructed.

- The sensor is water-resistant and can be worn while bathing, showering, or swimming. However, do not take your sensor deeper than 3 feet (or 1 meter) or immerse it longer than 30 minutes in water.

- Taking more than 1000 mg of Vitamin C per day may falsely raise your sensor readings, which could cause you to miss a severe low glucose event.

- You can safely have an MRI exam while wearing your sensor. Sensor readings may be inaccurate during the MRI, but sensor function returns fully back to normal after 1 hour.

- The Instinct CGM cannot be calibrated. But when the sensor is paired with the MiniMed<sup>TM</sup> 780G, the system uses BG meter readings as a BG check to verify system performance.

Carefully work through the troubleshooting guidance, and reach out to Medtronic Customer Support where appropriate. With your expertise and dedication, you are well equipped to help your patients fully benefit from their CGM.
