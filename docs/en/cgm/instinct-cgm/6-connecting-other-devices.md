## **<u>6. Connecting other devices (4.5 min)</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your Instinct CGM to other devices. In this video, we’ll tackle integrating your Instinct sensor with smartwatches, and smart insulin pens and pumps. Let’s proceed.

### ***6.1 Smartwatches***

The MiniMed™ Mobile app is compatible with Apple Watch. You can monitor your values on your watch face or tap to enter the app. The watch communicates with the iPhone device, not the insulin pump, so if your iPhone is out of range, you will not get updated glucose information on your watch.

To install MiniMed™ Mobile on your Apple Watch, make sure your Apple Watch and iPhone device are connected. You can check this in your Bluetooth settings. Download the MiniMed™ Mobile app from the App Store on your iPhone if you haven’t done so already. Next, you need the companion app on your Apple Watch. If this does not happen automatically, there are two ways of doing it manually.

First is to get the app via your Watch. Press the digital crown button to see the home screen, then tap the App Store online store. Tap Search and enter “MiniMed Mobile.” Find the right app with the MiniMed Mobile icon and tap get.

The second option is to get the app via your iPhone. On your iPhone device, open the Watch app. Tap the My Watch tab. Tap Install next to the MiniMed™ Mobile app.

<span class="mark">Access the MiniMed app on your Apple Watch to view your glycemia within your SmartGuard shield, and swiping left will display your sensor graph and Time In Range.</span>

<span class="mark">To have easier access to your glucose data, you can integrate your sensor glycemia and SmartGuard Shield as a complication on compatible watch faces. These watch faces include Portrait, Nike (hybrid), Breathe, Utility, Simple, Photos, Motion, Gradient, XL, and Astronomy. Clicking on the complication will allow you to access the full MiniMed Watch app.</span>

On the other hand, connectivity for Android watches requires the use of a third-party app like xDrip+. Please be aware that xDrip+ is not supported by Medtronic and lacks approval from regulatory authorities like the FDA or EMA, making its usage carry inherent risks that should be approached cautiously.

***\#1 Garmin smartwatch***

For Garmin smartwatches, download the xDrip+ app from [<u>github.com/nightscoutfoundation/xdrip.</u>](http://github.com/nightscoutfoundation/xdrip.) Go to the settings of the xDrip+ app and set the data source to Companion app. This allows xDrip+ to receive sensor data from the MiniMed<sup>TM</sup> Mobile app. Also in the settings, adjust the inter-app settings to send the xDrip+ data to your connected Wear OS smartwatch. Then go to the Connect IQ store app on your phone, download the xDrip+/Spike/Nightscout Watch Face, and set xDrip+ as your companion app. On your watch, you can now choose the xDrip+/Spike/Nightscout watch face to see your real-time Instinct data on your Garmin smartwatch.

***\#2 Samsung Galaxy smartwatch***

For Samsung Galaxy smartwatches, follow similar steps by downloading the xDrip+ app and setting the data source to Companion App in the settings. Adjust the xDrip+ app settings to send data to the G-Watch App by checking the Smartwatch and Inter-app settings. Next, install the G-Watch Wear app on both your phone and smartwatch and set xDrip+ as your companion app. Finally, choose the according watch face on your Galaxy watch, and customise the watch face and alerts on your watch as you prefer.

As a reminder, using open-source apps carries inherent risks and is not supported by Medtronic or approved by regulatory authorities. We are sharing this information for transparency, but we do not recommend this practice unless you fully understand the associated risks.

### ***6.2 Smart insulin pens***

<span class="mark">The Instinct sensor is FDA-approved for use within the MiniMed™ Go Smart MDI System. At the time this course was published, the MiniMed™ Go system had not yet been launched.</span>

### ***6.3 Smart insulin pumps***

The Instinct CGM is fully integrated with the MiniMed™ 780G insulin pump, allowing for coordinated diabetes management.

In contrast, the Instinct sensor is not compatible with open-source automated insulin delivery systems.

In this video, we examined how to connect your Instinct sensor with supported smartwatches, smart pens, and insulin pumps. With valuable insights and real-time data from these integrations, you can fine-tune your diabetes management and better support your long-term health goals.
