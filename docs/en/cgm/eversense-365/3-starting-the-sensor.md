## **<u>3. Starting the sensor</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on starting the Eversense 365 sensor.

In this video, we will delve into linking the Eversense 365 sensor to the smart transmitter and getting started with the mobile app. We will also highlight key do’s and don’ts for using the Eversense 365 system.

### ***3.1 Smart transmitter***

The first thing to do is download the Eversense 365 CGM Mobile App from the App Store or Google Play. The Eversense 365 App can be downloaded on most mobile phones, with separate versions available for iOS and Android devices. You can find a list of all compatible devices and software versions on this webpage: https://www.eversensecgm.com/compatibility/

Open the app, then review and accept the End User License Agreement. If you already have an Eversense account, you can log in to the Eversense 365 app with your existing login credentials. Otherwise, tap "Create an account" and complete the Eversense account creation screens. Be sure to use an email address you can access. Take note of your Eversense account email and password, then tap "Submit."

Check your email for the account activation link. Click the link in your email to activate your account. Go back to the Eversense 365 CGM Mobile App, tap to log in, and enter the account email and password you just created.

Enable Notifications and Bluetooth to allow system communication and to receive system alerts. Confirm your unit of measurement and tap "Next." Review and confirm the app tutorial.

From the Welcome screen, you can pair your mobile phone with your smart transmitter. Make sure the transmitter is fully charged and powered on.

To start pairing mode, tap the soft-touch button three times on your smart transmitter until the light blinks blue. The smart transmitter serial number should match. Tap on the serial number and accept the pairing request. Once connected, tap "Next."

To begin the linking process, tap "Sensor." Hold the smart transmitter, without the adhesive patch, in place over the sensor to find the best signal. Tap "Next." Continue to hold your smart transmitter in place until the sensor linking is complete. This could take several minutes.

When the detected serial number appears, tap "Link detected sensor." You'll see the linking process progress. When the sensor is successfully linked, you can remove the smart transmitter from over the sensor. Tap "Next."

Now the 24-hour Warm-Up Phase begins; we also call this period “Day 1.” Note the date and time when the 24-hour Warm-up Phase will be complete.

Also, you won't need to wear your smart transmitter over your sensor during this phase. But it’s a great time to program your personalized glucose settings into your Eversense 365 CGM System.

After that, you can return your smart transmitter to the charger until the Warm-Up Phase is complete.

During the Warm-Up Phase, you won't be getting any glucose data. Continue to monitor your glucose levels with your blood glucose meter throughout this period and until you begin to receive glucose values from your Eversense 365 CGM.

When you receive the "Warm-Up Complete" notification, tap "Continue." Remove your smart transmitter from the charger. It will automatically reconnect with your mobile app. The System Setup screen will take you to the next step, the Initialization Phase.

To begin, get an adhesive patch and peel off the paper backing with the Eversense logo. The center of the patch is sticky, and this is where you should place your smart transmitter, as shown. Press the smart transmitter firmly onto the adhesive. Then, remove the larger piece of the remaining clear backing on the adhesive patch. Finally, position the smart transmitter over the sensor.

If you have an over wrap on your arm, you will need to remove it so that you can see the Tegaderm bandage. It’s helpful to understand where your sensor is located. Your health care provider inserted the sensor above the incision, as shown here. This means that the sensor is likely above the center of your Tegaderm bandage.

There may also be visible marks where your health care provider marked the corners of the smart transmitter before placing your sensor. Aligning the smart transmitter in this location helps to place it appropriately over the sensor. If needed, go to a mirror in order to clearly see your Tegaderm bandage.

Open the Placement Guide in your app by going to Main Menu — Placement Guide. You’ll see a black X at first.

Hold the smart transmitter with adhesive, with the sides of the adhesive patch pulled back. If visible, use the smart transmitter corner marks on your skin to position the smart transmitter toward the top half of the Tegaderm bandage. Note that the smart transmitter needs to be touching your arm. Gently lift and slightly move the smart transmitter plus adhesive to find the location with the strongest signal, somewhere between 2 to 3 bars or good to excellent. Rotating the smart transmitter slightly may help. When moving the transmitter, allow a few seconds for the signal to update before moving the transmitter again. Once you have obtained a signal of two to three bars, leave the smart transmitter on your arm there.

Remove the smaller remaining piece of clear backing and smooth out the adhesive patch. Finally, close the Placement Guide by tapping the Next icon.

Now that you’ve successfully positioned your smart transmitter, you’ll see a Calibrate Now notification appear in your mobile app within a few minutes. This means it’s time to do your first of four fingerstick calibrations.

You can complete the calibrations in as little as 6 hours, since you can calibrate as often as every 2 hours. You must enter calibrations no more than 12 hours apart. But you won’t have to remember the time for each calibration because your Eversense 365 CGM System will remind you each time with a Calibrate Now notification.

To enter a calibration, do a fingerstick blood glucose check and tap on the blue Calibrate button.

Tap on Glucose and scroll to enter the correct blood glucose value, then tap Done. Tap Submit to confirm that the correct glucose value has been selected and the fingerstick glucose test was performed no more than 10 minutes ago. You'll receive a Calibration in Progress notification which you can clear by tapping OK.

Here’s an example. Let’s say you linked your sensor and smart transmitter at 9:45 am on Monday, so your Warm-Up phase ends at 9:45 AM on Tuesday.

You use the Placement Guide to put the smart transmitter on your arm over the sensor. Around 10:00 AM on Tuesday, you receive your first "Calibrate Now" notification. You perform a fingerstick blood glucose check and enter the result into the mobile app.

Two hours later, the 2nd Calibrate Now notification appears, and once you enter that 2nd blood glucose value, sensor glucose data begins to appear on your My Glucose home screen.

Calibration prompts 3 and 4 will follow in the same fashion. But remember, you don’t have to calibrate right away – you have up to 12 hours to perform the next required calibration. But it is ideal to complete all 4 before going to bed, so that you will have the benefit of continuous data that first night

Once the initialization phase is complete, the system requires just 1 calibration per day for 13 days.

After day 13, the system requires only 1 calibration per week.

### ***3.2 Eversense 365 app***

The MY GLUCOSE screen is the main display screen for the app. The Menu icon at the top left corner provides easy navigation to other app features. The following menu items are available:

- Calibrate;

- Placement Guide;

- Event Log;

- Reports;

- Share My Data;

- Alert History;

- System Setup;

- Connect;

- Settings; and

- About.

If you have activated the Do Not Disturb feature or the Temporary Profile feature, these icons also appear in the top left. At the top center, the status bar provides important information about your current glucose and system status. Meanwhile, the icons at the top right indicate your smart transmitter ID, the strength of your smart connection with your sensor or mobile device, and the battery power left in your smart transmitter.

The current glucose reading is your real-time glucose level in the indicated unit of measurement. The reading is updated every 5 minutes.

The trend arrow shows the current direction of your glucose levels, and how fast they are changing. There are seven different trend arrows. The app uses the last 20 minutes of continuous glucose data for calculating glucose trends.

Below the current glucose reading and unit of measurement, you'll find the date and time, and further down, the glucose trend graph. In this graph, the red dashed lines indicate your high and low glucose alert levels, while the green dashed lines indicate your target range. Any event that you enter into your app, such as a calibration, a meal, exercise or insulin — or any alerts you receive — show up on your trend graph with an icon.

### ***3.3 Do's & don’ts***

Now, let's discuss some do's and don'ts for using the Eversense 365 System.

- Link the sensor and the smart transmitter on the day of insertion.

- If the smart transmitter has been turned off to avoid vibrations during the Warm-Up Phase, turn it back on at the 24th hour. It will take about 5 minutes after the smart transmitter is placed over the sensor for the first calibration prompt to be displayed.

- If possible, start System Initialization early in the day. This will allow you to complete all four required initialization calibrations before going to bed.

- You can remove your smart transmitter from your upper arm at any time, except during calibration. Keep your smart transmitter in place over the sensor for at least five minutes before and 15 minutes after each calibration.

- Always wash your hands well and dry them thoroughly before calibration.

- Avoid calibrating when glucose levels may be changing rapidly such as after meals, after taking insulin, or during or after exercise.

- The smart transmitter comes with a 12-month warranty. The system will notify you once the transmitter has been in use for over 365 days.

- Your package contains a USB charging kit and silicone-based adhesive patches. Use only the supplied AC adapter and USB cable. Using other power sources can damage the transmitter or cause fire risk.

- When fully charged, the transmitter battery lasts approximately 24 hours. The transmitter must be removed from your body for charging. Charge it daily for about 15 minutes (or until the LED turns green). Reattach it only after charging is complete.

- While the transmitter is off your body — during charging or for any other reason — no glucose data or alerts are generated, and once reattached, the app will not backfill the missing data. To limit data gaps, keep the transmitter off your body for as short a time as possible.

- The smart transmitter has a soft-touch button. To use the smart transmitter, touch and hold or tap your finger directly on this button, similar to using a smartphone. The soft-touch button cannot detect a touch through clothing.

  - To turn the smart transmitter ON, press and hold the soft touch button for 3 seconds. You’ll feel a quick vibration, and the LED will blink once, indicating the power is ON.

  - To turn the smart transmitter OFF, touch and hold your finger on the soft-touch button for about 5 seconds. The LED will turn on, you will feel a quick vibe, and the LED will turn off, signaling the power is OFF.

- The communication distance between the app and the smart transmitter is up to 24.9 <span class="mark">feet or 7.6 meter. But this range will decrease in water, such as when you are in a bathtub or pool. When the transmitter and mobile device are not within range of each other, any data gathered by the transmitter is stored and sent to the app when the two devices are back within range.</span>

- <span class="mark">The smart transmitter can only be in water up to 1 meter (or 3.2 feet) deep for 30 minutes max. Exposing the smart transmitter to conditions beyond this will result in damage and void your warranty.</span>

- It is safe for you to wear your sensor and smart transmitter when you go through metal detectors at airports. Let security know you have a medical device and show your Medical Device Notification Card.

- While flying, the smart transmitter performs similarly to any other Bluetooth device.

- Do not use the smart transmitter if it is damaged or cracked as this could result in electrical shock.

- Do not wear the smart transmitter during X-rays, CT scans, or MRI scans to avoid interference with results. The sensor itself can safely remain in place during these procedures.

- Do not exchange smart transmitters with another person. Each smart transmitter can be linked to only one sensor at a time.

- As for the adhesive patches, you'll have to replace them daily. You can remove and replace the same adhesive patch more than once a day, but repeated removal and replacement may reduce adhesion strength.

- You can choose from either white or clear variants of the patch. The white adhesive is perforated and may be better at wicking away moisture. The clear adhesive is best for discretion. Try both types to determine which one works best for you.

- Only apply the adhesives to clean, dry skin. Remove any lotion or soap before placing the adhesive patch and smart transmitter.

- Although the patches use a medical-grade, non-reactive silicone adhesive, they may still cause a reaction or skin irritation.

If you or your patients encounter difficulties, don't get discouraged. Stay persistent, explore troubleshooting options, and reach out to customer support for assistance.
