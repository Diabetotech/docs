## **<u>6. Connecting other devices</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your Eversense 365 sensor to other devices.

In this chapter, you'll learn how to integrate your sensor with smartwatches and explore possibilities with smartpens and insulin pumps.

### ***6.1 Smartwatches***

Within the Eversense 365 App, the Share My Data feature allows you to share your Eversense data with Apple Health. In the Menu options, tap Share My Data, then Application Sharing, and Apple Health. Turn on Share Data. You may also need to allow sharing via the health app.

You can view a snapshot of your CGM data on your Apple Watch. Once you’ve downloaded and installed the Eversense 365 App on your mobile device, follow the device’s instructions for adding the app to your watch.

Tap the Eversense 365 icon on your watch HOME screen to open the app. The My Glucose screen shows your current glucose with a trend arrow, and a trend graph of your last three hours of CGM data.

You can also access the MY GLUCOSE screen if you turn on notifications from the Eversense 365 App in your Apple Watch settings. When you receive a notification, you can also tap on the message to see the MY GLUCOSE screen.

Swipe left to the next screen showing a pie chart of your total time within and outside your target range for the past 24 hours. Swipe up to display the same data shown as percentages. Lastly, swipe left to the next screen showing your current glucose with trend arrow, your next calibration time, the current system calibration phase, and battery level of your smart transmitter.

Take note that the Apple Watch is a secondary display of Eversense data and should not be used in place of the Eversense 365 App display. Any alerts and notifications that appear on your Apple Watch should be confirmed within the mobile device app. Any problems with mobile devices, wireless internet, data connection, the Eversense DMS, the smart transmitter being out of range of their mobile device, or charging the smart transmitter may cause data transfer to be delayed or not to be displayed.

<span class="mark">On the other hand, connectivity for Android watches requires the use of a third-party app like xDrip+. Please be aware that xDrip+ is not supported by Sensionics and lacks approval from regulatory authorities like the FDA or EMA, making its usage carry inherent risks that should be approached cautiously.</span>

***<span class="mark">\#1 Garmin smartwatch</span>***

<span class="mark">For Garmin smartwatches, download the xDrip+ app from github.com/nightscoutfoundation/xdrip. Go to the settings of the xDrip+ app and set the data source to Eversense. This allows xDrip+ to receive sensor data from the Eversense 365 app. Also in the settings, adjust the inter-app settings to send the xDrip+ data to your connected Wear OS smartwatch. Then go to the Connect IQ store app on your phone, download the xDrip+/Spike/Nightscout Watch Face, and set xDrip+ as your companion app. On your watch, you can now choose the xDrip+/Spike/Nightscout watch face to see your real-time Eversense data on your Garmin smartwatch.</span>

***<span class="mark">\#2 Samsung Galaxy smartwatch</span>***

<span class="mark">For Samsung Galaxy smartwatches, follow similar steps by downloading the xDrip+ app and setting the data source to Eversense in the settings. Adjust the xDrip+ app settings to send data to the G-Watch App by checking the Smartwatch and Inter-app settings. Next, install the G-Watch Wear app on both your phone and smartwatch and set xDrip+ as your companion app. Finally, choose the according watch face on your Galaxy watch, and customise the watch face and alerts on your watch as you prefer.</span>

<span class="mark">As a reminder, using open-source apps carries inherent risks and is not supported by Sensionics or approved by regulatory authorities. We are sharing this information for transparency, but we do not recommend this practice unless you fully understand the associated risks.</span>

<span class="mark">Remember, the setup steps may vary based on your phone and smartwatch, but you can find detailed tutorials in the provided downloads, making it easier to perform the setup with the devices on hand.</span>

### ***6.2 Smartpens***

The Eversense 365 system currently does not offer direct integration with smart insulin pens, but there is a workaround available.

Beyond Apple Health, the Eversense 365 system integrates with Glooko . Because smart insulin pens like the Novopen 6, NovoPen Echo Plus, inPen, Tempo Smart, and SoloSmart can also connect to Glooko, you can consolidate both your glucose and insulin data on a single screen, although this integration requires manual setup.

### ***6.3 Smart insulin pumps***

The Eversense 365 will soon integrate with commercial AID systems, starting with the twiist AID system. For the latest updates, you can visit Eversense's partners page: https://www.eversensecgm.com/partners/

The Eversense CGM is also compatible with open-source AID systems such as AndroidAPS. iPhone-based open-source systems do not yet support Eversense.

In this lesson, you learned how to connect the Eversense 365 CGM to compatible smart devices and diabetes management platforms. Use these integrations to build a seamless setup that supports smarter decisions and more personalized diabetes care.
