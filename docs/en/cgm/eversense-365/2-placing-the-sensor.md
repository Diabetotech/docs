## **<u>2. Placing the sensor</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on placing the Eversense 365 sensor.

In this video, we’ll guide you through the process of inserting and removing the Eversense 365 sensor, and cover some do’s and don’ts.

### ***2.1 Supplies***

Let's begin by gathering the essential supplies:

- A sensor kit, which contains the sensor within a sensor holder;

- An insertion tools kit, where you'll find the incision template, the blunt dissector, and the insertion tool; and

- A transmitter kit, which houses the smart transmitter.

You’ll also need:

- Sterile, non-latex surgical gloves of your size;

- Chlorhexidine OR Betadine solution;

- A sterile drape, roughly 22 by 25 inches or 50 by 60 centimeters — with a hole in it;

- A surgical skin marker;

- 2 mL of lidocaine hydrochloride with epinephrine;

- A sterile syringe and needle;

- A sterile towel drape;

- 10 mL of 0.9% sterile saline-filled syringe;

- A disposable sterile scalpel (number 15);

- Sterile gauze pads;

- A sterile surgical clamp (of 10 to 16 cm);

- Steri-Strip™ adhesive skin closure; and

- Tegaderm™ + pad film dressing.

<span class="mark">The sterile drapes are in the kits supplied by Senseonics.</span>

### ***2.2 Inserting the sensor*** 

[<u>Eversense Placement & Removal – Training Video on Vimeo</u>](https://vimeo.com/1086098653?fl=pl&fe=sh)

Sensor placement is done while the patient is fully awake. The entire procedure takes about five minutes and consists of five steps.

1\. Site preparation

2\. Incision

3\. Sensor insertion

4\. Site closure

5\. Sensor and smart transmitter linking

Begin by washing your hands. Next, identify the location for the sensor placement. Ideally, the insertion area is approximately at the midway point between the acromion process and the lateral epicondyle, parallel to the humerus, in the subcutaneous tissue. Ensure that the selected insertion site allows the smart transmitter to stay flat during movement and that the patient can easily reach it. Using the transmitter, mark the edges on the skin, then align the incision template inside the marked lines. Use the incision template’s slots to mark a vertical sensor pocket path and horizontal incision line. Place the patient in a reclined position preferably on their side, with the upper arm on a flat horizontal plane.

Now that the patient has been positioned, let's move to sensor preparation.

Set up a flat clean surface designated as the sterile field. <span class="mark">Put on the surgical gloves and present the tool tray.</span>

Remove the red locking tab from the white sensor insertion tool by pulling it back. Loosen the tools from the tray.

Cycle and test the blue slide forward and back twice. Test the lock, and leave the blue slide in the forward and lock position.

Snap the insertion tool back into the tray. Wet the cannula by filling the preformed well with approximately 10 mL of sterile saline to completely cover the cannula.

Place the sensor holder on a flat clean surface designated as the sterile field. Unsnap the insertion tool. Pick up the sensor holder and squeeze the ridges. Remove and discard the cap.

Next, keeping the insertion tool in the slightly upright position, retract the blue slide. Firmly support the tip of the sensor holder. Align slots of holder and the tool. Align the arrows and slide onto the cannula until it clicks.

Cover the sensor by slowly advancing the blue slide. Squeeze the ridges and remove the holder. Visually check the tip of the cannula for the sensor.

Snap the insertion tool into place, and hydrate for five minutes.

With the sensor prepared, you’re ready to insert it.

Start by scrubbing the antiseptic at the incision line and working outward in circles. Repeat this process two more times, for three total passes. Allow the antiseptic to dry between passes.

When dry, drape the patient. Inject lid<span class="mark">ocaine with epinephrine at 2</span>0 degrees. Bevel up, injectin<span class="mark">g 1 milliliter at the incision line and 1</span> milliliter up the sensor pocket path. Apply light pressure over the area. Wait 1½ to 2 minutes and check effect.

Hold the scalpel with a pencil grip. Punch through the dermal layer to a depth of 5 millimeters. Remove the scalpel. Rotate 180 degrees, reinsert, and advance for uniform edges. Apply pressure to control any bleeding.

Hold the blunt dissector tool with the index finger resting in the finger well. Avoid placing the fingers underneath.

Introduce into the midline of the incision at a 45-degree angle. When the depth guards touch the skin, drop the tool flat. The depth guards assist with proper placement in the subcutaneous tissue. Rotate the tool slightly as you advance the entire length.

Keep the tool in the pocket for 30 to 45 seconds to decrease bleeding. Remove the tool and apply pressure to control bleeding.

Hold the insertion tool with fingers on the side, not underneath. Introduce the insertion tool under the lip of the incision into the subcutaneous pocket. Using a 45- to 90-degree angle, drop the tool flat. Follow the same pocket and advance to between the marks on the cannula. Stabilize the tool with the dominant hand. Use the other hand to unlock the blue slide at the widest end and slide back. This deploys the sensor into the pocket.

Remove the tool, and check to make sure that the sensor is not in the cannula. Remember, a correctly placed sensor will be in the subcutaneous tissue parallel to the humerus.

Apply pressure for several minutes as needed to control any bleeding.

<span class="mark">Align the edges of the incision and apply 3 to 4 parallel Steri-Strips to fully close the wound. If desired, place 2 additional Steri-Strips in a criss-cross pattern over the top.</span> Cover with Tegaderm, ensuring the Steri-Strips are completely under the gauze pad.

Lastly, link the sensor and the transmitter. We’ll go into detail on linking these two components in the next lesson.

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

[<u>Eversense Placement & Removal – Training Video on Vimeo</u>](https://vimeo.com/1086098653?fl=pl&fe=sh)

Remember that removal of the Eversense 365 sensor takes place up to one year after its placement.

Like sensor insertion, removal takes place while the patient is fully awake. The approximately 5-minute process involves four steps, namely:

1\. Site preparation

2\. Incision

3\. Sensor removal

4\. Site closure

Start with the patient in the upright position. Assess the sensor location and clean the skin. Make a reference dot on each end of the sensor.

Place the patient in a lateral recumbent position with the upper arm on a flat horizontal plane and sensor markings facing up toward the ceiling.

With the reference dots as a guide, use the placement guide to find an excellent signal. Mark the transmitter edges.

Using the reference dots, palpate and mark the sensor ends with horizontal lines. Draw a rectangle around the sensor and a horizontal line through the middle of the rectangle. Mark the incision line about 3 to 5 millimeters below the distal end of the sensor and 8 millimeters in width.

Now scrub the antiseptic at the incision line, working outward in circles. Repeat this process two more times for three total passes. Allow the antiseptic to dry between passes.

When dry, drape the patient. Inject lidocaine at 20 degrees bevel up, injecting 1 milliliter at the incision line, and 1 milliliters on each side of the sensor. Apply light pressure over the area. Wait 1½ to 2 minutes, and check effect.

Holding the scalpel with the pencil grip, pop through the dermal layer, and make an 8-millimeter-wide incision. Remove the scalpel, rotate 180 degrees, reinsert, and advance for uniform edges. Apply pressure to control any bleeding.

Stretch the skin taut using the thumb and index finger of the non-dominant hand. Push down on the lower lip of the incision as you introduce the clamp tip at 30 degrees. Open and close the clamp to the width of the incision 3 to 5 times.

Now, with the clamp open the full width of the incision, maintain gentle pressure on the lower lip and advance to the horizontal line. Close the clamp. If there is no gap in the clamp jaws, the sensor hasn’t been grasped. Maintain the clamp location and lift the wrist slightly to change the clamp angle, and close. A gap indicates you have the sensor.

Release the thumb and finger tension. Make a quarter turn of the wrist and remove the sensor.

Apply pressure to the incision for several minutes as needed to control any bleeding.

<span class="mark">Align the edges of the incision and apply 3 to 4 parallel Steri-Strips to fully close the wound. If desired, place 2 additional Steri-Strips in a criss-cross pattern over the top.</span> Cover With Tegaderm. Make sure the Steri-Strips are completely under the gauze pad.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

Let's go over some do’s and don’ts when placing the Eversense 365 sensor.

- <span class="mark">Be aware that you need to be properly trained and certified by Sensionics before performing the insertion procedure.</span>

- Choose the insertion site such that the patient is comfortable wearing the smart transmitter 24/7.

- Ensure the insertion area is not too far to the side to allow the patient to easily apply the adhesive patch.

- Avoid areas with loose skin, such as the back of the arm, as well as areas with scar tissue, tattoo, nevus, or apparent or noticeable blood vessels that could be incised.

- Before inserting the sensor, confirm that the patient does not have allergies to the antiseptic and lidocaine to be used during insertion.

- Insert the sensor using only the insertion tools provided in the insertion tool kit. Other insertion tools may damage the sensor.

- <span class="mark">Pinch and tent the skin to aid in forming a small pocket in the skin for insertion.</span>

- <span class="mark">Do not create a pocket more than 3-5 mm below the skin. If the sensor is placed too deep, it may be difficult to communicate with the smart transmitter or to remove later.</span>

- <span class="mark">Make sure that the subcutaneous pocket is parallel to and along the same axis as the humerus bone. When you insert the sensor, it should be level in the pocket, which will facilitate communication between the sensor and the smart transmitter.</span>

- <span class="mark">Link the sensor and the smart transmitter on the day of insertion. Failure to link the sensor and smart transmitter could lead to a delay in receiving glucose readings.</span>

- <span class="mark">Finally, alternate arms for next insertion sites.</span>

We’ve now covered all the steps for the Eversense 365 sensor insertion and removal, from preparing the insertion site to closing the incision, as well as do’s and don’ts. By mastering this procedure, you are building a solid foundation for reliable, long-term monitoring, which ensures accurate data that empowers you to make the most informed treatment decisions.
