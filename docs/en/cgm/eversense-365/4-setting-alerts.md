## **<u>4. Setting alerts</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alerts with the Eversense 365 sensor.

In this session, we will explore available alerts and notifications with the Eversense 365 system and guide you through the process of setting them on the mobile app. Let's dive right in!

### ***4.1 Available alerts***

The Eversense 365 system gives you alerts on your glucose readings in three ways: vibrations of your smart transmitter, sounds from your mobile device, and visual notifications on the My Glucose screen. For example, if your glucose falls to, or below, your low glucose alert level, you’ll feel your smart transmitter vibrate in a unique pattern, hear a sound from your mobile device, and see the alert pop-up on your app.

In addition to low, high, and out-of-range glucose alerts, you can receive predictive and rate of change alerts with the Eversense 365. Predictive alerts allow you to receive an “early” warning 10, 20, or 30 minutes before your sensor glucose level is expected to cross the Low or High Glucose Alert threshold, based on current glucose trends. On the other hand, you can get Rate of Change Alerts when your sensor glucose level is rising or falling faster than the Rate of Change threshold you’ve set. If you are new to CGM, wait to set predictive or rate-of-change alerts until you are accustomed to wearing your system.

Another alert is the Calibrate Now alert. The Eversense 365 system maintains accuracy during the 13 days through once-daily calibrations. Then, starting at day 14, your system prompts you to calibrate once weekly. It will alert you with the Calibrate Now alert when a calibration is needed. Please note that when you do receive the Calibrate Now alert, you have a 24-hour window to complete the calibration to maintain uninterrupted access to your CGM data.

Besides alerts on your glucose levels, you may also receive system status alerts, including alerts on your sensor, smart transmitter, Eversense 365 account, and Internet connection.

With the smart transmitter, there are four vibration patterns dedicated to high-priority glucose alerts. The transmitter also uses four other patterns for lower-priority alerts and notifications. You'll keep receiving these on-body vibe alerts even when you're away from your mobile device. But keep in mind that you cannot change any of these patterns.

Also, take note that alerts are different from notifications. An alert is an important message that needs your attention. Alerts appear in yellow on your app. In contrast, a notification is a low priority message, such as a calibration reminder, that appears in blue.

If you ever need to look back and review any alert or notification that you have received, just go to the Main Menu and tap Alert History. The ALERT HISTORY screen will list ALL alerts and notifications for that day. Tap on any message to get more information. Alternatively, you can choose to review only certain messages by tapping the icons on the top of the screen.

### ***4.2 Setting alerts on the Eversense 365 app***

You can choose different ringtones for low vs. high alerts and determine how often they repeat. Let's say you want to customize the tone you hear for the Low Glucose Alert. To do this, tap Menu, then, Settings, and Sound Settings. Tap the different tones that are available to listen to a preview. Tones should get your attention and be different from any other sound notifications you use with other apps.

By setting the snooze alert, you can set how often an alert repeats after you have received a Low Glucose and High Glucose alert. Tap each snooze alert to set how often the alert repeats, then tap Done when complete.

If your mobile device is running iOS 12 and above or Android 6 and above, you can optionally allow Low and Out of Range Low Glucose alerts to always override your mobile device sound settings. When turned on, the Low and Out of Range Low Glucose alerts will be played at your phone's maximum volume. You cannot change the volume level for this override setting. This allows you to still receive Low and Out of Range Low Glucose alerts on your phone, even if your phone's Silent Mode is on, or Do Not Disturb is on. This feature works similar to apps with an alarm clock feature that will always sound regardless of your phone sound settings. You must allow this in your mobile device.

For iOS devices, start by tapping the button to turn on the override. Tap Settings, then tap Allow.

For Android devices, tap the button to turn on the override. Tap settings, then tap on Eversense 365 from the Do Not Disturb access page.

On a final note, turn on only the alerts that fit your diabetes management plan and your daily life. If you're unsure where to start, talk to your healthcare provider for advice on choosing and setting the right alerts. They can help you get the most out of your Eversense 365 system. Start with the most basic alerts, like low and high glucose alerts. You can add more alerts to your routine later. This helps you slowly adjust to the system.

In this lesson, we've examined the Eversense 365 alert system. By correctly utilizing its features and staying resilient, we can set and rely on alerts to signal critical shifts in our glucose levels, enabling us to take the necessary action to stay in control.
