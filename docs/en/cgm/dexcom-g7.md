## **<u>2. Placing the sensor</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on placing the Dexcom G7 sensor.

In this video, we will focus on correctly placing and removing the Dexcom G7 sensor to ensure accurate readings and comfortable wear. Let's walk through the process step by step.

### ***2.1 Supplies***

To successfully place the sensor, gather the following supplies: a sensor applicator with a preloaded Dexcom G7 sensor, and alcohol wipes for proper hygiene.

### ***2.2 Inserting the sensor*** 

To insert your sensor, first choose your sensor site. In the US the Dexcom G7 sensor is approved for placement on the back of the upper arm. <span class="mark">Children from 2 to 6 years may place the sensor on the buttocks.</span> In Europe, it is also approved for use on the abdomen for both children and adults.

Before you begin, wash and dry your hands. Clean your site with an alcohol wipe and let it dry for at least 10 seconds. Grab your applicator and unscrew the cap. Don't touch inside the applicator. Press and hold the applicator firmly against your skin until the clear safety guard is pushed in**.** Now push the button and remove the applicator. To help keep the patch on your skin, rub firmly around the patch three times, then press gently on the sensor for 10 seconds. Screw the cap back on the applicator.

Your overpatch will help you keep the sensor on, and is bundled with your instructions in the box. Carefully pull off the clear liners one at a time. Please don't touch the white adhesive area. Use the colored tab to place the overpatch around the sensor. <span class="mark">Using a mirror will make the overpatch application much easier.</span> Rub around the over patch. Use the tab to peel off the colored liner, then rub around the overpatch. <span class="mark">Avoid getting the adhesive patch wet, including in the shower, for 12 hours after application.</span>

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

When it's time to remove the sensor, carefully peel off the patch like a bandage. You can use body- or babyoil to help you with the removal.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

Here are a few key tips to keep in mind:

- On the box, you'll find various numbers. The one marked with "21" is the serial number, while the number prefixed with "10" is the lot number. You might need these numbers when asking for a replacement sensor. So it’s best to keep the box or take a picture of it.

- On the applicator, you can find a 4-digit pairing code, which you'll require to link your sensor to your desired data-receiving device. It's a good practice to either keep the applicator or take a picture of it for reference. Once connected, you can also find the pairing code on your Dexcom G7 app.

- If you ever observe the sensor wire disconnecting from the insertion needle, it indicates an issue with your applicator. This problem may arise from the applicator being dropped or damaged. In such situations, it is recommended to request a replacement sensor, as the sensor placement will not be successful otherwise. Do not use the damaged sensor.

- When placing t<span class="mark">he sensor, do not forget to press the applicator firmly against the skin until the clear safety guard is pushed in. Otherwise it will not be possible to press the white button on the applicator.</span>

- <span class="mark">If you notice the sensor filament protruding from the sensor after insertion, this is referred to as *goosenecking* and is a known malfunction. The sensor will not function correctly. It should be removed, and a free replacement can be requested from Dexcom.</span>

- <span class="mark">If your Dexcom G7 system detects that the sensor can no longer determine your glucose reading, you may receive a Brief Sensor Issue alert or a Sensor Failed alert.</span>

  - The Brief Sensor Issue alert means that the sensor is temporarily unable to measure glucose. If this issue occurs, it often happens during the first day of a sensor session, but it can happen anytime. It usually fixes itself within 3 hours. If it continues for more than 3 hours, it’s better to contact technical support.

  - The Brief Sensor Issue may lead to a Sensor Failed alert. <span class="mark">This means that the sensor is unable to measure glucose. If you receive this alert, you must remove your sensor and begin a new session.</span>

  - <span class="mark">To help prevent future sensor failures, make sure you use a wear location with enough fat to avoid muscle tissue. Make sure you clean your sensor site with soap and an alcohol wipe and let dry completely before you insert. And make sure you use the Dexcom overpatch and keep the sensor securely adhered to your skin, so the sensor wire doesn’t come out.</span>

- <span class="mark">Dexcom officially requires use of the overpatch, but it is</span> not necessary for everyone, especially if you don't sweat a lot or plan on swimming with the sensor. If you're not a fan of the white-colored Dexcom overpatches, you can buy clear ones online or at the pharmacy. <span class="mark">There are medical grade patch options in skin tone or colors sold by companies like Skin Grip.</span>

By following these guidelines and being resilient in the face of challenges, you can easily and confidently place and remove your Dexcom G7 sensor. The process of placing the sensor is simple, and we anticipate no issues. However, if you encounter any technical challenges, please don't hesitate to contact your local Dexcom Technical Support for assistance.

## **<u>3. Starting the sensor</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on starting and using the Dexcom G7 sensor.

As soon as you apply the Dexcom G7 sensor, it automatically activates, and the 30-minute warm-up period begins. <span class="mark">You may apply a new sensor while an older sensor is working and activate the new one whenever you choose. Keep in mind the 10 day wear period begins once the sensor exits the applicator.</span>

To access the data from your Dexcom G7 sensor, you will need to make a connection between the sensor and either your smartphone or a Dexcom G7 receiver, using the provided pairing code. You can simultaneously use both the Dexcom G7 app and receiver.

If you're using the Dexcom G7 sensor in conjunction with an automated insulin delivery system, the process of connecting the sensor may be integrated into the system, eliminating the need for the Dexcom G7 app or receiver.

To ensure your phone or receiver receives the sensor data, they should always be within a 6-meter or 20-foot range of the sensor.

In this video, we will guide you through the steps to connect your Dexcom G7 sensor to your Dexcom G7 receiver and Dexcom G7 app and how to use the Dexcom G7 receiver and app.

### ***3.1 Dexcom G7 receiver***

The Dexcom G7 receiver has a coloured screen and four navigation buttons on the front: Select, Scroll down, Go back and Scroll up.

At the bottom, you will find a microUSB port for charging and data transfer. It is recommended to charge the receiver using the provided cable. Full charge can take up to 3 hours, but the <span class="mark">battery can last for</span> up to 7 days of use on a single battery charge<span class="mark">.</span>

To turn on your receiver, press and hold the select button for three seconds.

The receiver walks you through the entire setup process step by step. Select next to continue. First you will accept the Dexcom terms of use and privacy policy, and set your date and time. The receiver will then display a series of educational screens so you can learn everything you need to know about your G7 receiver. This mini tutorial will teach you all about safety, treatment decisions, trend arrows, alerts and readings on your home screen.

After learning all about your new G7 receiver, you will need to confirm your sensor is inserted. You will then enter the pairing code. You will find the four digit pairing code on the applicator you use to insert the sensor. Wait for your sensor to pair. Once pairing is complete, select finish and you will enter into your 30 minute sensor warm-up. The sensor warm-up timer lets you know when you'll start getting readings and alerts.

After your warm up you will start getting readings and alerts.

The receiver home screen will display:

- The most recent sensor glucose reading and trend arrow.

  - If your glucose is below 40 mg/dl or 2.2 mmol/L, it will show as "LOW." If it exceeds 400 mg/dl or 22.2 mmol/L, it will display as "HIGH." In such cases, it is recommended to confirm your glucose level with a finger prick and take appropriate action for low or high glucose.

- The sensor glucose graph is displayed below the reading.

  - You can adjust the time frame for the graph and access the menu using the buttons on the device.

Within the menu, you can explore options such as reports, events, settings, information, help, replace sensor, stop sensor session, or turn off the receiver. It is advisable to keep the receiver turned on to receive important alerts.

For a comprehensive understanding of these menu choices, we suggest downloading the menu overview provided below this video and reviewing all the available options.

### ***3.2 Dexcom G7 app***

The Dexcom G7 app can be downloaded on most mobile phones, with separate versions available for Android and iPhone devices. You can find a list of all compatible devices and software versions on this website: dexcom.com/compatibility

The Dexcom G7 app has minimal impact on battery life during regular use. However, it is recommended to keep the battery Saver Mode off, because this can cause the Dexcom G7 alerts to be delayed. It is also recommended to keep the “Adaptive Battery”, “Battery Optimizing”, and “Optimized Battery Charging” setting off, as this can prevent the Dexcom G7 app from running in the background. <span class="mark">If the Dexcom G7 app is not running in the background, you won't get alerts.</span>

<span class="mark">The Dexcom G7 app uses Bluetooth to connect with your sensor. You must keep your device’s Bluetooth turned on to get sensor readings and alerts. It is also good practice to delete old Dexcom G7 sensors from your device’s Bluetooth list. Note: Dexcom G7 sensors connect, send data, and disconnect every 5 minutes. The pairing in Bluetooth for your current Dexcom G7 sensor will likely say “not connected” and is expected.</span>

When you open the Dexcom G7 app for the first time, you will need to create a Dexcom account or log in with your existing Dexcom account. Once logged in the app walks you through the setup process step by step. First the overview section will provide you with educational videos and screens, so you can learn everything you need to know about your Dexcom G7. Then you will connect your Dexcom G7 to your phone. You will enable Bluetooth and set other required phone settings. The app will then provide you with instructions on how to insert your sensor.

Enter the pairing code. You will find the four digit pairing code on the applicator of the sensor you used. Take a photo of the QR code or enter the code manually to pair. Wait for the sensor to pair. Once complete, you will enter into your 30 minute sensor warm-up. The sensor warm-up timer lets you know when you'll start getting readings and alerts. After your warm-up you will start getting readings and alerts.

<span class="mark">Tip: We advise you to take a separate photo of the applicator code outside of the Dexcom G7 app. This can be used in the rare case the Dexcom G7 app crashes and needs to be redownloaded and reconnected to your activated sensor.</span>

The home screen will open to the glucose tab, where you'll find the most recent sensor glucose reading along with a trend arrow.

- If your glucose level drops below 40 mg/dL (2.2 mmol/L), it will display as "LOW," while readings exceeding 400 mg/dL (22.2 mmol/L) will show as "HIGH."

- In such cases, it's important to perform a finger prick test to confirm your glucose level and take appropriate actions for either hypoglycemia or hyperglycemia.

Beneath the glucose reading, you can access your glucose graph and choose the desired time range for the data. The yellow line represents your high alert level, and the red line represents your low alert level.

Further down, you'll find a Clarity tab displaying your average glucose, GMI, and Time In Range. You can customize the time range for these reports.

On the bottom of the screen, there are four icons to navigate through different tabs: Glucose, History, Connections, and Profile.

- In the history tab, you can log events such as blood glucose readings, insulin doses, meals, activity, or other relevant information.

- The connections tab allows you to see details of the sensor, or to replace or stop the current sensor. Additionally, there is a link to access Share function and add followers. On iPhones, you'll also find an option to link with Apple Health. When activated, the G7 app will transmit data to the health app with a 3-hour delay.

- The profile tab offers settings for configuring alerts, customizing the Glucose Tab, enabling or disabling G7 notifications, optimizing app performance through phone settings, and accessing support information.

- You can download an overview of the menu options in the downloads below this video.

On the Dexcom G7 app home screen, when you turn your mobile phone horizontally into landscape mode, you can access a larger glucose graph, with your glucose readings from the past 3 to 24 hours, as well as events you've recorded. By sliding your finger across the graph, you'll be able to see the precise sensor glucose value for that specific moment.

<span class="mark">Tip: At the top right corner the home screen “+” may be used to enter events. At the top right corner of the graph, there is an ellipsis (...) which can be used to adjust ranges or turn on and off high and low alerts, as well as activate Quiet Modes which we will cover shortly.</span>

### ***3.3 Do's & don’ts***

Here are a few important considerations:

- The Dexcom G7 sensor is water-resistant up to 2.4 meters (8 feet) for up to 24 hours. You can shower and swim while wearing your Dexcom G7 sensor.

- The time and date on the Dexcom G7 app automatically synchronize with your mobile phone's settings, while on the Dexcom G7 receiver, you will have to manually adjust this. <span class="mark">You can do this by navigating to the settings menu, selecting Date / Time and then adjust it.</span>

<!-- -->

- Upon triggering the Dexcom G7 applicator, the sensor initiates automatically, triggering a 30-minute warm-up phase. This feature allows you to bridge the usual data gap caused by the warm-up period. <span class="mark">When you insert the new sensor and wait 30 minutes before replacing the previous sensor in your Dexcom G7 receiver or app, your app will seamlessly acquire the sensor data from the new sensor, ensuring uninterrupted data transmission between sensors.</span>

- <span class="mark">The Dexcom G7 sensor has the option to be calibrated with a finger prick value. You can do this by tapping the + icon on the homescreen or selecting the History icon, select Blood Glucose, and then the option “Use as calibration”.</span>

- <span class="mark">The Dexcom G7 sensor readings may seem a bit bouncier than those from the Dexcom G6. This happens because the G7 system doesn't smooth out the numbers as much as the G6 system does. But don't worry, the Dexcom G7 sensor is still very accurate, with an overall MARD or accuracy rating of 8.2%.</span>

- The Dexcom G7 sensor may encounter occasional connectivity issues. If your display device fails to receive sensor readings for two consecutive attempts, a Signal Loss banner will appear. After approximately 20 minutes without sensor readings, your display device will alert you. Once the connectivity is resolved, up to 24 hours of missed data will be filled in on the trend graph. To minimize the risk of signal loss, Dexcom recommends wearing your phone or receiver closer to the sensor and in line of sight. Additionally, ensure that your phone has sufficient battery charge, as a battery level below 20% may trigger low power mode and disable Bluetooth. If you encounter issues, consider stopping and restarting the Bluetooth connection on your phone or power-cycling the phone itself. Dexcom is actively working on improving the Bluetooth connection for future updates.

By adhering to these guidelines and maintaining resilience throughout your glucose monitoring journey, you can leverage the Dexcom G7 system to access accurate and valuable data for effective diabetes management.

## **<u>4. Setting alarms</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alarms with the Dexcom G7 sensor.

Throughout this session, we'll walk you through the available alarm options and demonstrate how to set them on the Dexcom G7 receiver and app on your phone. Let's get started!

### ***4.1 Available alarms***

The Dexcom G7 system provides a very extensive range of alarm options to keep you informed about specific glucose levels and sensor-related issues. You have the ability to set alarms for Urgent Low, Urgent Low Soon, Low glucose, High glucose, glucose Rising Fast or Falling Fast, Signal Loss and Brief Sensor Issue.

- The Urgent Low alert is automatically triggered when your glucose level reaches 55 mg/dL (3.1 mmol/L) or lower. It's important to note that this alert cannot be turned off, however there is an option to silence all alerts for up to 6 hours.

- The Urgent Low Soon Alert is designed to notify you when your glucose is rapidly decreasing and is projected to reach 55 mg/dL or 3.1 mmol/L within the next 20 minutes. This early warning allows you to take necessary actions and prevent your glucose levels from dropping dangerously low.

- The default Low alert triggers at 70 mg/dL or 3.9 mmol/L, while the High alert defaults to 250 mg/dL or 14 mmol/L, but these settings are customizable. You have control over the glucose level, sound, and the snooze intervals for both Low and High alerts. For the High alert, there's an option to delay the initial alert, activating only if your sensor reading remains high for an extended period.

- Rising Fast and Falling Fast alerts notify you when your glucose levels change rapidly. You can specify the rate of change and the threshold for these alerts.

When you receive an alert, it's important to take action, such as making a treatment decision or addressing any system issues. Afterward, you must acknowledge the alert on your receiver, phone, or compatible smartwatch by tapping "OK." Until you acknowledge the alert, it will re-alert every 5 minutes.

### ***4.2 Setting alarms on the Dexcom G7 receiver***

To configure alarms on the Dexcom G7 receiver, navigate to the Settings within the Menu. From there, select the Alerts option, where you can specify the different alerts. Per alert, you can specify the desired alert level and repetition interval when the condition persists.

For customizing alert sounds, access the Alert Sounds menu, where you have several options for vibration, sound volume, and alarm melodies. Additionally, you can temporarily silence all alerts for up to 6 hours, and preview the alert sounds.

Keep in mind that the receiver must remain powered on to receive alarms. If the receiver is fully powered off, it won't receive any alerts.

### ***4.3 Setting alarm on the Dexcom G7 app***

With the Dexcom G7 app, configuring alarms is easy through the App Settings menu. <span class="mark">You can access Alerts settings within the Profile tab.</span> For each alert, you can customize the level, sound, and snooze preferences. Sound options include Sound, Vibrate, or matching your phone's sound settings.

Within the app, you have two optional quiet modes: "Silence All" for up to 6 hours and a "Vibrate" option. You can set all alerts to vibrate instead of making sounds for up to 6 hours or indefinitely. Keep in mind that Urgent Low and Technical Alerts will still produce sound if you don't acknowledge <span class="mark">the first alert</span> when using the vibrate option.

The app also features some rare Technical Alerts that cannot be disabled but for which you can choose the sound.

Furthermore, the app provides the flexibility of setting a second alert schedule, or profile, enabling you to tailor alerts to specific time blocks that align with your personal preferences.

Effectively utilizing these alarm settings empowers you to stay informed and proactive in managing your glucose levels with the Dexcom G7. When managing alarms, it's essential to configure only the alerts that serve your needs and to disable unnecessary ones. Dexcom's options for different alert sounds and vibrating alerts can help create a quieter experience. Additionally, personalizing nighttime alerts can be beneficial, such as setting earlier alerts to prevent nighttime lows and reduce overnight disturbances. Ultimately, these alerts can contribute to improving your Time In Range, offering a proactive approach to respond to changing glucose levels.

## **<u>5. Data sharing</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on sharing data with the Dexcom G7 sensor.

In this video, we will discuss how to share your data with healthcare providers, create reports, and share data with your loved ones who play a significant role in supporting your diabetes management.

### ***5.1 Sharing data with your healthcare provider***

<span class="mark">If you are using the Dexcom G7 app, it will automatically link to your Clarity account during the start-up process.</span>

There are separate versions of Clarity available for patients and healthcare professionals. Both versions are free, and you can create your account at clarity.dexcom.eu for Europe, or [<u>clarity.dexcom.com</u>](http://clarity.dexcom.com/), at any time.

<span class="mark">In Dexcom Clarity for professionals, you can add a patient by entering their name and date of birth. Once the patient is added, you can connect the Dexcom receiver to the computer and upload the data after installing the Dexcom uploader software.</span>

<span class="mark">If the patient is using the Dexcom G7 app, the easiest way to link the patient’s personal Dexcom Clarity account to the professional Clarity account is by using a Clinic Code. The clinic code can be found in the professional Clarity settings.</span>

<span class="mark">The patient enters this clinic code in the Dexcom G7 app by going to Connections, selecting Clarity Clinic, and confirming the link. Once completed, the healthcare provider has real-time access to that person’s glucose sensor data.</span>

### ***5.2 Creating reports***

Once the glucose sensor data is uploaded, Clarity offers eight to nine different reports, which are found in the left side bar.

- The OVERVIEW report provides key glucose metrics from the date range you select within the last seven to 90 days. Patterns and the best glucose day are summarized here, which you click for more detail. There's also information about the Dexcom CGM system in use and the alerts and alarms the patient has set. Talk with your patients if they need to adjust their alerts to help them stay in range.

- PATTERNS provides detailed graphical views and statistics regarding the patterns found, and offers some possible considerations for improved glucose control. Day and night highs and lows are potential patterns that could be found within the date range selected. Help patients stay positive with best day which shows them when they were most within their target range for the selected date range.

- TRENDS provides a visual representation of where patients spend the majority of their time, but eliminates outliers. The black line indicates the average.

- OVERLAY features all CGM data points to help visualize patterns and individual events <span class="mark">for an entire week.</span> Hover over each day to view just that day. The DAILY report shows every glucose value and patient-entered events for each day. Highs and lows outside the customized target range are highlighted here, and in the trends report.

- Clarity's COMPARE report is useful to analyze how well diabetes is managed. Compare different days, weeks or even months to see the impact of a medication adjustment, or simply to see how a patient has progressed.

- STATISTICS provides detailed metrics displayed by time or day of the week. The Ambulatory Glucose Profile, AGP, is a standardized glucose report where you can see your Time In Range, a trends graph and daily profiles.

- PATIENT GLUCOSE RANGE is only available in Clarity for Clinics. Use this page to customize the patient target range. Changes you make on this page only apply to this Clarity Clinic view and do not change any settings on the patient's Dexcom CGM system or the patient's personal Clarity account. All the interactive reports are available to print, download or email for any date range.

Our general module on glucose sensors provides additional guidance on interpreting these reports effectively.

Sharing data with healthcare providers may seem daunting at first. Take it step by step, creating your Clarity account and exploring the options available. Remember that sharing data empowers your healthcare team to provide you with better care, making it worth the effort.

### ***5.3 Sharing data with family***

Using the Dexcom G7 app, you can share your data with up to 10 caregivers through the Dexcom Follow app.

The process involves these steps:

- In the Dexcom G7 app, users can navigate to the "Connections" section and select "Share" to invite a follower <span class="mark">using their email address.</span> Within this process, they have the option to share the current sensor reading along with the trend graph or opt to share without it. Additionally, they can make suggestions for notifications that the follower will receive.

- <span class="mark">The follower will then receive an email with an invitation to download the app from the app store or start following if they already downloaded the app. In the Dexcom Follow app, they can go through any information screens. They can now see the glucose information of the person wearing the Dexcom G7 sensor. The follower automatically gets the settings that were set for them, but they can go into settings and change them at any time. This only changes their settings and does not impact the alerts on the app of the person wearing the sensor. For example if they wants to be notified when the user is over 300 mg/dl, they can set a high notification. Using the Share and Follow function of the Dexcom G7 app can really offer peace of mind to the user and the follower.</span>

Sharing data with your healthcare provider and loved ones is a collaborative step towards better diabetes management. Remember, it takes a supportive network to navigate this journey successfully. <span class="mark">You can pause sharing or remove individual followers at any time.</span>

## **<u>6. Connecting other devices</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your Dexcom G7 sensor to other devices.

In this chapter, we explore the exciting possibilities of connecting your Dexcom G7 sensor with various devices, such as smartwatches, smart pens, and insulin pumps.

### ***6.1 Smartwatch***

Dexcom offers native applications that enable real-time monitoring of your Dexcom G7 sensor data on your smartwatch.

Any brand of smartwatch may display some alerts, even if it isn't set up with your G7. On your smartwatch lock screen, you can tap OK to acknowledge the alert. That will also acknowledge the alert in your app.

In this guide, we will explore the setup options for popular smartwatches such as Garmin, Samsung Galaxy, and the Apple Watch.

#### ***\#1 Garmin Smartwatch***

To view your Dexcom G7 glucose data on your Garmin watch, follow these steps:

- Install the Garmin Connect IQ Store app on your phone.

- In the Garmin Connect IQ Store app, search for "Dexcom", and select the "Dex CGM Watch Face" from the search results. If your Garmin watch is connected to your phone, you can proceed to install the watch face.

- Configure the Dex CGM Watch Face by entering your Dexcom username and password in its settings. If the watch face doesn't automatically appear on your Garmin watch, you can manually select it from the available watch faces.

- Another option is to install a Dexcom Widget from the Connect IQ store. Tapping on the Dexcom Widget will display your glucose trend graph on your watch.

- Alternatively, you can install a Dexcom Data Field via the Connect IQ Store. This allows you to view your sensor value while engaging in activities on your watch. If you have a Garmin Edge cycling computer, you can also use the Dexcom Data Field feature to display Dexcom data on it.

Garmin JUST introduced a new feature allowing you to place dexcom readings on the main screen of the Garmin smartwatch. You can find more information about this feature on Garmins website.

#### ***\#2 Samsung Galaxy Smartwatch*** 

To access your Dexcom G7 glucose data on your Samsung Galaxy Watch, follow these steps:

- Access the App Store on your Galaxy Watch and search for the "Blose" app.

- Download and install the Blose app on your Galaxy Watch.

- Configure the Blose app by following the provided instructions. This entails signing in to your Dexcom Clarity account via your connected phone.

- You can customize various settings such as glucose units, glucose target values, alert preferences, and the appearance of the watch face.

- Search for and install the Blose watch face from the available options provided within the app.

- Once installed, you will be able to view your Dexcom G7 sensor data directly on your Samsung Galaxy Watch.

#### ***\#3 Apple Watch***

<span class="mark">The Dexcom G7 app includes an Apple Watch app. If the watch app hasn't been automatically installed, you can manually install it by accessing the Watch app on your iPhone.</span>

<span class="mark">On your Dexcom G7 app of your Apple Watch, you can conveniently monitor your current glucose levels, observe the trend arrow, and view your glucose graph from the past 1 to 6 hours. You can zoom in and out by tapping or swiping on the screen.</span>

<span class="mark">To quickly access the G7 app from your watch, consider adding a watchface complication. This allows you to open the app with a simple tap on the complication.</span>

<span class="mark">Please note that these Dexcom G7 watchface complications are only compatible with specific watch faces. For instance, you can use the Infograph Modular watch face and place the Dexcom Complication in one of the available complication positions.</span>

<span class="mark">Keep in mind that Apple Watch complications update information at intervals of 15-45 minutes. Tapping the complication will display a larger Dexcom sensor graph with the most recent blood glucose reading.</span>

<span class="mark">With the Dexcom G7 sensor and an Apple Watch (Series 6 or later), you can also enable a Direct to Watch feature. This allows you to view your glucose readings directly on your Apple Watch, even when your iPhone is not with you. To activate this feature, open the Dexcom G7 app on your phone, navigate to *Connections*, and select Direct to Watch. The app will guide you through pairing your sensor with your Apple Watch. Once the setup is complete, the grey phone icon will disappear from your Dexcom G7 watch face.</span>

<span class="mark">Please note, however, that the Share function is not supported on smartwatches. Your Apple Watch cannot send glucose data to followers. To use the Share and Follow features, the primary user must rely on the Dexcom G7 app on their phone.</span>

<span class="mark">It’s clear that the process of setting up your Dexcom G7 data on your smartwatch depends heavily on the specific combination of phone and smartwatch you own. If the provided instructions appear challenging to follow, there's no need to worry. It often becomes much simpler when you have your devices with you and can follow along in real-time.</span>

### ***6.2 Smartpens***

Currently, the Dexcom G7 app does not have direct compatibility with any smartpens. However, there is a workaround available. You can make a Glooko account and link it to your Clarity account. Additionally, you can connect your Novopen 6, Novopen Echo Plus, SoloSmart Button, Tempo Smart Button or InPen to Glooko.

By logging into Glooko, you'll have access to integrated data from both Dexcom and your smartpen or smartpen cap. This setup offers a comprehensive solution for monitoring both your glucose levels and insulin data within a single platform.
