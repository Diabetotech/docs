## **<u>5. Sharing data (10.5 min)</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on sharing data with the CareSens Air CGM. This video will explain how you can share your glucose data with your healthcare provider and loved ones via the Sens365 app and Sens365 Web. We’ll also go over the details <span class="mark">of t</span>he available reports.

### ***5.1 Sharing data with your care provider via the Sens365 app***

<span class="mark">Care providers can install the Sens365 app on their smart devices to receive and monitor your glucose data. To do this, you must first generate a six-digit code on the CareSens Air app.</span>

<span class="mark">Tap Settings at the bottom of the home screen. Tap Care Providers. Tap Connect to link your account to your care provider’s account. Tap Generate Sharing Code. Choose the data you want to share with your care provider, then tap “Generate Sharing Code.” Then share the generated code shown on the screen with your care provider.</span>

<span class="mark">Now let’s enter the shared code on the Sens365 app. After running the Sens365 app on the care provider's smart device, tap the person icon at the bottom of the home screen. Tap Add. Enter the shared code provided by a sensor user and tap Ok. You can also enter the sharing code via the Settings at the bottom of the home screen. Tap Connections. Tap the Add Person icon at the upper right corner of the screen. Enter the shared code provided by a sensor user. Tap Ok to complete the process. Once completed, a notification pop-up will appear on your CareSens Air app.</span>

<span class="mark">You can also enter the code provided by your care provider on your CareSens Air app to connect with them. Open the CareSens Air app and tap Care Providers in the Settings, then tap Connect. Tap Enter Sharing Code. Enter the code from your care provider. If the code is valid, a care-provider confirmation alert will pop up on the screen. Make sure you are adding the right care provider. When the process is completed, a completion notification will appear.</span>

<span class="mark">You can temporarily stop sharing glucose data with your care provider or change items you share with them. At the bottom of the home screen, tap Settings, then tap Care Providers. On the Care Providers screen, tap the care provider whose sharing options you want to change.</span>

<span class="mark">Tap on Share Data to activate or deactivate sharing data. Tap the check boxes to stop sharing data on glucose reading and events or alerts.</span>

<span class="mark">If you no longer want to share data with a care provider, you can turn off sharing. On the Care Providers screen, tap the Delete icon next to the provider you want to stop sharing data with. Tap Disconnect on the pop-up to complete the process.</span>

### ***5.2 Sharing data with your care provider via the Sens365 Web***

All data saved by the CareSe<span class="mark">ns Air app on your smart device is automatically stored on the cloud server. To refresh the upload of your app data, tap Settings at the bottom of the home screen, then tap Upload.</span>

<span class="mark">You can view and manage your uploaded glucose data using the cloud-based platform Sens365 Web. Open your web browser and go to https://sens365.i-sens.com. Click Get Started on the start screen.</span>

<span class="mark">The first time you visit the site, you'll be prompted to choose your country of residence and language. Click Submit to confirm your selections.</span>

<span class="mark">Click create an account on the login screen if you don't have a Sens365 account yet. Enter and verify your email address. Accept the required terms, create your password and fill out all the required information. A "Registration completed successfully" pop-up will appear.</span>

<span class="mark">If you already have a Sens365 account, simply enter your registered email and password on the screen to log in.</span>

<span class="mark">On your first login to Sens365 Web, the user mode selection screen will be displayed. If you are a CareSens Air user, select Sensor User. If you are a care provider, select Care Provider.</span>

<span class="mark">Sensor users must click OK to confirm their country of residence and glucose reading unit. They will then be taken to the home screen.</span>

<span class="mark">To connect to a new care provider, click Connections in the navigation sidebar on the left. Click the Connect with Care Provider button on the right. Enter the sharing code of the care provider and click OK. Verify the name of the sharing group. After reading the agreement, check the box and click OK. Once completed, you can find the new care provider added on the list.</span>

<span class="mark">To disconnect from a care provider, click the Disconnect button, then Click Disconnect on the pop-up window.</span>

<span class="mark">Now, let’s discuss Care Provider mode. When you log in, the screen displays group names and a list of sensor users in each group. A summary of each user’s information is also displayed. You can drag a column to reposition it.</span>

<span class="mark">When clicking on the Dashboard button for a specific user, the Add to Dashboard pop-up appears. Assign a care provider to the user, then click OK. The dashboard will appear on the right side of the screen. You can open the dashboard at any time by clicking on the Dashboard button in the top-right corner.</span>

<span class="mark">Groups can be created or deleted. To create a group, click the three dots next to the group name. In the Group Settings pop-up, customize the group name and generate the group’s sharing code, then click Save.</span>

<span class="mark">You can also add a new sensor user or invite one to the group. If you are adding a sensor user, enter the sharing code you received from them. If you are inviting a sensor user, confirm the group name, then send your sharing code to the user. You can copy the code or send email invitations directly. Your care provider sharing code remains the same unless you change it; you can generate a new sharing code as needed.</span>

<span class="mark">Moreover, you can invite another care provider to manage the user groups with you. To do this, click the three-dot menu next to the group name and select Invite Care Provider. Enter the care provider's email address, then choose Admin or Coordinator Authority. Select the acknowledgment checkbox and click Send.</span>

### **5.3 Reports**

<span class="mark">In Care Provider mode, click the sensor user’s name to access the Reports screen. By default, you’ll be taken to the AGP report. On the left, you’ll find Overview. On the right, you can access Comparison, Patterns, Trends, Statistics, and Events.</span>

<span class="mark">In the row above, you can adjust the time period you want to view.</span>

<span class="mark">The Overview page displays Time in Range, Average Glucose, GMI, Glucose Variability, and Standard Deviation. Daily Average Glucose, Insulin, Daily Time in Ranges, Time CGM Active, and Device Information are also shown. A customization option is available, allowing you to adjust the layout by adding, deleting, or moving blocks.</span>

<span class="mark">AGP is a standardized glucose report that includes Time in Range, Time CGM Active, Glucose Metrics, Ambulatory Glucose Profile, and Daily Glucose Profiles. You can check the percentage of time spent in the target glucose range using the TIR graph and estimate A1c using GMI.</span>

<span class="mark">On the Comparison page, two graphs are displayed based on the selected time periods. This makes it easy to see changes in glucose trends and improvements in glucose management by comparing the two graphs.</span>

<span class="mark">On the Patterns page, low, target, and high glucose ranges are marked on the graph for the selected time period. This helps you identify patterns by showing when low or high glucose levels occur and which events may be associated with glucose changes.</span>

<span class="mark">The Trends page shows daily and weekly glucose trends. You can view day-to-day fluctuations or see all daily trends together in a weekly graph.</span>

<span class="mark">The Statistics page shows glucose readings by day of the week and hour of the day. You can review daily and hourly average glucose statistics within the selected time period.</span>

<span class="mark">The Events page shows all event logs recorded in the CareSens Air app in chronological order.</span>

<span class="mark">Further up the screen, next to the sensor user’s name, click Target Range Settings to adjust the target blood glucose range for daytime and night-time periods and tailor management to the user’s goals.</span>

<span class="mark">In both Sensor User and Care Provider modes, you can download, print, and send reports, as well as export data. To download a report, click the downward-arrow icon in the top-right corner; the report will be saved as a PDF. To print, click the printer icon. To send the report, click the envelope icon; it will be sent as a PDF to the email address you specify. Finally, to export your data in CSV format, click the Export Data icon.</span>

Meanwhile, on the CareSens Air Receiver, the Statistics option in the Main Menu lets you view reports for the past 7, 14, 30, or 90 days. Each report shows your average glucose, standard deviation (SD), and coefficient of variation (CV) for the selected period, along with your Time in Range.

### ***5.4 Tips***

Review these reminders before sharing your CareSens Air data:

- When connecting to a care provider via the CareSens app, the time limit for entering the generated sharing code is 5 minutes. If the code expires, create a new one.

- When you change your sharing options for a care provider, that provider is notified of the changes.

- Sens365 is a secondary viewer for the CareSens Air CGM System and is not intended to provide treatment decisions or to be used as a substitute for professional healthcare advice. Sens365 does not guarantee the real-time reception of glucose data of the CareSens Air CGM user.

- You cannot share data directly from the CareSens Air Receiver. To access data stored on the device, visit the Sens365 website, sign in, and upload the data from the receiver to the cloud server.

In this video, we tackled various options for sharing and uploading your CareSens Air data. With these features, you can work more closely with your care team, helping you make timely, informed decisions for better health.
