## **<u>1. Introduction</u>**

Welcome to this module on the CareSens Air CGM. In this series of videos, we'll examine the ins and outs of one of the newest CGMs on the market. Let's begin.

Measu<span class="mark">ring approximately 3.5 by 2 cm and weighing 4.5 grams, the CareSens Air CGM is placed on the back of your upper arm using an applicator and secured with sensor tape. After a 30-minute warm-up, the sensor monitors glucose levels in your interstitial fluid and delivers real-time readings to the CareSens Air smartphone app or the CareSens Air receiver via Bluetooth every five minutes. The sensor lasts for up to 15 days. It is intended for people with diabetes 18 years or older.</span>

<span class="mark">In our “Master Module on Glucose Sensors,” we covered CGM fundamentals, including when to trust sensor readings and how to manage issues such as disconnections and skin reactions. This module centers on the start-up education for the CareSens Air CGM and adds advanced tips and tricks.</span>

<span class="mark">This module will help both healthcare providers and individuals with diabetes get the most from the CareSens Air system. But before we start, please note that these videos do not serve as medical advice. Always consult with your healthcare provider before modifying your therapy in any way.</span>
