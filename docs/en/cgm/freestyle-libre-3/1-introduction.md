## **<u>1. Introduction (3 min)</u>**

Welcome to this module dedicated to FreeStyle L<span class="mark">ibre 3 and 3 Plus! Throughout this journey, we will delve into the intricacies of the world's most compact sensor.</span>

<span class="mark">The FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor easily attaches to your body with a simple click and measures your glucose every minute. You can conveniently track your glucose curve using either the FreeStyle Libre 3 app on your smartphone or the FreeStyle Libre 3 reader. The FreeStyle Libre 3 has a 14-day wear duration, while the FreeStyle Libre 3 Plus can be worn 15 days.</span>

<span class="mark">The other differences between the FreeStyle Libre 3 and FreeStyle Libre 3 Plus sensors are the approved age range (4 years and older for the Libre 3 and 2 years and older for the Libre 3 Plus) and the amount of vitamin C that can cause interference (500 mg/day for the Libre 3 and 1000 mg/day for the Libre 3 Plus). Beyond these distinctions, there are no other differences. For the remainder of this module, we will collectively refer to both sensors as the FreeStyle Libre 3.</span>

In the "Master Module on Glucose Sensors", we covered general information about glucose sensors, including insights on when to trust the sensor readings and tips for handling sensor disconnections and allergies. In the upcoming videos, we will focus on the start-up education for the FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor and provide advanced tips and tricks.

This course is designed to empower both healthcare providers and individuals with diabetes to unlock the full potential of FreeStyle Libre 3. But before we dive in, remember that these videos do not serve as medical advice. Always consult with your healthcare provider before making any changes to your therapy.

These videos have been created by Dr. Inge Van Boxelaer, the founder of Diabetotech and an endocrinologist at the St-Lucas Hospital in Ghent in Belgium. The content has been reviewed by Dr. Diana Isaacs, an endocrinologist at Cleveland Clinic Diabetes Center as well as Justin Eastzer, an individual living with diabetes who has firsthand experience with FreeStyle Libre 3 and has published numerous tech reviews about it on his Youtube and social pages called “Diabetech.”

Be assured, all the information provided here is completely independent from Abbott, the sensor manufacturing company, ensuring a genuine perspective on the advantages and drawbacks of the FreeStyle Libre 3 sensor.

By the end of this module, you will have a comprehensive understanding of the FreeStyle Libre 3 system. We will cover topics such as sensor placement, starting and scanning the sensor, setting alarms, sharing data, connecting the sensor to other devices and some extra tips & tricks. As a healthcare provider, you will gain the knowledge needed to guide individuals using FreeStyle Libre 3 effectively, while users themselves will discover a wealth of tips and tricks to enhance their experience. Let's embark on this empowering journey together. Good luck!
