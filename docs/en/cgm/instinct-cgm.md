## **<u>1. Introduction</u>**

Welcome to this module on the Instinct CGM! Throughout this video series, we’ll cover everything you need to know about this sensor designed exclusively for MiniMed™ systems. Let’s get started.

The Instinct sensor measures 21 by 21 by 3 millimeters and weighs 1 g. It is inserted on the back of your upper arm using an all-in-one applicator. After a one-hour warm-up, it measures your glucose levels in real time and delivers readings to your insulin pump every five minutes. You can then view your sensor and pump data on both your MiniMed™ 780G insulin pump and your smartphone via the MiniMed™ Mobile app. The sensor lasts for up to 15 days. It is intended for people with diabetes 7 and old<span class="mark">er in the US, but age indication may differ in other regions.</span>

## **<u>2. Placing the sensor</u>**

Welcome to the first lesson of the Instinct CGM module. In this video, we’ll guide you through inserting and removing the Instinct sensor, and highlight major dos and don’ts.

### ***2.1 Supplies***

Gather the following:

- an Instinct kit, which contains an all-in-one sensor applicator; and

- alcohol wipes.

### ***2.2 Inserting the sensor*** 

To apply your sensor, you’ll need the MiniMed™ Mobile app on a compatible smartphone paired with your MiniMed™ 780G pump. Visit this webpage to check device compatibility: https://www.medtronicdiabetes.com/app-check

On your phone, open the MiniMed™ Mobile app. Tap the Menu button, then select Start sensor, and tap to confirm "Yes, Instinct."

Choose your insertion site on the back of your upper arm. Select an area that is flat, free from scars, moles, or stretch marks, and at least 1 inch away from insulin injection sites. Prepare your site by washing with plain soap. Let it dry. Then, clean the site with an alcohol wipe and allow to air-dry. Next, unscrew the cap from the sensor applicator. Place the applicator over the site, then push down firmly to apply the sensor. Gently pull the applicator away and make sure the sensor is secure.

### ***2.3 Removing the sensor***

To remove the Instinct sensor, pull up the edge of the adhesive that keeps your sensor attached to your skin. Slowly peel away from your skin in one motion. Discard the used sensor following directions from your healthcare provider.

### ***2.4 Do's & don’ts***

- To avoid the risk of no results and/or infection, do not use the sensor kit package or sensor applicator if:

  - it appears to be damaged,

  - the tamper label indicates the sensor applicator has already been opened, or

  - the sensor kit contents are past expiration date.

- For the best connection, it’s important you wear your pump and sensor on the same side of your body. Your infusion set can be worn on either side.

- Do not push down on the sensor applicator until placed over the prepared site to prevent unintended results or injury.

- The sensor is designed to detect certain conditions which may occur where the sensor is not working as intended and needs to shut off. This may occur if the sensor gets knocked off from the skin, or if the sensor detects that it may not be performing as intended. When informed of the condition, replace your sensor. Contac<span class="mark">t MiniMed’s Cust</span>omer Service if you receive a "replace sensor" message before the end of the wear duration.

- Intense exercise may cause your sensor to loosen due to sweat or sensor movement. If the sensor is becoming loose or if the sensor tip is coming out of your skin, you may get no readings or unreliable readings, which may not match how you feel. Check to make sure your sensor has not come loose. If it has come loose, remove it, apply a new one, and contact Customer Service.

- Upon removal of the sensor, any remaining adhesive residue on the skin can be removed with warm soapy water or isopropyl alcohol.

You now know the correct procedure for inserting and removing your Instinct sensor. Adhering to these best practices will help you get dependable glucose readings each day, enabling you to make confident, informed choices about your treatment.

## **3. Starting the sensor** 

Welcome to this lesson on starting the Instinct sensor. In this video, you'll learn how to start the sensor and navigate the pump and the app. In addition, we'll go over some practical tips for living with your sensor. Let’s dive in.

### ***3.1 Starting the sensor***

After applying the sensor, tap Next in the app. You can follow the “How to scan a sensor” steps in the app — which adjusts automatically depending if you’re using an iOS or Android device — or scan on your own. Make sure your pump is on the Home screen, with no active alarms or alerts. Then hold your phone near the sensor. If you’re using an iOS device, hold the top of your device near the sensor. If you’re using an Android device, hold the back of your device near the sensor. Once you hear the beep or feel the vibration, this confirms that the sensor has been started and begins the warm-up period.

When the sensor is starting, keep your pump close to the sensor until pairing is complete. You’ll see a warm-up message on both the pump and app home screen. Once pairing is successful, the connection icon turns green. Once the countdown ends, your pump will begin receiving sensor glucose (SG) readings.

### ***3.2 The MiniMed™ 780G pump***

The MiniMed™ 780G pump's Sensor feature automatically turns on when your Instinct sensor is paired to the pump. When the Sensor feature is active, the Home screen with CGM in Manual Mode displays your SG reading, as well as a trend arrow and a real-time graph. Also displayed on the pump Home screen are status icons, the Suspend Icon, and Active Insulin.

When the pump is using the SmartGuard feature, the Home screen displays a shield with your current SG, if available.

In the Manual Mode sensor display, trend arrows indicate the rate at which the most recent SG readings are rising or falling. They could be single, double, or triple downward or upward arrows; the more arrows that are shown, the faster the SG value is changing. It's also possible for trend arrows to be absent, if the rate of SG change is less than 1 mg/dL per minute, or if the sensor may be outside of operating limits.

As for the sensor graph, it includes the following information:

- The most recent SG reading;

- Historical SG readings for the last 3-hour, 6-hour, 12-hour, or 24-hour periods;

- High and low SG limits;

- Carb entries;

- Delivered boluses;

- Suspend events; and

- Blood Glucose (BG) entries.

The sensor graph may also show a dotted line to represent backfilled SG values, which will be recorded anytime the sensor is out of wireless range from the pump.

To view the sensor graph from the home screen, press the Graph button on the pump. A full-screen view of the 3-hour graph appears. Press the Up button to navigate to the 6-hour, 12-hour, and 24-hour graphs. Press Left to view the SG readings and event details. To exit the full-screen view, press Back or press the Graph button again.

### ***3.3 The MiniMed™ Mobile app*** 

After downloading the MiniMed<sup>TM</sup> Mobile app on the Apple or Google Play app store, follow the prompts on the screen that explains how the app works. Create or sign in to your CareLink™ personal account. Read the agreement and tap ‘Agree’. Follow the prompts and select ‘Get started’. Select your pump. Pair your pump by selecting ‘Allow’. Allow use of Bluetooth by selecting ‘OK’. Read the Dosing Disclaimer and tap ‘Agree’. The app is now ready to use.

The MiniMed<sup>TM</sup> Mobile app Home screen shows an overview of data received from the pump. The Home screen is divided into two main areas: the status area and the graph. There is also a Menu button at the top of the Home screen.

The status area shows the most current data that the pump has sent to the MiniMed<sup>TM</sup> Mobile app and status messages that may require your attention. It shows your current SG value, trend arrows, active insulin, basal rate, status messages, and status icons.

The graph shows glucose values and event markers based on the data the app receives from the paired pump. To navigate the graph:

- Swipe left or right over the graph to navigate through past and current data.

- Stretch and pinch to zoom in and out from the 1-hour view to a 24-hour view.

- Double-tap the graph to cycle through the various graph zoom levels, namely 24 hour, 12 hours, 6 hours, and 3 hours.

If you turn on high and low SG limits on the pump, the limits appear on the graph. The high limit line appears in orange, and the low limit line appears in red. The SG target range of 70 to 180 mg/dL or 3.9 to 10 mmol/L appears as a green band across the graph.

From the Home screen, swipe left to access your Time in Range (TIR) data and graphs.

Tap on Menu on the Home screen. The Menu screen shows the status of the insulin pump system and paired devices. The Menu screen also gives you access to the Notifications settings, Display Options, Pair Device function, Sync to CareLink<sup>TM</sup> feature, Update pump feature, and About screen.

It's important to note that the MiniMed<sup>TM</sup> Mobile app is designed as a viewing tool and does not have the functionality to administer boluses or modify settings. Its primary purpose is to provide users with access to their Instinct sensor and insulin data, allowing them to monitor their glucose levels, insulin delivery, and Time in Range. Any adjustments or actions regarding bolus administration or settings should be made directly on the insulin pump itself.

### ***3.4 Do's & don’ts***

Now, we’ll outline some important tips when using the Instinct CGM.

- The sensor transmission range is 33 feet (or 10 meters) when unobstructed.

- The sensor is water-resistant and can be worn while bathing, showering, or swimming. However, do not take your sensor deeper than 3 feet (or 1 meter) or immerse it longer than 30 minutes in water.

- Taking more than 1000 mg of Vitamin C per day may falsely raise your sensor readings, which could cause you to miss a severe low glucose event.

- You can safely have an MRI exam while wearing your sensor. Sensor readings may be inaccurate during the MRI, but sensor function returns fully back to normal after 1 hour.

- The Instinct CGM cannot be calibrated. But when the sensor is paired with the MiniMed<sup>TM</sup> 780G, the system uses BG meter readings as a BG check to verify system performance.

## **<u>4. Setting alerts</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on setting alerts with the Instinct CGM. In this video, you’ll discover what kinds of alerts the Instinct sensor can send and how you can tailor them to your needs. Let’s jump right in.

### ***4.1 Available alerts***

The MiniMed™ 780G AID system has a sophisticated safety network. If the network detects anything unusual, it communicates this information through notifications. Notifications include alarms, alerts, and messages.

An alarm warns of a condition that requires immediate attention. Low glucose levels and stopped insulin delivery are the most common reasons for alarms. When an alarm occurs, the pump displays a notification with a red icon and instructions. The red notification light blinks twice, followed by a pause, in a continuous repeating pattern. Depending on the sound and vibration settings, the pump also emits a series of alarm tones and vibrations. The underlying problem that triggered the alarm must be resolved. In most cases, p<span class="mark">ress the Select or Down arrow button and then make a selection to clear the alarm. Sometimes the underlying problem is not resolved when the alarm is cleared. The alarm repeats until the underlying problem is fixed. If the alarm condition is not resolv</span>ed after 10 minutes, the alarm tone escalates to a loud emergency siren.

Alerts indicate that a situation may require attention. When an alert occurs, check the pump screen to see if any action is required. When an alert occurs, the pump displays a notification with a yellow icon and instructions. The red notification light on the pump blinks once, followed by a pause, then blinks once again in a continuous repeating pattern. Depending on the sound and vibration settings, the pump generates a series of tones and vibrations as well. To clear an alert, press Down and then make a selection. The pump beeps every 5 or 15 minutes, depending on the alert, until the alert is resolved. Some alerts will also escalate to a loud emergency siren after 10 minutes.

A message is a notification that shows the status of the pump or displays when a decision needs to be made. When a message occurs, the pump displays a notification with a blue icon and instructions; some messages show a yellow icon. The red notification light on the pump does not blink. In addition, the pump emits a tone, a one-pulse-only vibration, or a combination of both. To clear a message, press Down and then make a selection.

There are also several specific reminders that prompt a specific action. They include personal reminders on BG meter readings and medication, as well as bolus and insulin reservoir reminders.

Let's shift our focus to the alarms and alerts related to the Instinct CGM. Alarms include Alert on low, Low SG, Sensor ending soon, Suspend on low, and Medical device. Alerts can refer to SG levels, sensor status, blood glucose, and insulin delivery.

<span class="mark">All alerts are optional, except for the Urgent Low Alert that triggers when the sensor glucose value is at or below 54 mg/dl or 3.0 mmol/l, and the High alert when your sensor glucose is above 250 mg/dl or 13.8 mmol/l for more than 3 hours.</span>

### ***4.2 Setting alerts on the pump***

On the insulin pump, you can customize high and low SG alerts, set snooze durations for each, and silence alerts according to your preferences.

With High SG settings, you can enable the Rise Alert, Alert Before High, and Alert On High. To set up these high alerts, start from the Home screen. Press the middle circle button, select Settings, then choose Alert Settings, and tap High Alert. The High Setup screen will appear.

> Begin by selecting a time segment. The end time will flash. The start time of the first segment is always 12:00 AM, and you can set up to eight time segments, each with its own high limit. All segments must add up to a full 24-hour period. Set the End Time, then enter your High Limit, choosing a value from 100 to 400 mg/dL in increments of 5 mg/dL.
>
> To customize the high alerts for that segment, select the arrow beside the End Time. A screen will appear showing the available high alerts.

- Turn on Alert Before High to receive an alert before reaching the high limit. Set Time Before High from 5 to 30 minutes.

- ·Turn on Alert On High to receive an alert when the high limit is reached.

- Turn on Rise Alert to receive an alert when SG is rising quickly.

> Low SG settings alert you or suspend insulin delivery when SG approaches or reaches the low limit. To change low SG settings, start from the Home screen, press the middle circle button, and select Settings. Choose Alert Settings, then Low Alert. On the Low Setup screen, select Edit, adjust the time segment, and modify any low alert setting as needed. Select Next, then Review, and finish by selecting Save.
>
> The High Snooze feature sets how long the pump waits before repeating a high alert if the high condition continues. To adjust it, go to Settings, Alert Settings, and Snooze High & Low. On the Snooze screen, select High Snooze and enter a duration in 5-minute increments from 5 minutes to 3 hours, then select Save.
>
> The Low Snooze feature works the same way but for low alerts. From the Snooze screen, select Low Snooze and choose a delay from 5 minutes to 1 hour in 5-minute increments. Select Save.
>
> The Alert Silence feature silences selected sensor alerts. When active, the Alert Silence icon appears on the Home screen. Alerts will still appear visually, but sound and vibration are muted.
>
> To silence alerts, start at the Home screen and press the middle circle button. Select the volume icon, then choose Silence Sensor Alerts. Select from High Alerts Only, High & Low Alerts, or All Sensor Alerts. Set a Duration in 15-minute increments from 30 minutes to 24 hours, then select Begin.
>
> To cancel Alert Silence, open the volume icon again, select Alert Silence, and tap Cancel Alert Silence.

### ***4.3 Setting alerts on the MiniMed<sup>TM</sup> mobile app***

The MiniMed<sup>TM</sup> Mobile app lets you receive alarms, alerts, reminders, and messages from your pump on your compatible mobile device. All notifications from the pump appear at the top of the Home screen. Alarm notifications are displayed in red background, alerts in yellow, messages in blue, and reminders in gray.

There are two notification settings you can control from the Notifications screen. First are Notifications from the pump. When this setting is turned on, alarms, alerts, messages, and reminders from your pump will appear on both your pump and your compatible mobile device. Your mobile device must also have notifications from the MiniMed Mobile app turned on. If either setting is turned off, the notifications will appear on your pump only.

Second are Repeat notifications. When this setting is turned on, notifications repeat every minute on your compatible mobile device until they are cleared on the pump or dismissed in the app. If this setting is turned off, notifications will not repeat on the mobile device.

Tap the toggle buttons to turn desired notifications on or off. Then Tap Done at the bottom of the screen. If you have turned on Notifications, a message appears to remind you that alerts and alarms must always be cleared on the pump. Tap OK.

### ***4.4 Do's & don’ts***

Here are a few reminders about Instinct sensor notifications on the pump and the app.

- Since the Instinct sensor is free of calibrations, additional safety checks have been put into place. As a result, the Sensor Updating alert may occur when the system is confirming the reliability of the sensor. There is no need to take action unless recommended by the system. In the event that the updating lasts for more than 3 hours, consider changing the sensor.

- Do not rely solely on notifications from the MiniMed<sup>TM</sup> Mobile app. Notifications on your compatible mobile device or those sent to care partners may not occur as expected, and they may not always be noticeable. Always rely on your pump as the primary source for notifications.

- Always clear notifications on your pump. Dismissing a notification in the app does not clear it on the pump, so it is important to promptly respond to and clear all notifications that appear on your pump.

<span class="mark">To share your data with your healthcare provider, Medtronic provides the CareLink platform. ### ***<span class="mark">5.2 Generating Reports</span>***

<span class="mark">Once your data is uploaded to CareLink, you can generate reports by selecting the reports option. The process of creating and interpreting reports is explained in detail in the MiniMed 780G automated insulin delivery system reports video.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">5.3 Sharing data with loved-ones</span>***

<span class="mark">To share Instinct data with family members or caregivers, you have two options.</span>

- <span class="mark">Firstly, you can view CareLink data on your PC by creating a CareLink Personal account as a care partner. - <span class="mark">Alternatively, if you use the MiniMed Mobile app, you can share your data with up to 5 care partners using the CareLink Connect app. To begin, care partners need to download the CareLink Connect app on their compatible mobile phone. ### ***5.4 Tips***

Take note of the following guidelines on sharing your Instinct sensor data.

- For information to be sent to the CareLink™ Connect app, the MiniMed<sup>TM</sup> Mobile app must be:

  - within 6 meters (or 20 feet) of the pump

  - have internet connection

  - be logged into CareLink™ Personal on the app, and

  - have Sync to CareLink™ turned on.

- Both Pump User and Care Partner must be logged into Carelink™ Personal for Carelink™ Connect App to receive data.

- One MiniMed™ Mobile app user can have their data followed by up to 5 CareLink™ Connect app users, and one CareLink™ Connect app user can follow up to 5 MiniMed™ Mobile app users.

- If nothing happens when you try to generate reports, you may need to disable your browser’s pop-up blocker. Call Medtronic Customer Service if the problem persists.

## **<u>6. Connecting other devices</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your Instinct CGM to other devices. In this video, we’ll tackle integrating your Instinct sensor with smartwatches, and smart insulin pens and pumps. Let’s proceed.

### ***6.1 Smartwatches***

The MiniMed™ Mobile app is compatible with Apple Watch. You can monitor your values on your watch face or tap to enter the app. The watch communicates with the iPhone device, not the insulin pump, so if your iPhone is out of range, you will not get updated glucose information on your watch.

To install MiniMed™ Mobile on your Apple Watch, make sure your Apple Watch and iPhone device are connected. You can check this in your Bluetooth settings. Download the MiniMed™ Mobile app from the App Store on your iPhone if you haven’t done so already. Next, you need the companion app on your Apple Watch. If this does not happen automatically, there are two ways of doing it manually.

First is to get the app via your Watch. Press the digital crown button to see the home screen, then tap the App Store online store. Tap Search and enter “MiniMed Mobile.” Find the right app with the MiniMed Mobile icon and tap get.

The second option is to get the app via your iPhone. On your iPhone device, open the Watch app. Tap the My Watch tab. Tap Install next to the MiniMed™ Mobile app.

<span class="mark">Access the MiniMed app on your Apple Watch to view your glycemia within your SmartGuard shield, and swiping left will display your sensor graph and Time In Range.</span>

<span class="mark">To have easier access to your glucose data, you can integrate your sensor glycemia and SmartGuard Shield as a complication on compatible watch faces. These watch faces include Portrait, Nike (hybrid), Breathe, Utility, Simple, Photos, Motion, Gradient, XL, and Astronomy. Clicking on the complication will allow you to access the full MiniMed Watch app.</span>

On the other hand, connectivity for Android watches requires the use of a third-party app like xDrip+. Please be aware that xDrip+ is not supported by Medtronic and lacks approval from regulatory authorities like the FDA or EMA, making its usage carry inherent risks that should be approached cautiously.

***\#1 Garmin smartwatch***

For Garmin smartwatches, download the xDrip+ app from [<u>github.com/nightscoutfoundation/xdrip.</u>](http://github.com/nightscoutfoundation/xdrip.) Go to the settings of the xDrip+ app and set the data source to Companion app. This allows xDrip+ to receive sensor data from the MiniMed<sup>TM</sup> Mobile app. Also in the settings, adjust the inter-app settings to send the xDrip+ data to your connected Wear OS smartwatch. Then go to the Connect IQ store app on your phone, download the xDrip+/Spike/Nightscout Watch Face, and set xDrip+ as your companion app. On your watch, you can now choose the xDrip+/Spike/Nightscout watch face to see your real-time Instinct data on your Garmin smartwatch.

***\#2 Samsung Galaxy smartwatch***

For Samsung Galaxy smartwatches, follow similar steps by downloading the xDrip+ app and setting the data source to Companion App in the settings. Adjust the xDrip+ app settings to send data to the G-Watch App by checking the Smartwatch and Inter-app settings. Next, install the G-Watch Wear app on both your phone and smartwatch and set xDrip+ as your companion app. Finally, choose the according watch face on your Galaxy watch, and customise the watch face and alerts on your watch as you prefer.

As a reminder, using open-source apps carries inherent risks and is not supported by Medtronic or approved by regulatory authorities. We are sharing this information for transparency, but we do not recommend this practice unless you fully understand the associated risks.

### ***6.2 Smart insulin pens***

<span class="mark">The Instinct sensor is FDA-approved for use within the MiniMed™ Go Smart MDI System. At the time this course was published, the MiniMed™ Go system had not yet been launched.</span>

### ***6.3 Smart insulin pumps***

The Instinct CGM is fully integrated with the MiniMed™ 780G insulin pump, allowing for coordinated diabetes management.

In contrast, the Instinct sensor is not compatible with open-source automated insulin delivery systems.
