## **<u>3. Starting the sensor (4 min)</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on starting the Dexcom G6 sensor.

In this video, we will cover the process of starting the Dexcom G6 sensor and scanning it using the Dexcom G6 app. Let's delve into the details.

<span class="mark">For some automated insulin delivery systems, starting the Dexcom G6 sensor is seamlessly integrated into the automated insulin delivery system's software, eliminating the need for the Dexcom G6 app.</span>

Although there is a Dexcom G6 receiver available, we won't focus on it extensively since it is not widely used.

### ***3.1 Dexcom G6 app***

The Dexcom G6 app can be downloaded on most mobile phones, with separate versions available for Android and iPhone devices. You can find a list of all compatible devices and software versions on this website: dexcom.com/compatibility

The Dexcom G6 app has minimal impact on battery life during regular use. If your phone's battery dies, your data will not be lost. Additionally, you can view your sensor data while your phone is charging.

To receive sensor data on your Dexcom G6 app, follow these steps:

- When starting the Dexcom G6 app for the first time, you will be prompted to enter the serial number of your transmitter and establish a connection.

- Enter the sensor code provided with your sensor.

- Insert the sensor and attach the transmitter. Wait for the transmitter to pair with the app.

- Tap "Start New Sensor" to initiate the sensor.

- Please note that the sensor requires a 2-hour warm-up period before your glucose data becomes available. Keep the receiver within 6 meters of the transmitter for optimal data reception.

Once the sensor has completed the warm-up period, the initial screen will display the following information:

- In the middle, your sensor's current glucose reading and the associated trend arrow. If your glucose falls below 40 mg/dL (2.2 mmol/L), the reading will display as "LOW." Conversely, if your glucose exceeds 400 mg/dL (22.2 mmol/L), it will show as "HIGH." In such cases, perform a finger prick test to verify your glucose level and take appropriate actions for hypoglycemia or hyperglycemia.

- Below the glucose reading, you can view your glucose graph.

- Finally, you can access the settings located at either the bottom or top of the homescreen, depending on whether you're using an iPhone or an Android phone.

- You can also go to the Share icon to set up Dexcom Share & Follow.

The events section allows you to record additional information, such as insulin dosages.

By rotating your mobile phone to landscape mode, you can view your glucose readings of the last 24 hours along with any insulin dosages you have entered.

### ***3.2 Do's & don’ts***

Here are a few important considerations:

- The Dexcom G6 sensor is water-resistant up to 2.4 meters) for up to 24 hours. You can shower and swim while wearing your Dexcom G6 sensor.

- The time and date on the Dexcom G6 app automatically synchronize with your mobile phone's settings.

<!-- -->

- If there is a temporary loss of connection to the transmitter, your glucose graph may display a gap in the data. Once the connection is re-established, the gap will be filled with readings from the last 3 hours.

- You may encounter issues when a new sensor has the same code as the previous one. In such cases, the new sensor may not be accepted or recognized by the system. To prevent this, you can try not to use sensors with the same code consecutively. Instead, select a different sensor from the box to ensure proper acceptance and recognition by the system.

By adhering to these guidelines and maintaining resilience throughout your glucose monitoring journey, you can leverage the Dexcom G6 app to access accurate and valuable data for effective diabetes management.
