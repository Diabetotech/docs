## **<u>5. Data sharing</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on sharing data with the Dexcom G6 sensor.

In this video, we will discuss how to share your data with healthcare providers, create reports, and share data with your loved ones who play a significant role in supporting your diabetes management.

### ***5.1 Sharing data with your healthcare provider***

<span class="mark">Your healthcare provider can access your data through Clarity, Dexcom's reading software. There are separate versions of Clarity available for patients and healthcare professionals.</span> <span class="mark">Both versions are free, and you can create your account at clarity.dexcom.eu at any time.</span> <span class="mark">Use the same log-in details for your Dexcom G6 app as for Dexcom Clarity. You can also download the Dexcom Clarity app and log in with your Dexcom account. Looking at your glucose trends regularly can help you to fine tune your insulin setting to achieve an even better glucose control.</span>

<span class="mark">In Clarity for professionals, you can add a patient by entering their name and date of birth. Once added, you can send an email invitation to the patient from Clarity, inviting them to share their data. The patient needs to log into their personal Clarity account via the Clarity app or via the Clarity website and enter the provided code via the settings or via clarity.dexcom.eu/share.</span>

### ***5.2 Creating reports***

Once the glucose sensor data is uploaded, Clarity offers eight to nine different reports, which are found in the left side bar.

- The OVERVIEW report provides key glucose metrics from the date range you select within the last 90 days. Patterns and the best glucose day are summarized here, which you click for more detail. There's also information about the Dexcom CGM system in use and the alerts and alarms the patient has set. Talk with your patients if they need to adjust their alerts to help them stay in range.

- PATTERNS provides detailed graphical views and statistics regarding the patterns found, and offers some possible considerations for improved glucose control. Day and night highs and lows are potential patterns that could be found within the date range selected. Help patients stay positive with best day which shows them when they were most within their target range for the selected date range.

- TRENDS provides a visual representation of where patients spend the majority of their time, but eliminates outliers. The black line indicates the average.

- OVERLAY features all CGM data points to help visualize patterns and individual events. Hover over each day to view just that day.

- The DAILY report shows every glucose value and patient-entered events for each day. Highs and lows outside the customized target range are highlighted here, and in the trends report.

- Clarity's COMPARE report is useful to analyze how well diabetes is managed. Compare different days, weeks or even months to see the impact of a medication adjustment, or simply to see how a patient has progressed.

- STATISTICS provides detailed metrics displayed by time and day of the week. The Ambulatory Glucose Profile, AGP, is a standardized glucose report where you can see your Time In Range, a trends graph and daily profiles.

- PATIENT GLUCOSE RANGE is only available in Clarity for Clinics. Use this page to customize the patient target range. Changes you make on this page only apply to this Clarity Clinic view and do not change any settings on the patient's Dexcom CGM system or the patient's personal Clarity account. All the interactive reports are available to print, download or email for any date range.

Our general module on glucose sensors provides additional guidance on interpreting these reports effectively.

Sharing data with healthcare providers may seem daunting at first. Take it step by step, creating your Clarity account and exploring the options available. Remember that sharing data empowers your healthcare team to provide you with better care, making it worth the effort.

### ***5.3 Sharing data with family***

Using the Dexcom G6 app, you can share your data with up to 10 caregivers through the Dexcom Follow app.

The process involves these steps:

- The user of the Dexcom G6 app enables the Dexcom Share feature in their app and invites a caregiver.

- The caregiver needs to accept the invitation, install the Dexcom Follow app, and complete the setup process.

This video demonstrates how to enable the Dexcom Share feature and walks through the necessary screens to invite a caregiver. When creating the invitation, you can define specific settings for the caregiver, including data visibility and alert preferences. When the invitation is sent, the caregiver will receive an email with two options: to download the Dexcom Follow app or start following immediately if they already have the app. Upon accepting, they will see informational screens in the app, which they can accept. The caregiver can modify the suggested settings without affecting the patient's personal settings. However, the patient retains control over whether the caregiver can view glucose readings. This choice remains with the patient.

Sharing data with your healthcare provider and loved ones is a collaborative step towards better diabetes management. Remember, it takes a supportive network to navigate this journey successfully.
