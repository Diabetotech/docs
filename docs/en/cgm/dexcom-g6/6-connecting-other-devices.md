## **<u>6. Connecting other devices</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on connecting your Dexcom G6 sensor to other devices.

In this chapter, we explore the exciting possibilities of connecting your Dexcom G6 sensor with various devices, such as smartwatches, smart pens, and insulin pumps.

### ***6.1 Smart watch***

Dexcom provides extensive support for smartwatch integrations with the Dexcom G6 sensor, and we'll explore options with popular smartwatches such as Garmin, Samsung Galaxy, and Apple Watch.

To begin, you must activate your Dexcom Share account, which can be easily done by adding a follower in your Dexcom G6 app, even if you choose yourself as the follower.

Let's look at the setup process for each smartwatch.

**1) Connecting the Dexcom G6 app to your Garmin smartwatch.**

- Install the Garmin Connect app and Garmin Connect IQ app on your Android or iPhone.

- Download the Dex CGM Watch Face from the Connect IQ store on your phone.

- Enter your Dexcom username and password in the settings of the Dex CGM Watch Face.

- If the Dex CGM Watch Face doesn't appear automatically on your Garmin watch, you can manually select it from the available watch faces.

- Alternatively, you can use the Dexcom Widget or Dexcom Data Field from the Connect IQ store.

- If you have a Garmin Edge cycling computer, you can also display Dexcom <span class="mark">data using the Dexcom Data Field feature.</span>

- <span class="mark">Garmin recently introduced a new feature allowing you to place dexcom readings on the main screen of the Garmin smartwatch. You can find more information about this feature on Garmins website.</span>

**2) Connecting the Dexcom G6 app to your Samsung Galaxy smartwatch.**

- Install the Galaxy Wearable app on your Android phone.

- Download the Blose app on your smartwatch and enter your Dexcom credentials directly.

- Find and install the Blose watch face from the available options.

<span class="mark">Alternatively, you can explore the following options for Samsung Galaxy Smartwatch integration:</span>

- G-Watch Wear app: Install the G-Watch Wear app on both your phone and smartwatch, and enter your Dexcom credentials via settings.

- Wearable Widgets app: Combine the Wearable Widgets app with the G-Watch Wear app to display Dexcom data as a complication or a tile on your Samsung Galaxy smartwatch.

- Dexcom G6 app via Wear Installer (2) app: If you prefer a typical Dexcom watch face on your smartwatch, you can install the Dexcom G6 app on your phone using the Wear Installer (2) app. Additional steps and customization are involved in this method.

**3) Connecting the Dexcom G6 app to your Apple Watch**

- Open the Watch app on your iPhone, find the Dexcom G6 app, and install it on your Apple Watch.

- Once installed, access the Dexcom app on your watch from the app icons, displaying your Dexcom sensor graph.

- You can also integrate Dexcom sensor data into a complication on a modular watch face. Keep in mind that Apple Watch complication<span class="mark">s might only</span> update information every 15 to 45 minutes, and tapping the complication will show a larger Dexcom sensor graph with the most recent blood glucose reading.

- Alternatively, download the Sugarmate app from the App Store to display sensor data as a calendar appointment. Follow the app instructions, connecting Sugarmate to your Dexcom account and creating a new calendar in your iCloud account.

- On some modular watch faces, you can change the middle complication to "my calendar" to view real-time Dexcom data.

Please note that the setup steps may vary based on the type of phone and smartwatch you use. To simplify the process, a downloadable PDF with tutorial links is provided below this video. It's generally easier to follow the setup with the actual devices in front of you rather than trying to understand abstract instructions.

### ***6.2 Smartpens***

Currently, the Dexcom G6 app does not have direct compatibility with any smartpens. Howev<span class="mark">er, there is a workaround available. You can install the Glooko app and link it to your Clarity account. Additionally, in the Glooko app, you can connect your smart insulin pen, for example a Novopen 6 or Echo Plus pen, or a Tempo Smart or SoloSmart Button.</span>

<span class="mark">By logging into Glooko, you'll have access to integrated data from both Dexcom and Novopen, providing a convenient solution for monitoring your glucose levels and insulin data in one place.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">6.3 Smart insulin pumps</span>***

<span class="mark">Dexcom G6 is widely used in automated insulin delivery systems, including Tandem Control IQ, Omnipod 5, mylife Loop system, Diabeloop, and the iLet Bionic pancreas.</span>

<span class="mark">Moreover, Dexcom G6 is the preferred sensor for open-source automated insulin delivery systems. While DIY Loop, iAPS and Trio users don't need additional tools, AndroidAPS users require a third-party app like xDrip+ to transmit Dexcom G6 data to the AndroidAPS app.</span>

It's important to note that using open-source apps is not supported by Dexcom, nor is it approved by the FDA or European authorities. Engaging in this practice is entirely at your own risk. While we don't recommend it, we believe in being transparent about all available options.

Embrace the possibilities of connecting your Dexcom G6 sensor to other devices, as they allow you to personalize your Dexcom G6 experience and optimize your diabetes management strategy.
