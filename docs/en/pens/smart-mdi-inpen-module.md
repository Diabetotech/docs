### **2. Set Up the Smart MDI System**

#### ***2.1 Components of the Smart MDI System***

In this section, we’ll review the core components that make up the Smart MDI system — a connect<span class="mark">ed solution that links insulin dosing with real-time glucose monitoring.</span>

<span class="mark">The system consists of:</span>

- <span class="mark">The Simplera CGM: A wearable continuous glucose monitor that transmits glucose data every 5 minutes to the Simplera app on the user’s smartphone.The InPen: A reusable, Bluetooth-enabled insulin pen designed for rapid-acting insulin. It connects with the InPen app, which features</span>

- <span class="mark">An integrated bolus calculator and smart insulin dose reminders.</span>

- <span class="mark">A standard long-acting insulin pen: Since the InPen is for rapid-acting insulin only, basal insulin must be injected using a separate pen. Users are encouraged to manually log their long-acting insulin doses in the InPen app to maintain a complete data record.</span>

- <span class="mark">Data sharing tools:</span>

  - <span class="mark">CareLink allows healthcare teams to access insulin and CGM data remotely.</span>

  - <span class="mark">And the CareLink Connect app enables real-time glucose sharing with up to five care partners, such as family members or caregivers.\
    </span>

<span class="mark">The InPen is thoughtfully designed for usability and sa</span>fety:

- It includes a dose knob for selecting insulin in half-unit increments, offering precision dosing for both adults and children.

- It accom<span class="mark">modates a 3 mL insulin cartridge. Two versions are available:</span>

  - <span class="mark">The blue InPen is compatible with aspart insulin Novo Nordisk cartridges</span>

  - <span class="mark">The grey InPen is compatible with Lilly cartridges</span>

- <span class="mark">The pen has a built-in Bluetooth transmitter, which logs each dose automatically to the app.</span>

- <span class="mark">It contains a non-rechargeable battery th</span>at lasts approximately one year. No charging is required.

Together, these components bring intelligence and automation to diabetes therapy with multiple daily injections — making it easier to track insulin doses, avoid insulin stacking, and make informed insulin decisions based on real-time glucose trends.

#### ***2.2 Set Up the Simplera Sensor & App***

This section explains how to set up the Simplera CGM and start using the Simplera app to view and track glucose data.

<span class="mark">The Simplera app is available to download on specific Android and iOS devices. You can check the compatibility online at</span> <span class="mark">[<u>medtronic-diabetes.com</u>](http://medtronic-diabetes.com/).</span>

<span class="mark">When you open the app, one of the first things you'll need to do is create or log into your CareLink account. Once you've signed in, you'll need to accept a user agreement. Then the app will take you through some phone settings. It will ask if you want to share your data and teach you how to customize alerts. Then it is time to insert and pair your sensor. The app will take you through the onboarding education.</span>

<span class="mark">The Simplera sensor is approved for placement on the back of the upper arm. Children from 2 to 17 years may place the sensor on the upper thirds of the buttocks.</span>

<span class="mark">The sensor has a built-in-Bluetooth transmitter, so make sure Bluetooth is enabled on your smart device. Each time you insert a new sensor, it’s important to remove the old sensor from your Bluetooth list.</span>

<span class="mark">Begin by washing your hands and disinfecting the insertion site. Allow your skin to air dry completely before proceeding. Remove the cap and place the inserter on the back of your upper arm. You can reach this area by either lifting your arm or reaching around it. Make sure all edges of the inserter touch your skin. To insert the sensor, press the inserter against your skin in a fluent movement until you hear a click. Gently pull the inserter away from your body in a straight line. Smooth down the sensor adhesive with your finger to ensure the sensor stays on the body for the entire length of wear. You may use over the counter tape if needed to help the sensor stick better.</span>

<span class="mark">Dispose of the inserter in accordance with local laws and regulations, but keep the serial number and sensor code until expiration time as you may need them in case of sensor failure. In that case you can call Medtronic’s customer service for a replacement.</span>

<span class="mark">When you've inserted the inserter you can tap "I inserted it" on the Simplera app. Confirm the sensor serial number and enter the sensor code, and the app will connect to the sensor.</span>

<span class="mark">After the 2-hour warmup time, you will start getting glucose readings every 5 minutes. Note that the sensor readings within the first 12 hours are not always reliable (as in most glucose sensors from any other brand). So check with finger pricks during the warmup time and also within the first 12 hours if you don’t feel confident about the glucose shown on the screen. When necessary you can calibrate the sensor.</span>

<span class="mark">The homescreen shows the current glucose value and trend value, and also a sensor graph.</span>

<span class="mark">Below there are 4 buttons: settings, sensor, logbook and events.</span>

- <span class="mark">Via the settings you can customize alert and sync data with CareLink and caregivers</span>

- <span class="mark">Via sensor, you can see the lifespan of your sensor.</span>

- <span class="mark">When you tap the sensor and then options, you can replace your sensor or enter an optional calibration.</span>

- <span class="mark">Via events you can log different events like blood glucose for calibration, insulin, carbs and exercise. These icons can be found on the sensor graph and also sync to CareLink.</span>

<span class="mark">To customize alarms on your Simplera app, navigate to the settings menu.</span>

- <span class="mark">Use the toggle bar in the settings menu to change the alert volume. Here, you can also opt to mute all alerts for a duration of maximum 4 hours.</span>

- <span class="mark">Via Glucose Alerts, you can set your Low alerts, High alerts, fall & rise alerts and snooze time.</span>

- <span class="mark">For the Low Alerts, you can choose to set them at maximum volume at night or create a Predict Low alert.</span>

- <span class="mark">The Simplera app also has status alerts that provide information about actions needed to ensure the correct functioning of the system. Make sure to act on these alerts to continue to receive sensor glucose information in the future.</span>

- <span class="mark">It is important to note that when power saving settings are enabled, the mobile device may delay the alerts and notifications from the Simplera app.</span>

A separate Simplera CGM module is available for users or educators seeking more advanced configuration tips, troubleshooting support, or use-case guidance.

#### ***<span class="mark">2.3 Prepare the InPen Device</span>***

<span class="mark">This section explains how to prepare the InPen smart insulin pen for use, insert the insulin cartridge, attach a needle, and administer a dose safely and effectively.</span>

<span class="mark">Step 1: Insert the Insulin Cartridge</span>

<span class="mark">Before starting, wash hands thoroughly with soap and water.</span>

- <span class="mark">Remove the cartridge holder by twisting it to the right.</span>

- <span class="mark">Turn the internal screw counterclockwise until it stops to make space for the cartridge.</span>

- <span class="mark">Insert the 3 mL insulin cartridge (small end first) into the cartridge holder.</span>

- <span class="mark">Reattach the cartridge holder by twisting it to the left until it clicks.\
  </span>

<span class="mark">Step 2: Attach the Pen Needle</span>

- <span class="mark">Use a new, sterile pen needle. Remove the peel tab and push the needle straight onto the pen — not at an angle.</span>

- <span class="mark">Screw the needle on securely.</span>

- <span class="mark">Remove the outer needle cover and save it for later disposal.</span>

- <span class="mark">Remove the inner needle shield and discard it safely.</span>

<span class="mark">Step 3: Prime the Pen</span>

<span class="mark">Priming ensures proper insulin flow and removes air from the system.</span>

- <span class="mark">Dial a few units.</span>

- <span class="mark">Hold the pen upright.</span>

- <span class="mark">Press the injection button fully to release insulin.</span>

- <span class="mark">A small drop should appear at the needle tip. If not, repeat the priming step until it does.</span>

<span class="mark">Step 4: Select and Prepare the Injection Site</span>

<span class="mark">Choose an appropriate subcutaneous injection site, such as the abdomen, thighs, buttocks or back of the upper arms.</span>

- <span class="mark">The skin should be clean and dry, and free of scars, bruises, tattoos, or lipodystrophy</span>

- <span class="mark">Injection site rotation is essential. Divide each area into zones and rotate weekly. Space each injection at least one finger-width apart from the previous one.</span>

- <span class="mark">If hands are washed and skin is visibly clean, disinfection with alcohol is usually unnecessary. However, alcohol swabs are recommended if the area is visibly dirty, sweaty, or if injections are administered in a hospital or care facility. Allow alcohol to dry completely before injecting.</span>

<span class="mark">Step 5: Administer the Insulin Dose</span>

- <span class="mark">Dial the prescribed dose.</span>

- <span class="mark">Insert the pen at a 90° angle into the skin. Avoid injecting through clothing.</span>

- <span class="mark">Press the injection button all the way down and count slowly to 10 before removing the pen.\
  </span>

<span class="mark">Step 6: Remove and Dispose of the Needle</span>

- <span class="mark">Place the outer needle cap back on.</span>

- <span class="mark">Unscrew the needle and dispose of it in a sharps container.\
  </span>

<span class="mark">Best Practices and Clinical Tips</span>

- <span class="mark">Check insulin type and expiration date before use.</span>

- <span class="mark">Allow cold insulin to warm to room temperature for 30 to 60 minutes prior to injection to reduce pain and risk of lipodystrophy.</span>

- <span class="mark">Never store the InPen in the refrigerator.</span>

- <span class="mark">Use 4 mm needles for most patients to reduce risk of intramuscular injection. If using 6 mm needles, apply the skin-lift technique (gently pinch a 5 cm or 2 inch skin fold).</span>

- <span class="mark">Prefer thin, sharp needles (32 gauge or higher) for comfort.</span>

- <span class="mark">Avoid needle reuse. While occasional reuse is common and may not increase pain in the short term, repeated use increases the risk of infection, injection site trauma, lipodystrophy and injection pain due to dull or bent needles. If reuse is unavoidable, limit it to no more than 4–5 times and monitor for skin changes.</span>

- <span class="mark">Avoid pressing the needle too firmly into the skin; the base should rest lightly without causing an indentation.</span>

<span class="mark">Recognizing Unsuccessful Injections</span>

<span class="mark">Watch for signs such as:</span>

- <span class="mark">Insulin leakage at the site or from the needle tip</span>

- <span class="mark">Intradermal injection, indicated by a small raised bump under the skin</span>

<span class="mark">If these occur, the insulin dose may not have been fully delivered. Log the event in the app, and if this happens repeatedly, advise follow-up with a healthcare provider.</span>

<span class="mark">With these preparation steps and best practices in place, users can administer rapid-acting insulin using the InPen safely and confidently.</span>

#### ***2.5 Share your data***

Sharing glucose and insulin data can support collaborative diabetes care and enable timely intervention. The Smart MDI system allows users to share data with healthcare professionals and care partners through the CareLink platform.

Sharing with Healthcare Providers

To enable clinical review of CGM and insulin data, users must connect their CareLink Personal account to their provider’s CareLink Clinic account. There are several ways to establish this connection:

- Option 1: Link Code Provided by Clinic =\> The clinic creates a patient profile using name and date of birth, then generates a unique link code. The user enters this code into their CareLink Personal account to complete the connection.

- Option 2: Username-Based <span class="mark">Follow Request =\> The provider sends a follow request using the patient’s CareLink username. The user.can approve this request directly in the Simplera app. To do so, they navigate to settings, CareLink, Manage Care Partners, where they can view and approve follow requests.</span>

- <span class="mark">Option 3: Direct Login (In-Clinic Setup) =\> If the user is present at the clinic, the provider can link accounts directly by entering th</span>e user’s CareLink login credentials — with consent.

Once connected, insulin and CGM data from both the InPen app and Simplera app are available to the provider via CareLink Clinic, supporting remote monitoring and therapy adjustments.

Sharing with Family Members or Caregivers

In addition to clinical sharing, the Sim<span class="mark">plera app support</span>s real-time glucose sharing with up to five care partners, such as parents, partners, or trusted friends.

To set this up:

1.  The care partner downloads the CareLink Connect app on their phone.

2.  They create a Care Partner account and send a follow request using the user’s CareLink username.

3.  The user accepts the request in either the Simplera app (under Manage Care <span class="mark">Partners) or through their CareLink Personal account.\
    </span>

<span class="mark">Once approved, care partners can view real-time glucose values and trend arrows, historical data and receive alerts. This feature can enhance safety and peace of mind — particularly for children, older adults, or people with impaired hypoglycemia aware</span>ness.

With data sharing in place, users benefit from greater connectivity across their care network, helping to align support and clinical decisions with real-time insights.

### **3. Use the Smart MDI System**

<span class="mark">In this module, we will walk you through the process of using the Smart MDI System.</span>

#### ***3.1 Using the InPen App***

The InPen app serves as the central hub for insulin management in the Smart MDI system. By integrating insulin dosing with CGM data from the Simplera app, it enables more informed decision-making, timely boluses, and reduced risk of insulin stacking.

Using the Bolus Calculator

Before a meal, users can open the bolus calculator to determine an appropriate dose of rapid-acting insulin.

- Begin by entering the number of carbohydrates in the upcoming meal. If the user is using meal estimation or fixed-dose strategies, they can select a meal type instead.

- The most recent CGM glucose value and trend arrow are automatically displayed.

- <span class="mark">Based on the user’s therapy settings the app calculates a recommended dose. The dose is rounded to the nearest 0.5 units.</span>

<span class="mark">To review how the dose was calculated, tap the arrow on the recommendation banner. This reveals the formula, including carb coverage and any correction.</span>

<span class="mark">If the glucose value is below target, the app will not recommend insulin. Instead, it will advise the user to treat the low with fast-acting carbohydrates.</span>

<span class="mark">Delivering and Adjusting Doses</span>

<span class="mark">The InPen device automatically transmits each dose to the app via Bluetooth.</span>

- <span class="mark">To adjust a dose before injection, turn the dose knob forward or backward.</span>

- <span class="mark">If the user decides not to inject, return the dial to zero — no insulin is</span> delivered until the injection button is pressed.

<!-- -->

- Start with a prime dose (to clear air out of the needle) when placing a new needle, thereafter you inject the recommended dose.

- After injection, the app logs the dose and classifies it as either a priming dose or a therapeutic dose. If needed, the user can edit the dose entry in the Logbook by selecting the dose and adjusting the dose type.

<!-- -->

- If you also use any rapid-acting insulin from a device other than the InPen system, it must be logged manually in the app. The dose calculator won't account for unlogged doses and may suggest too much insulin. So for safety, it is important to log all rapid-acting insulin taken.

Logging Long-Acting (Basal) Insulin

Since long-acting insulin is delivered with a separate pen, you need to ensure that the name of the Long-Acting Insuline, the Usual Amount and the Administration time is registered in the Therapy Settings. An administration alert will be shown in the Home screen. In Notifications you turn the Long-acting Reminder ON. By default you can still register it manually in the app:

- Navigate to the Logbook, tap Register Dose, select Long-acting insulin, and enter the dose and administration time.

This ensures that reports and insulin-on-board estimates remain complete and clinically meaningful.

Actionable Alerts

The InPen app includes two intelligent alerts to support timely insulin delivery and prevent prolonged hyperglycemia:

1.  The Missed Dose Alert is triggered when glucose is rising quickly and no recent mealtime insulin has been logged. The alert uses a rate of change detection algorithm to detect meals. It prompts the user to open the calculator, enter the meal, and administer a bolus. Responses are tracked in the InPen therapy reports.

2.  The Correct High Glucose Alert is activated when glucose levels are elevated and the insulin on board is not enough to bring them down. It is triggered when a correction bolus exceeding the predefined threshold is needed to reach the glucose target. This alert prompts the user to open the bolus calculator and deliver an appropriate correction dose.

These alerts can be configured under Settings \> Notifications, where users can:

- Enable or disable alerts, and adapt day and night Notification Volumes

- Adjust high glucose thresholds

- And limit alerts to daytime hours or allow them at night

Using these actionable alerts — instead of traditional high alerts from the Simplera app — can reduce alert fatigue and improve adherence. Clinical data show that people who respond to these actionable notifications achieve better glycemic outcomes than those who do not.

InPen Reports

The InPen app generates detailed, exportable reports that summarize:

- Glucose sensor and insulin delivery data

- Missed doses and use of the bolus calculator

- And basal and bolus insulin trends

These reports are explored in more detail in Section 4.

### **4. Report Interpretation**

<span class="mark">Welcome to this lesson wherein we will discuss the report interpretation.</span>

#### ***4.1 Where to Find Reports***

One of the key advantages of the Smart MDI system is its ability to generate comprehensive, data-rich reports. These reports help both people with diabetes and their care teams identify patterns in insulin use, glucose trends, and therapy effectiveness — supporting informed clinical decisions.

Reports are accessible via the InPen app or via CareLink Personal or CareLink Clinic.

1\. To access reports in the InPen app:

- Tap the Reports tab at the b<span class="mark">ottom of the home scre</span>en

- Select a date range (e.g., 1 week, 2 weeks, 1 month, or 3 months)

- Export the report as a PDF if needed — for example, to send to a healthcare provider before a consultation

2\. To access reports via CareLink Personal or Clinic:

- Select the Reports section

- Navigate to the InPen tab

- Choose a date range

- Click Generate Report

- Reports can also be printed or downloaded for review with the healthcare team.

The InPen Insights Report t<span class="mark">ypically consists of three pages: 1 page with a summary overview and 2 pages with daily charts</span>

<span class="mark">At the top of the summary page, four circular charts provide a quick snapshot of key therapy metrics:</span>

1.  <span class="mark">Time in Range and average glucose</span>

2.  <span class="mark">Missed bolus doses per day</span>

3.  <span class="mark">Bolus calculator usage</span>

4.  <span class="mark">And total daily insulin, broken down into basal and bolus insulin. Priming doses are not used for this calculation.</span>

<span class="mark">Other metrics displayed in this section are the Glucose Management Indicator, the glucose variability, and the average amount of rapid-acting boluses per day.\
</span>

<span class="mark">The report then displays the Ambulatory Glucos</span>e Profile alongside the user’s current insulin therapy settings, including the glucose target, carb ratio, insulin sensitivity factor, duration of insulin action and maximum bolus setting.

The Long-Acting Insulin Assessment evaluates basal insulin coverage based on the identified fasting window — the time period with minimal or no rapid-acting insulin doses.

<span class="mark">It includes:</span>

- <span class="mark">Median bedtime and fasting glucose values</span>

- <span class="mark">Days included in the assessment - as days with overnight boluses are excluded</span>

- <span class="mark">The incidence of nocturnal hypoglycemia</span>

- <span class="mark">And median overnight glucose trend</span>

<span class="mark">The Meal Assessment is divided by standard mea</span>l times: breakfast, lunch, and dinner.

Each graph displays:

- Glucose patterns following bolus administration

- It only includes boluses recorded during defined meal time windows

- And excludes correction doses or boluses administered outside mealtimes

Each daily chart provides a visual timeline of insulin delivery and glucose trends, including:

- CGM glucose values plotted over 24 hours

- Rapid-acting insulin doses, shown as shaded blue curves

- Long-acting insulin, shown as blue circles

- Carb entries or meal annotations

- And missed doses, indicated by gold-colored bands

Bolus entries include icons to indicate user behavior:

- A tick icon for doses that match the calculator recommendation

- An arrow up or down for doses that exceed or are below the recommendation

- Or no icon if the calculator was not used

#### ***4.2 How to Interpret Reports***

This section outlines a step-by-step framework for interpreting data from the InPen Insights Report and associated CareLink reports. The goal is to identify patterns, assess insulin effectiveness, and guide data-driven therapy adjustments.

Step 1: Assess Glucose Cont<span class="mark">rol</span>

<span class="mark">Begin with the Summary page:</span>

- <span class="mark">Review Time in Range: The recommended target for most people with diabetes is more than 70% time in range and less than 4% time below range, with less than 1% below 54 mg/dl (or 3 mmol/l).</span>

- <span class="mark">Evaluate bolus frequency. The number of meal boluses per day should correspond to the individual’s usual eating pattern (typically 3 times per day).\
  </span>

Step 2: Optimize Insulin Settings

Use the Ambulatory Glucose Profile to spot patterns of highs or lows:

- For daytime trends, check if highs or lows are linked to meals in the Meal Assessment and Daily Charts.

- For overnight trends, use the Long-Acting Assessment to see the trend of the overnight glucose and the amount of nocturnal hypoglycemia before adjusting your basal insulin.

Step 3: Evaluate User Behavior and Alert Response:

Use the behavioral indicators in the report to assess therapy engagement:

- Missed dose count: Frequent missed boluses are associated with increased glycemic variability and reduced time in range.

- Bolus calculator usa<span class="mark">ge: Ideally, more than 70% of insulin doses should be administered using the bolus calculator. Skipping the calculator may result in inconsistent dosing or incorrect corrections.</span>

Also evaluate how users respond to actionable alerts (like missed meal alerts or correct high alerts). Addressing alert fatigue may require simplifying alert settings or reinforcing the importance of timely responses.

Step 4: Review Safety and Back-up Planning

Ensure that:

- Insulin types and settings in the app reflect the user’s actual prescriptions.

- A backup plan is in place, such as:

  - A manual pen for insulin administration if the InPen fails

  - Awareness of how to manually track insulin when offline or between devices

Healthcare professionals should use these reports during consultations to:

- Support goal setting

- Adjust therapy safely

- Reinforce positive behaviors

- And provide visual feedback on how glucose responds to insulin dosing decisions
