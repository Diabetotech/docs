## **<u>2. TouchCare Nano according to the CARES paradigm</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on the TouchCare Nano System, structured according to the CARES paradigm.

In this session, we'll delve into the system's insulin delivery calculations, adjustment capabilities, procedures for reverting to manual mode, user education essentials, sensor features, data sharing functionalities, indications for use, and software updates. Let's begin!​

### ***2.1 TouchCare Nano according to the CARES paradigm***

#### 

#### ***\#1 Calculate***

The TouchCare Nano system has an AI-enabled algorithm that adapts insulin delivery every two minutes. It combines two advanced control strategies—hybrid proportional-integral control and model predictive control. This allows the system not only to respond to current glucose levels but also to anticipate future trends and adjust proactively.

A key strength of the algorithm is its self-learning capability. It continuously analyzes glucose, meal, and insulin <span class="mark">data from the past two to six days and incorporates broader patterns from up to 90 days — it learns on a rolling basis. This enables highly personalized insulin delivery that evolves over time based on the user’s individual needs.</span>

<span class="mark">Users can select a target glucose level of 100, 110, or 120 mg/dL or 5.6, 6.1, or 6.7 mmol/l.</span>

For meal management, the system offers two options. The user can manually input the grams of carbohydrates using the bolus calculator, or simply announce the type of meal—such as breakfast, lunch, dinner, or a snack—via the Auto Meal Handling feature. The algorithm then calculates the appropriate insulin response.

#### ***\#2 Adjust***

The TouchCare Nano system allows for a range of personalized settings, giving users and care teams flexibility to fine-tune therapy.

- The target sensor gluco<span class="mark">se can be set to 100, 110, or 120 mg/dL—or 5.6, 6.1, or 6.7 mmol/L—depending on individual goals.</span>

- <span class="mark">Carbohydrate ratio and insulin sensitivity settings can be adjusted within the bolus calculator. However, in Auto Mode, it is primarily the carbohydrate ratio that influences insulin delivery, while changes to insulin sensitivity have minimal effect. Users can also modify traditional settings like basal rates, and blood glucose targets, and insulin-on-board time within the bolus calculator. However, these settings do not affect insulin delivery in Auto Mode.</span>

<span class="mark">Only the initial basal rates serve as the starting point for Auto Basal when Auto Mode is first activated—any manual changes made afterward do not influence how Auto Basal adjusts insulin delivery.</span>

Similarly, within the Auto Mode settings, users can enter their weight and total daily insulin dose, but these values also do not influence insulin delivery once Auto Mode is active.

For physical activity, the TouchCare Nano system includes an Exercise Activity feature. When this is activated, the system will automatically reduce insulin delivery to help lower the risk of hypoglycemia during exercise. Unlike some other closed-loop systems, the Exercise Activity in TouchCare Nano doesn’t use a specific target glucose level. Instead, it simply adjusts insulin delivery downward for the duration you’ve set, based on your activity needs.

#### ***\#3 Revert***

<span class="mark">In certain situations, the TouchCare Nano system may temporarily switch to manual mode.</span>

- <span class="mark">This can occur if sensor glucose data is not received for three hours, or if maximum or minimum insulin delivery limits are reached and sustained.</span>

- <span class="mark">When this happens, the system automatically reverts to the user’s preset basal rates, allowing the pump to operate in manual mode.</span>

- <span class="mark">Once sensor communication is restored, the user must manually re-enter Auto Mode.</span>

<span class="mark">If the connection between the insulin pump and the PDM or phone is lost, Auto Mode will still continue as long as the CGM maintains a stable connection with the Patch Pump, since the transmitter can send sensor glucose data directly to the pump base.</span>

#### ***\#4 Educate***

User education is key to effective use of the TouchCare Nano System.

The general education points covered in previous modules include:

- Using fewer carbohydrates to correct hypoglycemia.

- Considering infusion set problems if blood glucose levels remain high without an apparent reason.

- Trusting the automated insulin delivery system and avoiding unnecessary manual interventions.

- Timing meal boluses 15 minutes before meals.

- <span class="mark">Utilizing Exercise Mode 1 to 2 hours</span> before activity.

- Responding to alarms and being mindful of automatic updates on mobile phones.

- And seeking peer support for guidance and sharing experiences.

Alongside general diabetes education, it's important to approach the TouchCare Nano system with a bit of extra mindfulness. Since it's a newer technology, we don’t yet have <span class="mark">extensive data on its long-term safety and effectiveness.</span>

<span class="mark">That’s why it’s a good idea to occasionally perform an additional blood glucose finger prick—such as once a day in a fasting state when using Auto Mode, and anytime the user doesn’t feel well or their symptoms don’t match the sensor readings. This simple step can help ensure ongoing safety and confidence in the system.</span>

#### ***\#5 Sensor*** 

If there are doubts about the accuracy of sensor readings—especially if they don’t match how the user is feeling—it’s important to perform a confirmatory finger prick test. If there is a significant discrepancy, such as a difference greater than 20% between the sensor and finger prick values, even when glucose is stable and within the target range, calibration of the sensor may be necessary. If the difference persists, it is best to exit Auto Mode and consider replacing the sensor.

With the TouchCare Nano system, the CGM can be calibrated using a finger prick value. However, calibration should only be done when sensor readings are stable and within range, to avoid introducing further inaccuracy.

#### ***\#6 Share*** 

The TouchCare Nano system makes it simple to share glucose and insulin data with healthcare providers and family members, enabling real-time monitoring for added safety and support.

Users of the EasyPatch app benefit from automatic cloud syncing, while those using the PDM will need to download the EasyTouch app and connect it to their device to enable real-time data uploads.

For family members and caregivers, the EasyFollow app provides remote access to both glucose and pump data, helping them stay informed and respond if necessary.

Healthcare professionals can access and review data via the EasyVi<span class="mark">ew Pro</span> web platform, supporting more informed clinical decisions and personalized care.

### ***2.2 Indications***

The TouchCare Nano System is indicated for individuals aged 2 years and older who require insulin therapy.

<span class="mark">It is approved for use with fast-acting insulins—Humalog, NovoRapid, Admelog, Apidra, Fiasp and Lyumjev—in their 100 units per milliliter concentration.</span>

<span class="mark">Please note that the TouchCare Nano system does not have an FDA label at this time, and it is not approved for use in pregnancy and during dialysis.</span>

<span class="mark">It’s important to note that advanced features</span> like Auto Mode and Auto Meal Handling can only be activated following a consultation with the healthcare provider. Once approved, a company representative can unlock these features within the app or PDM.

Updates can be applied via the EasyPatch or EasyTouch apps and do not interrupt the delivery of basal insulin during the process.</span>

## **<u>3. Linking components together</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on how to establish connections between the different components of the TouchCare Nano System.

The TouchCare Nano System operates as follows:

- Glucose levels are measured every 2 minutes by the TouchCare Nano continuous glucose monitor, or CGM. These readings are sent to either the <span class="mark">TouchCare Nano Pump.</span>

- <span class="mark">The APGO algorithm on the PDM or EasyPatch app calculates the insulin requirement based on the most recent data and communicates with the TouchCare Nano patch pump—either the 200U or 300U model—to deliver the appropriate amount of insulin.</span>

- <span class="mark">Glucose data and insulin delivery can be monitored and adjusted via the PDM or the EasyPatch app on your Android or iPhone.</span>

- <span class="mark">All system data can be shared in real-time with caregivers and family through the EasyFollow app, and healthcare providers can access it via the EasyView web</span>

- For users of the PDM, it’s important to use the EasyTouch app to collect and sync data to the cloud, ensuring access and sharing of treatment information.

In previous modules, you’ve learned how to pair the TouchCare Nano CGM with the PDM and the EasyPatch app, and how to link the patch pump to these platforms.

In this guide, we’ll focus on three additional connection steps:

- Setting up an EasyView account

- Uploading PDM data via the EasyTouch app

- <span class="mark">And en</span>abling real-time data sharing via the EasyFollow app

Let’s get started.

### ***3.1 Creating an EasyView account:***

When setting up the TouchCare Nano System through the EasyPatch or EasyTouch app, you’ll be prompted to either create a Medtrum account or log in to an existing one. This same email and password can also be used to access your data via the EasyView platform.

To view your glucose trends, insulin delivery history, and device settings, simply visit [<u>https://easyview.medtrum.eu</u>](https://easyview.medtrum.eu) and log in with your Medtrum credentials.

Once logged in, a navigation bar will appear on the left side of the page. By selecting Settings, you can customize how reports and data are displayed.

If you're a healthcare provider, you can register for a professional account through EasyView Pro. After submitting your application, The Medtrum team will contact you and confirm that you are a healthcare professional administrator. After that you will receive your password in an email. You can change the password after signing in for the first time.

First, click on patients to go to the patient list page to send a request so that you can follow a patient. Click find patient. You will now need to enter the email address of the patient you wish to follow. Enter the email address and click search. The patient's details will appear on the next screen to the right of the patient's details. You will see a box which says send request. Click this box and the request to follow will be sent to the patient. Once the patient has accepted your follow request their name will appear in your patient list.

To view a patient's data you will need to click on their name. From here you will need to click the settings tab on the left of your browser page to select Data To View. ### ***3.2 Uploading your PDM data via the EasyTouch app***

If you're using the Personal Diabetes Manager (PDM), you can upload your data to the EasyView platform using the EasyTouch app.

The EasyTouch app is available for most iPhones and Android devices. To check if your phone is compatible, visit [<u>medtrum.com/compatibility</u>](https://www.medtrum.com/compatibility). You can find the app by searching “Medtrum” in the App Store or Google Play.

Once you open the app, it will prompt you to connect your PDM—either by scanning its QR code or manually entering the serial number.

To upload your data to the Medtrum cloud, open the main menu of the EasyTouch app, go to Settings, and select Upload Data. You’ll see four upload options: Last week, Last month, Last three months, and All data. Choose the desired time range and tap Upload Data. Once the Bluetooth connection between the PDM and the EasyTouch app is established, the data transfer will begin. When you see the “Upload Successful” message, your data will be available on the EasyView platform and on the Statistics page of the EasyTouch app.

Once the request is approved, the caregiver will be able to view real-time glucose values, insulin delivery information, and overall system status—providing peace of mind and an extra layer of support.

## **<u>4. How the TouchCare Nano System works</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on the functionality of the TouchCare Nano System.

In this guide, we will explore how to use the TouchCare Nano System’s features in both auto mode and manual mode, both on the PDM as on the EasyPatch app.

### ***4.1 Auto Mode*** 

The TouchCare Nano System automatically adjusts insulin delivery in real time, based on continuous glucose readings from the CGM.

Please note: Auto Mode and Auto Meal Handling can only be activated following a discussion with your healthcare provider. Once approved, a company representative must enable these features within the system.

#### ***Starting Auto Mode:***

- Auto Mode will only work if your basal pattern and bolus calculator have already been set up. And keep in mind, it can’t be turned on if insulin delivery is currently paused.

- To start Auto Mode from the PDM, go to the main menu, tap EasyLoop, then tap Auto Mode. Slide the switch to turn it on. You’ll be asked to enter your target sensor glucose, your weight, and your total daily insulin dose. Once Auto Mode is active, your Basal menu will change to an Activity menu, and a green shield icon will appear on your home screen. If the system is delivering 30% more or less insulin than your usual basal rate, you’ll see an arrow over the shield. If insulin delivery is suspended, a red line will appear across the icon. If you ever want to turn Auto Mode off again, you can do it from the same screen.

- If you’re using the EasyPatch app, go to the main menu, tap EasyLoop, then Auto Mode. Switch it on, and again enter your target glucose, weight, and total insulin dose. You’ll see the same green shield icon once it’s active.

#### ***Starting Auto Meal Handling:***

- Auto Meal Handling is a feature that adjusts insulin automatically when you announce that you’re having a meal like breakfast, lunch, dinner, or a snack.

- To turn it on from the PDM, go to the main menu, tap EasyLoop, then Auto Mode. In that screen, you’ll see an option to switch on Auto Meal Handling. You’ll need a password to activate it. Once it’s on, you can select which meal you’re having from the Activity Menu.

- In the EasyPatch app, go to the main menu, tap EasyLoop, then Auto Mode. You can switch on Auto Meal Handling there too. When it’s active, the bolus icon at the bottom of your home screen will change into an activity icon. Just tap it to announce your meal.

#### ***Starting Exercise Activity:***

- You can also start an Exercise activity which will reduce your insulin delivery during exercise.

- On the PDM, go to the main menu and tap Activity. If Auto Meal Handling is on, you’ll see options like breakfast, lunch, dinner, snack, and exercise. Tap Exercise and choose how long you want it to last—from 30 minutes up to 12 hours. Then slide to confirm.

- On the EasyPatch app, open the main menu, tap Events, and then tap Exercise<span class="mark">, or go to the Activity shortcut button directly from the home screen. Pick ho</span>w long your activity will last and slide to announce it.

#### ***Adjusting Parameters:***

- Th<span class="mark">e</span> settings that can be adjusted during Auto Mode are the target sensor glucose, the ca<span class="mark">rb ratio and the insulin sensitivity factor.</span>

- On the PDM, you can change your target glucose directly from the Auto Mode screen by going to EasyLoop in the main menu. To change your carb ratio, go to Settings, then Insulin Pump, then Bolus Setup, and finally Bolus Calculator Setup.

- On the EasyPatch app, go to EasyLoop in the main menu to adjust your target glucose. To change your carb ratio, go to Settings, then Insulin Pump, then Bolus Calculator Setup.

#### ***Bolus Delivery:***

- When it’s time to give a bolus, you have two options. If you’re using carb counting, you can use the bolus calculator. On the PDM, just tap Bolus in the main menu and follow the steps. If you’re using Auto Meal Handling, go to the Activity menu and select your meal.

- In the EasyPatch app, tap the bolus icon on the home screen to use the calculator. Or if Auto Meal Handling <span class="mark">is on, go to the Activity shortcut button on the home screen and choose your meal type.</span>

#### ***Suspending Insulin:***

- Sometimes you might want to pause insulin delivery. The system can also do this on its own if it predicts a low glucose.

- On the PDM, tap Suspend from the main menu. You can also resume insulin from the same place.

- In the EasyPatch app, go to Insulin Delivery from the main menu and tap Suspend. You’ll be able to set how long you want insulin paused, then slide to confirm. The circle around your glucose reading will turn red while insulin is suspended. To resume, go back to the same screen and tap Resume.

### ***4.2 Manual Mode and Safety Features***

When Auto Mode is turned off, the system works in Manual Mode. In this mode, you can set your own basal rates or use temporary basal rates, and deliver insulin boluses either manually or by using the bolus calculator.

For meals that are high in fat or eaten over a longer period of time, you can also use the extended or combination bolus options to better match insulin delivery with your food intake.

The system also includes a Predictive Low Glucose Suspend feature. If your glucose levels drop too low—or are predicted to go low within the next 30 minutes—the system will automatically suspend insulin delivery. This setting can be adjusted in the EasyLoop menu. Insulin will resume automatically when glucose levels stabilize, or after a maximum pause of two hours.

By understanding how both Manual Mode and Auto Mode work, along with the built-in safety features, you can get the most out of the TouchCare Nano System and feel more confident in your diabetes management. For detailed steps and settings, always refer to your device manual or consult with your healthcare provider.

### ***5.2 Interpreting Reports*** 

Understanding your reports is key to making the most of your TouchCare Nano System.

<span class="mark">The most useful report to start with is the Dashboard Report. This gives you a clear summary of your recent glucose control, insulin delivery, and auto mode performance. It includes:</span>

- <span class="mark">Your average glucose level, estimated HbA1c, average total daily insulin dose, and average recorded carb and exercise entries.</span>

- <span class="mark">An Ambulatory Glucose Profile</span>

- <span class="mark">Time In Range, Time Below Range, and Time Above Range</span>

- <span class="mark">Sensor usage and average insulin reservoir usage</span>

- <span class="mark">Time spent in Auto Mode and the reasons for any exits</span>

Other important reports are the Day by Day Overview and Device Settings Review.

Here’s a simple structure to follow:

1.  **Step 1: Review Glycemic Control\**
    Start with the Dashboard Report. Look at your Time In Range and Time Below Range over the past two weeks. In most cases, Time In Range should be over 70%, and Time Below Range should be under 4%. Also check how often boluses are delivered. At least three boluses per day is a good sign of consistent mealtime insulin. If Auto Meal Handling is used, make sure meals are being correctly announced.

2.  **Step 2: Fine-Tune Insulin Delivery Settings\**
    Use the Ambulatory Glucose Profile in the Dashboard Report to spot patterns of highs and lows. Then compare with the Day by Day Overview to see if these trends are linked to meals. If post-meal glucose is often high, check if carb counting is accurate and boluses are timed properly before adjusting the carb ratio. For patterns outside of meals, you may want to adjust the insulin sensitivity or glucose sensor target. Also consider other possible influences like physical activity, stress, alcohol, or missed meals.

3.  **Step 3: Encourage Good System Use\**
    Check the Dashboard Report to see how much time the system is in Auto Mode, how often the CGM is being worn, and whether carbs and activities are regularly entered. Daily reports can help identify whether people are overcorrecting highs or lows, or if Exercise mode is being used properly. You can also use the Alert Report and Device Settings Review to see if any alerts are frequent or if alarm settings need adjustment.

4.  **Step 4: Confirm Safety and Backup Plans\**
    Look at the basal rate programmed in Manual Mode and compare it with the average basal delivered in Auto Mode. If there’s a big difference, adjust the manual basal rate by about 10% pe<span class="mark">r time block. Adjusting the basal rate does not affect insulin delivery in Auto Mode, but it ensures a safe basal rate is in place if the system reverts back to manual mode. Always make sure an emergency plan is in place in case the pump stops working.</span> That includes access to insulin pens and knowing how to switch to backup insulin therapy.

<span class="mark">By following these steps, both users and healthcare providers can use EasyView reports to get more out of the TouchCare Nano System and make informed adjustments when needed.</span>

<span class="mark">If anything in a report is unclear, check the legend at the bottom of the report PDFs—it explains all the symbols and color codes.</span>

<span class="mark">And finally, if you’d like to check your data on the go, you can also use the EasyTouch or EasyPatch app. Just open the main menu and tap on Statistics. Here, you can scroll through different reports to view your weekly, monthly or quarterly Time In Range, along with other key insights.</span>

<span class="mark">It’s a simple way to stay connected to your data and make informed decisions about your diabetes management—anytime, anywhere.</span>

## **<u>6. Managing special situations</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on managing special situations with the TouchCare Nano System.

In this guide, we will provide specific tips on managing hypoglycemia, hyperglycemia, high-fat meals, exercise, illness, alcohol consumption, and travel with the TouchCare Nano System. These recommendations are designed to complement general diabetes management strategies and help you make the most of the system in various scenarios.

### ***6.1 Hypoglycemia*** 

With the TouchCare Nano System, you may require fewer carbohydrates to correct low blood sugar levels, especially if there is little insulin on board. You can monitor the insulin on board from the home screen of the PDM and EasyPatch app. For mild lows, treating with 10 grams or fewer of carbohydrates does not require entering these carbs into the system.

### ***6.2 Hyperglycemia***

For mild highs, trust the system’s automated corrections and avoid entering false carbs. If needed, you can administer a correction bolus using the bolus calculator by entering zero grams of carbohydrates. For persistent high blood sugar (over 270 mg/dl or 15 mmol/l) without improvement, check for pump issue<span class="mark">s. Change your pump if necessary and consider using an insulin pen if the problem continues. Keep spare infusion sets and insulin pens on hand.</span>

<span class="mark">The TouchCare Nano system will notify you if your glucose remains above 250 mg/dL (13.9 mmol/L) for more than three hours in auto mode.</span>

### ***6.3 High-fat Meals*** 

When eating high-fat meals without Auto Meal Handling, you can enter 100% of the carbs, or only 70% and allow the system to manage the remainder. <span class="mark">Alternatively, you can split the bolus—half before the meal and half two hours later. The Touc</span>hCare Nano system does not offer extended or combo insulin boluses during Auto Mode.

### ***6.4 Exercise*** 

Before exercising, it’s recommended to activate Exercise Activity about 1 to 2 hours before your planned activity and to keep it on during the exercise itself.

If you’re not using Auto Meal Handling, and you plan to exercise within 2 hours after a meal, you might also consider reducing your mealtime bolus by 25 to 33%. This can help lower the risk of low blood glucose during physical activity.

### ***6.5 Illness***

Most users can stay in Auto Mode during illness. However, if you experience prolonged hyperglycemia or elevated ketones, consider switching to manual mode and consult your healthcare team for specific adjustments. If you suspect sensor inaccuracies confirm glucose levels with finger pricks.

### ***6.6 Alcohol***

Be mindful of increased hypoglycemia risk after alcohol consumption. Instead of eating additional carbs, consider using the Exercise Activity for a few hours after drinking to help maintain stable glucose levels.

### ***6.7 Travel*** 

When traveling with the TouchCare Nano System, carry sufficient supplies: extra Reservoir Patches, sensors, insulin pens, charger for the PDM and transmitter, and a blood glucose meter with strips. If you use the PDM, remember to adjust the time clock settings when entering a new time zone.

In conclusion, managing special situations with the TouchCare Nano System involves flexibility, ongoing learning, and working closely with your healthcare team. For additional support, consider joining diabetes communities and online support groups for example on FaceBook, where you can share experiences and gather practical advice from others using the system.
