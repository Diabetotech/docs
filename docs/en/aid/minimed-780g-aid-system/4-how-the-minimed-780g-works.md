## **<u>4. How the MiniMed 780G works (7.5 min)</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on the functionality of the MiniMed 780G insulin pump.

In this video, we will explore the usage of the MiniMed 780G insulin pump in both auto mode and manual mode. Additionally, we'll cover the utilization of the MiniMed Mobile app, which functions as a view app.

### ***4.1 Auto Mode***

#### ***Starting SmartGuard Auto Mode***

To enable Auto Mode or the "SmartGuard function" in the MiniMed 780G, go to the shield icon in the menu, and turn the SmartGuard on. Before using the SmartGuard function, the pump must be in manual mode for a minimum of 48 hours starting at midnight, allowing it to consider the total daily insulin dose from the past 2-6 days.

Once the SmartGuard function is active, a blue shield will be displayed on the screen, showing sensor glucose readings and active insulin at the bottom right. If there are issues turning on the SmartGuard function, you can refer to the SmartGuard checklist for troubleshooting.

In the sensor chart, you'll find a 3-hour representation with purple bars indicating auto basal and light blue bars representing autocorrection boluses. Use the back arrow to return to the previous screen.

Keep in mind that while you can't see the exact amount of basal insulin, you can get an idea from the purple bars on the sensor graph and the insulin on board displayed on your home screen.

#### ***Using Temporary Target***

In the SmartGuard menu, you can select the Temp Target of 150 mg/dl or 8.3 mmol/l if you’re planning to do exercise. You can activate it after you select a duration. The home screen will display a green bar indicating how long the Temp Target will be active.

#### ***Adjusting parameters***

In the SmartGuard Settings, you can change the target value to 100, 110, or 120 mg/dl <span class="mark">or 5.6**,** 6</span>.1 or 6.7 mmol/l. You can also choose whether to enable or disable autocorrections.

To modify the carbohydrate ratio and active insulin time, follow these steps:

- Access the Delivery Settings on your MiniMed 780G pump via the Insulin or Settings menu.

- In the Bolus Wizard Setup, you will be able to click on Carb Ratio and Active Insulin Time to change it.

- Don’t forget to change the settings if you change something.

#### ***Delivering a bolus***

In Auto Mode, bolusing is performed similarly to Manual Mode.

However, delivering a manual bolus, overriding the bolus recommendation, or delivering an extended bolus is not possible.

You can access the bolus screen via the Insulin menu or use the downward arrow as a shortcut to the bolus screen.

To give a meal bolus, you have to enter the grams of carbs you are going to eat.

If the Safe Meal Bolus feature prevents you from administering insulin for the carbohydrates you've entered due to low glucose levels, you should monitor your glucose trend. Once an upward trend is detected, retry entering the carbs to receive a suggested insulin bolus.

To give a correction bolus, you can enter 0 grams of carbohydrates.

#### ***Suspending insulin***

To temporarily stop the insulin, such as when disconnecting for showering or swimming, follow the same process as in manual mode. Select "Suspend all delivery" in the Insulin menu or in the Status screen which will appear if you click on the upward arrow.

To restart the basal insulin delivery, you can follow the same process.

### ***4.2 Manual Mode & Suspend-before-low***

In Manual Mode, you can set basal insulin rates, as well as configure temporary basal rates as needed. Furthermore, the pump allows for manual bolus administration and boluses using the bolus calculator, including both regular and extended boluses. For detailed instructions on using Manual Mode effectively, please refer to the module of the MiniMed 780G insulin pump.

Besides Auto Mode, the MiniMed 780G pump also has a built in Suspend-Before-Low Mode.

- This suspend-before-low feature halts ba<span class="mark">sal insulin delivery if the sensor glucose is below 70 mg/dl or 3,9 mmol/l above the low limit, and the algorithm predicts that it will drop below 20 mg/dl or 1,1 mmol/l above the lower limit within the next 30 minutes.</span>

- <span class="mark">After 30 minutes, insulin resumes if the sensor glucose is 20 mg/dl or 1.1 mmol/l above the low limit and the algorithm predicts it to rise more then 40mg/dl or 2.2 mmol/l above the low limit within the subsequent 30 minutes.</span>

- Insulin delivery will always resume after two hours and it can always manually be resumed. After a suspend-before-low episode, the function becomes unavailable for 30 minutes to avoid prolonged periods without insulin.

To activate the Suspend-Before-Low Mode, go to the Alert Settings and choose Low Alert. Set the desired low limit, enable “Suspend before low” and save the changes you made.

It's important to note that the suspend-before-low function can only be used in Manual Mode, and it's recommended to activate it as a safer way to deliver insulin when exiting Auto Mode.

### ***4.3 MiniMed Mobile app***

The MiniMed Mobile app allows users to track sensor and insulin data but does not provide the capability to adjust insulin dosages.

The app's home screen features a hamburger menu in the top-left corner. The status area displays sensor glucose readings and active insulin on board. <span class="mark">The sensor graph and insulin delivery, as seen on the pump, are also displayed.</span> Swiping to th<span class="mark">e left r</span>eveals your Time In Range of the last 24 hours.

Via the menu you can turn on notifications. This is necessary to receive notifications from your pump. The notifications will appear as a color coded banner on your phone. Red is for alarms, orange for alerts, grey for reminders and blue for messages.

Via the menu you can also check your synchronisation with CareLink and manage your care partners.
