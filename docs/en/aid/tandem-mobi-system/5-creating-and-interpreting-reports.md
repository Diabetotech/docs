## **<u>5. Creating and interpreting reports</u>**

Greetings and welcome to this video on Tandem Mobi System report creation and interpretation.

<span class="mark">We will discuss how to do this with the Tandem Source Platform.</span>

<span class="mark">In this video, we will explore the types of reports available in theTandem Source Platform, and discuss how to interpret them.</span>

### ***5.1 Types of Reports***

<span class="mark">In the Tandem Source Platform, you can find the reports when you tap the report section on the left sidebar.</span>

<span class="mark">Tandem Source offers three standard reports:</span>

- <span class="mark">Overview which provides a general overview of your therapy data,</span>

- <span class="mark">Daily Timeline that shows glucose readings, basal insulin delivery, and boluses day by day</span>

- <span class="mark">and Pump Settings that includes the Displays pump settings, including all Personal Profiles and predictive technology usage (depending on your pump software),</span>

<span class="mark">Overview Report: The Overview report offers a summary of your pump and therapy data. The following summary information may be included in the default Overview report:.</span>

- <span class="mark">Summary of CGM usage (e.g., average glucose reading, total time in target range, total time of use). If you do not have CGM data, BG entries on the pump appear instead.</span>

- <span class="mark">Time in Range comparison between the current selected time period and the previous time period.</span>

- <span class="mark">Summary of predictive technology usage (depending on your pump software).</span>

- <span class="mark">A glucose trends graph showing a summary of glucose values during the selected period, including the median and interquartile range. If you do not have CGM data, the glucose trends graph is not displayed.</span>

- <span class="mark">Summary of average daily insulin dosage, such as total insulin delivered, basal insulin delivered, and boluses delivered.</span>

<span class="mark">The second report from the Tandem Source platform is the Daily Timeline Report:</span>

<span class="mark">The Daily Timeline report shows glucose readings, basal and bolus insulin delivered, insulin suspension events, and other pump and therapy-related events that occurred during the selected date range.</span>

<span class="mark">The default view includes a key that defines crucial icons. Click Key to collapse the key.</span>

<span class="mark">The 3rd report is the Pump Settings report. This includes Personal Profiles for the most recent pump upload associated with your account. This report is read-only and cannot be used to change any settings in the pump itself. Pump settings shown on this report may include:</span>

- <span class="mark">Profiles used during the selected time period</span>

- <span class="mark">Tiles describing individual pump settings, including predictive technology settings (depending on your pump software), alert and alarm settings, and insulin delivery settings.</span>

<span class="mark">Different areas within Tandem Source allow you to open the Print screen and save or print any report:</span>

<span class="mark">Once the Print screen appears, select the desired date range and reports in the Select Reports window, then click Save Reports or Print Reports.</span>

### ***5.2 Interpreting reports***

When interpreting reports with Tandem Control-IQ technology, the primary focus lies on optimizing the basal insulin rate, correction factor, and carbohydrate ratio, as these are crucial adjustable parameters. However, it's important to recognize that there is always room for improvement in the user's interaction with the pump.

<span class="mark">This includes factors such as proper utilization of features like sleep mode and activity settings, adherence to bolus recommendations, and effectively managing infusion set changes. By continuously evaluating and refining these aspects, users can further enhance their experience and outcomes with Tandem Control-IQ technology.</span>

<span class="mark">Here's a roadmap for interpreting the reports:</span>

<span class="mark">1. Assess Glycemic Information: Begin by evaluating the TIR (Time in Range) and TBR (Time Below Range) over the past 2-4 weeks on the Overview Report.</span>

- <span class="mark">Ensure that treatment goals are being met, with TIR above 70% and TBR below 4%.</span>

- <span class="mark">It is important to pay attention to the frequency of meal bolusing, which is also indicated in the Overview Report. On average, there should be around 3 to 4 boluses per day for meals. If you notice an unusually high number of boluses, such as 20 boluses per day, it may indicate that the user doesn’’t trust the system and is constantly interfering with manual correction boluses.</span>

<span class="mark">2. Optimize Closed-Loop Settings: Analyze the AGP (Ambulatory Glucose Profile) on the Summary Tab to identify trends of hypo- or hyperglycemia.</span>

<span class="mark">Check if these trends are related to boluses on the Daily Timeline reports.</span>

- <span class="mark">If you notice trends after meal boluses, it's important to assess various factors such as timing and accurate carbohydrate counting, before adjusting the carb ratio.</span>

- <span class="mark">If you see a trend after manual or autocorrection boluses, you can try to adjust the correction factor.</span>

<!-- -->

- <span class="mark">And for trends outside of boluses, consider adjusting the basal insulin rate with 10-20% from 1 hour before the trend is noticed. Additionally, explore possible correlations with factors such as exercise, alcohol consumption, or hypoglycemia. Remember that with the Tandem Mobi Pump, it is possible to create a separate personal profile for special circumstances such as sports and illness.</span>

<span class="mark">In Tandem Control-IQ technology, it is important to ensure that the basal insulin rate is appropriately set. If you notice a consistent pattern of increasing or decreasing basal insulin during a specific period of the day the aim is to adjust the basal insulin rate at that time to align with th</span>e <span class="mark">user's true basal insulin requirements.</span> Keep in mind that it is generally recommended to aim for a basal bolus ratio between 40/60 and 60/40 %.

<span class="mark">3. Guide Behavioral Recommendations: To ensure the proper usage of the automated insulin delivery system, it is important to review various aspects in the reports available. Here are some key considerations:</span>

- <span class="mark">Check the sensor wear duration, time spent in auto mode, and adherence to the Sleep Activity. The sensor wear time and the time in automode should be more than 85%.</span>

  - <span class="mark">- The time in Sleep Activity should be approximately 8 hours a day or around 30% of the time. Ensure that a sleep schedule is set consistently to support effective glucose management during sleep.</span>

- <span class="mark">Also in the Overview Report: check the amount of carbs entered for meals, evaluate if bolus calculator overrides are low (ideally 0%), and check the if the infusion set replacement is done every 3 days.</span>

- <span class="mark">In the Daily Timeline Reports, you can assess the adherence to pre-meal bolusing, overcorrection of hypo- and hyperglycemia, and the appropriate use of Exercise and Sleep Activity.</span>

- <span class="mark">And finally on the Pump Settings report, you can assess the appropriateness of alarm settings and reminders in the Tandem pump. Verify that the settings align with the user's needs and preferences.</span>

<span class="mark">4. Review Pump Settings and Emergency Plans:</span>

- <span class="mark">Update weight and total daily dose of insulin at each check-up if necessary.</span>

- <span class="mark">Document all pump settings and establish an emergency plan, including insulin pen usage, in case of a pump malfunction. Ensure access to insulin pens.</span>

<span class="mark">In conclusion, the accurate interpretation of reports and ongoing communication with your healthcare provider are essential for maximizing the benefits of Tandem Control-IQ technology. By effectively utilizing these tools and staying informed, you can enhance your diabetes management and lead a healthy lifestyle.</span>

<span class="mark">Remember, with the right tools and support, you can optimize your experience with Tandem Control-IQ technology and achieve better control over your diabetes. Stay proactive and empowered in your journey towards optimal health and well-being.</span>
