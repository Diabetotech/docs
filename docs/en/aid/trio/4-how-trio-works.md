## <u>4. How Trio works (9 min)</u>

Welcome to this video on the functionality of Trio.

Controlling your insulin delivery with the Trio automated insulin delivery system is done through the Trio app which is installed on your iPhone.

The app offers two main operating modes: Closed Loop or Auto Mode and Open Loop or Manual Mode.

- In Closed Loop, Trio automatically adjusts insulin delivery every 5 minutes based on real-time sensor glucose readings.
- If Closed Loop is disabled, you are in Open Loop or Manual Mode, and only your scheduled basal will be administered without manual intervention.

In this video, we will delve into the functionality of the Trio app in general, and explore how you can use the Trio app in both Closed Loop and Open Loop Mode.

### 4.1 Trio app

When you use the Trio app for the first time, an onboarding wizard will help you learn about the app.

When you open the Trio app, you can see the Trio homescreen. It has four main sections: Status Bar, Graph & History, Adjustment Status, and Action Buttons.

Let's start with the Status Bar.

- Here, you'll see three elements: Pump Info, Current Glucose, Current Metrics, and the Loop Indicator.
- Pump Info shows your reservoir level and how much Pod life is left.
- Your current glucose value is displayed together with the trend information.
- The Loop Indicator should normally be green. If the last loop cycle was more than five minutes ago, it turns yellow. After more than ten minutes, it turns red. And when Closed Loop is disabled, the indicator appears grey.
- Right below that, you'll find the Eventual Glucose — this is a glucose forecast for up to 6 hours in the future. It is typically around 4-hours.
- The second row of the Status Bar shows your Insulin on Board, Carbs on Board, and your current basal rate. Keep in mind that Insulin on Board can be negative when the algorithm has reduced the scheduled basal rate.

Next, let's look at the Graph and History section.

- This area displays basal adjustments, current and past activity, active insulin, active carbs, and various stats. You can customize the graph scale and additional graph options in the Settings menu.
- If you tap on "Stats," you'll see detailed statistics for glucose, insulin, looping, and meals.
- If you tap on "Info," you'll see a full chart legend.

Below that is the Adjustment Status, which tells you whether a bolus, override or temp target is currently active. This is also where you can cancel an active bolus, override, or temp target.

Finally, at the bottom of the screen, you'll see five Action Buttons.

- "Main" brings you back to the homescreen.
- "History" shows your recent treatments, meals, glucose readings, and adjustments.
- The plus icon lets you enter carbs and boluses.
- "Adjustments" is where you can start or stop an override or temp target, and manage your presets.
- And in "Settings," you can check the software version, enable or disable Closed Loop, access the Trio configuration, and find support and community resources.

Now, let's walk through the Trio Configuration menu in detail. This section includes six parts: Devices, Therapy, Algorithm, Features, Notifications, and Services.

- First, Devices. Here you select your insulin pump, CGM, and smartwatch, and you can access all device-specific settings.
- Next is Therapy Settings. In this section, you set your unit type — mg/dL or mmol/L — and define your delivery limits. You can also enter your glucose target, basal rates, carb ratios, and insulin sensitivities. All of these can be configured in 30-minute intervals if needed.
- The third section is Algorithm. This is where you adjust settings related to Autosens, Super Micro Bolus, dynamic decisions, target behaviour, and other algorithm parameters. The additional algorithm settings normally don't need any changes. Only adjust them if you fully understand what they do. Every parameter includes a short description, and if you tap the information icon, you'll see a more detailed explanation.
- Next, we have Features. Here you can configure the bolus calculator, meal settings, shortcuts, and remote control. You can also customize the user interface and app icon. This section also lets you enable anonymised data sharing through app diagnostics.
- Then we move to Notifications. This is where you adjust all Trio app notifications and enable the Live Activity feature on your phone. Live Activity can appear as a dynamic island on your home screen or as a live view on your lock screen. You can also enable calendar events, which allow your glucose values to show up on your Apple Watch and in Apple CarPlay.
- Finally, Services. Here you can connect your Trio app with Nightscout, Tidepool, and Apple Health.

By navigating through these sections, you can effectively manage your diabetes using the Trio app.

### 4.2 Closed-Loop

Now that we know how the Trio app works, we will discuss how to use Trio in Closed Loop mode.

**Starting Trio Closed loop mode**

Once you have completed the Onboarding Wizard, paired a pump and a CGM, you can enable Closed Loop via the main settings screen.

**Use a preset Temporary Target or Override**

In Trio, you can set a Temp Target or an Override via the Adjustments icon.

Overrides also allow temporary adjustments of your basal rate, carb ratio, ISF and SMB settings, according to your individual needs and preferences.

**Adjusting parameters**

In Trio, you have the flexibility to change your basal insulin rate, carb ratio, insulin sensitivity, and target glucose.

**Giving an insulin bolus**

You can deliver meal insulin using the plus icon.

The Bolus Calculator interface is divided into four sections: the dynamic glucose forecast, the meal entry, the dosing calculation, and the Enact Bolus button.

At the top, you'll see the dynamic forecast graph. As you enter carbs or adjust the insulin amount, this graph updates in real time. It shifts the forecast to reflect the expected impact of your upcoming meal and insulin dose.

**Suspending insulin**

If you need to temporarily stop the insulin delivery, you can go to your Pump screen by tapping the pump icons at the top left of the homescreen or via the settings. From there, you can choose the option to "Suspend Insulin Delivery" for a duration ranging from 30 minutes to 2 hours. This will temporarily suspend the insulin delivery from the pump. Here you can also resume insulin delivery.

### 4.3 Open Loop

In rare situations, such as extremely high and false glucose readings, it may be necessary to switch to Manual Mode in Trio. In these situations, you may lose the ability to activate Dynamic ISF when you resume closed loop, due to lack of looping history.

You can go to Manual Mode by disabling Closed Loop via the Settings.

In Manual Mode, Trio still has manual operations:

- you can adjust the profile basal insulin rate, carb ratio, insulin sensitivity, and target glucose value via the Settings
- you can set a temporary basal rate in the pump menu
- And you can deliver a bolus via the plus icon.
- You will not get glucose predictions or low glucose prevention when in open loop. Only use open loop if absolutely necessary.

Alternatively, if the concern is settings-related, it's advised to stay in closed-loop and set Maximum Insulin on Board to 0. This will maintain low glucose prevention without allowing Trio to automatically administer any additional insulin above your current scheduled basal rate. It's best to go through the documentation and have stable core settings before enabling more advanced settings, like Dynamic ISF.

While it may seem daunting to adjust the system yourself, it is important to remember that you are not alone in this journey. The Trio community is there to support you every step of the way. So, have confidence, seek help when needed, and refer to the extensive documentation as you work towards achieving optimal glycemic control with Trio.
