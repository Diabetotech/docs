## **<u>2. Tandem Control-IQ technology according to the CARES Paradigm</u>**

Welcome to this lesson about the Tandem Control-IQ technology according to the CARES paradigm.

Tandem Control-IQ technology offers a range of features that make it an effective automated insulin delivery system for diabetes management. Let's explore some key points to further understand this system within the CARES paradigm, its indications and its software updates.

### ***2.1 Tandem Control-IQ according to the CARES paradigm***

#### ***\#1 Calculate***

Tandem Control-IQ technology modulates preset basal insulin rates based on the predicted glucose levels over the next 30 minutes.

Additionally, hourly autocorrection boluses are administered at 60% of the standard dose when glucose levels exceed 180 mg/dL or 10 mmol/L.

The target range, or treatment values as they are called by Tandem, are set between 112.5-160 mg/dL (6.25 and 8.8 mmol/L), with the goal of achieving optimal glycemic control during the day.

During sleep mode, the treatment values are further refined to 112.5-120 mg/dL (6.3-6.7 mmol/L), promoting stable glucose levels without autocorrected boluses.

#### ***\#2 Adjust*** 

Users can modify the following settings:

- the basal insulin rate (different profiles are possible),

- the carbohydrate ratio,

- and the correction factor or insulin sensitivity.

With the updated Control-IQ+ software, users can even set temporary basal rates from 0% to 250% for up to 72 hours while continuing to benefit from automatic insulin adjustments and correction boluses from the Control-IQ+ algorithm.

In addition to these adjustments, Tandem Control-IQ technology offers different glucose targets or treatment values for sleep and exercise.

- Sleep Activity: This setting aims for narrower treatment values of 112.5-120 mg/dL (6.25-6.7 mmol/L) during nighttime to maintain stable glucose levels without using autocorrected boluses.

- Exercise Activity: This setting is designed for periods of increased physical activity. It sets higher treatment values of 140-160 mg/dL (7.8-8.9 mmol/L) to accommodate the metabolic demands of exercise. Additionally, during exercise activity, the pump does use autocorrected boluses to help manage glucose levels effectively.

During the initial setup of Control-IQ technology, users will input their total daily insulin dose and weight. These values can be modified later to ensure accurate insulin delivery based on individual requirements.

While Tandem Control-IQ technology provides flexibility in adjusting various parameters, it's important to note that certain aspects remain fixed. The default treatment values of 112.5-160 mg/dL (6.25-8.9 mmol/L), the duration of insulin action set at 5 hours, and the target value for correction boluses fixed at 110 mg/dL (or 6.1 mmol/L).

#### ***\#3 Revert***

In the event that no sensor data is available for more than 20 minutes, the Tandem Control-IQ technology “auto mode” will transition to “manual mode”. This can occur due to issues such as a loss of connection with the transmitter or during sensor warm-up.

If there are connectivity issues between the pump and CGM, recommend wearing the pump on the same side as the CGM and positioning the pump with the screen facing outwards. Keeping the software up to date can also help improve connectivity.

I<span class="mark">f you’re using the Dexcom G6 sensor and pers</span>istent connectivity problems arise, consider replacing the Dexcom transmitter. A functional transmitter battery is crucial for maintaining a stable connection.

While in manual mode:

- The suspend-before-low function is not available, as it is specific to the Basal-IQ algorithm and not compatible with Control-IQ software.

- If Control-IQ technology spontaneously reverts to manual mode, the basal insulin rate of your person<span class="mark">al</span> profile will be limited to 3 units per hour. To increase the insulin dose to more than 3 units per hour in manual mode, you will need to deactivate the Control-IQ technology. This 3 units/hour limit does not apply to the updated Control-IQ+, which was specifically designed for users who require higher basal insulin rates.

Once sensor values become available again, the pump will automatically switch back to Control-IQ technology, ensuring the continuous operation of the automated insulin delivery system.

#### ***\#4 Educate***

When starting with an automated insulin delivery system like Tandem Control-IQ technology, it's important to be aware of both general education points and specific education points for this system. <span class="mark">The general education points covered in the Master Module on AID Systems include:</span>

- Use fewer carbohydrates to correct hypoglycemia.

- Consider infusion set problems if blood glucose levels remain high for an extended period without a clear reason.

- Trust the AID system and avoiding unnecessary manual interventions.

- Timing meal boluses 15 minutes before meals.

- Utilizing Exercis<span class="mark">e Mode 1-2 hours minu</span>tes before activity

- Stopping insulin delivery if the pump is disconnected for more than 15 minutes.

- <span class="mark">Responding to alarms and being mindful of automatic updates on mobile phones</span>

- Seeking peer support for guidance and sharing experiences.

Now let's look at the specific education points for Tandem Control-IQ technology:

- Using Sleep Activity every night can lead to better glycemic control. When planning to sleep for more than 5 hours, utilizing Sleep Activity is advisable. For short afternoon naps, Sleep Activity is not necessary.

- It's best to activate Sleep Activity close to your actual sleep time and ideally not within 2 hours of your last meal. This ensures you receive autocorrect boluses if needed after an evening meal.

- If you skip breakfast, it's acceptable to keep Sleep Activity on for a while, but it's not intended for use throughout the entire day. If you do have breakfast, it's recommended to disable Sleep Activity to allow for autocorrect boluses if necessary.

- After a meal bolus, the pump may prompt you to adjust the bolus based on sensor glucose levels. Read these instructions carefully and consider whether it's appropriate for your situation.

- While it's possible to override the bolus calculator, it's generally not recommended.

- Utilize different basal profiles to optimize insulin release during activities like sports or illness.

- In Glooko reports, the total daily insulin dose is divided into a percentage of basal and bolus insulin, and all manual and autocorrection boluses are added to the bolus percentage. In general, it’s recommended to aim for a balanced percentage of basal and bolus insulin, for example 40-60% basal insulin.

By following these education points, you can maximize the benefits of Tandem Control-IQ technology and achieve better diabetes management. It is normal to experience some trial and error in finding the right settings when you begin using this pump. Have confidence and consult with your diabetes nurse specialist for any necessary adjustments.

#### ***\#5 Sensor***

The Tandem with Control-IQ technology can be used with the Dexcom G6 or Dexcom G7 sensor. Depending on your location, there is also a possibility to use the FreeStyle Libre 2 Plus or 3 Plus sensor. You can easily switch between sensors.

<span class="mark">If you have doubts about the accuracy of your sensor reading or do not feel it reflects your condition, perform a finger prick test to confirm.</span>

<span class="mark">If a significant discrepancy, such as a more than 20% difference, exists and persists between the sensor and finger prick readings, even when the blood glucose is stable and within the target range, it is advisable to revert back to Manual Mode and or replace the sensor.</span>

<span class="mark">If you use a Dexcom sensor, you can first try to calibrate the sensor to improve the accuracy of your readings. It's important to note that if you calibrate the Dexcom sensor, it will need one calibration each day until a new sensor is applied.</span>

#### ***<span class="mark">\#6 Share</span>***

<span class="mark">With automated insulin delivery systems, it is possible to share insulin and glucose data with healthcare providers and family members.</span>

<span class="mark">In the case of Tandem Control-IQ technology, healthcare providers can check pump reports via Glooko or Tandem Source.</span>

<span class="mark">Family members can remotely monitor glucose levels through the Dexcom Follow app, provided that the user uses a Dexcom sensor and also has the Dexcom G6 or G7 app installed.</span>

<span class="mark">While real-time monitoring may not be available with Tandem Control-IQ technology, the ability to share data still facilitates effective communication and collaboration between users, healthcare providers, and family members, leading to better diabetes management outcomes.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">2.2 Indications</span>***

<span class="mark">Tandem Control has both a CE-label and FDA-label for use in individuals with type 1 dia</span>betes who are at least 6 years old. The FDA-label also allows use in children from 2 years old and in adults aged 18 years and older with insulin-dependent type 2 diabetes.

It is suitable for individuals with a weight of 25 to 140 kg (or 55 to 308 pounds) and a total daily insulin dose of 10 to 100 units. The updated Control-IQ+ algorithm is approved for individuals with a weight of 9 to 200 kg (or 20 to 441 pounds) and a total daily insulin dose of 5 to 200 units.

The system is approved for use with Novorapid, Novolog, Humalog or Lyumjev 100 U/ml insulins.

Please note that Tandem Control is not approved for use in pregnancy or in individuals requiring dialysis.

## **<u>4. How the Tandem Control-IQ technology works</u>**

Welcome to this video, where we will discuss how Tandem Control-IQ technology works.

The t:slim X2 insulin pump with Control-IQ technology offers two choices of operation: Automatic insulin delivery through Control-IQ technology and Manual pump, each providing distinct features and functions for diabetes management. Let's explore both modes in detail.

### ***4.1 Control-IQ technology***

#### ***Starting Control-IQ technology***

To activate the Control-IQ technology, ensure your pump is connected to your sensor and that carbohydrates are enabled in your personal profile's bolus settings. Access the Control-IQ technology feature by selecting "My Pump" in the options menu, then choose "Control-IQ technology." Here, you will input your total daily dose of insulin and weight. The adjustable values align with the indication of Control-IQ and Control-IQ+.

Once Control-IQ technology is enabled, you will notice a gray rhombus and a blue "B" icon on the home screen. The colors of these icons will change based on basal insulin rate adjustments made by Control-IQ technology:

\- If a predicted drop in glucose levels is detected, the basal insulin rate decreases, and an orange rhombus and orange "B" icon appear.

\- In the event of a predicted hypoglycemic episode, the basal insulin is temporarily paused. The rhombus turns red, and the "B" becomes a red "0." The CGM graph displays a red zone. Basal insulin resumes if the predicted glucose level is expected to exceed 70 mg/dl or 3.9 mmol/l within the next 30 minutes.

\- For predicted hyperglycemia, the basal insulin rate increases. The rhombus turns blue, and the "B" changes to white on a blue background.

\- If a glucose level above 180 mg/dl or 10 mmol/l is anticipated, an automatic correction bolus is delivered, and an insulin drip symbol appears. The active bolus can be seen in the insulin-on-board status section.

Pressing the sensor chart button displays a 3-hour sensor chart. The sensor graph doesn’t provide specific information about basal insulin adjustments, but insulin and autocorrect boluses will be represented by blue icons at the bottom.

To monitor your current insulin delivery, you can press the status icon located at the top right of the home screen. The status screen will display your current basal rate and your last insulin bolus.

#### ***Using Sleep & Exercise Activity*** 

<span class="mark">Via Activity u</span>nder the options menu, you can explore three additional settings: Activity, Sleep, and Sleep schedules.

\- Exercise: Activating this activity increases the treatment values to 140-160 mg/dl (7.8-8.9 mmol/l). A movement icon is displayed alongside the window on the home screen. You can manually start and stop the exercise activity or set specific duration.

\- Sleep: By enabling Sleep Activity, the treatment values are adjusted to 112.5-120 mg/dl or 6.25-6.7 mmol/l. A "zzz" icon appears next to the window on the home screen. You can manually start or stop sleep mode or set specific sleep schedules. Two schedules can be configured for fixed days of the week, such as weekdays and weekends, ensuring they don't overlap.

#### ***Adjusting Parameters***

In Tandem Control-IQ technology, you have the ability to customize the basal rate, carb ratio, and correction factor settings. To access these options, navigate to the Options menu on your Tandem pump and select "My Pump." From there, you can choose "Personal Profiles" to switch between different profiles or adjust the parameters within each profile within the "Timed Settings". This allows you to fine-tune your insulin delivery according to your specific needs and preferences.

#### ***Giving an insulin bolus***

When delivering an insulin bolus, the process remains the same with or without Control-IQ technology enabled. However, it's generally recommended to follow the pump's bolus suggestions. At the end of a bolus, you can choose to convert it into an extended bolus. Pay close attention to the pump's bolus recommendations, as adjustments may be suggested based on your current glucose levels.

#### ***Suspending insulin***

To temporarily stop the pump, like during showers or swimming, you can access the options menu and choose "Stop Insulin." You will receive an alarm indicating that insulin delivery is suspended, and you can set a delay of 15 to 60 minutes before receiving the alarm. Restarting basal insulin delivery can also be done via "Options".

### ***4.2 Manual pump***

In manual mode, you have full control over the basal insulin rate and bolus calculator settings. The instructions for this are covered in the module about the Tandem t:slim X2 insulin pump.

### ***5.2 Interpreting Reports***

When interpreting reports with Tandem Control-IQ technology, the primary focus lies on optimizing the basal insulin rate, correction factor, and carbohydrate ratio, as these are crucial adjustable parameters. However, it's important to recognize that there is always room for improvement in the user's interaction with the pump. This includes factors such as proper utilization of features like sleep mode and activity settings, adherence to bolus recommendations, and effectively managing infusion set changes. By continuously evaluating and refining these aspects, users can further enhance their experience and outcomes with Tandem Control-IQ technology.

Here's a roadmap for interpreting the reports:

1\. Assess Glycemic Information: Begin by evaluating the TIR (Time in Range) and TBR (Time Below Range) over the past 2-4 weeks on the Summary Tab.

\- Ensure that treatment goals are being met, with TIR above 70% and TBR below 4%.

\- It is important to pay attention to the frequency of meal bolusing, which is also indicated in the Summary Tab of Glooko. On average, there should be around 3 to 4 boluses per day for meals. If you notice an unusu<span class="mark">ally high number of boluses, such as 20 boluses per day, it may indicate that the user doesn’t trust</span> the system and is constantly interfering with manual correction boluses.

2\. Optimize AID Settings: Analyze the AGP (Ambulatory Glucose Profile) on the Summary Tab to identify trends of hypo- or hyperglycemia. Check if these trends are related to boluses on the Weekly Overview reports.

- If you notice trends after meal boluses, it's important to assess various factors such as timing and accurate carbohydrate counting, before adjusting the carb ratio.

- If you see a trend after manual or autocorrection boluses, you can try to adjust the correction factor.

- And for trends outside of boluses, consider adjusting the basal insulin rate with 10-20% from 1 hour before the trend is noticed. Additionally, explore possible correlations with factors such as exercise, alcohol consumption, or hypoglycemia. Remember that with the Tandem Pump, it is possible to create a separate personal profile for special circumstances such as sports and illness.

In Tandem Control-IQ technology, it is important to ensure that the basal insulin rate is appropriately set. If you notice a consistent pattern of increasing or decreasing basal insulin during a specific period of the day, the aim is to adjust the basal insulin rate at that time to align with the algorithm's requirements. Keep in mind that it is generally recommended to aim for a basal/bolus ratio of around 40/60 or 60/40%.

3\. Guide Behavioral Recommendations: To ensure the proper usage of the automated insulin delivery system, it is important to review various aspects in the reports available. Here are some key considerations:

- Summary Tab: Check the sensor wear duration, time spent in auto mode, and adherence to the Sleep Activity. The sensor wear time and the time in automode should be more than 85%.

  - It is normal that the time in “manual mode” is not 0%, as the pump switches to manual mode during the sensor warm-up period or temporary connection loss with the pump. A high time in manual mode is mainly caused by connectivity issues between the sensor and the pump. In that case, recommend wearing the pump on the same side as the CGM and positioning the pump with the screen facing outwards. Keeping the software up to date can also help improve connectivity. Remember that by default, Control-IQ technology has an automatic switch-off feature that activates after 12 hours of inactivity on the pump. Consider disabling this automatic switch-off function of Control-IQ technology, if you think it causes a lower time in auto mode.

  - The time in Sleep Activity should be approximately 8 hours a day or around 30% of the time. Ensure that a sleep schedule is set consistently to support effective glucose management during sleep.

- Also in the Summary Tab: Evaluate if bolus calculator overrides are low (ideally 0%). Also, check the amount of carbs entered for meals.

- Weekly Overview Report: Assess the adherence to pre-meal bolusing, overcorrection of hypo- and hyperglycemia, and the appropriate use of Exercise and Sleep Activity.

- On the Insights tab, you can check if the infusion set replacement is done every 3 days.

- Devices Tab: Assess the appropriateness of alarm settings and reminders in the Tandem pump. Verify that the settings align with the user's needs and preferences.

4\. Review Pump Settings and Emergency Plans:

- Update weight and total daily dose of insulin at each check-up if necessary.

- Document all pump settings and establish an emergency plan, including insulin pen usage, in case of a pump malfunction. Ensure access to insulin pens.

## **<u>6. Managing special situations</u>**

Welcome to this video on managing special situations with Tandem Control-IQ technology

In this video, we will provide specific tips for managing hypo- and hyperglycemia, fatty meals, sports, illness, alcohol and travel while using Tandem Control–IQ technology. These guidelines complement the general recommendations for managing these situations while wearing an automated insulin delivery system, which can be found in the general module on automated insulin delivery system.

### ***6.1 Hypoglycemia***

With Tandem Control-IQ technology, you may require fewer carbohydrates to treat or prevent hypoglycemia compared to when not using an AID system, especially if you have little insulin on board. You can check your insulin on board on the home screen and the exact amount of basal insulin you are currently receiving on the status screen.

If you consume less than 10 grams of carbohydrates to treat or prevent a hypo, there is no need to enter this in the pump.

To prevent hypoglycemia, you can use the Exercise Activity.

### ***6.2 Hyperglycemia***

Tandem Control-IQ technology is not a learning algorithm, so manual autocorrection boluses can be given in between. However, it is important to follow the bolus advice.

In cases of prolonged severe hyperglycemia, it is important to consider infusion problems.

To avoid infusion set problems, it is recommended to replace the infusion set in a timely manner and set a reminder for this on your pump.

### ***6.3 High-fat meals***

The Tandem Pump allows you to use an extended bolus. For fatty meals, it is generally recommended to deliver 50% of the bolus immediately and the remaining 50% over the next 2 hours.

This is the default setting for an extended bolus, but further individual adjustments may be needed.

### ***6.4 Exercise***

When exercising with Tandem Control-IQ technology, you can activate the Exercise Activity and reduce the meal bolus as discussed in the general module on automated insulin delivery systems.

It's important to note that autocorrection boluses are still given during Activity Mode.

You can also use a separate personal profile for sports during that time. This provides more options to adjust the insulin release according to your personal needs.

If you are using the updated Control-IQ+ software, you also have the option to set a dedicated temporary basal rate during exercise, providing additional flexibility to tailor insulin delivery to your individual needs.

### ***6.5 Illness***

During illness, hyperglycemia is common. Follow the guidelines for managing hyperglycemia in such situations.

It’s recommended to preset a personal profile dedicated to illness with a higher % of basal insulin rate and carb ratio.

If you are using the updated Control-IQ+ software, you also have the option to set a dedicated temporary basal rate during illness, providing additional flexibility to tailor insulin delivery to your individual needs.

### ***6.6 Alcohol***

Refer to the specific guidelines for managing your automated insulin delivery in relation to alcohol consumption.

### ***6.7 Travel***

When traveling with the Tandem t:slim X2 pump, you have the option to request an additional insulin "loaner pump" to accompany you during your holiday. Moreover, in case of an emergency or if your pump requires replacement, Tandem can send you a replacement insulin pump promptly.

<span class="mark">When travelling, be sure to also bring an extra Dexcom G6 transmitter if you’re using the Dexcom G6 sensor, and necessary charging equipment to keep your pump powered during the journey.</span>

<span class="mark">When travelling across time zones, you will need to manually</span> adjust the time on your Tandem t:slim X2 insulin pump.

By following these tips, you can effectively manage various situations while using Tandem Control-IQ technology and maintain optimal control over your diabetes. Remember to consult with your healthcare provider for personalized guidance and support in managing specific scenarios.
