## **<u>6. Managing special situations</u>**

Welcome to this video on managing special situations with the Omnipod 5 automated insulin delivery system.

In this video, we will provide specific tips on managing hypo- and hyperglycemia, dealing with high-fat meals, participating in sports, managing illness, **alcohol** and navigating travel while using the Omnipod 5 system.

These guidelines complement the general recommendations for automated insulin delivery systems. For a comprehensive understanding, refer to the general module on automated insulin delivery systems.

### ***6.1 Hypoglycemia*** 

With the Omnipod 5 system, managing hypoglycemia becomes more efficient. You may require fe<span class="mark">wer carbohydrates to raise your blood sugar levels compared to when not using an aut</span>omated insulin delivery system, especially if you have minimal insulin on board.

You can check the insulin on board displayed on the home screen of the Omnipod 5 app. In landscape mode, you can also see your recent insulin history, which can be helpful in assessing your insulin levels over the past few hours.

It's important to note that if you consume a small amount of carbohydrates for treating hypoglycemia, typically less than 10 grams, you don't need to enter this amount into the bolus calculator.

When planning physical activity, consider using the Activity Feature or temporarily setting a higher Target Value to prevent hypoglycemia. These adjustments help maintain stable blood sugar levels and reduce the risk o<span class="mark">f recurrent hypoglycemia. Also, consider using activity mode for several hours after particu</span>larly intense exercise in order to prevent delayed bouts of hypoglycemia.

### ***6.2 Hyperglycemia***

When it comes to mild hyperglycemia, the idea is to trust Omnipod 5 and let it work itself. However, you can bring down hyperglycemia faster if you administer a manual correction bolus via the bolus calculator. You can leave the carbs at 0 grams. Don’t forget to press the Use CGM button to also take the glucose trend into account.

When it comes to prolonged severe hyperglycemia, you should always think of an infusion set problem. When in doubt, it is best to change your pod and place it in a new site.. The motto is "If in doubt, change it out".

Make sure to carry enough equipment to do an emergency pod change.

### ***6.3 High-fat meals***

Here are some recommendations for managing a high-fat meal with Omnipod 5:

1.  It is generally advised to enter only 70% of the total carbohydrates consumed for a high-fat meal. The algorithm in the Omnipod 5 system will adjust the basal insulin delivery to handle the remaining carbohydrates and prevent post-meal hypoglycemia.

2.  Another strategy is to split the bolus dose by entering 50% of the calculated carbohydrates before the meal and the remaining 50% approximately 2 hours after the meal. This allows for a more precise insulin response to the slower digestion of high-fat meals.

3.  While Automated Mode in the Omnipod 5 does not allow for extended boluses, you have the option to switch to Manual Mode if you prefer to use an extended bolus feature. In Manual Mode, you can set an extended bolus to match the digestion rate of the high-fat meal.

By employing these strategies, you can effectively manage glucose levels when consuming <span class="mark">high-fat meals with the Omnipod 5 system. It is always recommended to consult with your healt</span>hcare provider for personalized guidance and adjustments based on your specific needs.

***6.4 Exercise***

When exercising with Omnipod 5, it is recommended to turn on the Activity Feature 1-2 hours prior to the onset of activity. If exercise takes place after a meal, consider reducing the suggested meal bolus as recommended in the general module on automated insulin delivery systems.

<span class="mark">If you’re using a Dexcom sensor, you can try to connect your Dexcom G6 or G7 app to your smartwatch or cycling computer for easier monitoring during exercise.</span>

<span class="mark"></span>

If you ingest small amounts of carbohydrates while exercising, you don't usually need to enter them in the Omnipod 5 app.

### ***6.5 Illness***

During illness you may notice more prolonged hyperglycemia. Consider additional manual correction boluses.

Remember that the actual total insulin dose will only be taken into account when you change your pod. To better manage blood sugar levels during illness, you may consider switching to Manual Mode and adjusting the temporary basal and/or basal insulin rate. You may also choose to set up a basal insulin profile specifically for use when you are sick.

Follow hyperglycemia guidelines for managing high blood sugar levels during illness.

### ***6.6 Alcohol***

When consuming alcohol, it is recommended to take proactive measures to prevent hypoglycemia. This can be done by temporarily increasing your target value and/or utilizing the Activity Feature to reduce insulin delivery.

It is important to note that increasing carbohydrate intake will not be effective in preventing hypoglycemia, as the system will compensate by increasing insulin administration to balance any rise in glucose levels.

### ***6.7 Travel*** 

When traveling with Omnipod 5, it is important to pack an ample supply of necessary equipment and accessories. Remember to bring your Controller charger and, if needed, an <span class="mark">adapter for the country you are visiting. While a spare Controller is not available, you can consider packing an extra Dexcom G6 transmitter if you’re using this sensor type.</span>

<span class="mark"></span>

If you are traveling by air, you can keep the Bluetooth functionality of your handset, pump, and transmitter active. However, the SIM card's Wi-Fi and 4G capabilities will not be operational during the flight. This should not affect the functioning of the Omnipod 5 app on your Controller or mobile device, as it will continue to work seamlessly via Bluetooth. However, data transmission to Glooko will be temporarily unavailable.

When crossing different time zones, the Omnipod 5 app should automatically adjust the time. However, it is always a good idea to double-check the time settings upon arrival at your destination.

During take-off and landing, some airlines may require you to put your Controller or phone in airplane mode. You can do this via the settings. Putting your Omnipod 5 app in airplane mode will not affect your automated basal insulin delivery.

It is important to note that the Omnipod 5 Controller is not waterproof. If you plan to go swimming, you can leave it in the changing room and temporarily turn it off using the ON/OFF button to avoid triggering unnecessary alarms due to the disconnection from the Pod. Or you can use a waterproof case, which allows you screen access even if you have wet hands.
