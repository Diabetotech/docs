## **<u>2. Omnipod 5 according to the CARES paradigm</u>**

Welcome to this video where we discuss the Omnipod 5 system according to the CARES paradigm.

We will discuss how Omnipod 5 calculates and adjusts the automated insulin delivery, when it reverts back to manual mode, specific education tips and sensor issues, and how it shares data. After that we will go over Omnipod 5’s official indications and software updates.

### ***<span class="mark">1) Omnipod 5 ac</span>cording to CARES***

### ***\#1 Calculate***

The Omnipod 5 system operates as follows:

- The SmartAdjust algorithm calculates the basal insulin requirement or “adaptive basal rate” every 5 minutes. It considers the predicted glucose levels for the upcoming hour, the target value and the total insulin delivered by the previous Pod. When predicted hyperglycemia is detected, the algorithm increases the insulin delivery. On the other hand, if hypoglycemia is predicted, the basal insulin delivery is reduced or paused. If the glucose level falls below 60 mg/dL (3.3 mmol/l), the basal insulin delivery is always paused to prioritize safety.

- When initiating the Omnipod 5 system, you will have to enter starting settings including basal rates. Automated mode can be started immediately and the system will base the magnitude of basal rate adjustments on the basal rate provided, although the maximum delivery is more constrained with the first Pod for safety.

- After 48 hours of use, and the first Pod change, the system will use the historical basal insulin delivery as a base for the automated basal insulin and further adjust to individual needs thanks to machine learning. The longer the system is worn, the better it performs, so it is recommended to wear it consistently for a few weeks to fully experience the positive impact on Time in Range. This also means that changes in health conditions or medication, such as sickness or starting a steroid medication, will have a more noticeable effect after a pod change. It’s important to note that if you switch Controllers, you will lose your insulin delivery history, and the system will need to re-learn your insulin requirements.

- The SmartAdjust algorithm does not provide additional autocorrection boluses. It uses basal rate adjustments to prevent and correct hyperglycemia.

- The target glucose range can be adjusted in 60-minute time blocks, allowing customization between 110 and 150 mg/dL (6.1 and 8.3 mmol/l).

- It’s important to note that the SmartBolusCalculator of the Omnipod 5 system does not only take into account the current glucose, but also the glucose trend.

### ***\#2 Adjust***

With the Omnipod 5 system, users have the ability to adjust certain parameters to personalize their insulin therapy:

- The target glucose range can be set within 60-minute time blocks, allowing customization between 110 and 150 mg/dL (6.1 and 8.3 mmol/L).

- The bolus calculator parameters, such as the carbohydrate-to-insulin ratio, correction factor, duration of insulin action, and "Correct Above" value, can also be adjusted. It’s important to note that these bolus calculator parameters will only have an impact on the manual mealtime or correction boluses, and not on the calculated automated or “adaptive basal rate”. Of these bolus calculator parameters, the carbohydrate-to-insulin ratio is the most influential parameter in terms of clinical impact.

- An optional Activity Feature is available, which sets a higher target glucose value of 150 mg/dL (8.3 mmol/L) and reduces insulin delivery to accommodate physical activity or times of increased risk of hypoglycemia.

### ***\#3 Revert***

In the Omnipod 5 system, there are situations that can trigger a "Limited mode," including a lack of sensor data for more than 20 minutes or a minimum or maximum insulin release for an extended period of time. During Limited mode, the system maintains a fixed basal insulin rate based on recent history and settings. The maximum basal insulin rate in Limited mode will never exceed the basal rate settings of Manual Mode.

A lack of sensor data can occur due to sensor warm-up or communication issues between the sensor and the Pod, or between the Pod and the Omnipod 5 app. To prevent communication problems, it is recommended to wear the sensor and the CGM within line of sight. If communication problems persist, the app will display a "No Pod Communication" message, and you should follow the on-screen instructions to resolve the issue. Bringing the Controller or mobile phone closer to the Pod, within a distance of 1.5 meters (or 5 feet), can help restore the connection between the Controller and the Pod. If connection restoration fails, you have the option to deactivate the Pod. Once the issue causing the Limited Mode is resolved, Automated Mode will automatically resume.

In addition, Limited Mode may be triggered by minimum or maximum insulin release continuously for an extended period of time, resulting in an "Automated Delivery Restriction" alarm. It is important to respond appropriately by confirming the accuracy of the sensor and reactivating Automated Mode.

When deactivating a Pod, the system automatically switches to Manual Mode. After activating a new Pod, you will be advised to restart Automated Mode again.

It's worth noting that connecting the OmniPod 5 app to the Pod is only necessary for delivering bolus insulin, activating and deactivating a Pod, and for responding to alarms. The algorithm resides on the Pod itself, meaning that even without communication between the Pod and the Omnipod 5 app, the basal insulin will be adjusted every 5 minutes based on the sensor glucose values and trends.

### ***\#4 Educate***

When starting an automated insulin delivery system like Omnipod 5, it's important to consider both general education points for AID systems and specific education points for Omnipod 5.

For general education, please refer to the generic module, which includes the following key points:

- Use fewer carbohydrates to correct hypoglycemia.

- Consider an insulin delivery/absorption problem if glucose levels remain high for several hours without an apparent reason. "If in doubt, change it out."

- Trust the AID system.

- Administer a meal bolus 15 minutes before meals.

- Activate Exercise Mode 1 to 2 hours before exercising.

- Respond to alarms promptly.

- Disable automatic updates on your mobile phone.

- And Seek peer support for guidance and sharing experiences.

Specific education points for Omnipod 5 are as follows:

- Checking the compatibility of your phone with the Dexcom G6 app before starting. If in your country, you have access to the Omnipod 5 mobile app and wish to use this instead of your Controller, you should also check the compatibility of your phone with this app.

- When using the Omnipod 5 app on your mobile phone, it is recommended to leave it open in the background and avoid closing it. This ensures continuous communication and data exchange within the Omnipod 5 system.

- <span class="mark">Wear the Pod and the CGM on the same side of your body within sight of each other to minimize connection problems.</span>

- During the initial weeks of using the Omnipod 5 system, you may encounter the "Automated Delivery Restriction" alarm more frequently due to the limited maximum insulin delivery while using the first couple of Pods. However, as you continue using the system, this occurrence will decrease. It is important to respond promptly to this alarm by confirming the accuracy of the sensor and returning to Automated Mode.

- Press "Use CGM" to ensure that the bolus calculator considers the trend arrow.

- Brief interruptions in basal insulin delivery may occur if glucose levels are trending downward, even if they are above the target value. This is a normal occurrence with Omnipod 5's algorithm and typically lasts only 5 to 10 minutes unless glucose continues to fall.

### ***\#5 Sensor***

<span class="mark">The Omnipod 5 can be used with the Dexcom G6, G7 or the FreeStyle Libre 2 Plus sensor. You can easily switch between the sensor type.</span>

<span class="mark">If you have doubts about the accuracy of your sensor reading or do not feel it reflects your condition, perform a finger prick test to confirm.</span>

<span class="mark">If a significant discrepancy, such as a more than 20% difference, exists between the sensor and finger prick readings, even when the blood glucose is stable within the target range, it is advisable to switch to Manual Mode and / or replace the sensor.</span>

<span class="mark">The Dexcom sensors can be calibrated if a significant discrepancy exists between the sensor reading and finger prick results, assuming stable glucose levels.</span>

### ***\#6 Share***

With Omnipod 5, you have the ability to share insulin and glucose data with your healthcare provider and family members. The Omnipod 5 app on your Controller or on your phone are continuously connected to your Glooko account and send data to the cloud every 5 minutes, eliminating the need for manual data downloading. Healthcare providers can access this data through their professional Glooko accou<span class="mark">nt.</span>

<span class="mark">Family members can monitor the user’s glucose sensor values in real time via the Dexcom Follow app when a Dexcom sensor is used, or via the LibreLinkUp app when the user is wearing a FreeStyle Libre sensor. However, connectivity to the LibreView and LibreLinkUp cloud is only available in certain geographic regions.</span>

<span class="mark">Currently, there is no dedicated follow app for Omnipod 5, and the Omnipod View and Display app is not compatible. However, there is an experimental integration with Gluroo that enables one-way synchronization of insulin activity entered on a compatible phone running the Omnipod 5 app. This allows you to see insulin doses from the Omnipod 5 on any phone using Gluroo. Please note that this app is not approved or supported by Insulet, and also doesn’t have a CE label or FDA approval. Its use is at your own ri</span>sk.

### ***2) Indications***

Omnipod 5 has both a CE-label and FDA-label for use in individuals with type 1 diabetes who are at least 2 year<span class="mark">s old. In addition, it holds FDA approval for adults aged eighteen years and older with type 2 diabetes who use insulin.</span>

It is suitable for individuals with a minimum total daily insulin dose of 5 units.

The system is approved for use with Novolog, Novorapid, Humalog or Admelog 100 U/ml insulins.

Please note that Omnipod 5 is not approved for use in pregnancy or in individuals requiring dialysis.
