## **<u>4. How the Omnipod 5 system works (11.5 min)</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on the functionality of the Omnipod 5 system. Controlling the insulin delivery is done through the Omnipod 5 app on your Controller or on your mobile phone. Currently, the Omnipod 5 app for phone control is only available in the US, but broader roll-out is expected.

In this video, we will explore how to use the Omnipod 5 app in general, and how to use it in both Automated and Manual Mode.

### ***4.1 Omnipod 5 App***

The initial setup of the Omnipod 5 app needs to be completed on your Controller.

- During this process, you will be required to provide necessary details, including your Omnipod ID and Glooko account information. To create an Omnipod ID, you can visit Insulet's website at www.omnipod.com/setup. Creating an Omnipod ID is free of charge and is necessary for accessing the app and website functionalities. Once you have obtained your Omnipod ID, you can use it to log into Insulet's website and access their online training resources.

- When setting up the Omnipod 5 app on your Controller, you will also be prompted to confirm whether you have completed the appropriate training. After completing this step, you can choose to proceed with the setup either on the Controller or on the Omnipod 5 app installed on your mobile phone.

- Please note that switching between the Controller and mobile phone is only <span class="mark">possible when starting a new pod. Additionally, when you uninstall and reinstall the Omnipod 5 app on your phone, or change the Controller, all the data and history accumulated by the Pod will be lost, and the algorithm learning restarts. If necessary, you have the option to clear the data within your existing controller to restart the algorithm.</span>

- <span class="mark">For the Controller, you will choose a PIN, security message, and background, while the mobile app PIN will match your phon</span>e's PIN.

- If you choose to use the Omnipod 5 app on your mobile phone, there are specific settings that you need to consider for optimal functionality:

  - Enable Bluetooth.

  - Grant location permission to the app.

  - Allow notifications from the Omnipod 5 app.

  - Ensure that the "do not disturb" option is disabled.

  - Disable any battery-saving mode.

  - To utilize the bolus calculator, set a PIN code or a similar security measure on your mobile phone.

Using the Omnipod 5 app, as well as the Dexcom G6 <span class="mark">or G7 app,</span> should not significantly impact the battery life of your device. Both apps utilize Bluetooth Low Energy for communication, which is designed to be energy-efficient.

Internet connectivity is essential for receiving updates and automatically uploading data to Glooko. The Controller comes with a SIM card for internet access, while the mobile app utilizes your data plan. It's advisable to keep your Wi-Fi turned on.

The home screen of the Omnipod 5 app on the Controller or mobile phone resembles the Omnipod DASH's home screen with some differences:

- An additional mode indicator is displayed at the top right, showing either Automated, Manual, Limited or No Pod Communication.

- In Automated or Auto Mode, the icon and letters are purple, while in Manual Mode, they are blue.

- The CGM curve can be accessed at the bottom right, providing insulin on board information by pressing the question mark.

### ***4.2 Automated Mode***

#### ***Starting Omnipod 5 Automated Mode***

To enable Automated Mode on the Omnipod 5 app, go to the settings and tap on Switch Mode. Note that Automated Mode can only be activated if the Pod and sensor are properly paired.

If Automated Mode is functioning properly, the mode indicator on the home screen will change to "Automated," and the dominant color on the app will be purple.

In landscape mode, a pink line will appear at the bottom of the screen indicating that the basal insulin is automated based on the sensor glucose. Note that you cannot directly view the exact basal insulin delivery. If you wish to see this information, navigate to Settings, History Detail then Auto Events.

#### ***Using Activity Feature***

For physical activities, you can utilize the Activity Feature:

- Access the menu and select "Activity."

- Set a duration between 1 and 24 hours, then press "Start."

- The middle tab will turn light green and display "Activity." Tapping the tab will show the remaining duration and allow you to cancel the activity feature.

- Once the set time limit is reached, the system will automatically turn the Activity Feature off.

#### ***Adjusting parameters***

The Omnipod 5 system allows for adjustments in the target value and several parameters for when you use the bolus calculator.

- To modify the target value, you can navigate to the menu and select "Settings," then choose the "Bolus" option. Here you can adjust your target value per 60-minute time block.

- The bolus calculator parameters such as carb-to-insulin ratio, correction factor, duration of insulin action and “correct above value” can also be found here. It's important to note that any modifications made to these parameters will only impact the insulin doses calculated when using the bolus calculator feature. The reverse correction option is also available, which recommends a reduction to the meal dose of insulin when the glucose is lower than the target value.

Additionally, when initiating the Omnipod 5 system, you will need to enter a basal rate (or basal rates), which will be used by the algorithm to determine the adaptive <span class="mark">basal rate for the first pod. After 48 hours of use, and the first Pod change, the system will use the historical bas</span>al insulin delivery as a base for the automated basal insulin. If you are unsure of how to determine a starting basal rate, consider settings with approximately 50% of insulin needs going to the basal rate and the other 50% for bolus insulin.

#### ***Giving an insulin bolus***

When administering a meal bolus, follow these steps:

- Press the bolus button.

- Enter the number of grams of carbohydrates you will be consuming.

- The bolus calculator in the Omnipod 5 app automatically retrieves the sensor glucose for calculation. However, to incorporate the sensor glucose trend into the calculation, you need to tap the "Use Sensor" button. If the Omnipod 5 app does not have recent sensor glucose available, the "Use Sensor" button will be disabled, and you will need to manually enter a glucose value.

- To view the details behind the calculation of your bolus, you can press the calculations button.

- Extended bolus delivery is not available in Automated Mode and can only be done in Manual Mode.

- Finally, you can administer the bolus. Once the green bar appears, you can put away your Controller or mobile phone. If necessary, you can cancel the bolus during delivery.

#### ***Suspending insulin***

To temporarily stop insulin delivery, such as during exercise or other brief activities, you need to switch back to Manual Mode. Here you can choose to pause insulin or set a temporary basal rate of 0 units per hour or minus 100%.

To pause insulin delivery:

1.  Access the menu by pressing the menu button.

2.  Select "Pause Insulin" from the options.

3.  Specify the duration of the pause, which can be 0.5 hours, 1 hour, 1.5 hours, or 2 hours.

4.  Confirm the pause by selecting "Pause" and then confirming the action.

5.  A yellow banner will appear on the home screen indicating that insulin delivery is paused.

Note: During the pause period, the Pod will emit beeps every 15 minutes. At the end of the pause period, insulin delivery does not automatically resume! The Pod and Omnipod 5 App will notify you every minute for 3 minutes and repeat the notification every 15 minutes until you manually restart insulin delivery.

It is essential to tap the "Start Insulin" option to resume insulin delivery after a pause period in Manual Mode. Insulin delivery does not restart automatically, and failure to restart it could lead to hyperglycemia.

Alternatively, you can use a temporary basal rate of 0 units per hour or minus 100% for a specific duration, up to 12 hours. In this case, insulin delivery will automatically resume after the set time period. The app will emit an audible beep at the beginning and every hour to indicate the temporary basal rate. This approach is particularly useful when you need to disconnect for a longer duration, as it reduces the number of alarms and interruptions. However, it is never advisable to suspend all insulin delivery for more than two hours unless you plan to take insulin by injection, as ketoacidosis can develop.

Many individuals choose to turn off their Controller using the ON/OFF button when temporarily stopping insulin delivery, as it prevents alarms from being triggered since the Controller is no longer connected to <span class="mark">the Pod.</span>

### ***4.3 Manual Mode***

In certain situations, it may be necessary to switch to Manual Mode in the Omnipod 5 system. This can be done to adjust insulin delivery according to specific needs, such as during illness, menstrual cycles, periods of stress, extended physical activity, or to pause insulin delivery.

In Manual Mode, the menu of the Omnipod 5 app differs from that of Automated Mode. It provides options for setting the basal insulin rate, switching basal profiles, creating temporary basal rates, and pausing insulin. Extended boluses can also be administered in Manual Mode.

For a detailed understanding of Manual Mode operation, you can refer to the module on the Omnipod DASH system, as the functionality is similar.

In conclusion, the Omnipod 5 system empowers users to take control of their insulin management. With the flexibility to switch between Automated Mode and Manual Mode, users have the ability to adapt and make adjustments according to their individual needs.

Remember, mastering any new technology may take time and practice. If you encounter any challenges along the way, don't hesitate to consult the manual for detailed instructions or reach out to your diabetes team. The app itself is designed to be user-friendly, with intuitive features and a range of adjustable settings. Stay patient, explore the various options available, and soon you'll be harnessing the full potential of the Omnipod 5 System for personalized diabetes management.
