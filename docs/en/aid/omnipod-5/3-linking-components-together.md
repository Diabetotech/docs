## ***<u>3. Linking components together</u>***

In this video, we will guide you through the process of creating links between the various components of the Omnipod 5 system to ensure seamless communication and data sharing.

The Omnipod 5 Pods and Controller share a similar appearance to those used in the Omnipod DASH system, but they incorporate significant differences:

- The Omnipod 5 Pods contain the SmartAdjust algorithm, which is responsible for making insulin delivery adjustments.

- You can identify the Omnipod 5 Pods by examining the packaging at the back and the specific box they come in. Additionally, the needle cap of the Omnipod 5 Pods is clear, while the needle cap of the Omnipod DASH Pods is blue.

- <span class="mark">The Omnipod 5 Controller, while slightly larger than its predecessor, the Omnipod DASH PDM, offers several notable improvements. It incorporates additional software to seamlessly integrate with a Dexcom or FreeStyle Libre sensor, and it is equipped with a SIM card, ensuring continuous internet connectivity. One advantage that users have observed is the extended battery life of the Omnipod 5 Controller, surpassing that of the Omnipod DASH PDM.</span>

<span class="mark"></span>

<span class="mark">The Omnipod 5 system integrates multiple components to provide seamless glucose monitoring and insulin delivery. Here's how the system operates:</span>

<span class="mark"></span>

1.  <span class="mark">Glucose data is collected every</span> 1 to <span class="mark">5 minutes from the Dexcom or FreeStyle Libre transmitter.</span>

2.  <span class="mark">The data is transmitted to the Omnipod 5 Pod, which houses the algorithm responsible for calculating insulin delivery. The Pod takes instructions from the Omnipod 5 app on a Controller or mobile phone.</span>

3.  <span class="mark">The Pod administers the necessary insulin and transmits all the glucose and insulin data to the Omnipod 5 app.</span>

4.  <span class="mark">If you’re using a Dexcom sensor, you’ll need to download the dedicated Dexcom app to start the sensor and receive glucose alerts. In this case, the Omnipod 5 app will only trigger an alarm when glucose is below 54 mg/dl or 3 mmol/l.</span>

5.  <span class="mark">If you’re using a FreeStyle Libre sensor, you can start the sensor with the Omnipod 5 app on your Controller or on your phone. All glucose alerts will come from the Omnipod 5 app.</span>

6.  <span class="mark">All the data from the Omnipod 5 app is sent to the Glooko and Omnipod Discover platform, offering a comprehensive overview of your diabetes management.</span>

7.  <span class="mark">Although there is no dedicated follow app for Omnipod 5 itself, family members and caregivers can still view the user’s glucose data. If a Dexcom sensor is used, up to 10 people can follow the user via the Dexcom Follow app. If a FreeStyle Libre sensor is used, up to 20 people can follow via the LibreLinkUp app.</span>

> <span class="mark"></span>

<span class="mark">By utilizing these components, the Omnipod 5 system provides accurate and efficient glucose monitoring and insulin delivery for improved diabetes management.</span>

<span class="mark"></span>

<span class="mark">In this video, we will cover the step-by-step process of</span>

- <span class="mark">linking a glucose sensor to the Omnipod 5 app on the Controller or smartphone,</span>

- <span class="mark">connecting the Pod to the Omnipod 5 app on the Controller or smartphone,</span>

- <span class="mark">creating a Glooko account,</span>

- <span class="mark">establishing the link between the Omnipod 5 app and Glooko,</span>

- <span class="mark">and connecting the Omnipod 5 app to LibreView.</span>

<span class="mark"></span>

<span class="mark">Please note that the usage of the Dexcom G6, G7 and Dexcom Follow app is covered in the module about Dexcom G6 and Dexcom G7.</span>

<span class="mark">By following the instructions in this video, you will be able to establish the necessary links between the different components of the Omnipod 5 system, enabling seamless data transfer and ensuring effective mana</span>gement of your diabetes.

### ***3.1 Linkin<span class="mark">g a glucose sensor to t</span>he Omnipod 5 app***

In order to establish a connection between th<span class="mark">e glucose sensor and the Omnipod 5 app, follow these steps:</span>

1.  <span class="mark">If you haven't already, place your Dexcom G6, G7 or FreeStyle Libre 2 Plus sensor on your skin according to the instructions provided.</span>

<!-- -->

2.  <span class="mark">On the Omnipod 5 app on your Controller or mobile phone, you'll have the option to select your sensor type during the initial setup. The available options will vary depending on your location. To start a new sensor or to switch sensor types, you can navigate to the main menu and select Manage Sensor.</span>

<span class="mark">**For people using a Dexcom G6 sensor,** you’ll first have to connect your Pod to the Dexcom G6 transmitter. You can do this by entering the serial number of the Dexcom G6 transmitter in the Omnipod 5 app. Please note that it may take up to 20 minutes for the connection to be established. Any time you switch to a new Dexcom G6 transmitter, make sure to update the serial number in this section as well.</span>

<span class="mark"></span>

<span class="mark">Please note that the activation of the Dexcom G6 sensor is done through the dedicated Dexcom G6 app. For detailed instructions on how to start the sensor, please refer to the specific module on Dexcom G6.</span>

<span class="mark"></span>

<span class="mark">After successfully activating the sensor, you can expect to receive glucose readings on both the Dexcom G6 app and the Omnipod 5 app on your Controller or mobile phone after approximately two hours. It's important to be aware that there may be a slight delay in the glucose readings displayed on your Omnipod 5 app compared to the Dexcom G6 app. This delay occurs because the sensor data first transmits to the Omnipod Pod and then to the Omnipod 5 app, while the Dexcom G6 app directly receives the data from the Dexcom transmitter.</span>

<span class="mark">**For people using a Dexcom G7 sensor,** you can select Dexcom G7 as your sensor type, and connect the Dexcom G7 sensor by entering the 4-digit pairing code and the 12-digit serial number. You can do this manually or by taking a photo with your phone or Controller. To receive glucose alerts, it is also necessary to pair your Dexcom G7 sensor to the Dexcom G7 app on your phone. You will need to connect each new Dexcom G7 Sensor to both the Omnipod 5 App and Dexcom G7 app for your Pod and Sensor to stay connected.</span>

<span class="mark"></span>

<span class="mark">**For people using a FreeStyle Libre 2 Plus sensor**, you can select FreeStyle Libre 2 Plus as your sensor type. First you will need to enter your preferred settings for your low glucose alert, high glucose alert and missing sensor values alert. Next, you can follow the on-screen instructions to start your sensor by scanning the inserted sensor. Tap OK if the Sensor scans successfully. Please note that it can take up to 20 minutes for the Pod and Sensor to connect. When the Pod and Sensor have connected, you will be asked to switch to Automated Mode.</span>

<span class="mark">If you receive a “Failed to Connect” message, try reconnecting the sensor. Make sure there are no other Pods nearby that you may have previously used or attempted to use, as other Pods within Bluetooth range (that is within approximately 9 meters or 30 feet) can interfere with the connection. Check that the Pod and sensor are in direct line of sight and worn on the same side of the body, without your body blocking communication. Also confirm that the sensor is still properly attached to your skin.</span>

<span class="mark">You can also try turning Airplane Mode on and then off again via the Settings Menu to re-establish the connection, and ensure that Bluetooth is turned back on afterward. After Pod activation, allow up to 20 minutes for sensor values to appear in the Omnipod 5 app.</span>

<span class="mark">If the issue persists, delete the sensor and add a new one in the Omnipod 5 app. Once a sensor is deleted, it cannot be reconnected and a new sensor must be started. Please note that you must be in Manual Mode to manage your sensor.</span>

<span class="mark">Although the FreeStyle LibreLink app or Libre app is not required to start the FreeStyle Libre 2 Plus sensor when using the Omnipod 5 system, you may still choose to use this app after sensor start. Scanning the sensor with the Libre app allows you to view glucose readings directly on your phone and can be useful during temporary connection issues, for example when the Controller displays a “sensor searching” message.</span>

<span class="mark">If you experience frequent sensor connection interruptions, you might consider disabling the Missing Sensor Values alert - as most of these sensor connection issues resolve on their own.</span>

<span class="mark">A mandatory alert will still be triggered if the connection is lost for more than one hour. In most cases, simply changing your body position to improve line of sight between the sensor and the Pod is sufficient to restore the connection.</span>

### ***3.2 Linking the Omnipod 5 Pod to the Omnipod 5 app***

In order to activate an Omnipod 5 Pod and link it to the Omnipod 5 app, you can follow the same steps as you would for the Omnipod DASH:

1.  Open the Omnipod 5 app on your device and navigate to the "Pod Info" tab.

2.  Choose the option to set up a new Pod.

3.  The app will guide you through the necessary steps, which include:

o Filling the Pod with insulin

o Pairing the Pod with the Omnipod 5 app

o Placing the Pod on your body

o And inserting the cannula to initiate insulin delivery

By following these steps, you will be able to activate an Omnipod 5 Pod and establish the connection with the Omnipod 5 app for seamless control and monitoring of your insulin delivery.

### ***3.3 Creating a Glooko account***

The pump and sensor data can be shared with your healthcare provider via Glooko.

We will describe how to create a Glooko account as a user via Glooko Personal, and as a healthcare professional via Glooko for Professional Use.

#### ***\#1 Glooko Personal~~:~~***

- As a user, you can visit my.glooko.com and choose "Create Account."

- <span class="mark">At a</span> certain moment you will be able to enter a "proconnect code" that connects your account to your <span class="mark">clinic.</span>

- After completing the registration, you will receive an email with further instructions. It is also possible to receive this email directly from your healthcare provider.

- Create a password for your account and keep it accessible for future logins, especially when you need to upload data from your pump.

<!-- -->

- When logging into Glooko, you will be prompted to select the devices you use. For Omnipod 5, you can simply choose the option for Omnipod 5 from the list of available devices. This will ensure that the data from your Omnipod 5 system is properly synced and integrated into the Glooko platform for comprehensive data analysis and reporting.

#### ***\#2 Glooko for Healthcare Providers:***

1.  Healthcare providers or institutions can purchase a professional Glooko account directly from Glooko. You can then log in via my.glooko.com using the username and password provided by your hospital.

2.  Upon logging in, you will find an overview of the latest data uploads and a separate tab displaying an overview of your patients. On the top right corner you will find the "proconnect code" of your hospital.

3.  You can customize the patient list by sorting it according to your preferences or using flags to identify patients with high or low glycemic levels easily.

4.  You can add a new patient via the button in the top right corner and you can add their email address to invite them to Glooko.

Additionally, healthcare providers can access patient reports without a Glooko Pro account if the patient shares their login credentials.

### ***3.4 Linking the Omnipod 5 app to Glooko***

To connect the Omnipod 5 app to Glooko, follow these steps:

1.  During the setup process of the Omnipod 5 app, you will be asked to provide your email address and password associated with your Glooko account.

2.  Once you have entered your credentials, the Omnipod 5 app will automatically start sending data to your Glooko account every 5 minutes.

3.  Please note that there may be a one-hour delay in the data appearing in Glooko.

4.  To verify if your Omnipod 5 app is properly linked to Glooko, log in to your Glooko personal account, go to the settings section, and navigate to "Apps and Devices". You should see the Omnipod 5 listed as a connected device.

By successfully connecting the Omnipod 5 app to Glooko, you can ensure that your Omnipod 5 data is seamlessly transferred and available for comprehensive analysis and reporting within the Glooko platform.

### ***3.5 Connecting the Omnipod 5 app to LibreView***

<span class="mark">In some regions, it is now possible to connect the Omnipod 5 app to LibreView when using the FreeStyle Libre 2 Plus sensor. To set this up, log in to your Omnipod account with your Omnipod ID on a desktop computer and then click on the LibreView link button and follow the steps. Once you are linked to LibreView, click on the Manage LibreLinkUp button to invite family and caregivers to view your glucose data with the LibreLinkUp app. Any caregivers you have previously linked to LibreLinkUp will already be able to see your sensor information.</span>

<span class="mark">It is important to keep your Omnipod 5 Controller and Pod within range to enable glucose values to be shared with your caregiver. If the Controller is not in range of the Pod, there may be gaps in the data available to the caregiver.</span>

With these step-by-step instructions, you are now ready to fully integrate the Omnipod 5 system into your diabetes management and make the most of its advanced glucose monitoring and insulin delivery capabilities.
