## **<u>2. Tandem Mobi System’s Control IQ-technology according to the CARES Paradigm</u>**

Welcome to this lesson about the Control-IQ-technology according to the CARES paradigm, that is used in the Tandem Mobi System.

Tandem Mobi System offers a range of features that make it an effectiv<span class="mark">e automated insulin delivery system for diabetes management. Let's explore some key poin</span>ts to further understand this system within the CARES paradigm, its indications and its software updates.

### **1. Tandem Mobi System According to the CARES paradigm**

#### ***1.1 Calculate***

Tandem Mobi System’s Control-IQ technology modulates preset basal insulin rates based on the predicted glucose levels over the next 30 minutes.

Additionally, hourly autocorrection boluses are administered at 60% of the standard dose when glucos<span class="mark">e levels are predicted to exceed 180 mg/dL or 10 mmol/L within the next 30 minutes.</span>

The target range, or treatment values as they are called by Tandem, are set between 112.5-160 mg/d<span class="mark">L (6.25</span> and 8.8 mmol/L), with the goal of achieving optimal glycemic control during the day.

During sleep mode, the treatment values are further refined to 112.5-120 mg/dL (<span class="mark">6.25 and 6.7 mmol/l</span>), promoting stable glucose levels without autocorrected boluses.

#### ***1.2 Adjust*** 

Users can modify the following settings:

- the basal insulin rate (different profiles are possible),

- the carbohydra<span class="mark">te ratio,</span>

- <span class="mark">and the correction factor or insulin sensitivity.</span>

<span class="mark">With the updated Control-IQ+ software, users can even set temporary basal rates from 0% to 250% for up to 72 hours while continuing to benefit from automatic insulin adjustments and correction boluses from the Control-IQ+ algorithm.</span>

<span class="mark">In addition to these adjustments, Control-IQ technology offers different glucose targets or treatment values for sleep and exercise.</span>

- <span class="mark">Sleep Activity: This setting aims for narrower treatment values of 112.5-120 mg/dL (or 6.25 and 6.7 mmol/l) during</span> nighttime to maintain stable glucose levels without using autocorrected boluses.

- Exercise Activity: This setting is designed for periods of increased physical activity. It sets higher treatment values of 140-160 mg/dL (or 7.8-8.9 mmol/L) to accommodate the metabolic demands of exercise. Additionally, during exercise activity, the pump does use autocorrected boluses to help manage glucose levels effectively.

During the initial set-up of the Tandem Mobi System, users will input their total daily insulin dose and weight. These values can be modified later to ensure accurate insulin delivery based on individual requirements.

While Tandem Mobi Control-IQ technology provides flexibility in adjusting various parameters, it's important to note that certain aspects remain fixed. The default treatment values are fixed at 112.5-160 mg/dL (or 6.25-8.9 mmol/L), the duration of insulin action is fixed at 5 hours, and the target value for correction boluses is fixed at 110 mg/dL (or 6.1 mmol/L).

#### ***1.3 Revert***

The algorithm for automated insulin delivery is called Control-IQ technology, and is located on the Tandem Mobi pump itself.

In the event that there is no sensor data available for more than 20 minutes, the Control-IQ technology will stop and the pump will revert from automode to manual mode.

In manual mode, the pump will deliver insulin according to your Personal Profile settings.

Once CGM readings are available, automode or Control-IQ technology will automatically resume.

If you see the Out of Range icon on your Dashboard in the place where your sensor glucose reading normally shows, then your Tandem Mobi pump is not communicating with your CGM and sensor glucose readings will not show on your Dashboard.

- When a sensor session is active, you may sometimes experience loss of communication for 10 minutes at a time. This is normal. If you see the Out of Range icon for more than 10 minutes, move your Tandem Mobi pump and CGM closer together and remove any obstructions. Wait 10 minutes and communication should be restored.

The transmission range from the CGM to the pump is up to 6 meters or 20 feet without obstruction. Wireless communication does not work well through water so the range is much less if you are in a pool, bathtub, or on a water bed, etc.

#### ***1.4 Educate***

When starting with an automated <span class="mark">insulin delivery</span> system like the Tandem Mobi System, it's important to be aware of both general education points and specific educat<span class="mark">ion points for this system. The general education points covered in the Master Module on AID Systems include:</span>

- <span class="mark">Use fewer carbohydrates to correct hypoglycemia.</span>

- <span class="mark">Consider infusion set problems if blood glucose levels remain high for an extended period without a clear reason.</span>

- <span class="mark">Trust the AID system and avoid unnecessary manual interventions.</span>

- <span class="mark">Time meal boluses 15 minutes before meals.</span>

- <span class="mark">Use Exercise Mode 90 minutes before activity</span>

- <span class="mark">Stop insulin delivery if the pump is disconnected for more than 15 minutes.</span>

- <span class="mark">Be mindful of automatic updates on mobile phones.</span>

- <span class="mark">And seek peer support for guidance and sharing experiences.</span>

Now let's look at the specific education points for the Tandem Mobi System with Control-IQ technology:

- Using Sleep Activity every night can lead to better glycemic control. When planning to sleep for more than 5 hours, utilizing Sleep Activity is advisable. For short afternoon naps, Sleep Activity is not necessary.

- It's best to activate Sleep Activity close to your actual sleep time and ideally not within 2 hours of your last meal. This ensures you receive autocorrection boluses if needed after an evening meal.

- If you skip breakfast, it's acceptable to keep Sleep Activity on for a while, but it's not intended for use throughout the entire day. If you do have breakfast, it's recommended to disable Sleep Activity to allow for autocorrection boluses if necessary.

- When delivering a bolus, the pump may prompt you to adjust the bolus based on sensor glucose levels. Read these instructions carefully and consider whether it's appropriate for your situation.

- While it's possible to override the bolus calculator, it's generally not recommended.

- Utilize dif<span class="mark">ferent profiles to</span> optimize insulin release during activities like sports or illness.

- When adjusting parameters, keep in mind that the correction factor functions as a lever to adjust the aggressiveness of the system, and is the most influential parameter to adjust.

By following these education points, you can maximize the bene<span class="mark">fits of the Tan</span>dem Mobi System and achieve better diabetes management. It is normal to experience some trial and error in finding the right settings when you begin using this pump. Have confidence and consult with your <span class="mark">healthcare provider</span> for any necessary adjustments.

#### ***1.5 Sensor***

<span class="mark">The accuracy of sensor values is vital for the proper functioning of an automated insulin delivery system. If you have doubts about the accuracy of your sensor reading, it is recommended to perform a finger prick blood glucose test for confirmation. If there is a significant discrepancy between the sensor glucose reading and your finger prick result, you may consider calibrating the Dexcom G6 or G7 sensor. However, it is important to perform calibration only when your blood glucose levels have been stable and within your target range for the past few minutes. Calibrating under these conditions helps ensure more accurate sensor readings and enhances the overall performance of the automated insulin delivery system.</span>

### ***3.1 How to link the Tan<span class="mark">dem Mobi pump to the Tandem Mobi M</span>obile app***

In order to use your Mobi pump, you must first pair your pump to your iPhone. You can do as follows:

- Connect your charging pad to a power source using the supplied USB-C cable and place the pump on the charging pad.

- Ensure that your pump is turned on. You can check to see if your pump is on by pressing the Pump button once. If the pump status lights turn on, your pump is on.

- Download the Tandem Mobi Mobile app from the App store on your compatible iPhone.

- If you are a new user, the app will first ask you to create an account on Tandem Source.

- <span class="mark">Next tap "Begin", and follow the on-screen instructions to pair your Mobi pump.</span>

- <span class="mark">At a certain moment, you will be asked to enter the PIN code of your pump, which can be found under the serial number.</span>

When your pump is paired, you will see the homescreen of the Tandem Mobi mobile app.

### ***3.2 How to link a Dexcom G6 sensor to the Tandem Mobi insulin pump***

With the Tandem Mobi Insulin Pump, linking the sensor to the pump is a straightforward process, but it’s a little different for Dexcom G6 and Dexcom G7. Here's how you can do it for the Dexcom G6 sensor:

First link the Dexcom G6 transmitter by navigating to the Settings menu on the pump, tap CGM, and then Dexcom G6. Tap the Transmitter ID field and enter the unique transmitter ID. You will be asked to verify it, after which you can save it.

Once you have successfully linked the transmitter to the pump, you can proceed to start the sensor. Here's how:

1.  From the Dashboard screen, select "Settings," then “CGM”

2.  Choose "Start Sensor" and follow the instructions.

3.  During this step, you will be prompted to enter the sensor code, which can be found on the protective foil of the sensor. If you happen to misplace the sensor code, you can select "skip." In this case, the pump will ask you to calibrate the sensor after 2 hours, allowing you to still use the sensor. However, the pump will ask for a calibration every 24 hours so it's advisable not to lose your sensor code to avoid these extra steps.

4.  On the Start Sensor screen, tap Start. The Sensor session started banner will be displayed at the top of the Tandem Mobi mobile app to let you know your 2-hour warmup time has begun. During the sensor startup period, Control-IQ technology will not affect the basal rates or deliver automatic correction boluses. The sensor must be actively providing readings for Control-IQ technology to operate.

If you have already started the sensor with the Dexcom G6 app, the pump will pick up the sensordata without asking for a sensorcode.

By following these steps, you can successfully link the Dexcom G6 transmitter and start the Dexcom G6 sensor with the Tandem Mobi Insulin Pump.

### ***3.3 How to link a Dexcom G7 sensor to the Tandem Mobi insulin pump***

If you have access to Dexcom G7 sensors, the linking process is even easier. Just choose Dexcom G7 as your sensor type, and enter the G7 pairing code twice.

The Dexcom G7 sensor needs a 30-minute startup period to adjust to being under your skin. This startup period begins automatically as soon as the sensor is inserted. So if you insert your Dexcom G7 sensor 30 minutes before pairing it with your pump, you will directly receive sensor values, and Control-IQ technology will not need to stop when switching sensors.

### ***3.4 <span class="mark">How to create a Tandem Source account </span>***

<span class="mark">The pump and sensor data can be shared with a parent, guardian or healthcare provider via Tandem Source platform.</span>

<span class="mark">Users of the Tandem Mobi Mobile app create a Tandem Source account when they first sign into the app. All sensor and pump data are automatically synchronized to the Tandem Cloud approximately once per hour.</span>

<span class="mark">Healthcare providers can ask for a professional Tandem Source account via their Tandem representative. Admin users of a clinic account can add other team members.</span>

<span class="mark">Users and healthcare providers can both login to Tandem Source via sso.tandemdiabetes.com. Users can connect to a clinic by going to their account settings, and then tap Share Reports, and then Add Clinic.</span>

### ***6.1 Hypoglycemia***

With the Tandem Mobi System, you may require fewer carbohydrates to treat or prevent hypoglycemia compared to when not using an AID system, especially if you have little insulin on board. You can check your insulin on board on the home screen.

If you consume less than 10 grams of carbohydrates to treat or prevent a hypo, there is no need to enter this in the pump.

To prevent hyp<span class="mark">oglycemia for example during exercise, you can use the Exercise Activity from 1-2 hours before exercise, until after the exercise.</span>

### ***6.2 Hyperglycemia***

The Tandem Mobi System is not a learning algorithm, so manual autocorrection boluses can be given in between. However, it is important to follow the bolus advice.

<span class="mark">In cases of prolonged severe hyperglycemia, it is important to consider infusion problems.</span>

<span class="mark">To avoid infusion set problems, it is recommended to replace the infusion set in a timely manner and set a reminder for this on your pump. Remember to rotate your infusion sites and avoid lipodystrophy.</span>

### ***6.3 High-fat meals***

The Tandem Mobi System allows you to use an extended bolus. For high-fat meals, it is generally recommended to deliver 50% of the bolus immediately and the remaining 50% over the next 2 hours.

This is the default setting for an extended bolus, but further individual adjustments may be needed.

### ***6.4 Exercise***

When exercising with the Tandem Mobi System, you can activate the Exercise Activity <span class="mark">1-2 hours prior to the activity and manually reduce the suggested meal and correction boluses as discussed in the ge</span>neral module on automated insulin delivery systems.

It's important to note that autocorrection boluses are still given during Activity Mode.

You can also use a separate personal profile for spo<span class="mark">rts or a dedicated temporary basal</span> during that time. This provides more options to adjust the insulin release according to your personal needs.

### ***6.5 Illness***

During illness, hyperglycemia is common. Follow the guidelines for managing hyperglycemia in such situations.

It’s recommended to preset a personal profile dedicated to illness with a higher % of basal insulin rate, and more aggressive carb ratio and correction factor.

<span class="mark">You also have the option to set a dedicated temporary basal rate during illness, providing additional flexibility to tailor insulin delivery to your individual needs.</span>

### ***6.6 Alcohol***

R<span class="mark">efer to the specific guidelines of the general module on automated insulin delivery systems for managing your automated insulin delivery in relation to alcohol consumption.</span>

### ***6.7 Travel***

When traveling with the Tandem Mobi Insulin pump, you have the option to request an additional insulin "loaner pump" to accompany you during your holiday. Moreover, in case of an em<span class="mark">ergency or if your pump requires replacement, Tandem can send you a replacement insulin pump promptly.</span>

<span class="mark">When traveling, be sure to also bring enough sensor supplies and charging equipment to keep your pump powered during the journey. Also make sure you have access to injectable basal and bolus insulin in case of pump failure.</span>

When traveling across time zones, you will need to manually adjust the time on your Tandem Mobi App.

By following these tips, you can effectively manage various situations while using Tandem Mobi System and maintain optimal control over your diabetes. Remember to consult with your healthcare provider for personalized guidance and support in managing specific scenarios.
