**<u>2. Mylife Loop according to the CARES paradigm</u>**

Welcome to this video where we will dive into mylife Loop, examining its functionality based on the CARES paradigm.

We will discuss how mylife Loop calculates and adjusts the automated insulin delivery, when it reverts back to manual manual, specific education tips and sensor issues, and how it shares data. After that we will go over mylife Loop’s official indications and software updates.

### ***1) Mylife Loop according to the CARES paradigm***

***\#1 Calculate***

Mylife Loop operates by providing extended insulin boluses every 8 to 12 minutes, based on the predicted glucose levels for the next 2.5 to 4 hours, along with the user's total daily insulin dose and weight.

The system incorporates a learning algorithm through three approaches:

- Overall learning: Analyzing daily data with recent data given higher weightage.

- Diurnal learning: Assessing insulin requirements at specific times of the day.

- Post-meal learning: Observing insulin requirements after meals.

The target value can be adjusted within 30-minute time blocks, ranging from 80 to 200 mg/dl or 4.4 to 11 mmol/l.

### ***\#2 Adjust***

Mylife Loop allows for adjustments in the following parameters:

- Target Value: this is adjustable within 30-minute time blocks between 80 and 200 mg/dl or 4.4 and 11 mmol/l.

- Temporary Target: Mylife Loop offers a higher and lower temporary target via its Ease-Off and Boost function

- and the carb ratio.

Ease-Off: With the optional Ease-Off feature, users can temporarily receive reduced insulin delivery. It allows for an adjustable duration of up to 24 hours. During Ease-Off, the target value increases by 45 mg/dl (or 2.5 mmol/l), insulin sensitivity increases by 50%, and insulin delivery stops when glycemia reaches less than 126 mg/dl (or 7 mmol/l).

Boost: The Boost function provides a temporary increase in insulin delivery when glycemia exceeds the target value. It can be adjusted for a duration of up to 13 hours. Insulin delivery is enhanced by 35% only when glycemia is higher than the target value.

During the initial setup of mylife Loop, you can enter your total insulin dose and weight, with the option to adjust the weight later on.

In mylife Loop, the basal insulin rate, insulin sensitivity, and duration of insulin action are not adjustable. These are determined by the system.

In mylife Loop, meals can be managed effectively through two methods: the bolus calculator and the "add meal" function.

- Bolus Calculator: Via the regular bolus screen, users can input grams of carbs to calculate the appropriate insulin dose using the bolus calculator. It accurately determines the insulin needed based on the carbohydrates consumed.

- Add Meal Function: Alternatively, the "add meal" function allows insulin administration only if glucose levels rise above a specific threshold after the meal. Users can choose options such as meal and snacks, hypoglycemia treatment, or slowly absorbed meals.

This additional meal bolus method offers flexibility and precise insulin administration for optimal post-meal glucose control.

### ***\#3 Revert***

Mylife Loop reverts to Manual Mode under various circumstances:

- If CGM data are unavailable, including during sensor warmup

- If there is a loss of connection with the insulin pump

- <span class="mark">If the pump insulin delivery is suspended.</span>

- Or if Bluetooth is turned off on the pump or the smartphone

When there is no connection to the pump or sensor, the app will first enter an “Auto Mode Attempting" status. By clicking on the i-icon in the bottom left, you can access information about why Auto Mode is not possible. If the issue persists for 30 minutes, the system will switch to the pre-programmed basal rate on your pump or “Manual Mode”.

In Manual Mode:

- A suspend-for-low function is not available.

- The basal insulin settings on your pump will be used, and you can only check and adjust them on the pump.

- However, if the mylife CamAPS FX app is still connected, you can still administer a bolus.

Once the connection to the pump and transmitter is restored, Auto Mode will automatically restart.

In theory, the connection between the mobile phone and the mylife YpsoPump and sensor should be maintained within a range of 6 meters (20 feet). However, in practice, it is recommended to keep your mobile phone as close to you as possible for optimal connectivity. For children, using a backpack to carry the mobile phone can be a convenient solution.

### ***\#4 Educate***

Proper education is crucial when starting an AID system. For general education points, please refer to the generic module on AID systems.

The general education points covered were:

- Using fewer carbohydrates to correct hypoglycemia.

- Considering infusion set problems if blood glucose levels remain high without an apparent reason.

- Trusting the automated insulin delivery system and avoiding unnecessary manual interventions.

- Timing meal boluses 15 minutes before meals.

- Utilizing Exercise Mode 1 to 2 hours before activity.

- Stopping insulin delivery if the pump is disconnected for more than 15 minutes.

- Responding to alarms and being mindful of automatic updates on mobile phones.

- Seeking peer support for guidance and sharing experiences.

Specific education points for mylife Loop include:

1.  Checking the compatibility of your Android mobile phone with both the mylife CamAPS FX app and the sensor (Dexcom G6 or FreeStyle Libre 3) before starting the mylife Loop AID system.

2.  Allowing a two-week adaptation period for the system to adjust to your body before making parameter adjustments.

3.  Using the Ease-off and Boost functions conservatively during the initial stages.

4.  Avoiding correction boluses when giving meal boluses using the bolus calculator in Auto Mode, as the algorithm often accounts for them. Correction boluses are more appropriate in Manual Mode.

5.  A high amount of basal insulin after a meal suggests that there might not have been enough meal bolus insulin administered, while a very low or even no basal rate after a meal suggests that there might have been too much meal bolus insulin administered.

6.  Entering carbohydrates through the "add meal" function in case you want insulin administered only if glucose levels rise. This function can be used for carbs as treatment for hypoglycemia, for big or fatty meals and for snacks.

7.  Lowering the target value during the night if necessary.

8.  When there is no basal rate, the system will give very small insulin boluses or patency pulses to keep the tubing open. These patency pulses are an indication of a very low insulin on board, which might lead to a hyperglycemia later on.

9.  Utilizing the free e-learning program offered by CamDiab for patients and healthcare providers, allowing users to obtain a certificate and gain comprehensive system knowledge. The e-learning program typically takes about 3 hours to complete. While it may not be easy, it is highly recommended as it provides essential knowledge. To activate the app, you will need a training code. However, you can install the mylife CamAPS FX app beforehand and test it using a virtual pump and sensor.

10. Keeping the mobile phone, which hosts the algorithm, nearby at all times.

11. And finally, consider having a spare mobile phone for emergencies.

### ***\#5 Sensor***

Mylife Loop can be used with the Dexcom G6 or the FreeSt<span class="mark">yle Libre 3 or 3 Plus</span> sensor. You can easily switch between the 2 sensors.

If you have doubts about the accuracy of your sensor reading or do not feel it reflects your condition, perform a finger prick test to confirm.

If a significant discrepancy, such as a more than 20% difference, exists between the sensor and finger prick readings, even when the blood glucose is stable within the target range, it is advisable to discontinue Auto Mode and promptly replace the sensor.

The Dexcom G6 sensor can be calibrated if a significant discrepancy exists between the sensor reading and finger prick results, assuming stable glucose levels. It's important to note that if you calibrate the sensor, it will need one calibration each day until a new sensor is applied.

When you first open the mylife CamAPS FX app, you will be prompted to create an account that will link your information to the CamAPS cloud. In such cases, the algorithm will restart its learning process from the beginning and require approximately two weeks to optimize itself again.

### ***4.2 Auto Mode***

#### ***Starting Auto Mode***

To activate Auto Mode in the mylife CamAPS FX app, simply tap the Auto Mode button located at the bottom of the home screen. If Auto Mode is successfully enabled, the app screen will transition from a gray to a green color, indicating that the system is in Auto Mode. When the app is attempting to start Auto Mode, the background becomes orange. If you're unsure why Auto Mode remains orange instead of turning green, you can tap the i-icon in the lower left beside of auto mode for more information.

In landscape mode, the mylife CamAPS FX app displays the automatic basal insulin as a blue graph below, while the insulin boluses and carbohydrate entries are shown on top. The gray bar on top indicates when Auto Mode is Enabled or Attempting to activate. Additionally, a red bar positioned in the middle signifies the usage of the Boost function, while a yellow bar represents the Ease-Off function.

In Auto Mode, you won't have the ability to modify any settings on your mylife YpsoPump. Instead, you will only be able to observe the delivery of extended boluses on your mylife YpsoPump.

#### ***Using Ease-off and Boost***

In Auto Mode, you have the option to temporarily adjust the amount of insulin you receive by activating the ease-Off or Boost function.

- The "Ease-off" function is designed to reduce insulin delivery.

  - It can be utilized to prevent hypoglycemia during exercise, as well as to mitigate the risk of low blood sugar in hot weather, after consuming alcohol, or following episodes of hypoglycemia.

  - When activating "Ease-off," you will be prompted to set a time duration of up to 23 hours.

  - You can choose whether the change should take effect immediately or at a later time. If you select a later time, you can specify the exact timing.

  - 

  - A blue status bar labeled "Scheduled reduction" will appear below the graph, indicating that the Ease-Off Mode will commence at the designated time.

- On the other hand, the "Boost" function increases the algorithm's insulin delivery.

  - It can also be used to treat hyperglycemia resulting from factors such as errors in carbohydrate counting during a meal, late or missed boluses, non-functioning infusion sets, or mild illness without the presence of ketones.

  - Additionally, Boost can be used to prevent hyperglycemia in situations involving temporary insulin resistance, such as the days preceding menstruation or when using corticosteroids.

  - By activating the "Boost" function, you can set a time duration of up to 13 hours.

#### ***Adjusting parameters***

Mylife Loop allows for adjustments in the target value or the carb ratio.

- The target value can be found in the settings as the personal glucose target

- And the carb ratio can be found in the configuration options of the bolus calculator.

When you launch the app for the first time, it will prompt you to provide your average total insulin dose over the last 5 days and your current weight. The weight setting can be changed afterwards via the settings.

#### ***Delivering a bolus***

To administer a bolus in Auto Mode, you can utilize the meal bolus tab within the app.

- Upon tapping the meal bolus tab, the app will establish a connection with your insulin pump. Please note that there might be a slight delay during this process.

- You can then input your carbohydrate intake in various ways:

  - Choose a preset meal size.

  - Utilize a slider to select the number of grams of carbohydrates.

  - Or enter the exact number of grams manually.

- This is how you give an insulin bolus for a specific amount of carbohydrates in Auto Mode. The bolus can be canceled at any time. In landscape mode, the bolus and carbohydrate values are prominently displayed at the top of the graph.

It's important to note that if you are in Auto Mode and use the bolus calculator, the only thing you have to do is initiate the suggested bolus.

- If you do an extra step and tap on the glucose value and confirm it, the app will calculate and add a correction bolus based on the current sensor glucose reading, which is not necessary. If Auto Mode is active, the algorithm has already accounted for out-of-range glucose levels. Administering an additional insulin correction bolus in this manner often leads to hypoglycemia 1-3 hours later.

- On the other hand, if Auto Mode is disabled, it is useful to tap on the glucose value and confirm it, to calculate a correction using the bolus calculator. If Auto Mode is disabled the glucose will be corrected automatically in the bolus calculator. The insulin needed for correction is shown to the right of the glucose. The insulin on board will be subtracted.

- Additionally, if you are in Auto Mode and you want to give a manual insulin bolus, for example because you suspect an infusion set blockage, it might also make sense to calculate an additional correction bolus by tapping on and confirming your glucose.

There are situations where it is beneficial not to enter carbohydrates in the bolus calculator, but instead utilize the additional "add meal" option. By doing so, the app will administer insulin only if necessary. Within this option, there are three choices that correspond to specific scenarios:

- Carbs as a meal or snack: This is useful when consuming a small snack between meals but only requiring insulin if hyperglycemia occurs. It can also be employed to split the carbohydrates from larger meals, entering a portion here and the remainder via the bolus calculator.

- Carbohydrates to treat hypoglycemia: Use this option when carbohydrates are consumed to address low blood sugar, and insulin administration is not required.

- Slowly absorbed meal: If carbohydrates are entered here, the insulin for these carbohydrates will be administered gradually over the n<span class="mark">ext 3 to 4</span> hours, but only if blood glucose rises.

#### ***Suspending insulin***

If you need to temporarily stop the pump, such as when disconnecting it for showering or swimming, you can only do so directly on the pump itself via the main menu. The mylife CamAPS FX app will then send notifications every 30 minutes to remind you to reactivate the pump.

### ***4.3 Manual Mode***

If there is no connection with the sensor or no sensor data available, such as during sensor warm-up, mylife Loop will revert to Manual Mode.

If the mylife YpsoPump is still connected to the mylife CamAPS FX app, you can utilize the app to administer a bolus using the integrated bolus calculator.

However, it's important to remember that you cannot adjust the basal insulin settings of Manual Mode within the app itself; these adjustments can only be made directly on the pump. In Manual Mode, there is no suspend-before-low function available.

In the event that you forget your mobile phone or its battery is depleted, you can still control your pump using the pump itself. Detailed instructions on how to do this can be found in the mylife YpsoPump module.

On the mylife YpsoPump, you have the ability to change the basal insulin settings and set temporary basal rates if necessary. Additionally, you can administer a manual bolus, choosing between a regular or extended bolus. Please note that the mylife YpsoPump itself does not have a bolus calculator feature.

### ***5.2 Interpreting reports***

When using mylife Loop, the ability to adjust parameters is limited, primarily focused on the target value and carbohydrate ratio. However, understanding and effectively utilizing the system's additional features, such as Add Meal, Ease-Off and Boost, is key to maximizing its potential.

Additionally, it's important to consider the time spent in Auto Mode, as the algorithm operates on your mobile phone, which may not be in proximity of your sensor and pump at all times.

Allowing a grace period of approximately two weeks for the system to adapt to the user is highly recommended before implementing significant adjustments. After this time, it is common to fine-tune the system by setting a more aggressive carbohydrate ratio and reducing the personal glucose target value.

When analyzing the reports, it is beneficial to follow a structured approach, as emphasized in the general module on automated insulin delivery systems. Here is a roadmap to guide your interpretation:

1\. Assess Glycemic Information: Begin by evaluating the TIR (Time in Range) and TBR (Time Below Range) over the past 2-4 weeks on the Summary Tab.

- Ensure that treatment goals are being met, with TIR above 70% and TBR below 4%.

- It is important to pay attention to the frequency of meal bolusing, which is also indicated in the Summary Tab of Glooko. On average, there should be around 3 boluses per day for meals. If you notice an unusually high number of boluses, such as 20 boluses per day, it may indicate that the user does not trust the system and is constantly interfering with manual correction boluses.

2\. Optimize Automated Insulin Delivery Settings: Analyze the AGP (Ambulatory Glucose Profile) on the Summary Tab to identify trends of hypo- or hyperglycemia, and check if these trends are related to boluses on the Weekly Overview reports.

- If you notice trends after meal boluses, it's important to assess various factors such as timing and accurate carbohydrate counting, before adjusting the carb ratio.

  - Persistent hypos after meals indicate a too strong carbohydrate ratio.

  - Persistent basal insulin administration after meals, with possible late post-meal hypo, indicates a too weak carbohydrate ratio.

- For trends outside of meal boluses, consider adjusting the target value. Keep in mind that you have the flexibility to adjust the target value based on different time blocks. Consider lowering the target value further during overnight periods to optimize your Time in Range (TIR) and improve overall glycemic control.

- Additionally, explore possible correlations with factors such as exercise, alcohol consumption, or hypoglycemia.

If you come across "patency pulses" in the Glooko reports, there's no need to be concerned.

- These pulses represent small insulin boluses of 0.04 E delivered every 90 minutes when the basal insulin is turned off. Their purpose is to prevent catheter or infusion set blockage. Although they may appear as 0.2 E on the graph, they actually amount to only 0.04 E. This minimal dose of insulin has no significant clinical effect and will not cause hypoglycemia.

- If you notice frequent occurrences of patency pulses throughout the day, it's advisable to investigate possible reasons of this temporary low insulin requirement. For very young children, patency pulses are often observed during the latter part of the night due to their lower insulin requirements.

3\. Guide Behavioral Recommendations: To ensure the proper usage of the automated insulin delivery system, it is important to review various aspects in the reports available. Here are some key considerations:

- Summary Tab: Check the sensor wear duration and time spent in Auto Mode.

  - If the time in Auto Mode is \<85%, emphasize that the mobile phone, which hosts the algorithm, should be kept nearby at all times.

- Also in the Summary Tab: Evaluate if bolus calculator overrides are low (ideally 0%). Also, check the amount of carbs entered for meals.

- Weekly Overview Report: Assess the adherence to pre-meal bolusing, overcorrection of hypo- and hyperglycemia, and the appropriate use of the Ease-Off and Boost function.

  - In this example, you can observe a situation where a bolus was missed, leading to elevated blood glucose levels.

By doing so, you provide the algorithm with an opportunity to establish the optimal balance and adapt to your body's needs. It's common for individuals to be overly aggressive in correcting hypoglycemia and hyperglycemia, as illustrated in this example.

- On the Insights tab, you can check if the infusion set replacement is done every 3 days.

- Devices Tab: Assess the appropriateness of alarm settings and reminders in the mylife CamAPS FX app. Verify that the settings align with the user's needs and preferences.

4\. Review Pump Settings and Emergency Plans:

- It is important to update your weight during each check-up if necessary, especially during pregnancy, as body weight plays a crucial role in the post-meal insulin calculation. However, outside of pregnancy, when aiming for less stringent post-meal targets, weight updates are less critical.

## **<u>6. Managing special situations</u>**

Welcome to this module where we will discuss managing special situations with the mylife Loop automated insulin delivery system!

By following these guidelines, you'll be equipped with practical strategies to address various scenarios and ensure optimal glucose control with mylife Loop. Let's dive in and explore these valuable tips together!

### ***6.1 Hypoglycemia***

In the event of hypoglycemia, you will need fewer carbs with mylife Loop compared to traditional methods, e<span class="mark">specially when you have minimal insulin on board. You can utilize the landscape mode to check your insuli</span>n on board.

When consuming carbohydrates to treat or prevent a hypo, it is recommended to enter this information under the "Add Meal" function in the system.

To prevent recurring hypoglycemic episodes, you can take advantage of the Ease-Off function. This feature can also be used to prevent hypoglycemia during exercise, alcohol consumption, hot weather, and other relevant situations.

Additionally, mylife Loop allows you to temporarily set a higher target value. This adjustment can be beneficial in the following scenarios:

\- During periods of frequent hypoglycemia

\- When engaging in an active holiday or visiting warmer climates

\- Or when starting a new job or returning to school or training after a break

### ***6.2 Hyperglycemia***

When dealing with mild hyperglycemia, it's important to trust the mylife Loop AID system and allow it to work autonomously. Avoid giving excessive manual correction boluses, as this often leads to subsequent hypoglycemia 1-3 hours later. In the example shown, someone gave a correction bolus for a hyperglycemia following a meal, resulting in a subsequent hypoglycemic episode. Since the algorithm already provides additional insulin when hyperglycemia occurs, it's safer to use the "Boost" function to address hyperglycemia, especially after a meal.

Using "Boost" to support the system in correcting glucose levels quickly, is preferable to adjusting the carbohydrate ratio or personal glucose target. However, especially in the beginning, it's advisable not to use the "Boost" and "Ease-off" functions too much. The system needs time to understand your patterns and preferences. When you use a lot of Boost or Ease-Off, the system's learning process is hindered. Overusing these features can be compared to driving a car with one foot on the accelerator and the other on the brake simultaneously.

In cases of prolonged severe hyperglycemia, it's important to consider the possibility of an infusion set issue. "If in doubt, change it out." It's recommended to have enough supplies with you, including an extra inserter for the infusion set. To prevent infusion set problems, it's advisable to replace the set every 2-3 days and set a reminder in your mylife CamAPS FX app under the "refill pump" message.

### ***6.3 High-fat meals***

For large meals with more than 60 grams of carbs, you can try to split the bolus.

- Administer 50-75 % 15 minutes before the meal using the bolus calculator & the remaining insulin dose during or after the meal using either the bolus calculator or the "Add meal" option with the "meal or snack" setting.

For meals that are slowly absorbed due to factors such as low glycemic index, high fat and protein content, or gastroparesis, you can consider to enter the second part of the bolus as a slowly digested meal in the add meal function. The algorithm will then provide small additional insulin boluses every 30 minutes for the next 3-4 hours, depending on the glucose level.

If you have a reduced appetite or irregular eating pattern, you can enter the minimum amount of carbohydrates you reliably consume into the bolus calculator. If you end up eating more carbohydrates than initially planned, you can add the remaining amount later through a second bolus or by using the "Add meal" option, selecting "meal or snack."

### ***6.4 Exercise***

Utilize the Ease-Off function and reduce the meal bolus if prolonged exercise is anticipated within 2 hours of a meal, as recommended in the general module on automated insulin delivery systems.

If you reduce the meal bolus to prevent hypoglycemia, it is recommended to enter the full number of grams of carbohydrates but adjust the bolus recommendation accordingly. You can achieve this by tapping the suggested insulin bolus and adjusting the dose using the scroll wheel. This way, the algorithm is aware that <span class="mark">more carbohydrates were consumed.</span>

<span class="mark">In addition to using Ease-Off, recent guidelines recommend raising the glucose target based on glucose trends before and during exercise. If sensor glucose is trending downward, increase the target to 120 to 150 mg/dL (or 6.7 to 8.3 mmol/L) or higher.</span>

Please note that these recommendations serve as a starting strategy, and insulin dosage should be further adjusted based on your individual observations and patterns during exercise.

### ***6.5 Illness***

During illness, hyperglycemia is common. Follow the guidelines for managing hyperglycemia in such situations.

### ***6.6 Alcohol***

If you drink alcohol, you can proactively try to prevent hypoglycemia by temporarily increasing your target value and/or using the "Ease-off" function to reduce insulin delivery.

It does NOT help to take in more carbohydrates, as the system will compensate for any increase in glucose levels by increasing the administration of insulin.

### ***6.7 Travel***

When travelling with mylife Loop, be sure to take plenty of supplies with you. Be sure not to forget your mobile phone <span class="mark">charger and, if necessary, an appropriate travel plug for the country you are travelling to. If you’re using the Dexcom G6 sensor, it is best to take a spare Dexcom G6 transmitter with you.</span>

Consider using the "Ease-off" function or setting a higher personal glucose target for the first few days of your holiday. This can help prevent the risk of hypoglycemia, especially if you anticipate being more active than usual or traveling to a destination with warmer weather.

The mylife CamAPS FX app automatically adjusts the time when you travel through different time zones. This ensures that your system continues to operate smoothly without any manual intervention required.

Some airlines require passengers to put their mobile phones on Airplane Mode during take-off and landing, which can temporarily interrupt Bluetooth communication with mylife Loop. If this happens, don't worry. After 30 minutes, the system will switch to the preset basal insulin delivery until Bluetooth connectivity is restored. Once you're allowed to use your phone again, remember to turn off Airplane Mode promptly to ensure Auto Mode resumes.
