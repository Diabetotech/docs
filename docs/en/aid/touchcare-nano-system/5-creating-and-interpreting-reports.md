## **<u>5. Creating and Interpreting Reports</u>**

<span class="mark">Welcome to this lesson on how to create and interpret reports using the EasyView platform for the TouchCare Nano System.</span>

<span class="mark">In earlier lessons, we showed you how to create an EasyView account and upload data from your device. Now, we’ll look at how to view your reports, understand what they show, and use them to support better diabetes management.</span>

### ***5.1 Creating Reports*** 

To access your reports, log into EasyView Personal or, if you're a healthcare provider, open EasyView Pro and select the patient you’d like to review.

<span class="mark">Then go to the Reports section. You will now have the option to look at the following:</span>

- <span class="mark">Dashboard, which gives you an overview and averages on the patient's data.</span>

- <span class="mark">Daily summary which gives you a detailed overview of a selected date.</span>

- <span class="mark">Day by day overview which gives you either the last 7 or 14 days worth of data on one page.</span>

- <span class="mark">Sensor overlay which gives you the last seven days worth of CGM readings all on top of each other.</span>

- <span class="mark">Trend analysis which gives you details of both pump and CGM averages.</span>

- <span class="mark">Events which gives you the history of every event that has been logged through the patient's phone or PDM handset.</span>

- <span class="mark">Logbook which gives you details of the last 7 or 14 days worth of data in regards to BG levels, carbohydrate intake, bolus dosages along with further information.</span>

- <span class="mark">Alert which gives you a history of every alert that has appeared on the mobile phone or PDM handset, as well as the pump.</span>

- <span class="mark">And finally Device Settings Review which gives you details on the settings of the pump and CGM.</span>

<span class="mark">You can print the reports or save them as PDF. To do this you will need to click on the print tab on the left of your browser page. From here you can select what parts of the report you want to save as a PDF and also the time frame of data you want to save. Once you have done this you will need to scroll down to the bottom of the page and click on create report. The system will now open up a new tab on your web browser from here you can click on print and then save the document as a PDF.</span>

<span class="mark">Via Report Settings, you can adjust the glucose target of the reports and choose whether to include alerts and glucose values in the reports.</span>

<span class="mark">If the CGM or pump is connected via Bluetooth to the EasyPatch app, you can also view real-time data under the Monitor tab.</span>

### ***5.2 Interpreting Reports*** 

Understanding your reports is key to making the most of your TouchCare Nano System.

<span class="mark">The most useful report to start with is the Dashboard Report. This gives you a clear summary of your recent glucose control, insulin delivery, and auto mode performance. It includes:</span>

- <span class="mark">Your average glucose level, estimated HbA1c, average total daily insulin dose, and average recorded carb and exercise entries.</span>

- <span class="mark">An Ambulatory Glucose Profile</span>

- <span class="mark">Time In Range, Time Below Range, and Time Above Range</span>

- <span class="mark">Sensor usage and average insulin reservoir usage</span>

- <span class="mark">Time spent in Auto Mode and the reasons for any exits</span>

Other important reports are the Day by Day Overview and Device Settings Review.

Here’s a simple structure to follow:

1.  **Step 1: Review Glycemic Control\**
    Start with the Dashboard Report. Look at your Time In Range and Time Below Range over the past two weeks. In most cases, Time In Range should be over 70%, and Time Below Range should be under 4%. Also check how often boluses are delivered. At least three boluses per day is a good sign of consistent mealtime insulin. If Auto Meal Handling is used, make sure meals are being correctly announced.

2.  **Step 2: Fine-Tune Insulin Delivery Settings\**
    Use the Ambulatory Glucose Profile in the Dashboard Report to spot patterns of highs and lows. Then compare with the Day by Day Overview to see if these trends are linked to meals. If post-meal glucose is often high, check if carb counting is accurate and boluses are timed properly before adjusting the carb ratio. For patterns outside of meals, you may want to adjust the insulin sensitivity or glucose sensor target. Also consider other possible influences like physical activity, stress, alcohol, or missed meals.

3.  **Step 3: Encourage Good System Use\**
    Check the Dashboard Report to see how much time the system is in Auto Mode, how often the CGM is being worn, and whether carbs and activities are regularly entered. Daily reports can help identify whether people are overcorrecting highs or lows, or if Exercise mode is being used properly. You can also use the Alert Report and Device Settings Review to see if any alerts are frequent or if alarm settings need adjustment.

4.  **Step 4: Confirm Safety and Backup Plans\**
    Look at the basal rate programmed in Manual Mode and compare it with the average basal delivered in Auto Mode. If there’s a big difference, adjust the manual basal rate by about 10% pe<span class="mark">r time block. Adjusting the basal rate does not affect insulin delivery in Auto Mode, but it ensures a safe basal rate is in place if the system reverts back to manual mode. Always make sure an emergency plan is in place in case the pump stops working.</span> That includes access to insulin pens and knowing how to switch to backup insulin therapy.

<span class="mark">By following these steps, both users and healthcare providers can use EasyView reports to get more out of the TouchCare Nano System and make informed adjustments when needed.</span>

<span class="mark">If anything in a report is unclear, check the legend at the bottom of the report PDFs—it explains all the symbols and color codes.</span>

<span class="mark">And finally, if you’d like to check your data on the go, you can also use the EasyTouch or EasyPatch app. Just open the main menu and tap on Statistics. Here, you can scroll through different reports to view your weekly, monthly or quarterly Time In Range, along with other key insights.</span>

<span class="mark">It’s a simple way to stay connected to your data and make informed decisions about your diabetes management—anytime, anywhere.</span>
