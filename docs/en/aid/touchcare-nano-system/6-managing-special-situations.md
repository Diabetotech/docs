## **<u>6. Managing special situations</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on managing special situations with the TouchCare Nano System.

In this guide, we will provide specific tips on managing hypoglycemia, hyperglycemia, high-fat meals, exercise, illness, alcohol consumption, and travel with the TouchCare Nano System. These recommendations are designed to complement general diabetes management strategies and help you make the most of the system in various scenarios.

### ***6.1 Hypoglycemia*** 

With the TouchCare Nano System, you may require fewer carbohydrates to correct low blood sugar levels, especially if there is little insulin on board. You can monitor the insulin on board from the home screen of the PDM and EasyPatch app. For mild lows, treating with 10 grams or fewer of carbohydrates does not require entering these carbs into the system.

### ***6.2 Hyperglycemia***

For mild highs, trust the system’s automated corrections and avoid entering false carbs. If needed, you can administer a correction bolus using the bolus calculator by entering zero grams of carbohydrates. For persistent high blood sugar (over 270 mg/dl or 15 mmol/l) without improvement, check for pump issue<span class="mark">s. Change your pump if necessary and consider using an insulin pen if the problem continues. Keep spare infusion sets and insulin pens on hand.</span>

<span class="mark">The TouchCare Nano system will notify you if your glucose remains above 250 mg/dL (13.9 mmol/L) for more than three hours in auto mode.</span>

### ***6.3 High-fat Meals*** 

When eating high-fat meals without Auto Meal Handling, you can enter 100% of the carbs, or only 70% and allow the system to manage the remainder. <span class="mark">Alternatively, you can split the bolus—half before the meal and half two hours later. The Touc</span>hCare Nano system does not offer extended or combo insulin boluses during Auto Mode.

### ***6.4 Exercise*** 

Before exercising, it’s recommended to activate Exercise Activity about 1 to 2 hours before your planned activity and to keep it on during the exercise itself.

If you’re not using Auto Meal Handling, and you plan to exercise within 2 hours after a meal, you might also consider reducing your mealtime bolus by 25 to 33%. This can help lower the risk of low blood glucose during physical activity.

### ***6.5 Illness***

Most users can stay in Auto Mode during illness. However, if you experience prolonged hyperglycemia or elevated ketones, consider switching to manual mode and consult your healthcare team for specific adjustments. If you suspect sensor inaccuracies confirm glucose levels with finger pricks.

### ***6.6 Alcohol***

Be mindful of increased hypoglycemia risk after alcohol consumption. Instead of eating additional carbs, consider using the Exercise Activity for a few hours after drinking to help maintain stable glucose levels.

### ***6.7 Travel*** 

When traveling with the TouchCare Nano System, carry sufficient supplies: extra Reservoir Patches, sensors, insulin pens, charger for the PDM and transmitter, and a blood glucose meter with strips. If you use the PDM, remember to adjust the time clock settings when entering a new time zone.

In conclusion, managing special situations with the TouchCare Nano System involves flexibility, ongoing learning, and working closely with your healthcare team. For additional support, consider joining diabetes communities and online support groups for example on FaceBook, where you can share experiences and gather practical advice from others using the system.
