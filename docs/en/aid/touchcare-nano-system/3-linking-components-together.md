## **<u>3. Linking components together</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on how to establish connections between the different components of the TouchCare Nano System.

The TouchCare Nano System operates as follows:

- Glucose levels are measured every 2 minutes by the TouchCare Nano continuous glucose monitor, or CGM. These readings are sent to either the <span class="mark">TouchCare Nano Pump.</span>

- <span class="mark">The APGO algorithm on the PDM or EasyPatch app calculates the insulin requirement based on the most recent data and communicates with the TouchCare Nano patch pump—either the 200U or 300U model—to deliver the appropriate amount of insulin.</span>

- <span class="mark">Glucose data and insulin delivery can be monitored and adjusted via the PDM or the EasyPatch app on your Android or iPhone.</span>

- <span class="mark">All system data can be shared in real-time with caregivers and family through the EasyFollow app, and healthcare providers can access it via the EasyView web</span>

- For users of the PDM, it’s important to use the EasyTouch app to collect and sync data to the cloud, ensuring access and sharing of treatment information.

In previous modules, you’ve learned how to pair the TouchCare Nano CGM with the PDM and the EasyPatch app, and how to link the patch pump to these platforms.

In this guide, we’ll focus on three additional connection steps:

- Setting up an EasyView account

- Uploading PDM data via the EasyTouch app

- <span class="mark">And en</span>abling real-time data sharing via the EasyFollow app

Let’s get started.

### ***3.1 Creating an EasyView account:***

When setting up the TouchCare Nano System through the EasyPatch or EasyTouch app, you’ll be prompted to either create a Medtrum account or log in to an existing one. This same email and password can also be used to access your data via the EasyView platform.

To view your glucose trends, insulin delivery history, and device settings, simply visit [<u>https://easyview.medtrum.eu</u>](https://easyview.medtrum.eu) and log in with your Medtrum credentials.

Once logged in, a navigation bar will appear on the left side of the page. By selecting Settings, you can customize how reports and data are displayed.

If you're a healthcare provider, you can register for a professional account through EasyView Pro. After submitting your application, The Medtrum team will contact you and confirm that you are a healthcare professional administrator. After that you will receive your password in an email. You can change the password after signing in for the first time.

First, click on patients to go to the patient list page to send a request so that you can follow a patient. Click find patient. You will now need to enter the email address of the patient you wish to follow. Enter the email address and click search. The patient's details will appear on the next screen to the right of the patient's details. You will see a box which says send request. Click this box and the request to follow will be sent to the patient. Once the patient has accepted your follow request their name will appear in your patient list.

To view a patient's data you will need to click on their name. From here you will need to click the settings tab on the left of your browser page to select Data To View. If the patient is using the EasyPatch app, you will need to make sure Real-time Data is selected. If the patient is using the PDM, you will need to make sure Uploaded Data is selected.

### ***3.2 Uploading your PDM data via the EasyTouch app***

If you're using the Personal Diabetes Manager (PDM), you can upload your data to the EasyView platform using the EasyTouch app.

The EasyTouch app is available for most iPhones and Android devices. To check if your phone is compatible, visit [<u>medtrum.com/compatibility</u>](https://www.medtrum.com/compatibility). You can find the app by searching “Medtrum” in the App Store or Google Play.

Once you open the app, it will prompt you to connect your PDM—either by scanning its QR code or manually entering the serial number.

To upload your data to the Medtrum cloud, open the main menu of the EasyTouch app, go to Settings, and select Upload Data. You’ll see four upload options: Last week, Last month, Last three months, and All data. Choose the desired time range and tap Upload Data. Once the Bluetooth connection between the PDM and the EasyTouch app is established, the data transfer will begin. When you see the “Upload Successful” message, your data will be available on the EasyView platform and on the Statistics page of the EasyTouch app.

### ***3.3 Real-time sharing via EasyFollow app***

The **EasyFollow app** allows caregivers and family members to monitor a user’s real-time pump and CGM data remotely—even when they’re far away.

To get started, caregivers simply download the EasyFollow app and create their own Medtrum account.

Within the app, they can tap Manage in the top right corner, then select Add Account. By entering the username associated with the user’s EasyView account, they send a follow request.

Once the request is approved, the caregiver will be able to view real-time glucose values, insulin delivery information, and overall system status—providing peace of mind and an extra layer of support.

Congratulations on completing this lesson! By now, you should have a solid understanding of how all the components of the TouchCare Nano System—CGM, PDM or EasyPatch app, patch pump, and the connected platforms EasyView, EasyTouch, and EasyFollow—work together to deliver intelligent, real-time diabetes management.

Rest assured, once you have the devices in hand, the setup process is quite intuitive and largely self-explanatory. And of course, you’ll always have customer service and your care team to support you along the way.
