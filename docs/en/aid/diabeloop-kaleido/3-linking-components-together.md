## **<u>3. Linking components together</u>**

In this video, we will demonstrate the process of establishing connections between the components of the DBLG1 System with Kaleido.

- This system functions by continuously measuring glucose levels with the Dexcom G6 sensor every 5 minutes.

- The glucose data is then transmitted to the DBLG1 handset, which calculates and sends insulin instructions to the Kaleido insulin pump.

- Additionally, the DBLG1 handset communicates with the YourLoops server, where therapy settings and therapy data is stored.

While it is possible for users to install the Dexcom G6 app on their mobile phones and enable carers to follow their glucose levels through the Dexcom Follow app, it is generally not recommended. This is because using both the Dexcom G6 app and the DBLG1 handset simultaneously may sometimes result in connection issues between the Dexcom G6 transmitter and the DBLG1 handset. It is advisable to rely on the DBLG1 handset as the primary device for glucose monitoring and insulin delivery to ensure optimal system performance.

During this video, we will cover the steps to

- link the Dexcom G6 sensor to the DBLG1 handset,

- establish a connection between the Kaleido insulin pump and the DBLG1 handset,

- and guide you through the process of creating a YourLoops account

These connections are crucial for the seamless functioning of the DBLG1 System with Kaleido.

### ***3.1 Linking the Dexcom G6 transmitter and starting Dexcom G6 sensor with the DBLG1 handset***

If you are using the Dexcom G6 transmitter with the Dexcom G6 app, it’s best to turn off your mobile phone during the pairing process.

To connect the Dexcom G6 transmitter to the DBLG1 handset and start the Dexcom G6 sensor, follow these steps:

- Go to the system status in the main menu and press "Pair" at the glucose sensor.

- Enter the sensor code and serial number of the transmitter. Confirm the information, and the handset will start searching for the transmitter. If you do not enter a sensor <span class="mark">code,</span> you must manually calibrate your G6 daily, using values obtained from fingersticks.

- Wait for the connection to be established. If no connection is made after 30 minutes, you will receive a notification.

- Once connected, the sensor will start its warm-up time of approximately 2 hours.

- Once the sensor is paired, you will see a green tick next to the glycemic sensor in the system status, and you can also press more. In this sensor overview scree<span class="mark">n**,**</span> you will see the serial number and expiration date of the transmitter and sensor.

- After the warm-up period, the sensor glycemia will be transmitted to the handset every 5 minutes and displayed on the home screen and lock screen.

- Now you can restart the Loop mode. If you are afraid of forgetting this, you can also start Loop mode immediately after placing the new sensor in the system status. The Loop mode will then switch to "Waiting for <span class="mark">glucose values</span>" and start automatically at the end of the warm-up period.

After 10 days, you will receive an alert that the sensor has expired. Or you can stop the sensor manually by selecting "Stop" in the system status screen. You can then change the sensor and enter a new sensor code.

Make sure you wait 15 to 20 minutes before entering a new sensor code, otherwise the sys<span class="mark">tem might not recognize the sensor as a new one and will display an error message! You need to do this even if the new sensor has the same code as the previous one.</span>

Every 3 months, you’ll need to change your Dexcom G6 transmitter. You can remove the transmitter and pair a new one from the sensor screen in the system status. This option is only visible if there is no active sensor.

### ***3.2 Linking the Kaleido insulin pump to the DBLG1 handset***

Although you will receive a Kaleido handset, it’s important that you do not pair your Kaleido pumps with the Kaleido handset when using the DBLG1 System. If you were already using the Kaleido System before starting on the DBLG1 System, you will first need to unpair both of your pumps from the Kaleido Handset before you are able to pair your pumps with the DBLG1 handset.

To pair a Kaleido insulin pump to your DBLG1 handset, you first need to fill the reservoir with insulin, and place the reservoir in the back of the pump. If you have successfully inserted an insulin reservoir, your pump will beep twice to let you know that everything has gone according to plan and that it is ready to pairto your DBLG1. Your DBLG1 will only be able to respond if it is turned on and within range of your pump.

To pair the Kaleido insulin pump with the DBLG1 handset, follow these steps:

1.  On the handset, go to the System status in the main menu and click on "Pair" in the insulin pump section. The handset searches for the pump and displays the list of pumps detected.

2.  Look for your pump’s 12-digit serial number and colour, then select it by tapping it on the screen. The Kaleido pump will beep 4 times and you tap OK if the serial number is correct.

3.  Then you have to announce if the pump has a new insulin reservoir and or infusion set. If you have placed a new infusion set, the handset will ask you the length of your cannula to know the priming volume and ask you if you want to fill your cannula.

4.  Then your Kaleido pump will be paired. You can check that the checkmark is green and you can see more pump details on your handsets screen.

After a maximum of 3 days, you’ll need to change your infusions set, insulin reservoir and swap over your Kaleido pump. Unlike other patch pumps, a Kaleido pump does not get thrown away, but you’ll have 2 pumps that you’ll use alternately.

You can disconnect your pump via the System Status Screen. To do this, select More and Stop. After removal of the reservoir, you should charge your pump, insert a new fully-filled reservoir in your other pump, and pair the DBLG1 handset to your other pump. If you don’t disconnect your pump, the DBLG1 handset will not be able to pair with your other pump.

Always stop your pump before removing an insulin cartridge and ensure that you do both of these steps within range of your DBLG1 handset. Failure to do so will prevent therapy data from your pump from being transferred to your DBLG1 handset.

### ***3.3 Creating a YourLoops account***

The way to create a YourLoops account as a user or a healthcare provider is different.

- As a user, you should only create a YourLoops account by entering your email address and password when you initialize your DBLG1 handset for the first time. You can then use these credentials to log on to [<u>www.your-loops.com</u>](http://www.your-loops.com).

- As a healthcare provider, you can easily create an account at www.your-loops.com. After confirming your email address and logging in as a healthcare provider, your account will display a patient list. This patient list will be empty until some patients share their data with you or with a care team whom you are part of.

To get started as a healthcare provider, you need to create a new team and add your hospital's contact details. YourLoops will automatically assign a unique identification code to your team. You can then invite patients by entering their email addresses and providing them with the identification code.

- As a patient, you can either click on the invitation email to access YourLoops or log in to YourLoops directly. You will be prompted to accept the invitation and enter the 9-digit team code. Once the patient accepts the invitation, their data can be tracked in real time through your patient list.

- The patient list can be sorted by name, first name, time in range, and time below range. However, please note that this data is only available for the last 24 hours and not for the past 14 days, which would be more clinically relevant for healthcare providers.

- When you click on a patient, you will see the same view as the patients themselves, with the patient's name displayed at the top left. Additionally, there are two buttons at the top for patients and treatment teams.

### ***3.4 Linking the DBLG1 handset to YourLoops*** 

When you first start up the DBLG1 handset, you will be prompted to create a YourLoops account, which will link your information to the YourLoops cloud.

- After creating your account, you will receive an email.

- It's important to remember the email address and password you used for your YourLoops account, as you will need them for logging in or resetting your PIN.

Each DBLG1 handset is associated with a specific YourLoops account, and this cannot be changed later. However, you can modify your YourLoops login details and password directly on the handset and check the connection status to your YourLoops account.

Data from the handset is transmitted to the YourLoops cloud every 5 minutes using the handset's built-in SIM card, which utilizes 4G networks.

It is normal to experience occasional connectivity issues when establishing links between the different components of the DBLG1 System with Kaleido. If you encounter difficulties, troubleshooting steps can be taken to resolve the problem. Remember to be patient and remain calm as these issues can often be resolved. Even if you temporarily lose network connection, it's important to know that you can still use the DBLG1 system as the network is only used to send data to the server. Rest assured that the DBLG1 system is designed to provide reliable and effective insulin management, ensuring your well-being even in the absence of a network connection.
