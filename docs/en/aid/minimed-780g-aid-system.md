## **<u>2. MiniMed 780G according to the CARES paradigm</u>**

Welcome to this lesson about the MiniMed 780G according to the CARES paradigm.

Let's begin our exploration of the MiniMed 780G insulin pump, delving into its insulin delivery calculation and adjustment methods, the process of reverting to manual mode, user education on its usage, and the unique features of its sensor and share function. We'll also cover its indications and the importance of software updates. Let's get started!

**1) The Minimed 780G according to the CARES paradigm**

### ***<span class="mark">\#1 Calculate</span>***

<span class="mark">The MiniMed 780G operates by delivering microboluses of insulin every 5 minutes, based on the sensor glucose value and the total insulin delivery over the past 2 to 6 days.</span>

<span class="mark">Additionally, an autocorrection bolus is given every 5 minutes when the glucose levels exceed 120 mg/dl or 6.7 mmol/l and the maximum basal insulin dose is reached. These autocorrection boluses are constrained to a maximum of 8% of the total daily insulin dose within the last 45 minutes.</span>

<span class="mark">You can set the target value at 100, 110, or 120 mg/dl or 5.6, 6.1, or 6.7 mmol/l.</span>

<span class="mark">The MiniMed 780G incorporates a helpful feature called Safe Meal Bolus, which aims to prevent hypoglycemia by adjusting your insulin dose at mealtimes. If the system predicts a potential risk of hypoglycemia, it may lower your insulin dose or even suggest not bolusing at all for that particular meal. The primary goal of this feature is to ensure your safety and prevent low blood sugar levels.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">\#2 Adjust</span>***

<span class="mark">The MiniMed 780G allows you to adjust various parameters:</span>

- <span class="mark">Target value: You can choose between 100, 110, or 120 mg/dl (or 5.5, 6.1, or</span> 6.7 mmol/l).

- Temporary target value: You can set a temporary higher target value of 150 mg/dl or 8.3 mmol/l specifically for sports activities. It allows for an adjustable duration of up to 24 hours. It's important to note that if you use the temporary target value, no autocorrections will be given during that period.

- Carbohydrate ratio: You can modify the r<span class="mark">atio based on your individual needs.</span>

- <span class="mark">Active insulin time: This parameter determines how long the system considers the insulin to be active in your body and can be set between 2 and 8 hours. It is a means to make the algorithm more aggressive and is usually set at 2 hours.</span>

- Autocorrection boluses: you have the option to disable this in the system, although they are usually left enabled as they enhance the SmartGuard algorithm.

### ***<span class="mark">\#3 Revert</span>***

There are certain conditions that will cause the MiniMed 780G to revert back to manual mode:

- If the maximum basal is given for more than 7 hours or the minimal basal is given for 3-6 hours, you will be asked to enter a blood glucose (BG) or go into baseline delivery (which is the same as the former safe basal) for 4 hours. If the problem is not fixed after 4 hours, you will revert to manual mode.

- You will also revert back to manual mode if there is no sensor data for more than 4 hours, indicated by a blue shield with three dots.

- Lastly, if there are concerns about the integrity of the sensor, the MiniMed 780G might revert back to manual mode.

In CareLink, you can review the reason for auto mode being stopped. In most cases, auto mode is discontinued because of sensor failure.

In manual mode, the system automatically activates the suspend-before-low function, provided it is enabled in the settings. To switch back to auto mode, you will need to perform a finger prick test.

### ***<span class="mark">\#4 Educate</span>***

When starting with an automated insulin delivery system like the MiniMed 780G, it is important to be educated on both general automated insulin delivery system concepts and specific details of the MiniMed 780G.

The general education points covered in previous modules include:

- Using fewer carbohydrates to correct hypoglycemia.

- Considering infusion set problems if blood glucose levels remain high without an apparent reason.

- Trusting the automated insulin delivery system and avoiding unnecessary manual interventions.

- Timing meal boluses 15 minutes before meals.

- <span class="mark">Utilizing Exercise Mode 1 to 2 hours</span> before activity.

- Stopping insulin delivery if the pump is disconnected for more than 15 minutes.

- Responding to alarms and being mindful of automatic updates on mobile phones.

- And seeking peer support for guidance and sharing experiences.

In addition to the general education, specific education points for the MiniMed 780G include:

- <span class="mark">If possible, opt f</span>or the lowest target value of 100 mg/dl or 5.5 mmol/l and the shortest active insulin time of 2 hours for a more aggressive algorithm and improved time-in-range results.

- Avoid fake carbohydrate entries as it can lead to hypoglycemia and disrupt the system's calculation of basal insulin.

- To maintain auto mode, comply with the MiniMed 780G's request for additional finger pricks. These finger pricks are necessary for the algorithm and are different from sensor calibrations. Follow the pump's instructions, which will indicate when a finger prick is required through the "BG required" message and countdown timer.

- If the entered BG is not accepted and the pump asks for a new BG, wait at least an hour before doing so. Failing to wait could increase the likelihood of your sensor being completely deactivated.

- Be aware of the Safe Meal Bolus feature, which can reduce insulin delivery during meals to prevent hypoglycemia. Verify this before making any adjustments to your carbohydrate ratio. If you have a low carbohydrate ratio (like less than 5 grams per unit), you may be at a higher risk of experiencing the Safe Meal Bolus effect. If you encounter postprandial hyperglycemia due to this feature, consider using a higher carb ratio. If you find that your mealtime insulin is still insufficient, you can try adding additional carbs to your meal, such as increasing the amount by 20%.

- Autocorrection boluses are limited to a maximum of 8% of the total insulin dose in the last 45 minutes. If you are experiencing high glucose levels and have not received any autocorrection boluses, you can restart them by performing a finger prick test.

- Understand the distribution of the total daily insulin dose in CareLink reports. Autocorrection boluses are included in the bolus percentage, so a relatively higher bolus percentage may be observed (for example 30% basal and 70% bolus instead of 50/50). Ideally, the percentage of autocorrection boluses should be kept below 30% of the bolus percentage.

### ***<span class="mark">\#5 Sensor</span>*** 

<span class="mark">The MiniMed 780G works with the Guardian 4 sensor and, in some regions, also with the newer Simplera Sync and Instinct sensors. Availability of these sensor options varies by region, and not all are currently accessible everywhere.</span>

<span class="mark">If you have doubts about the accuracy of your sensor reading or do not feel it reflects your condition, perform a finger prick test to confirm. If there is a significant difference between your sensor glucose and finger prick reading, calibrating the Guardian 4 or Simplera sensor is an option, although it may lead to sensor failure.</span>

- It's important to note that if the finger prick value differs from the sensor glucose by more than 36 mg/dl or 2 mmol/l, the sensor will display an error and request a second finger prick test.

- If the subsequent blood glucose entry still shows a significant difference from the sensor glucose, the sensor will be stopped. To avoid sensor loss, you may consider delaying the second glucose entry.

This is why it is advisable not to link your blood glucose meter to your pump, as finger prick tests would then be automatically accounted for as calibrations, potentially leading to sensor failure. Another option is to wait an hour before doing a new finger prick test.

A common issue with the Guardian 4 sensor is the 'sensor update' alarm. This alarm indicates that the sensor is not functioning properly, as it has temporarily lost the subcutaneous signal, and the system is trying to reestablish it. There can be several reasons for this signal disruption. For guidance on how to address this issue, please refer to the module on the Guardian 4 sensor for helpful tips and troubleshooting.

### ***<span class="mark">\#6 Share</span>*** 

The MiniMed 780G allows you to share insulin and glucose data with your healthcare provider and family members. Real-time monitoring of glucose values is possible for both parties.

- If you use the MiniMed Mobile app, the data is accessible in real-time on CareLink, eliminating the need for manual pump readings.

- Data from the pump can also be manually uploaded to CareLink using the blue USB stick.

- Healthcare providers can access the data through CareLink .

- Family members and caretakers can track the data by downloading the CareLink Connect app as a mirror app if the user is utilizing the MiniMed Mobile app.

### ***<span class="mark">2) Indications</span>***

The MiniMed 780G has both a CE-label and FDA-label for use in individuals with type 1 diabetes who are at le<span class="mark">ast 7 years old. In Europe, the CE approval extends its use to children as young as 2 years old. The system is also approved for adults aged eighteen years and older with insulin-dependent type 2 diabetes.</span>

It is suitable for individuals with a minimum total daily insulin dose of 8 units.

<span class="mark">The system is approved for use with Novorapid, Novolog or Humalog 100 U/ml insu</span>lins. The use of Fiasp and Lyumjev 100 U/ml is approved in Europe.

Please note that, as of the latest update of this course, the MiniMed 780G is approved for use during pregnancy in Europe, but it is not approved for use in individuals requiring dialysis.

### ***3) Software updates***

<span class="mark">The MiniMed 780G pumps with software version 6.7 or higher can be updated if necessary. To use the Simplera Sync or Instinct sensor, your pump might need a higher software version. You can check your pump’s software version by going to Device Info in the Settings menu.</span>

<span class="mark">You can update the software version of your pump via the MiniMed Mobile app, as long as Medtronic has approved the update for your pump’s serial number.</span>

- <span class="mark">The update download, which requires an internet connection, may take 45 to 90 minutes, depending on your internet speed.</span>

- <span class="mark">After downloading the update on your phone, you’ll need to install it on your insulin pump. This installation process takes 10 to 20 minutes, during which you must pause insulin delivery and disconnect your pump.</span>

- <span class="mark">Once updated, the pump will enter Manual Mode since the SmartGuard function requires a 5-hour warm-up period. Therefore, it’s best to avoid updating in the evening and to enable the suspend-before-low function.</span>

In conclusion, to maximize the performance of the MiniMed 780G, it is crucial to familiarize yourself with its features, parameters, and recommended practices. Should you encounter any difficulties or have questions, don't hesitate to reach out to Medtronic's customer service, known for their friendly and helpful assistance. Additionally, always consult with your healthcare provider for personalized guidance in managing your diabetes journey. Together, with knowledge, support, and resilience, you can navigate the path to better health and well-being.

## **<u>4. How the MiniMed 780G works</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on the functionality of the MiniMed 780G insulin pump.

In this video, we will explore the usage of the MiniMed 780G insulin pump in both auto mode and manual mode. Additionally, we'll cover the utilization of the MiniMed Mobile app, which functions as a view app.

### ***4.1 Auto Mode***

#### ***Starting SmartGuard Auto Mode***

To enable Auto Mode or the "SmartGuard function" in the MiniMed 780G, go to the shield icon in the menu, and turn the SmartGuard on. Before using the SmartGuard function, the pump must be in manual mode for a minimum of 48 hours starting at midnight, allowing it to consider the total daily insulin dose from the past 2-6 days.

Once the SmartGuard function is active, a blue shield will be displayed on the screen, showing sensor glucose readings and active insulin at the bottom right. If there are issues turning on the SmartGuard function, you can refer to the SmartGuard checklist for troubleshooting.

In the sensor chart, you'll find a 3-hour representation with purple bars indicating auto basal and light blue bars representing autocorrection boluses. Use the back arrow to return to the previous screen.

Keep in mind that while you can't see the exact amount of basal insulin, you can get an idea from the purple bars on the sensor graph and the insulin on board displayed on your home screen.

#### ***Using Temporary Target***

In the SmartGuard menu, you can select the Temp Target of 150 mg/dl or 8.3 mmol/l if you’re planning to do exercise. You can activate it after you select a duration. The home screen will display a green bar indicating how long the Temp Target will be active.

#### ***Adjusting parameters***

In the SmartGuard Settings, you can change the target value to 100, 110, or 120 mg/dl <span class="mark">or 5.6**,** 6</span>.1 or 6.7 mmol/l. You can also choose whether to enable or disable autocorrections.

To modify the carbohydrate ratio and active insulin time, follow these steps:

- Access the Delivery Settings on your MiniMed 780G pump via the Insulin or Settings menu.

- In the Bolus Wizard Setup, you will be able to click on Carb Ratio and Active Insulin Time to change it.

- Don’t forget to change the settings if you change something.

#### ***Delivering a bolus***

In Auto Mode, bolusing is performed similarly to Manual Mode.

However, delivering a manual bolus, overriding the bolus recommendation, or delivering an extended bolus is not possible.

You can access the bolus screen via the Insulin menu or use the downward arrow as a shortcut to the bolus screen.

To give a meal bolus, you have to enter the grams of carbs you are going to eat.

If the Safe Meal Bolus feature prevents you from administering insulin for the carbohydrates you've entered due to low glucose levels, you should monitor your glucose trend. Once an upward trend is detected, retry entering the carbs to receive a suggested insulin bolus.

To give a correction bolus, you can enter 0 grams of carbohydrates.

#### ***Suspending insulin***

To temporarily stop the insulin, such as when disconnecting for showering or swimming, follow the same process as in manual mode. Select "Suspend all delivery" in the Insulin menu or in the Status screen which will appear if you click on the upward arrow.

To restart the basal insulin delivery, you can follow the same process.

### ***4.2 Manual Mode & Suspend-before-low***

In Manual Mode, you can set basal insulin rates, as well as configure temporary basal rates as needed. Furthermore, the pump allows for manual bolus administration and boluses using the bolus calculator, including both regular and extended boluses. For detailed instructions on using Manual Mode effectively, please refer to the module of the MiniMed 780G insulin pump.

Besides Auto Mode, the MiniMed 780G pump also has a built in Suspend-Before-Low Mode.

- This suspend-before-low feature halts ba<span class="mark">sal insulin delivery if the sensor glucose is below 70 mg/dl or 3,9 mmol/l above the low limit, and the algorithm predicts that it will drop below 20 mg/dl or 1,1 mmol/l above the lower limit within the next 30 minutes.</span>

- <span class="mark">After 30 minutes, insulin resumes if the sensor glucose is 20 mg/dl or 1.1 mmol/l above the low limit and the algorithm predicts it to rise more then 40mg/dl or 2.2 mmol/l above the low limit within the subsequent 30 minutes.</span>

- Insulin delivery will always resume after two hours and it can always manually be resumed. After a suspend-before-low episode, the function becomes unavailable for 30 minutes to avoid prolonged periods without insulin.

To activate the Suspend-Before-Low Mode, go to the Alert Settings and choose Low Alert. Set the desired low limit, enable “Suspend before low” and save the changes you made.

It's important to note that the suspend-before-low function can only be used in Manual Mode, and it's recommended to activate it as a safer way to deliver insulin when exiting Auto Mode.

### ***5.2 Interpreting reports***

Interpreting reports on the MiniMed 780G system is generally straightforward due to the limited number of adjustable parameters. These parameters include the target value, carbohydrate ratio, and active insulin time. However, it is important to remember that optimizing the user's interaction with the pump is always a possibility for further improvement.

Interpreting reports from the MiniMed 780G system can be done effectively by following a structured approach. Here are the recommended steps:

1\. Assess Glycemic Information: Begin by evaluating the Time In Range and Time Below Range over the past 2 weeks on the Assessment and Progress Report. Ensure that treatment goals are being met, with Time In Range above 70% and Time Below Range below 4%. Pay attention to the frequency of meal bolusing, which should be at least 3 times a day.

2\. Optimize Automated Insulin Delivery Settings: Analyze the Ambulatory Glucose Profile on the Assessment and Progress Report to identify trends of hypo- or hyperglycemia. Take note of the additional information provided below the AGP graph and check if these trends are related to meal boluses on the daily reports.

- If you notice trends in post-meal glucose levels, it's important to assess various factors such as timing, accurate carbohydrate counting, and the activation of the Safe Meal Bolus feature. To check if the Safe Meal Bolus feature is being used, manually divide the amount of carbohydrates by the carbohydrate ratio and verify if the given insulin corresponds to this number. It's also essential to assess if the patient is entering false carbohydrates, which is generally not recommended. If you observe consistent entries of the same grams of carbohydrates in response to high glucose levels, inquire with the user if these were fake carbs. Encourage them to avoid this behavior and develop trust in the system.

- For trends outside of meals, consider adjusting the active insulin time if you observe trends after autocorrection boluses. Similarly, if you notice trends outside of any boluses, consider changing the target value. Additionally, explore possible correlations with factors such as exercise, alcohol consumption, or hypoglycemia.

3\. Guide Behavioral Recommendations: Ensure proper usage of the automated insulin delivery system by checking sensor wear, activation of auto mode, and accurate carb entries in the Assessment and Progress Report. Evaluate the adherence to pre-meal bolusing in the Meal Bolus Wizard Report and the regularity of infusion set changes in the Adherence Report. Review daily reports for overcorrection of hypo- or hyperglycemia, correct use of temporary target values, and the frequency of alarms. Assess the appropriateness of alarm settings and reminders in the Device Settings.

4\. Review Pump Settings and Emergency Plans:

- Verify that the preset basal insulin rate matches the actual basal insulin in auto mode. If there is a discrepancy, adjust the basal rate by 10% in each time block.

- Document all pump settings and establish an emergency plan, including insulin pen usage, in case of a pump malfunction. Ensure access to insulin pens.

By following these steps, users, caregivers, and healthcare providers can effectively interpret reports from the MiniMed 780G system and make informed decisions to optimize its performance. If something is not clear on the reports, you can always look at the legend, where the meaning of each icon and color on the graph is explained. Although the MiniMed 780G does not use the regular AGP reporting system, interpreting the reports is generally straightforward in this context.

**<u>6. Managing special situations</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on managing special situations with the Minimed 780G system.

<span class="mark">In this video, we will provide specific tips on managing hypo- and hyperglycemia, high-fat meals, sports, illness, and travel with the MiniMed 780G system.</span>

These recommendations complement the general guidelines for managing similar situations with automated insulin delivery systems. Please refer to the general module on automated insulin delivery systems for further information.

### **<u>6.1 Hypoglycemia</u>**

With an automated insulin delivery system, you require fewer carbohydrates to treat or prevent hypoglycemia compared to non-automated insulin delivery systems, especially if you have little insulin on board. With the MiniMed 780G you can check your insulin on board on the home screen or on the sensor graph.

If you consume less than 10 grams of carbohydrates to address a hypo, there is no need to enter this in the pump.

To prevent hypoglycemic events, you can activate the temporary target.

### ***6.2 Hyperglycemia***

In case of mild hyperglycemia, it is generally recommended to trust the MiniMed 780G system and avoid entering fake carbs. However, you can give a correction bolus by entering 0 grams of carbohydrates in the bolus calculator.

In cases of prolonged severe hyperglycemia for example more than 270 mg/dl or 15 mmol/l with no decreasing trend or no 50 mg/dl or 2.7 mmol/l decrease within one hour after an insulin bolus, consider potential infusion set problems. If in doubt, change it out. You can administer an extra insulin bolus with your pump in Manual Mode or by entering fake carbs. If this doesn’t work or if there is an infusion set problem, use an insulin pen.

<span class="mark">Carry sufficient equipment to change your infusion set and give insulin via an insulin pen when needed.</span>

<span class="mark">To avoid infusion set problems, replace the infusion set as recommended and set a reminder through the Device Settings on your pump or o</span>n your phone.

### ***6.3 High-fat meals***

It is recommended to enter 70% of the carbohydrates during a high-fat meal, allowing the automated insulin delivery system to handle the remaining portion.

If necessary, consider splitting the bolus by giving 50% of the carbohydrates before the meal and the remaining 50% two hours later.

Recent studies suggest that simply entering 100% of the carbohydrates usually does not lead to hypo- or hyperglycemia when using the MiniMed 780G.

### ***6.4 Exercise***

If you exercise with the MiniMed 780G, use the temporary target and meal bolus reduction as recommended in the general module on automated insulin delivery systems.

For light prolonged activity, you can also choose to use the normal targets and disable autocorrections.

If you use the MiniMed Mobile app, consider using the native Apple Watch connection or open-source apps to transfer your sensordata on your smartwatch or cycling computer for easier monitoring.

### ***6.5 Illness***

Most of the time, you will be able to remain in Auto Mode during illness with the MiniMed 780G system. However, there are exceptions to this rule. If you experience elevated ketones or prolonged hyperglycemia, which can be caused by factors such as high doses of corticosteroids, it may be necessary to temporarily exit Auto Mode and take additional measures to manage your glucose levels effectively. In such cases, it is important to consult with your healthcare team for specific guidance and adjustments to your treatment plan.

Note that higher doses of paracetamol may cause false high values on the Guardian <span class="mark">4 and Simplera sensor. In such cases, perform additional finger prick tests. If uncertain about the sen</span>sor's integrity, it is advisable to exit Auto Mode.

### ***6.6 Alcohol***

<span class="mark">While the risk of false high values on the Guardian 4 sensor due to alcohol consumption is rare, it has been mentioned.</span>

<span class="mark">It is essential to be aware of a higher risk of hypoglycemia after consuming alcohol.</span>

Eating more carbohydrates does not help prevent alcohol-related hypoglycemia. <span class="mark">Instead, consider using the Temp Target preventively for a few hours after drinking high amounts of</span> alcohol.

### ***6.7 Travel***

When traveling with the MiniMed 780G pump, make sure to have an adequate supply of essential items. Don't forget to bring some insulin pens with rapid-acting insulin to cover any emergencies that may arise.

In the event of pump failure, a new emergency replacement insulin pump will be sent to you wi<span class="mark">thin 24 hours, except for remote locations like islands. If you are traveling to such areas, you can request a loaner pump through the WeCare app or platform, but ensure to do so at least 3 weeks in advance of your trip.</span>

<span class="mark">If you’re using the Guardian 4 sensor, take good care of your transmitters, especially while traveling, as they are not rushed for replacements. Consider bringing a new transmitter if the current one is close to expiration.</span>

<span class="mark">Remember to pack AA batteries for your pump and AAA batteries for your transmitter charger if you’re using the Guardian 4 sensor.</span>

<span class="mark">Additionally, don't forget to adjust the time on your MiniMed 780G insulin pump when arriving in a new time zone.</span>

In conclusion, managing special occasions with the MiniMed 780G requires individualized approaches and a willingness to adapt and learn through trial and error. While we have provided starting point recommendations for various situations, it's important to remember that everyone's diabetes management needs are unique. Working closely with your healthcare team is crucial in fine-tuning your insulin dosages and strategies to suit your specific circumstances. Additionally, seeking support from online communities, such as Facebook groups, can provide valuable insights and a sense of solidarity, as you connect with others who may have faced similar challenges. Remember, this information on managing special occasions is meant to complement the general recommendations from the overall video on AID systems.
