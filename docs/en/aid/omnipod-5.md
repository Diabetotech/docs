## **<u>2. Omnipod 5 according to the CARES paradigm</u>**

Welcome to this video where we discuss the Omnipod 5 system according to the CARES paradigm.

We will discuss how Omnipod 5 calculates and adjusts the automated insulin delivery, when it reverts back to manual mode, specific education tips and sensor issues, and how it shares data. After that we will go over Omnipod 5’s official indications and software updates.

### ***<span class="mark">1) Omnipod 5 ac</span>cording to CARES***

### ***\#1 Calculate***

The Omnipod 5 system operates as follows:

- The SmartAdjust algorithm calculates the basal insulin requirement or “adaptive basal rate” every 5 minutes. It considers the predicted glucose levels for the upcoming hour, the target value and the total insulin delivered by the previous Pod. When predicted hyperglycemia is detected, the algorithm increases the insulin delivery. On the other hand, if hypoglycemia is predicted, the basal insulin delivery is reduced or paused. If the glucose level falls below 60 mg/dL (3.3 mmol/l), the basal insulin delivery is always paused to prioritize safety.

- When initiating the Omnipod 5 system, you will have to enter starting settings including basal rates. Automated mode can be started immediately and the system will base the magnitude of basal rate adjustments on the basal rate provided, although the maximum delivery is more constrained with the first Pod for safety.

- After 48 hours of use, and the first Pod change, the system will use the historical basal insulin delivery as a base for the automated basal insulin and further adjust to individual needs thanks to machine learning. The longer the system is worn, the better it performs, so it is recommended to wear it consistently for a few weeks to fully experience the positive impact on Time in Range. This also means that changes in health conditions or medication, such as sickness or starting a steroid medication, will have a more noticeable effect after a pod change. It’s important to note that if you switch Controllers, you will lose your insulin delivery history, and the system will need to re-learn your insulin requirements.

- The SmartAdjust algorithm does not provide additional autocorrection boluses. It uses basal rate adjustments to prevent and correct hyperglycemia.

- The target glucose range can be adjusted in 60-minute time blocks, allowing customization between 110 and 150 mg/dL (6.1 and 8.3 mmol/l).

- It’s important to note that the SmartBolusCalculator of the Omnipod 5 system does not only take into account the current glucose, but also the glucose trend.

### ***\#2 Adjust***

With the Omnipod 5 system, users have the ability to adjust certain parameters to personalize their insulin therapy:

- The target glucose range can be set within 60-minute time blocks, allowing customization between 110 and 150 mg/dL (6.1 and 8.3 mmol/L).

- The bolus calculator parameters, such as the carbohydrate-to-insulin ratio, correction factor, duration of insulin action, and "Correct Above" value, can also be adjusted. It’s important to note that these bolus calculator parameters will only have an impact on the manual mealtime or correction boluses, and not on the calculated automated or “adaptive basal rate”. Of these bolus calculator parameters, the carbohydrate-to-insulin ratio is the most influential parameter in terms of clinical impact.

- An optional Activity Feature is available, which sets a higher target glucose value of 150 mg/dL (8.3 mmol/L) and reduces insulin delivery to accommodate physical activity or times of increased risk of hypoglycemia.

### ***\#3 Revert***

In the Omnipod 5 system, there are situations that can trigger a "Limited mode," including a lack of sensor data for more than 20 minutes or a minimum or maximum insulin release for an extended period of time. During Limited mode, the system maintains a fixed basal insulin rate based on recent history and settings. The maximum basal insulin rate in Limited mode will never exceed the basal rate settings of Manual Mode.

A lack of sensor data can occur due to sensor warm-up or communication issues between the sensor and the Pod, or between the Pod and the Omnipod 5 app. To prevent communication problems, it is recommended to wear the sensor and the CGM within line of sight. If communication problems persist, the app will display a "No Pod Communication" message, and you should follow the on-screen instructions to resolve the issue. Bringing the Controller or mobile phone closer to the Pod, within a distance of 1.5 meters (or 5 feet), can help restore the connection between the Controller and the Pod. If connection restoration fails, you have the option to deactivate the Pod. Once the issue causing the Limited Mode is resolved, Automated Mode will automatically resume.

In addition, Limited Mode may be triggered by minimum or maximum insulin release continuously for an extended period of time, resulting in an "Automated Delivery Restriction" alarm. It is important to respond appropriately by confirming the accuracy of the sensor and reactivating Automated Mode.

When deactivating a Pod, the system automatically switches to Manual Mode. After activating a new Pod, you will be advised to restart Automated Mode again.

It's worth noting that connecting the OmniPod 5 app to the Pod is only necessary for delivering bolus insulin, activating and deactivating a Pod, and for responding to alarms. The algorithm resides on the Pod itself, meaning that even without communication between the Pod and the Omnipod 5 app, the basal insulin will be adjusted every 5 minutes based on the sensor glucose values and trends.

### ***\#4 Educate***

When starting an automated insulin delivery system like Omnipod 5, it's important to consider both general education points for AID systems and specific education points for Omnipod 5.

For general education, please refer to the generic module, which includes the following key points:

- Use fewer carbohydrates to correct hypoglycemia.

- Consider an insulin delivery/absorption problem if glucose levels remain high for several hours without an apparent reason. "If in doubt, change it out."

- Trust the AID system.

- Administer a meal bolus 15 minutes before meals.

- Activate Exercise Mode 1 to 2 hours before exercising.

- Respond to alarms promptly.

- Disable automatic updates on your mobile phone.

- And Seek peer support for guidance and sharing experiences.

Specific education points for Omnipod 5 are as follows:

- Checking the compatibility of your phone with the Dexcom G6 app before starting. If in your country, you have access to the Omnipod 5 mobile app and wish to use this instead of your Controller, you should also check the compatibility of your phone with this app.

- When using the Omnipod 5 app on your mobile phone, it is recommended to leave it open in the background and avoid closing it. This ensures continuous communication and data exchange within the Omnipod 5 system.

- <span class="mark">Wear the Pod and the CGM on the same side of your body within sight of each other to minimize connection problems.</span>

- During the initial weeks of using the Omnipod 5 system, you may encounter the "Automated Delivery Restriction" alarm more frequently due to the limited maximum insulin delivery while using the first couple of Pods. However, as you continue using the system, this occurrence will decrease. It is important to respond promptly to this alarm by confirming the accuracy of the sensor and returning to Automated Mode.

- Press "Use CGM" to ensure that the bolus calculator considers the trend arrow.

- Brief interruptions in basal insulin delivery may occur if glucose levels are trending downward, even if they are above the target value. This is a normal occurrence with Omnipod 5's algorithm and typically lasts only 5 to 10 minutes unless glucose continues to fall.

### ***2) Indications***

Omnipod 5 has both a CE-label and FDA-label for use in individuals with type 1 diabetes who are at least 2 year<span class="mark">s old. In addition, it holds FDA approval for adults aged eighteen years and older with type 2 diabetes who use insulin.</span>

It is suitable for individuals with a minimum total daily insulin dose of 5 units.

The system is approved for use with Novolog, Novorapid, Humalog or Admelog 100 U/ml insulins.

Please note that Omnipod 5 is not approved for use in pregnancy or in individuals requiring dialysis.

## **<u>4. How the Omnipod 5 system works</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on the functionality of the Omnipod 5 system. Controlling the insulin delivery is done through the Omnipod 5 app on your Controller or on your mobile phone. Currently, the Omnipod 5 app for phone control is only available in the US, but broader roll-out is expected.

In this video, we will explore how to use the Omnipod 5 app in general, and how to use it in both Automated and Manual Mode.

### ***4.1 Omnipod 5 App***

The initial setup of the Omnipod 5 app needs to be completed on your Controller.

- During this process, you will be required to provide necessary details, including your Omnipod ID and Glooko account information. To create an Omnipod ID, you can visit Insulet's website at www.omnipod.com/setup. Creating an Omnipod ID is free of charge and is necessary for accessing the app and website functionalities. Once you have obtained your Omnipod ID, you can use it to log into Insulet's website and access their online training resources.

- When setting up the Omnipod 5 app on your Controller, you will also be prompted to confirm whether you have completed the appropriate training. After completing this step, you can choose to proceed with the setup either on the Controller or on the Omnipod 5 app installed on your mobile phone.

- Please note that switching between the Controller and mobile phone is only <span class="mark">possible when starting a new pod. Additionally, when you uninstall and reinstall the Omnipod 5 app on your phone, or change the Controller, all the data and history accumulated by the Pod will be lost, and the algorithm learning restarts. If necessary, you have the option to clear the data within your existing controller to restart the algorithm.</span>

- <span class="mark">For the Controller, you will choose a PIN, security message, and background, while the mobile app PIN will match your phon</span>e's PIN.

- If you choose to use the Omnipod 5 app on your mobile phone, there are specific settings that you need to consider for optimal functionality:

  - Enable Bluetooth.

  - Grant location permission to the app.

  - Allow notifications from the Omnipod 5 app.

  - Ensure that the "do not disturb" option is disabled.

  - Disable any battery-saving mode.

Using the Omnipod 5 app, as well as the Dexcom G6 <span class="mark">or G7 app,</span> should not significantly impact the battery life of your device. Both apps utilize Bluetooth Low Energy for communication, which is designed to be energy-efficient.

Internet connectivity is essential for receiving updates and automatically uploading data to Glooko. The Controller comes with a SIM card for internet access, while the mobile app utilizes your data plan. It's advisable to keep your Wi-Fi turned on.

- In Automated or Auto Mode, the icon and letters are purple, while in Manual Mode, they are blue.

- The CGM curve can be accessed at the bottom right, providing insulin on board information by pressing the question mark.

### ***4.2 Automated Mode***

#### ***Starting Omnipod 5 Automated Mode***

To enable Automated Mode on the Omnipod 5 app, go to the settings and tap on Switch Mode. Note that Automated Mode can only be activated if the Pod and sensor are properly paired.

If Automated Mode is functioning properly, the mode indicator on the home screen will change to "Automated," and the dominant color on the app will be purple.

In landscape mode, a pink line will appear at the bottom of the screen indicating that the basal insulin is automated based on the sensor glucose. Note that you cannot directly view the exact basal insulin delivery. If you wish to see this information, navigate to Settings, History Detail then Auto Events.

#### ***Using Activity Feature***

For physical activities, you can utilize the Activity Feature:

- Access the menu and select "Activity."

- Set a duration between 1 and 24 hours, then press "Start."

- The middle tab will turn light green and display "Activity." Tapping the tab will show the remaining duration and allow you to cancel the activity feature.

- Once the set time limit is reached, the system will automatically turn the Activity Feature off.

#### ***Adjusting parameters***

The Omnipod 5 system allows for adjustments in the target value and several parameters for when you use the bolus calculator.

- To modify the target value, you can navigate to the menu and select "Settings," then choose the "Bolus" option. Here you can adjust your target value per 60-minute time block.

- The bolus calculator parameters such as carb-to-insulin ratio, correction factor, duration of insulin action and “correct above value” can also be found here. It's important to note that any modifications made to these parameters will only impact the insulin doses calculated when using the bolus calculator feature. The reverse correction option is also available, which recommends a reduction to the meal dose of insulin when the glucose is lower than the target value.

Additionally, when initiating the Omnipod 5 system, you will need to enter a basal rate (or basal rates), which will be used by the algorithm to determine the adaptive <span class="mark">basal rate for the first pod. After 48 hours of use, and the first Pod change, the system will use the historical bas</span>al insulin delivery as a base for the automated basal insulin. If you are unsure of how to determine a starting basal rate, consider settings with approximately 50% of insulin needs going to the basal rate and the other 50% for bolus insulin.

#### ***Giving an insulin bolus***

When administering a meal bolus, follow these steps:

- Press the bolus button.

- Enter the number of grams of carbohydrates you will be consuming.

- The bolus calculator in the Omnipod 5 app automatically retrieves the sensor glucose for calculation. However, to incorporate the sensor glucose trend into the calculation, you need to tap the "Use Sensor" button. If the Omnipod 5 app does not have recent sensor glucose available, the "Use Sensor" button will be disabled, and you will need to manually enter a glucose value.

- To view the details behind the calculation of your bolus, you can press the calculations button.

- Extended bolus delivery is not available in Automated Mode and can only be done in Manual Mode.

- Finally, you can administer the bolus. Once the green bar appears, you can put away your Controller or mobile phone. If necessary, you can cancel the bolus during delivery.

#### ***Suspending insulin***

To temporarily stop insulin delivery, such as during exercise or other brief activities, you need to switch back to Manual Mode. Here you can choose to pause insulin or set a temporary basal rate of 0 units per hour or minus 100%.

To pause insulin delivery:

1.  Access the menu by pressing the menu button.

2.  Select "Pause Insulin" from the options.

3.  Specify the duration of the pause, which can be 0.5 hours, 1 hour, 1.5 hours, or 2 hours.

4.  Confirm the pause by selecting "Pause" and then confirming the action.

5.  A yellow banner will appear on the home screen indicating that insulin delivery is paused.

Note: During the pause period, the Pod will emit beeps every 15 minutes. At the end of the pause period, insulin delivery does not automatically resume! The Pod and Omnipod 5 App will notify you every minute for 3 minutes and repeat the notification every 15 minutes until you manually restart insulin delivery.

It is essential to tap the "Start Insulin" option to resume insulin delivery after a pause period in Manual Mode. Insulin delivery does not restart automatically, and failure to restart it could lead to hyperglycemia.

Alternatively, you can use a temporary basal rate of 0 units per hour or minus 100% for a specific duration, up to 12 hours. In this case, insulin delivery will automatically resume after the set time period. The app will emit an audible beep at the beginning and every hour to indicate the temporary basal rate. This approach is particularly useful when you need to disconnect for a longer duration, as it reduces the number of alarms and interruptions. However, it is never advisable to suspend all insulin delivery for more than two hours unless you plan to take insulin by injection, as ketoacidosis can develop.

Many individuals choose to turn off their Controller using the ON/OFF button when temporarily stopping insulin delivery, as it prevents alarms from being triggered since the Controller is no longer connected to <span class="mark">the Pod.</span>

### ***4.3 Manual Mode***

In certain situations, it may be necessary to switch to Manual Mode in the Omnipod 5 system. This can be done to adjust insulin delivery according to specific needs, such as during illness, menstrual cycles, periods of stress, extended physical activity, or to pause insulin delivery.

In Manual Mode, the menu of the Omnipod 5 app differs from that of Automated Mode. It provides options for setting the basal insulin rate, switching basal profiles, creating temporary basal rates, and pausing insulin. Extended boluses can also be administered in Manual Mode.

For a detailed understanding of Manual Mode operation, you can refer to the module on the Omnipod DASH system, as the functionality is similar.

In conclusion, the Omnipod 5 system empowers users to take control of their insulin management. With the flexibility to switch between Automated Mode and Manual Mode, users have the ability to adapt and make adjustments according to their individual needs.

Remember, mastering any new technology may take time and practice. If you encounter any challenges along the way, don't hesitate to consult the manual for detailed instructions or reach out to your diabetes team. The app itself is designed to be user-friendly, with intuitive features and a range of adjustable settings. Stay patient, explore the various options available, and soon you'll be harnessing the full potential of the Omnipod 5 System for personalized diabetes management.

### ***5.2 Interpreting reports***

With Omnipod 5, the target value, correction factor, duration of insulin action and carbohydrate-to-insulin ratio are the key parameters that can be adjusted to personalize your insulin therapy. The target value represents the desired glucose level, and it can be set for each time block to accommodate different insulin needs throughout the day. Similarly, the carbohydrate-to-insulin ratio determines the amount of insulin required for a certain amount of carbohydrates consumed. Both of these parameters can be easily adjusted within the bolus settings, allowing you to fine-tune your insulin delivery based on your individual needs and preferences.

When analyzing the reports, it is beneficial to follow a structured approach, as emphasized in the general module on automated insulin delivery systems. Here is a roadmap to guide your interpretation:

1\. Assess Glycemic Information: Begin by evaluating the Time in Range and Time Below Range over t<span class="mark">he past 2-4 weeks on the Summary Tab.</span>

- <span class="mark">Ensure that treatment goals are being met, with Time in Range above 70% and Time Below Range below 4%.</span>

- <span class="mark">It is important to pay attention to the frequency of meal bolusing, which is also indicated in the Summary Tab of</span> Glooko. On average, there should be around 3 boluses per day for meals, with a few additional boluses for snacks. If you notice an unusually high number of boluses, such as 20 boluses per day, it may indicate that the user doesn't trust the system and is constantly interfering by delivering manual correction boluses. On the other hand, fewer than 3 boluses per day may indicate that the user is neglecting to bolus for food and mistakenly believes that the algorithm will “cover” their meals and snacks.

2\. Optimize AID Settings: Analyze the AGP (Ambulatory Glucose Profile) on the Summary Tab to identify trends of hypo- or hyperglycemia, and check if these trends are related to boluses on the Weekly Overview reports.

- If you notice trends after meal boluses, it's important to assess various factors such as bolus timing and accurate carbohydrate counting, before adjusting the carb ratio.

- If you see a trend after manual or autocorrection boluses, you can try to adjust the correction factor, the duration of insulin action or the “Correct Above” threshold.

- For trends outside of meal boluses, consider adjusting the target value. Keep in mind that you have the flexibility to adjust the target value based on different time blocks. If glucose levels are modestly elevated through the night, consider lowering the target value further during overnight periods to optimize your Time in Range (TIR) and improve overall glycemic control.

- Additionally, explore possible correlations with factors such as exercise, alcohol consumption, or hypoglycemia.

3\. Guide Behavioral Recommendations: To ensure the proper usage of the automated insulin delivery system, it is important to review various aspects in the reports available. Here are some key considerations:

- Check the sensor wear duration and time spent in Automated Mode in the Summary Tab

- The time in Automated Mode should be \>90%, and the time in Limited Mode should be low. If the time in Limited Mode is high, this could be caused by a poor connection between the transmitter and Pod. You can check to ensure that the sensor and Pod are worn on the same side of the body, in sight of each other, to have fewer connection problems.

- Another cause of excessive Limited Mode is a frequent "Automated Delivery Restriction" alarm. Assure the user that this is more common during the first few weeks of system use, and that it is important to respond to it by confirming the accuracy of the CGM and then turning Automated mode back on.

- Also in the Summary Tab: Evaluate if bolus calculator overrides are low (ideally 0%). Also, check the amount of carbs entered for meals.

- Weekly Overview Report: Assess the adherence to pre-meal bolusing, overcorrection of hypo- and hyperglycemia, and the appropriate use of the Activity Feature.

- On the Insights tab, you can check if the Pod replacement is being performed as recommended (which is usually every 3 days)

- Ask the patient if the sensor alert settings align with the user's needs and preferences. These can be adjusted in the Dexcom G6 app.

4\. Review Pump Settings and Emergency Plans:

- Devices Tab: Verify that the preset basal insulin rate on your Omnipod Pod aligns approximately with the amount of basal insulin being delivere<span class="mark">d in</span> Automated Mode. This can be accomplished by dividing the average daily basal delivery by 24.

- Keep a detailed record of all pump settings and create an emergency plan that includes instructions for using long-acting and short-acting insulin pens in case of a pump malfunction. Ensure easy access to insulin pens for such situations.

## **<u>6. Managing special situations</u>**

Welcome to this video on managing special situations with the Omnipod 5 automated insulin delivery system.

These guidelines complement the general recommendations for automated insulin delivery systems. For a comprehensive understanding, refer to the general module on automated insulin delivery systems.

### ***6.1 Hypoglycemia*** 

With the Omnipod 5 system, managing hypoglycemia becomes more efficient. You may require fe<span class="mark">wer carbohydrates to raise your blood sugar levels compared to when not using an aut</span>omated insulin delivery system, especially if you have minimal insulin on board.

You can check the insulin on board displayed on the home screen of the Omnipod 5 app. In landscape mode, you can also see your recent insulin history, which can be helpful in assessing your insulin levels over the past few hours.

It's important to note that if you consume a small amount of carbohydrates for treating hypoglycemia, typically less than 10 grams, you don't need to enter this amount into the bolus calculator.

When planning physical activity, consider using the Activity Feature or temporarily setting a higher Target Value to prevent hypoglycemia. These adjustments help maintain stable blood sugar levels and reduce the risk o<span class="mark">f recurrent hypoglycemia. Also, consider using activity mode for several hours after particu</span>larly intense exercise in order to prevent delayed bouts of hypoglycemia.

### ***6.2 Hyperglycemia***

When it comes to mild hyperglycemia, the idea is to trust Omnipod 5 and let it work itself. However, you can bring down hyperglycemia faster if you administer a manual correction bolus via the bolus calculator. You can leave the carbs at 0 grams. Don’t forget to press the Use CGM button to also take the glucose trend into account.

When it comes to prolonged severe hyperglycemia, you should always think of an infusion set problem. When in doubt, it is best to change your pod and place it in a new site.. The motto is "If in doubt, change it out".

Make sure to carry enough equipment to do an emergency pod change.

### ***6.3 High-fat meals***

Here are some recommendations for managing a high-fat meal with Omnipod 5:

1.  It is generally advised to enter only 70% of the total carbohydrates consumed for a high-fat meal. The algorithm in the Omnipod 5 system will adjust the basal insulin delivery to handle the remaining carbohydrates and prevent post-meal hypoglycemia.

2.  Another strategy is to split the bolus dose by entering 50% of the calculated carbohydrates before the meal and the remaining 50% approximately 2 hours after the meal. This allows for a more precise insulin response to the slower digestion of high-fat meals.

3.  While Automated Mode in the Omnipod 5 does not allow for extended boluses, you have the option to switch to Manual Mode if you prefer to use an extended bolus feature. In Manual Mode, you can set an extended bolus to match the digestion rate of the high-fat meal.

By employing these strategies, you can effectively manage glucose levels when consuming <span class="mark">high-fat meals with the Omnipod 5 system. It is always recommended to consult with your healt</span>hcare provider for personalized guidance and adjustments based on your specific needs.

***6.4 Exercise***

When exercising with Omnipod 5, it is recommended to turn on the Activity Feature 1-2 hours prior to the onset of activity. If exercise takes place after a meal, consider reducing the suggested meal bolus as recommended in the general module on automated insulin delivery systems.

<span class="mark">If you’re using a Dexcom sensor, you can try to connect your Dexcom G6 or G7 app to your smartwatch or cycling computer for easier monitoring during exercise.</span>

<span class="mark"></span>

If you ingest small amounts of carbohydrates while exercising, you don't usually need to enter them in the Omnipod 5 app.

### ***6.5 Illness***

During illness you may notice more prolonged hyperglycemia. Consider additional manual correction boluses.

Remember that the actual total insulin dose will only be taken into account when you change your pod. To better manage blood sugar levels during illness, you may consider switching to Manual Mode and adjusting the temporary basal and/or basal insulin rate. You may also choose to set up a basal insulin profile specifically for use when you are sick.

Follow hyperglycemia guidelines for managing high blood sugar levels during illness.

### ***6.6 Alcohol***

When consuming alcohol, it is recommended to take proactive measures to prevent hypoglycemia. This can be done by temporarily increasing your target value and/or utilizing the Activity Feature to reduce insulin delivery.

It is important to note that increasing carbohydrate intake will not be effective in preventing hypoglycemia, as the system will compensate by increasing insulin administration to balance any rise in glucose levels.

### ***6.7 Travel*** 

When traveling with Omnipod 5, it is important to pack an ample supply of necessary equipment and accessories. Remember to bring your Controller charger and, if needed, an <span class="mark">adapter for the country you are visiting. While a spare Controller is not available, you can consider packing an extra Dexcom G6 transmitter if you’re using this sensor type.</span>

<span class="mark"></span>

If you are traveling by air, you can keep the Bluetooth functionality of your handset, pump, and transmitter active. However, the SIM card's Wi-Fi and 4G capabilities will not be operational during the flight. This should not affect the functioning of the Omnipod 5 app on your Controller or mobile device, as it will continue to work seamlessly via Bluetooth. However, data transmission to Glooko will be temporarily unavailable.

When crossing different time zones, the Omnipod 5 app should automatically adjust the time. However, it is always a good idea to double-check the time settings upon arrival at your destination.

During take-off and landing, some airlines may require you to put your Controller or phone in airplane mode. You can do this via the settings. Putting your Omnipod 5 app in airplane mode will not affect your automated basal insulin delivery.

It is important to note that the Omnipod 5 Controller is not waterproof. If you plan to go swimming, you can leave it in the changing room and temporarily turn it off using the ON/OFF button to avoid triggering unnecessary alarms due to the disconnection from the Pod. Or you can use a waterproof case, which allows you screen access even if you have wet hands.
