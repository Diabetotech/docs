## 5.1 Types of reports

Tidepool provides a modern, web-based interface that works on both desktop and mobile devices. Once your twiist app is linked to your Tidepool account, data uploads automatically and can be reviewed anytime.

### Basics View

The Basics view shows summary statistics including daily time in range, average blood glucose, sensor usage percentage, average daily total insulin breakdowns, average daily carbs, and GMI (glucose management indicator).

### Trends View

The Trends view overlays blood glucose meter or CGM data over a 24-hour period to help you and your patients better understand where blood glucose trends and variability are currently occurring.

### Daily View

The Daily view syncs up all blood glucose meter, CGM, and insulin pump data. Summary statistics include Time In Range, Average Blood Glucose, Total Insulin, Total Carbs, Standard Deviation and Coefficient of Variation.

### Print Data

You can click the print button to generate a PDF that includes the Basics view, the last six days from the Daily view, the last 30 days from the Blood Glucose Log section and the insulin pump profile settings.

## 5.2 Interpreting reports

### 1. Assess Glycemic Information

- Check Time in Range over the last 2-4 weeks. Aim for at least 70% in range and less than 4% below range.

### 2. Optimize AID Settings

- Review the AGP for patterns of hypoglycemia, then evaluate patterns of hyperglycemia.
- If patterns are linked to meals, check carb counting accuracy and consider adjusting the carb ratio.
- If patterns occur after correction boluses, review the insulin sensitivity factor.
- Review basal insulin delivery patterns. If consistently modulating up, increase scheduled basal rate by 10-20%. If consistently modulating down, decrease by 10-20%.
- If there is a pattern of plateau of basal insulin delivery, consider increasing Max Basal rate.

### 3. Guide Behavioral Recommendations

- Use the Daily Report to check pre-meal bolusing, carb entry timing, overriding the bolus recommendations and usage of Workout and Pre-Meal Presets.
- Confirm consistent sensor wear time and time in Automated Mode.

### 4. Review Pump Settings and Emergency Plans

- Record the basal rates, carb ratio, insulin sensitivity, correction range, and glucose safety limit in the electronic health record.
- Establish an emergency plan with backup insulin pens in case of pump or system failure.
