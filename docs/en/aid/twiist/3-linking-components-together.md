In this instructional video, we will guide you through the process of linking the components of the twiist AID system:

- Linking the Libre 3 Plus sensor to the twiist app.
- Linking the twiist pump to the twiist app.
- Connecting your account to Tidepool for data sharing.
- Using your Apple Watch to control the system.
- And using the insiight app for real-time follow-up by caregivers.

## 3.1 Linking Libre 3 Plus sensor to twiist App

Be aware that the twiist AID system cannot use a sensor that is already in session at start up. You must start a new sensor.

On your twiist app, tap Add CGM in the CGM status or tap Settings and Add CGM, and choose Start New Sensor.

Choose a sensor site on the back of the upper arm, at least 2.5 cm or 1 inch from an insulin injection site. Start by washing your hands, wash the application site with plain soap and water, wipe dry with a clean towel, clean with an alcohol wipe, unscrew the cap of the inserter, place the loaded inserter over the prepared site, and push down firmly to apply the FreeStyle Libre 3 Plus sensor on your body.

Tap next, and then Scan new sensor. Hold the top of your iPhone very close to the sensor.

## 3.2 Linking the twiist Pump to the twiist App

A step by step process on how to link the pump base to the twiist app is discussed in the module about the twiist pump. Be aware that you need wifi or cellular data to do this.

## 3.3 Connecting to Tidepool

To connect your app to Tidepool, go to the settings and select Data Sharing. Here, you can login with your Tidepool account credentials.

Once linked, your glucose, insulin delivery, and carb data are automatically uploaded to Tidepool when internet is available.

Be aware that Tidepool is HIPAA-compliant and claims GDPR compliance, but it is not officially FDA-cleared. Before using it in a clinical setting, approval from your data protection manager is recommended.

## 3.4 Using your Apple Watch to control the system

1. Pair your Apple Watch with your iPhone.
2. On your iPhone, open the Watch app and install the twiist companion app.
3. Once installed, you'll be able to view glucose, insulin status, and recent trends.
4. From the watch, you can enter carbs, confirm bolus recommendations and activate Pre-Meal or Workout ranges.

Remember that the Apple Watch relies on your iPhone for communication with the pump. If your iPhone is out of Bluetooth range, actions requested from the watch will not take effect until the iPhone reconnects.

## 3.5 Using the insiight App

With the twiist insiight app you can follow multiple twiist users if the user grants you permission.

The insiight app also offers a widget that you can add to your iPhone homepage, making it possible to see your glucose value simply by swiping your lock screen to the right.
