Welcome to this module on the twiist Automated Insulin Delivery (or AID) System, the first FDA-cleared version of one of the earlier versions of DIY Loop.

In this module, we will explore how the twiist AID system works step by step, and how it helps simplify and improve daily diabetes management.

The twiist AID system combines three essential components:

- The twiist pump -- a small, tubed insulin pump that holds 300 units of insulin.
- A compatible glucose sensor -- currently the FreeStyle Libre 3 Plus sensor, which streams glucose readings every minute.
- And the twiist Loop algorithm - hosted on the twiist pump and controlled via the twiist app on an iPhone and/or a compatible Apple Watch.

Reports and data sharing is possible through Tidepool, allowing you and your healthcare team to review patterns, make informed decisions, and personalize your therapy. The twiist insiight app allows family and caregivers to follow the user in real-time.

The twiist AID system is FDA-cleared and backed by Sequel Med Tech. It includes robust safety features such as occlusion detection, glucose safety limits, and customizable alerts. Unlike open-source systems, updates and customer support are provided directly by the manufacturer.

Before we begin, please note that this module is for educational purposes only and is not a substitute for medical advice. Always consult with your healthcare provider before making any changes to your diabetes treatment.

The videos were produced by Dr Inge Van Boxelaer, the founder of Diabetotech and an endocrinologist at the AZ St-Lucas hospital in Ghent in Belgium. The content was reviewed by Gary Scheiner, a Certified Diabetes Care and Education Specialist from Integrated Diabetes Services in the US.

By the end of this module, you will have a clear understanding of how the twiist AID system works, how to set it up, how to navigate the app, and how to handle real-life situations such as exercise, meals, sick days, and travel.
