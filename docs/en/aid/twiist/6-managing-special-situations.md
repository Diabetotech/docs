In this video, we will provide practical tips for handling special situations when using the twiist AID system.

We will review strategies for managing hypoglycemia, hyperglycemia, high-fat meals, exercise, illness, alcohol, and travel.

## 6.1 Hypoglycemia

With an AID system, fewer carbohydrates are needed to correct a low compared to traditional pump therapy. This is because the system suspends or reduces insulin earlier when it predicts an approaching low.

You can check the Insulin on Board (IOB) in the twiist app before treating a low. If you have little insulin active, smaller carb amounts are usually sufficient.

When eating carbs to treat hypoglycemia, it is recommended to add the carbs in the twiist app, so the algorithm can take these carbs into account.

## 6.2 Hyperglycemia

For mild hyperglycemia, it is often best to let the twiist system handle the correction automatically.

- If needed, you can give a correction bolus from the app via the triangle icon.
- Using the Pre-Meal target or a lower correction range can also help bring glucose down faster.
- If you experience prolonged or severe hyperglycemia, always check for pump or infusion site issues. "If in doubt, change it out."

## 6.3 High-Fat Meals

Meals high in fat and protein (such as pizza or creamy pasta) can cause delayed glucose rises.

- When Loop is on, you can use a longer absorption time in the bolus calculator to spread insulin delivery more evenly.
- When Loop is off, you can use an extended or dual wave bolus.
- Some people enter "extra carbs" to cover for fat and protein, especially if they are on a low-carb diet.

## 6.4 Exercise

Aerobic physical activity lowers glucose levels more quickly and increases insulin sensitivity.

- Start the Workout Preset 1-2 hours before and during exercise.
- Reduce the meal bolus with 25-30% if a meal is taken within 3 hours of exercise.
- Take a small snack if your glucose drops below 126 mg/dl or 7 mmol/l.

## 6.5 Illness

Illness often causes higher glucose levels due to stress hormones.

- Keep twiist in Auto Mode or Loop On unless advised otherwise by your healthcare provider.
- Consider using frequent correction boluses or a lower correction range if the illness leads to hyperglycemia.
- Check frequently for ketones if glucose remains elevated despite corrections.
- Always hydrate well.

## 6.6 Alcohol

When consuming alcohol, consider taking proactive steps to prevent hypoglycemia, such as activating the Workout Preset. Increasing carbohydrate intake is not an effective approach.

## 6.7 Travel

When traveling, always bring extra supplies of sensors, pods, chargers, and backup insulin pens.

Flying with twiist:

- Bluetooth communication between your iPhone, Libre 3 Plus, and twiist pump can remain active during flights.
- If airplane mode is required, keep Bluetooth on so the pump and CGM remain connected.
- Internet access is not required for twiist to function, but Tidepool uploads will pause until you are back online.

Time zone changes: The twiist pump time and date automatically synchronize with your iPhone through the twiist app.
