## **<u>2. Tandem Control-IQ technology according to the CARES Paradigm</u>**

Welcome to this lesson about the Tandem Control-IQ technology according to the CARES paradigm.

Tandem Control-IQ technology offers a range of features that make it an effective automated insulin delivery system for diabetes management. Let's explore some key points to further understand this system within the CARES paradigm, its indications and its software updates.

### ***2.1 Tandem Control-IQ according to the CARES paradigm***

#### ***\#1 Calculate***

Tandem Control-IQ technology modulates preset basal insulin rates based on the predicted glucose levels over the next 30 minutes.

Additionally, hourly autocorrection boluses are administered at 60% of the standard dose when glucose levels exceed 180 mg/dL or 10 mmol/L.

The target range, or treatment values as they are called by Tandem, are set between 112.5-160 mg/dL (6.25 and 8.8 mmol/L), with the goal of achieving optimal glycemic control during the day.

During sleep mode, the treatment values are further refined to 112.5-120 mg/dL (6.3-6.7 mmol/L), promoting stable glucose levels without autocorrected boluses.

#### ***\#2 Adjust*** 

Users can modify the following settings:

- the basal insulin rate (different profiles are possible),

- the carbohydrate ratio,

- and the correction factor or insulin sensitivity.

With the updated Control-IQ+ software, users can even set temporary basal rates from 0% to 250% for up to 72 hours while continuing to benefit from automatic insulin adjustments and correction boluses from the Control-IQ+ algorithm.

In addition to these adjustments, Tandem Control-IQ technology offers different glucose targets or treatment values for sleep and exercise.

- Sleep Activity: This setting aims for narrower treatment values of 112.5-120 mg/dL (6.25-6.7 mmol/L) during nighttime to maintain stable glucose levels without using autocorrected boluses.

- Exercise Activity: This setting is designed for periods of increased physical activity. It sets higher treatment values of 140-160 mg/dL (7.8-8.9 mmol/L) to accommodate the metabolic demands of exercise. Additionally, during exercise activity, the pump does use autocorrected boluses to help manage glucose levels effectively.

During the initial setup of Control-IQ technology, users will input their total daily insulin dose and weight. These values can be modified later to ensure accurate insulin delivery based on individual requirements.

While Tandem Control-IQ technology provides flexibility in adjusting various parameters, it's important to note that certain aspects remain fixed. The default treatment values of 112.5-160 mg/dL (6.25-8.9 mmol/L), the duration of insulin action set at 5 hours, and the target value for correction boluses fixed at 110 mg/dL (or 6.1 mmol/L).

#### ***\#3 Revert***

In the event that no sensor data is available for more than 20 minutes, the Tandem Control-IQ technology “auto mode” will transition to “manual mode”. This can occur due to issues such as a loss of connection with the transmitter or during sensor warm-up.

If there are connectivity issues between the pump and CGM, recommend wearing the pump on the same side as the CGM and positioning the pump with the screen facing outwards. Keeping the software up to date can also help improve connectivity.

I<span class="mark">f you’re using the Dexcom G6 sensor and pers</span>istent connectivity problems arise, consider replacing the Dexcom transmitter. A functional transmitter battery is crucial for maintaining a stable connection.

While in manual mode:

- The suspend-before-low function is not available, as it is specific to the Basal-IQ algorithm and not compatible with Control-IQ software.

- If Control-IQ technology spontaneously reverts to manual mode, the basal insulin rate of your person<span class="mark">al</span> profile will be limited to 3 units per hour. To increase the insulin dose to more than 3 units per hour in manual mode, you will need to deactivate the Control-IQ technology. This 3 units/hour limit does not apply to the updated Control-IQ+, which was specifically designed for users who require higher basal insulin rates.

Once sensor values become available again, the pump will automatically switch back to Control-IQ technology, ensuring the continuous operation of the automated insulin delivery system.

#### ***\#4 Educate***

When starting with an automated insulin delivery system like Tandem Control-IQ technology, it's important to be aware of both general education points and specific education points for this system. <span class="mark">The general education points covered in the Master Module on AID Systems include:</span>

- Use fewer carbohydrates to correct hypoglycemia.

- Consider infusion set problems if blood glucose levels remain high for an extended period without a clear reason.

- Trust the AID system and avoiding unnecessary manual interventions.

- Timing meal boluses 15 minutes before meals.

- Utilizing Exercis<span class="mark">e Mode 1-2 hours minu</span>tes before activity

- Stopping insulin delivery if the pump is disconnected for more than 15 minutes.

- <span class="mark">Responding to alarms and being mindful of automatic updates on mobile phones</span>

- Seeking peer support for guidance and sharing experiences.

Now let's look at the specific education points for Tandem Control-IQ technology:

- Using Sleep Activity every night can lead to better glycemic control. When planning to sleep for more than 5 hours, utilizing Sleep Activity is advisable. For short afternoon naps, Sleep Activity is not necessary.

- It's best to activate Sleep Activity close to your actual sleep time and ideally not within 2 hours of your last meal. This ensures you receive autocorrect boluses if needed after an evening meal.

- If you skip breakfast, it's acceptable to keep Sleep Activity on for a while, but it's not intended for use throughout the entire day. If you do have breakfast, it's recommended to disable Sleep Activity to allow for autocorrect boluses if necessary.

- After a meal bolus, the pump may prompt you to adjust the bolus based on sensor glucose levels. Read these instructions carefully and consider whether it's appropriate for your situation.

- While it's possible to override the bolus calculator, it's generally not recommended.

- Utilize different basal profiles to optimize insulin release during activities like sports or illness.

- In Glooko reports, the total daily insulin dose is divided into a percentage of basal and bolus insulin, and all manual and autocorrection boluses are added to the bolus percentage. In general, it’s recommended to aim for a balanced percentage of basal and bolus insulin, for example 40-60% basal insulin.

By following these education points, you can maximize the benefits of Tandem Control-IQ technology and achieve better diabetes management. It is normal to experience some trial and error in finding the right settings when you begin using this pump. Have confidence and consult with your diabetes nurse specialist for any necessary adjustments.

#### ***\#5 Sensor***

The Tandem with Control-IQ technology can be used with the Dexcom G6 or Dexcom G7 sensor. Depending on your location, there is also a possibility to use the FreeStyle Libre 2 Plus or 3 Plus sensor. You can easily switch between sensors.

<span class="mark">If you have doubts about the accuracy of your sensor reading or do not feel it reflects your condition, perform a finger prick test to confirm.</span>

<span class="mark">If a significant discrepancy, such as a more than 20% difference, exists and persists between the sensor and finger prick readings, even when the blood glucose is stable and within the target range, it is advisable to revert back to Manual Mode and or replace the sensor.</span>

<span class="mark">If you use a Dexcom sensor, you can first try to calibrate the sensor to improve the accuracy of your readings. It's important to note that if you calibrate the Dexcom sensor, it will need one calibration each day until a new sensor is applied.</span>

#### ***<span class="mark">\#6 Share</span>***

<span class="mark">With automated insulin delivery systems, it is possible to share insulin and glucose data with healthcare providers and family members.</span>

<span class="mark">In the case of Tandem Control-IQ technology, healthcare providers can check pump reports via Glooko or Tandem Source.</span>

People who use the Tandem t:slim mobile app can be followed remotely as the pump data from the Tandem t:slim mobile app is uploaded to the Tandem Cloud approximately once per hour.

<span class="mark">Family members can remotely monitor glucose levels through the Dexcom Follow app, provided that the user uses a Dexcom sensor and also has the Dexcom G6 or G7 app installed.</span>

<span class="mark">While real-time monitoring may not be available with Tandem Control-IQ technology, the ability to share data still facilitates effective communication and collaboration between users, healthcare providers, and family members, leading to better diabetes management outcomes.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">2.2 Indications</span>***

<span class="mark">Tandem Control has both a CE-label and FDA-label for use in individuals with type 1 dia</span>betes who are at least 6 years old. The FDA-label also allows use in children from 2 years old and in adults aged 18 years and older with insulin-dependent type 2 diabetes.

It is suitable for individuals with a weight of 25 to 140 kg (or 55 to 308 pounds) and a total daily insulin dose of 10 to 100 units. The updated Control-IQ+ algorithm is approved for individuals with a weight of 9 to 200 kg (or 20 to 441 pounds) and a total daily insulin dose of 5 to 200 units.

The system is approved for use with Novorapid, Novolog, Humalog or Lyumjev 100 U/ml insulins.

Please note that Tandem Control is not approved for use in pregnancy or in individuals requiring dialysis.

### ***2.3 Software updates***

The Tandem t slim X2 insulin pump is capable of receiving updates. As a user, you will receive notifications if an update is required. You can check the software version directly on your pump by navigating to "Options," then "My Pump," and selecting "Pump Info." Ensure that your t:slim pump has the latest software version. It should be something around version 7.8 to 7.10, de<span class="mark">pending on your location. If your software version is lower, it’s advisable to update the software on your pump if this is possible.</span>

<span class="mark">To perform an update, you need to download the Tandem Device Updater on your computer and have an update-ID. This update-ID will be emailed to you when an update is required. The update process usually takes around 10 minutes. It is important to disconnect the infusion set from your body and suspend all insulin delivery prior to using the Tandem Device Updater. Once the update is complete, your pump will show the Update Successful message.</span>
