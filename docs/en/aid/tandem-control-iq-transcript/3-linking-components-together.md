## **<u>3. Linking components together</u>**

Welcome to this instructional video, where we will explore the process of linking the various components of Tandem Control-IQ technology. Let's dive in!

Tandem Control-IQ technology operates using the following steps:

- Every 5 minutes, the Dexcom G6, G7 or FreeStyle Libre 2 or 3 Plus sensor measures your glycemia or blood glucose levels.

- The sensor then transmits this glycaemia data to the Tandem t:slim X2 insulin pump. The algorithm on the pump calculates the appropriate insulin dose and automatically adjusts the insulin delivery accordingly.

- The pump data can be sent to the Tandem t:slim mobile m<span class="mark">obile app, that will automatically synchronize the data every hour to Tandem Source.</span>

- <span class="mark">To access the pump data, the pump can also be manually uploaded to Tandem Source or Glooko, using an USB cable.</span>

- <span class="mark">Additionally, if you’re using a Dexcom sensor, you can install the Dexcom G6 or G7 app on your mobile phone, and send your data to Dexcom Clarity and Dexcom Share. This allows family members and caregivers to monitor your glycaemia using the Dexcom Follow app.</span>

<span class="mark">In this video, we will guide you through the process of establishing these crucial links. Specifically, we will cover:</span>

1.  <span class="mark">How to link a Dexcom or FreeStyle Libre sensor to the Tandem t:slim X2 insulin pump</span>

2.  <span class="mark">How to Link th</span>e Tandem t:slim mobile m<span class="mark">obile app to the Tandem t:slim X2 pump.</span>

3.  <span class="mark">How to create a Tandem Source account.</span>

4.  <span class="mark">How to make a Glooko account and upload the sensor and pump data.</span>

5.  <span class="mark">and how to use the Dexcom Follow app in combination with Tandem Control-IQ technology.</span>

So, let's get started and learn how to establish these important connections within the Tandem Control-IQ technology AID system.

### ***3.1 Linki<span class="mark">ng a Dexcom or FreeStyle Libre sensor with the Tandem t:slim X2 Insulin Pump</span>***

<span class="mark">With the Tandem t:slim X2 Insulin Pump, linking a sensor to the p</span>ump is a straightforward process. Here's how you can do it:

1\. Navigate to the options menu on the pump and select "my CGM."

<span class="mark">2. Choose the CGM type you want to use.</span>

<span class="mark">3. If you choose Dexcom G6, you’ll first have to link the transmitter. Choose "Enter Transmitter ID" and follow the prompts. The pump will ask you to confirm the transmitter ID twice.</span>

This linking process is usually seamless and hassle-free. However, ensure that the old transmitter is not near the new transmitter; otherwise, Bluetooth may still detect the old transmitter. Keep them at least 6 meters (20 feet) apart to avoid any interference.

Once you have successfully linked the transmitter to the pump, you can proceed to start the sensor. Here's how:

1.  From the options menu, select "my CGM" again.

2.  Choose "Start Sensor" and follow the instructions.

3.  During this step, you will be prompted to enter the sensor code, which can be found on the protective foil of the sensor.

If you happen to misplace the sensor code, you can select "skip." In this case, the pump will ask you to calibrate the sensor after 2 hours, allowing you to still use the sensor. However, the pump will ask for a calibration every 24 hours so it's advisable not to lose your sensor code to avoid these extra steps.

If you have already started the sensor with the Dexcom G6 app, the pump will pick up the sensordata without asking for a sensorcode.

<span class="mark">If you are using Dexcom G7 sensors, you can select Dexcom G7 from the CGM options. You will see an option to start the G7 sensor on the pump, and will be prompted to enter the Dexcom G7 pairing code twice. This pairing code can be found on the Dexcom G7 inserter.</span>

<span class="mark">If you’re using Fr</span>eeStyle Libre 2 or 3 Plus sensors, you’ll need to start your sensor using the paired Tandem t:slim mobile app rather than your pump. To begin, tap Settings in the app's navigation bar and select CGM and then FreeStyle Libre 2 or 3 Plus. For iOS users, tap Start Scanning; Android users should ensure NFC is enabled in their phone’s settings. Then, hold the back of your Android phone or the top of your iPhone near the sensor until it vibrates or you hear an alert. The Tandem t:slim mobile app will display a Sensor Session Started screen. Tap Ok to proceed.

Once a sensor is started, you can follow the warmup period on the Tandem t:slim mobile app and on the pump.

By following these steps, you can successfully link a Dexcom G6, G7 or Free<span class="mark">Style Libre 2 Plus sensor with the Tandem t:slim X2 Insulin Pump.</span>

### ***3.2 How** to Link the* Tandem t:slim mobile *app to the Tandem t:slim X2 pump.*

The Tandem t:slim mobile a<span class="mark">pp serves as a companion to the t:slim X2 insulin pump. Please note that the app's availability and features may vary depending on your location. For instance, as of the latest update to this course, the ability to deliver an insulin bolus from your smartphone is only available in the United States.</span>

<span class="mark">Ensure that your smartphone is compatible wi</span>th the Tandem t:slim mobile app. You can check this on the Tandem website. Using an unsupported phone may limit some features of the app.

<span class="mark">After downloading the</span> Tandem t:slim mobile app from your device’s Play Store or App Store, locate the app on your smartphone and open it. The app will request various permissions and prompt you to log in or register for a Tandem Source account. If you are 13 years or older, you can create a personal account or a <span class="mark">caretaker account.</span>

<span class="mark">On your pump’s Home screen, tap OPTIONS, navigate to Device Settings, tap Bluetooth Settings, and toggle Mobile Connection on. The Pair Device option will appear—tap it, and a blue checkmark will generate a device pairing code.</span>

<span class="mark">On your smartphone, tap begin to start the pairing with the pump, choose the appropriate serial number of the pump, and enter the device pairing code in the app.</span>

<span class="mark">After you tap Pair with Pump on the app, you can tap OK to confirm on your pump.</span>

<span class="mark">Now you can tap Sync Pump Data in th</span>e Tandem t:slim mobile a<span class="mark">pp, and the app will display your Dashboard and start showing pump data.</span>

<span class="mark">Keep in mind the following recommendations:</span>

- <span class="mark">To ensure uninterrupted communication between your pump and the app, it's recommended to turn off battery optimization settings.</span>

- <span class="mark">T</span>he t:connect Tandem t:slim mobile ap<span class="mark">p must run in the background to transmit and receive data between your pump and the Tandem cloud.</span>

- <span class="mark">If you wish to use the phone-bolus feature, you must enable security features on your smartphone, such as a screen lock, passcode, or face recognition.</span>

- <span class="mark">Be sure to disable automating updates of the operating software of your phone. Before manually updating your phone's operating system, ensure that th</span>e Tandem t:slim mobile app is compatible with the new operating system <span class="mark">version.</span>

- <span class="mark">When updates for t</span>he Tandem t:slim mobile <span class="mark">app become available on Google Play or the App Store, do not uninstall the app, but simply update the app.</span>

- After pairing and syncing your pump with the Tandem t:slim mobile a<span class="mark">pp, you can expect about 15 megabytes of data usage every three days. Data usage may increase with poor cellular reception. To avoid using cellular data for uploads to your Tandem Source account, adjust the app settings to upload only on Wi-Fi by going to Settings \> App \> Data Control.</span>

### ***3.3 How to create a Tandem Source account.***

<span class="mark">The pump and sensor data can be shared with a parent, guardian or healthcare provider via the Tandem Source platform.</span>

- <span class="mark">Users and parents or caregivers can create a Tandem Source account via [<u>source.tandemdiabetes.com/login</u>](http://source.tandemdiabetes.com/login).</span>

- <span class="mark">Users of the</span> Tandem t:slim mobile app <span class="mark">create a Tandem Source account when they first sign into the app.</span>

- <span class="mark">Healthcare providers can ask for a professional Tandem Source account via their Tandem representative.</span>

- <span class="mark">Admin users of a clinic account can add other team members.</span>

<span class="mark">When you log in your Tandem Source account, you can find several menus on the left side: Home, Reports, Orders, My Pump and Upload Pump. In the top right corner you can access your Tandem account information and the help menu.</span>

<span class="mark">You can upload your pump data manually by connecting it with a USB cable to your computer, or you can use th</span>e Tandem t:slim mobile a<span class="mark">pp that automatically synchronizes all pump data to the Tandem Cloud approximately once per hour.</span>

<span class="mark">Tandem Source offers 3 standard reports: the Overview Report, the Daily timeline and Pump settings. Via the account settings you can share reports with your healthcare provider.</span>

### ***3.4 How to create a Glooko account and upload the sensor and pump data***

The pump and sensor data can be shared with the healthcare provider via Glooko.

We will describe how to do this as a user via Glooko Personal, and as a healthcare professional via Glooko for Professional Use,

#### ***Glooko Personal:***

1\. As a user, you can visit my.glooko.com and choose "Create Account."

2\. <span class="mark">At a certain moment you will b</span>e able to enter a "proconnect code" that connects your account to your hospital.

3\. Create a password for your account and keep it accessible for future logins, especially when you need to upload data from your pump.

When logging into Glooko, you will be prompted to indicate the devices you use. For Control-IQ technology, this will include the Tandem t:slim X2 pump.

Then you will need to download the Glooko Uploader software, which is available via the settings in the top right corner.

Once installed, you'll find a Glooko uploader icon on your desktop. If you open the Glooko uploader software and connect your Tandem pump to your computer using the provided USB cable, the pump data will be downloaded to your computer within seconds. When you open your Glooko account, you'll find the downloaded data available for viewing.

#### ***Glooko for Healthcare Providers:***

1\. Healthcare providers or institutions can purchase a professional Glooko account directly from Glooko. You can then log in via my.glooko.com using the username and password provided by your hospital.

2\. Upon logging in, you will find an overview of the latest data uploads and a separate tab displaying an overview of your patients. On the top right corner you will find the "proconnect code" of your hospital.

3\. You can customize the patient list by sorting it according to your preferences or using flags to identify patients with high or low glycemic levels easily. You can add a new patient via the button in the top right corner and you can add their email address to invite them to Glooko.

To upload pump and sensor data, healthcare professionals can either acquire the Glooko uploader software, which requires one license per computer, or opt for the Glooko transmitter device.

Many clinics prefer the Glooko transmitter due to its rapid device uploading capability and the elimination of frequent updates. The Glooko transmitter is compatible with most diabetes devices. Despite only being plugged into an electrical socket, it swiftly transfers data to the Glooko cloud platform in mere seconds.

You will find the uploaded data in the assign devices tab. Here you can assign the data to the appropriate patient. If the device has been previously assigned to a specific patient, their name will be suggested.

By following these steps, you can manually read the Tandem t:slim X2 insulin pump data to Glooko, whether for personal use or as a <span class="mark">healthcare provider, and conveniently access and manage the data. Additionally, healthcare pro</span>viders can access patient reports without a Glooko Pro account if the patient shares their login credentials.

<span class="mark">It's important to note that some users have reported instances where the Tandem Pump loses connection more easily when using the Dexcom G6 or G7 app. If you experience this issue, it's recommended to discontinue using the Dexcom G6 or G7 app while using the dedicated Dexcom app.</span>
