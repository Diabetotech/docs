**<u>4. Reservoir and infusion set replacement</u>**

Welcome, in this video we offer comprehensive guidance on how to change the infusion set and reservoir of your mylife YpsoPump.

Our video commences with an overview of various infusion sets, followed by a systematic, four-step procedure to replace both the insulin and the infusion set. Lastly, we address some essential do's and don'ts.

***4.1 Different infusion sets***

Our demonstration centers around the commonly used mylife Orbit Soft infusion set. It's worth noting that you can also utilize the mylife Orbit Micro infusion set with a steel cannula with the mylife YpsoPump.

- Both infusion sets feature a 90-degree angle cannula, but there are no options for angled cannulas.

- These infusion sets can be manually inserted, or you can use the mylife Orbit inserter for assistance.

- A distinctive aspect of these infusion sets is their 360-degree rotatability, which allows for easy connection and disconnection without needing to look at the infusion site.

- The infusion sets have a unique skin-colored patch that makes them less noticeable, but they might not stick as firmly, causing some users to require additional plaster.

- Finally, the tubing of the mylife Orbit infusion sets are colored blue for easier detection of any air bubbles.

Below this video, we provide an overview of the different available infusion sets, including their tube and cannula lengths and filling volumes.

***4.2 Supplies***

To change the infusion set and insulin, you will need: your room-temperature insulin Pumpcart, your infusion set, a mylife Orbit inserter, an alcohol swab, and your mylife YpsoPump.

***4.3 Reservoir replacement***

Start by disconnecting the infusion set from your body. To do so, gently push the two marked sides of the infusion set, which will detach the tubing cap from the cannula base.

<span class="mark">To replace the insulin cartridge, navigate to the insulin cartridge icon from the main menu. Here, you can retract the drive rod by pressing the double back arrow and confirming. After the drive rod has been retracted to 0%, confirm this and the pump will initiate a self-test.</span>

<span class="mark">Now, rotate the adapter a quarter turn counterclockwise to disconnect it and remove the insulin reservoir, adapter, and tube. Insert a new insulin Pumpcart into the cartridge holder (with the opening upwards) and position the adapter on top. The adapter should be positioned at a 90-degree angle to the pump and rotated a quarter turn towards the pump until it clicks into place.</span>

<span class="mark">If prefilled PumpCarts are not available, you need to fill a mylife Reservoir manually. Disinfect the insulin vial with an alcohol wipe. Take the mylife Reservoir and adapter from the package and attach the adapter to the Reservoir. Move the plunger up and down, then pull it back to fill the Reservoir with air. Attach the adapter to the insulin vial, inject the air into the vial, turn the vial and Reservoir upside down, and draw insulin into the Reservoir. If you see any air bubbles, tap the reservoir, and inject the air back into the insulin vial. Remove the adapter and unscrew the plunger from the Reservoir. You now have a filled mylife Reservoir ready for use in the mylife YpsoPump.</span>

***4.4 Filling the tubing***

Next, fill the tube, a process which is quicker with the mylife YpsoPump than other insulin pumps. From the main menu, navigate to the icon with the needle and insulin droplets (representing infusion set pre-fill) and select tube filling. Set and confirm the filling volume. While the filling is in progress, hold the insulin pump upright and gently tap it against your palm to dislodge any air bubbles in the cartridge. Repeat this process until insulin exits the cannula and no air bubbles are visible in the tube. If you've filled the insulin reservoir yourself, check for any remaining air bubbles in the reservoir using the transparent cartridge holder.

***4.5 Infusion set placement***

You are now ready to insert the infusion set.

First, pick your infusion site, clean your hands, disinfect the site with an alcohol wipe and let it dry. You can manually insert the infusion set at a 90° angle or use the mylife Orbit inserter.

We will demonstrate the placement of a mylife Orbit Soft infusion set with the inserter:

- Insert the infusion set into the inserter until it clicks into place.

- Peel off the protective film from the plaster without touching the adhesive layer.

- Gently rotate the protective cap to remove it from the cannula.

- Turn the lower part of the inserter clockwise until it locks into place. Caution: the inserter is now activated. Avoid pointing it towards your eyes to prevent accidental injuries.

- Position the inserter on the skin and press the button until it clicks.

- Smooth the patch onto the skin, rubbing it gently for a few seconds to enhance adhesion.

- While holding the plaster against your skin, use two fingers of your other hand to grasp the infusion set and remove the Insertion Needle or Insertion Cap by pressing the outer wings of the Insertion Cap and pulling it away.

- Recap the removed Insertion Needle with the blue protective cap and dispose of it in a needle container.

- Return the inserter to its original position.

- Click the tubing cap directly onto the infusion set's cannula base. Turn the tube left and right, pulling up the cap to ensure it's securely engaged.

***4.6 Filling the cannula***

The final step is to fill the subcutaneous cannula. Navigate to the pre-fill infusion set icon from the main menu and select fill cannula. Here, set and administer the filling volume.

***4.7 Do's & don’ts***

- The <span class="mark">mylife Ypsopump is compatible with all short-acting insulins, but PumpCarts are only available for Novorapid and Fiasp. If you prefer Humalog, Lyumjev or Apidra, or you don't have access to PumpCarts, you can use mylife YpsoPump reservoirs. Always use short-acting insulin with a normal concentration of 100 E/ml.</span>

- <span class="mark">Mylife Reservoirs can be refilled once within 7 days of the initial fill. Do not store a refilled reservoir. You can refill it using either a syringe and needle or the adapter. If using a syringe and needle, draw insulin from the vial and transfer it into the empty reservoir, making sure to remove any air bubbles. If using the adapter, you can first fill a new Reservoir from the package and store it in the refrigerator. Next, connect your used reservoir to the adapter, draw insulin into it, and reuse it once.</span>

- <span class="mark">Always verify your glucose level 2 hours after inserting a new in</span>fusion set. This is the only way to confirm proper infusion set insertion and insulin delivery.

- With the mylife Ypsopump, you can change the reservoir, tube and infusion set separately. So, you don't have to swap out your tube and infusion set each time you put in a new insulin reservoir. The infusion sets are also available separately from the tube.

- The infusion sets of the mylife Ypsopump are connected and disconnected differently than other pump infusion sets. To disconnect the mylife Orbit infusion set, gently push the side ridges to release it. To reconnect, click the tubing cap onto the cannula base. Always make sure the tubing cap is securely in place by rotating the tube and pulling the cap upwards.

By now, you should feel confident in replacing both the infusion set and the insulin in your mylife YpsoPump. Thanks to pre-filled insulin vials, the process is relatively smooth. It may take a bit of practice to get used to the infusion sets and the inserter, but it's not more difficult than with other pumps. If you have any doubts, you can always refer back to this tutorial. Best of luck!
