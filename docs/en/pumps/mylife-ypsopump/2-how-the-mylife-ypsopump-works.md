***2.1 Parts & buttons on the pump***

The front panel features a black and white touchscreen. The top right corner houses the battery, secured by a battery cap, while the bottom left corner is the home of the prefilled insulin PumpCart and adapter. This adapter is connected to the tubing. The cap of the tubing can be clicked on the cannula base. The sole button on the pump is the Function button, designed for multiple purposes.

***2.2 Battery replacement***

The mylife Ypsopump operates on a standard AAA alkaline battery. When replacing, ensure the negative side goes in first. The battery usually lasts for about a month and should be replaced accordingly. Once in place, place the battery cap on top.

It is advisable to replace the battery cap every three months to maintain the pump's waterproof attribute. You can secure the battery cap with a penny ensuring it sits flush with the pump.

Upon battery replacement, the pump will display a screen featuring the serial number and software version. Accept it by pressing the tick mark, which will then initiate the pump's self-test. Set the time and date next, and you will be directed to the status screen.

***2.3 Unlocking the pump***

By default, the pump is set to the key lock. Pressing the function button will activate the pump. To access the main menu, swipe left on the status screen, and enter 1-2-3.

***2.4 Home screen & pump menu***

The home screen displays the next items.

- On top, you can find the time, date, lock status, and battery level.

- In the center, the status icon represents the current state of insulin activity. This may appear as dashes with a square, indicating halted basal insulin. Alternatively, it could be dashes accompanied by the letter A or B, indicating the active basal profile. If a temporary basal is in effect, the icon will show a percentage sign. And lastly, if a normal, square, or dual bolus is being delivered, the icon will display the corresponding bolus symbol.

- It also shows the rate of basal insulin delivery or the remaining bolus insulin to be delivered.

Swipe left on the home screen and enter 1-2-3 to access the main menu, which comprises seven options: Bolus, Basal rates, Cartridge change, Prime infusion set, Data, Stop/Run mode, and Settings. Many of these options have submenus. A complete menu map and icon overview is available for download below this video.

From the home screen, swiping right will lead you to the Last Bolus view, which displays all information related to the last fully delivered bolus.

Menu navigation is easy – just tap on the icons. A cross indicates cancel, a tick confirms, a forward arrow with a lock symbolizes forward to unlock, an arrow denotes forward, an up arrow represents going up a menu level, and a back arrow means going back one step. Occasionally, an icon may be grayed out, indicating its unavailability.

The function button can guide you back to the status screen at any point. It can also switch the screen on/off and deliver a blind insulin bolus if configured. The function button can be operated in two ways. A short press will execute most functions. For others, like activating the blind bolus, press the function key for over 2 seconds until the pump vibrates.

***2.5 Do's & don’ts***

- Keep the menu overview from the downloads section easily accessible. It will help healthcare providers guide patients remotely.

- The pump is waterproof, but only for an hour until <span class="mark">a depth of 1</span> meter (or 3.3 feet).

- To simplify function button operations, consider rotating the pump's screen 180 degrees. You can do this by going to the settings in the main menu. The icon with the three squares and arrows symbolizes screen rotation. Confirm this to rotate the screen 180 degrees.

- Consider using the pump clip or other carrying material as needed. Also consider using a cover as the pump body can get scratched easily. A list of carrying and protective systems from Ypsomed is available in the downloads. Several other companies also provide carrying and protecting solutions.

- The mylife YpsoPump does not automatically adjust the time and date. So when changing between winter and summer time, or when traveling to a different time zone, you have to adjust the time yourself. You can do that via the settings.
