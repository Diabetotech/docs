**<u>5. Pump alarms</u>**

Welcome to this informative session about potential alarms on the mylife YpsoPump.

We'll delve into the different alarms and warnings that can appear on the mylife YpsoPump and offer some supplementary advice.

***5.1 Alarms***

Alarms are the highest priority alerts. They're signified by an exclamation mark inside a triangle icon, and always result in a cessation of insulin delivery.

Alarms given are primarily tactile and audible and there are 4 escalation levels. The levels escalate at intervals of 5 minutes. If an alarm is confirmed but not dealt with, it is given again by audible and tactile means after 30 minutes.

Alarms are arranged based on their priority, namely no battery, empty battery, battery not suitable, charging internal rechargeable battery, occlusion, no insulin, cartridge empty, automatic stop, or electronic error. What to do in these situations can be found in the manual, accessible in the downloads at the end of this video.

You can access all warnings in the alarm history. To do this, from the main menu, select the data icon depicted by a sheet of paper, and then the alert history icon represented by an exclamation mark within a triangle on a piece of paper.

***5.2 Warnings***

Warnings are lower priority alerts than alarms. They're symbolized by an 'i' icon but do not result in the cancellation of insulin delivery.

Possible warnings include cartridge low, battery low, drive rod not fully retracted, priming not completed, bolus canceled, temporary basal function completed or canceled, insulin pump stopped, and Bluetooth connection failure. Steps to be taken in these scenarios can be found in the manual, accessible in the downloads at the end of this video.

Like alarms, warnings can also be felt and heard in four escalating levels of intensity.

These warnings aren't displayed in the alarm history but need to be acknowledged on the status screen.

***5.3 Do's & don’ts***

- If you're uncertain about what to do, always refer to the user manual or contact customer service. The manual not only covers warnings and notifications, but it also outlines other incidents. It details what actions to take if certain icons or functions are unavailable, or if there is insulin, dirt, or water in the pump. In such situations, always consult the manual.

- A specific warning to note is the internal battery charging. The mylife YpsoPump features an internal rechargeable battery that's powered by the inserted alkaline battery. If the internal battery is heavily charged, it may deplete. In this case, the "charge internal battery" warning displays. The internal battery charges through the inserted AAA battery and is signified by arrows circling an hourglass. Charging could take up to 20 minutes. Active boluses and temporary basal will be halted during this period.

- Another peculiar warning is the electronic error. In this situation, you'll immediately receive an audible and palpable signal, and all pump functions will be canceled. This is, thankfully, a rare occurrence. You'll be presented with two alternating screens instructing how to restart the pump. Disconnect the infusion set from your body and remove the battery. Press the function button for 2 seconds to put the pump into storage mode. The pump can then be restarted by reinserting the battery. Verify the settings and insert a new insulin reservoir and infusion set. Then, you can restart the pump.

- If for any reason you plan on temporarily not using the pump, it's recommended to place it in storage mode. Achieve this by stopping the pump and removing the battery. Replace the battery cover, and after 5 minutes, the pump will switch to storage mode.

Now, you're familiar with the primary alarms on the pump and what steps to take. Thus, there's no need to panic when you receive a pump alarm. If your pump is linked to the mylife Loop app, most alarms will typically display on your mobile phone, not your pump. If there's any confusion, always refer to the user manual or contact customer service. Good luck!
