## **<u>2. How the Accu-Chek Insight insulin pump works</u>**

Welcome to this informative video on how the Accu-Chek Insight pump operates. In this segment, we will explore the different parts and buttons of the pump, learn how to change the battery, unlock its functions, and navigate through its screens and menus.

### ***2.1 Parts & buttons on the pump***

Let's start with the various components and buttons on the pump:

Here, you can see the different parts of the Accu-Chek Insight insulin pump: the pump itself, the insulin, the battery, the battery cover, and the adapter and tubing. When the pump is inserted, it looks like this. You can connect the tube to your body via the infusion set to administer the insulin.

On the front of the pump, you'll find three buttons for navigation: two arrows and a middle OK button. Below the screen, there is a Zoom button that allows you to adjust the size of the characters displayed. At the top of the pump, you'll find two buttons that serve the same purpose in most situations, primarily used to unlock the pump. The back of the pump displays the serial number, which you may need when contacting customer service.

### ***2.2 Replacing the battery***

To replace the battery, use one AAA battery, preferably lithium. Be prepared to change your battery every 2 weeks, although they often last longer. Insert the battery with the + side first, and the - side facing out. Make sure to push the battery cover all the way into the battery compartment until you hear a click. Rotate the adapter to seal the pump securely.

To maintain a strong battery connection and prolong battery life, it is recommended to replace the battery cover every six months. This helps prevent oxidation and ensures optimal performance.

If you want to replace the battery without replacing the adapter, you can simply rotate the adapter 90° to the left. Press the latch of the battery compartment cover towards the ampoule compartment. You can use the flip-up lever to easily remove the battery compartment cover.

When inserting a new battery and closing the cover, the pump will perform a function test, indicated by a series of color screens, vibrations, and beeps. If the pump has been without a battery for more than an hour, you'll need to set the date and time. Confirm the battery type and insulin start by pressing the OK button.

### ***2.3 Unlocking the pump***

To unlock the pump, press one of the top keys within one second, followed by pressing the up and down arrows or vice versa. The pump is set to key lock mode by default, and if the keys are not pressed for a certain duration, the pump goes into lockdown. <span class="mark">This duration defaults at 10 seconds, but</span> can be adjusted according to user preferences, with the option to extend it up to 30 seconds.

### ***2.4 Home screen and pump menu*** 

Upon unlocking the pump, you will typically see the status screen, which displays the time, battery status, and date at the top. An icon at the top right indicates the pump's status, with a green triangle indicating insulin delivery, a red square indicating stopped, and two blue dashes representing pause. Additional icons may include an airplane mode icon or a lock icon for an active key lock. The middle section of the screen shows information about active basals, temporary basals, or boluses.

Pressing the OK button on the home screen will take you to the pump menu, which can be navigated using the arrow keys. The OK button is used to confirm or open a menu, while one of the top buttons allows you to go back to the previous level. Each menu also has a home icon that takes you back to the home screen.

The pump menu offers options such as bolus, basal, stop pump, start pump, pump data, modes, settings, <span class="mark">cartridge</span> and infusion set, and home. A white arrow indicates submenus, and a scroll bar on the right suggests more menu items. If an action requires multiple steps, the top right corner shows the remaining steps. A radio button indicates whether a function is enabled or disabled.

In the settings menu, you can adjust various parameters, such as sound alarms, key lock duration, display brightness, orientation, and background color.

### ***2.5 Do's & don’ts***

Remember these do's and don'ts:

- You can find a comprehensive menu overview in the downloads section, which can be saved for easy reference when providing remote guidance as a healthcare provider.

- <span class="mark">The pump is waterproof, but only for an hour until a depth of 1.3 meter (or 4.3 feet).</span>

- Utilize the pump clip or other carrying materials, such as a clip case, flip case, or leather case, to secure the pump to your clothing. The holes on the left side of the pump can accommodate a lanyard if needed, although this is not commonly used. Roche provides a list of carrying systems in the downloads section, and there are also other companies offering carrying solutions.

- The Accu-Chek Insight insulin pump does not automatically adjust the time and date. So when changing between winter and summer time, or when traveling to a different time zone, you have to adjust the time yourself. You can do that via the settings.
