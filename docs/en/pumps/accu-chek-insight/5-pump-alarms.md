## **<u>5. Pump alarms</u>**

Welcome to this video on the alarms in the Accu-Chek Insight insulin pump.

<span class="mark">We will discuss 4 types of alarms: errors, maintenance messages, warnings and reminders.</span>

All alarms are indicated by beeps and/or vibrations.

When an alarm appears, you should always confirm it by pressing OK. Failure to confirm within 60 seconds will increase the sound and vibration signals to the maximum level until confirmation is given.

Let's go over the different types of notifications, along with some do's and don'ts.

### ***5.1 Errors***

Error messages indicate a malfunction in the pump. The pump will enter STOP mode and display a red cross.

<span class="mark">Examples are mechanical error, electronic error, rewind error, or language error.</span>

If you can't resolve the error, contact Roche Diabetes Service.

### ***5.2 Maintenance messages***

Maintenance messages require immediate action to ensure proper pump functioning. The pump will enter PAUSE or STOP mode and display an orange lozenge.

Examples are ampoule not inserted, empty ampoule, low battery, automatic shutdown, clogging, end of term, incomplete ampoule replacement, data download failed, pause mode ended, battery type not set, or ampoule type not set.

After a maintenance message, the pump will be in STOP or PAUSE mode.

### ***5.3 Warnings***

Warning messages indicate upcoming maintenance or attention needed. The pump will continue to operate as usual and display a yellow triangle.

Possible warnings are ampoule almost empty, low battery, invalid time/date, end of guarantee, cancelled temporary basal dosage, cancelled quick bolus or loantime warning.

### ***5.4 Reminders***

You can set a reminder for a bolus administration, missed bolus, alarm clock, infusion set replacement, or completed temporary basal dosage.

### ***5.5 Do's & don’ts***

- If in doubt, refer to the user manual or call customer service.

- The Accu-Chek Insight insulin pump uses 4 signal modes that allow you to quickly set your notifications.

  - You can choose the normal setting,

<!-- -->

- via vibrations,

- quieter than usual (if you are in a meeting, for example)

- or louder than usual (if you are in a noisy environment, for example).

<!-- -->

- You can set the volume of the notifications and whether the notifications should be accompanied by a vibration or not via the device settings. Through these device settings, it is also possible per signal mode to delay the signal during a certain time block. You can do this once or all days in a certain time block.

- If you will not be using your pump for an extended period, you can put the pump in storage mode. You can do this by putting the pump in STOP mode and then remove the battery to extend its usage time, remove the ampoule and replace the battery compartment cover.

Knowing the alarms and what to do next will help you navigate pump notifications confidently. If you have any doubts, refer to the user manual or contact customer service. Good luck!
