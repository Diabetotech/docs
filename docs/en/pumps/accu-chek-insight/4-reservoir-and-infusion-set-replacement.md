## **<u>4. Reservoir and infusion set replacement</u>**

Welcome to this instructional video on how to replace the insulin reservoir and infusion set on the Accu-Chek Insight insulin pump.

In this video, we will cover the available infusion sets, the 4-step process for changing the insulin and infusion set, and last but not least, some important do's and don'ts.

### ***4.1 Different infusion sets***

The Accu-Chek Insight pump offers different types of infusion sets, including the Accu-Chek Insight Flex, Accu-Chek Insight Tender, and Accu-Chek Insight Rapid. Below this video you can find an overview of the available infusion sets, their tubing and cannula lengths, and filling volumes.

### ***4.2 Supplies***

To replace your infusion set and reservoir, gather the following items in addition to your Accu-Chek Insight insulin pump:

- an insulin PumpCart ampoule (at room temperature)

- an Accu-Chek Insight Adapter and Tube

- an Accu-Chek Insight Flex cannula

- an Accu-Chek Insight Flex LinkAssist Plus insertion aid (optional)

- and some disinfectant wipes

### ***4.3 Reservoir replacement***

The Accu-Chek Insight pump uses pre-filled insulin PumpCarts, which are easy to replace. However, note that each PumpCart contains only 160 units of insulin, requiring more frequent replacements compared to tubed pumps with larger reservoirs.

To replace the PumpCart, simply open the insulin compartment, and the pump will prompt you to confirm that you want to replace the ampoule. The pump will go into the “pauze” mode for maximum 15 minutes, so you have time to change your ampoule, infusion set and cannula.

Follow the prompts to remove the existing adapter and ampoule, and dispose them properly.

Next, insert a new PumpCart in the insulin compartment while holding the pump upright. Make sure that the PumpCart fits correctly. Place a new adapter on the bayonet socket of the insulin compartment. Make sure you insert the nose of the adaptor into the notches of the pump's housing at 90 degrees. Otherwise the needle could <span class="mark">bend. Turn the adaptor clockwise until it is aligned with the pumps housing. Then press the select b</span>utton to confirm. Confirm the ampoule type, and the pump will perform a function test.

### ***4.4 Filling the tubing***

After inserting the new insulin PumpCart, the pump will prompt you to fill the tube. Confirm the filling process, and the pump will guide you through the steps. Once insulin appears at the end of the connector, press one of the buttons at the top of the pump to stop filling. By default, the pump stops filling at 14 units of insulin. If necessary, repeat the procedure to eliminate air bubbles from the tubing.

After filling the tubing, the pump suggests connecting the tubing to the infusion set. To do this, you must first insert the infusion set.

### ***4.5 Infusion set placement***

The most commonly used infusion set is the AccuCheck Insight Flex, which includes an adhesive plaster, a connector plate, an introducer needle, a needle box with a button and a tube with disconnect cover.

If you have the Accu-Chek LinkAssist Plus insertion aid, you can use it to insert the infusion set automatically.

- First, wash your hands thoroughly.

- Then choose an infusion site on your abdomen and disinfect it well.

- Insert the needle holder into the filling chamber of the insertion aid, the coloured part towards the safety catch.

- Remove the two sections of cover film from the adhesive patch.

- Hold the base of the Accu-Chek LinkAssist Plus firmly with one hand and with the other hand, pull the clamping element out and up as far as possible. The insertion needle with the soft cannula folds out.

- Place the applicator on the selected infusion site. Press the applicator onto the skin until the safety catch is no longer visible.

- Push the release button to insert the infusion set.

- Remove the insertion aid.

- Return the clamping element to its home position and put the insertion aid away.

- Stick the patch on the infusion site and press it to ensure good contact with the skin.

- Remove the needle holder with the insertion needle at a right angle, while holding the plaster back. The plaster and the connecting system remain on the skin. The Insertion Needle automatically jumps back into the needle holder.

So, you have now placed your infusion set correctly. You can now proceed on the pump to fill the cannula.

### ***4.6 Filling the cannula***

To fill the cannula, navigate to the menu, select "Ampoule and Infusion Set," and choose "Fill Cannula." Connect the tube to the infusion set located on your body. Confirm the connection, and the pump will begin filling the cannula.

Check the status icon on the pump to ensure insulin delivery is active, indicated by a green triangle.

### ***4.7 Do's & don’ts***

- When using the Accu-Chek Insight insulin pump, you have the option to use either NovoRapid or Fiasp PumpCarts.

- Check your glucose levels two hours after inserting a new infusion set to confirm proper insertion and insulin delivery.

- When disconnecting the tube from the infusion set, press on the side clamps of the connector and ensure a firm click when reconnecting.

Congratulations! You have successfully replaced the insulin reservoir and infusion set on the Accu-Chek Insight pump. Remember, if you encounter any difficulties, don't hesitate to try again or seek assistance. Good luck!
