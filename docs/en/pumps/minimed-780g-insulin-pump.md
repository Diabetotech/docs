**<u>2. How the MiniMed 780G insulin pump works</u>**

Welcome to this informative video on how the MiniMed 780G pump <span class="mark">works! To</span>day, we'll explore its various features, including the pump's components, battery replacement, unlocking process, and the screens and menu options you'll encounter.

### ***2.1 Parts & buttons on the pump***

Let's start by familiarizing ourselves with the parts and buttons on the pump. The front of the pump boasts a color screen that you can control using the buttons located below it. At the top right, you'll find the power button, while the back button sits at the top left. The center button, featuring a small circle, acts as the select or OK button, and the surrounding arrows allow for easy navigation through the menu.

The MiniMed 780G pump operates on a single AA battery, housed in the right-side battery compartment. On the left side, you'll notice the insulin reservoir, which connects to your body through a tube and infusion set. The backside of the pump displays the serial number, and there's also a space available for attaching a pump clip.

### ***2.2 Battery replacement***

To ensure proper functioning, it's recommended to replace the AA battery every 1-2 weeks. Alkaline batteries work perfectly well. To replace the battery, simply twist open the battery cover using a penny or the pump belt clip. When inserting the new battery, ensure that the battery cover slot remains horizontal. Consider replacing the battery cover once a year to maintain its waterproof seal, especially when entering frequently in wet conditions.

### ***2.3 Unlocking the pump***

Now let's move on to unlocking the pump. Pressing any button will power on the pump and bring you to the home screen.

### ***2.4 Home screen and pump menu***

On the home screen, you'll see the most recent glucose reading in the center, along with icons indicating the battery, reservoir, and sensor connection status. The active insulin amount is displayed at the bottom. By selecting the <span class="mark">right</span> arrow, you can access your time in range, this i<span class="mark">s the time that your blood sugar is between 70 and 180 milligram per deciliter or 3.9 and 10 millimoles per liter. Further details can be accessed by using the down arrow button. Please note that the time in range mentioned only covers the last 24 hours. Unfortunately, at the moment, the MiniMed pump does not provide the average time in range for longer periods, such as weeks.</span>

When SmartGuard is activated, a blue shield icon appears on the home screen.

If your sensor is paired, pressing the sensor graph button will take you to the sensor graph display. By pressing the arrow at the top, you can extend the time frame to view more hours. Here, you can also observe the insulin delivery performed by the SmartGuard algorithm. The purple bars represent basal insulin micro boluses, and the blue bars signify autocorrect boluses, which will be covered in detail later in this module.

By pressing the select button, you'll be directed to the menu screen. From here, you can access various options, including History & Graph, SmartGuard, Sound & Vibration, Blood Glucose, Insulin, Reservoir & Set, Status, Paired Devices and Settings.

Take some time to explore the internal menu of the MiniMed 780G pump so you can become familiar with the different functions. The pump operates somewhat similarly to a mobile phone and intuitively guides you through its features.

### ***2.5 Do's & don’ts***

Before we conclude, let's go over a few do's and don'ts:

- We strongly recommend downloading the menu overview for convenient access. This resource will greatly enhance your troubleshooting skills, especially when assisting someone remotely. <span class="mark">Below this video, you will be able to download this MiniMed 780G pump overview, and this simplified menu map.</span>

- The pump is waterproof for up to 24 hours, with a maximum depth of 3.6 meters or 11.8 feet.

- Ensure the battery cap is horizontally aligned to prevent cracking. Over-tightening the cap can lead to cracks.

- The MiniMed 780G pump is susceptible to cracking, especially around the pump clip area. Periodically check the back of the pump for any cracks. If you spot a crack, the pump will still function but will no longer be waterproof. Contact Medtronic for a replacement pump in such cases.

- The pump comes with a complimentary pump clip that can be easily attached. To remove it, press on the sides at the top.

- Additionally, consider using silicone sleeves or other protective measures for the pump. Medtronic prov<span class="mark">ides plenty c</span>ompatible carrying solutions, and various other companies offer alternative options.

- The MiniMed 780G pump does not automatically adjust the time and date. So when changing between winter and summer time, or when traveling to a different time zone, you have to adjust the time yourself. You can do that via the settings menu.

And there you have it! You now have a comprehensive understanding of how to operate the MiniMed 780G pump and replace its battery. In our next video, we'll delve into the process of configuring insulin delivery.

## **<u>3. Insulin setup</u>** 

Welcome to this comprehensive video guide on setting insulin delivery using the MiniMed 780G pump.

In this segment, we will cover programming basal insulin, setting temporary basals, delivering a bolus with or without the bolus wizard, and important do's and don'ts to keep in mind.

### ***3.1 Programming basal insulin*** 

Let's begin by exploring basal insulin programming. To adjust basal insulin levels, navigate to the pump menu and select Insulin or Settings. From there, choose "Delivery Settings" and "Basal Pattern Setup" to specify the pattern you wish to modify. Under the options, you can edit the hourly blocks and insulin rates. Always begin at 0 hours and end at 24 hours. For example, you can set a basal rate of 0.9 units per hour from 0 to 8AM, 0.65 units per hour from 8AM to 6PM and 0.9 units per hour until midnight. Once you have made the desired adjustments, save the changes.

To stop basal insulin, go to the insulin bottle in the menu or use the up arrow for quicker access. Choose "Suspend All Delivery" to stop insulin delivery and click yes to confirm. The home screen will show "Delivery Suspended," and you'll receive reminders every 15 minutes. To resume, click on the up button, select "Resume Basal" and confirm to restart insulin delivery.

### ***3.2 Temporary basal***

When you need to administer a temporary basal, select the insulin bottle in the menu and select "Basal" followed by "Temp Basal." Adjust the duration and percentage as needed. Alternatively you can also choose a specific basal rate. While the temporary basal is active, you will see a green banner displayed on the home screen, indicating its remaining duration. To cancel a temporary basal, return to the insulin bottle in the menu, where you will find the option to cancel the temp basal. This process is intuitive and self-explanatory.

### ***3.3 Delivering a bolus*** 

Next, we'll delve into setting up the bolus calculator, known as the bolus wizard at Medtronic. To configure it, go to the menu, and select settings. Select "delivery Settings" and then select "Bolus Wizard Setup". Select "Bolus Wizard" to turn on the feature. Here it is shown how you adjust the necessary parameters for the bolus wizard, namely the carbohydrate ratio, the insulin sensitivity, the target glucose and the active insulin time.

Now, let's discuss giving a bolus. We will cover two methods: manual bolus and bolus using the bolus wizard.

- For a manual bolus, access the insulin bottle in the menu and select the "bolus" option or use the down arrow as a shortcut. The down arrow serves as a shortcut to the bolus screen, where you can enter the desired insulin amount. For instance, you can give a bolus of 0.5 units using this method.

- To give a meal bolus using the bolus wizard, select the insulin bottle, choose "give bolus," and activate the bolus wizard. The wizard will consider factors such as blood glucose levels, carbohydrate intake, and active insulin to calculate the recommended bolus amount. Simply enter the relevant information, and the wizard will present the suggested bolus. Confirm and deliver the bolus as instructed.

In the manual mode of the MiniMed 780G pump, you can choose between 3 types of bolus deliveries, including normal bolus, Square Wave bolus, and Dual Wave bolus. Square and Dual Wave boluses can be set up and delivered only after the Dual/Square Wave feature has been turned on in the Delivery Settings.

### ***3.4 Do's & don’ts***

Before we conclude, let's go over some important do's and don'ts:

- Ensure maximum bolus limits are set slightly above expected bolus doses. You can set this betwee<span class="mark">n 0 and 25 units. </span>

- <span class="mark">Consider adjusting the Bolus increment in the Delivery Settings to 0.025 units for optimal SmartGuard performance.</span>

- <span class="mark">The bolus speed determines how quickly the pump delivers bolus insulin. You can choose a standard rate (1.5 units per minute) or a quick rate (15 units per minute). If you’re experiencing a lot of infusion set blockages with the quick rate, it might be helpful to switch to standard rate.</span>

- <span class="mark">The MiniMed pump offers an Easy bolus feature, although it is not commonly utilized. This feature allows you to deliver a regular</span> bolus using only the up button. For more information, please refer to the pump's manual.

Congratulations! You now possess the knowledge to set and adjust insulin delivery on the MiniMed 780G pump. In our next video, we will guide you through changing the insulin reservoir and infusion set.

## **<u>4. Reservoir and infusion set replacement</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on replacing the infusion set and insulin reservoir on the MiniMed 780G pump.

In this video, we will cover the different infusion set options, step-by-step instructions for replacing the reservoir and infusion set, and helpful tips for success.

### ***4.1 Different Infusion Sets***

Let's begin by introducing the available infusion sets for the MiniMed 780G pump, which include the Extended Wear, Mio Advance, Mio, Mio 30, Silhouette, Quickset, and Sure-T infusion sets. A detailed overview of these infusion sets, including tubing and cannula lengths and filling volumes, can be found below this video.

Now, let's dive into the process of replacing the insulin reservoir and infusion set on the MiniMed 780G pump. <span class="mark">We will show this with the Extended Wear Infusion Set.</span>

***4.2 Supplies***

Before you begin, remember to wash your hands and gather all the necessary supplies for the replacement procedure. That is an Extended Wear infusion set with the included Insertion Device, an Extended Reservoir, some alcohol wipes, and your insulin on room temperature.

### ***4.3 Reservoir replacement***

- To replace the insulin reservoir, access the <span class="mark">"Reservoir and Set" menu on your pump, and choose for "New Reservoir".</span>

- Remove the used infusion set by gently loosening the adhesive and pulling it away from your body.

- Then, turn the tubing connector counterclockwise to detach the reservoir from the pump. Dispose of the used infusion set and reservoir appropriately.

- Next, rewind the piston in your pump.

- Ensure that the insulin vial is at room temperature and wipe the top with an alcohol wipe, allowing it to dry.

- Remove the new reservoir from its packaging and position the plunger according to the desired insulin amount. Hold the reservoir by the blue transfer guard and connect it to the insulin vial by pressing down. Flip the vial over and release your thumb pressure from the plunger to fill the reservoir with insulin. Tap the reservoir to remove any air bubbles.

- To fill the tubing connector, ensure both the top of the reservoir and the inside of the connector are dry. Connect the tubing <span class="mark">connector</span> to the top of the reservoir, turning it clockwise until it locks with a click. Tap the reservoir to allow air bubbles to rise, then push the plunger to purge the bubbles from the reservoir into the tubing.

- <span class="mark">If you're not able to push insulin into the tubing, disconnect the tubing connector and then reconnect it.</span>

- <span class="mark">Unscrew the plunger rod counter-clockwise until it completely separates from the reservoir.</span>

- <span class="mark">Put the reservoir in the pump and turn the tubing connector clockwise until you feel the reservoir lock into place. The tubing connector should line up with the groove in the battery cap of your pump.</span>

- <span class="mark">Now you will need to load the reservoir in the pump. On your pump select "Next" to go to the Load Reservoir screen. With "Load" highlighted press and keep holding the select button until the screen shows complete. When you see "Complete" and a yellow check mark on the screen, select "Next".</span>

### ***4.4 Filling the tubing***

- Remove the cap from the infusion set and hold it with the needle facing downwards.

- <span class="mark">Fill the tubing by pressing the fill option, ensuring there is no air in the tubing and drops of insulin appear at the needle's end. Follow the pump's prompts to proceed.</span>

### ***4.5 Infusion set placement***

- Clean the infusion site thoroughly with an alcohol wipe and allow it to air dry.

- Pick up the insertion device and remove the paper backing, being careful not to touch the adhesive. Remove the disconnect cover by gently squeezing the sides and pulling it away from the insertion device. Store the disconnect cover for later use to protect the cannula housing.

- When disconnected, stretch the skin until smooth. Place the insertion device on the site that you have prepared.

- The raised arrows indicate the side where the tubing will be connected. To insert, firmly press the top button. Carefully pull the insertion device away from your body. Smooth the adhesive onto the skin. Hold the cannula housing steady with your finger, then push the sight connector straight into the cannula housing until you hear a click.

### ***4.6 Filling the cannula***

- Now that the introducer needle is removed, the cannula is empty and will need to be filled with insulin. Using the pump, fill the cannula with insulin. Both the six millimeter and nine millimeter cannula of the Extended Wear Infusion <span class="mark">Set, tak</span>e 0.6 units of insulin to fill. Here you can see the required fill volumes of the other infusion sets.

### ***4.7 Do's & don’ts***

Lastly, let's go <span class="mark">over some important do's and don'ts:</span>

- <span class="mark">The insulin approved in the MiniMed 780G pump is Novorapid, Humalog, and Novolog. The use of Fiasp and Lyumjev is approved in Europe.</span>

- <span class="mark">Remember, 2 hours after inserting a new set, you should check your blood glucose. Checking your blood glucose is the only way to confirm your infusion set is properly inserted and that you're receiving insulin.</span>

- <span class="mark">For swimming or showering, you can disconnect your tubing from the infusion set and apply the provided protective cover. To reconnect, simply remove the cover and snap the tubing back on.</span>

- <span class="mark">The extended infusion set and extended reservoir can both be worn</span> for up to seven days. However, you may require more insulin than the extended reservoir can hold. In this case, you will need to change only the reservoir while keeping the same set.

- Always use the Extended Wear Reservoir with the Extended Wear Infusion Sets. If you use another in<span class="mark">fusion set, you can use the regular reservoirs of Medtronic.</span>

- <span class="mark">Consider setting a reminder for a set change at intervals of 2, 3 or 7 days, depending on the infusion set that you are using.</span>

<span class="mark">In the rare event of encountering difficulties during the replaceme</span>nt process, remain calm and follow the pump's instructions. If needed, reach out to the provided support number or consult user videos on platforms like YouTube for additional guidance. Also, don't forget that you can contact your diabetes team, who also have an on-call service for medical support.

Congratulations! You are now equipped with the knowledge to confidently replace the insulin reservoir and infusion set on the MiniMed 780G pump.

## **<u>5. Pump alarms</u>**

Welcome to this informative video about the alarms and notifications on the MiniMed 780G pump.

The MiniMed 780G pump differentiates between alarms, alerts, and status messages. When you see a white triangle in the bottom-right corner of the screen, press <span class="mark">the select or</span> the down arrow button. The notification will remain until you clear it, and sometimes, responding to one notification may trigger another.

It's crucial to pay attention and respond promptly to all notifications that appear on the pump. Let's dive in!

### ***5.1 Alarms***

An alarm signifies a situation that requires immediate attention, such as a stopped insulin delivery or a hypoglycemic event.

During an alarm, a red icon and instructions will be displayed on the pump screen.

The red light will flash twice, followed by a pause, and repeat continuously.

Depending on your settings, the pump will emit beeps and/or vibrations in a pattern of three pulses followed by a pause.

To clear an alarm, press the designated button and select the appropriate action. Clearing the alarm may resolve the issue, but if not, the alarm will persist until the underlying problem is addressed. If the alarm remains unresolved after 10 minutes, it will escalate to a loud emergency siren.

### **5.2 Alerts**

Alerts indicate a situation that requires attention but may not be as urgent as an alarm.

During an alert, a yellow icon and instructions will be displayed on the pump screen.

The red light will flash once, followed by a pause, and then flash once again in a continuous pattern.

And the pump will emit beeps and/or vibrations in a pattern of three pulses followed by a pause, depending on your settings.

To clear an alert, press the designated button and make the appropriate selection. The pump will beep at intervals of 5 or 15 minutes (depending on the alert) until the alert is cleared. Some alerts may escalate to a loud emergency siren after 10 minutes.

### ***5.3 Messages***

Messages provide information about the pump's status or require your input.

When a message appears, a blue icon and instructions will be displayed on the pump screen. The red light on the pump will not flash, but the pump will emit a single beep and/or vibration, depending on your settings.

To delete a notification, press the designated button and make the necessary selection.

### ***5.4 Do's & don’ts***

- If you are unsure about what to do, consult the user manual, contact customer service for guidance or discuss with your diabetes team.

- A critical alarm to watch out for is a pump failure. If a critical pump failure occurs, you will see a specific screen, and an emergency siren will activate. Disconnect the pump immediately and do not use it again. Contact Medtronic's service number for assistance.

- If you need to temporarily stop using the pump, such as during a defect, you can activate storage mode. You can do this by removing the pump's battery and pressing and holding the back arrow until the screen turns off.

- Medtronic offers the WeCare portal and app specifically designed for users of the MiniMed 780G pump, and we highly recommend signing up for it. By registering, users gain access to their 24/7 customer service, online services, and personalized diabetes management support. It includes replacements for defective sensors and belt clips, CareLink data insights, notifications of software updates, a diabetes community, and the StartRight coaching program for the initial 3 months of therapy.

With the knowledge of this lesson, you can confidently address the main alerts and notifications on your pump. Don'<span class="mark">t be concerned whe</span>n they occur—simply refer to the on-screen instructions. If you need further assistance, consult the user manual or reach out to customer service or your diabetes team. Best of luck on your journey!
