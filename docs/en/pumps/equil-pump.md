## **Equil Pump**

## **<u>1. Introduction</u>**

Welcome to this module on the Equil Insulin Pump! In the upcoming videos, we’ll examine the components and functionality of this modular, tubeless insulin pump system.

Measuring 59.5 × 40 × 11.1 mm and weighing 23 g, the Equil Pump is a modular patch pump, combining a reusable pump body and pump battery with a disposable insulin reservoir and pump base. The cannula is built into the pump base which adheres directly to the skin. The pump is controlled using a wireless Personal Diabetes Assistant (or PDA). Depending on your system version, the pump capacity and PDA interface may vary slightly. There is also a physical bolus button on the pump for quick insulin delivery without the remote. The device features a reservoir capacity of 200 to 300 units of U-100 rapid-acting insulin and is designed for people with diabetes aged 3 years or older.

Our Master Module on insulin pumps provided an overview of standard pump management, including basal and bolus rate configuration, reservoir and infusion set changes, and system alarm settings.

In this module, you will receive in-depth training on the Equil Pump, along with advanced tips for healthcare providers and people living with diabetes. But please note that these videos are for educational purposes only and do not constitute medical advice. Always consult your healthcare provider before making changes to your therapy.

These videos were produced by Dr. Inge Van Boxelaer, the founder of Diabetotech and an endocrinologist at the St-Lucas Hospital in Ghent, in Belgium. The content was reviewed by Anna Kreczmer, a diabetes educator from Poznań in Poland, with personal experience using the Equil Pump, who trains patients in pump therapy. Together, they provide a transparent and independent evaluation of the Equil Pump.

Rest assured, the content in this module is independent of MicroTech Medical, the manufacturer of the Equil Pump. As a result, you receive a genuine, balanced assessment of the device’s strengths and limitations.

Upon completing this module, you’ll be able to:

- identify the components of the Equil Pump and operate the device;

- configure the pump’s insulin delivery settings;

- customize and respond to Equil system alarms.

If you are a healthcare provider, this module will equip you to support Equil Pump users with assurance; if you are using the pump yourself, you will learn practical strategies to get the most from the device. We wish you every success as you get started with the Equil Pump!

## **<u>2. Pump operation</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on operating the Equil Insulin Pump.

In this video, we’ll introduce the key components of the Equil Pump system. You’ll learn how to set up and charge the Personal Diabetes Assistant (PDA), navigate its buttons, and complete the initial Settings Wizard. We’ll also walk through the PDA Home Screen and explain its main functions before outlining essential do’s and don’ts for safe and effective use of the Equil system. Let’s begin.

The Equil Pump system has evolved through different versions, with updates to the PDA, software, and reservoir capacity.

The earliest version of the PDA, which we’ll refer to as the classic PDA, is a dedicated medical device with physical buttons and a grid-style menu interface. It was designed primarily for manual pump control and includes a built-in blood glucose meter, allowing users to check their glucose levels directly on the device.

A newer version, Equil 1.5, features a touchscreen PDA similar to a smartphone. Its interface replaces the icon-based menu with a home screen that includes tappable buttons and a live CGM graph, enabling users to view key information at a glance. The Equil 1.5 pump also has an increased reservoir capacity of up to 300 units of insulin.

Building on these developments, MicroTech Medical introduced a closed-loop version, the Equil S. Like the Equil 1.5, it has a 300-unit capacity. Note that the classic and Equil 1.5 pumps are similar in size, while the Equil S is slightly larger, weighing 31 g.

While the classic and newer Equil systems differ in interface and capacity, their core operating principles are the same. In this module, demonstrations will primarily use the classic pump and PDA, with key differences in the newer versions highlighted where relevant.

The Equil Pump system includes the following reusable, non-consumable components:

- 1 pump body (which is covered by a 4-year warranty)

- 1 Personal Diabetes Assistant (or PDA) with battery

- 1 cannula inserter (also covered by a 4-year warranty)

- 2 pump batteries

- 1 pump battery charger

- 1 PDA charger, and

- 1 PDA charging cable.

These durable parts are designed for long-term use.

You will also need the following disposable, consumable components, which are not included in the system and have to be purchased separately:

- insulin reservoirs

- and infusion sets (made up of the cannula and the pump base).

### ***2.1*** Setting up the PDA

Remove the battery cover by holding the PDA firmly in one hand and lifting the cover using your fingernail in the slot shown in the figure. Next, insert the microSD memory card into the slot, then install the battery in the orientation shown. Replace the battery cover, ensuring it is secured around the perimeter of the PDA; you may hear a snap as it locks into place.

The PDA has several buttons and ports for operating and controlling the pump. To use it effectively, it is helpful to know them well.

One, the Power button. If the PDA is off, press and hold it until the PDA vibrates. After 30 seconds, the Home Screen appears. If the screen is on, press and hold the button to lock the PDA. Generally, the PDA screen turns off after a predefined period of inactivity. In this case, you will see the Lock Screen. When the screen is off, you can either tap the power button to turn it on and bring up the Lock Screen or press and hold it to turn the device off.

Two, the Help button. This button provides information about the device's commands and functions. It is active on certain screens, such as the main screen, the Base and Bolus screens, and the Log screen.

Three, the Back button. Its function is to take you back to the previous screen, or to close dialog boxes after reading them and verifying that they are not needed.

Four, the Home button. This button will always return you to the Home Screen.

Five, Up button. Six, Down button. These buttons change the selection. Their function is to help you navigate between options without using the touchscreen.

Seven, the Enter button. Its function is to select an action by pressing it, without using the touchscreen.

Eight, Touchscreen.

Nine, the Power Charging Port. It is also used for data transfer to a personal computer or cloud service.

Ten, Headphone Output.

Eleven, the port for the blood glucose meter integrated with the PDA. Use Exactive EQ strips to activate the Blood Glucose Test menu.

Twelve, Strip Ejector. Use it to remove the glucose test strip after the measurement has been completed. This is located on the back of the device.

Thirteen, the External Speaker. It can also be found at the back.

### ***2.***2. ***Charging the PDA and the pum***p battery

Before use, fully charge both the PDA battery and the patch pump battery. The PDA battery can only be charged while it is inside the PDA. The PDA may be on or off during charging, but it will charge faster when turned off.

To charge it, connect the narrow (USB-C) end of the cable to the PDA and the wide (USB-A) end to the PDA charger. Then plug the charger into a power outlet. If the PDA is on, the battery icon will change to a charging icon; if it is off, a charging animation will appear.

To charge the patch pump battery, insert it into the pump battery charger until you hear a click, which indicates it is properly seated. Connect the narrow end of the cable to the pump battery charger and connect the wide end to the PDA charger, then plug the PDA charger into a wall outlet. The blue LED will turn on to indicate charging; if the charger is faulty, the indicator will flash. When the battery is fully charged, the blue LED will turn off. You can then disconnect the cable and chargers, and remove the pump battery by lifting it out of the charger groove.

### 2.3. The PDA Settings Wizard

If you are using the pump for the first time, the first step is to press and hold the Power button on the PDA. The device will automatically take you to the Settings Wizard. This is where the pump’s basic settings are set before use. The rest of the settings can be adjusted later.

At this stage, you will see five screens for operating settings. The Next button will take you to the next screen, and the Previous button will return you to the previous screen. During this stage, none of the buttons on the PDA device will be active until you’re done configuring the initial settings.

The first screen you see is where you set the time and date. When a dialog box pops up, enter the exact date using the plus and minus buttons, then click OK.

Similarly, set the time, select OK to save and exit, and then set the hour format. It is automatically set to 24 hours, but you can choose 12 hours, which will allow you to choose morning or evening. Once that’s done, click Next.

The next page will appear. Here you adjust the basic basal rate parameters, with three options. First is the Maximum Basal Rate. Selecting it brings up a dialog box where you can set the maximum basal rate. It’s helpful for putting a threshold on the basal rate injection if there’s any error caused by insufficient pump knowledge. After that, you choose the Base Basal Rate. Use the plus and minus buttons to select the appropriate value. The last option is a Temporary Basal Rate, which can be disabled or displayed in units of insulin delivered per hour. Alternatively, you can use a percentage of the current basal rate.

Usually, this screen shows all the options selected by your healthcare provider. It’s also possible to skip it at the current stage and reset it at any time from the Home Screen via Settings and Insulin Delivery Settings.

Proceed to the third screen in the Settings Wizard. Here you will find the basic bolus settings, and this contains three options. First, the Bolus Increment setting allows you to set the intervals used when increasing or decreasing bolus amounts. The second option lets you set the Maximum Bolus value. Having a maximum dose limit will prevent human errors. Last is the Extended Bolus setting. It will open a dialog box, which can be disabled or expressed as insulin units, or as a percentage of the total calculated bolus amount. This screen shows doses set by your healthcare provider, which can be skipped and reset later.

Click Next to enter the fourth screen of the Settings Wizard. This page displays advanced bolus settings, where you can adjust two main pump features. The first is a Quick Bolus function, which allows you to administer a bolus using only one button located on the pump. This feature is turned off by default. If you choose it, you can set the Quick Bolus Increment, which is how much insulin will be injected every time you press the button. Selecting OK will automatically turn it on.

The second feature is a Bolus Calculator, which you can use to determine the size of a bolus based on blood glucose levels, carbohydrate consumption, and other parameters. With the assistance of your healthcare provider, enter these parameters here once. This feature is switched off by default.

In general, MicroTech Medical recommends not activating or interacting with these features unless you have received sufficient training on the use of the pump from an authorized trainer.

Afterwards, click the Next button and enter the last screen of the pump’s basic settings. Here you specify how many insulin units remain in the insulin reservoir in order to receive an alarm when the reservoir is about to run out. This is done through an option called the Low Reservoir Threshold.

Also from the same screen, you can set the full automatic shutdown of the pump. This is an option that will not be activated by default. That said, you can choose to activate it and adjust the number of hours the pump has been operating without any human interaction, such as entering data or pressing buttons. After that time passes, the pump will stop delivering insulin and shut down completely. A 15-minute alarm will begin before the auto-off time expires, and will continue until we acknowledge it. This is to let you know that insulin delivery has stopped. In this case, you have to refill the reservoir in order for the pump to work again. Click Finish.

Once you have completed the Settings Wizard, you will be able to use all the basic functions of the pump. If you wish to modify anything afterward, from the Home Screen, select Settings, then Settings Wizard. The pump is now ready for use.

### ***2.5 Do’s & don’ts***

- Use only accessories and disposables manufactured by MicroTech Medical or labelled under the Equil brand. Using non-standard components may compromise device safety.

- For safety, the PDA can control only one pump at a time.

- Each PDA battery supports at least 300 charge–discharge cycles. On a full charge, it typically lasts around 2–3 days. Heavy or frequent use will drain the battery faster, so charge as needed based on your usage.

- Each pump battery supports approximately 200 charge–discharge cycles. The pump system package includes two pump batteries so you can alternate charging between them. One fully charged battery lasts about 5 days. Repeated plunger operation increases power consumption and may shorten battery life.

- Insert the microSD memory card only after removing the battery. A microSD card is not included in the pump package, but you may provide your own. The microSD card is not required for the pump system’s primary functions; however, secondary features — such as data transfer and the MP3 player — will not function correctly without a memory card inserted.

- The blood glucose testing function of the PDA works exclusively with MicroTech Exactive EQ blood glucose test strips. Other test strips are not supported and will either produce an error or no result.

- The newer versions of the Equil Pump are water-resistant under specific conditions, meaning routine activities such as showering, sweating, and brief water exposure are generally supported within manufacturer limits. However, full submersion is not advised; it is therefore recommended to disconnect the pump before swimming. In contrast, the classic pump and the PDA are not water-resistant and must be kept dry at all times.

- Avoid exposing the pump and PDA to temperatures above 40°C or below 0°C.

- If you are scheduled for an X-ray, CT scan, MRI, or any procedure involving radiation, make sure to remove both the pump and the PDA before entering the room with the imaging equipment.

- Dedicated covers are available for both the PDA and the pump.

- If needed, clean the outer surfaces of the pump and PDA with a soft, damp cloth and mild detergent, then dry with a separate cloth. You can also disinfect them using an alcohol wipe. Do not use solvents like nail polish remover or paint thinner on the device surfaces.

- If the pump system is not in use for a short time, keep all components in a clean, dry, cool, and well-ventilated storage space.

- If the pump will not be used for an extended period, remove the battery and store it separately.

### ***3.1 Setting a basal rate***

From the Home Screen, press the Basal function button. You will see three basal program tabs, out of the seven programs the pump can run. You can navigate between them using the touchscreen. Here, you are viewing data for a lighter basal program. The top row displays the total basal amount, the program status — that is, whether it is active or not — and a list of basal program periods with their corresponding values and times. Swipe down in this area to scroll and view additional entries. All values in this list will always be lower than the Maximum Basal Rate. At the bottom of the screen, you’ll find the basal program action buttons, which allow you to activate a program or change its settings.

If you rotate the PDA to landscape orientation, the basal program graph mode is activated. In this view, the vertical axis represents the basal rate, while. Because the basal rate is currently set to a fixed value and no program periods have been defined, it appears as a straight line. Once a program is configured, the different basal periods will be shown both as a list and as a graphical display. Basal rates often vary throughout the day depending on daily activities. Since activity patterns can differ across days (such as workdays versus weekends, or fasting days versus regular days), you may need to create and activate different basal programs, switching between them as needed.

The Equil Pump also offers a quick basal program setup option, making it easier and faster to edit basal programs. To begin, enter Edit Mode by selecting the top row below the program name and tapping the Edit icon. The Basal Initial Setup window will appear. Use the plus and minus buttons to adjust the total daily basal amount, then choose how many time segments you want — either 24 or 6. Based on these settings, the system will automatically assign basal time periods to the program. To modify any time period, tap its Edit icon and, in the pop-up window, set the start time, end time, and basal rate. Keep in mind that changing individual time periods may slightly affect the total daily basal amount. You can also delete a time period by selecting the Cancel icon; in this case, the period will revert to the base basal rate. Note that the PDA may automatically merge adjacent time periods that have the same basal rate.

Basal programs can also be edited in graphical mode. Tap and hold a section of the graph to select a time period; the selected area will appear darker. You can move between periods using the left and right arrows, or by using the touchscreen. As in portrait mode, this icon opens the Basal Rate Settings window. Selecting the Cancel icon removes the selected time period and returns it to the base basal value, which you’ll see reflected immediately on the chart. This icon exits Edit Mode and cancels any changes, while selecting the Save option exits Edit Mode and saves your settings.

When you are not in Edit Mode, you can use the touchscreen to move between different basal programs. Renaming a basal program is done in portrait mode. Enter Edit Mode, tap Change Basal Program Name, and enter a new name in the dialog box. After renaming, tap Save Settings. The program can then be activated by selecting Activate and confirming by tapping OK. The active basal program’s name will appear on the Home Screen. If you edit a program that is already active, an Update Pump dialog box will appear after saving. Select Cancel to discard the changes, or OK to apply the updates to the pump’s current basal program.

### ***3.2 Setting a temporary basal rate***

Before you can use a temporary basal rate, the feature must be enabled. You can activate it either from page 2 of the Settings Wizard or by going to Settings \> Insulin Delivery Settings \> Temporary Basal Rate.

The Temporary Basal option can be used only within the currently active basal program. After the program is activated, the same activation button changes to Temporary Basal Rate. The button appears green when the Temporary Basal option is enabled. If it is not enabled, the button will appear gray.

Press the Temporary Basal Rate button to open the settings dialog box. Enter the Temporary Basal rate value in units per hour, based on the settings you selected earlier. Then set the duration and tap OK. The PDA will send the command to the pump to begin delivering the updated rate, and the Home Screen will display the Temporary Basal status.

### ***3.3 Delivering a bolus***

There are several methods to administer a bolus. The first is a manual bolus. From the Home Screen, tap the Bolus button to open the Bolus screen. In the upper-left corner, you’ll see a green Bolus button. Initially, the bolus amount is zero, and the Start button is greyed out. Tap the Bolus button to open a dialog box and enter the desired bolus amount, then press OK. The insulin amount will appear in the box, and the Start button will turn green. Tap Start to begin delivery. A “Connecting to Pump” animation will appear, followed by a confirmation window showing your last bolus amount and time. Tap OK to proceed. The PDA will return to the Home Screen and display the bolus delivery status at the previously configured rate. The Bolus button will remain inactive until the bolus has been fully delivered.

The Bolus screen also includes three programmable preset bolus buttons. Tap the Edit icon to open the Preset dialog box. Adjust the dose using the plus and minus buttons, assign a name, and tap OK. When you tap the left side of a preset button, its preset value appears in the bolus amount box on the left, along with a small arrow linking the preset to the selected dose. The Start button turns green, and you can proceed as before. Preset boluses are especially useful for fast-digesting foods, as the full dose is delivered immediately.

For meals that digest more slowly, you can use the Extended Bolus option. Extended boluses are delivered in two parts: Amount Now, which is delivered immediately, and the remaining amount, which is delivered over a set period of time. First, enter the total bolus amount as usual. Before starting, tap Extend Bolus to open the Extended Bolus window. Under Amount Now, enter the portion to be delivered immediately, using absolute units as defined in your settings. Then set the duration over which the remaining insulin will be delivered. Tap OK, then Start, and follow the same confirmation steps as before.

While delivery is in progress, the PDA returns to the Home Screen and displays the Extended Bolus status, including how much insulin has been delivered from each part. A countdown timer shows how much time remains for the extended portion. For high-fat meals, an extended bolus without an immediate dose may be appropriate. In this case, set Amount Now to zero. No insulin will be delivered immediately, and the full dose will be spread over a period ranging from 30 minutes to 8 hours.

The Equil Pump also includes a Quick Bolus feature. This allows you to administer a bolus directly using the Bolus button on the patch pump, without using the PDA. This can be useful if you are away from your PDA or prefer more privacy. Press and hold the Bolus button on the patch pump. An audible alert indicates that Quick Bolus mode is active. Each additional press increases the bolus by a fixed increment. The total bolus equals the number of button presses multiplied by the Quick Bolus Increment. For example, if the increment is 2 units and you need a 10-unit bolus, press the button five times. Each press is confirmed by a beep, with the tone increasing in pitch up to five tones to help you count presses. The total Quick Bolus dose cannot exceed the preset Maximum Bolus value. After entering the desired dose, wait three seconds. The pump will replay the tones to confirm the dose. If correct, press the button once more to start delivery. If the tones are incorrect, wait more than 10 seconds and the Quick Bolus will be cancelled automatically. You can also deliver a Quick Bolus using the Enter button on the PDA instead of the pump button.

In some situations, you may need to cancel a bolus after it has started — for example, if you entered an incorrect dose, miscalculated carbohydrates, entered the wrong blood glucose value, or changed your meal plan. While a bolus is in progress, tap Cancel on the Home Screen. A confirmation window will appear. Tap OK to confirm, and a dialog box will display the amount and time of insulin already delivered. A bolus can also be cancelled directly from the pump by pressing and holding the Quick Bolus button. You will hear an audible confirmation, and the bolus will stop immediately. A dialog box will then show the delivered dose details.

Be aware that canceling a bolus stops the Total Bolus, Amount Now, and Extended Amount. If you are manually recalculating a new dose, you must account for the insulin already delivered and subtract it from the next dose. If you are using the Bolus Calculator, the cancelled dose is automatically included as Active Insulin and deducted from the new bolus.

### 3.4 Using the Bolus Calculator

The Bolus Calculator can suggest a bolus dose after you test your blood glucose level and enter the amount of carbohydrates you will be eating, helping keep your blood glucose within the target range. This feature determines the amount of insulin needed to cover the rise in blood glucose associated with carbohydrate intake as the Meal Bolus. In addition, a Correction Bolus is added to help bring your current blood glucose level back to the target range. And if you have recently taken a bolus, there may still be active insulin in your body, so the Bolus Calculator will automatically subtract the active insulin from the Total Suggested Bolus dose.

The Bolus Calculator is switched off by default. To activate it from the Home screen, press Settings \> Blood Glucose Settings, then turn it on. You’ll see five settings displayed; these values should be adjusted in consultation with your healthcare provider. First is the Target Blood Glucose Range, which sets the default target range. This is a user setting confirmed by the healthcare provider. The Equil Pump also allows you to set different target ranges for different time periods, and the calculator takes the average adjusted value into account. Next is the Carbohydrate Ratio (ICR), which refers to how many carbohydrates are covered per unit of insulin and is used to calculate the Meal Bolus. Because metabolism varies from person to person, set this value under the guidance of your healthcare provider. Note that different carbohydrate ratios can also be set for different time periods. The Insulin Sensitivity Factor (ISF) indicates how much your blood glucose may drop after taking one unit of insulin. Since this value also differs between individuals, consult your healthcare provider, and remember that ISF can be set for different time periods as well. Even if you have recently taken a bolus, you may still have active insulin in your body, as insulin typically remains active for four to six hours, and this value must be determined by your doctor. Finally, Reverse Correction affects how correction boluses are calculated. If it is off, the Bolus Calculator will consider a correction bolus only when your current blood glucose is higher than the target range. If it is on, a correction bolus is always calculated. The default setting is on, but your doctor can change it if necessary. Once you’ve adjusted the settings and understood the inputs, you can use the Bolus Calculator quickly and easily.

Only two inputs are needed to generate a bolus suggestion: your current blood glucose level and the amount of carbohydrates you will be eating. On the Home screen, select Actions, then click Enter BG Reading to open the manual entry screen. Using the plus and minus buttons, enter your blood glucose reading, select the appropriate tag — Before/After Exercise or Before/After Meal — and press Save. When the dialog box appears, choose OK to save the result. You’ll notice that the Bolus Calculator button changes from grey to green, indicating that it has been activated. The internal PDA blood glucose meter can also activate the Bolus Calculator. If the display is on, insert an Exactive EQ test strip and the blood glucose meter screen will appear. Apply enough blood sample, then the screen will count down five seconds. The test result will be displayed, and the Bolus Calculator button will turn green once a blood glucose reading has been recorded. Note that your blood glucose reading is valid for only ten minutes. If you do not deliver a bolus within 10 minutes of your test, please test again.

Now, press the Bolus Calculator button. It will ask you, “Will you be eating now?” If you choose Yes, a dialog box will appear asking you to enter the amount of carbohydrates. You can estimate the carbohydrates in your food by reading packaging labels, using carbohydrate-counting books or phone apps, or referring to the PDA’s Food Library. Enter the total amount of carbohydrates you intend to eat and press OK. The next page is the Suggested Bolus screen. At the top, you’ll see the suggested bolus dose. The middle section shows the calculation parameters, and the bottom contains action buttons. The suggested bolus amount appears automatically in the Bolus Amount area. Tapping the number opens a dialog box that allows you to change the value if your physician has advised adjustments, or you can use it as is. The Details button opens a window that explains how the suggested bolus was calculated. By pressing Start, you can administer the bolus dose. Alternatively, you may use the Extended Bolus option, as explained earlier. However, if you choose No in the “Will you be eating now?” dialog box, the Meal Bolus will not be included.

### ***3.***5 ***Suspending delivery***

Occasionally, you may need to temporarily pause insulin delivery using the Suspend function — for example, if you do not need insulin infusion for a short period or you need to remove the pump from the infusion set. From the Home Screen, tap Suspend to stop insulin delivery. A confirmation dialog box will appear; select OK to suspend or Cancel to exit. You may also choose to stop delivery and allow the pump to rewind. Keep in mind that if the pump rewinds, you will need to prime the reservoir again before resuming use.

Once Suspend mode is activated, the PDA returns to the Home Screen, and the information area will show how long insulin delivery has been paused. The Suspend/Stop button will change to a Resume button.

To restart insulin delivery, tap Resume. When the confirmation dialog box appears, select OK to resume insulin delivery.

### 3.6 Do's & don’ts

- At pump start-up, specific bolus settings are configured using the Settings Wizard, in consultation with your healthcare provider. These settings can be adjusted later from the Home Screen \> Settings \> Insulin Delivery Settings \> Bolus Rate.

  - The normal bolus delivery rate is 3.0 units per minute. This can be reduced to 1.5 units per minute if needed.

  - The Bolus Increment setting determines how much the requested bolus changes each time you press the plus or minus button. It can be set between 0.1 and 2 units.

  - The Maximum Bolus setting defines the largest single bolus dose the pump can deliver. This safety feature helps reduce the risk of overdose due to user error. It is recommended to set this value to the largest bolus you would normally administer. Setting it too high increases the risk of overdosage. You can also configure the Extended Bolus option. By default, this feature is turned off and the corresponding button appears grayed out. Once enabled, you can choose how much insulin is delivered immediately, either in insulin units or as a percentage of the total bolus dose.

  - The final setting is Quick Bolus, which is inactive by default. Select the checkbox next to Quick Bolus to enable it. The Quick Bolus Increment determines how much the bolus increases with each press of the Quick Bolus button.

- When you open the Basal screen for the first time, no basal program is active. If you activate one of the three basal programs, the previously running basal rate will stop automatically.

- The Initial Basal Setup provides only an estimate of daily basal needs. For individualised dosing, consult your healthcare provider.

- You cannot switch between basal programs in Graph Display mode. Return to List Display to select a different basal program.

- If you edit a basal program that is currently running on the pump, a dialog box will ask whether you want to send the updated program to the pump. Select OK to update and save, or Cancel to return to the Basal Edit screen.

- The maximum number of increments for a Quick Bolus is 20.

- To cancel a Quick Bolus using the PDA, press the Back button, or wait 10 seconds for the system to return to the Lock Screen.

- If there is no activity for 10 seconds, the pump will exit Quick Bolus mode automatically. To cancel a Quick Bolus using the pump, simply wait 10 seconds for the system to exit Quick Bolus mode.

## **<u>4. Reservoir and infusion set replacement</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on replacing the Equil Insulin Pump reservoir and infusion set.

In this video, we’ll walk through the full process of installing the Equil Pump, starting with the necessary preparations and attaching the pump base. Next, we’ll discuss how to fill the insulin reservoir, assemble the pump, and complete reservoir and cannula priming as required. We’ll then explain how to replace an empty reservoir and finish with important do’s and don’ts. Let’s get started.

### ***4.1*** Preparing for pump installation

To replace an Equil patch pump, gather the following supplies:

- an alcohol wipe,

- a pump base pack,

- a cannula pack,

- your cannula inserter,

- an insulin reservoir pack,

- an insulin vial or cartridge,

- a fully charged pump battery,

- and your PDA.

Before beginning the pump installation process, wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water, or disinfect them using alcohol. Then select an appropriate infusion site from the recommended areas, which include the abdomen on either side of the navel, the back of the upper arm, the thighs, or the lower back. Once a suitable site has been selected, clean the area with an alcohol wipe and allow it to dry completely without using a towel or tissue.

### ***4.2*** Attaching the pump base to the skin

Open the pump base pack, remove the protective covering, and place the pump base on the selected infusion site. Next, open the cannula pack as indicated on the packaging. Insert the cannula assembly into the cannula inserter until it reaches the cocked position and you hear two clicking sounds. Once the inserter is securely cocked, remove the protective sleeve. Hold the cannula inserter and align its front end with the lower protruding section of the pump base, as shown in the video. Press the back of the inserter until you hear a click, confirming that it is locked in place. To insert the cannula, press the two orange release buttons on both sides of the inserter at the same time. The cannula will be inserted into both the pump base and the infusion site. After insertion, release the inserter by pressing the button at the lower end and lifting it away. The pump base should now appear as shown, indicating successful cannula insertion. If the needle remains in the pump base, carefully remove it from the infusion site and dispose of it according to safety guidelines. Normally, however, the needle remains inside the inserter. In this case, replace the protective sleeve over the needle hub, press firmly on the top of the inserter to eject the needle hub, and dispose of it safely.

### ***4.3*** Filling the insulin reservoir

Remove the insulin reservoir from its packaging. The reservoir is fitted with a plastic component called the fill adapter, which is used during filling. Before filling the reservoir, pull the pull rod fully out and then push it back in. Repeat this action three times to ensure smooth operation. Clean the insulin vial or cartridge with an alcohol wipe and attach it to the fill adapter. With the vial positioned on top, slowly pull the pull rod to draw insulin into the reservoir. Gently tap the reservoir to allow any air bubbles to rise to the top. Slowly push the pull rod to expel the air back into the vial, then pull again to draw more insulin. Repeat this process until the reservoir contains enough insulin and no visible air bubbles remain. Remove the insulin vial from the fill adapter. Pull the two release tabs outward to detach the fill adapter, and carefully unscrew the pull rod counterclockwise without pushing or pulling. Before proceeding, ensure that the pump battery is fully charged.

### ***4.4*** Assembling the pump and priming

Assemble the pump and reservoir in the correct orientation, then press them together until you hear a click. If this is the first time you are installing the pump, you will need to complete the initial setup, including setting the time and date. This process is covered in Lesson 2: Pump Operation. Continue through the Settings Wizard until you reach page five, then select Finish. From the Home Screen, tap Actions, select Change Pump, and press OK to continue. Enter the pump serial number printed on the pump. The PDA will then attempt to connect to the pump, and an animation will indicate when the connection is successful. Select Continue. Hold the pump in the position shown in the video, then tap Prime Reservoir. When you see an insulin drop at the tip of the reservoir needle, confirm this by selecting OK. The Continue button will turn from gray to green; tap Continue. Attach the pump to the pump base in the correct orientation and press it into place until you hear a click. You will then be prompted to choose whether to prime the cannula or skip this step. Cannula priming is required only when changing the pump base, as a new cannula must be primed. If you are changing only the reservoir or battery, select Skip to avoid delivering unnecessary insulin. For demonstration purposes, let’s select Skip. After the pump connects, it is ready for use.

### ***4.5*** Replacing the insulin reservoir

When the insulin reservoir is empty, or when replacing the battery, follow the same procedure. From the Home Screen, select Actions, then Change Reservoir/Battery. An animation will prompt you to remove the pump from its base. Remove the pump and select Continue. A confirmation message will appear; select OK. The pump will reconnect and begin retracting the push rod to its original position. Wait until the rod is fully retracted before separating the pump from the reservoir, as failure to do so may damage the pump. Hold the pump as shown, with the printed side facing upward, and carefully separate the pump and reservoir. Dispose of the used reservoir according to safety instructions. Fill a new reservoir as previously demonstrated. Ensure the battery is fully charged, and assemble the battery correctly within the reservoir. Reattach the pump and reservoir, select Continue, and hold the pump in the indicated position. Select Prime Reservoir and wait for an insulin drop to appear, then confirm by selecting OK, and tap Continue. Finally, attach the pump to the base following the same steps as before. Choose whether to prime the cannula as appropriate, then select Finish.

### ***4.***6 ***Do’s & don’ts***

- Infusion sets consist of two components: the cannula pack and the pump base. The most commonly used cannula is the HRN-S-60, a straight 6 mm Teflon cannula. Other available options include the HRN-S-90, HRN-H-45, and HRN-H-65 cannulas.

## **<u>5. Pump alarms</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on Equil Insulin Pump alarms.

In this video, we’ll break down how the Equil Pump and PDA work together to notify you when attention is needed. We’ll review the alarm types you may encounter and finish with practical tips for responding to alarms. Let’s jump right in.

### ***5.1 Available*** a***larms***

When an issue is identified, the system can generate alarm notifications through sounds, LED indicators, or vibrations. It also provides on-screen details on the PDA. The system operates as a two-part setup: the pump detects events and triggers the alarms, while the PDA receives those alarms and delivers the user notifications. Medium-priority alarms occur when insulin delivery has stopped due to a technical problem, meaning you must intervene — for instance, troubleshooting, replacing the pump, or, if necessary, administering insulin manually to manage blood glucose. Meanwhile, low-priority alarms indicate that an event is likely to occur soon, but insulin delivery continues as normal; these alerts are intended to keep you informed so you can plan ahead and maintain reliable therapy.

Here’s an overview of the PDA and pump alarm priority levels, along with their corresponding visual, audio, and vibration signals. Note that the pump does not generate audio alerts, while the PDA does, at a sound pressure level of 60 to 90 dB.

Regarding pump alarms, you may receive medium-priority alerts when there is no insulin remaining, a blockage is detected, insulin delivery stops unexpectedly, or the pump battery is depleted. In these situations, you will need to take the appropriate corrective action before insulin delivery can resume. Low-priority alarms, on the other hand, occur when there is not enough insulin left to deliver a full bolus, or when the pump battery level is running low. These alerts give you time to prepare a new reservoir or a fully charged battery so that therapy can continue without interruption.

Your PDA will issue alarms for the same pump issues, using both LED and vibration signals. In addition, you may receive a low-priority alarm for a PDA failure. In that case, you should restart the device, and if the problem persists, contact your supplier for a replacement.

Because the alarm system is split between the pump and the PDA, there is a built-in delay between detection and notification. First, a safety sensor inside the pump detects a problem in less than a tenth of a second. Next, the pump generates its own alarm signal almost immediately, so the pump can alert you within about two-tenths of a second. The pump then sends the alarm information wirelessly to the PDA. This transfer can take up to four seconds. As soon as the PDA receives the information, it triggers its own alarm, again in less than a tenth of a second. In short, the pump responds almost instantly, while the PDA typically follows within four seconds.

On a final note, alarm settings on the Equil Pump — including alarm volume — are fixed and cannot be adjusted.

### ***5.***2 Tips

- The system does not provide alarms for expired/old insulin or insulin leakage. You should remain alert to these situations and respond appropriately if they occur.

- If the pump and PDA power on normally and no alarms are triggered, this indicates the alarm system is functioning properly.

- If the Auto-Off function is enabled, insulin infusion may stop automatically according to the preset configuration.

- When a medium-priority alarm occurs, the pump will rewind automatically and stop all insulin delivery. A pop-up message will appear on the PDA with details and suggested actions. Tap OK to close the pop-up. Replace the reservoir to re-initialize the system and resume delivery.

- Low-priority alarms appear once only and do not repeat.
