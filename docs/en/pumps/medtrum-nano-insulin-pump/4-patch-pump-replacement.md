## **<u>4. Patch Pump replacement</u>** 

In this section, we’re going to show you how to replace your TouchCare Nano Patch Pump.

In just a few easy steps, we'll guide you through the process of changing a TouchCare Nano Pump. We'll cover everything from connecting the pump base to the PDM or EasyPatch app, filling the Reservoir Patch with insulin, priming it, and applying the pump to your body.

### ***4.1 Pump Replacement with the PDM*** 

**If it’s your first time using the pump base, you’ll need to connect it to the PDM.** Just go to the main menu, tap on Settings, then choose Insulin Pump, and tap Add Pump Base. You can use your phone’s camera to scan the QR code on the back of the pump <span class="mark">base or the box it came in. Or, if you prefer, you can enter the serial number manually.</span>

<span class="mark">**Once the pump base is connected, it’s time to attach it to the reservoir patch.** First, wash your hands with soap and water and let them air dry. Then, open the reservoir patch package and place the patch on a clean flat surface. Take the pump base and gently press it into the patch at a 90-degree angle until you hear a click. After that, press again at a 45-degree angle to make sure it’s securely attached. You’ll hear four beeps when everything is correctly connected.</span>

<span class="mark">**Next, it’s time to fill the patch with insulin.** Start by cleaning the top of your insulin vial with an alcohol wipe. Attach the needle to the syringe and pull down the plunger to draw insulin. If you’re using an insulin vial, before drawing insulin, inject an amount of air into the vial equal to the volume of insulin you plan to withdraw. Tap the syringe gently to make air bubbles rise, then push the plunger slightly to remove the bubbles. Insert the needle straight into the fill port on the bottom of the patch. Pull the plunger back just a bit to remove any trapped air, then withdraw the needle. Remove any remaining bubbles from the syringe, then reinsert the needle and slowly fill the patch with insulin. When you’re done, remove the needle and put the protective cap back on before safely disposing of the syringe.</span>

<span class="mark">**Now you’re ready to prime the pump.** Make sure the patch pump is placed on a clean surface and kept at least 1 meter or 3.2 feet away from magnetic objects like phones or other pumps. Hold the pump with one hand and press the needle button down with the other hand until it locks halfway. In the app, tap Prime. The priming process takes a little time, and it’s important not to remove the safety lock while it’s priming.</span>

<span class="mark">**When priming is finished, the app will show you that it’s time to attach and insert the patch.** Choose a clean, flat, and pinchable spot on your body. Apply the patch horizontally on the abdomen, back, or buttocks, or vertically on the upper arm or thigh. Wipe it with an alcohol wipe and let it dry completely. Slide off the safety lock and peel off the adhesive liners starting from the side with the insulin fill port. Be careful not to press the needle button yet. Place the patch on your skin and press down firmly, especially around the edges of the adhesive and the area near the needle by pressing the center of the patch for a few seconds. Then, press the needle button in one quick motion. You’ll feel it lock in place. In the app, tap Continue — and that’s it! Your patch pump is now active and ready to deliver insulin.</span>

**When it’s time to remove the patch, start by deactivating it in the app**. Go to the main menu. Select patch, then select deactivate patch. Slide to deactivate patch. Follow the instructions to remove your Reservoir patch. Insert the needle eject tool vertically into the needle withdrawal hole and press vertically to eject the infusion needle. Gently lift the edge of the adhesive tape from your skin and remove the entire patch pump. We recommend that you wipe around the tape with alcohol wipes or baby oil to make the tape peel off easily. Fold and break the tab of the reservoir patch. Remove the pump base from the old Reservoir patch. Discard the used Reservoir patch according to your local waste disposal regulations and reuse the pump base.

### ***4.3 Do’s and don’ts***

Here are some important points to keep in mind

- S<span class="mark">ome people use a Skin Barrier product or extra medical tapes to make the wearing experience of the patch pump more comfortable. Medtrum can provide an alternative patch for users of the 200 U patch pump who experience an allergic reaction to the adhesive layer.</span>

- <span class="mark">The TouchCare Nano Patch pumps are compatible with Humalog, NovoRapid, Admelog, Apidra, Lyumjev and Fiasp with a concentration of 100 U per milliliter.</span>

- <span class="mark">The TouchCare Nano Pump has a fixed 90-degree steel cannula of 5 mm or 0.2 inch.</span>

- <span class="mark">The 200 units TouchCare Nano pump holds 200 U of insulin and can be worn for up to 3 days. If you require more than 200 units of insulin within 3 days, the 300 units TouchCare Nano pump will be more suitable for you. It is reassuring to know that the pump is not automatically disabled after three days or a fixed period. This flexibility allows you to choose a convenient and calm moment for a patch change.</span>

- <span class="mark">Remember to check your blood glucose levels 2 hours after inserting a new pump. This is the only way to confirm proper insertion and insulin delivery. It's best to avoid changing the pump right before bedtime.</span>

- <span class="mark">During a patch change, inspect the contact pins on the Pump Base for any discoloration and clean them with an alcohol wipe if needed. The pump is sensitive to moisture and dirt, and a small number of users have reported pump errors—most likely due to poor contact between the patch and the pump base. To minimize this risk, always place the pump base carefully and clean the contacts with an alcohol wipe at each change.</span>

- <span class="mark">If you ever feel unsure, don’t panic! Take your time, follow the steps, and if needed, reach out to custo</span>mer service or your diabetes care team.

You did a great job! Now you're familiar with the process of replacing the TouchCare Nano Pump with <span class="mark">the PDM or EasyPatch app. Enjoy and trust the process!</span>
