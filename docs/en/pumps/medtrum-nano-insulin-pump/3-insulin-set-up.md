## **<u>3. Insulin set-up</u>**

Welcome to the part where we set up your insulin delivery — don’t worry, it’s easier than it sounds! We’ll show you how to program your basal insulin delivery, set a temporary basal, suspending insulin, deliver an insulin bolus, and give you some simple tips to make it all go smoothly.

We’ll always first show you how to do it with the PDM, and then with the EasyPatch app.

### ***3.1 Programming Your Basal Insulin***

**If you’re using the PDM,** go to the Main Menu, and tap on ‘Settings,’ then ‘Insulin Pump,’ then ‘Basal Set-up.’ Tap Edit Basal,’ and Select ‘Standard’ to edit the pattern

Note: The active basal pattern is check-marked.

The edit screen appears. Tap +Add time segment to create and add a new segment. Enter the end time for this segment. Then tap Done. Your first time segment starts at midnight. The last basal segment always ends at 24:00 (or 12:00 pm).

Tap each section to type in the numbers. When you’re happy with them, tap ‘Save.’ If you need different settings at different times of the day, tap the plus icon to add more segments.

To Delete, select segment, slide to left on each and a delete button will appear. Tap Delete to delete the selected segment. Slide back to right to close Delete. The last segment cannot be deleted, and the starting time of all segments cannot be changed.

**If you’re using the EasyPatch app,** <span class="mark">to set up your basal rate on your EasyPatch app,</span>

<span class="mark">tap on the three lines in the top left hand corner to access the main menu. Tap on settings. Tap on insulin pump. Tap on edit basal pattern. Tap on Standard. You can add different time segments for the day depending on your need for different rates of insulin at different times. Tap add time segment to add a new time segment. You can adjust the insulin rate for each time segment by tapping on the blue number next to each time segment. Now tap done. If you want to delete a time segment, simply slide your finger across the time segment from right to left and then tap delete. Once you've completed the basal set-up, tap done in the top right hand corner of your smart device to save your basal rate.</span>

### ***3.2 Setting a Temporary Basal Rate*** 

Sometimes you might need a little more or less insulin — like during exercise or illness. To <span class="mark">do this, you can set a temporary basal.</span>

**If you’re using the PDM**<span class="mark">, to deliver a temporary basal rate on your pump, slide to unlock your PDM. Slide your finger from right to left to access the main menu. Tap on basal. Tap on temp basal. Decide if you are increasing or decreasing your basal rate. Tap on the blue number next to percent. Now select how much basal insulin you wish to deliver. Tap done. Tap on the blue number next to duration. Now select the time for how long you would like the temporary basal rate to run for. Tap done. Now tap next. The PDM will show you a breakdown of how the temporary basal will be delivered. Now slide to activate the temporary basal rate. The pump will now activate the delivery of the temporary basal and this will be indicated with a dark green circle on your home screen with the temporary basal rate below.</span>

**If you’re using the EasyPatch app**, <span class="mark">to deliver a temporary basal on your pump, tap on the three lines in the top left hand corner to access the main menu. Tap on insulin delivery. Tap on temp basal. Decide if you are increasing or decreasing your basal rate. Now select how much basal insulin you wish to deliver. Tap done. Tap on the blue number next to duration. Now select the time for how long you would like your temporary basal rate to run for. Tap done. Now tap next. Your smart device will now show you a breakdown of the percentage and length of the temporary basal. Now slide to activate the temporary basal rate. The pump will now activate the delivery of the temporary basal and this will be indicated with a dark green circle on your home screen with the temporary basal rate shown below.</span>

### ***3.3 Suspending insulin***

**If you’re using the PDM,** <span class="mark">to suspend the delivery of insulin on your pump, slide to unlock your PDM. Slide your finger from right to left to access the main menu. Click on suspend. Now you can select the amount of time you wish to suspend your pump for. This can be set anywhere from 15 minutes up to 2 hours with 15 minutes increments. Select done. Select next. Now slide to suspend the delivery of insulin. The pump will now suspend the delivery of insulin and this will be indicated with a red circle on your home screen with the suspend time remaining below. To resume the delivery of insulin on your pump, slide to unlock your PDM. Slide your finger from right to left to access the main menu. Click on resume. Now slide to resume the delivery of your basal insulin. The pump will now resume the delivery of your basal insulin and this will be indicated with the green circle on your home screen with your basal delivery below.</span>

**If you’re using the EasyP<span class="mark">atch app</span>**<span class="mark">, to suspend the delivery of insulin on your pump, tap on suspend at the bottom of your smart device. Now you can select the amount of time you wish to suspend your pump for. This can be set anywhere from 15 minutes up to 2 hours with 15 minutes increments. Select done. Select continue. Now slide to suspend the delivery of insulin. Now tap ok. The pump will now suspend the delivery of insulin and this will be indicated with a red circle on your home screen with the suspend time remaining below. To resume the delivery of insulin on your pump, tap on resume at the bottom of your smart device. Now slide to resume the delivery of your based insulin. The pump will now resume the delivery of your basal insulin and this will be indicated with a green circle on your home screen with your basal delivery below.</span>

### ***3.4 Delivering a bolus***

<span class="mark">With the TouchCare Nano pump, you can choose for a Normal bolus, Extended bolus, Combo bolus – whatever that works better for you. In the settings menu, you can also enable the bolus calculator. This feature allows you to set your carbohydrate ratio, insulin sensitivity, target glucose level, and the duration of insulin action, to make a more correct estimation of the insulin requirement for meals and corrections.</span>

**If you’re using the PDM, <span class="mark"></span>**<span class="mark">Slide to unlock your pdm. Slide your finger from right to left to access the main menu. Tap on bolus. Tap on bolus calculator enter your blood glucose by tapping the three dashes under bg. Now tap done. Enter your carbohydrates by tapping the zero on the carbs. Now tap done. Tap next. Tap next again. Now slides to deliver the bolus. To deliver a manual bonus on your pump, slide to unlock your pdm. Slide your finger from right to left to access the main menu. Tap on bolus. Tap on manual bolus. Enter the amount of insulin you wish to administer by tapping the three zeros to set bolus. Now tap done. Tap next. Now slide to deliver the bolus. The pump will now deliver your manual bolus.</span>

**If you’re using the EasyPatch app**, Tap the bolus button on your app. <span class="mark">Enter your blood glucose by tapping on the two dashes next to bg. Enter your carbohydrates by tapping on the zero next to carbs. Now tap done. Now tap next. Now tap next again. Now slide to deliver the bolus. The pump will now deliver your bolus. To deliver a manual bonus on your pump, tap on the three lines in the top left hand corner to access the main menu. Tap on insulin delivery. Tap on manual bolus. Enter the amount of insulin you wish to administer by tapping on the three zeros next to set. Now tap done. Now tap next. Now slide to deliver the bolus. The pump will now deliver your manual bolus.</span>

### ***3.5 Do’s & Don’ts***

- When you use an insulin pump, it’s always best to set your maximum basal and bolus delivery for safety. <span class="mark">On both the PDM and the EasyPatch app, this can be done via the settings. The maximum basal should be at least 2 times your highest current basal insulin delivery rate, and your max bolus should be set a little higher than your usual big</span>gest dose.

- The TouchCare Nano pump allows you to work with preset bolus amounts for Breakfast, lunch, dinner and snacks. These can be preset via the settings menu.

That's it! You may now confidently program, adjust, and administer insulin. Keep it up!
