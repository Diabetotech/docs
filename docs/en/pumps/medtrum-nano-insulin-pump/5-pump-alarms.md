## **<u>5. Pump alarms</u>**

Let’s talk about alarms and notifications on your TouchCare Nano Insulin Patch Pump — these are here to keep you safe and informed.

Like <span class="mark">in most insulin pumps, there are 3 types of notifications: alarms for high and medium pri</span>ority, alerts for low priority, and reminders.

### ***5.1 Alarms*** 

Alarms are triggered by serious or potentially serious conditions. You must respond to the alarm by taking appropriate action in order to clear the alarm condition. For example: When Alarm “PATCH EXPIRED” occurs, the Lock Screen and Alarm screen display the following screen.

- If it is a high priority alarm, the PDM or EasyPatch app will display an alarm message with instructions and a red triangle with three exclamation marks. The PDM or EasyPatch app will emit 10 beeps or vibrations and the patch pump will emit 3 beeps.

- If it is a medium priority alarm, the PDM will display an alarm message with instructions and a red triangle with two exclamation marks. The PDM or EasyPatch app will emit 3 beeps or vibrations and the patch pump will emit 3 beeps.

If a Pump alarm is not cleared within 10 minutes, both your PDM or EasyPatch app and Patch Pump will make a siren sound until the alarm is cleared.

### ***5.2 Alerts*** 

Alerts are triggered by conditions that may require your attention. Alerts are less

serious than alarms. You must respond to an alert by pressing buttons and/or taking actions.

For example: When Alert “PATCH BATTERY LOW” occurs, the Lock Screen and Alert screen display the following screen.

The PDM or EasyP<span class="mark">atch app displays an alert message with instructions and an empty triangle with an exclamation mark. The PDM or EasyPatch app can beep or vibrate 1 time every 3 minutes, and the</span> patch pump can beep 1 time.

### ***5.3 Reminders***

Reminding messages are automatically displayed to remind you of a condition, function or event. Reminding messages include the notifications you get after setting reminders and the low-priority reminding notifications. A Message requires you to press buttons to clear it and/or to take action if necessary.

<span class="mark">For example: When Message “BASAL RESUMED” occurs, the Lock Screen and Message screen display the following screen. Your PDM emits two beeps and/or one vibration every three minutes, three times in total.</span>

With the TouchCare Nano PDM or the EasyPatch app, you can set a specific reminder called the **Bolus Reminder**. For example, if you choose a 2-hour reminder, and you forget to deliver a bolus between two time points — let’s say time A and time B — you’ll automatically get a reminder at time B. It’s a helpful way to make sure you don’t miss your insulin dose.

### ***5.4 Do’s and don’ts***

- You can customize how you receive alerts. Go to the main menu, then tap on Settings, then General Settings, and finally Audio Options. Here, you can choose between sound, vibration, both, or none. If you set both Audio and Vibrate to "Off," your PDM won’t <span class="mark">make a sound or vibrate for most CGM alerts — except in urgent situations. For example, if your glucose drops below 56 mg/dL (or 3.1 mmol/L), or stays above 250 mg/dL (or 13.9 mmol/L), for more than 3 hours during auto mode, your PDM will vibrate once every three minutes; if you don’t clear the alert within nine minutes, a loud siren will sound until you acknowledge it. On the EasyPatch app, the urgent low or high alerts will repeat once every minute until cleared.</span>

- If you ever feel unsure, it’s totally okay to call customer service or check your user guide. There’s also a complete list of all alarms in the manual.

- If you use the EasyPatch app, you can turn on push notifications so alerts pop up right on your phone. Just check your app settings and make sure notifications are enabled.

- And if you ever need a break, the Snooze function within the CGM alerts lets you temporarily silence alerts — just make sure to check back in later.

- If you won’t be using your pump for a while, be sure to deactivate the patch pump and disconnect the pump base from your PDM or EasyPatch app.

Remember, alarms and alerts are there to help — not to stress you out. Now that you are aware of what to do, stay calm, follow the prompts, and reach out for help if needed. You’re doing great! In the event that the message is unclear, refer to the user manual or contact customer service. Good luck!!
