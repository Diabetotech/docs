## **<u>2. Pump operation</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on operating the DANA-i pump.

In this video, we’ll identify its parts, install the battery, and get familiar with the pump’s display screen and the AnyDANA mobile app. We'll also highlight essential do's and don'ts for using the DANA-i system. Let’s get started!

The DANA-i insulin pump kit includes:

- 1 insulin pump

- 1 Auto Setter

- 2 syringe caps

- 2 battery caps

- 2 linking screws

- 2 size ½ AA batteries, and

- 1 user manual.

Please note that this reflects earlier kits. The updated Auto Setter now uses one AAA battery, and the ½ AA batteries are no longer included in current versions.

### ***2.1 Parts of the pump***

The insulin pump consists of the reservoir cap, the battery cap, the reservoir window, the control panel, and the LCD screen.

The reservoir cap is where you insert the insulin reservoir and linking screw. To open it, turn a quarter-turn clockwise.

The battery cap houses the AAA battery and opens the same way — a quarter-turn clockwise.

The reservoir window lets you check the remaining insulin.

The control panel has four buttons for navigating menus, adjusting settings, and selecting functions. Press the Plus button (+) to increase values, the Minus button (-) to decrease values or return to the previous screen, the Right (▷) button to move to the next option, and OK to select or confirm an option.

The LCD screen displays the status of the pump, as well as system options and messages. It serves as the main user interface and lights automatically when you press any control-panel button.

### ***2.2 Installing the battery***

The DANA-i pump requires one AAA 1.5 V alkaline battery. To install it, open the battery cap by turning it clockwise 45 degrees. You can use a key or a coin for this purpose. Place the edge of the coin in the cap. Insert the battery with the positive (+) facing up and the negative (-) end into the pump. Replace the cap and lock it by turning 45 degrees counterclockwise. For reference, see the illustrations showing the cap in the locked and open positions.

### ***2.3 The LCD home screen and the main menu***

At the top of the LCD home screen, you'll find the current date and time. On the middle-left, a flashing B indicates basal delivery, while T indicates a temporary basal.

The current basal rate is shown with "u/h", which stands for "units per hour." Any value above or below 100% indicates a temporary basal is active; 100% reflects the normal basal delivery state.

The Battery Status appears in the lower-left.

The Button Lock icon appears at the bottom center when the LCD screen is locked. This function is especially useful for children unable to program their own pump or patients who are sleeping. Meanwhile, the remaining insulin volume in the lower right side shows the volume of insulin left in the reservoir.

Status messages that may include Extended Bolus, Dual Bolus, and No Delivery. The No Delivery status is displayed when the pump is not delivering insulin.

When a compatible mobile device is connected to the DANA-i pump, the Remote Control Mode symbol appears and the pump’s buttons are disabled. If you do not intend to connect, you can exit this mode by pressing the minus (-) button for more than 5 seconds.

In Airplane Mode, Bluetooth is disabled.

Additional symbols include Bolus Block, which prevents a repeat bolus during a preset block period; and Allowable Daily Max, which appears when the total daily dose is nearing the configured maximum. Remaining units when the total drops below 20 are displayed.

From the home screen, press OK to open the Main Menu. The Main Menu scrolls in a carousel through Review, Bolus, Suspend, Button Lock, Temporary Basal, Basal, Refill, and Options. After Options, it returns to Review. Use OK to select an item, the Right button to move to the next item, and the Minus button to go back to the previous one.

Here’s an overview of the DANA-i pump main menu and sub-menus. A full overview can be found in the download section.

### ***2.4 The AnyDANA app***

AnyDANA is the smartphone app that pairs with the DANA-i pump. AnyDANA-i is for iOS devices (with iOS 9 or later), while AnyDANA-A is for Android devices (with Android 4.3 or later). Download either from the App Store or Google Play.

Via Bluetooth, AnyDANA lets you configure and operate the pump on your phone’s larger display for added convenience. After installing and opening the app for the first time, allow all requested permissions and ensure Bluetooth is turned on. You may see an update notice before being prompted to create a password. Set one that has 9 to 12 characters and includes lowercase and uppercase letters, numbers, and at least one special character. For quicker access, enable biometric authentication.

The app will then let you search for your pump. Tap Search \[Device\] and wait for your serial number to appear; this may take a couple of minutes on the first connection. When it appears, tap it to begin pairing. The app will request a Pairing Key from the pump. On the pump screen, enter Pairing Key 1 first, then Pairing Key 2. (You’ll repeat this process whenever pairing with a new phone.) After a message confirms the connection is certified, tap Done to enter the AnyDANA app.

The home screen shows key details such as the connected pump’s serial number, pump battery status, remaining insulin, the previous bolus, today’s total insulin delivered, and any active insulin. Tapping the button in the top-right corner opens the main menu, where you’ll find quick access to both pump-specific and app-specific settings.

By tapping Review, you can also view your pump data history, which you can share anytime using the button in the top-right corner.

### ***2.5 Do’s & don’ts***

Keep the following important tips in mind when using the DANA-i pump:

- The pump is indicated for <span class="mark">short-acting insulin of 100 units per milliliter only. Other insulins have not been</span> tested and may be incompatible with the DANA-i pump.

- The warranty period is four years from the date of purchase and applies only to the original purchaser of the pump.

- The pump has an IP68 rating, meaning it is dust-tight and can be immersed in water up to 1.5 meters for 30 minutes. However, drops or impacts may allow moisture to enter the display. The pump may continue to function, but the screen can fail. Swimming with the pump is therefore not recommended. Showering generally does not cause problems.

- Avoid exposing the pump to strong electromagnetic fields or radiation sources, such as those generated by MRI, CT scans, or X-rays. The pump can emit radio-frequency energy, which may interfere with nearby medical devices.

- Use one AAA 1.5 V alkaline battery. <span class="mark">The manufacturer</span> recommends Duracell Gold or Energizer Advanced alkaline AAA batteries. Do not use carbon-zinc batteries, as they are incompatible with the pump. Lithium batteries are not recommended either because the battery-level indicator may be inaccurate.

- The battery life is approximately 3 to 4 weeks.

- Do not attempt to change the battery while a bolus is in progress.

- Remove the battery for long-term storage.

- The battery cap has an O-ring that seals the battery compartment. If the O-ring is damaged or missing, replace the battery cap.

- To conserve power, the screen blanks after one minute of inactivity. Press any button to wake the display and activate the backlight for approximately 10 seconds.

- Press the pump buttons with your finger pad. DO NOT use fingernails or any sharp objects.

- Clean the exterior of the insulin pump and accessories monthly. Wipe with a soft cloth moistened with water or a neutral-pH detergent, then dry with a clean cloth. Do not use thinner, alcohol, benzene, or similar solvents.

## **<u>3. Programming the pump</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on programming the DANA-i pump.

In this video, we’ll guide you through setting basal and temporary basal rates, delivering a bolus, suspending delivery, and programming settings through the anyDANA app. Plus, we’ll cover some best practices for adjusting the pump’s insulin settings. Let’s begin.

### ***3.1 Setting a basal rate***

The DANA-i pump lets you store adjusted basal rates in four profiles, A, B, C, and D. These profiles are useful for exercise, sick days, or other events that may affect your insulin sensitivity.

To edit basal rates, go to the MAIN MENU, select BASAL, choose EDIT BASAL, and press OK. The current Basal Profile is displayed; the default profile is A. Confirm with OK. You’ll be taken to the Edit A screen.

Let’s first identify the parts of the screen. The hourly basal-rate bar graph contains 24 bars, each representing one hour from 00 to 24 (12:00 a.m.). When you select a specific time period, that bar turns black.

The active basal profile letter appears in the title line. In the example, Profile A is selected.

<span class="mark">Total Daily Basal, or TDB, shows the total basal insulin to be delivered over 24 hours. In the example, TDB is 5.2 units.</span>

<span class="mark">Start and end times are shown for each hourly segment. The first number is the start of the hour, while the second one is the end.</span>

<span class="mark">Last is the basal rate. In the example, the basal rate between 18 and 19 (6:00–7:00 p.m.) is 0.3 u/h.</span>

Going back to setting basal rates, the Edit Basal screen opens with the default time 00-01. Use the plus and minus buttons to adjust the Start Time, then press Right to move to the End Time. Use Plus and Minus to adjust the End Time, then press Right again to move to the Basal Rate. Use Plus and Minus to set the Basal rate for the selected hour. Press OK to save the rate. A SETTING SAVED screen appears. Press OK to finish or Right to return to the Start Time and set the next hourly rate. When you save changes, a confirmation message indicates that the basal rate has been updated; press OK to confirm.

To view basal rates, go to Basal, select View Basal Rate, and press OK. The current Basal Profile letter is shown; confirm with OK. The cursor is positioned at the current time. Press Right to move to the next time value, or press Minus to exit.

### ***3.2 Setting a temporary basal rate***

The Temporary Basal Rate feature helps manage blood glucose during unexpected, short-term situations, such as exercise, illness, or stress. It lets you make temporary changes that automatically revert to your usual basal rates when the set period ends.

To set a Temporary Basal Rate, go to the Main Menu, select TEMPORARY BASAL, and press OK. Press Right to toggle between HR, which is the duration in hours, and % (the percentage of your usual basal). Use the Plus or Minus buttons to adjust the selected rate. For example, choosing 150% for 1 hour raises the basal to one-and-a-half times the regular rate for the next hour. Press OK to confirm.

Remember that a flashing T on the home screen indicates an active Temporary Basal Rate. To review details, open the Main Menu and select TEMPORARY BASAL. The TEMP. BASAL screen shows how long the Temporary Rate has been running, the time remaining, and the percentage selected. In the example, the Temporary Basal Rate is 150% for a total time of 1 hour, and there are 25 minutes remaining for this rate.

When a Temporary Basal Rate ends, the pump does not provide a notification. It automatically switches back to the programmed standard basal profile.

To stop a Temporary Basal Rate, select TEMPORARY BASAL from the main menu. When the TEMP. BASAL screen appears, press OK. Confirm STOP TEMP. RATE by pressing OK.

### ***3.3 Delivering a bolus***

The DANA-i pump can deliver a bolus as a Quick Bolus, or with the integrated bolus calculator. The main parameters of the bolus calculator are set in BOLUS SETTING, where you can also enable dual and extended boluses and define how the Quick Bolus function operates. The Quick Bolus can deliver insulin based on entered carbohydrates or fixed units, or let the user choose between both options. The glucose target (Ideal BG) is adjusted separately under USER OPTION, while the duration of insulin action is configured in Doctor Mode, discussed later.

To use the integrated bolus calculator, go to the Main Menu, select Bolus, then BG BOLUS CALCULATOR, and press OK. In the BOLUS CALCULATOR screen, set the parameters for this bolus. BG is the current blood glucose level, CARBO the grams of carbohydrate in a meal, IDEAL the target blood glucose, CIR the carb ratio, and CF the correction factor or insulin sensitivity. Press the Right button to move through fields and Plus or Minus to adjust values; press OK to confirm. The Review screen shows the suggested bolus dose. At the top of the screen, G indicates glucose, C indicates carbohydrates, and A indicates active insulin. Press OK to start bolus delivery.

After choosing either a Quick Bolus or a bolus with the integrated bolus calculator, the DANA-i pump can deliver three bolus types: Step Bolus, Extended Bolus, and Dual Pattern Bolus.

To use the Step or Quick Bolus option, open the Main Menu, select Bolus, then select BOLUS from the BOLUS Sub menu. Press OK. Adjust the grams of carbohydrate with Plus or Minus. Move down the menu using the Right button to adjust the carb ratio, then press OK for the next step. Keep in mind that if BOLUS CALCULATION is set to “UNIT”, this carbohydrate step is skipped. Use Plus or Minus to increase or decrease bolus volume or delivery speed. Press OK to confirm and start bolus delivery.

During delivery, the INSULIN INJECT screen is shown and you can hear the motor; the pump beeps or vibrates for every 1.0 unit delivered. When the bolus completes, a DELIVERED BOLUS message displays the amount. Press OK to return to the initial screen.

To stop delivery mid-bolus, press the Minus button, and confirm STOP with OK. The DELIVERED BOLUS message then shows the amount delivered before stopping.

### ***3.4 Suspending delivery***

The Suspend function stops all insulin delivery, including Basal and Bolus. To activate it, select SUSPEND from the MAIN MENU. The initial screen will then alternate between SUSPEND and NO DELIVERY. While suspended, the pump sounds an alarm every 4 minutes to remind you that no insulin is being delivered.

To restart delivery, select SUSPEND OFF from the MAIN MENU and confirm with OK. The pump will issue an alarm, and the basal-active status will reappear on the home screen.

### ***3.5 Programming settings through the AnyDANA app***

Now that you’ve seen how to program settings on the pump itself, let’s switch to the AnyDANA app, which lets your phone act as a remote control.

In the Pump Settings, open Bolus Settings to adjust your ratios. Tap a ratio to edit the value; when you’re done, tap the checkmark in the top-right corner to save. You can also turn Extended Bolus on or off and enable Missed Bolus Alerts, which you can tailor to your schedule and preferences. Remember to tap the checkmark to save any changes.

From the same menu, open Basal Settings to view, switch, and edit your basal profiles. You can review rates hour by hour, tap Edit to change individual hours, or select multiple hour blocks to make larger adjustments at once. When finished, tap the checkmark on the upper right and Yes to save.

Back on the Home screen, select Bolus to access the two calculators and a shortcut to Bolus Settings. The Carb Calculator is ideal for snacks or top-up carbs, and you can choose among Step, Extended, or Dual Pattern delivery. In a Step Bolus, you can adjust both delivery speed and insulin amount, and you can start delivery from the pump and stop it instantly at any time. After delivery, the Home screen updates to show your pre-bolus and Active Insulin.

For corrections, use the BG Bolus Calculator to calculate a correction dose and a carb dose together. You can set custom targets and edit ratios directly within the calculator when needed. At the bottom of the screen, you’ll see the calculated dose breakdown and the app’s suggestion; tap Continue and choose your preferred delivery type. With a Dual Pattern Bolus, decide how much to give immediately and how much to extend over time for up to eight hours. You can interrupt delivery at any point, and the Home screen will indicate that a Dual Pattern bolus is in progress.

Temporary Basal is quick to set: choose a percentage from 0 to 200% and a duration up to 24 hours. The Home screen will show both the percentage and a Temporary Basal icon. To cancel, return to Temporary Basal and select Cancel. You can also Suspend the pump from within the app; the Home screen will confirm when the pump is in suspend mode.

### 3.6 Do's & don’ts

The following guidelines and cautions apply to setting insulin delivery rates with the DANA-i pump.

### Doctor Mode or Professional Mode settings on the DANA-i insulin pump need to be configured when starting a new pump, as the device ships with factory default values for all safety parameters, including basal and bolus limits. These advanced settings are typically adjusted by a healthcare professional or certified pump trainer before the pump is handed over for patient use, to personalize the device according to the individual's insulin needs and safety requirements. Here’s how to do this:

> First, we need to know the password. To determine what it is, we need to press OK to head into the Main Menu, OK, and Review, across to Shipping Information, and OK again. Here, number three will tell us the date of manufacture, which informs the Doctor Mode password. 16th of October 2020, in this example. Press OK to leave, Minus, and Minus again to head back to the home screen. To bring up the password selection box, you must press and hold the Right arrow and Plus and Minus all at the same time while looking at the home screen. A password box should appear. The password will be 3-0 followed by the day of manufacture, in our case 16.
>
> Pressing OK should take you through to Doctor Mode. Much like User Options, Doctor Mode is a checklist. Right moves you down the list, Plus and Minus are your edit buttons, and OK will allow us to exit when we reach Option 15.
>
> Number 1 is Preset Bolus. Preset Bolus should remain off for the most part. This removes the bolus calculator and instead provides you options for set times and sizes of boluses.
>
> Melody is your “Check Glucose” alarm. You may set a time in 10-minute increments, whereby that increment post-bolus, an alarm will go off asking you to check your glucose. If you would not like any “Check Glucose” alarms, make sure your Melody is set to 0.
>
> Number 3 is Bolus Block. This allows you to set a limit, again in 10-minute increments, on how frequently you can deliver boluses. This is designed for people that might accidentally bolus twice back to back, forgetting that they’ve just bolussed.
>
> Number 4 is Bolus Increments, the increments that we can move in when calculating a bolus. For the most part, we’d set this to the minimum, which is 0.05.
>
> Number 5 is Basal Increments. Again, for the most part, we’d set this to the minimum, 0.01.
>
> Number 6 is your Ideal Blood Glucose. We can change this inside of User Options, but we can also change it inside of Doctor Mode.
>
> Number 7 shows you your Active Insulin. This is just a value for reference so that you can see what effect changing number 8, your Decreasing Ratio, has on your active insulin.
>
> Number 8 is Decreasing Ratio. This is how we express duration of action, and we show it as a percentage. A Decreasing Ratio of 20% means that 20% of your bolus insulin is used each hour post-bolus, giving your bolus a 5-hour duration of action. A 25% Decreasing Ratio would be equal to a 4-hour duration of action.
>
> Number 9 is Basal Max. This is the most amount of basal insulin the pump will allow you to deliver inside of an hour. This includes temporary basal rate changes.
>
> Number 10 is Bolus Max. This is the largest bolus the pump will allow you to deliver in a single go.
>
> Number 11 is Daily Max. This is the daily total maximum of insulin — both bolus and basal — the pump will allow you to deliver.
>
> Number 12, Safety Ratio, is a nuanced safety feature whereby it looks at the three-day average of bolus size, and you may set a ratio whereby should you choose to deliver a bolus, it will check to see whether it’s that ratio or higher than the average. Should it be higher than the average, it will prevent the bolus from going through. For the most part, we leave this off.
>
> Number 13 is Block Sensitivity, or your occlusion alarm sensitivity. We have low (L), medium (M), and high (H) settings. For the most part, we’d recommend setting this to M, although certain closed-loop systems may have different requirements.
>
> Number 14 allows you to change your UTC time zone settings.
>
> Number 15 is Exit. Press OK on Exit, and OK to save your settings, and Doctor Mode has been programmed.

### Setting the correct date and time is essential for accurate basal insulin delivery and for maintaining accurate records of all insulin doses. The date and time themselves can only be adjusted in Doctor Mode, where Greenwich Mean Time, or UTC, must be entered. In the Main Menu under Options and then Clock Settings, you can change the time zone, for example time zone 1 or 2 for standard time and daylight saving time. 

- If you want to know your current active insulin, you can go to the Main Menu, select Review, and choose More Information.

## **<u>4. Reservoir and infusion set replacement</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on replacing the DANA-i pump reservoir and infusion set.

In this video, you’ll learn the necessary supplies and step-by-step process for loading and refilling the DANA-i pump with insulin. We’ll then cover infusion-set specifics — namely connecting the set to the pump, priming the tubing, and inserting the set into the body — before wrapping up with key reminders for performing these procedures properly. Let’s dive in.

### ***4.1 Different infusion sets***

The DANA infusion sets are designed for subcutaneous insulin delivery with SOOIL pumps, including the DANA-i Pump. Available options include Soft Release O, Soft Release ST, Soft-Release Micro Steel, EasyRelease, and DANA Inset II. You can find an overview of the specifics of each infusion set in the download section. Availability of specific infusion sets may vary depending on your region.

### ***4.2 Supplies***

Gather the following materials and place them on a clean dry surface:

- Your DANA-i insulin pump

- Insulin at room temperature

- 1 Alcohol swab

- 1 DANA 3 ml syringe and needle

- 1 Linking screw

- 1 DANA Auto Setter

- And 1 DANA infusion set

Remember to wash and dry your hands before opening any sterile packets and starting the filling process.

### ***4.3 Reservoir replacement***

Firstly, make sure your linking screw is moving up and down the thread smoothly before setting it to the top. The blue top hat should be flush with the head of the linking screw.

Next, remove the round cap at the back end of the syringe and discard it. Take off the small white reservoir cap from the plunger and keep it for later use.

There are teeth all around the base of the syringe. There are two corresponding teeth on either side of the linking screw. Clip the linking screw into the reservoir, making sure the teeth make a proper connection. Push the plunger up and down 2 to 3 times to lubricate the reservoir.

Now, decide on how much insulin you would like to fill and set your plunger to that length. Each milliliter is 100 units, and each of the short markers is 20 units.

Clean the insulin vial’s rubber lid with an alcohol swab. Carefully remove the clear protective needle cover, insert the needle into the vial, inject air from the reservoir, then draw the desired amount of insulin. Pull the plunger until the reservoir volume matches the required volume.

Afterwards, replace the needle cover. Gently tap the reservoir so any air bubbles rise to the top, then press the plunger gently to expel the air from the reservoir.

Hold the syringe as vertically as possible and gather all the air at the top. Push all the air out until you only have insulin filling the neck; you should see a smooth dome of insulin at the top. Retrieve the white reservoir cap from earlier and place it gently on top, just enough so that it grips. Avoid pressing the cap too hard. Doing so can force air back into the neck, which you’ll need to remove before priming.

With the reservoir filled with insulin, you're ready to adjust the length of the linking screw. This can be done by hand. Wind the linking screw until the black O-ring that you can see on the head of the linking screw is parallel to this point of overlap between the top and bottom half of the cartridge.

Alternatively, you can use the DANA Auto Setter to set the linking-screw length. Loosen the linking screw until the shaft-cap portion is covered by the head (blue) portion. This step is critical for accurate length adjustment. Insert the insulin-filled reservoir into the Auto Setter and turn it on by pressing the button; ensure the hexagonal end of the linking screw engages the Auto Setter’s hexagonal socket. Dashed lines leading into a “3” means the Auto Setter is ready. Press the button again to start measurement. The Auto Setter will first wind the reservoir out before withdrawing it in while counting the number of units on the screen. Have your insulin pump ready as when the Auto Setter finishes, it will send the volume in units to your pump via Bluetooth. When the Confirm Refill Volume screen appears on the pump, press OK. The pump will then prompt you to insert the reservoir, connect the infusion set, and keep the pump upright during priming.

At this point, you can unlock the reservoir cap, remove it, and insert the reservoir. Replace the cap, remove the white cover, and lock the cap — you should hear and feel a click. While inserting, rotate the reservoir 45° until the side notch slides into place, then let the reservoir and linking screw fall into place gently. Align the reservoir cap in a straight line with the guide mark and turn it 45° counterclockwise to lock.

When it’s time to reload the pump, remove the old reservoir first. Disconnect the infusion site from the pump tubing before opening the pump to prevent unintended insulin delivery. Open the pump's reservoir compartment by turning the reservoir cap a quarter-turn clockwise, then lift out the old reservoir. While holding the reservoir barrel, firmly pull the linking screw out of the reservoir.

### ***4.4 Filling the tube***

Attach the infusion-set tubing by turning counter-clockwise until firmly seated. Hold the pump upside down while connecting the tubing to prevent insulin from leaking into the pump.

On the pump, go to Refill in the main menu. Select Prime, and press OK. Once more, press OK to start. The screen will display reminders for priming.

Press OK twice to start Tube Prime. Make sure that your insulin pump is facing upright so the insulin and air bubbles rise easily to the surface. During priming, the pump may beep or vibrate after every unit of insulin primed. Press Minus to check whether insulin droplets appear at the end of the tube. Inspect the entire length of the tubing for bubbles. If you find any, press OK to re-start priming. Otherwise, press Minus again to finish the process. Keep in mind that droplets of insulin must be clearly visible at the tube or needle tip before stopping the prime. Unless you stop it earlier, priming automatically stops after 25 units.

### ***4.5 Infusion set placement***

The recommended insertion sites are shown. Rotate sites — ideally among four separate palm-sized areas of the body — to minimize skin damage and allow healing. Avoid recent insertion sites, scars, scar tissue, and bruised areas.

We will demonstrate how to insert the **Inset II infusion set**. For other infusion sets, follow the instructions provided in their packaging or refer to online tutorials.

The inset™ infusion set has a soft cannula inserted at a 90-degree angle, and comes with an integrated insertion device called the serter. The serter is an incorporated part of the product and contains the set and tubing.

Here you can see the parts of the infusion set: disconnect cover, sterile paper, insertion device, introducer needle, lined indentations (press here when loading), round indentations (press here when releasing), tubing, adhesive, soft cannula, cannula housing, connector needle, needle guard, lid, three raised dots (press here when lifting the lid off).

***Preparation***

Before you begin, check the tamperproof seal and sterile paper. Do not use if the seal or paper have been broken. Wash your hands before inserting the infusion set, and choose an insertion site as recommended by your healthcare provider. Clean the area for insertion with a disinfectant as directed by your health care provider. Be sure the area is air-dried before inserting the infusion set.

***Preparing the serter***

Pull the red tape to remove the tamperproof seal. Remove the sterile paper. To remove the lid, firmly press the three raised dots on each side while lifting the lid up. Be careful not to bend or touch the introducer needle when preparing the serter and infusion set. The needle should come placed securely in the serter: pointing straight, not at an angle or loose. If it is not straight or if it is loose and insertion is attempted, pain or minor injury may occur. To avoid this, please ensure that the following steps are done gently so the infusion set is not loosened or removed by accident.

Unwind the tubing by carefully unlocking the tubing from the slot. Now unwind the tubing from the serter by pulling it carefully upwards. Be careful not to touch the introducer needle while unwinding the tubing or pull too hard when the tubing is fully released, as this could accidentally remove the infusion set from the serter. Connect the infusion set to the pump. Before attaching the set to your body, fill the infusion set until insulin dispenses from the tip.

- Ensure there are no air bubbles remaining in the tubing.

- Never try to fill or free clogged tubing while the tubing is attached to the infusion set which has been inserted on the body. This may result in an unknown amount of insulin.

- Consult your pump manufacturer’s instructions for use for further details about pump connection and filling of the infusion set.

When filling the infusion set tubing, hold the infusion set with the needle pointing down to ensure that insulin does not get in contact with the adhesive backing paper. Pull up gently on the white tab to remove the adhesive backing paper. Place your fingers on the lined indentations on each side. Pull the spring back firmly until you hear a click. Carefully remove the needle guard by gently twisting and then pulling it up, taking care not to touch the needle itself. Check that the soft cannula does not exceed the introducer needle.

***Inserting***

Before positioning the inset™ II infusion set over a clean insertion site, place the tubing in the slot to ensure that the tubing is not caught underneath the infusion set during insertion. Insert the inset™ II infusion set by positioning it on the clean insertion site. Simultaneously, press the round indentations on each side of the serter to insert the inset™ II infusion set. Push gently on the center of the serter to secure the adhesive on the skin. Remove the serter and introduce a needle by grasping the center and pulling it gently back. Gently massage the adhesive tape onto the skin. Fill the empty space in the cannula. Push the lid back in place until you hear a click.

***Disconnecting***

The inset™ II infusion set allows you to temporarily disconnect your pump without having to change the infusion set. Hold the adhesive tape in place by placing a finger just in front of the cannula housing and gently squeeze the sides of the connector. Pull the connector needle straight out from the cannula housing.If preferred, insert the disconnect cover into the cannula housing until you hear a click.

***Reconnecting***

Remove the disconnect cover from the cannula housing. Ensure the flow by filling the tubing until insulin dispenses from the needle. When filling the tubing, hold the connector needle pointing down. Place a finger on the adhesive tape just in front of the cannula housing and push the connector needles straight in until you hear a click. Now you’re ready to restart your insulin delivery.

### 4.6 Filling the cannula

<span class="mark">When you fill the tubing of the Inset infusion set, the cannula is filled automatically. If you ever replace only the infusion set but keep the existing tubing, you should manually fill the cannula. This can be done through the same pump menu.</span>

<span class="mark">Go to the Main Menu on the pump’s LCD screen, select REFILL and press OK. Choose PRIME CANN</span>ULA from the sub menu and press OK. Verify that the PRIME CANNULA VOLUME is correct and press OK to confirm. Press OK again to start the process; the PRIME CANNULA window will display the delivery.

### ***4.***7 ***Do’s & don’ts***

### Take note of these tips and guidelines for reservoir and infusion-set procedures.

- Always verify your glucose level 2 hours after inserting a new infusion set. This is the only way to confirm proper infusion set insertion and insulin delivery.

- Do not push or force the reservoir into the pump, as this can damage the pump or force insulin out. If repeated insertion attempts fail, use a new reservoir.

- Disconnect the pump from both the infusion set and your body before starting any part of the refill procedure, as remaining connected can lead to unintended insulin delivery.

- The linking screw is part of the pump and is reused. Keep this part at every refill.

- Do not reuse an old reservoir or infusion set.

- Do not use an infusion set if the package is damaged, inadvertently opened, or wet.

- PRIME is essential to ensure accurate delivery, as air in the tube commonly causes delivery problems and occlusion alarms. Users must understand how to prime correctly and why it matters.

- Never start a tube prime while the infusion set is connected to the body.

- If the prime amount is less than 7 U, an alarm will display because infusion sets used with the DANA-i require a minimum prime of 7 U.

- A successful tube prime with visible insulin droplets at the tube tip confirms proper engagement of the linking screw with the motor gear. If no insulin appears after more than one prime, readjust the linking screw length. Contact your healthcare provider or pump trainer if this occurs frequently.

- After insertion of the infusion set, the site should not feel uncomfortable to the touch.

- The cannula fill volume is set in the USER OPTION menu and can be adjusted between 0.1 and 0.9 units. Follow the cannula instructions to determine individual fill requirements.

- <span class="mark">The Release infusion sets of the DANA-i pump are connected and disconnected differently than other pump infusion sets. To disconnect the Release infusion set, gently push the side ridges to release it. To reconnect, click the tubing cap onto the cannula base. Always make sure the tubing cap is securely in place by rotating the tube and pulling the cap upwards.</span>

## **<u>5. Pump alarms</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on alarms, errors, alerts, and reminders with the DANA-i pump.

In this video, we’ll differentiate among these notifications, outline the steps for changing alarm settings, and share practical do’s and don’ts. Let’s jump right in!

The DANA-i pump uses visual, sound, and vibration cues to signal alarms, errors, and alerts.

You can choose SOUND, VIBRATION, or BOTH for pump alarms and alerts. To do this, open the Main Menu, select Option, then User Option, and scroll to ALARM. But keep in mind that for safety, alarms will sound even if VIBRATION is selected.

### ***5.1 Alarms***

<span class="mark">Alarms are safety-critical messages that require immediate attention. They will sound even if VIBRATION is selected. There are 10 alarms, including those for LOW BATTERY, EMPTY RESERVOIR, SHUTDOWN, OCCLUSION, CHECK ERROR, SYSTEM ERROR, PRIME INCOMPLETE, BASAL MAX, and DAILY MAX. To silence an alarm, press any button, then take the appropriate corrective action right away.</span>

### ***5.2 Errors***

Errors indicate a problem with the pump but are less urgent than alarms. They include LOW RESERVOIR, SUSPEND, or DELIVERY LESS THAN BASAL SET RATE.

Regarding LOW RESERVOIR, this error depends on the Low Reservoir threshold set in User Option. Until you refill the pump, the Low Reservoir alert repeats every hour when more than 20 units remain, and every 30 minutes when 20 units or fewer are left.

### ***5.3 Alerts***

Alerts inform you about pump status or prompt a decision. They include MISSED BOLUS, PRIME AMOUNT IS NOT ENOUGH, CHECK GLUCOSE, CONFIRM PAIRING, and NO DELIVERY.

MISSED BOLUS 1 to 4 are reminders for regular meal boluses. When turned ON, you can set a time window; if no bolus is delivered in that window, an alarm reminds you of a missed bolus. To disable the MISSED BOLUS setting, set it to OFF.

For full descriptions and step-by-step responses to all alarms, errors, and alerts, please refer to the DANA-i pump user manual.

### ***5.***4 ***Do’s & don’ts***

Let's go over some important reminders about the DANA-i pump's alarm system.

- Always keep spare batteries on hand so you can respond immediately to a LOW BATTERY alarm.

- In case of an EMPTY RESERVOIR or NO DELIVERY alarm, the pump cannot deliver basal or bolus insulin and you will have no access to any delivery function until the issue is fixed.

- Even after clearing an OCCLUSION alarm, check your blood glucose to confirm that insulin delivery has fully resumed.

- CHECK ERROR and SYSTEM ERROR stop all delivery. After restarting the pump, check if it delivers insulin. If the alarm persists, contact your local insulin-pump technical support.

- Regularly verify that the display turns on, audible beeps can be heard, and vibration can be felt. If any of these functions are not working, discontinue pump use and contact your healthcare provider or your local insulin-pump distributor’s technical support.
