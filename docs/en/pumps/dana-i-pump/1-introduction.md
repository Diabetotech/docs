## **<u>1. Introduction</u>**

Welcome to this module on the DANA-i Pump! In this series of videos, we’ll explore the workings of one of the smallest and lightest insulin pumps in the world.

Released in 2020, the DANA-i insulin pump is the successor to the DANA RS — the world’s first insulin pump to make use of a patient’s own smartphone as a fully-fledged remote control. Measuring 97 × 47 × 22 mm and weighing 86 g (including the battery), the DANA-i is about half the weight of a standard insulin pump. This compact de<span class="mark">vice has a 300-unit reservoir and is indicated for subcutaneous delivery of short-acting 100 units per milliliter insulin in individuals with diabetes aged 1 year and up. The DANA-i utili</span>zes secure Bluetooth Low Energy connectivity for full, discreet control through the AnyDANA app. As an Alternate Controller Enabled (ACE) infusion pump, it can be used on its own or with digitally connected medical devices for closed-loop therapy. It is compatible with Diabeloop and CamAPS FX, depending on your region, and several open-source AID systems.

In our Master Module on insulin pumps, we covered general information about these devices, including how to set basal and bolus rates, change reservoir and infusion sets, and configure system alarms.

This module will provide comprehensive start-up training for the DANA-i Pump, plus advanced tips and tricks for healthcare providers and diabetes patients alike. However, please note that these videos are not intended as medical advice. Make it a point to consult with your healthcare provider before making any adjustments to your therapy.

These videos were produced by Dr. Inge Van Boxelaer, the founder of Diabetotech and an endocrinologist at the St-Lu<span class="mark">cas Hospital in Ghent, in Belgium. The content was reviewed by Dr. Liesbeth Van Huffel, an endocrinologist in the AZORG Hospital in Aalst in Belgium. Their expertise and perspectives ensure that you receive honest opinions about the DANA-i Pump.</span>

Be assured, all the information in this module is completely independent from the manufacturer of the DANA-i insulin pump, ensuring a genuine perspective on the pros and cons of the device.

By the end of these videos, you’ll be able to:

- identify the parts of the DANA-i insulin pump and operate the device;

- program the pump’s insulin settings;

- replace the DANA-i reservoir and infusion sets; and

- respond to DANA-i system alarms.

As a healthcare provider, you will acquire the knowledge to confidently support DANA-i users; as a user, you will gain key insights for optimizing your experience with the device. We wish you all the best as you get started with the DANA-i pump!
