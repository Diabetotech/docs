## **<u>2. Pump Operation</u>**

Welcome to this video on how the Omnipod DASH insulin pump works! In this segment, we will explore the parts and buttons of the Pod and PDM, how to charge and unlock the PDM, the home screen and pump menu of the PDM, and some do's and don'ts.

### ***2.1 Parts & buttons*** 

The Omnipod DASH insulin pump consists of two main components: the Pod and the Personal Diabetes Manager (PDM). The Pod is a patch pump that needs to be replaced every 3 days, while the PDM allows you to control the Pod.

The PDM should be kept with you at all times, as it is not only used to deliver boluses but also to respond to Pod alarms. The PDM communicates with the Pod via Bluetooth and needs to be within a range of 1.5 meters (5 feet) to establish a connection. Once programmed, the Pod does not require continuous communication with the PDM for basal insulin delivery.

At the back of Omnipod DASH Pods there is a blue needle cap and a hole for insulin injection using the provided syringe and needle.

The Omnipod DASH PDM has various buttons and ports:

- On the side, you will find the power button and a button for adjusting sound or vibration settings.

- The front of the PDM features a large color touchscreen.

- At the bottom, three buttons are present, with the left button serving as the previous screen button. The other buttons don’t have a function.

- The top of the PDM has a micro USB port for charging and data transfer purposes.

- The back of the PDM houses a rechargeable lithium battery, like a regular mobile phone.

### ***2.2 Charging the PDM***

It is recommended to charge the PDM using the cable and charger provided with the device. Charging the battery every day while you sleep is ideal, as it ensures a fully charged battery for approximately 2 days of usage. Charging the battery to 80% or more takes a maximum of 1.5 hours.

When the battery level is low, the PDM conserves power by disabling vibrations and sounds. If the battery completely drains, the PDM will turn off, but the Pod will continue administering basal insulin. You can continue using the PDM while it is charging.

### ***2.3 Unlocking the PDM***

The PDM is locked with a PIN code, requiring you to enter it before accessing certain functions or delivering boluses. You can customize the security message and background on the lock screen. If you forget the PIN code, you can use the last 4 digits of the PDM's serial number as an alternative PIN code. The serial number can be found on the back of the PDM.

### ***2.4 Home screen and pump menu***

Key elements on the home screen include:

- A status bar at the top displaying the remaining insulin, battery status, and PDM time.

- A hamburger menu, date, settings icon, and an icon for alarms and notifications.

- Three tabs in the center: Dashboard, Basal, and Pod Information. You can swipe or tap to navigate between the tabs.

  - The Dashboard tab shows a welcome message or displays insulin on board if the bolus calculator is enabled.

  - The Basal tab indicates the active basal profile and the total insulin dose for that profile. If a temporary basal is active, the tab turns green and displays "Temporary Basal."

  - The Pod Information tab shows the remaining insulin in the Pod and provides information on when it needs to be replaced.

- Information about the last bolus given and the last glucose measurement is displayed below the tabs.

- At the bottom of the screen, there is the bolus button.

When you click on the menu, you can set a Temp Basal, see Pod information, enter a Blood Glucose, Suspend your insulin, access your Basal Programs, change your PDM and Bolus Settings, and find information about your PDM version and serial number.

During operation, the PDM shows confirmations at the bottom of the screen through a green banner, which disappears after five seconds. You can swipe the banner to the right to close it earlier.

### ***2.5 Do's & don’ts***

Here are some important do's and don'ts when using the Omnipod DASH insulin pump:

- Save the menu overview provided in the downloads for easy reference. As a healthcare provider, it will help you guide individuals through the pump menu remotely.

- Omnipod DASH Pods are waterproof up to 7.6 meters or 25 feet for up to 1 hour.

- The PDM is not waterproof, so avoid exposing it to water.

- Take good care of the PDM, as it should last for approximately 4 years. The starter pack includes a free black silicone case for added protection, but you can also buy other colors online. It is also recommended to use a screen protector for the PDM.

- The PDM does not automatically adjust the time, date, and time zone. You need to manually update the time when transitioning between winter and summer time or traveling to different time zones. This can be done in the settings under the PDM device.

- Additionally, adjusting screen brightness and timeout settings can help conserve battery life.

With this information, you now have a better understanding of the main features of the Omnipod DASH system. One of the key advantages is the convenience of a patch pump without the hassle of catheters. You won't have to worry about getting stuck, and the Pods are barely noticeable when worn. However, keep in mind that you will need to carry both the PDM and the Pod, which can be a minor inconvenience. Knowing this in advance will help you adjust and adapt. Overall, the PDM's size and weight are manageable and shouldn't cause significant issues.

## **<u>3. Insulin setup</u>**

In this video, we will guide you through the process of setting the insulin delivery on the Omnipod DASH PDM.

Please note that the insulin will only start running once you have successfully paired a Pod with your PDM.

We will cover the following topics:

- Programming basal insulin

- Setting up temporary basal rates

- Giving an insulin bolus

- and some extra do’s and don'ts.

Let's get started!

### ***3.1 Programming basal insulin***

When you initially set up the PDM, you will be prompted to enter a basal insulin rate.

To edit an existing basal program, tap the Basal tab on the home screen, then select "View and edit." Pause the insulin delivery for a moment, and you can now edit the program's name and add labels if necessary. Tap Next to proceed. To modify an existing basal segment, tap that segment and adjust the end time or basal rate. To add a new basal segment, tap the row with the start time of the new segment. We recommend writing down your complete basal program before adding a new segment. Enter the end time of the previous segment as the start time of the new segment. Continue this process until you have completed the 24-hour basal program. To delete a basal segment, tap the segment preceding the one you wish to remove and enter the end time for that segment. The modified basal program will be saved, and you can tap "Resume insulin" to proceed.

To create a new basal program, tap the "Display" button in the Basal tab, then select "Create New" at the bottom. Enter a name for the program and add labels if needed. Tap Next to proceed and set the end time and basal rate for each time segment. Repeat this process until the last segment ends at midnight. Review the basal program for accuracy and tap "Save" to confirm.

To interrupt insulin delivery temporarily, go to the menu and tap "Suspend Insulin." You can set the desired duration, ranging from 30 minutes to 3 hours. A yellow banner will appear on the home screen, indicating that insulin delivery has been interrupted. During this interruption, the Pod will beep every 15 minutes. Once the duration expires, you will be prompted to resume insulin delivery. Insulin will not resume automatically, and both the Pod and PDM will continue beeping every 15 minutes until you choose to resume.

### ***3.2 Temporary basal*** 

You can only set a temporary basal if this option is enabled. To do this, access the menu, tap "Settings," then select "Basal" and "Temporary Basal." By default, the temporary basal rate is set as a percentage change. If you prefer an absolute speed in units per hour, you can modify this setting here.

To set a temporary basal rate, tap "Set Temp Basal" under the menu. Enter the desired basal rate and duration, ranging from 30 minutes to 12 hours. Confirm and activate the temporary basal rate.

To expedite the process, you can create presets for temporary basal rates. Up to 12 presets can be defined. To create a preset, tap "Preset Temporary Basal Speed" in the menu, then select "Create New." Enter a name and label if desired, followed by the basal rate and duration. Save the preset, and it will be accessible under the "Temporary Basal" option.

### ***3.3 Delivering a bolus***

During the PDM setup, you will be required to configure the bolus calculator. These settings can be checked and changed via the menu, if you navigate to the Bolus Settin<span class="mark">gs. He</span>re you can see the settings for the Target BG, Correct Above value, Minimal BG for calculations, Insulin to Carb Ratio, Correction Factor (which is the insulin sensitivity), Reverse Correction and Duration of Insulin Action.

Specific for Omnipod is the Correct Above and Minimal BG value, which is the value above and below which a correction bolus will be calculated. If Reverse Correction is toggled on, the bolus calculator will also calculate a negative correction bolus if the blood glucose is below the target value.

To deliver a bolus, tap the bolus button on the PDM.

- Enter the carbs you are going to eat, for example 15 grams, and enter your blood glucose, for example 100 mg/dl or 5.5 mmol/l.

- You can also add labels to the blood glucose measurement by selecting one of the options.

- The calculator will suggest meal and correction boluses based on the entered values. Confirm and start the bolus.

- A progress bar will display during bolus delivery, and you have the option to cancel the bolus if needed.

To give an extended bolus, follow the same steps as for a normal bolus, but instead of confirming, choose "Extend Bolus." Enter the percentage of the meal bolus to be delivered immediately in the "Now" field. Specify the duration for the extended portion of the bolus, then confirm and start.

For a fixed bolus, just enter the desired number of units in the "Total Bolus" field, then tap on Confirm

***3.4 Do's & don’ts***

Here are some do's and don'ts to keep in mind:

- Ensure maximum bolus limits are set slightly above expected bolus doses. You can set this between 0 and 30 units. 

- In the bolus calculator settings, you can configure the minimum blood glucose for calculations and enable reverse correction.

  - The minimum blood glucose for calculations ensures that an insulin bolus is not given when the glucose level falls below a certain threshold for safety reasons. This can typically be set between 50-70 mg/dL or 2.8-3.9 mmol/l, with 60 mg/dL or 3.3 mmol/l being a common value.

  - Reverse correction determines whether the bolus calculator reduces the suggested insulin bolus when the glucose level is below the target value. It is generally recommended to have this feature enabled.

- When setting up the PDM, you will be asked when you want to be reminded that your Pod is nearing empty and expiration. You can customize this reminder to suit your preferences, such as setting it at 10 units for the remaining insulin, and 4 hours before expiration of the Pod..

In conclusion, setting the insulin delivery on the Omnipod DASH is a straightforward and intuitive process. For a visual demonstration of each step, you can find helpful videos on YouTube. As a healthcare provider, it is crucial to familiarize yourself with the different pumps and their settings. With the Omnipod DASH, you can easily access all the necessary settings under the Basal tab and the Menu button.

## **<u>4. Replacing the Pod</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on how to replace the Pod on your Omnipod DASH insulin pump!

In just 4 easy steps, we'll guide you through the process of changing a Pod. We'll cover everything from filling the Pod to linking it with the PDM, applying the Pod to your body, and finally, initiating it.

Get ready to dive into the nitty-gritty, and we'll also share some essential do's and don'ts along the way.

### ***<span class="mark">1) Pod filling and replacement</span>***

To insert a Pod, gather the following supplies: your PDM, a new Pod, alcohol wipes, and insulin at room temperature.

***\#1 Filling the Pod***

On the PDM, navigate to Pod Info and select "Set New Pod." If you already have an active Pod, tap "Show Pod Data" first, and then tap "Replace Pod." Deactivation takes a few seconds. Once the green banner confirms deactivation, you can remove the Pod. The PDM is now ready to activate a new Pod.

The PDM will guide you through the process of filling a new Pod. Start by opening a sealed Pod, preferably keeping it in its holder during preparation and filling. Use an alcohol wipe to clean the top of your insulin vial. Attach the needle to the syringe and remove the protective cap. Draw an amount of air into the syringe equal to the insulin you want to fill the Pod with. Inject the air into the insulin vial and then invert the vial. Fill the syringe with the desired amount of insulin. To remove air bubbles, allow them to rise and gently push them back into the vial. Adjust the plunger to draw the desired insulin amount into the syringe and remove the needle from the vial. The filling port of the Pod is indicated by a black arrow at the bottom. Hold the syringe perpendicular and insert the needle into the fill port. Slowly push the plunger to fill the Pod with insulin. You will hear two beeps once the Pod is filled with at least 85 units of insulin. Keep pushing the plunger to empty the syringe completely even after hearing the two beeps.

### ***\#2 Linking the Pod to the PDM***

To pair the Pod with the PDM, place the filled Pod next to the PDM, ensuring that the holder and PDM are touching. On the PDM, press "Next" and wait for the Pod to connect and perform safety checks automatically. Wait for the audible confirmation indicating that the Pod is paired and ready for application.

### ***\#3 Pod placement***

Before applying the Pod, remove the blue needle cap. Use the pull tabs to remove and discard the white paper covering the adhesive tape. Carefully position the Pod in the desired infusion site. Ensure the Pod is applied evenly and flat on the skin to avoid adhesive wrinkles. Once applied, run your finger over the edge of the adhesive tape to secure it in place.

### ***\#4 Starting the Pod***

After applying the Pod, it's time to insert the cannula under the skin. If the infusion site has little body fat, consider using the squeeze method. Place your hand on the Pod and gently squeeze the skin around the infusion site. Keep the skin squeezed until the cannula is inserted. Find a comfortable position before proceeding. When ready, tap "Start" on the PDM, then confirm. Within seconds, the cannula will be inserted with minimal discomfort.

The PDM will prompt you to verify the correct insertion of the cannula. Check the pink insertion area on the surface of the Pod. A light pink square above the view window indicates that the cannula has been properly inserted. You can also check the light blue cannula in the view window. If the cannula is correctly inserted, tap "Yes" on the PDM. A green banner will indicate that the Pod is set.

### ***4.2 Do's & don’ts***

Here are some important points to keep in mind:

- It is necessary to replace the Pod when the reservoir is empty or after 72 hours of use. The PDM will give you an alarm in advance for both scenarios. You should also replace the Pod if it detaches from the infusion site or when indicated by the PDM alarm.

- The Omnipod DASH Pods are compatible with Novorapid, Novolog, Humalog, Apidra, Fiasp and Lyumjev. However, Apidra requires Pod replacement every 2 days according to the manual. The Pods are only approved for insulin with a concentration of 100 U/ml.

- The Pod can hold a minimum of 85 units and a maximum of 200 units of insulin. If you require more than 200 units of insulin within 3 days, this system may not be suitable for you.

- Some individuals experience a slight increase in blood glucose levels after Pod changes. To prevent this, consider giving a 1-unit bolus with each Pod change.

- The Pods come with a fixed cannula length of 6.5 mm <span class="mark">or 0.25 inch a</span>t a 45-degree angle. There is no option for a longer cannula for individuals with thicker skin. It's important to be aware that these short cannulas can sometimes get kinked like other infusion sets.

- In some countries, PodPals are included in the starter pack. These are overtapes for the Omnipod Pods. Alternatively, you can purchase other over<span class="mark">tapes o</span>nline to ensure the Pod stays securely in place.

- Remember to check your blood glucose levels 2 hours after inserting a new Pod. This is the only way to confirm proper insertion and insulin delivery. It's best to avoid changing the Pod right before bedtime.

- Pods must be replaced every 72 hours or 3 days. Plan Pod changes for different days of the week since you receive exactly one Pod every 3 days. The PDM will provide an alarm at 72 hours, but the Pod will continue working for an additional 8 hours before it stops functioning.

- If you wish to recycle the Pods, you can request a recycling box from the company, depending on the country you live in.

That's it! Now you're familiar with the process of replacing the Pod. We personally found it comfortable to wear the pump, hardly noticing its presence. If the cannula hits a muscle, it might lead to discomfort, in which case it's better to insert another Pod. The replacement process can go very fast, and experienced users report completing it in just 3.5 minutes! This is notably shorter than the procedure for catheter pumps since there's no need to fill an additional catheter. You also don't have to worry about forgetting how to change the Pod, because the PDM will guide you through the entire process!

## **<u>5. Pump alarms</u>**

Welcome to this lesson about the alarms on the Omnipod DASH insulin pump.

The Pod and the PDM can alert you to various issues through beeping or vibrating**<span class="mark">.</span>** In Omnipod DASH, the alerts are categorized as hazard alarms, advisory alarms, notifications, and communication errors.

Let's explore these alarms and provide some additional tips.

### ***5.1 Hazard alarms***

Hazard alarms are high-priority alarms indicating a serious problem that has occurred.

After a hazard alarm, the Pod must be replaced.

You will hear a continuous beep from the Pod or the PDM, accompanied by a notification with a red triangle.

Possible causes include an empty reservoir, an expired Pod, an occlusion, or auto-off (an adjustable automatic shutdown of the pump after a period of inactivity).

If you hear a continuous beep, your PDM will display the specific problem.

After reading the message, the Pod will often be deactivated, and it's important to replace it as soon as possible. In certain cases, contacting customer service may be necessary.

### ***5.2 Advisory alarms***

Advisory alarms have a lower priority but require prompt action.

You will hear intermittent beeps from the Po<span class="mark">d and or PD</span>M and see a notification with an orange sphere.

Possible causes of a warning alarm include an expired Pod, low reservoir, low battery, resume insulin alert, and auto-off signal.

If you delay addressing a warning alarm, it can escalate into a hazard alarm.

### ***5.3 Notifications***

Notifications are customizable reminders that you can set.

Examples include reminders for an almost expired Pod, no active Pod, a blood glucose check after a bolus, or a missed bolus within a specific time segment.

You can set these reminders yourself via the menu.

### ***5.4 Communication errors***

Communication errors can occur when the PDM is too far from the Pod or if external signals disrupt the communication.

The PDM may indicate a communication error with two dots in the status bar. This is usually a common issue that can be resolved easily.

If the PDM fails to establish communication with the Pod during an action, you will receive a communication error message. In such cases, you can try repeating the action, cancel it, or replace the Pod if communication cannot be restored.

Communication failures can be caused by:

- Your body: If administering a bolus while the Pod is on the back of your body, you may need to hold the PDM next to your body, aligned with the Pod, during delivery.

- Your environment: Some places may have better communication than others. Certain locations, such as remote outdoor areas, may pose communication challenges. If you face difficulty in one location, try moving to another.

- Interaction with other electronics: Rooms with high radio wave activity, Wi-Fi routers, or USB-3.0 ports may affect communication. Avoid such spaces when establishing communication.

To refresh the communication with the Pod without issuing a command, simply tap the "Pod Info" tab on your PDM. This ensures your PDM receives the latest information, such as insulin remaining in the Pod.

### ***5.5 Do's & don’ts***

Here are some important points to remember:

- To properly acknowledge an alert, it is not enough to just read it. You must click on the blue bell icon, read the dark blue notification, scroll down if needed, and swipe left on the message.

- If you are unsure how to respond to an alarm, consult the user manual or contact customer service.

- To familiarize yourself with the alarm sounds or vibrations, you can test them through the menu settings by selecting "Check Alarms."

- If a Pod alarm becomes persistent and cannot be turned off using the PDM, you can use the end of a paperclip to press the small hole at the back of the Pod (opposite the cannula). This will silence the alarm of the "screaming Pod."

- The PDM can store up to 90 days of alarm history. To access previous hazard and advisory alerts, tap the bell icon in the top right corner. New alerts are displayed with a blue icon, while previously viewed notifications have a grey icon.

- If you temporarily won't be using the PDM, you can simply switch it off.

Congratulations! You have completed the final lesson on the Omnipod DASH pump. Now you have all the necessary knowledge to successfully start using this pump. Initial setup is usually supervised by a diabetes nurse, and if you have any further questions, don't hesitate to consult them. Well done on reaching this point!
