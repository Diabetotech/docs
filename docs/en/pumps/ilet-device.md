## **<u>2. How the iLet Device works</u>**

Welcome to this informative video on how the iLet Device works! In this segment, we will explore the various aspects of the pump, including its parts, buttons, charging process, unlocking mechanism, and the different screens and menus.

### ***2.1 Parts & buttons on the pump***

Let's start with an overview of the parts and buttons on the pump. The iLet pump has a sleek design. At the front of the pump, you'll find a large touchscreen that allows for easy navigation.

At the top of the pump, there's a single button for putting it to sleep or waking it up with just a light touch.

On the bottom left side, you'll find a compartment for the insulin reservoir, called the "iLet Cartridge." This cartridge connects to a plastic luer connector, known as an “iLet Connect”, ensuring a sealed connection between the pump and the insulin tubing and infusion set.

The pump works with prefilled insuli<span class="mark">n pump cartridges or</span> you can manually fill the iLet Cartridges with insulin, using a needle and syringe.

At the back of the pump, you can find the serial number and 2 small holes designed for attaching the pump clip. With this “iLet clip” you can attach the pump to your clothes.

Additionally the pump comes with a black soft pump case, providing protection for the device.

### ***2.2 Charging the pump***

The iLet Device contains a rechargeable battery that is not replaceable. It can be charged using a dedicated inductive charger that comes with the device. Only use the power adapter and USB cable provided with the iLet Device.

Connect the charger to the power adapter with the micro-USB cable and plug it into an electrical outlet. Place your iLet Device onto the charger. If the charger status light is solid and the battery indicator animates on the touchscreen, your iLet Device is charging properly.

If the charger status light is blinking, your iLet Device is not charging properly. Remove it from the charger for at least 10 seconds and then place it back on the charger.

To optimize battery life, charge the battery daily. It typically takes around 2 hours to fully charge a depleted battery, providing 4 to 5 days of usage when fully charged. Battery life depends on factors such as usage intensity, backlight, and insulin delivery.

The iLet remains operational and keeps delivering insulin while charging, although it might not be convenient to stay connected to the pump while it is charging.

<span class="mark">To minimize disruption to insulin therapy, consider charging during activities like bathing or showering to maintain an optimal battery level.</span>

### ***2.3 Unlocking the pump***

To unlock the pump, simply tap the Sleep/Wake button to turn on the touchscreen and backlight. Tapping the button can also turn off the screen if desired.

Drag the Unlock slider to the right to unlock the touchscreen.

The touchscreen will automatically go to sleep after 45 seconds of inactivity. During this time, your iLet will continue dosing insulin, the display will continue to provide basic status information, but the touchscreen cannot be activated by finger taps.

### ***2.4 Home screen and pump menu***

Let's now delve into the home screen and pump menu of your iLet Device.

The home screen presents a variety of information.

- At the top, you'll find the status bar, indicating the pump's time, paired sensor or phone status, any alerts, and the device's battery level.

- Below this, there are three icons granting access to the main menu, insulin cartridge details and management, and notifications. If there is a number displayed in the notification bell icon, it indicates the number of alerts that acquire your attention.

- In the center, your current glucose level and trend arrow are displayed. Below 40 mg/dl or 2.2 mmol/l, it will indicate LOW, and above 400 mg/dl or 22.2 mmol/l, it will show HIGH. If the sensor connection is lost, three dashes appear, and during warming up, the warming up time is shown. The circle around the glucose status spins when the iLet is operational.

- Finally, at the bottom of the home screen, there's a meal announcement button where you can specify the meal type and estimated size.

<span class="mark">Tapping the menu icon</span> reveals <span class="mark">seven</span> icons for different submenus.

- The blood glucose meter icon allows entry of fingerprick blood glucose for sensor calibration or when there's no active sensor session.

- <span class="mark">The pause insulin icon can be used to pause and resume insulin delivery.</span>

- The Dexcom sensor icon facilitates sensor pairing, alert adjustments, and viewing sensor information.

- The phone icon enables pairing with a compatible device via the iLet Mobile app.

- The cogwheel icon grants access to additional device information and settings.

- The clock icon displays your history, including alerts, meal announcements, cartridge and infusion set changes, algorithm steps, and insulin history.

- Finally, the microphone icon allows volume selection. Even when set to Off, the iLet still vibrates.

You can close the menu by tapping on the X.

Tapping on the glucose status leads to a graph screen displaying recent glucose data and insulin delivery. You can switch between 3, 6, 12, and 24-hour views using the left and right arrows. Zoom in by tapping anywhere on the graph, and tap again to zoom out.

If you tap the chart icon within the graph screen, a summary of your glucose control and total daily insulin dose appears. You can switch between 1, 7, 30, and 90-day views using the left and right arrows. To return to the Graph screen, tap the graph icon in the upper righthand corner.

### ***2.5 Do's & don’ts***

Here are a few important tips to keep in mind:

- You can find a comprehensive menu overview in the downloads section, which can be saved for easy reference when providing remote guidance as a healthcare provider.

- The iLet Device is waterproof for 30 minutes until a depth of 3.6 meter or 12 feet. However, it is recommended to remove the iLet Device for all water related activities like swimming.

- The pump comes with a soft black pump case and iLet clip. If you want to charge the pump, you’ll need to remove the clip. The pump case however can stay on the device.

- For individuals bothered by the glare on the iLet Device screen, an anti-glare screen protector offers a solution. Additionally, some users have found the iLet clip lacking in strength and have chosen to upgrade to a sturdier clip, such as the one available from EDC Solutions (https://edcsolutionstype1.com/).

- The iLet Device does not automatically adjust the time and date. So when changing between winter and summer time, or when traveling to a different time zone, you have to adjust the time yourself. You can do that via the settings.

## **<u>3. Insulin setup</u>**

Welcome to our comprehensive guide on setting up insulin delivery for the iLet Device.

With the iLet Device, you won't need to configure basal delivery rates, carb ratios, or insulin sensitivity settings. The integrated iLet Dosing Decision Software handles all insulin delivery automatically. The specifics of how this algorithm calculates insulin delivery are explained in the module dedicated to the iLet System.

In this lesson, we'll cover how to set up the iLet Device using only your weight and how to administer insulin boluses for meals.

### ***3.1 Initial setup of iLet Device***

<span class="mark">When you first use your iLet Device, it will prompt you to:</span>

- <span class="mark">Set the time and date,</span>

- <span class="mark">Connect your glucose sensor,</span>

- <span class="mark">And install your insulin cartridge and infusion set.</span>

<span class="mark">The final step is to input your weight. Once done, you can activate insulin delivery and "Go Bionic".</span>

<span class="mark">If you need to stop insulin delivery, for example to prevent hypoglycemia during exercise, you can tap pauze insulin from the menu. First you will need to select when you want to be reminded to resume insulin delivery.</span>

### ***3.2 Delivering a bolus***

The sole method for delivering an insulin bolus is by announcing a meal. It's recommended to <span class="mark">do this only when you're about to eat, rather than to correct high blood sugar levels, as all correction insulin is automatically delivered by the system.</span>

<span class="mark">Announce your meal at the start of your eating session. You can do this up to 30 minutes after you begin eating. If you forget to an</span>nounce your meal and more than 30 minutes have passed since you ate, refrain from announcing your meal to prevent insulin "stacking".

To announce a meal, access the Meal Announcement button on the home screen. Select the meal type: breakfast, lunch, or dinner. Then indicate the meal size: usual, more than usual, or less than usual.

For the majority of your meals, choose "Usual." Opt for "Less" if your meal has approximately half the carbs of your usual meal, and select "More" if your meal contains around 50% more carbs than your usual meal. If the meal or snack contains less than one quarter of the carbs in your usual meal, announcing it is unnecessary. Individuals consuming very few carbohydrates in a meal or snack, for example less than 15 grams of carbs for an adult, do not need to announce the meal or snack to the iLet.

### ***3.3 Do's & don’ts***

- The iLet delivers insulin boluses at an even rate of one unit every five seconds. Therefore, a dose of 6 units will take 30 seconds to deliver. The speed of insulin delivery cannot be adjusted.

- <span class="mark">The maximum meal dose is 30 units. There is no limit on the number of meals that can be announced per day.</span>

- Maintain consistency in selecting meal sizes within each meal type. Choose "Breakfast" for breakfast, "Lunch" for lunch, and "Dinner" for dinner.

- Announce snacks as meals if they contain as many carbs as a regular meal. If a snack matches the carb content of your usual meals for that meal type, announce it as a meal. If a snack doesn't meet the carb criteria for your "Less" meal type, refrain from announcing it as a meal.

- Only announce meals containing carbs; do not factor in protein or fat. So although users don’t need to count carbs, they’ll need to be carbs aware to understand the portion of carbs in their meals. If you follow a very low carb <span class="mark">diet, consider not announcing meals altogether.</span>

- <span class="mark">For coursed meals, the user can announce appropriately for each course. For very large meals the user may need to announce more than one "usual for me" meal.</span>

- <span class="mark">It's optimal to announce meals and snacks just before eating, or within 15 minutes prior up to 30 minutes after starting to eat. Avoid using meal announcements to correct high blood sugar levels, or to announce carbs used to treat lows.</span>

- <span class="mark">To prevent over-treating post</span>-meal lows and aid the iLet in learning, avoid over-correcting low blood sugar levels that occur after meals.

- Initially, the "Less" meal option may not be available as the iLet learns your insulin needs for your "Usual for me" meal. Once the iLet learns your requirements for each meal type, the "Less" option becomes available. <span class="mark">If the Less option is not yet available and you are eating less carbs than your Usual for me meal, do not announce that meal.</span>

- In the first few days, consume meals within the "Usual for me" carb range and wait at least 4 hours before eating and announcing again. This helps the iLet learn your insulin needs for your typical meals.

- If your glucose is low before a meal, treat the low with rapid-acting carbohydrates before eating and announcing the meal. Don't include carbs used to treat lows in your meal announcement size and avoid announcing your meal as smaller than it actually is to receive less insulin as a "reverse correction."

Congratulations! You're now equipped with the knowledge to effectively manage insulin on the iLet Device. In our next segment, we'll walk you through changing the insulin reservoir and infusion set.

## **<u>4. Reservoir and infusion set replacement</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on reservoir and infusion set replacement on the iLet Device.

In this video, we will cover the different infusion set options, step-by-step instructions for replacing the insulin cartridge and infusion set, and helpful tips for success.

***4.1 Different infusion sets***

The Inset 1 infusion set is the most widely used infusion set for the iLet Device. It <span class="mark">has a Teflon cannula of 6 mm in a straight angle</span>

<span class="mark">If you prefer a steel cannula, you can opt for the Contact Detach infusion set. A detailed overview of these infusion sets, including tubing and cannula lengths and filling volumes, can be found in the download below this video.</span>

<span class="mark">We’ll demonstrate changing the insulin reservoir and infusion set using the Inset 1 infusion set. For demonstration videos of the Contact Detach infusion sets, please check the link below this video.</span>

*Contact Detach =\> [<u>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8xtQm11BeKk</u>](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8xtQm11BeKk)*

### ***4.2 <span class="mark">Insulin cartridge and infusion set replacement</span>***

Start by washing your hands and ensuring your workspace is clean, and gather these items: your iLet Device, an infusion set with tubing, an iLet Connect luer lock, some alcohol wipes and your insulin at room temperature. This can be a prefilled insulin cartridge for example of 160 U of Fiasp, or it can be your insulin vial with an iLet Cartridge of 180 U, syringe and needle.

### ***\#1 Reservoir replacement***

To fill the iLet Cartridge with insulin, gather the following supplies: an Islet Cartridge, a syringe and a needle, a vial of Humalog or Novolog <span class="mark">or Fiasp</span> insulin, and an alcohol wipe. If you are using prefilled Fiasp cartridges, you can skip this step.

First, wipe the top of the insulin vial with an alcohol wipe and let it dry. Next, open the iLet Cartridge package and remove the empty iLet Cartridge from the packaging. Open the syringe package and remove the syringe from the packaging. Last, open the needle package and remove the needle from the packaging.

Attach the syringe to the needle by twisting one onto the other. Remove the needle guard without touching the needle. Pull back the plunger of the syringe. Make sure the plunger is at the 1.8 milliliter mark, which is about halfway between 1.5 and 2 milliliter marks. Insert the syringe into the insulin vial while the vial is placed upright on a table or countertop. Push down the syringe plunger rod to fill the vial with the air from the syringe. Without letting go of the plunger rod, invert the insulin vial and syringe.

<span class="mark">Carefully and slowly, pull back the syringe plunger rod to fill the syringe with the amount of insulin you require in 2 to 3 days but no more than 1.8 milliliters. Remove any air remaining in the syringe. Pull back as needed, again, to fill the syringe to about 1.8 milliliters.</span>

To fill the iLet cartridge, place the base of the empty cartridge upright on a flat surface. Hold the cartridge with one hand. With the other hand, insert the syringe needle into the empty cartridge septum. Press down slowly on the syringe plunger rod to transfer the insulin from the syringe into the cartridge. It's important not to overfill the cartridge. The rubber plunger in the back might get pushed out if too much insulin is transferred. If this happens, dispose of the cartridge and use a new one. The cartridge does not hold more than 1.8 milliliters of insulin.

Remove as much air from the cartridge as you can by pulling back slightly on the syringe plunger rod. Once there are no air bubbles remaining inside the cartridge, remove the syringe and discard the syringe needle in a sharps disposal.

When filling the insulin cartridge, remember to keep the needle straight and do not angle the needle to try to remove bubbles on the sides of the cartridge. This can tear the septum of the cartridge, which can cause leaking of insulin. If the septum tears, discard the cartridge and restart the process. If there is still a bubble present near the top of the cartridge, slowly withdraw the syringe needle from the cartridge while maintaining pressure on the plunger. The needle tip should now be surrounded by the air bubble. Pull back on the plunger until the air bubble has been removed.

It's important to remember to be gentle when filling your insulin cartridge. Do not insert the needle too many times or wiggle the needle around too much while it is inserted in the cartridge. These things can tear the septum, which will cause leaking of insulin into the <span class="mark">iLet.</span> Take care to remove large bubbles from your cartridge, but lots of tiny little bubbles are okay and will be primed out once you prime the tubing.

Do not attach your filled insulin cartridge to your iLet Connect or insulin tubing yet. These should never be attached outside of the iLet.

First, you need to prepare your iLet for your cartridge and prepare your insulin tubing. Remember not to insert your filled cartridge into the iLet until it has completely rewound.

To prepare your insulin tubing, pull the red tape to remove the tamper-proof seal from the Inset infusion set. Remove the sterile paper. To remove the lid, firmly press the three raised dots on each side while lifting the lid up. Be careful not to bend or touch the introducer needle when preparin<span class="mark">g the in</span>fusion set. The needle should come placed securely in the inserter, pointing straight, not at an angle or loose. If it is not straight or if it is loose and insertion is attempted, pain or minor injury may occur. To avoid this, please ensure that the following steps are done gently so the infusion set is not loosened or removed by accident. Unwind the tubing by carefully unlocking the tubing from the slot. Now unwind the tubing from the inserter by pulling it carefully upwards. Be careful not to touch the introducer needle while unwinding the tubing or pull too hard when the tubing is fully released, as this could accidentally remove the infusion set from the inserter. <span class="mark">Connect the iLet Connect Luer adapter and insulin infusion set flexible tubing by twisting one into the other until tight. Make sure this connection is straight and tight.</span>

Once the insulin tubing is ready, prepare the iLet Device for the new insulin cartridge. From the home screen, select the Insulin Cartridge icon. Select Change Cartridge & Tubing. Disconnect any current insulin infusion set tubing from your body. Allow the iLet Device to complete the rewind process. Then remove the old insulin cartridge and attached tubing. Only insert the insulin cartridge once the rewind process is complete.

Insert the new insulin cartridge into the insulin chamber of the iLet Device. After the cartridge has been inserted, insert the iLet Connect into the chamber of the iLet Device to lock the filled insulin cartridge into place. While continuing to push the iLet Connect into the chamber, rotate the connector a quarter turn to the right until the iLet Connect is fitted tightly.

### ***\#2 Filling the <span class="mark">tubing</span>***

Once the new insulin cartridge is in place, tap “Go to Fill Tubing” on the iLet Device screen. Press and hold the “Press & Hold” button until the tubing is filled, clear of bubbles, and you see drops at the end of the insulin infusion set. Ensure that there are no air bubbles remaining in the tubing. When filling the infusion set tubing, hold the infusion set with the needle pointing down to ensure that insulin does not get in contact with the adhesive backing paper. Once the insulin tubing is filled and you see drops at the end, tap “Yes” on the screen to complete the process.

The pump will now ask if you also need to replace your infusion set.

- If you do not need to insert a new insulin infusion set base, select “No” and connect the new insulin infusion set tubing to the insulin infusion set base already on your body.

- If you do need to insert a new insulin infusion set base, select “Yes”.

### ***\#3 Infusion set placement***

Now, we're ready to apply the infusion set. Pull up gently on the white tab to remove the adhesive backing paper. Place your fingers on the lined indentations on each side. Pull the spring back firmly until you hear a click. Carefully remove the needle guard by gently twisting and then pulling it up, taking care not to touch the needle itself. Check that the soft cannula does not exceed the introducer needle. Before positioning the inset infusion set over a clean insertion site, place the tubing in the slot to ensure that the tubing is not caught underneath the infusion set during insertion. Insert the inset infusion set by positioning it on the clean insertion site. Simultaneously, press the round indentations on each side of the inserter to insert the inset infusion set. Push gently on the center of the inserter to secure the adhesive on the skin. Remove the inserter and introduce a needle by grasping the center and pulling it gently back. Gently massage the adhesive tape onto the skin.

### ***\#4 Cannula Filling***

<span class="mark">If a new</span> insulin infusion set base is inserted, and your insulin tubing is connected to it, drag “Next” to go to the Fill Cannula screen.

Drag “Fill” to the right to fill the cannula. The user must select the kind of insulin set they are using. If a Teflon infusion set has been selected, “Fill Cannula” will deliver the appropriate volume of insulin to fill the cannula and reset the reminder to replace the infusion set to count down from three days. For a steel infusion set, “Fill Cannula” will not deliver any insulin (the steel cannula is filled during priming) but will reset the reminder to replace the infusion set to count down from two days.

When the cannula is done filling, you’ll see a screen confirming the process is complete! Nice job! You've now successfully replaced the insulin reservoir and infusion set!

***4.3 Do's & don’ts***

- The insulin approved in the iLet Device is Novolog, Humalog and Fiasp.

- The insuli<span class="mark">n reservoir or insulin cartridge needs to be replaced when it runs out of insulin or every 3 days for teflon infusion sets, or every 2 days for steel infusion sets, wh</span>ichever occurs sooner. There is no hard shutdown of your iLet Device, but you’ll get a notification. The iLet Connect and infusion set tubing need to be replaced every time you replace your iLet insulin cartridge. The iLet insulin cartridge, iLet connect, and tubing should always be replaced together. None of these components should be reused, and they should not be separated once connected.

- Your infusion set needs to be replaced every 3 days for teflon cannulas or every 2 days for steel cannulas, even if the reservoir is not empty. Always replace your iLet infusion set (as well as cartridge, tubing and connector) if you have any doubt it is not working!

- If there is a large bubble in the iLet Connect or tubing, you can use “Fill Tubing Only” via the “Cartridge icon” to prime the bubble out and ensure consistent insulin delivery. Make sure the user disconnects from their infusion site before priming the tubing.

- <span class="mark">The infusion set should be placed at least 7.5 cm or 3 inches apart from a Dexcom CGM, and 2.5 cm or 1 inch apart from an Abbott CGM.</span>

- Avoid placing the tubing in the notch of the inserter before pulling the spring-loaded inserter, as this will create tension and may result in the cannula bending. Only insert the tubing into the notch after loading the inserter.

- Prior to disposing of the insertion device, reattach the lid until it clicks.

- Remember, 2 hours after inserting a new set, you should check your glucose <span class="mark">level. Checking your glucose level is the on</span>ly way to confirm your infusion set is properly inserted and that you're receiving insulin.

- The Inset infusion set allows you to temporarily disconnect your pump without having to change the infusion set. Hold the adhesive tape in place by placing a finger just in front of the canular housing and gently squeeze the sides of the connector. Pull the connector needle straight out from the canular housing. If preferred, insert the disconnect cover into the canular housing until you hear a click. To reconnect the tubing to the infusion set, remove the disconnect cover from the canular housing. Ensure the flow by filling the tubing until insulin dispenses from the needle. When filling the tubing, hold the connector needle pointing down. Place a finger on the adhesive tape just in front of the canular housing and push the connector needle straight in until you hear a click. Now you are ready to restart your insulin delivery.

So now, you're equipped with the knowledge to replace the insulin reservoir and infusion set on the iLet Device. If you stumble, don't worry – it's perfectly normal. Keep calm, follow the steps, and remember, if all else fails, there's always the free customer service of Beta Bionics available. Also, don't forget that you can contact your diabetes team, who also have an on-call service for medical support. Good luck!

## **<u>5. Pump alarms</u>**

In this video, we dive into the iLet Device's alarm system. <span class="mark">We'll discuss the different kinds of alarms, and share some best practices.</span>

Your iLet Device will alert you when important issues need to be addressed. Alerts are categorized into high priority, medium priority and low priority alerts, recognizable by 3, 2 or 1 exclamation mark.

When multiple alerts occur at the same time, alerts with the highest priority will be displayed first. Each one requires individual confirmation until all are acknowledged.

### ***5.1 High priority alerts***

The only high priority alert is an urgent low glucose alert, for which you need to take glucose immediately. <span class="mark">This alarm goes off when your sensor glucose is below 54 mg/dl or 3.0 mmol/l and cannot be turned off.</span> The iLet Device will alert you every 5 minutes with 20 beeps (if volume is on) and 5 vibrations. These will escalate to the highest volume level after 5 minutes. The alert will not stop until acknowledged or resolved.

### ***5.2 Medium priority alerts***

Medium priority alerts require attention as possible. Your iLet Device may have stopped delivering insulin and you need to fix the issues that trigger the alert. You may need to contact Beta Bionics for further resolution.

These alerts are expressed every 5 minutes with 12 beeps (if volume is on) and 3 vibrations. These will escalate to the highest volume level after 15 minutes. The alert will not stop until acknowledged or resolved.

Examples are Urgent Low Soon, Low Glucose, High Glucose, Glucose Falling Quickly, Enter a BG Value, Insulin Very Low, Change Insulin, Insulin Occlusion, Recharge iLet Soon, Recharge iLet Now, Change Insulin, Insulin Fill Tubing, Dosing Stops Soon and Dosing Stopped.

You can find an overview of all alerts and what to do in the manual.

### ***5.3 Low priority alerts***

Low priority alerts are reminders about an upcoming event, or an alert that a component of your iLet System may stop functioning soon. You need to acknowledge the alert by tapping the button at the bottom of the alert message.

Depending on the level, you’ll get just a<span class="mark">n alert without b</span>eeps or vibrations, or the iLet Device will alert every 5 minutes with 2 beeps and 1 vibration. These will not escalate to the next volume level.

Possible low priority alerts are Calibration Failed, Sensor Expiring, Sensor Expired, Sensor Failed, Replace Your Sensor, Sensor Reconnecting, CGM Battery Low, Pair a New Transmitter, Transmitter Not Found, Sensor Failed, Brief Sensor Issue, Pairing Failed, Calibration Not Used, Sensor Expiring, Sensor Expired, Readings Stop, Replace Sensor New, Insulin Low, iLet Setup, Insulin Set Expired and Check Body Weight.

### ***5.4 Do's & don’ts***

- A 'malfunction' alarm indicates a system fault. Take note of the fault code and contact customer service.

- If in doubt, refer to the user manual or call customer service.

- If you won't be using your pump for a significant period, put it in storage mode. To do this, go into Settings and choose Other, then choose Shutdown. All dosing will stop. To turn the iLet Device back on, place the iLet Device on the charger. Upon waking up if the battery has been fully depleted, the iLet Device may require you to go through the iLet Startup Sequence again. In that case, a new treatment session will be started using a new body weight entry, but all other user settings on the iLet Device will be retained.

You're now equipped with knowledge about the pump's primary alarms and what actions to take. Therefore, there's no need to panic when you hear an alarm from your iLet Device. Simply check the display screen - it usually provides instructions. If the message isn't clear, refer to the user manual or call customer service. Best of luck!
