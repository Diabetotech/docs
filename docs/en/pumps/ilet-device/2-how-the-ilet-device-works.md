## **<u>2. How the iLet Device works</u>**

Welcome to this informative video on how the iLet Device works! In this segment, we will explore the various aspects of the pump, including its parts, buttons, charging process, unlocking mechanism, and the different screens and menus.

### ***2.1 Parts & buttons on the pump***

Let's start with an overview of the parts and buttons on the pump. The iLet pump has a sleek design. At the front of the pump, you'll find a large touchscreen that allows for easy navigation.

At the top of the pump, there's a single button for putting it to sleep or waking it up with just a light touch.

On the bottom left side, you'll find a compartment for the insulin reservoir, called the "iLet Cartridge." This cartridge connects to a plastic luer connector, known as an “iLet Connect”, ensuring a sealed connection between the pump and the insulin tubing and infusion set.

The pump works with prefilled insuli<span class="mark">n pump cartridges or</span> you can manually fill the iLet Cartridges with insulin, using a needle and syringe.

At the back of the pump, you can find the serial number and 2 small holes designed for attaching the pump clip. With this “iLet clip” you can attach the pump to your clothes.

Additionally the pump comes with a black soft pump case, providing protection for the device.

### ***2.2 Charging the pump***

The iLet Device contains a rechargeable battery that is not replaceable. It can be charged using a dedicated inductive charger that comes with the device. Only use the power adapter and USB cable provided with the iLet Device.

Connect the charger to the power adapter with the micro-USB cable and plug it into an electrical outlet. Place your iLet Device onto the charger. If the charger status light is solid and the battery indicator animates on the touchscreen, your iLet Device is charging properly.

If the charger status light is blinking, your iLet Device is not charging properly. Remove it from the charger for at least 10 seconds and then place it back on the charger.

To optimize battery life, charge the battery daily. It typically takes around 2 hours to fully charge a depleted battery, providing 4 to 5 days of usage when fully charged. Battery life depends on factors such as usage intensity, backlight, and insulin delivery.

The iLet remains operational and keeps delivering insulin while charging, although it might not be convenient to stay connected to the pump while it is charging.

<span class="mark">To minimize disruption to insulin therapy, consider charging during activities like bathing or showering to maintain an optimal battery level.</span>

### ***2.3 Unlocking the pump***

To unlock the pump, simply tap the Sleep/Wake button to turn on the touchscreen and backlight. Tapping the button can also turn off the screen if desired.

Drag the Unlock slider to the right to unlock the touchscreen.

The touchscreen will automatically go to sleep after 45 seconds of inactivity. During this time, your iLet will continue dosing insulin, the display will continue to provide basic status information, but the touchscreen cannot be activated by finger taps.

### ***2.4 Home screen and pump menu***

Let's now delve into the home screen and pump menu of your iLet Device.

The home screen presents a variety of information.

- At the top, you'll find the status bar, indicating the pump's time, paired sensor or phone status, any alerts, and the device's battery level.

- Below this, there are three icons granting access to the main menu, insulin cartridge details and management, and notifications. If there is a number displayed in the notification bell icon, it indicates the number of alerts that acquire your attention.

- In the center, your current glucose level and trend arrow are displayed. Below 40 mg/dl or 2.2 mmol/l, it will indicate LOW, and above 400 mg/dl or 22.2 mmol/l, it will show HIGH. If the sensor connection is lost, three dashes appear, and during warming up, the warming up time is shown. The circle around the glucose status spins when the iLet is operational.

- Finally, at the bottom of the home screen, there's a meal announcement button where you can specify the meal type and estimated size.

<span class="mark">Tapping the menu icon</span> reveals <span class="mark">seven</span> icons for different submenus.

- The blood glucose meter icon allows entry of fingerprick blood glucose for sensor calibration or when there's no active sensor session.

- <span class="mark">The pause insulin icon can be used to pause and resume insulin delivery.</span>

- The Dexcom sensor icon facilitates sensor pairing, alert adjustments, and viewing sensor information.

- The phone icon enables pairing with a compatible device via the iLet Mobile app.

- The cogwheel icon grants access to additional device information and settings.

- The clock icon displays your history, including alerts, meal announcements, cartridge and infusion set changes, algorithm steps, and insulin history.

- Finally, the microphone icon allows volume selection. Even when set to Off, the iLet still vibrates.

You can close the menu by tapping on the X.

Tapping on the glucose status leads to a graph screen displaying recent glucose data and insulin delivery. You can switch between 3, 6, 12, and 24-hour views using the left and right arrows. Zoom in by tapping anywhere on the graph, and tap again to zoom out.

If you tap the chart icon within the graph screen, a summary of your glucose control and total daily insulin dose appears. You can switch between 1, 7, 30, and 90-day views using the left and right arrows. To return to the Graph screen, tap the graph icon in the upper righthand corner.

### ***2.5 Do's & don’ts***

Here are a few important tips to keep in mind:

- You can find a comprehensive menu overview in the downloads section, which can be saved for easy reference when providing remote guidance as a healthcare provider.

- The iLet Device is waterproof for 30 minutes until a depth of 3.6 meter or 12 feet. However, it is recommended to remove the iLet Device for all water related activities like swimming.

- The pump comes with a soft black pump case and iLet clip. If you want to charge the pump, you’ll need to remove the clip. The pump case however can stay on the device.

- For individuals bothered by the glare on the iLet Device screen, an anti-glare screen protector offers a solution. Additionally, some users have found the iLet clip lacking in strength and have chosen to upgrade to a sturdier clip, such as the one available from EDC Solutions (https://edcsolutionstype1.com/).

- The iLet Device does not automatically adjust the time and date. So when changing between winter and summer time, or when traveling to a different time zone, you have to adjust the time yourself. You can do that via the settings.
