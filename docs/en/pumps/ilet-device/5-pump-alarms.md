## **<u>5. Pump alarms</u>**

In this video, we dive into the iLet Device's alarm system. <span class="mark">We'll discuss the different kinds of alarms, and share some best practices.</span>

Your iLet Device will alert you when important issues need to be addressed. Alerts are categorized into high priority, medium priority and low priority alerts, recognizable by 3, 2 or 1 exclamation mark.

When multiple alerts occur at the same time, alerts with the highest priority will be displayed first. Each one requires individual confirmation until all are acknowledged.

### ***5.1 High priority alerts***

The only high priority alert is an urgent low glucose alert, for which you need to take glucose immediately. <span class="mark">This alarm goes off when your sensor glucose is below 54 mg/dl or 3.0 mmol/l and cannot be turned off.</span> The iLet Device will alert you every 5 minutes with 20 beeps (if volume is on) and 5 vibrations. These will escalate to the highest volume level after 5 minutes. The alert will not stop until acknowledged or resolved.

### ***5.2 Medium priority alerts***

Medium priority alerts require attention as possible. Your iLet Device may have stopped delivering insulin and you need to fix the issues that trigger the alert. You may need to contact Beta Bionics for further resolution.

These alerts are expressed every 5 minutes with 12 beeps (if volume is on) and 3 vibrations. These will escalate to the highest volume level after 15 minutes. The alert will not stop until acknowledged or resolved.

Examples are Urgent Low Soon, Low Glucose, High Glucose, Glucose Falling Quickly, Enter a BG Value, Insulin Very Low, Change Insulin, Insulin Occlusion, Recharge iLet Soon, Recharge iLet Now, Change Insulin, Insulin Fill Tubing, Dosing Stops Soon and Dosing Stopped.

You can find an overview of all alerts and what to do in the manual.

### ***5.3 Low priority alerts***

Low priority alerts are reminders about an upcoming event, or an alert that a component of your iLet System may stop functioning soon. You need to acknowledge the alert by tapping the button at the bottom of the alert message.

Depending on the level, you’ll get just a<span class="mark">n alert without b</span>eeps or vibrations, or the iLet Device will alert every 5 minutes with 2 beeps and 1 vibration. These will not escalate to the next volume level.

Possible low priority alerts are Calibration Failed, Sensor Expiring, Sensor Expired, Sensor Failed, Replace Your Sensor, Sensor Reconnecting, CGM Battery Low, Pair a New Transmitter, Transmitter Not Found, Sensor Failed, Brief Sensor Issue, Pairing Failed, Calibration Not Used, Sensor Expiring, Sensor Expired, Readings Stop, Replace Sensor New, Insulin Low, iLet Setup, Insulin Set Expired and Check Body Weight.

### ***5.4 Do's & don’ts***

- A 'malfunction' alarm indicates a system fault. Take note of the fault code and contact customer service.

- If in doubt, refer to the user manual or call customer service.

- If you won't be using your pump for a significant period, put it in storage mode. To do this, go into Settings and choose Other, then choose Shutdown. All dosing will stop. To turn the iLet Device back on, place the iLet Device on the charger. Upon waking up if the battery has been fully depleted, the iLet Device may require you to go through the iLet Startup Sequence again. In that case, a new treatment session will be started using a new body weight entry, but all other user settings on the iLet Device will be retained.

You're now equipped with knowledge about the pump's primary alarms and what actions to take. Therefore, there's no need to panic when you hear an alarm from your iLet Device. Simply check the display screen - it usually provides instructions. If the message isn't clear, refer to the user manual or call customer service. Best of luck!
