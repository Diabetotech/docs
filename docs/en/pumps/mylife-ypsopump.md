***2.1 Parts & buttons on the pump***

The front panel features a black and white touchscreen. The top right corner houses the battery, secured by a battery cap, while the bottom left corner is the home of the prefilled insulin PumpCart and adapter. This adapter is connected to the tubing. The cap of the tubing can be clicked on the cannula base. The sole button on the pump is the Function button, designed for multiple purposes.

***2.2 Battery replacement***

The mylife Ypsopump operates on a standard AAA alkaline battery. When replacing, ensure the negative side goes in first. The battery usually lasts for about a month and should be replaced accordingly. Once in place, place the battery cap on top.

It is advisable to replace the battery cap every three months to maintain the pump's waterproof attribute. You can secure the battery cap with a penny ensuring it sits flush with the pump.

Upon battery replacement, the pump will display a screen featuring the serial number and software version. Accept it by pressing the tick mark, which will then initiate the pump's self-test. Set the time and date next, and you will be directed to the status screen.

***2.3 Unlocking the pump***

By default, the pump is set to the key lock. Pressing the function button will activate the pump. To access the main menu, swipe left on the status screen, and enter 1-2-3.

***2.4 Home screen & pump menu***

The home screen displays the next items.

- On top, you can find the time, date, lock status, and battery level.

- In the center, the status icon represents the current state of insulin activity. This may appear as dashes with a square, indicating halted basal insulin. Alternatively, it could be dashes accompanied by the letter A or B, indicating the active basal profile. If a temporary basal is in effect, the icon will show a percentage sign. And lastly, if a normal, square, or dual bolus is being delivered, the icon will display the corresponding bolus symbol.

- It also shows the rate of basal insulin delivery or the remaining bolus insulin to be delivered.

Swipe left on the home screen and enter 1-2-3 to access the main menu, which comprises seven options: Bolus, Basal rates, Cartridge change, Prime infusion set, Data, Stop/Run mode, and Settings. Many of these options have submenus. A complete menu map and icon overview is available for download below this video.

From the home screen, swiping right will lead you to the Last Bolus view, which displays all information related to the last fully delivered bolus.

Menu navigation is easy – just tap on the icons. A cross indicates cancel, a tick confirms, a forward arrow with a lock symbolizes forward to unlock, an arrow denotes forward, an up arrow represents going up a menu level, and a back arrow means going back one step. Occasionally, an icon may be grayed out, indicating its unavailability.

The function button can guide you back to the status screen at any point. It can also switch the screen on/off and deliver a blind insulin bolus if configured. The function button can be operated in two ways. A short press will execute most functions. For others, like activating the blind bolus, press the function key for over 2 seconds until the pump vibrates.

***2.5 Do's & don’ts***

- Keep the menu overview from the downloads section easily accessible. It will help healthcare providers guide patients remotely.

- The pump is waterproof, but only for an hour until <span class="mark">a depth of 1</span> meter (or 3.3 feet).

- To simplify function button operations, consider rotating the pump's screen 180 degrees. You can do this by going to the settings in the main menu. The icon with the three squares and arrows symbolizes screen rotation. Confirm this to rotate the screen 180 degrees.

- Consider using the pump clip or other carrying material as needed. Also consider using a cover as the pump body can get scratched easily. A list of carrying and protective systems from Ypsomed is available in the downloads. Several other companies also provide carrying and protecting solutions.

- The mylife YpsoPump does not automatically adjust the time and date. So when changing between winter and summer time, or when traveling to a different time zone, you have to adjust the time yourself. You can do that via the settings.

Congratulations! You now understand the basic operations of the mylife YpsoPump, including battery replacement. Our next video will guide you on setting the insulin delivery.

**<u>3. Insulin setup</u>**

In this video, we're exploring how to program insulin delivery on the mylife YpsoPump.

Do bear in mind: If you plan to use this pump as a part of the mylife Loop automated insulin delivery system, insulin delivery settings will be managed via the mylife CamAPS FX app. A dedicated module covers this in detail. However, in case you forget your phone, understanding how to manually control the pump is critical, and that's our focus in this video.

We'll cover:

- Programming basal insulin,

- Starting and stopping a temporary basal,

- And administering an insulin bolus.

- Finally, we'll touch upon some useful tips and precautions.

***3.1 Programming basal insulin***

The mylife Ypsopump permits only two basal insulin rates - basal A and basal B. If a single basal rate suffices for you, simply set basal B to match basal A.

To program the basal rate, navigate to "basal rates" in the main menu and select Basal A. Adjust the insulin units per hour for the first hour of the day by scrolling, then confirm with the check mark. Repeat this process for all 24 hours. Swipe right to revisit the previous hour. Upon completion, the pump displays the total basal insulin dose over a day along with a graph of the scheduled basal rates. Confirm to save the settings. Under basal rates submenu, you'll now see a total daily basal insulin dose listed against Basal A.

As stated, Basal B must be set up similarly to Basal A. Select basal B in the main menu and program the basal insulin rate accordingly.

<span class="mark">If you</span> want to stop basal insulin delivery**<span class="mark">,</span>** this can be done via the main menu, opting for stop mode. Resuming insulin delivery involves the same steps.

A notification will pop up if the pump is in stop mode for over an hour. To receive this alert instantly, long-press the function key, and confirm.

***3.2 Temporary basal***

To start a temporary basal, visit the basal rates submenu and look for the icon symbolizing temporary basal. Here, you can set the rate (from 0 to 200%) and duration (15 minutes to 24 hours). Once confirmed, a percentage symbol appears next to the active basal profile (A or B) on the home screen. The basal rate alternates between the set percentage and the remaining duration of the temporary basal.

When the temporary basal expires, a message prompts for your acknowledgment. Regardless of your response, the pump defaults to the scheduled basal rate.

To cancel the temporary basal, access the basal rates submenu and select the temporary basal icon, which switches the pump back to the pre-set basal rate.

***3.3 Delivering a bolus***

To administer a bolus, access the main menu and click on the bolus icon represented by a knife and fork. A submenu presents three options: standard bolus, extended bolus, and combination bolus. The mylife YpsoPump lacks an inbuilt bolus calculator, so this setting isn't available here.

- For a standard bolus, you can select an amount between 0.1 and 30 units. An icon on the status screen denotes an active bolus, with a countdown showing the remaining units to be delivered. The bolus can be canceled by pressing the cross.

- If you opt for an extended bolus, after setting the bolus units, specify the delivery duration (ranging from 15 minutes to 12 hours). The status screen will then display not only the remaining insulin units to be delivered but also the time left.

- For a combination bolus, input the total bolus units first, then specify the immediate units, and lastly, the duration for the remaining bolus delivery. The top-left icon indicates the active bolus settings.

***3.4 Do's & don’ts***

- The pump lacks a built-in bolus calculator. Hence, if you wish to administer an insulin bolus based on carbohydrate intake, it should be done via the mylife Loop app or the mylife Dose app.

- The maximum bolus on the mylife YpsoPump is fixed at 30 U.

- The mylife Ypsopump also features a blind bolus function to deliver a bolus without viewing the pump. How to activate this function and the steps involved can be found in the instruction manual.

- If you prefer a larger bolus increment, you can modify this. From the main menu, select the settings icon represented by a wrench, and choose Set bolus increment. You can adjust it to 0.1, 0.5, 1, or 2 units.

- At times, you may not be able to deliver a combination bolus. This occurs when the remaining insulin in the reservoir is less than twice the bolus increment.

And there you have it, a guide on how to program and modify insulin settings on the mylife YpsoPump. In our next video, we'll discuss how to replace the insulin reservoir and infusion set.

**<u>4. Reservoir and infusion set replacement</u>**

Welcome, in this video we offer comprehensive guidance on how to change the infusion set and reservoir of your mylife YpsoPump.

Our video commences with an overview of various infusion sets, followed by a systematic, four-step procedure to replace both the insulin and the infusion set. Lastly, we address some essential do's and don'ts.

***4.1 Different infusion sets***

Our demonstration centers around the commonly used mylife Orbit Soft infusion set. It's worth noting that you can also utilize the mylife Orbit Micro infusion set with a steel cannula with the mylife YpsoPump.

- Both infusion sets feature a 90-degree angle cannula, but there are no options for angled cannulas.

- These infusion sets can be manually inserted, or you can use the mylife Orbit inserter for assistance.

- A distinctive aspect of these infusion sets is their 360-degree rotatability, which allows for easy connection and disconnection without needing to look at the infusion site.

- The infusion sets have a unique skin-colored patch that makes them less noticeable, but they might not stick as firmly, causing some users to require additional plaster.

- Finally, the tubing of the mylife Orbit infusion sets are colored blue for easier detection of any air bubbles.

Below this video, we provide an overview of the different available infusion sets, including their tube and cannula lengths and filling volumes.

***4.2 Supplies***

To change the infusion set and insulin, you will need: your room-temperature insulin Pumpcart, your infusion set, a mylife Orbit inserter, an alcohol swab, and your mylife YpsoPump.

***4.3 Reservoir replacement***

Start by disconnecting the infusion set from your body. To do so, gently push the two marked sides of the infusion set, which will detach the tubing cap from the cannula base.

<span class="mark">To replace the insulin cartridge, navigate to the insulin cartridge icon from the main menu. Here, you can retract the drive rod by pressing the double back arrow and confirming. After the drive rod has been retracted to 0%, confirm this and the pump will initiate a self-test.</span>

<span class="mark">Now, rotate the adapter a quarter turn counterclockwise to disconnect it and remove the insulin reservoir, adapter, and tube. Insert a new insulin Pumpcart into the cartridge holder (with the opening upwards) and position the adapter on top. The adapter should be positioned at a 90-degree angle to the pump and rotated a quarter turn towards the pump until it clicks into place.</span>

<span class="mark">If prefilled PumpCarts are not available, you need to fill a mylife Reservoir manually. Disinfect the insulin vial with an alcohol wipe. Take the mylife Reservoir and adapter from the package and attach the adapter to the Reservoir. Move the plunger up and down, then pull it back to fill the Reservoir with air. Attach the adapter to the insulin vial, inject the air into the vial, turn the vial and Reservoir upside down, and draw insulin into the Reservoir. If you see any air bubbles, tap the reservoir, and inject the air back into the insulin vial. Remove the adapter and unscrew the plunger from the Reservoir. You now have a filled mylife Reservoir ready for use in the mylife YpsoPump.</span>

***4.4 Filling the tubing***

Next, fill the tube, a process which is quicker with the mylife YpsoPump than other insulin pumps. From the main menu, navigate to the icon with the needle and insulin droplets (representing infusion set pre-fill) and select tube filling. Set and confirm the filling volume. While the filling is in progress, hold the insulin pump upright and gently tap it against your palm to dislodge any air bubbles in the cartridge. Repeat this process until insulin exits the cannula and no air bubbles are visible in the tube. If you've filled the insulin reservoir yourself, check for any remaining air bubbles in the reservoir using the transparent cartridge holder.

***4.5 Infusion set placement***

You are now ready to insert the infusion set.

First, pick your infusion site, clean your hands, disinfect the site with an alcohol wipe and let it dry. You can manually insert the infusion set at a 90° angle or use the mylife Orbit inserter.

We will demonstrate the placement of a mylife Orbit Soft infusion set with the inserter:

- Insert the infusion set into the inserter until it clicks into place.

- Peel off the protective film from the plaster without touching the adhesive layer.

- Gently rotate the protective cap to remove it from the cannula.

- Turn the lower part of the inserter clockwise until it locks into place. Caution: the inserter is now activated. Avoid pointing it towards your eyes to prevent accidental injuries.

- Position the inserter on the skin and press the button until it clicks.

- Smooth the patch onto the skin, rubbing it gently for a few seconds to enhance adhesion.

- While holding the plaster against your skin, use two fingers of your other hand to grasp the infusion set and remove the Insertion Needle or Insertion Cap by pressing the outer wings of the Insertion Cap and pulling it away.

- Recap the removed Insertion Needle with the blue protective cap and dispose of it in a needle container.

- Return the inserter to its original position.

- Click the tubing cap directly onto the infusion set's cannula base. Turn the tube left and right, pulling up the cap to ensure it's securely engaged.

***4.6 Filling the cannula***

The final step is to fill the subcutaneous cannula. Navigate to the pre-fill infusion set icon from the main menu and select fill cannula. Here, set and administer the filling volume.

***4.7 Do's & don’ts***

- The <span class="mark">mylife Ypsopump is compatible with all short-acting insulins, but PumpCarts are only available for Novorapid and Fiasp. If you prefer Humalog, Lyumjev or Apidra, or you don't have access to PumpCarts, you can use mylife YpsoPump reservoirs. Always use short-acting insulin with a normal concentration of 100 E/ml.</span>

- <span class="mark">Mylife Reservoirs can be refilled once within 7 days of the initial fill. Do not store a refilled reservoir. You can refill it using either a syringe and needle or the adapter. If using a syringe and needle, draw insulin from the vial and transfer it into the empty reservoir, making sure to remove any air bubbles. If using the adapter, you can first fill a new Reservoir from the package and store it in the refrigerator. Next, connect your used reservoir to the adapter, draw insulin into it, and reuse it once.</span>

- <span class="mark">Always verify your glucose level 2 hours after inserting a new in</span>fusion set. This is the only way to confirm proper infusion set insertion and insulin delivery.

- With the mylife Ypsopump, you can change the reservoir, tube and infusion set separately. So, you don't have to swap out your tube and infusion set each time you put in a new insulin reservoir. The infusion sets are also available separately from the tube.

- The infusion sets of the mylife Ypsopump are connected and disconnected differently than other pump infusion sets. To disconnect the mylife Orbit infusion set, gently push the side ridges to release it. To reconnect, click the tubing cap onto the cannula base. Always make sure the tubing cap is securely in place by rotating the tube and pulling the cap upwards.

By now, you should feel confident in replacing both the infusion set and the insulin in your mylife YpsoPump. Thanks to pre-filled insulin vials, the process is relatively smooth. It may take a bit of practice to get used to the infusion sets and the inserter, but it's not more difficult than with other pumps. If you have any doubts, you can always refer back to this tutorial. Best of luck!

**<u>5. Pump alarms</u>**

Welcome to this informative session about potential alarms on the mylife YpsoPump.

We'll delve into the different alarms and warnings that can appear on the mylife YpsoPump and offer some supplementary advice.

***5.1 Alarms***

Alarms are the highest priority alerts. They're signified by an exclamation mark inside a triangle icon, and always result in a cessation of insulin delivery.

Alarms given are primarily tactile and audible and there are 4 escalation levels. The levels escalate at intervals of 5 minutes. If an alarm is confirmed but not dealt with, it is given again by audible and tactile means after 30 minutes.

Alarms are arranged based on their priority, namely no battery, empty battery, battery not suitable, charging internal rechargeable battery, occlusion, no insulin, cartridge empty, automatic stop, or electronic error. What to do in these situations can be found in the manual, accessible in the downloads at the end of this video.

You can access all warnings in the alarm history. To do this, from the main menu, select the data icon depicted by a sheet of paper, and then the alert history icon represented by an exclamation mark within a triangle on a piece of paper.

***5.2 Warnings***

Warnings are lower priority alerts than alarms. They're symbolized by an 'i' icon but do not result in the cancellation of insulin delivery.

Possible warnings include cartridge low, battery low, drive rod not fully retracted, priming not completed, bolus canceled, temporary basal function completed or canceled, insulin pump stopped, and Bluetooth connection failure. Steps to be taken in these scenarios can be found in the manual, accessible in the downloads at the end of this video.

Like alarms, warnings can also be felt and heard in four escalating levels of intensity.

These warnings aren't displayed in the alarm history but need to be acknowledged on the status screen.

***5.3 Do's & don’ts***

- If you're uncertain about what to do, always refer to the user manual or contact customer service. The manual not only covers warnings and notifications, but it also outlines other incidents. It details what actions to take if certain icons or functions are unavailable, or if there is insulin, dirt, or water in the pump. In such situations, always consult the manual.

- A specific warning to note is the internal battery charging. The mylife YpsoPump features an internal rechargeable battery that's powered by the inserted alkaline battery. If the internal battery is heavily charged, it may deplete. In this case, the "charge internal battery" warning displays. The internal battery charges through the inserted AAA battery and is signified by arrows circling an hourglass. Charging could take up to 20 minutes. Active boluses and temporary basal will be halted during this period.

- Another peculiar warning is the electronic error. In this situation, you'll immediately receive an audible and palpable signal, and all pump functions will be canceled. This is, thankfully, a rare occurrence. You'll be presented with two alternating screens instructing how to restart the pump. Disconnect the infusion set from your body and remove the battery. Press the function button for 2 seconds to put the pump into storage mode. The pump can then be restarted by reinserting the battery. Verify the settings and insert a new insulin reservoir and infusion set. Then, you can restart the pump.

- If for any reason you plan on temporarily not using the pump, it's recommended to place it in storage mode. Achieve this by stopping the pump and removing the battery. Replace the battery cover, and after 5 minutes, the pump will switch to storage mode.

Now, you're familiar with the primary alarms on the pump and what steps to take. Thus, there's no need to panic when you receive a pump alarm. If your pump is linked to the mylife Loop app, most alarms will typically display on your mobile phone, not your pump. If there's any confusion, always refer to the user manual or contact customer service. Good luck!
