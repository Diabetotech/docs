## **<u>4. Reservoir and infusion set replacement</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on reservoir and infusion set replacement on the Tandem t:slim X2 insulin pump.

In this video, we will cover the different infusion set options, step-by-step instructions for replacing the reservoir and infusion set, and helpful tips for success.

### ***4.1 Different infusion sets***

The Autosoft 90 infusion set is the most widely used infusion set for the Tandem pump.

Other alternatives include the Autosoft 30, the Varisoft with an angled cannula, and the TruSteel with a steel cannula. A detailed overview of these infusion sets, including tubing and cannula lengths and filling volumes, can be found in the download below this video.

### ***4.2 Supplies***

Start by washing your hands and ensuring your workspace is clean. Gather these items: a reservoir, a pull-up syringe, a pull-up needle, an infusion set, your insulin (at room temperature), and your pump.

### ***4.3 Reservoir replacement***

To determine how much insulin to pull into the syringe, multiply your total daily dose by 3 (for 3 days) and add approximately 50 units for the tube filling. If using a vial, disinfect the port and inject air to help the insulin flow out easily.

Once filled, remove any air from the reservoir by inserting the needle into the filling port and drawing out air. Then, slowly inject insulin into the new reservoir.

Next, navigate to 'options' on your pump, choose 'charge', and then 'replace reservoir'. Disconnect the infusion set from your body and remove the old reservoir. Insert the new reservoir securely. The pump will detect the reservoir and suggest tube filling.

### ***4.4 Filling the tube***

Start with a new infusion set and its tube. Carefully open the packaging, then release the lid by applying pressure to the three points on the plastic sheath with one hand while pulling off the lid with the other. Remember, the Autosoft 90 system is quite delicate due to its soft plastic construction, so avoid bending it too much or touching the needle. Gently extract the tube from the sheath, taking care not to tug too forcefully. Now, connect the tube to the reservoir's luer lock and give it an extra quarter-turn clockwise to ensure a secure fit.

Next, activate your pump to begin the tube filling process. It is essential to keep your pump upright during this procedure so that any air bubbles can rise to the top. Pay close attention to the tube for the presence of air bubbles. Once you observe a few drops of insulin coming out from the needle, you can stop the filling process on the pump.

Important to note, you should never perform this process while the infusion set is connected to your body. Also, ensure that you fill the tube with a minimum of 10 units of insulin.

### ***4.5 Infusion set placement***

Now, we're ready to apply the infusion set. We will demonstrate the Autosoft 90 infusion set. Start by sanitizing your skin and peel away the paper backing from the infusion set patch. Be sure to keep the infusion set firmly in place on the needle during this process. Next, prep the insertion set by tugging on the white plastic until you hear a click. At this point, you can safely remove the needle guard. Locate one of the two openings on the insertion device and insert the tube. Position the prepared device on your cleaned skin and press the needle into place by applying gentle pressure on the two circular depressions. Press the white plastic one more time. Lastly, take care to gently remove the insertion device.

<span class="mark">You've now successfully replaced the insulin reservoir and infusion set! Remember to do this every three days, even if the reservoir is not yet empty.</span>

### ***4.6 Cannula Filling***

<span class="mark">When you fill the tubing of the Autosoft infusion set, the cannula is filled automatically. If you ever replace only the infusion set but keep the existing tubing, you should manually fill the cannula. This can be done through the same pump menu. Fill the cannula with 0.3 units for 6-mm needles or 0.5 units for 9-mm needles, then resume insulin delivery. Don't forget to</span> click "OK" when prompted to continue insulin delivery. If you forget, an alarm will sound as a reminder.

### ***4.7 Do's & don’ts***

- <span class="mark">The insulin approved in the Tandem t:slim X2 pump is Novorapid, Humalog and Novolog. Lyumjev is approved for use in Europe. Fiasp remains off-label.</span>

- Remember, 2 hours after inserting a new set, you should check your blood glucose. Checking your blood glucose is the only way to confirm your infusion set is properly inserted and that you're r<span class="mark">eceiving insulin.</span>

- <span class="mark">Prior to disposing of the insertion device, reattach the lid until it clicks.</span>

- <span class="mark">For swimming or showering, you can disconnect your tubing from the infusion set and apply the provided protective cover. To reconnect, simply remove the cover and snap the tube back on.</span>
