## **<u>5. Pump alarms</u>**

In this video, we dive into the Tandem t:slim X2 insulin pump's alarm system. We'll discuss the different kinds of alarms, alerts, and reminders, and share some best practices.

When multiple reminders, alerts, or alarms are activated concurrently, the system displays them in the order of priority – alarms first, then alerts, and finally reminders. Each one requires individual confirmation until all are acknowledged.

### ***5.1 Alarms***

Alarms act as urgent notifications, alerting you to a current or imminent interruption in insulin delivery, such as when the insulin reservoir is depleted. These require immediate attention.

These alarms are signaled with three cycles of three tones or vibrations, as per your chosen volume/vibration settings. If left unacknowledged, alarms escalate to the maximum sound and vibration level and continue to repeat until the issue is rectified.

### ***5.2 Alerts***

Alerts inform you about safety conditions that you need to know about, like low insulin levels.

These alerts are expressed with two sequences of three tones or vibrations, following your volume/vibration preference. They continue to repeat until they are acknowledged, but they do not escalate.

### ***5.3 Reminders***

Reminders notify you about an option you've set, such as a reminder to check your blood glucose after a bolus.

Reminders notify you with a single sequence of 3 notes or a single vibration depending on your volume and vibrate settings. They repeat every 10 minutes until acknowledged. Reminders do not escalate.

### ***5.4 Do's & don’ts***

- A 'malfunction' alarm indicates a system fault. Take note of the fault code and contact customer service.

- If in doubt, refer to the user manual or call customer service.

- If you won't be using your pump for a significant period, put it in storage mode. To do this, connect it to a power source and hold the Screen on/Quick bolus button for 30 seconds. You'll hear three beeps before it switches to storage mode. Disconnect the pump from the power source. To deactivate storage mode, reconnect the charger.

You're now equipped with knowledge about the pump's primary alarms and what actions to take. Therefore, there's no need to panic when you hear an alarm from your pump. Simply check the display screen - it usually provides instructions. If the message isn't clear, refer to the user manual or call customer service. Best of luck!
