**<u>2. Pump Operation</u>**

Welcome to our comprehensive guide on the Tandem Mobi insulin pump! By the end of this module, you'll have a thorough understanding of how the Tandem Mobi insulin pump works, its various features, including the pump's components, charging the pump, unlocking process, the screens and menus on the Tandem Mobi Mobile app and do’s and don’ts.

### ***2.1 Parts & buttons*** 

The Tandem Mobi pump is a modular semi-patch pomp. It has around the same size and weight as an Omnipod pump. It consists of a durable pump base that has a guarantee of 4 years, and a disposable cartridge and t:lock Connector that should be replaced every 3 days.

The pump has no screen and is controlled by the Tandem Mobi Mobile app. It has 1 pump button on the bottom. This button is used to turn the pump on or off, deliver a Quick Bolus, snooze alerts and alarms and activate the pump status lights.

Above the pump button there are 2 status lights that light up in different colors and patterns to let you know what the pump is doing even if you don't have your smartphone with you. To view the lights you need to press and release the pump button. After a few seconds the pump status lights will light up and the pattern will display for 10 seconds.

Each color has a different meaning:

\- RED represents an alarm or malfunction. This means that all insulin delivery has stopped. If you see this please check your pump as soon as possible.

\- YELLOW represents an alert or a reminder such as a sensor expiring soon or low battery

\- BLUE represents bolus delivery or the tubing being filled during the cartridge loading process.

\- WHITE represents either that the pump is charging or insulin delivery has been stopped manually

\- GREEN represents basal delivery or a temp basal rate is being delivered.

Refer to your user guide to learn more about the pump status lights blinking patterns. Understanding these components and indicators will ensure you can effectively manage your insulin delivery with the Tandem Mobi Insulin Pump.

### ***2.2 Charging the Pump***

When you receive the pump, you'll need to charge it first. The Tandem Mobi pump does not have a charging port. Instead it comes with an inductive charging pad that connects to a power source using a USB-A to a USB-C cable. It also includes accessories for charging from a wall outlet. You should only use accessories that have been provided by Tandem Diabetes Care and we don't recommend using adapters or extension cords.

While charging your system make sure the inductive charging pad is plugged into a functioning wall outlet. There is a logo on the back of the pump and a t- logo on the top of the charging pad. Align these logos and note that the pump will not be centered on the pad.

There are two signs to look for that your pump is charging

- first the charging pad will briefly light up

- second you'll see a lightning bolt icon on the top of the battery symbol in the upper left corner of the tandem Mobi mobile app.

The pump status lights can also be used to show the charge amount

- one of the lights will blink if the charge is less than 50%

- if the charge is more than 50% one light will be solid white while the other blinks until the pump is fully charged.

You will know charging is complete when the pump status lights above the pump button are white. A full charge will typically last between 3 and 5 days depending on your use.

If your pump power is very low, one light will blink red or it may not turn on. It is recommended to avoid fully draining the battery and charging the pump daily for a short period of time, like 10 to 15 minutes.

The pump will work normally while it is charging so you don't need to disconnect if you don't want to. However if you do disconnect while charging you may need to replace missed bas<span class="mark">al insulin. Never disconnect your pump during the delivery of an insulin bolus, and always ch</span>eck your blood glucose levels before disconnecting and again before reconnecting.

If you lose any accessories or need replacements, you can contact Tandem customer technical support.

### ***2.3 Unlocking the pump***

Next, we'll proceed to show you how to turn the pump on and off.

To turn the pump off, place it on the charging pad and make sure the logos on the pump and the pad align. Don't worry if it looks a little off center. Now hold the pump button for 20 seconds. The pump will beep three times before it will turn off.

To turn on the pump, place it on the charging pad. While it is on the charging pad, press and hold the pump button until you see the pump status lights on the pump light up. The pump will beep four times when it has turned on and is ready to use.

Be aware that when you first receive your pump you may be able to see the pump status lights when the pump button is pressed. This does not necessarily mean that the pump is turned on.

### ***2.5 Do's & don’ts***

Here are a few important tips to keep in mind:

- You can find a comprehensive menu overview in the downloads section, which can be saved for easy reference when providing remote guidance as a healthcare provider.

- The pump is water resistant, for up to 2 hours at a depth of 2,4 meters (or 8 feet).

- <span class="mark">The Tandem Mobi mobile app is available only on selected iPhone models and is now gradually rolling out to selected Android devices. You</span> can check your device’s compatibility on Tandem’s website.

- The Tandem Mobi mobile app must run in the background in order to receive and transmit data to and from your pump, as well as to the Tandem cloud. Be sure to disable battery optimization on your smartphone to ensure the Tandem Mobi mobile app can receive alerts and alarms.

- It’s also possible that there is a Bluetooth disconnection between the Mobi pump and your smartphone.

<!-- -->

- In that case you will see a “Pump connection lost” notification banner at the top of the homescreen of the Tandem Mobi Mobile app, together with how much time has lapsed since your pump disconnected from your smartphone. You will also see a gray shaded area on the graph since no data can be displayed when the connection is lost.

<!-- -->

- The Mobi pump will continue to deliver insulin as intended, receive glucose values if paired to a CGM, and all pump alerts, alarms, reminders, and malfunctions will annunciate on your pump even when disconnected from your smartphone.

- The Bolus screen on your Tandem Mobi Mobile app will be inaccessible when disconnected. However, if enabled previously, the Pump button on the pump may still be used to deliver a Quick Bolus.

- Take these steps to reconnect your pump to your smartphone:

  - Make sure that your pump and smartphone are within <span class="mark">1.5 meter or f</span>ive feet of one another and without any obstruction between the two (including body parts).

  - If you have persistent issues with the Tandem Mobi mobile app, close or force stop the Tandem Mobi mobile app to end the current session. If the issue persists, try re-pairing the pump.

- The Tandem Mobi pump does not automatically adjust the time and date. When changing between winter and summer time, or when traveling to a different time zone, you need to adjust the time yourself. You can do that via the pump settings.

- Finally, it’s important to note that you should avoid placing metal objects on the charging pad.

**<u>3. Insulin set-up</u>**

Welcome to our detailed guide on insulin delivery set-up for the Tandem <span class="mark">Mobi</span> insulin pump.

We'll explore how to program basal insulin, set a temporary basal rate, and administer an insulin bolus. Additionally, we'll share some crucial do's and don'ts to enhance your pump experience.

### ***3.1 Programming basal insulin*** 

- To access your personal profile tap ‘settings’ from the navigation bar, next tap ‘Pump’, tap ‘Personal profiles’, tap ‘Add New Profile’ then tap the ‘Profile Name field’.

- Enter a profile name up to 16 characters and tap ‘next’. Tap ‘Add Segment’ to begin setting insulin delivery settings. The first Time Segment will start at 12 AM.

- Within each time segment, you must program a basal rate, correction factor, carb ratio and target BG. To enter values for each variable, tap the area labeled “Tap to set up”. A number keypad will appear, and once you have entered the first one, simply tap the field to enter the next value. Tap Done to close the keypad.

- Review all of the values you just entered and tap ‘Save’. A Time Segment has been saved banner is displayed at the top of the Tandem Mobi mobile app.

- If you need to add more time segments tap the plus icon. When adding more time segments, any settings that you entered in the previous time segment are copied and appear in the new segment. This allows you to simply adjust only the specific settings you want rather than having to enter them all over again.

- Tap ‘Add Segment’ then tap ‘ Start Time’. Using the on-screen keypad enter the time that you want the time segment to begin and tap’ Done’. Remember that this segment will be active from the start time until 12 AM or until the next segment you enter.

- When you have confirmed your settings and are done adding time segments, tap the back arrow icon to program your bolus settings. First tap the ‘Bolus Settings’ panel to get started. <span class="mark">By default the insulin duration is set at 5 hours and the carbohydrates are turned on. We recommend to leave these settings on the default values.</span>

- To delete a time segment, tap the start time of the time segment you wish to delete and tap ‘Delete Segment’. Tap ‘Yes’ to confirm.

- If you want to Rename an existing Profile, from navigation bar, tap ‘ setting’. Tap ‘ Pump’, then ‘Personal Profiles’. Tap the name of the Personal Profile to be renamed. Tap ‘Profile Options’ then tap ‘ Rename’.

- When you have confirmed your settings, tap the ‘Dashboard’ to return to the home screen. If you need to add additional personal profiles repeat the steps outline in this video.

- You may also duplicate an existing personal profile. Then select edit to change only certain settings to create a new profile.

To stop basal insulin, from the navigation bar, tap ‘Actions’ then select 'Stop insulin' and tap ‘Yes’. When insulin is stopped, the home screen will show the status “All deliveries stopped”. Restarting basal insulin can be done in the same way via ‘Actions’.

### ***3.2 Temporary basal***

To start a temporary basal, navigate to 'Actions' from the navigation bar and select 'Temp Rate'. Here, you can set the percentage and duration of your choice. Tap Dashboard on the Navigation bar to view the Dashboard screen and confirm the icon indicating a Temp Rate is active. A 'T' in a yellow box means Temp Rate is Active, while a ‘T’ in a red box means Temp Rate of 0 u/hr is active. You can always manually turn off the temporary basal, which reverts the 'T' back to the current system settings.

***3.3 Delivering a bolus***

Administering a bolus is straightforward. From the start screen, hit the bolus button, input the carbohydrate grams and, if necessary, a finger prick value. If paired with a glucose sensor, the last glucose value will auto-fill. The pump recommends an insulin dose, which you can override.

A handy tip is the option of extended bolus delivery. When confirming a bolus, you can opt to make it an extended bolus and adjust the percentage and time duration as needed.

### ***3.4 Do's & don’ts***

- Ensure maximum bolus limits are set slightly above expected bolus doses. You can find the Max Bolus via the pump settings. Tap ‘Delivery Limits’, then tap ‘Max Bolus’. The maximum bolus defaults to 10 units, but you can set it between 1 and 25 U.

- <span class="mark">It's recommended to set the Target BG value at 110 mg/dl or 6.5 mmol/l, as this is consistent with the target used when using Control-IQ technology</span>**.**

- It is recommended that all users set up the Quick Bolus function which enables you to deliver a bolus by simply pressing the pump button. It is a way to deliver a bolus by following beep/vibration commands without navigating through or viewing the pump screen.

And that's it! With this knowledge, you can effectively set-up and adjust insulin on the Tandem Mobi Insulin pump. In our next video, we'll walk you through changing the insulin reservoir and infusion set.

**<u>4. Reservoir and infusion set replacement</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on reservoir and infusion set replacement on the Tandem Mobi insulin pump!

In this video, we will cover the different infusion set options, step-by-step instructions for replacing the reservoir and infusion set, and helpful tips for success.

### ***4.1 Different infusion sets***

The Autosoft 90 infusion set is the most widely used infusion set for the Tandem pump. Other alternatives include the Autosoft 30, the Varisoft with an angled cannula, and the TruSteel with a steel cannula.

Additionally, Tandem provides different lengths of tubing. Most people use the short tubing of (12 cm or 5 inches) to keep the pump close to the infusion site on the skin, but you can also opt for the longer tubing options of (60 to 110 cm or 23 to 43 inches) to wear the pump in your pocket.

A detailed overview of these infusion sets, including tubing and cannula lengths and filling volumes, can be found in the download below this video.

### **4.2 Reservoir & Infusion Set Replacement**

Start by washing your hands and ensuring your workspace is clean. Gather these items: a reservoir, a cartridge, your insulin (at room temperature), an infusion set, some alcohol wipes, and your pump.

### ***\#1 Reservoir replacement***

Make sure your hands are clean and you've swabbed the top of the vial with alcohol first. Let's prepare the new cartridge. You do this by pulling down the fill rod and then pushing it back up to force air out of the cartridge. Next pull the fill rod down to the desired volume. You can measure this by using the top ring on the plunger to align <span class="mark">with the de</span>sired amount of insulin. Now it's time to fill the cartridge. Start by placing the insulin vial upright on a flat surface. You'll push the vial adapter down onto the vial. Next push the fill rod down to force air from the cartridge into the vial. Be sure to maintain pressure on the fill Rod. Then turn the set with the vial still secured upside down and slowly release the fill Rod. Insulin will begin to flow from the vial into the cartridge. From here, you can slowly pull back the fill Rod to the desired amount of insulin. Keep in min<span class="mark">d that there must be at least 30 units of insulin remaining in the cartridge once the tubing is filled. Next tap and rotate the cartridge to release any potential air bubbles. If you see air bubbles, bubbl</span>es push up on the fill Rod to force them out. Keep in mind that pushing out air bubbles may also push out some insulin. Make sure to check that you have enough insulin in the cartridge. Finish the fill process by removing the vial from the vial adapter.

Next, it's time to remove the cartridge from the set. Start by unscrewing the fill Rod counterclockwise then gently release the cartridge from the set. Now unscrew the

vial adapter counterclockwise to remove it from the t-lock connector.

You'll be prompted to remove the empty cartridge from the pump by

rotating it counterclockwise. Be sure the piston on your pump is in the down position. It should look like this, not this. Now place the newly filled cartridge on the pump and rotate it clockwise until it clicks into place then tap continue.

<span class="mark">Be sure you're following all proper disposal methods for needles, cartridges, set components and infusion sets.</span>

### ***\#2 Filling the tube***

Next you'll fill the tube. Confirm that the infusion set is disconnected from your body. Attach tubing from a new infusion set to the t-lock connector on the cartridge. Then tap continue. Hold the pump with the t-lock connector facing up. This will make sure any air in the cartridge will be pushed through the tubing first. Then tap yes on the mobile app. Make sure that your infusion set is disconnected from your body. Then press and hold the pump button to fill the infusion set tubing with insulin. Hold the pump upright and keep the pump button pressed, until you see drops of insulin at the end of the infusion set. Once you see drops of insulin release the pump button. This process is much faster with tandem Mobi than <span class="mark">with</span> the t-slim X2 pump. So pay close attention. Keep in mind that the pump may pause at regular intervals during the process. This is normal and allows you to check for drops of insulin. Tap yes if you see drops of insulin at the end of your tubing

### ***\#3 Infusion set placement***

Now, we're ready to apply the infusion set. We will demonstrate the Autosoft 90 infusion set. Start by sanitizing your skin and peel away the paper backing from the infusion set patch. Be sure to keep the infusion set firmly in place on the needle during this process. Next, prep the insertion set by tugging on the white plastic until you hear a click. At this point, you can safely remove the needle guard. Locate one of the two <span class="mark">depressions</span> on the insertion device and insert the tube. Position the prepared device on your cleaned skin and press the needle into place by applying gentle pressure on the two circular depressions. Press the white plastic one more time. Lastly, take care to gently remove the insertion device.

<span class="mark">Don't forget to click "Finish" when prompted to continue insulin delivery. If you forget, an alarm will sound as a reminder.</span>

<span class="mark">You've now successfully replaced the insulin cartridge and infusion set! Remember to do this at least once every three days, even if the cartridge is not yet empty.</span>

### ***\#4 Cannula Filling***

<span class="mark">When you fill the tubing of the Autosoft infusion set, the cannula is filled automatically. If you ever replace only the infusion set but keep the existing tubing, you should manually fill the cannula. This can be done through the same pump menu. Fill the cannula of the Autosoft 90 infusion set with 0.3 units for 6-mm needles or 0.5 units for 9-mm needles. Angled cannula's need a filling volume of 0.7 units, and steel cannula's do not need to be filled.</span>

***4.3 Do's & don’ts***

- The insulin approved in the Tandem Mobi Insulin pump is NovoLog <span class="mark">or NovoRapid</span> and Humalog with a concentration of 100 U/ml..

- Remember, 2 hours after inserting a new set, you should check your blood glucose. Checking your blood glucose is the only way to confirm your infusion set <span class="mark">is properly inserted and that you're receiving insulin.</span>

- <span class="mark">Prior to disposing of the insertion device, reattach the lid until it clicks.</span>

- <span class="mark">When temporarily removing your insulin pump, it’s best to cover the connect piece of your infusion set. If you leave the protective cover open, dirt may enter into the opening which may cause infections or occlusions.</span>

- <span class="mark">Make sure you're inspecting the tubing for air bubbles. Air and the tubing occupy space where insulin should be. And this could affect insulin delivery. Remember that changes in temperature or weather can cause bubbles. If you find bubbles in your cartridge, consider wearing the pump with the tubing angled down or to the side to help prevent the air from getting into the tubing. If you see air bubbles in the tubing, disconnect the infusion site from your body. Then under the load cartridge menu, select fill tubing and prime the tubing until the air is purged completely out. This will push the air out of the tubing.</span>

- <span class="mark">Be aware that some skin care products such as lotions, sunscreens and insec</span>t repellant can cause cracks in the plast<span class="mark">ic of t</span>he cartridge or pump. Do not allow these products to come into contact with the pump or cartridge. Remember to always wash your hands before handling your pump or cartridge after using these types of products.

So now, you're equipped with the knowledge to replace the insulin cartridge and infusion set of the Tandem Mobi Insulin pump. If you make a mistake, don't worry – it's perfectly normal. Keep calm, follow the steps. Remember, if all else fails, there's always the <span class="mark">Customer Service</span> and a lot of useful YouTube videos available. Also, don't forget that you can contact your diabetes team, who also have an on-call service, for medical support. Keep up the good work! Good luck!

**<u>5. Pump alarms</u>**

In this video, we dive into the Tandem Mobi Insulin pump's alarm system. <span class="mark">We'll discuss the different kinds of alarms, alerts, and reminders, and share some best practices.</span>

<span class="mark">The Notifications screen informs you when information related to your pump or CGM requires attention. The outline color of the notification indicates the importance of the information.</span>

- <span class="mark">Red stands for alarms, and indicate that a malfunction has occurred on the pump and insulin delivery has been stopped.</span>

- <span class="mark">Yellow stands for Pump or CGM alerts.</span>

- <span class="mark">Blue indicates a reminder or informational message. Insulin therapy is not impacted.</span>

<span class="mark">Dismiss appears if you slide an alert notification with your finger to the left. Tap this icon to dismiss the alert.</span>

<span class="mark">Delete appears if you slide a reminder or informational notification with your finger to the left. Tap this icon to delete the reminder or informational message</span>

<span class="mark">If multiple reminders, alerts, and alarms happen at the same time, alarms will be displayed first, alerts will be displayed second, and reminders will be displayed third. Each must be acknowledged separately until all have been acknowledged.</span>

<span class="mark">If push notifications are enabled on your smartphone, and the Tandem Mobi mobile app is open, you will get the alert notification on the lock screen of your smartphone.</span>

### ***5.1 Alarms***

Alarms act as urgent notifications, alerting you to a current or imminent interruption in insulin delivery, such as when the insulin cartridge is empty. These require immediate attention.

<span class="mark">These alarms are signaled with three sequences of three tones or vibrations, as per your chosen volume/vibration settings. If left unacknowledged, alarms escalate to the maximum sound and vibration level and continue to repeat until the issue is fixed. The pump status lights will light up in red.</span>

<span class="mark">The Auto-Off Alarm is a user settable alarm. Other alarms are Resume Insulin Alarms, Low Power Alarm, Empty Cartridge Alarm, Cartridge Error Alarm, Temperature Alarm, Occlusion Alarms, Pump Button Alarm and Pump and IOB Reset Alarm.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">5.2 Alerts</span>***

<span class="mark">Alerts inform you about safety conditions that you need to know about, like low insulin levels.</span>

<span class="mark">These alerts are expressed with two sequences of three tones or two vibrations, following your volume/vibration preference. They continue to repeat until they are acknowledged, but they do not escalate. The pump status lights will light up in yellow.</span>

<span class="mark">Enabling the Snooze function allows you to silence this beep or vibration for a set period of time in the event that you are unable to look at your Tandem Mobi mobile app.</span>

<span class="mark">The Low Insulin Alert and Max Basal Alert are user settable alerts.</span>

<span class="mark">Other pump alerts are Pump Low Power Alert, Incomplete Bolus Alert, Incomplete Load Sequence Alert, Incomplete Setting Alert, Basal Rate Required Alert, Basal and Carb Ratio Required Alert, Max Hourly Bolus Alert, Max Bolus Alert, Max Basal Alert, Min Basal Alert, Pump Button Alert, Quick Bolus Alert, Data Error Alert and Temperature Alert.</span>

<span class="mark">Possible CGM alerts are different Calibration Alerts and Transmitter Alerts, CGM High Alert, CGM Low Alert, CGM Fixed Low Alert, CGM (Rapid) Rise Alert, CgM (Rapid) Fall Alert, Unknown Sensor Glucose Reading, Out of Range Alert, Failed Sensor Error, and CGM Unavailable.</span>

<span class="mark">Possible Control-IQ technology Alerts are Out of Range Alert, Control-IQ Technology Low Alert, Control-IQ Technology High Alert and Max Insulin Alert.</span>

***5.3 Reminders***

Reminders notify you about an option you've set, such as a reminder to check your blood glucose after a bolus.

<span class="mark">Reminders notify you with a single sequence of 3 notes or 3 vibrations depending on your volume and vibrate settings. They repeat every 10 minutes until acknowledged. Reminders do not escalate.</span>

<span class="mark">Examples are Low BG reminder, High BG Reminder, After Bolus BG Reminder, Missed Meal Bolus Reminder, Bolus Confirmation Reminder and Site Reminder.</span>

### ***5.4 Do's & don’ts***

- <span class="mark">A specific alarm is the 'malfunction' alarm. This indicates a critical error and all deliveries have been stopped. You will need to switch to your backup insulin delivery method. Take note of the fault code and contact customer service.</span>

- <span class="mark">If in doubt, refer to the user manual or call customer service.</span>

- <span class="mark">You can enable push notifications through the tandem Mobi mobile app. Just tap settings then alerts and sounds and then app notification settings. Make sure your notification preferences are enabled and that your smartphone will allow notifications from outside of the app. To do this, go to settings, then notifications, then toggle allow notifications to the on position. It’s recommended that you turn on critical alerts and time sensitive notifications. It’s also recommended that you leave the app running in the background and do not close or force quit the app. Acknowledging and clearing alerts within the tandem Mobi app will prevent the notification from recurring and can also help save your battery life.</span>

- <span class="mark">Enabling the Snooze function allows you to silence this beep or vibration for a set period of time in the event that you are unable to look at your Tandem Mobi mobile app. To enable and set up Snooze, go to the Pump Sounds in the Alerts & Sounds menu.</span>

- <span class="mark">If you won't be using your pump for a significant period, put it in storage mode. Place the pump on the charging pad and then press and hold down the Pump button for 20 seconds. The pump will beep 3 times before going into storage mode. Remove the pump from the power source.</span>

<span class="mark">Now you know what to do in the event of the pump's primary alarm, and you are more prepared. Therefore, if you hear a sound in your pump, there’s no need to panic. Just look at the screen and instructions are typically pr</span>ovided there. Consult the user manual or call customer care if the message is unclear. Wishing you luck!
