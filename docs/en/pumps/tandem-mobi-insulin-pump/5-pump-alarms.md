## **<u>5. Pump alarms</u>**

In this video, we dive into the Tandem Mobi Insulin pump's alarm system. <span class="mark">We'll discuss the different kinds of alarms, alerts, and reminders, and share some best practices.</span>

<span class="mark">The Notifications screen informs you when information related to your pump or CGM requires attention. The outline color of the notification indicates the importance of the information.</span>

- <span class="mark">Red stands for alarms, and indicate that a malfunction has occurred on the pump and insulin delivery has been stopped.</span>

- <span class="mark">Yellow stands for Pump or CGM alerts.</span>

- <span class="mark">Blue indicates a reminder or informational message. Insulin therapy is not impacted.</span>

<span class="mark">Dismiss appears if you slide an alert notification with your finger to the left. Tap this icon to dismiss the alert.</span>

<span class="mark">Delete appears if you slide a reminder or informational notification with your finger to the left. Tap this icon to delete the reminder or informational message</span>

<span class="mark">If multiple reminders, alerts, and alarms happen at the same time, alarms will be displayed first, alerts will be displayed second, and reminders will be displayed third. Each must be acknowledged separately until all have been acknowledged.</span>

<span class="mark">If push notifications are enabled on your smartphone, and the Tandem Mobi mobile app is open, you will get the alert notification on the lock screen of your smartphone.</span>

### ***5.1 Alarms***

Alarms act as urgent notifications, alerting you to a current or imminent interruption in insulin delivery, such as when the insulin cartridge is empty. These require immediate attention.

<span class="mark">These alarms are signaled with three sequences of three tones or vibrations, as per your chosen volume/vibration settings. If left unacknowledged, alarms escalate to the maximum sound and vibration level and continue to repeat until the issue is fixed. The pump status lights will light up in red.</span>

<span class="mark">The Auto-Off Alarm is a user settable alarm. Other alarms are Resume Insulin Alarms, Low Power Alarm, Empty Cartridge Alarm, Cartridge Error Alarm, Temperature Alarm, Occlusion Alarms, Pump Button Alarm and Pump and IOB Reset Alarm.</span>

### ***<span class="mark">5.2 Alerts</span>***

<span class="mark">Alerts inform you about safety conditions that you need to know about, like low insulin levels.</span>

<span class="mark">These alerts are expressed with two sequences of three tones or two vibrations, following your volume/vibration preference. They continue to repeat until they are acknowledged, but they do not escalate. The pump status lights will light up in yellow.</span>

<span class="mark">Enabling the Snooze function allows you to silence this beep or vibration for a set period of time in the event that you are unable to look at your Tandem Mobi mobile app.</span>

<span class="mark">The Low Insulin Alert and Max Basal Alert are user settable alerts.</span>

<span class="mark">Other pump alerts are Pump Low Power Alert, Incomplete Bolus Alert, Incomplete Load Sequence Alert, Incomplete Setting Alert, Basal Rate Required Alert, Basal and Carb Ratio Required Alert, Max Hourly Bolus Alert, Max Bolus Alert, Max Basal Alert, Min Basal Alert, Pump Button Alert, Quick Bolus Alert, Data Error Alert and Temperature Alert.</span>

<span class="mark">Possible CGM alerts are different Calibration Alerts and Transmitter Alerts, CGM High Alert, CGM Low Alert, CGM Fixed Low Alert, CGM (Rapid) Rise Alert, CgM (Rapid) Fall Alert, Unknown Sensor Glucose Reading, Out of Range Alert, Failed Sensor Error, and CGM Unavailable.</span>

<span class="mark">Possible Control-IQ technology Alerts are Out of Range Alert, Control-IQ Technology Low Alert, Control-IQ Technology High Alert and Max Insulin Alert.</span>

***5.3 Reminders***

Reminders notify you about an option you've set, such as a reminder to check your blood glucose after a bolus.

<span class="mark">Reminders notify you with a single sequence of 3 notes or 3 vibrations depending on your volume and vibrate settings. They repeat every 10 minutes until acknowledged. Reminders do not escalate.</span>

<span class="mark">Examples are Low BG reminder, High BG Reminder, After Bolus BG Reminder, Missed Meal Bolus Reminder, Bolus Confirmation Reminder and Site Reminder.</span>

### ***5.4 Do's & don’ts***

- <span class="mark">A specific alarm is the 'malfunction' alarm. This indicates a critical error and all deliveries have been stopped. You will need to switch to your backup insulin delivery method. Take note of the fault code and contact customer service.</span>

- <span class="mark">If in doubt, refer to the user manual or call customer service.</span>

- <span class="mark">You can enable push notifications through the tandem Mobi mobile app. Just tap settings then alerts and sounds and then app notification settings. Make sure your notification preferences are enabled and that your smartphone will allow notifications from outside of the app. To do this, go to settings, then notifications, then toggle allow notifications to the on position. It’s recommended that you turn on critical alerts and time sensitive notifications. It’s also recommended that you leave the app running in the background and do not close or force quit the app. Acknowledging and clearing alerts within the tandem Mobi app will prevent the notification from recurring and can also help save your battery life.</span>

- <span class="mark">Enabling the Snooze function allows you to silence this beep or vibration for a set period of time in the event that you are unable to look at your Tandem Mobi mobile app. To enable and set up Snooze, go to the Pump Sounds in the Alerts & Sounds menu.</span>

- <span class="mark">If you won't be using your pump for a significant period, put it in storage mode. Place the pump on the charging pad and then press and hold down the Pump button for 20 seconds. The pump will beep 3 times before going into storage mode. Remove the pump from the power source.</span>

<span class="mark">Now you know what to do in the event of the pump's primary alarm, and you are more prepared. Therefore, if you hear a sound in your pump, there’s no need to panic. Just look at the screen and instructions are typically pr</span>ovided there. Consult the user manual or call customer care if the message is unclear. Wishing you luck!
