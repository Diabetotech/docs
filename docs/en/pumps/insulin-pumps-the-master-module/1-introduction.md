## **<u>1. Introduction (3 min)</u>**

Welcome to this journey into the world of insulin pumps!

Insulin pumps are revolutionizing diabetes management, allowing individuals to fine-tune their insulin dosages, mimicking the operations of a healthy pancreas. Compared to multiple daily injections, insulin pump therapy is linked to reductions in HbA1c levels, fewer episodes of hypoglycemia, tighter glucose control, and typically, an enhancement in the quality of life as insulin can be dosed more easily.

<span class="mark">Modern medical technology has brought us automated insulin delivery systems, linking your insulin pump, a glucose sensor, and an algorithm. This marvel of innovation adjusts your insulin delivery every five minutes based on sensor-detected glucose levels. These automated insulin delivery systems are so effective, that they are recommended for all people with typ</span>e 1 diabetes and those with insulin-deficient diabetes.

However, transitioning to an insulin pump isn't always straightforward. A thorough understanding of how the pump operates, its usage, and potential risks is essential. Ensuring accurate and safe insulin dosing demands precise pump settings and prompt action in case of malfunctions or blockages.

This comprehensive module on insulin pumps is here to guide you. The videos are created by Dr. Inge Van Boxelaer, Diabetotech's founder and an endocrinologist at the St-Lucas Hospital in Gh<span class="mark">ent in Bel</span>gium. All content has been reviewed by Dr. Liesbeth Van Huffel, endocrinologist at the OLV Hospital in Aalst in Belgium, and Melissa Holloway from the UK and Renate Groenendijk from the Netherlands, two individuals living with type 1 diabetes.

Be assured, all the information provided here is completely independent from the insulin pump manufacturing companies, ensuring a genuine perspective on the advantages and drawbacks of different insulin pumps.

Before we delve into the details, it is important to note that these videos are not intended as medical advice. Always consult with your healthcare provider before making any adjustments to your diabetes therapy.

In the next 45 minutes, you will learn:

- The available insulin pumps and their key differences,

- How an insulin pump works and how to set basal rates and bolus insulin ratios,

- <span class="mark">What to keep in mind when changing the insulin</span> and infusion set,

- How to respond to an alarm,

- And how people with diabetes experience the transition from pens to a pump.

This module will equip you with insights into the typical start-up education of an insulin pump, enabling you to have more confidence to initiate or guide others with an insulin pump.

Enjoy the journey!
