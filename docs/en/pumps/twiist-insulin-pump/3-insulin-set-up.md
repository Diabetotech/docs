## 3. Insulin set-up (4.5 min)

Welcome to our detailed guide on insulin delivery set-up for the twiist Pump.

In this section, you'll learn how to program basal insulin, suspend delivery, set a temporary basal rate, and give a bolus when Loop is off. Use of the pump when Loop is on is covered separately in the twiist AID System module.

We'll also outline key do's and don'ts to help you use your pump safely and efficiently.

### 3.1 Programming basal insulin

Tap Settings on the Toolbar. Tap Therapy Settings. Tap Basal rates. Here you can edit your basal rate and add new time blocks if needed. To change your basal rate for a specific time block, just tap the value, adjust the setting, and save it.

### 3.2 Suspending insulin

To suspend insulin delivery, tap the pump status icon to open the pump menu, then select "Suspend Delivery." Tap "Confirm" to stop insulin. You'll hear the delivery suspended sound, unless Quiet Mode is on.

To resume insulin, tap the temporary status banner or the insulin suspended icon, then tap "Resume Delivery." The screen will show "Communicating with Pump," and you'll hear the delivery sound when insulin starts again.

### 3.3 Temporary basal

When Loop is off, you can use a Temporary Basal to adjust your basal rate for a short time, such as during exercise or illness.

Tap the heart-shaped button on the toolbar. Choose the duration and percentage for insulin delivery, then tap "Start Temporary Basal." Authenticate with your iPhone passcode, Face ID, or Touch ID.

While a Temporary Basal is active, a status banner shows its progress and lets you stop it if needed. The insulin delivery icon will show whether the basal rate is increased or reduced.

### 3.4 Delivering a bolus

When Loop mode is off, you can give a bolus using the built-in bolus calculator.

Your carb ratio, insulin sensitivity, and correction range are set when you first start the twiist app but can be changed anytime in Therapy Settings. Here you can view these settings and tap any value to adjust it.

* For a meal bolus, tap the meal icon, enter your carbs, and enter your current glucose.
* For a correction bolus, tap the triangle icon and enter your glucose value.

You can review and modify the suggested dose before delivering the bolus.

### 3.5 Do's & don'ts

* The twiist app lets you set two key delivery limits: maximum basal rate and maximum bolus.
    * Set the max basal rate to about 3–4 times your highest scheduled basal and adjust if needed.
    * Set the max bolus slightly above your largest expected meal dose (up to 25 units) to prevent overdosing while keeping flexibility.
* The app uses a fixed insulin action duration of 6 hours, but you can adjust the peak time: Go to Settings, tap Therapy Settings, tap Insulin Model and select either:
    * Fast-Acting Adults (with a peak at 75 minutes) or
    * Fast-Acting Children (with a peak at 65 minutes).
* Before changing settings or delivering insulin, authentication is required with your iPhone passcode, Face ID, or Touch ID.
* The One-Button Bolus feature is off by default and should only be enabled with your healthcare provider's guidance. To turn it on, go to Settings, tap Therapy Settings and toggle on One-Button Bolus.
    * Each press of the pump button adds 1 unit to the bolus (up to 10 units total). You'll hear a confirmation sound each time.
    * When ready, press and hold the pump button until you hear the playback of the selected dose.
    * If the playback is correct, press and hold again until you hear the Delivering sound.

That's it! You now know how to set up basal insulin, program temporary adjustments, and deliver boluses on your twiist pump in manual mode. In the next section, we'll cover how to replace the reservoir and infusion set to keep your pump ready for reliable insulin delivery.
