## 5. Pump alarms

Welcome to this lesson on alarms and alerts for the twiist insulin pump.

The twiist app and pump play sounds to help you recognize the system's current status.

Ensure your iPhone volume is turned up, vibration is turned on, and the twiist App and CGM Notifications and Critical Alerts are enabled. Notifications are played based on your iPhone ringtone and alert volume.

The twiist app displays messages in order of priority: Alarms, Alerts, and Notices — with the Urgent Low Glucose alert taking the highest priority.

When two or more alerts occur, the alerts are displayed in the twiist app with the most recent alert first and progressing to earlier alerts as they are addressed.

To ensure twiist App notifications are displayed on your iPhone lock screen, Notifications must be enabled within your iPhone settings. Notifications inform you of conditions that require attention. Critical Alerts inform you of conditions that require immediate action.

### 5.1 Alarms

Alarms occur when insulin delivery is stopped and require immediate attention. Check your blood glucose and take the appropriate action to treat high or low glucose. When an Alarm occurs, the twiist App plays the Alarm sounds (3 beeps + 2 beeps, occurring 2 times, and repeating every 10 seconds) and displays a message with an Alarm symbol followed by the Alarm name.

* If the alarm has not been resolved within 5 minutes, the alarm notification will be played regardless of your iPhone volume or Focus settings.
* After an additional 5 minutes, the alarm notification will be repeated on your iPhone and will play on your twiist Pump every 15 seconds until silenced.
* Silenced alarms that have not been resolved will display again after 15 minutes.
* If the twiist App and pump are out of communication during an alarm, Alarm sounds will play on the pump immediately.
* Alarms can frequently be resolved by changing the cassette.

Common alarms include: Cassette Empty, Cassette Error, Cassette Not Attached, Line Blocked, Pump Battery Empty, or Pump Error.

### 5.2 Alerts

Alerts display information about the twiist Automated Insulin Delivery system. Alerts should be addressed as soon as possible.

When an Alert occurs, the twiist App plays the Alert sounds (2 beeps) and displays a message with an alert symbol followed by the Alert name. The Alert sounds are played based on your iPhone ringtone and alert volume. If your iPhone is silenced or Focus is enabled, alert notifications may be missed. Alert sounds repeat every 15 minutes until the alert is dismissed.

Possible alerts include: Cassette Low, Pump Battery Low, Interrupted Bolus, Pump Inactive, Reattach Cassette, No Communication, and others.

CGM alerts display information about your current glucose and CGM status. CGM alerts should be addressed as soon as possible. The Urgent Low Glucose alert is displayed with higher priority than all other alerts. CGM alerts (except Urgent Low Glucose) are only played on your iPhone. Alert sounds repeat every 5 minutes until the CGM alert is dismissed.

### 5.3 Notices

Notices provide information about the status of the twiist Automated Insulin Delivery system. CGM and Loop notices are displayed to keep you informed about your CGM and your automated insulin delivery.

Examples:

* Critical Alerts Disabled
* Internet Connection Required
* No Communication
* Notifications Disabled
* Phone Battery Low at Start of Software Update
* Prime Stopped
* Pump Battery Low
* Security Features Disabled
* System Time Updated
* The twiist App is Closed
* Workout is Still Active
* CGM Ending Soon (3 Days / 1 Day / 1 Hour)
* CGM Ready
* Loop is Off / On / Waiting
* Timer Expired

### 5.4 Do's & don'ts

* To understand what triggered an alarm, review the details in the Event History menu. You can tap Active Insulin or Insulin Delivery Charts to access and view past events.
* Quiet Mode limits some sounds played by the twiist pump when in communication with the twiist app. Quiet Mode is OFF by default, but it can be enabled via the Therapy Settings.
* 3 additional alerts can be changed via the Therapy Settings:
    * An optional low insulin alert to occur when the system estimates between 20 and 100 Units of insulin remaining in your cassette.
    * An optional Phone Out of Range Alert that notifies you when the twiist pump and app have been out of communication for 1 to 120 minutes.
    * A Pump Inactive alert is set to 60 minutes by default, but can be set to 15, 30, 60, or 90 minutes.
* When not using the twiist pump for an extended period, remove the cassette, remove the battery and re-attach the pump cover.

Now you know what to do in the event of the pump's primary alarm, and you are more prepared. Therefore, if you hear a sound in your pump, there's no need to panic. Just look at the screen and instructions are typically provided there. Consult the user manual or call customer care if the message is unclear.
