## 4. Reservoir and infusion set replacement

Welcome to this lesson on cassette and infusion set replacement with the twiist Pump!

In this guide, we'll walk through your options for infusion sets, step-by-step instructions for changing both the cassette and infusion set, and some tips to help you get the best results.

### 4.1 Different infusion sets

The twiist pump works with several luer-lock infusion sets, including Contact Detach, Inset, Quick-set, Silhouette, Comfort, and Cleo 90.

Currently, it is distributed only with the Cleo 90 infusion set, which has a 6 mm, 90-degree Teflon cannula and 61 cm (or 24 inch) tubing.

Replace the infusion set every 2–3 days, following the manufacturer's guidance or your healthcare provider's instructions.

### 4.2 Supplies

To change the cassette, infusion set, and insulin, gather the following: room-temperature insulin, a Cleo infusion set with inserter, the dedicated tubing, an alcohol swab, a Skin Tac or Skin Prep wipe, a needle and syringe, a new twiist cassette, your twiist pump base with a charged battery, and your phone with the twiist app open.

### 4.3 Cassette & Infusion Set Replacement

If it's your first time using the pump base, you'll need to set up your twiist AID system and pair your pump.

* Create an account at www.twiist.com/register and download the twiist app from the App Store. When you open the app, you'll be prompted to pair your CGM and pump base.
* Insert a fully charged battery into the pump base. The charger light turns green when it's ready. Tap Pair Pump, then select your pump's serial number from the list. If it doesn't appear, press and hold the pump button for five seconds until you hear a ready beep, then release it. You can pair the pump by scanning a photo or entering the PIN manually.
* After pairing, the pump will check for software updates. Follow the on-screen instructions if an update is available.

Once your pump base is connected, you can change the cassette. There are four steps: cassette replacement, filling the tube, infusion set placement, and cannula filling.

Before starting, wash your hands with soap and water and let them dry.

**Step 1 – Cassette replacement**

* In the twiist app, open the pump menu and tap Change Cassette. The app will guide you through each step, starting with suspending insulin delivery.
* Disconnect the infusion set from your body by pinching the connector arms inward and sliding it fully open to detach the tubing cap from the cannula base.
* Twist off the old cassette and tubing from the pump base by turning counterclockwise. Replace the battery in your pump base with a fully charged one.
* Attach a new empty cassette by aligning the ridges on the pump bump and cassette top. Push down and rotate the pump clockwise until the ridge aligns with the tubing.
* Wait for the self-test to finish. Then tap Scan Cassette, position the cassette's QR code inside the green box, and tap OK.

**Step 2 – Filling the tube**

* Select your Cassette Fill Volume. The twiist system remembers your previous setting. Choose a volume between 100 and 300 units, enough for two to three days, then tap Next.
* Clean the insulin vial top with an alcohol wipe. Open a new syringe and needle, attach them securely, and draw air into the syringe equal to the fill volume. Insert the needle into the vial, and inject the air. Keeping the syringe in place, turn the vial upside down and slowly pull the plunger to draw insulin to the selected fill volume. Tap the syringe to remove air bubbles, then withdraw the needle.
* Open new tubing and attach it to the cassette by twisting the luer connectors together.
* Insert the needle gently into the cassette filling port and push the plunger down to fill the cassette. Hold the plunger down while removing the needle. If resistance occurs, remove and reinsert the needle gently. Dispose of the syringe and needle in a sharps container, then tap Next.
* On the twiist app, tap Start Prime. The pump will make priming sounds as insulin moves through the tubing. Watch for drops at the tubing tip — this confirms priming is complete. Tap Stop Prime, confirm with Yes, or tap No to repeat the step.

**Step 3 – Infusion set placement**

Now you're ready to apply the Cleo infusion set.

Choose an insertion site where you normally inject insulin and where the pump and CGM can stay on the same side of your body. Rotate sites regularly to maintain good absorption.

When selecting an infusion site:

* Avoid areas that may constrict the site, such as the waistline, beltline, or where the body naturally bends.
* Avoid the area 5 cm or 2 inches around your belly button.
* Keep the infusion site at least 2.5 cm or 1 inch from your CGM.
* Avoid placing the infusion site on scars, moles, stretch marks, and tattoos.
* Avoid broken skin, cuts, and scrapes.
* Keep the infusion site at least 2 to 5 cm or 1 to 2 inches from your previous infusion site.

Wipe the skin with Skin Tac for enhanced adhesion. Twist off the cap from the inserter and check the adhesive and insertion needle.

Press the inserter firmly on the site for about 20 seconds. Remove it straight or with a rolling motion. Press to secure the adhesive.

Pinch the side connector arms inward and slide open. Attach the site connector so the tubing faces the desired direction. Push until you hear a click. Make sure the needle has retracted, replace the cap, and dispose of materials properly.

In the twiist app, confirm the Cannula Fill Volume and Cassette Fill Volume, then tap Done. Tap Start Basal to begin insulin delivery. The pump will play the "Delivering" sound as basal delivery starts.

**Step 4 – Cannula Filling**

The infusion set cannula is normally filled during a cassette change. If you replace your infusion site separately, you'll need to fill the cannula manually.

For the 6 mm Cleo infusion set, fill with 0.2 units of insulin.

* In the twiist app, open the Pump Menu and tap Fill Cannula.
* Select Cannula Fill Volume to adjust if needed, then tap Fill Cannula and Confirm to deliver the insulin.

### 4.4 Do's & don'ts

* Change your infusion set every 2–3 days. The app does not remind you, so track this yourself.
* Check your blood glucose 1–2 hours after inserting a new site to confirm proper insulin delivery.
* The cassette can be used for up to 3 days and changed separately from the infusion set.
* Inspect tubing regularly for air bubbles. If present, perform a cassette change.
* Some users report fewer cassette failures when they wear the cassette with the tubing pointing downward to prevent air in the tubing. Others adjust the clip to make this positioning easier.
* For infusion site success:
    * Rotate sites to avoid irritation or scarring.
    * Avoid contact with lotions, sunscreen, or insect repellent, which can damage plastic parts.
    * Many users find Skin Tac or Skin Prep works better than alcohol wipes for adhesion.
    * If the Cleo set sticks to the applicator, loosen the seal at the small dot or use a fork to hold the set while retracting the inserter.
* Pairing reminders:
    * Pairing a pump and iPhone requires Wi-Fi or cellular connection.
    * The twiist app can connect to only one pump at a time.
    * To pair a new pump, go to Settings → Configure Devices → Delete Pump before adding the new one.

You did it! You now know how to replace the twiist pump's cassette and infusion set from start to finish. With practice, this process will become second nature, and you'll be able to complete it quickly and confidently. Remember: if you get stuck, your twiist User Guide, customer support, and your diabetes care team are always there to help.
