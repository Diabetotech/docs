## 2. Pump Operation

Welcome to the twiist Pump Operation module!

By the end of this section, you'll have a thorough understanding of how the twiist pump works, its components, charging process, navigation menus, and important do's and don'ts for safe and effective use.

### 2.1 Parts & Buttons

The twiist pump has a reusable pump base with a lifespan of 3 to 4 years. At the bottom sits the insulin cassette, which you fill with insulin and replace every 3 days.

Both can be separated by twisting the pump base to the left, hence the name "twiist Pump".

To reattach, align the ridge on top of the pump-bump with the ridge on the cassette. When the ridges are aligned, turn the pump clockwise toward the infusion set tubing. When the ridge on the pump-bump is aligned with the infusion set tubing, the cassette has been fully attached.

* The cassette can be filled with up to 300 units of insulin via a fill port on the side.
* The pump base holds a rechargeable battery and a pump button.

This pump button provides the ability to: Confirm that delivery is in progress, Resume insulin delivery, Place the pump into pairing mode (when not connected to a cassette), or Deliver a One-Button Bolus if this is enabled. Press and hold the pump button for 3 seconds to resume delivery or confirm that delivery is in progress.

The pump is shipped with a pump cover, pump clip, 4 rechargeable batteries and dedicated battery chargers.

### 2.2 Charging the Pump

When you change the cassette, you should also replace the battery in the pump base with a fully charged one. A fully charged pump battery will power the pump for 3 days.

The pump batteries typically take 6 hours or less to charge. The pump batteries should be charged using the provided battery charger and wall power adapter.

* A green light on the charger indicates that the pump battery is fully charged.
* Orange means that the battery is charging.
* Red means something is wrong — the battery is not charging as expected.

### 2.3 Home Screen & Pump Menus

The twiist app shows you how the system is working through the icons and charts on the Home Screen. You can see what is happening at a glance or you can tap the icons and charts for additional information. Some features of the twiist app work differently when Loop is off.

The Home Screen has three main areas: Status, Charts & a Toolbar.

**Status icons**

The top of the Home Screen displays the different Status icons.

* The **CGM Status icon** displays your current glucose and glucose trend arrow when you are connected to a CGM. When your CGM reports HIGH or LOW, check your fingerstick glucose and treat your high or low glucose. Tap the CGM Status icon to open the CGM Status screen and view additional CGM information.
* The **twiist Loop status icon** shows the status of your AID system.
    * A closed circle means Loop is on, and your basal insulin delivery is adapted to the predicted glucose value. The color of the circle indicates how much time has gone since the last successful Loop adaptation based on the last received CGM value.
    * An open circle means Loop is off, and you are receiving your scheduled basal rate. You can use the twiist system with Loop off, with or without a CGM.
    * You can tap the twiist Status icon for more information.
* The **insulin delivery status icon** shows whether basal insulin is being delivered at your scheduled basal rate, or whether your basal delivery has been reduced or increased.
    * A solid line with a value of zero indicates that your basal is being delivered at your scheduled rate. It does NOT mean your basal rate is zero units per hour.
    * A dropped icon indicates that your basal is reduced by the value shown. A reduced basal rate is always indicated with a negative value such as -1.0 U/hr.
    * A raised icon indicates that your basal is increased by the value shown.
* The **twiist Pump Status icon** displays an estimation of how much insulin remains in your cassette, and your pump battery level.
    * Grey icons mean more than 20% of your insulin remaining in your cassette and your pump battery has more than 20% charge. Yellow icons mean less than 20%, and red icons mean less than 10%.
    * Note that the cassette and pump battery icons are independent from each other and may show different percentages and colors.
    * When you tap the pump status icon, the pump menu will be displayed.

The twiist app may display a **Temporary Status Banner** below the Status icons. You will see and can interact with this banner for example when a bolus is being delivered, when a temporary basal is active, when a cannula fill is in progress or when you have turned on a Pre-Meal or Workout Preset.

**Charts**

The largest portion of the Home Screen has four charts. Here you can see your glucose and how your insulin and the carbs you have entered are working to impact your glucose. Touch and hold anywhere within the Charts to see more information. As you hold your finger on the chart, you can slide left and right to view data for a specific time. Rotating your phone will allow you to see additional history.

* The **Glucose Chart** displays your glucose prediction, and shows your eventual glucose of 6 hours into the future. The twiist system will update this prediction every 5 minutes as it works to bring your glucose into your Correction Range. When Loop is off, no glucose predictions are made or displayed.
* The **Active Insulin Chart** displays how much insulin has been delivered but has yet to take action within your body. Your current Active Insulin is displayed at the top-right corner. This can be a negative value if you have less insulin active in your body than you usually have scheduled for this time.
* The **Insulin Delivery Chart** displays both your basal and bolus delivery history over the last few hours. Your current basal rate is displayed in the top-right corner.
    * Basal is displayed as a series of orange bars. Bars above the orange line indicate increased basal, while bars below the orange line indicate reduced basal.
    * Boluses that have been delivered are displayed as orange triangles.
    * Tap the Active Insulin or Insulin Delivery Charts to see your Event History. The twiist app displays up to 14 days of event history.
* The **Active Carbohydrates Chart** displays the carbs that you have entered into the twiist app and how the twiist system expects them to impact your glucose over time. Your current active carbohydrates are displayed in the top-right corner.

**Toolbar**

The bottom of the Home Screen displays a Toolbar with buttons for some of the most common features:

* The **Carb Entry** button is used to enter details about what you are eating and to get a bolus recommendation.
* The **Pre-Meal Preset** temporarily lowers your Correction Range before a meal to reduce post-meal glucose spikes. The Pre-Meal Preset will be in effect for up to one hour, until canceled, or until carbs are entered.
* The **Bolus Entry** button allows you to deliver a correction bolus to bring down high glucose. No bolus is recommended when it is predicted that an additional bolus will lead to hypoglycemia.
* The **Workout Preset** temporarily raises your Correction Range for activity. Workout Preset will be in effect for the time you indicate when you activate it, or until you cancel it.
* The **Settings** button opens the Settings Menu to turn Loop On or Off, view and edit your Therapy Settings, and other settings.

### 2.4 Do's & Don'ts

* Attach your twiist pump to a belt or clothing using the supplied pump clip. Slide the pump into the clip with the pump bump facing upward.
* You can also place the pump in a pocket. It's about the size of an Omnipod or Tandem Mobi pump. Check that the infusion set tubing is not pinched.
* Some users secure the pump directly to their body with tape or a Mobi Adhesive Pocket. Others personalize their pump and clip with stickers.
* In order to maximize the signal between your pump and CGM, and minimize communication interruptions, your pump and CGM should be placed on the same side of your body. The twiist logo on the pump should face away from your body.
* The twiist pump is not waterproof. It may tolerate minor splashes, but immersion in water may lead to pump malfunctions.
* Sequel MedTech does not provide a spare pump base, so take good care of your pump.
* If you need to clean a pump base, gently wipe it with a clean, damp cloth using mild soap and water or you can use an alcohol wipe to clean the surface. Do not submerge the pump or expose it to running water.
* The twiist pump time and date automatically synchronizes with your iPhone through the twiist app. Make sure the time and date is set correctly on your iPhone. If the time and date is not set correctly, delivery errors may occur resulting in low or high blood glucose.
* Note that the twiist app can be switched to dark mode at any time via your iPhone settings.
