## **<u>5. Pump alarms</u>**

Welcome to this lesson about the alarms on the Kaleido insulin pump.

The handset and or pump can alert you about various issues through beeping or vibrating. <span class="mark">Kaleido classifies these alerts as either low or high priority alarms.</span>

If multiple alarms happen simultaneously, the intelligent alarm system will first notify you of high priority alarms, followed by low priority ones. Each alarm must be acknowledged individually before the next one appears. The alarm sounds and vibrations will repeat until you acknowledge the message on your handset. To do so, simply press the Enter button after reading and understanding the alarm message.

Let's delve into these alarms and share some extra tips.

### ***5.1 High priority alarms***

High priority alarms demand immediate attention. They will notify you with 10 short beeps on your pump or handset and <span class="mark">will stop your pump if it's related to the pump, or turn off your handset if it's related to it.</span>

You’ll get a high priority alarm related to the pump if:

• Your pump battery is empty and infusion stopped

• Your insulin cartridge is empty

• The insulin cartridge you’re using has expired

• Your pump has an insulin blockage

• Your insulin cartridge has come out of your pump while it was pumping

• There’s a problem with your Kaleido pump

You’ll get a high priority alarm related to the handset if:

• There’s a problem with your Kaleido handset

### ***5.<span class="mark">2 Low priority alarms</span>***

<span class="mark">Low priority alarms are important but not urgent. They notify you with 2 or 3 short beeps and won't stop your pump or handset.</span>

You’ll get a low priority alarm related to the pump if:

• Your pump battery is getting low

• You’ve been using the same insulin cartridge for three days

• Your insulin cartridge volume is getting low

You’ll get a low priority alarm related to the handset if:

• Your handset battery is getting low

• There is a communication error between your handset and your pump

• Your Insulin on Board has been reset

• Your handset battery is empty

• Your handset and pump software versions do not match

### ***5.3 Do's & don’ts***

Here are some important points to remember:

- You should always keep your handset within reach in case there’s an alarm and keep the handset battery sufficiently charged.

- Old alarms can be reviewed in the History Menu. Active alarms will be visible if you wake your handset from sleep mode.

- If you are unsure how to respond to an alarm, consult the user manual or contact customer service.

- For some pump and handset alarms, the handset will show a 4 digit number in the bottom right corner. The alarm number can also be found in your History on the handset and in the manual. In case you are not able to resolve the issue, contact customer service and they will ask you for the 4 digit number.

- If you temporarily won't be using your handset, you can simply switch it off. <span class="mark">To do this, hold the power button until you can confirm to turn off the handset.</span>
