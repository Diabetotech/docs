## **<u>4. Replacing the insulin reservoir and infusion set</u>**

Welcome to this lesson on how to replace the insulin reservoir, or cartridge, and infusion set of the Kaleido insulin pump.

In this video, we will cover the available infusion sets, the 4-step process for changing the insulin cartridge and infusion set, and last but not least, some important do's and don'ts.

<span class="mark">It’s important to note that if you intend to use the Kaleido pump as an Automated Insulin Delivery system with Diabeloop, you should not pair your pump with the Kaleido handset, but instead with the DBLG handset.</span>

### ***4.1 Different infusion sets***

The Kaleido pump has one type of infusion set with a teflon cannula in a 90 degree angle. You can choose between a cannula of 6 or 9 mm.

Additionally, Kaleido provides two lengths of tubing, which are included with the insulin cartridge. Most people use the short tubing (5 cm or 2 inches) to keep the pump close to the infusion site on the skin, but you can also opt for the longer tubing (30 cm or 15 inches) to wear the pump in your pocket.

Below this video you can find an overview and comparison of the available infusion sets of different insulin pumps, their tubing and cannula lengths, and filling volumes.

### ***4.2 Insulin cartridge and infusion set replacement***

<span class="mark">To replace the insulin cartridge and infusion set, gather the following supplies: your Kaleido handset, a charged pump, a body and pump patch, insulin at room temperature, a new insulin cartridge, a syringe and needle, an infusion set, an infusion set inserter and alcohol wipes.</span>

<span class="mark">It’s important to note that if you intend to use the Kaleido pump as an Automated Insulin Delivery system with Diabeloop, you should not pair your pump with the Kaleido handset, but instead with the DBLG handset.</span>

#### ***\#1 Step: Filling the insulin cartridge and the tubing***

Start off by washing your hands and preparing a clean flat surface to work on. Be sure to check the expiry date and ensure the packaging and insulin cartridge are not damaged. If there is any damage present or if it's expired, discard and use another one. Start by opening the packaging from your insulin cartridge by slowly pulling from the bottom. Place your insulin cartridge so that the numbers 1 and 2 are facing towards you.

Remove the syringe from its packaging. Without moving the plunger, push the tip of the syringe firmly into Hole 1. Carefully pull the plunger all the way up to the top of the syringe. You should feel some resistance. This will remove any excess air from your insulin cartridge. Now, remove the syringe from Hole 1 and push the plunger back towards the tip of the syringe.

Next, remove your needle from its packaging. Push the needle onto the tip of the syringe until they are connected. Take your room temperature insulin vial, check the expiry date and clean the rubber stopper with an alcohol wipe before use. Remove the needle cap from the needle. Draw the plunger back to fill the syringe with 2.5ml of air. Place the insulin vial in front of you onto the flat surface. Carefully push the needle through the rubber stopper of the insulin vial. Push the plunger all the way in to put all the air from the syringe into the vial. Keep holding the plunger down. Turn the vial with the syringe upside down and slowly pull the plunger down till you’ve drawn 2.5 ml of insulin. Give the syringe a few taps on the bottom to push any air bubbles up towards the needle. Push the plunger upwards to push the air bubbles back into the insulin vial. Ensure that there is 2.5ml of insulin in your syringe. Check the syringe for air bubbles, some small champagne sized air bubbles are acceptable. Remove the syringe from the insulin vial, be careful not to touch the needle. Turn the syringe so the needle is pointing downwards and give a few firm taps on the syringe. This will ensure that any air bubbles that remained in the syringe go upwards near the plunger.

Holding your insulin cartridge, gently place the needle into Hole 2. Slowly push down the plunger, filling your cartridge with insulin. You must fill your insulin cartridge completely. Stop pushing the plunger when you see a drop of insulin coming out of Hole 1 (stop before any air bubbles get transferred into the insulin cartridge). It is normal that some excess insulin remains in the syringe after filling the insulin cartridge. Remove the syringe and dispose of the needle and syringe into a sharps bin.

Now you will need to prime and then remove your insulin cartridge from the filling cradle. First, hold the top of your insulin cartridge on both of the round edges and squeeze it further into the filling cradle. This will unhook the plastic tab. Then squeeze downwards fully into the filling cradle twice and then release the insulin cartridge. It is important that this action is done twice. You can now remove your insulin cartridge from the filling cradle. Turn your insulin cartridge over. Squeeze the tabs on either side of the insulin cartridge connector and then lift up to unclip it.

Finally, have one last check to make sure there are no large air bubbles in the reservoir. If large air bubbles are present, discard and use another insulin cartridge. If they are champagne sized air bubbles, that’s generally okay and you can use this insulin cartridge. If you see any air bubbles in your insulin cartridge, check your User Manual for what is acceptable or what is not. Your insulin cartridge is now fully filled, primed and ready to use! Now it is time to prepare and wear your pump.

#### ***\#2 Step: Infusion set placement***

You’ll need to consider where your pump will be placed in relation to your infusion set. Start by washing your hands and choose where you’d like to position your infusion set. Prepare the area by cleaning it with an alcohol wipe. Allow your skin to dry naturally. Check the expiry date and ensure the packaging and infusion set are not damaged or expired before use. Peel open the paper lid. Remove the protective circle of plastic that’s sitting on top of your infusion set. Press the button on the top of your inserter to make sure it’s ready to use. Take your inserter and line it up with the top of your infusion set. You can do this by making sure the gap in the base of the inserter sits above the tabs on the infusion set’s plastic cap. Push the inserter firmly onto the top of your infusion set. Push down until you hear the Infusion set click into your inserter. Lift your inserter out of the packaging and check if the needle is straight. Be careful not to touch the needle! It’s important that it stays sterile. Peel the paper backing off the sticky part. Try not to touch the sticky area. If possible, use one hand to hold your skin taut of the area where you want to place your infusion set. Use the gap in the base of your inserter as a guide for where your insulin cartridge connector will connect to your infusion set. Use your other hand to position your inserter on your skin. When your inserter is in the right place, firmly press the button at the top of your inserter. Keep the inserter pressed firmly onto your skin to aid the insertion. This will push the infusion set into your body. Now pull your inserter away from your body in a straight line without twisting it. Put the inserter aside for now. Be careful not to touch the needle. Gently rub the adhesive plaster around the edge of your infusion set to make sure it’s sticking to your skin properly. If the Infusion set is peeling from the skin at this stage, it’s unlikely that it will last for three full days of wear. If this is the case, you should remove it and try again with a new one. Now, you need to dispose of your needle. Don’t forget to keep your inserter safe, ready for next time!

#### ***\#3 Step: Preparing your pump***

Turn your handset on. Have your fully filled insulin cartridge ready to go. Hold your pump with the pump’s white parts facing towards you. Hold your insulin cartridge above the space and line it up, tipping it to feed the tubing into the cartridge tubing groove. Gently press the cartridge tab to feel it click into place. Never push your insulin cartridge straight in or put excessive force on it. This can damage the pump and cartridge. When inserted correctly, you will hear 2 beeps to signal your pump is now ready to be connected to the handset. If everything’s good, the back of the insulin cartridge will sit flush with the back of the pump.

Only if this is the first time you use this pump, a message will pop up on your handset, letting you know your pump is ready to pair. Make sure your pump color and serial number matches the pump you are trying to pair. When you are ready to pair, press Enter to select Pair. The pairing process may take up to a minute. Once pairing is successful, the Handset will show a confirmation message. The pump will beep three times to let you know pairing was successful. If you have already used and paired your pump previously, your Handset will ask you to connect to your pump. Press Enter to select connect and now your pump is connected.

Now your handset will ask if you have used a new, fully filled insulin cartridge. If you just filled your insulin cartridge, press Enter to select Yes.

It is time to apply your patches! You will need your pump and body patch. Take your pump patch and peel off the paper backing. This will reveal the patch’s adhesive. Stick the pump patch to the bottom of your pump, so that it covers the insulin cartridge and the fuzzy hexagon shape is facing outwards. Now take your body patch and without removing its paper backing, stick it to your pump patch by pressing together the hexagon shapes. Make sure the body patch is aligned with your pump.

Support the weight of your pump, and clip the connector at the end of your insulin cartridge tubing into your infusion set. You’ll know it’s secure when you hear the two clicks as the right and left hand sides connect. Check whether the connector is smoothly aligned with your infusion set. You should not feel bumps or gaps. Holding onto your pump, peel off the paper backing from the top of your body patch. Now it is time to place your pump where you’d like to wear it. Be careful not to stretch or kink your insulin cartridge tubing. When you are using the 30 cm <span class="mark">or 15 inch</span> insulin cartridge tubing, place your pump in a secure place. If you are using a new infusion set, you need to prime it first. Select the cannula length you are using and press Enter. Press Enter again to select Yes to start pumping. Your cannula is now being primed. Once priming has completed, your handset will confirm that pumping has started. Your pump is now pumping!

#### ***\#4 Step: Removing your pump***

If your insulin reservoir is empty or when you have used the pump for 3 days, you’ll have to stop and remove your pump. You can do that by going to the status section and tap stop pumping. You'll receive a confirmation.

Then you can remove your pump. Start by washing your hands. Gently squeeze the tabs on both sides of your insulin cartridge connector. Lift it up to unclip it from your infusion set. Lift the pump away from your body patch. Next, gently remove the body patch from your skin, as well as the patch from the back of your pump. Take your insulin cartridge out of your pump by pulling on the tab. And finally, remove your infusion set. Dispose of your infusion set, insulin cartridge, body and pump patches according to local regulations.

Place your used pump on the charging dock. It will take about 2-4 hours to fully charge your pump.

### ***4.6 Do's & don’ts***

Here are some important points to keep in mind:

- You should replace your insulin cartridge when it's empty or after 3 days of use. This is also a good time to switch between your two pumps for recharging. The infusion set should also be changed at least every 3 days. Ideally, you can do everything together, but if needed, you can replace the parts separately. If your insulin cartridge empties before 3 days, or your pump battery is empty, you can do a separate replacement more quickly.

- The Kaleido pump is compatible with Novorapid and Humalog with a concentration of 100 U/ml.

- The cartridge always has to be filled with 200 units of insulin.

- Do not reuse insulin reservoirs. Doing so may contaminate your insulin, lead to infection, and possible under or no delivery of insulin.

- Do not reuse body and pump patches or use them longer than three days. Doing so may lose their adhesive properties, which could result in your pump becoming detached during use.

- Make sure that the back of your pump is clean and dry before you attach a pump patch. This will ensure a better bond between the patch and pump, and prevent your pump from coming loose.

- Do not pair your pumps to your Kaleido Handset if you intend to use the system <span class="mark">as part of the Diabeloop System.</span>

- Once you’ve placed your pump where you want it, try not to play with it too much or bring it into contact with water during the first hour of wear. This will make sure it’s stuck securely.

- Remember to check your blood glucose levels 2 hours after inserting a new infusion set. This is the only way to confirm proper insertion and insulin delivery. It's best to avoid changing your infusion set right before bedtime.

- <span class="mark">When temporarily removing your insulin pump, it’s best to close the protective cover on your infusion set. If you leave the protective cover open, dirt may enter into the opening which may cause infections or occlusions.</span>
