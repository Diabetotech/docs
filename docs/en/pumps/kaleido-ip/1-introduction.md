## **<u>1. Introduction</u>** 

Welcome to this <span class="mark">module</span> on the Kaleido insulin pump! In this <span class="mark">module,</span> we will explore the features and operation of the Kaleido insulin pump.

This modular insulin pump can be used as a separate st<span class="mark">andalone pump, but also in an automated insulin delivery system such as DBLG1 from Diabeloop with Kaleido. This module specifically focuses on the Kaleido insulin pump, using the dedicated Kaleido handset, providing valuable insights into its usage and functionality.</span>

<span class="mark">The Kaleido pump is a semi-patch pump operated using a handheld device, and known for its wide range of color options.</span> Each user is provided with two pumps and can select from ten different color combinations for their pumps and handset, offering choices from vibrant orange to classic black.

Both the handset and pumps are rechargeable and reusable for a period of four years. However, it's important to note that the insulin reservoir, or cartridge, and infusion set should be replaced at least every three days.

One of the standout features of the Kaleido pump is its compact size, making it one of the smallest durable pumps available. It can be attached to the skin using a short tube measuring 5 cm (or 2 inches), or it can be conveniently carried in a pocket with a longer tube of 30 cm (or 12 inches).

In the general module on insulin pumps, you have already learned about the fundamentals of insulin pumps, such as their operation, setting basal and bolus insulin, changing insulin and infusion sets, and managing alarms.

In the upcoming videos, we will showcase Kaleido in action, focusing on specific aspects of the onboarding education for this semi-patch pump. Please be aware that the content presented here may change without prior notification. The User Manual for the medical device remains the primary document for ensuring the safe operation, and it’s important to note that these videos are not intended as medical advice. Always consult your healthcare provider before making any adjustments to your therapy.

These videos have been created by Dr. Inge Van Boxelaer, the founder of Diabetotech and an endocrinologist at the St-Lucas Hospital in Ghent in Belgium. The content has been reviewed by Dr. Sarah <span class="mark">Siegelaar, an endocrinologist at Amsterdam UMC in the Netherlands, as well as Vanessa Buitendijk form the Netherlands, an individual living with type 1 diabetes wh</span>o uses the Kaleido pump. Their contributions ensure that the information provided is unbiased and independent.

By the end of these videos, you will have a comprehensive understanding of how the Kaleido insulin pump works, how to set basal and bolus insulin, how to change the insulin reservoir and infusion set, and what actions to take in case of an alarm.

Whether you are a caregiver looking to guide individuals using Kaleido, or a user preparing for a successful start-up, these videos will equip you with valuable knowledge, along with practical tips and tricks to enhance your experience with Kaleido.

We wish you the best of luck on your journey with the Kaleido insulin pump!
