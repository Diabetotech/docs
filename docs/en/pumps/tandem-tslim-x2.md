## **<u>2. How the Tandem t:slim X2 insulin pump works</u>**

Welcome to this informative video on how the Tandem t:slim X2 pump works! In this segment, we will explore the various aspects of the pump, including its parts, buttons, charging process, unlocking mechanism, and the different screens and menus.

We will focus on the pump with Control IQ software. It's important to note that there is another version available with the Basal IQ software, which features a slightly different menu. However, we will not delve into the details of the Basal IQ software in this module.

### ***2.1 Parts & buttons on the pump***

Let's start with an overview of the parts and buttons on the pump. The Tandem t:slim X2 pump has a sleek design. At the front of the pump, you'll find a large color touchscreen that allows for easy navigation.

The back of the pump features a slide-out reservoir connected to a tube via a luer lock. It's important to note that on the Tandem pump, the luer lock is situated 5 cm away from the pump itself. From there, a longer tube extends to the infusion set, which is attached to your skin.

At the top of the pump, you'll find one button. The color of this button indicates the pump's status:

- A green light every 30 seconds signifies normal operation.

- Three green lights every 30 seconds indicate the pump is charging.

- A yellow light every 30 seconds signifies a warning or reminder.

- A red light every 30 seconds indicates a fault or alarm.

On the side of the pump, there is a USB port used for charging the pump and accessing data.

### ***2.2 Charging the pump***

To charge the pump, you have a rechargeable lithium battery inside. It's best to charge the pump daily for 10-15 minutes using a laptop, PC, wall socket, car, or power bank. It's recommended not to charge it overnight. If left uncharged, the battery will last approximately 5 days, so it's important to charge it for at least 30-60 minutes every 5 days.

Make sure to slide the pump's USB cover aside gently to avoid damage. You will hear a few short beeps when the battery starts charging. During the charging process, you can keep the pump connected to your body.

When the battery is completely flat, the screen may not turn on immediately upon connecting the USB cable. However, the button will light up green every 2 seconds until there is enough battery power to activate the screen.

### ***2.3 Unlocking the pump***

To unlock the pump, simply press the button and then enter the unlock code, which is a sequence of 1-2-3. Pressing the button can also turn off the screen if desired.

### ***2.4 Home screen and pump menu***

Now let's explore the home screen and pump menu. The home screen displays various information, depending on whether you are connected to a glucose sensor such <span class="mark">as a Dexcom or FreeStyle Libre sensor. At the top left, you'll find the battery status, while the top right shows the remainin</span>g insulin in the pump. The bottom section displays the insulin on board, indicating the amount of active insulin from previous boluses and automatic changes of the basal insulin.

Tapping the remaining insulin on the top right takes you to a status screen, where you can view the active personal profile, basal insulin delivery, time of the last bolus, and your current settings used to calculate a bolus. Additionally, you can also see the last calibration, sensor start time, transmitter battery, and mobile connection information.

On the right side of the screen, you can adjust the number of hours displayed in the sensor graph, ranging from 1 to 24 hours. Tapping on the sensor graph enlarges the view, but please note that basal insulin delivery is not visible in this view.

By tapping the Tandem logo, you can always return to the home screen.

Through the options menu, you can access various settings, including Stopping or resuming insulin delivery, Load, Activity, My Pump, My CGM, Device Settings and History.

- Under "My Pump," you can access different options such as Personal Profiles, Control IQ, Alerts & Reminders, and Pump Info. The alerts and reminders section allows you to set up various reminders and customize alerts, such as the low insulin level alert and the Auto-Off alarm.

- Within "my CGM," you can start/stop the sensor, calibrate the CGM with finger prick values, set CGM alerts, and enter the transmitter ID (this can only be done when the sensor is stopped).

- In "Device Settings," you can customize the screen timeout duration, set the time and date, adjust the sound volume for Alarms, Alerts, Reminders, Keypad, Bolus, Quick Bolus, and Fill Tubing, and even set a security PIN. The security PIN can be useful for individuals, particularly children, who may not be operating the pump themselves.

- Lastly, the "history" option allows you to review all the pump and CGM history in a logical manner.

Here are a few important tips to keep in mind:

- <span class="mark">The pump is waterproof, but only for 30 minutes until a depth of</span> 0.9 meter (or 3 feet).

## **<u>3. Insulin setup</u>**

Welcome to our detailed guide on insulin delivery setup for the Tandem t:slim X2 insulin pump.

We'll explore how to program basal insulin, control temporary basal rates, and administer insulin boluses. <span class="mark">Additionally, we'll share some crucial do's and don'ts to enhance your pump experience.</span>

### ***3.1 Programming basal Insulin*** 

Programming basal insulin in the Tandem t:slim X2 insulin pump is unique as it requires you to input both basal insulin and bolus calculator settings in the same time segments.

- To access your personal profile settings first tap options, next tap my pump, tap personal profiles, tap the plus icon to create a new profile using the on-screen keypad.

- Enter a profile name up to 16 characters and tap the green check mark icon. Tap press to set up to begin setting insulin delivery settings. The first settings you enter always start at 12 AM.

- Next to basal, tap press to set up. Using the on-screen keypad, enter your basal rate and tap the blue checkmark icon.

- Next to correction factor, tap press to set up. Using the on-screen keypad, enter your correction factor and tap the blue check mark icon.

- Next to carb ratio tap press to set up. Using the on-screen keypad, enter your insulin to carbohydrate ratio and tap the blue check mark icon.

- Next to target BG tap press to set up. Using the on-screen keypad enter your target BG and tap the blue checkmark icon.

- Review all of the values you just entered and tap the green checkmark icon. To confirm your settings, tap the blue check mark icon if your data is correct or tap the x icon to make changes.

- If you need to add more time segments tap the plus icon or tap the back arrow icon to program your bolus settings. When adding more time segments, any settings that you entered in the previous time segment are copied and appear in the new segment. This allows you to simply adjust only the specific settings you want rather than having to enter them all over again.

- On the add segment screen next to start time tap press to setup. Using the on-screen keypad enter the time that you want the segment to begin and tap the blue check mark icon. Remember that this segment will be active from the start time until 12 AM or until the next segment you enter.

- When you have confirmed your settings and are done adding time segments, tap the back arrow icon to program your bolus settings. First tap the bolus settings panel to get started.

- Next tap insulin duration. Using the on-screen keypad enter the desired time for the duration of insulin action and tap the blue check mark icon.

- Next tap max bolus. Using the on-screen keypad enter the desired amount for your maximum bolus size and tap the blue check mark icon.

- Next tap the carbohydrate slider to turn carbs on meaning the bolus calculator will use your carb ratio when calculating boluses.

- Now review all the values you entered and tap the green check mark icon. The pump will th<span class="mark">en ask you to confirm your settings, tap the blue check mark icon to confirm or the x icon to make changes.</span>

- <span class="mark">When you have confirmed your settings tap the Tandem logo to return to the home screen. If you need to add additional personal profiles repeat the steps outlined in this vid</span>eo.

- You may also duplicate an existing personal profile. Then select edit to change only certain settings to create a new profile.

To stop basal insulin, select 'Stop insulin' under Options. When insulin is stopped, the home screen will show the status “All deliveries stopped”. Restarting basal insulin can be done in the same way via Options.

### ***3.2 Temporary basal***

For temporary basal control, navigate to 'Activity' under Options and select 'Temporary basal'. Here, you can set the percentage and duration of your choice. When it's on, an orange 'T' appears in the status bar. You can always manually turn off the temporary basal, which reverts the 'T' back to the current system settings and insulin delivery status icon.

### ***3.3 Delivering a bolus***

Administering a bolus is straightforward. From the start screen, hit the bolus button, input the carbohydrate grams and, if necessary, a finger prick value. If paired with a Dexcom G6, the last glucose value will auto-fill. The pump recommends an insulin dose, which you can override.

A handy tip is the option of extended bolus delivery. When confirming a bolus, you can opt to make it an extended bolus and adjust the percentage and time duration as needed.

### ***3.4 Do's & don’ts***

- Ensure [<u>maximum bolus</u>](https://kajabi-storefronts-production.kajabi-cdn.com/kajabi-storefronts-production/file-uploads/blogs/34729/images/d88cd-28b-7fc4-b6b7-c8da8833002_Schermafbeelding_2023-04-15_174210.png) limits are set slightly above expected bolus doses. This defaults to 10 units, but you can set it between 1 and 25 E

- It's recommended to set the Target BG value at 110 mg/dl or 6.5 mmol/l, as this is consistent with Control IQ's default value. Once Control-IQ technology is turned on, the default Target BG is set to 110 mg/dl or 6.5 mmol/l.

-  If you need more than 25 units for the meal, you will get 2 alarms. [<u>At the first alarm</u>](https://kajabi-storefronts-production.kajabi-cdn.com/kajabi-storefronts-production/file-uploads/blogs/34729/images/bcb1d1-de6e-3621-44af-8d5da183a5_Schermafbeelding_2023-04-15_174405.png), confirm that you want to get the maximum bolus set. [<u>At the second alarm</u>](https://kajabi-storefronts-production.kajabi-cdn.com/kajabi-storefronts-production/file-uploads/blogs/34729/images/2bc8bbc-d8b6-ecd-c20a-fc3edf6a714c_Schermafbeelding_2023-04-15_174557.png), you must confirm that you also want to get the rest of the bolus.

- Advanced users can set up the Quick Bolus function which enables you to deliver a bolus by simply pressing a button. It is a way to deliver a bolus by following beep/vibration commands without navigating through or viewing the pump screen.

## **<u>4. Reservoir and infusion set replacement</u>** 

Welcome to this lesson on reservoir and infusion set replacement on the Tandem t:slim X2 insulin pump.

In this video, we will cover the different infusion set options, step-by-step instructions for replacing the reservoir and infusion set, and helpful tips for success.

### ***4.1 Different infusion sets***

The Autosoft 90 infusion set is the most widely used infusion set for the Tandem pump.

Other alternatives include the Autosoft 30, the Varisoft with an angled cannula, and the TruSteel with a steel cannula. A detailed overview of these infusion sets, including tubing and cannula lengths and filling volumes, can be found in the download below this video.

### ***4.2 Supplies***

Start by washing your hands and ensuring your workspace is clean. Gather these items: a reservoir, a pull-up syringe, a pull-up needle, an infusion set, your insulin (at room temperature), and your pump.

### ***4.3 Reservoir replacement***

To determine how much insulin to pull into the syringe, multiply your total daily dose by 3 (for 3 days) and add approximately 50 units for the tube filling. If using a vial, disinfect the port and inject air to help the insulin flow out easily.

Once filled, remove any air from the reservoir by inserting the needle into the filling port and drawing out air. Then, slowly inject insulin into the new reservoir.

Next, navigate to 'options' on your pump, choose 'charge', and then 'replace reservoir'. Disconnect the infusion set from your body and remove the old reservoir. Insert the new reservoir securely. The pump will detect the reservoir and suggest tube filling.

### ***4.4 Filling the tube***

Start with a new infusion set and its tube. Carefully open the packaging, then release the lid by applying pressure to the three points on the plastic sheath with one hand while pulling off the lid with the other. Remember, the Autosoft 90 system is quite delicate due to its soft plastic construction, so avoid bending it too much or touching the needle. Gently extract the tube from the sheath, taking care not to tug too forcefully. Now, connect the tube to the reservoir's luer lock and give it an extra quarter-turn clockwise to ensure a secure fit.

Next, activate your pump to begin the tube filling process. It is essential to keep your pump upright during this procedure so that any air bubbles can rise to the top. Pay close attention to the tube for the presence of air bubbles. Once you observe a few drops of insulin coming out from the needle, you can stop the filling process on the pump.

Important to note, you should never perform this process while the infusion set is connected to your body. Also, ensure that you fill the tube with a minimum of 10 units of insulin.

### ***4.5 Infusion set placement***

Now, we're ready to apply the infusion set. We will demonstrate the Autosoft 90 infusion set. Start by sanitizing your skin and peel away the paper backing from the infusion set patch. Be sure to keep the infusion set firmly in place on the needle during this process. Next, prep the insertion set by tugging on the white plastic until you hear a click. At this point, you can safely remove the needle guard. Locate one of the two openings on the insertion device and insert the tube. Position the prepared device on your cleaned skin and press the needle into place by applying gentle pressure on the two circular depressions. Press the white plastic one more time. Lastly, take care to gently remove the insertion device.

<span class="mark">You've now successfully replaced the insulin reservoir and infusion set! Remember to do this every three days, even if the reservoir is not yet empty.</span>

### ***4.6 Cannula Filling***

<span class="mark">When you fill the tubing of the Autosoft infusion set, the cannula is filled automatically. If you ever replace only the infusion set but keep the existing tubing, you should manually fill the cannula. This can be done through the same pump menu. Fill the cannula with 0.3 units for 6-mm needles or 0.5 units for 9-mm needles, then resume insulin delivery. Don't forget to</span> click "OK" when prompted to continue insulin delivery. If you forget, an alarm will sound as a reminder.

### ***4.7 Do's & don’ts***

- <span class="mark">The insulin approved in the Tandem t:slim X2 pump is Novorapid, Humalog and Novolog. Lyumjev is approved for use in Europe. Fiasp remains off-label.</span>

- Remember, 2 hours after inserting a new set, you should check your blood glucose. Checking your blood glucose is the only way to confirm your infusion set is properly inserted and that you're r<span class="mark">eceiving insulin.</span>

- <span class="mark">Prior to disposing of the insertion device, reattach the lid until it clicks.</span>

- <span class="mark">For swimming or showering, you can disconnect your tubing from the infusion set and apply the provided protective cover. To reconnect, simply remove the cover and snap the tube back on.</span>

<span class="mark">So now, you're equipped with the knowledge to replace the insulin reservoir and infusion set on the Tandem t:slim X2 insulin pump. If you stumble, don't worry – it's perfectly normal. Keep calm, follow the steps, and remember, if all else fails, there's always the free customer service helpline and a multitude of useful YouTube videos available. Also, don't forget that you can contact your diabetes te</span>am, who also have an on-call service, for medical support. Good luck!

## **<u>5. Pump alarms</u>**

In this video, we dive into the Tandem t:slim X2 insulin pump's alarm system. We'll discuss the different kinds of alarms, alerts, and reminders, and share some best practices.

When multiple reminders, alerts, or alarms are activated concurrently, the system displays them in the order of priority – alarms first, then alerts, and finally reminders. Each one requires individual confirmation until all are acknowledged.

### ***5.1 Alarms***

Alarms act as urgent notifications, alerting you to a current or imminent interruption in insulin delivery, such as when the insulin reservoir is depleted. These require immediate attention.

These alarms are signaled with three cycles of three tones or vibrations, as per your chosen volume/vibration settings. If left unacknowledged, alarms escalate to the maximum sound and vibration level and continue to repeat until the issue is rectified.

### ***5.2 Alerts***

Alerts inform you about safety conditions that you need to know about, like low insulin levels.

These alerts are expressed with two sequences of three tones or vibrations, following your volume/vibration preference. They continue to repeat until they are acknowledged, but they do not escalate.

### ***5.3 Reminders***

Reminders notify you about an option you've set, such as a reminder to check your blood glucose after a bolus.

Reminders notify you with a single sequence of 3 notes or a single vibration depending on your volume and vibrate settings. They repeat every 10 minutes until acknowledged. Reminders do not escalate.

### ***5.4 Do's & don’ts***

- A 'malfunction' alarm indicates a system fault. Take note of the fault code and contact customer service.

- If in doubt, refer to the user manual or call customer service.

- If you won't be using your pump for a significant period, put it in storage mode. To do this, connect it to a power source and hold the Screen on/Quick bolus button for 30 seconds. You'll hear three beeps before it switches to storage mode. Disconnect the pump from the power source. To deactivate storage mode, reconnect the charger.

You're now equipped with knowledge about the pump's primary alarms and what actions to take. Therefore, there's no need to panic when you hear an alarm from your pump. Simply check the display screen - it usually provides instructions. If the message isn't clear, refer to the user manual or call customer service. Best of luck!
