## **<u>2. Pump Operation</u>**

Welcome to the next part of your TouchCare Nano journey!

In this lesson, we'll explore the parts and buttons of the pump, walk you through the home screen and main menu of both the PDM and the EasyPatch app, and share some key tips to remember. By the end of this section, you'll have a clearer understanding of how the pump operates using either the PDM or the EasyPatch app.

There are 2 versions of the TouchCare Nano pump: 200 U and 300 U. While we’ll focus on the 200 U TouchCare Nano Pump, the 300 U patch pump looks and works very similarly.

### ***2.1 Parts & Buttons of the Pump*** 

The TouchCare Nano Insulin Patch Pump is made up of two main parts:

- a disposable insulin Reservoir Patch that you’ll change about every 3 days.

- and a small reusable Pump Base that can be reused for up to 4 years,

The Pump Base stores your insulin settings and communicates with the EasyPatch app or PDM via Bluetooth, with a range of up to 4 meters (approximately 13 feet) when there are no obstructions.

The disposable Reservoir Patch includes an insulin reservoir that can be filled through the fill port, an insulin delivery mechanism, a built-in battery, a buzzer, an adhesive patch to attach it to the skin, and a needle insertion system secured with a safety lock.

Once the Pump Base is attached to the Reserv<span class="mark">oir Patch, the 200 U TouchCare Nano insulin pump measures just 4 x 3 cm and weighs only 13.8</span> grams, making it the smallest and lightest insulin patch pump available.

### ***2.2 PDM Home Screen and Main Menu*** 

When you turn on the PDM, the **Home Screen** appears with a status bar at the top showing the battery level, any recent alerts, the current time, audio settings, and the status of the Bluetooth connection to the pump.

In the center of the screen, you'll see the current glucose value along with a trend arrow, the amount of insulin on board, the current insulin delivery status, current basal rate, and how much insulin remains in the reservoir. Just below that, a graph displays your glucose levels over the past 3 hours.

A colored ring surrounds the glucose value to show what kind of insulin delivery is active.

- A grey indicates that no pump is currently connected.

- green ring indicates basal delivery,

- dark green shows a temporary basal rate,

- blue means a normal bolus is being given,

- purple represents an extended bolus,

- and red signals that insulin delivery is paused,

If you swipe from right to left on the home screen, the Main Menu will appear. This menu includes icons for bolus, basal settings, suspending insulin delivery, patch management, sensor data, history, events, EasyLoop, and general settings.

Swiping in the opposite direction, from left to right, takes you to the pump Status Screen, where you can view more detailed information about your current insulin delivery.

## **<u>3. Insulin set-up</u>**

Welcome to the part where we set up your insulin delivery — don’t worry, it’s easier than it sounds! We’ll show you how to program your basal insulin delivery, set a temporary basal, suspending insulin, deliver an insulin bolus, and give you some simple tips to make it all go smoothly.

We’ll always first show you how to do it with the PDM, and then with the EasyPatch app.

### ***3.1 Programming Your Basal Insulin***

**If you’re using the PDM,** go to the Main Menu, and tap on ‘Settings,’ then ‘Insulin Pump,’ then ‘Basal Set-up.’ Tap Edit Basal,’ and Select ‘Standard’ to edit the pattern

Note: The active basal pattern is check-marked.

The edit screen appears. Tap +Add time segment to create and add a new segment. Enter the end time for this segment. Then tap Done. Your first time segment starts at midnight. The last basal segment always ends at 24:00 (or 12:00 pm).

Tap each section to type in the numbers. When you’re happy with them, tap ‘Save.’ If you need different settings at different times of the day, tap the plus icon to add more segments.

To Delete, select segment, slide to left on each and a delete button will appear. Tap Delete to delete the selected segment. Slide back to right to close Delete. The last segment cannot be deleted, and the starting time of all segments cannot be changed.

**If you’re using the EasyPatch app,** <span class="mark">to set up your basal rate on your EasyPatch app,</span>

<span class="mark">tap on the three lines in the top left hand corner to access the main menu. Tap on settings. Tap on insulin pump. Tap on edit basal pattern. Tap on Standard. You can add different time segments for the day depending on your need for different rates of insulin at different times. Tap add time segment to add a new time segment. You can adjust the insulin rate for each time segment by tapping on the blue number next to each time segment. Now tap done. If you want to delete a time segment, simply slide your finger across the time segment from right to left and then tap delete. Once you've completed the basal set-up, tap done in the top right hand corner of your smart device to save your basal rate.</span>

### ***3.5 Do’s & Don’ts***

- When you use an insulin pump, it’s always best to set your maximum basal and bolus delivery for safety. <span class="mark">On both the PDM and the EasyPatch app, this can be done via the settings. The maximum basal should be at least 2 times your highest current basal insulin delivery rate, and your max bolus should be set a little higher than your usual big</span>gest dose.

- The TouchCare Nano pump allows you to work with preset bolus amounts for Breakfast, lunch, dinner and snacks. These can be preset via the settings menu.

## **<u>4. Patch Pump replacement</u>** 

In this section, we’re going to show you how to replace your TouchCare Nano Patch Pump.

In just a few easy steps, we'll guide you through the process of changing a TouchCare Nano Pump. We'll cover everything from connecting the pump base to the PDM or EasyPatch app, filling the Reservoir Patch with insulin, priming it, and applying the pump to your body.

### ***4.1 Pump Replacement with the PDM*** 

**If it’s your first time using the pump base, you’ll need to connect it to the PDM.** Just go to the main menu, tap on Settings, then choose Insulin Pump, and tap Add Pump Base. You can use your phone’s camera to scan the QR code on the back of the pump <span class="mark">base or the box it came in. Or, if you prefer, you can enter the serial number manually.</span>

<span class="mark">**Once the pump base is connected, it’s time to attach it to the reservoir patch.** First, wash your hands with soap and water and let them air dry. Then, open the reservoir patch package and place the patch on a clean flat surface. Take the pump base and gently press it into the patch at a 90-degree angle until you hear a click. After that, press again at a 45-degree angle to make sure it’s securely attached. You’ll hear four beeps when everything is correctly connected.</span>

<span class="mark">**Next, it’s time to fill the patch with insulin.** Start by cleaning the top of your insulin vial with an alcohol wipe. Attach the needle to the syringe and pull down the plunger to draw insulin. If you’re using an insulin vial, before drawing insulin, inject an amount of air into the vial equal to the volume of insulin you plan to withdraw. Tap the syringe gently to make air bubbles rise, then push the plunger slightly to remove the bubbles. Insert the needle straight into the fill port on the bottom of the patch. Pull the plunger back just a bit to remove any trapped air, then withdraw the needle. Remove any remaining bubbles from the syringe, then reinsert the needle and slowly fill the patch with insulin. When you’re done, remove the needle and put the protective cap back on before safely disposing of the syringe.</span>

<span class="mark">**Now you’re ready to prime the pump.** Make sure the patch pump is placed on a clean surface and kept at least 1 meter or 3.2 feet away from magnetic objects like phones or other pumps. Hold the pump with one hand and press the needle button down with the other hand until it locks halfway. In the app, tap Prime. The priming process takes a little time, and it’s important not to remove the safety lock while it’s priming.</span>

<span class="mark">**When priming is finished, the app will show you that it’s time to attach and insert the patch.** Choose a clean, flat, and pinchable spot on your body. Apply the patch horizontally on the abdomen, back, or buttocks, or vertically on the upper arm or thigh. Wipe it with an alcohol wipe and let it dry completely. Slide off the safety lock and peel off the adhesive liners starting from the side with the insulin fill port. Be careful not to press the needle button yet. Place the patch on your skin and press down firmly, especially around the edges of the adhesive and the area near the needle by pressing the center of the patch for a few seconds. Then, press the needle button in one quick motion. You’ll feel it lock in place. In the app, tap Continue — and that’s it! Your patch pump is now active and ready to deliver insulin.</span>

**When it’s time to remove the patch, start by deactivating it in the app**. Go to the main menu. Select patch, then select deactivate patch. Slide to deactivate patch. Follow the instructions to remove your Reservoir patch. Insert the needle eject tool vertically into the needle withdrawal hole and press vertically to eject the infusion needle. Gently lift the edge of the adhesive tape from your skin and remove the entire patch pump. We recommend that you wipe around the tape with alcohol wipes or baby oil to make the tape peel off easily. Fold and break the tab of the reservoir patch. Remove the pump base from the old Reservoir patch. Discard the used Reservoir patch according to your local waste disposal regulations and reuse the pump base.

### ***4.3 Do’s and don’ts***

Here are some important points to keep in mind

- S<span class="mark">ome people use a Skin Barrier product or extra medical tapes to make the wearing experience of the patch pump more comfortable. Medtrum can provide an alternative patch for users of the 200 U patch pump who experience an allergic reaction to the adhesive layer.</span>

- <span class="mark">The TouchCare Nano Patch pumps are compatible with Humalog, NovoRapid, Admelog, Apidra, Lyumjev and Fiasp with a concentration of 100 U per milliliter.</span>

- <span class="mark">The TouchCare Nano Pump has a fixed 90-degree steel cannula of 5 mm or 0.2 inch.</span>

- <span class="mark">The 200 units TouchCare Nano pump holds 200 U of insulin and can be worn for up to 3 days. If you require more than 200 units of insulin within 3 days, the 300 units TouchCare Nano pump will be more suitable for you. It is reassuring to know that the pump is not automatically disabled after three days or a fixed period. This flexibility allows you to choose a convenient and calm moment for a patch change.</span>

- <span class="mark">Remember to check your blood glucose levels 2 hours after inserting a new pump. This is the only way to confirm proper insertion and insulin delivery. It's best to avoid changing the pump right before bedtime.</span>

- <span class="mark">During a patch change, inspect the contact pins on the Pump Base for any discoloration and clean them with an alcohol wipe if needed. The pump is sensitive to moisture and dirt, and a small number of users have reported pump errors—most likely due to poor contact between the patch and the pump base. To minimize this risk, always place the pump base carefully and clean the contacts with an alcohol wipe at each change.</span>

- <span class="mark">If you ever feel unsure, don’t panic! Take your time, follow the steps, and if needed, reach out to custo</span>mer service or your diabetes care team.

## **<u>5. Pump alarms</u>**

Let’s talk about alarms and notifications on your TouchCare Nano Insulin Patch Pump — these are here to keep you safe and informed.

Like <span class="mark">in most insulin pumps, there are 3 types of notifications: alarms for high and medium pri</span>ority, alerts for low priority, and reminders.

### ***5.1 Alarms*** 

Alarms are triggered by serious or potentially serious conditions. You must respond to the alarm by taking appropriate action in order to clear the alarm condition. For example: When Alarm “PATCH EXPIRED” occurs, the Lock Screen and Alarm screen display the following screen.

- If it is a high priority alarm, the PDM or EasyPatch app will display an alarm message with instructions and a red triangle with three exclamation marks. The PDM or EasyPatch app will emit 10 beeps or vibrations and the patch pump will emit 3 beeps.

- If it is a medium priority alarm, the PDM will display an alarm message with instructions and a red triangle with two exclamation marks. The PDM or EasyPatch app will emit 3 beeps or vibrations and the patch pump will emit 3 beeps.

If a Pump alarm is not cleared within 10 minutes, both your PDM or EasyPatch app and Patch Pump will make a siren sound until the alarm is cleared.

### ***5.2 Alerts*** 

Alerts are triggered by conditions that may require your attention. Alerts are less

serious than alarms. You must respond to an alert by pressing buttons and/or taking actions.

For example: When Alert “PATCH BATTERY LOW” occurs, the Lock Screen and Alert screen display the following screen.

The PDM or EasyP<span class="mark">atch app displays an alert message with instructions and an empty triangle with an exclamation mark. The PDM or EasyPatch app can beep or vibrate 1 time every 3 minutes, and the</span> patch pump can beep 1 time.

The following table lists alert messages for PDM or EasyPatch app. The following table lists alert messages for Patch Pump.

### ***5.3 Reminders***

Reminding messages are automatically displayed to remind you of a condition, function or event. Reminding messages include the notifications you get after setting reminders and the low-priority reminding notifications. A Message requires you to press buttons to clear it and/or to take action if necessary.

<span class="mark">For example: When Message “BASAL RESUMED” occurs, the Lock Screen and Message screen display the following screen. Your PDM emits two beeps and/or one vibration every three minutes, three times in total.</span>

With the TouchCare Nano PDM or the EasyPatch app, you can set a specific reminder called the **Bolus Reminder**. For example, if you choose a 2-hour reminder, and you forget to deliver a bolus between two time points — let’s say time A and time B — you’ll automatically get a reminder at time B. It’s a helpful way to make sure you don’t miss your insulin dose.

### ***5.4 Do’s and don’ts***

- You can customize how you receive alerts. Go to the main menu, then tap on Settings, then General Settings, and finally Audio Options. Here, you can choose between sound, vibration, both, or none. If you set both Audio and Vibrate to "Off," your PDM won’t <span class="mark">make a sound or vibrate for most CGM alerts — except in urgent situations. For example, if your glucose drops below 56 mg/dL (or 3.1 mmol/L), or stays above 250 mg/dL (or 13.9 mmol/L), for more than 3 hours during auto mode, your PDM will vibrate once every three minutes; if you don’t clear the alert within nine minutes, a loud siren will sound until you acknowledge it. On the EasyPatch app, the urgent low or high alerts will repeat once every minute until cleared.</span>

- If you ever feel unsure, it’s totally okay to call customer service or check your user guide. There’s also a complete list of all alarms in the manual.

- If you use the EasyPatch app, you can turn on push notifications so alerts pop up right on your phone. Just check your app settings and make sure notifications are enabled.

- And if you ever need a break, the Snooze function within the CGM alerts lets you temporarily silence alerts — just make sure to check back in later.

- If you won’t be using your pump for a while, be sure to deactivate the patch pump and disconnect the pump base from your PDM or EasyPatch app.

Remember, alarms and alerts are there to help — not to stress you out. Now that you are aware of what to do, stay calm, follow the prompts, and reach out for help if needed. You’re doing great! In the event that the message is unclear, refer to the user manual or contact customer service. Good luck!!
